The Art of the Interview: Techniques for Extracting Compelling Stories


In the world of journalism, storytelling is key. Whether you’re a seasoned reporter or an aspiring journalist, mastering the art of the interview is essential for extracting compelling stories. A great interview can make all the difference in capturing the essence of a person’s experiences, thoughts, and emotions, and bringing them to life on the page. In this blog post, we will explore some techniques that can help you conduct impactful interviews and uncover captivating stories.

1. Research and Preparation

Before conducting an interview, it’s crucial to thoroughly research your subject. Familiarize yourself with their background, achievements, and any relevant news or events related to their work. This will not only demonstrate your professionalism but also help you ask informed and engaging questions. By showing that you’ve done your homework, you’ll be able to establish a rapport with your interviewee and create a comfortable environment for them to share their story.

2. Active Listening

The key to extracting compelling stories lies in active listening. Instead of simply waiting for your turn to speak, make a conscious effort to fully engage with your interviewee’s responses. Pay attention to the nuances in their tone, body language, and emotions. These subtle cues can provide valuable insights and lead to deeper and more revealing answers. By actively listening, you show respect and genuine interest in your interviewee’s story, which encourages them to open up and share their experiences in a more meaningful way.

3. Open-Ended Questions

To elicit detailed and thought-provoking responses, ask open-ended questions that go beyond simple yes or no answers. Instead of asking, “Did you enjoy your time at the university?”, try asking, “What were some of the most memorable experiences you had during your time at the university and why?”. This allows your interviewee to delve into their memories and provide rich, descriptive answers. Open-ended questions also give you the opportunity to uncover unexpected angles and perspectives, making your interview more captivating and unique.

4. Follow-Up Questions

Don’t be afraid to ask follow-up questions to dig deeper into a particular topic or to clarify any ambiguous responses. These questions can help you uncover hidden gems and untold stories that might have otherwise been missed. Follow-up questions also demonstrate your active engagement in the conversation and show your interviewee that you are genuinely interested in their story.

5. Create a Safe and Supportive Environment

Creating a safe and supportive environment for your interviewee is crucial for extracting compelling stories. Make sure to establish trust and reassure them that their words will be treated with respect and sensitivity. This will encourage them to open up and share their experiences more freely. Be mindful of their emotional state and provide empathy and understanding when discussing sensitive topics. Remember, the goal is not to exploit their vulnerabilities but to create a platform for their story to be heard and understood.

6. Allow for Silence

Silence can be a powerful tool during an interview. Sometimes, the most profound thoughts and reflections come in moments of quiet contemplation. Don’t rush to fill every gap in the conversation. Instead, allow for silence and give your interviewee the space to gather their thoughts and share something truly meaningful. Embrace these moments of silence as opportunities for deeper connections and more impactful storytelling.

7. Be Respectful and Ethical

Lastly, always maintain respect and ethical standards throughout the interview process. Obtain consent before recording or publishing any interview material, and ensure that your interviewee is comfortable with the final product. Be transparent about your intentions and provide them with the opportunity to review and approve any quotes or excerpts used in your article. Respecting your interviewee’s boundaries and wishes is not only ethically responsible but also fosters trust and strengthens the relationship between journalist and subject.

In conclusion, mastering the art of the interview is a skill that takes time and practice. By conducting thorough research, actively listening, asking open-ended and follow-up questions, creating a safe environment, allowing for silence, and maintaining respect and ethics, you can extract compelling stories that captivate your audience. Remember, the power of storytelling lies in the hands of the storyteller, and as a journalist, it’s your responsibility to bring those stories to light.
