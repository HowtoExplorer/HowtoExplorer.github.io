How to Handle Illegal Questions About Disabilities in Interviews


When it comes to job interviews, it’s essential to be prepared for all sorts of questions. However, there are certain questions that interviewers are not allowed to ask, especially those that pertain to disabilities. Discrimination based on disability is illegal and goes against the principles of equal opportunity employment. In this blog post, we will discuss how to handle illegal questions about disabilities in interviews and ensure that your rights are protected.

Understanding Illegal Questions

Before diving into strategies for handling illegal questions, let’s first understand what constitutes an illegal question. In the context of disabilities, any question that directly or indirectly asks about a candidate’s disability or medical history is considered illegal. Examples of illegal questions include:

  • Do you have any disabilities that may affect your ability to perform this job?
  • Have you ever been diagnosed with a mental illness?
  • How often do you need to take sick leave due to your condition?

Stay Calm and Composed

When faced with an illegal question about your disability during an interview, it’s important to remain calm and composed. Remember that you have the right to keep your personal information private. Take a deep breath and respond in a professional and assertive manner.

Redirect the Conversation

Rather than directly answering an illegal question, try redirecting the conversation to focus on your qualifications and skills. Politely steer the interview back to relevant topics by highlighting your accomplishments and experiences. For example, if asked about your disability-related absences, you could respond with:

“I appreciate your concern, but I believe my professional achievements and dedication to my work speak for themselves. I have a proven track record of meeting deadlines and exceeding expectations, which I believe are more relevant to this position.”

Provide General Information

If you feel comfortable, you can provide general information about accommodations or resources that could benefit all employees, not just those with disabilities. This approach helps educate the interviewer about inclusivity without disclosing personal information. For instance:

“I believe it’s important for employers to create an inclusive work environment for all employees. By providing flexible work arrangements or accessibility accommodations, companies can ensure that everyone can contribute their best. Implementing these practices can benefit not only individuals with disabilities but the entire workforce.”

Seek Legal Advice if Necessary

If you encounter repeated illegal questions or face discrimination during the interview process, it may be necessary to seek legal advice. Employment laws vary by country and state, so consult with an attorney specializing in employment law to understand your rights and legal options.

Conclusion

Handling illegal questions about disabilities in interviews can be challenging, but it’s crucial to assert your rights and maintain professionalism throughout the process. Remember to stay calm, redirect the conversation, and focus on your qualifications. By advocating for yourself and educating employers about inclusivity, you can contribute to a more equal and diverse workforce.

Note: This blog post is intended for informational purposes only and should not be considered legal advice. Please consult with a qualified professional for specific legal guidance.
