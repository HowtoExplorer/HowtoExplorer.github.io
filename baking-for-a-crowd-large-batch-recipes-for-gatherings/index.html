Baking for a Crowd: Large Batch Recipes for Gatherings

3 min · 550 words

Are you planning a gathering or hosting a party? One of the biggest challenges when it comes to feeding a crowd is figuring out how much food to make. It can be quite a daunting task, especially when it comes to baking. But fear not! In this article, we’ll explore some delicious large batch recipes that will satisfy everyone and make your baking experience a breeze.

The Benefits of Large Batch Recipes

Baking for a crowd comes with its own unique set of challenges. You want to ensure that there’s enough food for everyone, without spending hours in the kitchen. That’s where large batch recipes come in handy. Not only do they save you time and effort, but they also allow you to focus on other aspects of hosting, knowing that your guests will be well-fed and satisfied.

Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Let’s start with a classic favorite: chocolate chip cookies. These timeless treats are always a hit at gatherings. Instead of making several small batches, why not whip up a large batch that can be easily divided and stored for later?

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 2 cups packed brown sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 4 cups chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing until just combined.
  6. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  7. Using a cookie scoop or spoon, drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until golden brown around the edges.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Tangy Lemon Bars

If you’re looking for a refreshing and tangy dessert, lemon bars are a fantastic choice. Their bright flavor and buttery crust are sure to please any crowd. This large batch recipe is perfect for parties or potlucks.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup lemon juice (about 6-8 lemons)
  • Zest of 2 lemons
  • 8 large eggs
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the flour, powdered sugar, and softened butter until crumbly.
  3. Press the mixture into a greased 9x13-inch baking dish to form the crust.
  4. Bake the crust for 15-20 minutes, or until lightly golden.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, flour, lemon juice, lemon zest, and eggs until well combined.
  6. Pour the lemon mixture over the warm crust.
  7. Return the baking dish to the oven and bake for an additional 25-30 minutes, or until the filling is set.
  8. Allow the lemon bars to cool completely before cutting into squares.
  9. Dust the bars with powdered sugar before serving.

Savory Spinach and Feta Muffins

Baking isn’t just about sweets! These savory spinach and feta muffins are a delightful addition to any gathering. They are packed with flavor and can be made ahead of time for added convenience.

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and black pepper.
  3. Add the spinach, feta cheese, Parmesan cheese, dill, and parsley to the flour mixture. Stir until well combined.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, melted butter, and eggs.
  5. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until just combined.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among greased or lined muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Conclusion

Baking for a crowd doesn’t have to be overwhelming. With these large batch recipes, you can easily satisfy everyone’s sweet and savory cravings. Whether it’s classic chocolate chip cookies, tangy lemon bars, or savory spinach and feta muffins, these recipes are sure to impress your guests and make your gathering a memorable one. So, get your apron on, preheat that oven, and let the baking begin!
