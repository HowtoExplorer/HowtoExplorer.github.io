Baking for a Crowd: Large-Batch Recipes for Gatherings


Are you hosting a gathering or planning a party? One of the biggest challenges when it comes to feeding a large group is figuring out what to bake. But fear not! In this blog post, we’ll explore some delicious large-batch baking recipes that are sure to satisfy even the biggest appetites. Whether you’re hosting a birthday party, a family reunion, or a holiday get-together, these recipes will have you covered.

The Benefits of Large-Batch Baking

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s talk about the benefits of baking in large quantities. First and foremost, it saves you time. Instead of making multiple small batches, you can prepare one big batch and be done with it. This means less time spent in the kitchen and more time enjoying the company of your guests.

Another benefit of large-batch baking is that it allows you to easily scale up or down the recipe to suit the number of guests you expect. Whether you’re hosting a small gathering or a big party, you can adjust the quantities accordingly.

Large-batch baking is also cost-effective. Buying ingredients in bulk is often cheaper than purchasing them in smaller quantities. Plus, you’ll save on electricity or gas by baking multiple items at once.

Recipe 1: Classic Chocolate Chip Cookies

Who can resist a warm, gooey chocolate chip cookie? This recipe yields approximately 48 cookies, making it perfect for a crowd. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Beat in the vanilla extract and eggs, one at a time, until well combined.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  6. Stir in the chocolate chips.
  7. Drop rounded tablespoons of dough onto ungreased baking sheets.
  8. Bake for 9-11 minutes, or until the edges are golden brown.
  9. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheets for a few minutes before transferring them to wire racks to cool completely.

Recipe 2: Savory Spinach and Feta Muffins

If you’re looking for a savory option, these spinach and feta muffins are a crowd-pleaser. This recipe yields approximately 24 muffins. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 4 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 2 large eggs
  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and dried thyme.
  3. Stir in the chopped spinach, feta cheese, and Parmesan cheese.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, melted butter, and eggs.
  5. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among greased or lined muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Recipe 3: Easy Sheet Pan Brownies

When it comes to feeding a crowd, it doesn’t get much easier than sheet pan brownies. This recipe yields approximately 36 brownies. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 1/3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup chopped nuts (optional)
  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar and melted butter until well combined.
  3. Beat in the eggs, one at a time, until well incorporated.
  4. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, and salt.
  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  7. Stir in the chopped nuts, if using.
  8. Pour the batter into a greased or lined sheet pan, spreading it evenly.
  9. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs.
  10. Allow the brownies to cool in the pan before cutting them into squares.

Conclusion

Baking for a crowd doesn’t have to be stressful. With these large-batch recipes, you can easily feed a group of hungry guests without sacrificing taste or quality. So the next time you’re planning a gathering, whip up one of these delicious treats and watch your guests’ faces light up with delight. Happy baking!
