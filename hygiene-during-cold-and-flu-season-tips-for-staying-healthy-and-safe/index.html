Hygiene during Cold and Flu Season: Tips for Staying Healthy and Safe


As cold and flu season approaches, it’s essential to take preventative measures to maintain our health and safety. Proper hygiene plays a significant role in combating the spread of respiratory illnesses. In this article, we’ll discuss practical tips and practices to help you stay healthy and avoid catching a cold or the flu.

  1. Wash your hands consistently and thoroughly

Washing your hands with soap and water is one of the most effective ways to combat germs and viruses. Ideally, you should wash your hands for at least 20 seconds, focusing on rubbing your fingertips, between your fingers, and under your fingernails. If soap and water are unavailable, use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer.

  1. Use a tissue, not your hands

When sneezing or coughing, use a tissue to cover your mouth and nose. Dispose of the tissue afterward and wash your hands immediately. Doing so prevents the spread of germs and viruses via direct contact.

  1. Maintain a healthy lifestyle

Certain habits and lifestyle choices can weaken your immune system, making you more susceptible to illness. To strengthen your resistance, sleep well, eat balanced meals, drink enough water, and exercise regularly. Staying physically fit and healthy is key to fending off infections.

  1. Stay home when unwell

Did you know that transmission of viruses can occur even when a person is in the early stages of a cold or flu? To protect others around you, stay home and avoid close contact with friends and family when you’re feeling under the weather. Rest and recuperate while minimizing your germs’ reach.

  1. Practice respiratory etiquette

Frequent handwashing isn’t the only precaution worth taking—respiratory etiquette is equally crucial. Avoid close contact with those around you when coughing or sneezing, and cover your mouth and nose with a tissue when possible. Likewise, do not share utensils, cups, or other personal items, as they can harbor germs.

  1. Sanitize common areas

The reality of life is that we can’t always avoid close contact with others. If you’re in a shared living environment, take advantage of available cleaning supplies to wipe down common areas such as doorknobs, keyboards, and phones. Sanitizing frequently-touched surfaces can contribute to a cleaner, healthier environment.

  1. Stay informed and stay prepared

Keep track of current health news and stay alert to new strains of viruses or illnesses. By being aware of the risks, you can better protect yourself. Furthermore, stock up on essential items such as tissues, cough drops, and over-the-counter medicines for relief when needed.

As cold and flu season approaches, being proactive in your hygiene habits could mean the difference between staying healthy or succumbing to a bout of illness. Incorporate these tips into your routine, and you’ll be better equipped to face the season with resilience and confidence. Though no strategy is 100% effective at keeping all germs and viruses at bay, taking proper care of yourself is an excellent place to start. Remember, staying healthy is a joint effort; protect yourself and in turn, help protect those around you.
