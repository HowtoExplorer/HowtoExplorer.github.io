The Best Food Documentaries to Watch This Year


Food documentaries have a unique way of capturing our attention, igniting our curiosity, and tantalizing our taste buds. They take us on a journey through diverse cultures, highlight pressing issues in the food industry, and celebrate the art and science of cooking. As we delve into the world of food documentaries, this year promises a rich selection that will inspire both your culinary endeavors and your understanding of the global food landscape. Here’s a curated list of the best food documentaries to watch this year that are sure to enrich your palate and spark conversations.

1. “Chef’s Table: Pizza”

The acclaimed series “Chef’s Table” returns with a focus on one of the world’s most beloved dishes: pizza. This season features the stories of renowned pizzaiolos from Italy to the United States, showcasing their craft, passion, and the cultural significance of pizza. Each episode offers an intimate look at the chefs’ dedication to their art, their personal journeys, and the innovations they bring to traditional recipes. Through stunning cinematography and engaging storytelling, viewers will gain a deeper appreciation for what makes pizza a global phenomenon.

2. “The Game Changers”

This groundbreaking documentary challenges the conventional wisdom surrounding meat consumption and presents a compelling case for plant-based diets. Featuring elite athletes, scientists, and nutrition experts, “The Game Changers” highlights the benefits of a plant-based lifestyle, not just for health but also for athletic performance. The film takes viewers through various studies and personal anecdotes, making it an eye-opening experience for anyone interested in nutrition and wellness. It’s a must-watch for those looking to rethink their dietary choices and explore the impact of food on our bodies.

3. “Salt, Fat, Acid, Heat”

Based on the best-selling cookbook by Samin Nosrat, this documentary series takes viewers on a culinary journey across the globe. Each episode explores one of the four essential elements of cooking: salt, fat, acid, and heat. Nosrat’s infectious enthusiasm for food and cooking shines through as she meets local chefs and home cooks, discovering the magic behind their signature dishes. The show not only educates viewers on the importance of these elements but also inspires them to experiment in their kitchens, making it a delightful watch for food lovers everywhere.

4. “The Last Pig”

“The Last Pig” offers a poignant and thought-provoking perspective on the ethical implications of meat consumption. The documentary follows the journey of a pig farmer who decides to confront the realities of his profession. Through his reflections and the lives of the pigs he raises, viewers are invited to contemplate the moral complexities surrounding animal farming and food production. It’s a powerful narrative that challenges audiences to consider their own choices and the impact of those decisions on the environment and animal welfare.

5. “Rotten”

This investigative series delves into the global food system, examining the complexities and challenges that come with food production and distribution. Each episode focuses on a specific food item, such as honey, garlic, or avocados, uncovering the hidden truths behind their journeys from farm to table. “Rotten” highlights issues such as fraud, exploitation, and environmental concerns, making it an essential watch for anyone interested in understanding the intricacies of food supply chains. It’s an eye-opening exploration that encourages consumers to be more mindful of their food choices.

6. “Cooked”

In “Cooked,” filmmaker Alex Gibney takes viewers on a sensory exploration of the transformative power of cooking. Based on Michael Pollan’s book, the series is divided into four episodes, each representing one of the four natural elements: fire, water, air, and earth. Through beautiful storytelling and stunning visuals, the documentary illustrates how cooking connects us to our culture, our history, and each other. “Cooked” is not just about food; it’s about the communal experience of sharing meals and the rituals that surround them.

7. “The True Cost of Food”

This documentary sheds light on the hidden costs of our food system, from environmental degradation to social injustice. “The True Cost of Food” explores the consequences of industrial agriculture, examining how our food choices affect the planet and the people who produce it. Through powerful interviews and impactful storytelling, the film emphasizes the importance of sustainable practices and ethical consumption. It’s a crucial watch for anyone interested in the future of food and the role we play in shaping it.

8. “Jiro Dreams of Sushi”

A classic in the realm of food documentaries, “Jiro Dreams of Sushi” follows Jiro Ono, an esteemed sushi chef in Tokyo, who is dedicated to perfecting his craft. The film provides an intimate look at Jiro’s relentless pursuit of culinary excellence and the philosophy he applies to his work. Through his story, viewers gain insight into the artistry of sushi-making and the deep respect for ingredients that defines Japanese cuisine. This documentary is a celebration of passion, discipline, and the pursuit of perfection in food.

9. “The Mind of a Chef”

This series offers a behind-the-scenes look at some of the most innovative chefs in the world. Each season features a different chef who takes viewers on a journey through their creative process, culinary philosophy, and inspirations. With a blend of cooking techniques, travel, and storytelling, “The Mind of a Chef” immerses audiences in the world of gastronomy. It’s a fascinating exploration of the artistry involved in cooking and the diverse influences that shape a chef’s culinary vision.

10. “What the Health”

From the creators of “Cowspiracy,” “What the Health” investigates the links between diet, health, and the food industry. The documentary challenges the health narratives propagated by major health organizations and advocates for a plant-based diet as a means to prevent chronic diseases. By featuring interviews with doctors, nutritionists, and individuals who have transformed their health through dietary changes, this film serves as a wake-up call for those seeking to improve their well-being through food.

Conclusion

Food documentaries offer a unique lens through which we can explore the world of cuisine, culture, and ethics. This year’s selection captivates the senses and sparks important conversations about our relationship with food. Whether you’re a culinary enthusiast, a health-conscious individual, or simply someone who enjoys a good story, these films provide insight, inspiration, and a deeper understanding of the food we consume. So grab your popcorn and get ready to be entertained and educated by these compelling documentaries that celebrate the diverse and complex world of food.
