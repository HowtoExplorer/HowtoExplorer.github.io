The Dos and Don'ts of Including References on Your Resume in 2023


In my many iterations, I have frequently observed the puzzlingly common exclusion of references from a professional resume. While this seemingly trendy practice might have gained prominence in recent years, I firmly believe that including well-curated references can drastically augment the credibility and potency of your resume. The right references can tell a powerful story, bridging the gap that your accomplishments cannot cover on their own. To help you navigate this crucial aspect of the resume-writing process, I have gathered essential dos and don’ts in this comprehensive guide.

Do: Opt for a Strategic Approach

Before you proceed to list your references, it’s critical to understand the underlying rationale. Including references on a resume is ultimately for showcasing how your former colleagues, supervisors, or educators can vouch for your accomplishments, work ethic, and expertise. Thus, ensuring that your chosen references align with the core message you’d like to convey in your resume is essential.

Furthermore, remember that it’s no longer common to have a separate references page. Rather, it’s more avant-garde to succinctly list one or two professional references within your resume, usually near the end. This allows potential employers to gauge your reputation in the field, thus confirming their belief in your competence before even evaluating your experience, qualifications, and achievements.

Do: Choose Relevant References Wisely

When picking your references, prioritize practicality and relevancy. As the adage goes, “quality over quantity.”

Instead of listing a multitude of references who once vouched for you, select the ones that can vouch for the exact skills and traits that the target job entails. The more specific and related the reference, the more persuasive it becomes. Paradoxically, an outdated or irrelevant reference can have a counterproductive effect and potentially damage your credibility.

Don’t: Leave Out Key Information

While brevity is essential, don’t be too cryptic. It’s crucial to include the full name, title, and company of each reference, as well as their contact information. However, taking it a step further and including a personalized note showcasing the relationship between you and the reference is an efficient way to convey the authenticity and importance of their endorsement.

Don’t: Overdo It

While the emphasis is on being strategic and selective, it’s equally crucial to avoid overdoing things. Including references excessively on your resume will not demonstrate enhanced expertise but might lead the employer to question your ability to choose wisely. Furthermore, as previously mentioned, only include one or two references; any more would be excessive and portray disarray.

Don’t: Be Too Similar to Connections in Your Network

While it might seem strategic to have a common reference to your professional connections or mentor, avoid appearing formulaic. Employers tend to appreciate diversity in references, which means having people from different levels of the hierarchy and from different professional backgrounds becomes crucial. This reinforces your adaptability and the broad network of support in your field.

Don’t: Skip the Update

Maintaining strong relationships with your references is critical. By keeping in touch and allowing them to know about significant professional achievements or opportunities, you are more likely to be at the forefront of their mind and potentially be more persuasive for any potential employers they encounter.

In summary, incorporating thoughtful and relevant references on your resume in 2023 can be a game-changer. A strategic approach to selecting your references, favoring quality over quantity, and adhering to a straightforward yet informative style can make all the difference. However, it’s equally critical to understand the ‘do nots’ of including references, as the wrong approach can diminish the validity of your entire resume. By maintaining a curated and strategic list of references, you can exhibit a well-rounded understanding of what employers seek, ultimately elevating your chances of landing your dream job.
