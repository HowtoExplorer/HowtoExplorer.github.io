The Dos and Don'ts of Cleaning Your Hands: Common Mistakes to Avoid


Keeping your hands clean is one of the most effective ways to prevent the spread of germs and infections. However, many people unknowingly make mistakes when it comes to hand hygiene. In this guide, we’ll explore the dos and don’ts of cleaning your hands to help you avoid common pitfalls and keep yourself and others healthy.

The Dos

1. Do Use Soap and Water

Using soap and water is one of the most effective ways to clean your hands. Be sure to lather your hands thoroughly, including the backs of your hands, between your fingers, and under your nails. This helps to remove dirt, grease, and germs effectively.

2. Do Wash for at Least 20 Seconds

The recommended time for handwashing is at least 20 seconds. Singing the “Happy Birthday” song twice can help you keep track of the time. This duration allows the soap to break down and remove germs from your hands effectively.

3. Do Dry Your Hands Properly

After washing your hands, use a clean towel or air dryer to dry them thoroughly. Damp hands can attract and spread germs more easily, so proper drying is essential.

4. Do Use Hand Sanitizer When Soap and Water Aren’t Available

When you don’t have access to soap and water, using an alcohol-based hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol is a good alternative. Apply the sanitizer all over your hands and rub them together until they feel dry.

5. Do Wash Your Hands at Key Times

Be sure to wash your hands at key times, such as before and after preparing food, before eating, after using the restroom, after touching animals, and after coughing or sneezing.

The Don’ts

1. Don’t Rely Solely on Hand Sanitizer

While hand sanitizer is convenient, it’s not as effective as soap and water at removing certain types of germs, such as norovirus, Cryptosporidium, and Clostridium difficile. Whenever possible, opt for soap and water.

2. Don’t Touch Your Face with Unclean Hands

Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands. These are common entry points for germs, and touching them with dirty hands can lead to infections.

3. Don’t Rush the Handwashing Process

Take your time when washing your hands. Rushing through the process may result in inadequate cleaning, leaving behind germs and dirt.

4. Don’t Overuse Antibacterial Soap

Regular soap is effective at removing germs, and overuse of antibacterial soap can contribute to the development of antibiotic-resistant bacteria. Stick to regular soap and water for everyday handwashing.

5. Don’t Neglect Hand Hygiene in Public Spaces

Public spaces, such as public transportation, shopping areas, and workplaces, can harbor germs. Be mindful of touching surfaces in these areas and wash or sanitize your hands as soon as possible afterward.

By following these dos and don’ts of hand hygiene, you can significantly reduce the risk of spreading germs and infections. Remember, clean hands are the cornerstone of good health, so make hand hygiene a priority in your daily routine.
