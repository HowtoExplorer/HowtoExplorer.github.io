How to Navigate Your Friendships with Your Partner: Avoiding the Elephant in the Room


Friendships, especially in committed relationships, can be a delicate balance. Sometimes, it’s like navigating a labyrinth with hidden pitfalls, with your partner by your side trying to find the path. How do you handle such situations, especially when the “elephant in the room” makes its unwelcome appearance?

First off, let’s define the problem. The elephant in the room refers to an issue or situation that everyone is conscious of but no one wants to talk about. Such situations often lead to discomfort and tension. In friendships with our partners, these can arise when friends from our pre-existing social circle are integrated with our new partner’s friends. To avoid the elephant in the room and ensure a smooth transition, we can follow these steps:

1. Address the issue outright

Unlike the elephant in the wild, the “elephant in the room” thrives on silence and avoidance. By addressing it initially, you can slash its life expectancy. Be open and honest about how you feel, and why you feel that way. This could be challenging, especially if the issue involves your partner’s feelings. Be prepared for an emotional response, but keep your communication lines open to ensure both parties are on the same page.

2. Encourage transparency

The cornerstone of any good relationship is transparency. Encourage your partner and friends to be open about their feelings, so they don’t have to hide them. Assure them that their honesty won’t damage the friendship but rather strengthens it. Candor can be difficult to navigate, especially for those unpracticed in its art. Guide them gently to understand that honesty is critical in forging strong bonds.

3. Be mindful of compounding relationships

Compounding relationships, where friends from before your partner’s social circle are integrated with new ones, can be tricky. Each relationship could have its own history and expectations, making it a challenge to navigate. Agree on common ground rules respecting each friend’s position and your partner’s bond, ultimately fostering a healthy balance of acknowledgment and respect.

4. Set and respect boundaries

Boundaries are an essential aspect of maintaining a healthy relationship. Talk to your partner about what feels comfortable for you and what you’d like to avoid. Setting boundaries demonstrates self-respect and ensures everyone is on board with what’s acceptable and not. Remember, boundaries are not about keeping anyone out, but rather about keeping you in.

5. Practice empathy

Empathy is a valuable tool in navigating friendships with your partner. Put yourself in your friends’ and partner’s shoes to understand their perspectives. Being empathetic fosters an environment of understanding and collaboration that makes resolving conflicts more comfortable.

6. Be prepared for change

People grow, and friendships evolve — that’s the essence of life. Be prepared for change, and avoid clinging to the past. Encourage your friends and partner to forge new bonds, keeping in mind that no relationship is static.

In conclusion, navigating friendships in a relationship is no walk in the park, but implementing these steps consistently can create clarity and foster healthy relationships. It’s essential to remember that every person’s needs and experiences differ, so continually evaluate and adjust your communication strategies accordingly. Remember, effective communication is not just about talking; it’s about understanding and cooperation — a bond that makes the difference.
