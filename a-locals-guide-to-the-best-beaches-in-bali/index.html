A Local's Guide to the Best Beaches in Bali


Bali, the Island of the Gods, has a coastline that stretches for over 250 kilometers. From the popular hubs of Kuta and Seminyak to the secluded coves of the eastern coast, every inch of Bali's shoreline has its own unique character and charm. As a local, I've had the privilege of exploring every nook and cranny, and I'm excited to share with you my insider's guide to the best beaches in Bali.

  1. Padang Padang Beach Located in the scenic village of Labuan Sait, Padang Padang Beach is a hidden gem that's off the beaten path. Also known as Labuan Sait Beach, this secluded cove is accessible by a scenic coastal walkway and offers breathtaking views of the Indian Ocean. The beach itself is a picture-perfect crescent of sand, surrounded by towering limestone cliffs and lush tropical gardens. The waters at Padang Padang are crystal-clear, making it an ideal spot for snorkeling and swimming. The locals are friendly and welcoming, making it a great spot for cultural immersion. Don't miss the stunning sunsets that can be seen from the beach, it's simply breathtaking.
  2. Balangan Beach Balangan Beach is located in Southern Bali, and is one of the island's most popular tourist spots. This stunning stretch of coastline is known for its picturesque white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and coral reefs that make it a popular spot for snorkeling and scuba diving. The beach is lined with coconut trees and offers a variety of amenities, including restaurants, bars, and sun loungers. When the tide goes out, the beach reveals a string of limestone reefs that are perfect for swimming and snorkeling. Be sure to head out early in the morning to catch the sunrise, or stay until late at night to see the beach light up with string lights - it's truly a magical experience!
  3. Lovina Beach Located in the north of Bali, Lovina Beach is a tranquil retreat that's unlike any other. This 11-kilometer stretch of coastline is where the sea meets the sky, and the result is breathtaking beauty. Lovina is a laid-back beach town that offers a relaxing atmosphere, where the pace of life is as slow as the current. Visit the Lovina Dolphin Show, where you can interact with beautiful dolphins in their natural habitat. Afterward, enjoy a leisurely stroll along the beach, watching the sunset over the sea. For dinner, try some of the local seafood at one of the many beachside restaurants - their nasi goreng is truly a culinary highlight!
  4. Nusa Dua Beach Nusa Dua Beach is a secluded cove located in the southeastern part of Bali. This picturesque beach is known for its white sand, crystal-clear waters, and towering limestone cliffs. Visitors can enjoy swimming, snorkeling, and diving in the waters, which are home to an incredible array of marine life. Explore the underwater world of Nusa Dua, where colorful fish swim alongside coral reefs. Check out the beach's funky beach bars and restaurants, which offer a fun and lively atmosphere for visitors. For those looking for adventure, head to nearby Crystal Bay for snorkeling and diving excursions.
  5. Geger Beach Geger Beach, or Crystal Beach as it's also known, is a stunning stretch of coastline in the south of Bali. This stunning beach offers breathtaking sunrises and sunsets, crystal-clear waters, and plenty of amenities, including restaurants, bars, and water sports. The beach is a favorite among locals, who come here to watch the sunrise, have a picnic, or simply relax on the sand. Head to the nearby Geger Point, which offers stunning views of Nusa Dua Bay - on a clear day you can see all the way to Lombok!
  6. Keramas Beach Keramas Beach is a world-class surfing spot located in the south of Bali. This beach break offers reasonably easy waves that are perfect for all levels of surfers. The beach itself is stunning, with towering coconut trees that line the shore. Visit the nearby Keramas Surf Resort, which offers world-class amenities and service. Enjoy a delicious meal at their restaurant, which serves delicious seafood and local specialties. Afterward, lie back on the beach and watch the sunset, which is truly unforgettable.
  7. Thomas Beach Thomas Beach is a secluded cove located in the south of Bali, close to the village of Labuhan Badung. This picturesque beach is a favorite among locals, who come here to enjoy the peaceful atmosphere and crystal-clear waters. Swim in the shallow waters, snorkel in the reef pass, or simply relax on the sand. The nearby village offers plenty of amenities, including restaurants, bars, and shops. Visit the nearby Turtle Conservation Center, which raises sea turtles to help preserve their threatened populations.
  8. Kuta Beach Kuta Beach is one of Bali's most popular beaches, located in the south of the island. This bustling tourist spot offers everything you need for a fun day out, from sun loungers and beach bars to restaurants and shopping centers. Watch the sunrise from the beach, which is truly breathtaking. Enjoy a leisurely stroll along the shore, shopping for souvenirs and local delicacies. Take a surf lesson, or simply lie back on the sand and soak up the sun.
  9. Tibumana Waterfall Beach Located in the Gianyar regency, Tibumana Waterfall Beach is a stunning natural wonder that offers breathtaking scenery and plenty of activities. The beach is located just a short walk from the stunning Tibumana Waterfall, which cascades down a lush hillside into a crystal-clear pool. Visit the nearby Bona Village, which is famous for its traditional Balinese architecture and stunning scenery. For dinner, try some of the local specialties at a local restaurant - their Babara Pica dishes are truly unforgettable!
  10. Tegal Wangi Beach Tegal Wangi Beach is a secluded cove located in the south of Bali, close to the village of Nusa Dua. This picturesque beach is surrounded by towering limestone cliffs and crystal clear waters, offering breathtaking scenery and a relaxed atmosphere. Snorkel in the clear waters, or simply lie back on the sand and enjoy the peaceful atmosphere. Visit the nearby Tegalwangi Resort, which offers world-class amenities and service. Enjoy a delicious meal at their restaurant, which serves delicious seafood and local specialties. Being a local in Bali, I have had the pleasure of exploring every one of these incredible beaches. From the secluded coves of the eastern coast to the bustling tourist hubs of the south, each beach has its own unique charm and character that will leave you spellbound. Whether you are looking for excitement and adventure, relaxation and tranquility, or simply a romantic getaway with your loved ones, Bali has the perfect beach for you.
