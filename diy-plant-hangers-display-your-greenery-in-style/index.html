DIY Plant Hangers: Display Your Greenery in Style


If you’re looking for a creative way to showcase your indoor plants, DIY plant hangers are the perfect solution. Not only do they add a touch of greenery to any space, but they also provide a unique and stylish way to display your favorite plants. In this blog post, we’ll explore different types of plant hangers, materials you can use, and step-by-step instructions to create your own. Let’s get started!

Types of Plant Hangers

There are various types of plant hangers you can choose from, depending on your personal style and the type of plants you want to display. Here are a few popular options:

  1. Macrame Plant Hangers: Macrame plant hangers are made using a knotting technique that creates intricate patterns. They give a boho-chic vibe to any room and work well with trailing plants like pothos or spider plants.

  2. Wooden Plant Hangers: Wooden plant hangers provide a rustic and natural look. You can create simple wooden dowel hangers or experiment with different shapes and designs. These hangers are ideal for showcasing succulents or small potted plants.

  3. Beaded Plant Hangers: Beaded plant hangers add a touch of elegance to your plant display. You can use colorful beads or opt for a monochromatic look. These hangers look great with flowering plants or plants with vibrant foliage.

Materials Needed

Before diving into the step-by-step instructions, gather the following materials:

  • Rope or twine (for macrame hangers)
  • Wooden dowels or rods (for wooden hangers)
  • Beads and string (for beaded hangers)
  • Scissors
  • Measuring tape
  • Potted plants

DIY Plant Hanger Instructions

Macrame Plant Hanger

  1. Cut four pieces of rope or twine, each measuring around 4 feet long.
  2. Fold the ropes in half and tie a knot at the folded end, creating a loop.
  3. Divide the ropes into pairs and tie knots approximately 6 inches from the top loop.
  4. Take one rope from each pair and tie a knot with the rope from the adjacent pair, approximately 4 inches below the previous knots.
  5. Repeat step 4 until you reach the desired length for your plant hanger.
  6. Gather all the ropes and tie a large knot at the bottom to secure the hanger.
  7. Insert a potted plant into the hanger and adjust the knots to ensure it hangs evenly.

Wooden Plant Hanger

  1. Cut a wooden dowel or rod to the desired length for your hanger.
  2. Drill two small holes at each end of the dowel, ensuring they are aligned.
  3. Cut four pieces of rope or twine, each measuring twice the desired length of your hanger.
  4. Thread each rope through the holes in the dowel and tie a knot at each end to secure it.
  5. Gather all the ropes and tie a large knot at the top to create a loop for hanging.
  6. Insert a potted plant onto the dowel and adjust the ropes to ensure it hangs evenly.

Beaded Plant Hanger

  1. Cut a piece of string to the desired length for your hanger.
  2. Thread beads onto the string in your preferred pattern or design.
  3. Tie a knot at each end of the string to secure the beads.
  4. Cut four additional pieces of string, each measuring twice the desired length of your hanger.
  5. Attach the beaded string to the middle of the four longer strings.
  6. Gather all the strings and tie a knot at the top to create a loop for hanging.
  7. Insert a potted plant onto the beaded string and adjust the lengths to ensure it hangs evenly.

Conclusion

DIY plant hangers are a fantastic way to add a personal touch to your indoor gardening. By creating your own plant hangers, you can display your greenery in style while also showcasing your creativity. Whether you prefer the boho-chic look of macrame, the natural charm of wood, or the elegance of beads, there’s a plant hanger design to suit your taste. So grab your materials, let your imagination run wild, and start creating stunning plant hangers that will transform your space into a green oasis. Happy crafting!
