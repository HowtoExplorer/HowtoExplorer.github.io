5 Common Credit Myths Debunked


If you’ve ever applied for a credit card or a loan, you’ve probably heard a fair share of credit myths. These myths can often lead to confusion and misinformation, making it difficult to navigate the world of credit. In this blog post, we will debunk five common credit myths to help you make informed financial decisions. So, let’s dive right in!

Myth #1: Checking Your Credit Score Will Lower It

One of the most prevalent credit myths is that checking your credit score will negatively impact it. This is simply not true. When you check your own credit score, it’s considered a “soft inquiry,” which has no effect on your credit score. In fact, regularly monitoring your credit score can help you stay informed about your financial health and detect any potential errors or fraudulent activity.

Myth #2: Closing Credit Cards Will Improve Your Credit Score

Another common misconception is that closing credit cards will boost your credit score. In reality, closing credit cards can actually have a negative impact on your credit score. When you close a credit card, you reduce your overall available credit, which can increase your credit utilization ratio. This ratio is an important factor in determining your credit score. So, it’s generally advisable to keep your credit cards open, especially if they have a long credit history.

Myth #3: Carrying a Balance on Your Credit Card Helps Your Credit Score

Contrary to popular belief, carrying a balance on your credit card does not improve your credit score. In fact, it can potentially harm your credit score. Credit scoring models assess your credit utilization ratio, which is the amount of credit you’re using compared to your total available credit. Paying off your credit card balance in full each month shows responsible credit management and keeps your credit utilization ratio low, positively impacting your credit score.

Myth #4: Closing Inactive Credit Cards Will Boost Your Credit Score

Some people believe that closing inactive credit cards will help improve their credit score. However, closing inactive credit cards can actually have a negative impact. Credit history length is an essential factor in credit scoring models, and closing an inactive credit card can shorten the average age of your credit history. It’s generally beneficial to keep old credit cards open, as they contribute to a longer credit history, which can positively impact your credit score.

Myth #5: Paying Off a Collection Account Will Automatically Remove It from Your Credit Report

If you have a collection account on your credit report, you might think that paying it off will automatically remove it. Unfortunately, this is not the case. Paying off a collection account will update the status to “paid,” but it will remain on your credit report for a certain period of time. The impact of a collection account on your credit score will lessen over time, but it’s important to address and resolve any outstanding debts to improve your overall credit health.

Now that we’ve debunked these common credit myths, you can approach credit management with a clearer understanding. Remember to regularly check your credit score, keep your credit cards open, pay off your balances in full, maintain inactive credit cards, and address any collection accounts. By dispelling these myths, you can make better financial decisions and pave the way for a healthier credit future.
