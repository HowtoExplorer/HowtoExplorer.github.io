Handling Illegal Interview Questions: What You Should Know


As a job seeker, you may find yourself in a position where you are asked challenging or even illegal questions during a job interview. It’s crucial to know your rights and how to handle such situations professionally and effectively. In this blog post, we will discuss what constitutes an illegal interview question, your rights as a candidate, and strategies for navigating these sensitive situations.

What Are Illegal Interview Questions?

Illegal interview questions are those that touch on protected characteristics such as age, race, religion, gender, sexual orientation, marital status, disability, and pregnancy. These questions are prohibited under various anti-discrimination laws, including the Civil Rights Act of 1964, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), and the Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA).

Examples of illegal interview questions include inquiries about your age, citizenship status, marital status, plans for having children, religious beliefs, and disabilities. These questions are not relevant to your ability to perform the job and can potentially lead to discrimination in the hiring process.

Your Rights as a Candidate

It’s essential to understand that you have the right to refuse to answer illegal interview questions. You can politely decline to respond or redirect the conversation to focus on your qualifications, skills, and experiences relevant to the position. Additionally, you have the right to file a complaint with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) if you believe you have been subjected to unlawful hiring practices.

Strategies for Handling Illegal Interview Questions

  1. Stay Informed: Familiarize yourself with the laws and regulations that protect job seekers from discrimination. Knowing your rights will empower you to respond confidently if you encounter illegal interview questions.

  2. Redirect the Conversation: When faced with an illegal question, tactfully steer the conversation back to your qualifications and the value you can bring to the role. For example, if asked about your plans for having children, you can emphasize your dedication to your career and your ability to balance work and personal responsibilities effectively.

  3. Seek Clarification: Sometimes, interviewers may inadvertently ask a question that borders on illegality without intending to discriminate. In such cases, you can politely seek clarification about the relevance of the question to the job requirements.

  4. Document the Incident: If you feel that you have been subjected to illegal interview questions, it’s advisable to document the details of the interaction, including the date, time, interviewer’s name, and the specific questions asked. This information may be valuable if you decide to pursue a complaint.

Conclusion

Handling illegal interview questions requires poise, knowledge, and a clear understanding of your rights as a candidate. By staying informed, confidently asserting your rights, and redirecting the conversation to your qualifications, you can navigate these challenging situations with professionalism. Remember that the interview process is a two-way street, and both parties should engage in a respectful and lawful manner. If you encounter illegal interview questions, don’t hesitate to advocate for yourself and seek support from relevant authorities. Your skills and experience should be the focus of the interview, and you deserve to be evaluated based on your merit and suitability for the position.

Thank you for reading. Good luck in your job search journey!
