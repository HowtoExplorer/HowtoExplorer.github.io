Super Scraps: Easy Recipes for Turning Food Waste Into Irresistible Treasures


In today’s fast-paced world, we often find ourselves cramming meals in between appointments or work schedules. This rush can lead to excess food waste, which not only hurts our wallets but also harms the environment. However, there’s a difference between throwing away food scraps and creatively repurposing them to make irresistible, delicious dishes. Here are some easy and practical recipes for turning those food scraps into tasty culinary treasures.

  1. Fermented Vegetables

During meal prep or while cooking, most of us generate organic scraps like carrot peels, cucumber rinds, and onion ends. Instead of discarding them, try fermenting them into delicious and healthy pickled vegetables. Equipment needed includes mason jars, a weight to keep the vegetables submerged, and breathable jar lids that allow air exchange. Using clean ingredients, layer the scraps in the jar without overcrowding, and pour over a mixture of salt and water brine (ensure the vegetables stay submerged throughout the process).

“Leave the jar at room temperature for a week or two (until tangy), then refrigerate for storage,” says Drew Ramsey MD, author of Eat to Save Your Life. Be prepared for fantastic flavors and health benefits when you have your freshly fermented vegetables.

  1. Vegetable Broth

Not many people realize that all those vegetable scraps that would typically end up in our compost bin can be used to create a flavorful and nutrient-packed vegetable broth. Collect your scraps like celery stalks, ends of carrots and onions, and herbs. In a large pot, combine your scraps with a few whole garlic cloves, bay leaves, and any leftover chicken or vegetable stock you have stored in the fridge. Fill it up with water, bring it to a boil, and then let it simmer for at least an hour.

After straining the broth, let it cool before storing in the fridge or freezer. An easy way to make this process happen daily is by keeping a jar in the fridge and adding to it as you prep meals throughout the day. Your kitchen will smell amazing, you’ll save money on store-bought broth, and you’ll reduce food waste, too.

  1. Veggie Pancakes

Did you know that you can turn leftover, wilted, or slightly spoiled vegetables into tasty veggie pancakes? Simply puree the vegetables in a food processor or chop them finely, and mix the puree with one egg and some flour, breadcrumbs, onion, garlic, and herbs. Fry spoonfuls of the mixture in a frying pan with oil until golden brown, and voilà – you have delicious Veggie Pancakes to serve for breakfast, lunch, or even dinner. Plus, they’re a great way to use up those veggies that would otherwise end up in the trash.

  1. Creative Smoothies

Smoothies are a fantastic way to use up overripe fruits or even fruit scraps. Bananas with blemishes, bruised berries, and old apples all make delicious additions to smoothies. You can also freeze these scraps to use in a future smoothie. Pair them with greens like kale or spinach, yogurt or milk, and your favorite sweetener for a tasty, nutrient-packed beverage. The possibilities are endless, and you’ll be surprised at just how delicious overripe fruit can be when blended with other ingredients.

Conclusion

The act of reducing food waste not only benefits the environment but also your budget and potentially your health. These Super Scraps recipes fulfill this need by educating you in converting your “rejected” kitchen food waste into unique culinary delights. So, be resourceful, and create delicious dishes from the quality ingredients that are sometimes forgotten, ultimately making awesome dishes to treasure and enjoy.
