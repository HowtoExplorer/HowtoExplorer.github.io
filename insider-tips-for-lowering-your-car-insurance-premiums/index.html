Insider Tips for Lowering Your Car Insurance Premiums


As a driver, you know that car insurance is a necessary expense, but that doesn't mean you have to break the bank. With a few insider tips and tricks, you can lower your car insurance premiums and save money in the long run. Know Your Company If you're already a loyal customer of a particular car insurance company, you may be eligible for discounts. Many companies offer loyalty discounts to customers who have been with them for a certain number of years. For example, State Farm offers a 5% discount to customers who have been with them for five years or more. Shop Around Shopping around for car insurance may seem like a daunting task, but it's worth it in the long run. Different companies offer different rates for the same coverage, so it's essential to compare prices and policies before making a decision. A survey conducted by Consumer Reports found that drivers who shopped around saved an average of $200 per year on their car insurance premiums. Increase Your Deductible Raising your deductible can lower your car insurance premiums, but be careful not to raise it too high. If you can't afford to pay your deductible, you may end up paying more out of pocket than you would have with a lower deductible. A good rule of thumb is to raise your deductible by $100-$200 at a time until you reach a comfortable level. Bundle Your Policies Many car insurance companies offer discounts to customers who bundle their policies. For example, if you have a home insurance policy with the same company as your car insurance policy, you may be eligible for a discount on both policies. Bundling your policies can save you up to 20% on your car insurance premiums. Consider a Usage-Based Insurance Policy Usage-based insurance policies, also known as pay-as-you-drive policies, may be a good option for drivers who don't drive much. These policies charge customers based on their actual driving habits, rather than a fixed rate per month. For example, if you drive an average of 20 miles per day, you may pay less for your car insurance than someone who drives an average of 50 miles per day. Take Advantage of Driver Discounts Many car insurance companies offer discounts to certain types of drivers, such as students, seniors, or military personnel. For example, Geico offers a 12% discount to customers who are members of the United States military. Make sure to ask about any driver discounts you may be eligible for when shopping around for car insurance. Cancel Unnecessary Coverages When shopping around for car insurance, it's essential to cancel any unnecessary coverages on your current policy. For example, if you own an older car, you may not need comprehensive coverage, which covers damage to your vehicle that is not related to a collision. Canceling unnecessary coverages can save you up to 20% on your car insurance premiums. Use a Car Insurance Broker Using a car insurance broker can help you find the best car insurance policy for your needs. Brokers have access to a wide range of insurance companies and can compare prices and policies on your behalf. Using a broker can save you up to 10% on your car insurance premiums. Review Your Policy Annually Reviewing your car insurance policy annually can help you stay on top of your coverage and save money. For example, if you've recently moved, you may need to update your policy to reflect your new address. Reviewing your policy annually can help you avoid being over-insured and save you up to 10% on your car insurance premiums. Use Electronic Payments Using electronic payments can help you save money on your car insurance premiums. Many car insurance companies offer discounts to customers who pay their premiums electronically. For example, State Farm offers a 3% discount to customers who pay their premiums electronically. Drive Safely Driving safely is one of the best ways to lower your car insurance premiums. A good driving record can save you up to 20% on your car insurance premiums. For example, if you've never been in an accident, you may be eligible for a safe driver discount. Consider a Low-Mileage Discount If you drive less than a certain number of miles per year, you may be eligible for a low-mileage discount. For example, if you drive less than 10,000 miles per year, you may be eligible for a low-mileage discount with GEICO. Use a Usage-Based App Using a usage-based app can help you save money on your car insurance premiums. These apps track your driving habits and can provide you with discounts for safe driving. For example, the Allstate Driver Advisor app rewards safe drivers with discounts and insights into their driving habits. Tip Your Way to Savings Many car insurance companies offer discounts to customers who are members of certain organizations or groups. For example, if you're a member of the AARP, you may be eligible for a discount on your car insurance premiums. Shop Around for Car Insurance Quotes When shopping around for car insurance quotes, make sure to use the same information for each quote. This will ensure that you're comparing apples to apples and not getting misled by different prices for the same coverage. Get Multiple Quotes When shopping around for car insurance quotes, it's essential to get multiple quotes. Different insurance companies may offer different rates for the same coverage, so it's crucial to compare prices and policies before making a decision. Don't Buy the Cheapest Policy While it may seem like a great idea to buy the cheapest policy, it's not always the best option. Cheap policies may not offer the same level of coverage as more expensive policies, which can leave you vulnerable in case of an accident. Make sure to read the fine print and compare prices and policies before making a decision. Be Patient Shopping around for car insurance may take time, but it's worth it in the long run. Be patient and take your time to compare prices and policies before making a decision. Remember, the goal is to save money on your car insurance premiums, not to rush into a decision. Many car insurance companies use credit scores to determine rates, so maintaining a good credit score can help you save money. A good credit score can save you up to 10% on your car insurance premiums. Don't Lie on Your Application When applying for car insurance, it's essential to be honest and accurate. Lying on your application can result in your policy being canceled or increased rates. For example, if you lie about your driving history, you may be eligible for a higher rate or have your policy canceled.
