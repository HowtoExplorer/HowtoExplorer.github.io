DIY Planters: Creative Containers for Your Greenery


Are you a plant lover looking for unique and creative ways to display your greenery? Look no further! In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of DIY planters that will not only enhance the aesthetic appeal of your plants but also add a touch of personality to your space. Get ready to roll up your sleeves and unleash your creativity!

1. Mason Jar Terrarium

Create a miniature garden in a mason jar terrarium. Start by layering small stones at the bottom for drainage, followed by activated charcoal to keep the soil fresh. Add a layer of potting soil and carefully arrange your choice of small plants. Finish off with decorative elements like tiny figurines or colorful pebbles. This charming and low-maintenance planter will surely become a focal point in any room.

2. Upcycled Tea Tin Planters

Give your old tea tins a new lease on life by turning them into planters. Clean the tins thoroughly and make a few drainage holes in the bottom. Fill them with nutrient-rich potting soil and plant your favorite herbs or small flowering plants. These upcycled planters are not only eco-friendly but also add a vintage touch to your indoor or outdoor space.

3. Hanging Gutter Garden

Repurpose old gutters to create a stunning hanging garden. Cut the gutters into desired lengths and drill small holes along the bottom for drainage. Fill them with soil and plant cascading plants like trailing vines or colorful flowers. Hang the gutters horizontally using hooks or chains, and watch as your plants transform your walls into living art.

4. Succulent Log Planter

Bring nature indoors with a unique succulent log planter. Find a hollow log or a thick tree branch and hollow out a section to fit your succulents. Fill the cavity with a well-draining soil mix and carefully plant your selection of succulents. This rustic planter will add a touch of woodland charm to any space, making it a perfect centerpiece or conversation starter.

5. Book Planter

If you have old, unused books lying around, why not turn them into planters? Hollow out the pages of a book, leaving a deep well. Line the well with plastic or a small container to protect the book from moisture. Fill the well with soil and plant small, low-maintenance plants like cacti or succulents. This whimsical planter will not only breathe new life into your books but also add a literary flair to your decor.

6. Hanging Basket Planters

Create a stunning hanging garden by repurposing old baskets. Line the baskets with coco coir or burlap to hold the soil in place. Fill them with a mix of flowering plants, trailing vines, or even edible herbs. Hang the baskets at different heights to add visual interest and create a lush, cascading effect. This DIY project will transform any dull corner into a vibrant oasis.

7. Vintage Tea Cup Planters

Give your plants a touch of elegance with vintage tea cup planters. Visit thrift stores or flea markets to find beautiful and unique tea cups and saucers. Fill them with well-draining soil and plant small, delicate plants like African violets or ferns. These dainty planters will add a touch of whimsy to your windowsill or tabletop.

Remember, the possibilities are endless when it comes to DIY planters. Don’t be afraid to let your imagination run wild and experiment with different materials and ideas. Whether you choose to upcycle old items or repurpose everyday objects, these creative containers will not only showcase your greenery but also reflect your personal style. So, grab your tools, gather your plants, and embark on a journey of DIY planters that will bring life and beauty to your space. Happy planting!
