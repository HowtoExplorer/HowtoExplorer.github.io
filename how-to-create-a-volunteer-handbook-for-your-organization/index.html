How to Create a Volunteer Handbook for Your Organization


As an organization that relies on the commitment and dedication of volunteers, it’s important to have a well-structured volunteer handbook in place. A volunteer handbook serves as a comprehensive guide that outlines the policies, procedures, and expectations for volunteers within your organization. It not only helps create a positive and productive volunteer experience but also ensures consistency and clarity across all aspects of volunteer engagement. In this blog post, we will walk you through the steps to create an effective volunteer handbook for your organization.

Step 1: Define Your Organization’s Values and Mission

Before diving into the specifics of your volunteer handbook, it’s essential to clearly define your organization’s values and mission. This will provide the foundation for all the policies and procedures outlined in the handbook. Take the time to articulate your organization’s core principles and the impact you aim to make in the community. This will help volunteers understand the bigger picture and feel more connected to the cause they are supporting.

Step 2: Identify Volunteer Roles and Responsibilities

The next step is to identify the different volunteer roles and responsibilities within your organization. Determine the specific tasks and duties that volunteers will be expected to perform. This could range from event support, administrative tasks, fundraising activities, or even specialized skills such as graphic design or social media management. Clearly outline the expectations for each role to ensure that volunteers understand what is required of them.

Step 3: Establish Policies and Procedures

Now it’s time to establish the policies and procedures that will govern volunteer engagement. This includes guidelines on attendance, punctuality, dress code, confidentiality, and code of conduct. Make sure these policies are aligned with your organization’s values and mission. It’s also important to outline the process for reporting any incidents or concerns, as well as the consequences for violating the policies.

Step 4: Provide Training and Orientation

To ensure that volunteers are equipped with the necessary knowledge and skills, it’s important to provide training and orientation. Outline the training process and any required certifications or qualifications. This could include in-person training sessions, online modules, or shadowing experienced volunteers. Also, provide information on the organization’s history, programs, and any relevant policies or procedures that volunteers need to be aware of.

Step 5: Communicate Expectations

Clearly communicate your expectations to volunteers in terms of commitment, availability, and communication. Outline the expected time commitment for each role, as well as the preferred method of communication. Provide information on how volunteers can stay updated on upcoming events, changes in schedules, or any other important announcements. This will help volunteers feel more engaged and informed.

Step 6: Recognize and Appreciate Volunteers

Lastly, it’s crucial to recognize and appreciate your volunteers for their time and effort. Include a section in the handbook that outlines your organization’s volunteer recognition program. This could include certificates, awards, appreciation events, or even small tokens of gratitude. Recognizing volunteers not only boosts morale but also encourages them to continue their involvement with your organization.

Conclusion

Creating a volunteer handbook for your organization is a vital step towards ensuring a positive and productive volunteer experience. By clearly outlining your organization’s values, defining roles and responsibilities, establishing policies and procedures, providing training and orientation, communicating expectations, and recognizing volunteers, you can create a strong foundation for volunteer engagement. Remember, a well-structured volunteer handbook not only benefits your volunteers but also contributes to the overall success of your organization’s mission.
