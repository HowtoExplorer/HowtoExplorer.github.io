The Dos and Don'ts of Body Language in an Interview


Congratulations! You’ve landed an interview for a job you really want. Now it’s time to make sure your body language doesn’t let you down. After all, first impressions are crucial, and your body language can speak volumes before you even say a word. In this guide, we’ll explore the dos and don’ts of body language in an interview, helping you present yourself in the best possible light and increase your chances of acing that interview.

The Dos

1. Eye Contact

Maintaining good eye contact shows confidence and interest. It’s a subtle but powerful way to convey your attentiveness and engagement. When you meet your interviewer, make sure to greet them with a warm smile and maintain eye contact throughout the conversation. This shows that you are focused and actively listening.

2. Posture

Your posture says a lot about your confidence and professionalism. Sit up straight, with your shoulders back, to project an air of confidence and competence. Avoid slouching or fidgeting, as these can be interpreted as signs of nervousness or disinterest.

3. Hand Gestures

Using your hands to emphasize key points can make you appear more animated and engaging. However, be mindful of overdoing it. Subtle, natural gestures can help convey your passion and enthusiasm for the role.

4. Mirroring

Subtly mirroring your interviewer’s body language can create a sense of rapport and connection. Pay attention to their body language and, when appropriate, subtly mimic their gestures and posture. This can help create a sense of harmony and understanding between you and the interviewer.

5. Confidence

Confidence is key, and your body language should reflect this. Walk into the interview room with purpose, offer a firm handshake, and exude positivity and self-assuredness in your movements and expressions.

The Don’ts

1. Avoiding Eye Contact

Lack of eye contact can be interpreted as shyness, evasiveness, or lack of interest. Make sure to maintain a comfortable level of eye contact without staring, as this can come across as confrontational.

2. Fidgeting

Fidgeting with your hands, tapping your feet, or playing with objects can convey nervousness or distraction. It’s important to appear calm and collected, so be mindful of any nervous habits and try to keep them in check.

3. Closed Body Language

Crossing your arms or legs can create a barrier between you and the interviewer, signaling defensiveness or reservation. Keep your posture open and welcoming to convey openness and receptiveness to the conversation.

4. Excessive Hand Gestures

While using hand gestures can be effective, excessive or erratic movements can be distracting and convey nervousness. Keep your gestures natural and purposeful to enhance your communication without overwhelming the conversation.

5. Lack of Confidence

Uncertainty and lack of confidence can be conveyed through hesitant movements, weak handshakes, and a lack of assertiveness in your posture. Project confidence in your body language to instill faith in your abilities and suitability for the role.

Mastering the art of body language in an interview takes practice and self-awareness. By paying attention to these dos and don’ts, you can ensure that your body language aligns with your verbal communication, presenting a cohesive and compelling image of yourself. Remember, your body language can be a powerful ally in making a positive impression and securing that dream job.

Good luck!
