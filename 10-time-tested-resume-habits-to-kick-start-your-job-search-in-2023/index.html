10 Time-Tested Resume Habits to Kick Start Your Job Search in 2023


Are you preparing to kick-start your job search in 2023? One of the crucial steps you’ll undertake is updating and optimizing your resume. A well-crafted resume can be the difference between landing your dream job or facing continued unemployment. In light of this, we’ve curated a list of 10 time-tested resume habits to help elevate your job application game in 2023.

1. Focus on Your Professional Summary

Your resume’s first section - the professional summary - is what employers will read first. Use this space to highlight your standout skills, experiences, and achievements. Aim for a compelling narrative that makes your case for the job you desire. Employers want to read a concise yet informative summary, providing an overview of your career aspirations and qualifications.

2. Customize to the Position

Show employers you’re serious about the job by tailoring your resume to the specific position requirements. Cut out irrelevant details and focus on including keywords from the job posting. This increases your chances of passing Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) and catching the attention of the hiring manager who prioritizes candidates who demonstrate a fit for the role.

3. Quantify Your Achievements

Turn potential employers into enthusiasts by including quantifiable achievements. Use numbers, percentages, and other metrics to show concrete evidence of your accomplishments. For instance, instead of stating, “increased sales,” say “increased sales by 40% in six months.”

4. Emphasize Core Competencies

Your core competencies or skills section, ideally placed at the top of your resume, should showcase the skills you possess that align with the job requirements. Organize these skills in the specific order that employers will prioritize. Use a clean, easy-to-read format with bullet points.

5. Outline Your Work Experience

Your work experience section should be organized in reverse chronological order, starting with your most recent job. In each role, describe your responsibilities and achievements using action verbs and succinct bullet points. Don’t neglect to include the company name, location, and employment dates.

6. Include Education Details

A dedicated section for your academic background, including your degrees, certifications, and training programs, should follow directly after your work experience. Consider mentioning relevant coursework or projects that demonstrate your professional preparation.

7. Keep it Clean and Simple

Design matters in a world of information overload. Employers are more inclined to read clean, well-organized resumes. Ensure there is enough white space between sections and use clear headings. Stick to a professional font like Calibri or Arial and optimize for readability.

8. Keep it to Two Pages (at most)

Recruiters typically spend less than 30 seconds on each resume, so keep the length reasonable. Aim for a concise and information-dense resume. If you have an extensive work history, focus on including the most relevant experiences and achievements.

9. Abide by Traditional Resume Writing Conventions

While presenting a unique value proposition, remember to adhere to standard resume writing conventions. This includes:

  • Using traditional formatting
  • Maintaining consistency in fonts and spacing
  • Spell-checking every word
  • Proofreading for grammar and punctuation errors

10. Get a Professional Audit

Consider having a professional resume reviewer look over your document. Friends and family members can provide feedback or consult a professional resume service. Get an unbiased opinion on your resume’s effectiveness and make any necessary improvements.

By following these time-tested resume habits, you’ll be one step closer to landing the job that’s right for you in 2023. Stay diligent, stay persistent, and shine through in your endeavors. Good luck with your job search!
