How to Encourage Your Child to Try New Things


It’s easy to fall into routines—whether as an adult or a child. While routines bring comfort and stability, they can sometimes limit growth and opportunity. Children, in particular, are at an age where exploration and trying new things can shape their development, build confidence, and create lifelong memories. Yet, it’s not uncommon for kids to hesitate or resist when it comes to stepping out of their comfort zone. Encouraging them to embrace new experiences isn’t always simple, but it’s an invaluable gift you can give to your child. In this post, we’ll explore practical strategies to inspire your child to say “yes” to new opportunities while fostering a sense of curiosity and resilience.

Understanding Your Child’s Hesitation

Before diving into tactics, it’s important to understand why children may be reluctant to try new things. Here are some common reasons:

  • Fear of Failure: Many children worry they’ll embarrass themselves or won’t succeed at an unfamiliar activity.
  • Shyness or Social Anxiety: Meeting new people or joining a group can feel intimidating for kids who are naturally introverted.
  • Preference for Familiarity: Some children thrive on consistency and may feel stressed when asked to move outside their regular routines.
  • Bad Past Experiences: A negative memory, such as struggling with an activity before, can discourage them from trying again.

Understanding these underlying factors allows parents to approach the situation with empathy, setting the stage for a more effective approach to encouraging exploration.

Celebrate Curiosity: A Key to Overcoming Reluctance

Encouraging your child to try new things starts with fostering a culture of curiosity within your household. When children grow up in an environment where questions are welcomed, imagination is celebrated, and new ideas are embraced, they’re more likely to adopt an open mindset. Here’s how you can nurture curiosity:

  • Model Curiosity Yourself: Be an example by trying new things, learning new skills, or experimenting with hobbies. When children see a parent enthusiastically exploring the unknown, they view it as a positive experience.
  • Praise Effort Over Outcome: Reinforce the idea that it’s okay not to be perfect. Celebrate your child’s bravery and curiosity, even if they don’t succeed immediately.
  • Normalize Mistakes: Talk openly about times you’ve failed or struggled and what you gained from the experience. This will help remove the stigma associated with failure.

When curiosity leads the way, children are less likely to see new experiences as daunting and more likely to view them as exciting opportunities.

Start Small and Build Gradually

It’s important not to overwhelm your child with massive changes or challenges right away. The journey toward trying new things begins with small, manageable steps. Think of it as building a muscle—start with light lifts before tackling heavier weights. Here are strategies to ease your child into new experiences:

  • Introduce Small Changes in Daily Life: Ask them to try a new food, walk a different route to school, or play with a new toy. These minor changes help normalize novelty.
  • Offer Choices: When introducing something new, present options so your child feels they have some control. For instance, instead of saying, “Let’s join a soccer team,” say, “Would you like to try soccer, karate, or dance?”
  • Explore Together: Try something new as a family, like going to a museum, hiking trail, or restaurant you’ve never visited. Shared experiences can make unfamiliarity feel safer.

Gradual, intentional steps allow your child to build confidence without becoming overwhelmed by drastic changes.

Create a Safe Environment for Experimentation

Children need to feel secure in order to take risks. A nurturing environment that emphasizes support and encouragement is crucial. Here’s how you can create a sense of safety for your child:

  • Avoid Negative Reactions: Criticism or frustration when your child hesitates can create fear or resistance. Instead, remain calm and patient.
  • Celebrate Attempts: Even if the result isn’t what they hoped for, praise their bravery for trying. This reinforces a growth mindset.
  • Keep Expectations Realistic: Set achievable goals, and avoid pressuring your child to succeed immediately. Unrealistic expectations can increase anxiety and reduce their willingness to try.

When children know they’ll be supported no matter the outcome, they’re more likely to take that first step into the unknown.

Frame New Experiences as Adventures

Kids love a good story. Framing new things as exciting adventures can transform nervousness into anticipation. Use vivid language and a positive attitude to build excitement. For example:

  • “Trying sushi for the first time is like going on a trip to Japan right from our table!”
  • “Learning to ride a bike is like becoming your own superhero—you can go anywhere!”

The way you present an opportunity has the power to shift their mindset from fear to enthusiasm.

Leverage Their Passions and Interests

If your child has a particular interest, use it as a gateway to trying new activities. For example:

  • If They Love Dinosaurs: Take them to a new museum exhibit or sign up for a fossil-digging event.
  • If They Enjoy Building: Encourage them to try a robotics or engineering workshop.
  • If They’re Into Art: Enroll them in a new type of class, such as pottery or watercolor painting.

When the new experience aligns with their existing passions, it feels like a natural step rather than an intimidating leap.

Teach Problem-Solving Skills

Facing something unfamiliar often comes with challenges. Teaching your child problem-solving skills will help them navigate new situations with more confidence. Practice these strategies with your child:

  • Break Tasks Into Steps: If an activity feels overwhelming, guide them in breaking it into smaller, manageable pieces.
  • Encourage Questions: Teach your child to ask questions when faced with uncertainty. This reduces confusion and empowers them to seek help.
  • Reflect on Successes: After trying something new, discuss how they overcame challenges. This helps reinforce their problem-solving abilities.

When kids know they can handle obstacles, they’re more willing to face the uncertainties of novel experiences.

Don’t Underestimate the Role of Patience

Encouraging a child to step outside their comfort zone is rarely a one-time event. It requires patience, consistency, and understanding. Children have unique timelines for growth, and it’s important to honor their pace. Keep the following points in mind:

  • Respect Their Limits: If your child is adamantly against trying something, don’t force it. Instead, revisit the idea later, when they might be more open.
  • Celebrate Small Wins: Each tiny step forward is progress. Acknowledge even the smallest victories to build momentum.
  • Keep Encouraging: Avoid giving up if your first few attempts fail—it can take time for children to feel ready.

By showing patience and persistence, you send the message that trying new things isn’t just important—it’s also a continuous process.

Be Mindful of Your Own Reactions

Your child observes and absorbs more than you might realize. Your attitude toward novelty can influence how they react themselves. Here’s how to set a positive example:

  • Reframe Your Own Experience: When you’re nervous about something new, verbalize your feelings and share how you overcame them.
  • Stay Calm if They Resist: If your child hesitates, avoiding frustration or coercion helps maintain an atmosphere of trust.
  • Celebrate as a Team: When you and your child try new things together, it strengthens your bond and allows you to model resilience.

Your behavior sets the stage for how your child interprets new experiences, so showing enthusiasm and support is essential.

The Long-Term Benefits of Trying New Things

Encouraging your child to embrace new opportunities isn’t just about short-term experiences—it’s about instilling skills and values they’ll carry into adulthood. Over time, trying new things helps children:

  • Build Confidence: Successes and failures alike teach them that they’re capable of tackling challenges.
  • Develop Resilience: Each new experience becomes a lesson in adapting and overcoming obstacles.
  • Cultivate Curiosity: A love of learning and exploration leads to a lifetime of growth and discovery.

Ultimately, helping your child step outside their comfort zone equips them with the tools they need to thrive in an unpredictable world.

In conclusion, encouraging your child to try new things isn’t about forcing them to do something they dislike—it’s about guiding them to see the world as full of possibilities. By celebrating curiosity, starting small, creating safe spaces, and demonstrating patience, you can gently encourage your child to embrace new opportunities. As they explore, learn, and grow, you’ll not only watch their confidence blossom but share in the joy of discovery alongside them.
