How to Follow Up After a Networking Event: Best Practices


Networking events are a goldmine for building professional relationships, exchanging ideas, and discovering new opportunities. However, the real magic happens after the event when you follow up with the connections you’ve made. A well-crafted follow-up can solidify your new relationships and open doors to future collaborations. In this guide, we’ll explore the best practices for following up after a networking event, ensuring that your efforts yield fruitful results.

Why Follow-Up Matters

Following up after a networking event is crucial for several reasons:

  1. Reinforces Connections: A follow-up message helps to reinforce the connection you made during the event. It shows that you value the interaction and are interested in maintaining the relationship.

  2. Keeps You Top of Mind: In a world where professionals are constantly meeting new people, a follow-up can keep you at the forefront of someone’s mind. This is especially important if you discussed potential collaborations or opportunities.

  3. Opens Doors for Future Interactions: A thoughtful follow-up can lead to future meetings, collaborations, or referrals. It’s an opportunity to explore how you can help each other professionally.

  4. Demonstrates Professionalism: Following up shows that you are organized and professional. It reflects positively on your personal brand and can enhance your reputation in your industry.

Timing is Everything

The timing of your follow-up is critical. Ideally, you should reach out within 24 to 48 hours after the event. This timeframe ensures that the interaction is still fresh in both your minds. If you wait too long, the connection may fade, and your message might not have the same impact.

Tips for Timing Your Follow-Up

  • Set a Reminder: As soon as you leave the event, set a reminder on your phone or calendar to follow up within the next couple of days.
  • Prioritize Your Connections: If you met several people, prioritize your follow-ups based on the strength of the connection and the potential for collaboration.
  • Consider the Day of the Week: Sending your follow-up on a Tuesday or Wednesday can be more effective than on a Monday or Friday, as people are often busy catching up or winding down.

Crafting the Perfect Follow-Up Message

Your follow-up message should be personalized, concise, and actionable. Here’s how to craft a message that resonates:

1. Personalize Your Message

Start by addressing the person by name and referencing something specific from your conversation. This shows that you were engaged and that you remember the details of your interaction.

Example:
“Hi [Name], it was great meeting you at [Event Name] on [Date]. I really enjoyed our discussion about [specific topic].”

2. Express Gratitude

Thank the person for their time and for the conversation. A little appreciation goes a long way in building rapport.

Example:
“Thank you for sharing your insights on [specific topic]. I found it incredibly valuable.”

3. Provide Value

Offer something of value in your message. This could be an article related to your discussion, a resource, or even an introduction to someone in your network who might benefit from knowing them.

Example:
“I came across this article on [relevant topic] and thought you might find it interesting. I’d love to hear your thoughts on it!”

4. Suggest a Next Step

Encourage further interaction by suggesting a next step. This could be a coffee meeting, a phone call, or an invitation to another event.

Example:
“Would you be open to grabbing coffee next week? I’d love to continue our conversation and explore how we can collaborate.”

5. Keep It Concise

While it’s important to be personable, keep your message concise. Aim for a few short paragraphs that get straight to the point.

Different Follow-Up Methods

There are various methods to follow up after a networking event, and the best choice depends on your relationship with the person and the context of your interaction.

Email

Email is the most common and professional way to follow up. It allows for a more detailed message and can be easily referenced later.

LinkedIn

If you connected on LinkedIn during the event, sending a follow-up message through the platform can be effective. It’s less formal than email and can feel more personal.

Phone Call

For connections that you feel particularly strong about, a phone call can be a great way to follow up. It allows for a more personal touch and can lead to a deeper conversation.

Social Media

If you connected on social media, a direct message can be a casual way to follow up. Just ensure that your message is still professional and relevant.

Following Up with a Group

If you met a group of people, consider sending a follow-up message to the entire group. This can help foster a sense of community and encourage further interactions among all parties.
