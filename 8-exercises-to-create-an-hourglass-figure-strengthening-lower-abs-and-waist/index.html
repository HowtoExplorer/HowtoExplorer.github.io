8 Exercises to Create an Hourglass Figure: Strengthening Lower Abs and Waist


Are you struggling to achieve that envied hourglass figure? Don’t feel discouraged, as achieving a toned lower abs and waist is a common goal for many people. The key is consistency and determination. In this post, we’ve outlined the top eight exercises to help sculpt your body and create that perfect figure.

  1. Russian Twists

This exercise primarily targets your oblique muscles and helps to tonify your lower abs. To perform this exercise, sit on the floor with your knees bent and lean back slightly, keeping your back straight. Hold your hands together, and twist your torso to the left, then to the right, as if you were trying to touch the ground next to your hips. Perform three sets of 10 reps for the best results.

  1. Side Plank Hip Dips

The side plank hip dip exercise tonifies your oblique muscles and hip flexors. To complete the exercise, lie on your side with your legs straight, stacked on top of each other. Raise your hips to form a straight line from your head to your feet, with your core engaged. Slowly lower your hips until they’re just above the ground, then raise them back up. Complete three sets of 10 reps on each side.

  1. Leg Raises

Leg raises are a classic exercise targeting lower abs muscles. To execute this exercise, lie flat on your back with your legs straight and arms by your sides. Slowly lift both legs up towards the ceiling, keeping them as straight and as close to perpendicular to the ground as possible. Lower your legs back down without fully touching the ground, then raise them up again. Do three sets of 10 reps.

  1. Mountain Climbers

Mountain climbers work multiple muscle groups, including lower abs and obliques. Start by getting into a high plank position, with arms straight and hands shoulder-width apart, legs straight as well. Bring your right knee towards your chest, then return it to the starting position. Alternate knees as if you’re running in place, completing three sets of 25 reps each.

  1. Bicycle Crunches

Bicycle crunches are a fantastic exercise that works on both your lower abs and obliques. Lie on your back with your hands behind your head and your elbows pointing outwards. Slowly bring your right knee in towards your chest while lifting your upper body and twisting to the left, then switch sides. Perform three sets of 20 reps.

  1. Standing Side Kicks

Standing side kicks target your inner thighs and lower abs. Stand tall with your hands on your hips, and kick your right leg out to the side, then back down to the starting position. Repeat with the left leg, performing three sets of 20 reps.

  1. The Dead Bug

The dead bug is a challenging exercise that targets your entire midsection. Begin by lying on your back with your hands above your head, legs straight up, and knees bent at 90 degrees. Slowly lower your right arm and left leg, pointing them towards each other but not letting them touch. Then, switch sides. Perform three sets of 10 reps each.

  1. Plank Hip Dips

The plank hip dip exercise targets both your obliques and your lower abs. Start in a forearm plank position with your elbows directly under your shoulders and your body forming a straight line from head to feet. Slowly lower your hips to the right, then return to the center, and finally dip to the left. Perform three sets of 10 reps for the best results.

Conclusion

Creating an hourglass figure with a strong waist and lower abs may seem like an impossible task, but with persistence and a combination of these exercises, you will undoubtedly see positive results. Make sure to maintain a balanced diet while incorporating these exercises into your workout routine for the best results. Good luck on your fitness journey, and happy exercising!
