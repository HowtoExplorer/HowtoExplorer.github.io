Preparing for Phone and Video Interviews: Best Practices


In today’s digital age, phone and video interviews have become increasingly common in the job application process. These types of interviews offer convenience and flexibility, allowing employers to connect with candidates regardless of their geographical location. However, it’s important to remember that phone and video interviews require just as much preparation as in-person interviews. To help you ace your next phone or video interview, we’ve compiled a list of best practices to follow:

1. Test your technology and internet connection

Before the interview, make sure you have a reliable internet connection and that your technology is functioning properly. Test your microphone, camera, and any software or platforms that will be used for the interview. This will help you avoid any technical glitches that could disrupt the flow of the interview.

2. Choose a quiet and well-lit location

Find a quiet and well-lit location for your interview. Ideally, this should be a distraction-free space where you can focus and speak clearly. Make sure the background is clean and professional, as it will be visible to the interviewer. Avoid any potential interruptions by informing your household members or colleagues about the interview and asking them to minimize noise during that time.

3. Dress professionally

Even though you’ll be participating in the interview from the comfort of your own home, it’s essential to dress professionally. This will not only create a positive impression but also help you get into the right mindset for the interview. Dress as you would for an in-person interview, paying attention to your attire, grooming, and overall appearance.

4. Prepare your environment

Create a conducive environment that will allow you to focus on the interview. Have a copy of your resume, the job description, and any notes or questions you want to reference during the interview. Keep a pen and paper nearby to jot down important points or any follow-up questions you may have. Having these materials at hand will help you stay organized and demonstrate your preparedness.

5. Practice your body language and communication skills

Non-verbal communication plays a significant role in phone and video interviews. Practice maintaining good posture, making eye contact with the camera, and using hand gestures appropriately. Pay attention to your tone of voice, speaking clearly and confidently. Remember to listen actively to the interviewer and pause before responding to give yourself time to formulate thoughtful answers.

6. Research the company and role

Just like in any other interview, it’s crucial to research the company and the role you’re applying for. Familiarize yourself with the company’s mission, values, recent news, and any relevant projects. Understand the requirements of the position and think about how your skills and experiences align with them. This knowledge will enable you to answer questions effectively and demonstrate your interest in the company.

7. Prepare for common interview questions

While you can never predict every question that will be asked during an interview, it’s useful to prepare for commonly asked questions. Practice answering questions about your strengths, weaknesses, previous experiences, and why you’re interested in the role. Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to structure your responses and provide concrete examples that showcase your skills and achievements.

8. Be mindful of your online presence

Before the interview, review your online presence, including your social media profiles and any public information about you. Ensure that your online persona aligns with the professional image you want to portray. Remember that employers often search for candidates online, so it’s essential to curate a positive and professional digital footprint.

9. Follow-up after the interview

After the interview, send a thank-you email to the interviewer to express your appreciation for their time and reiterate your interest in the position. Use this opportunity to highlight any additional qualifications or thoughts that may not have been covered during the interview. This follow-up gesture demonstrates professionalism and can leave a lasting impression on the interviewer.

By following these best practices, you’ll be well-prepared and confident for your phone or video interview. Remember to treat these interviews with the same level of importance as in-person interviews, as they are a crucial step in the hiring process. Good luck!
