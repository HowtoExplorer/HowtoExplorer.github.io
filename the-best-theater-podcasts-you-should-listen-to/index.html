The Best Theater Podcasts You Should Listen To


As a theater enthusiast, you're probably always on the lookout for new and exciting ways to stay connected to the world of performing arts. From Broadway shows to regional theater, podcasts are an excellent way to experience the magic of theater whenever and wherever you want. In this post, we'll delve into the best theater podcasts that are sure to become your new favorite companions. **1. The Theater Podcast by Laura Fuller and Lindy Nicolangelo Starting off our list is a podcast that's entirely dedicated to theater. The Theater Podcast is a treasure trove of interviews with some of the most talented actors, directors, and playwrights in the industry. Hosts Laura Fuller and Lindy Nicolangelo have a knack for asking thoughtful questions, and their conversations are both informative and entertaining. You'll find yourself learning about the behind-the-scenes stories of your favorite Broadway shows and getting a glimpse into the creative process of the people who bring them to life. **2. The Broadway Podcast Network's Shows within a Show The Broadway Podcast Network's "Shows Within a Show" is another standout podcast that's sure to captivate theater fans. Host Rachel Rodensky takes you on a journey through some of the most iconic Broadway shows, exploring the music, the drama, and the people behind the curtain. From the Swinging Sixties of Hair to the dazzling spectacle of Wicked, this podcast is a love letter to the world of Broadway. **3. Under the Couch with Dan Kaufman Under the Couch is a podcast that's all about exploring the weird and wonderful world of off-Broadway theater. Host Dan Kaufman takes you on a journey through the lesser-known corners of the theater world, highlighting innovative productions and spotlighting the artists who create them. You'll discover hidden gems and get a glimpse into the creative process of some of the most innovative theater-makers working today. **4. The Drama Department with Sally Ann Triplett The Drama Department is a theater podcast that focuses on in-depth interviews with industry professionals. Host Sally Ann Triplett has an impressive list of interviews with the likes of Marc Blitzstein, Andrew Lloyd Webber, and Arthur Miller. Her conversations are insightful and engaging, and she always brings a sense of curiosity and enthusiasm to the discussion. **5. How We Roll with Ciaron Davies How We Roll is another off-Broadway podcast that's focused on exploring the weird and wonderful world of live theater. Hosts Ciaron Davies and John Carinky take a deep dive into the world of experimental theater, highlighting some of the most innovative productions in the city. Their podcast is a must-listen for anyone who loves the innovative and the unexpected. Why You Should Listen to These Podcasts Listening to theater podcasts is an incredible way to stay connected to the world of performing arts. Whether you're a theater enthusiast or just looking to expand your cultural horizons, these podcasts will provide you with a wealth of information and insights into the creative process of the artists who bring theater to life. Here are just a few reasons why you should start listening to these theater podcasts:

Beachside companionship: Listen to your favorite theater podcasts on your daily commute or while on vacation. Behind-the-scenes stories: Gain an insider's perspective on the creative process of your favorite shows. Creative inspiration: Meet the talented artists who bring theater to life and learn from their experiences. Discover new shows: Stay up-to-date with the latest productions, reviews, and recommendations. Broadway nostalgia: Revisit old favorites or relive your first encounter with a classic Broadway show.

Whether you're a seasoned theater enthusiast or just looking for something new to listen to, these podcasts are sure to captivate and inspire. So, hit play, grab your headphones, and let the magic of theater flow through you. How to Stay Connected to the Theater Community In addition to these top-notch podcasts, here are a few ways to stay connected to the theater community:

Theater blogs: Follow blogs like Playbill, The New York Times' Theater Section, and TheaterMania for the latest news, reviews, and interviews. Arts journalism: Read classic theater criticism online or in magazines like The Guardian and The American Theatre. Theater events and workshops: Attend theater festivals, workshops, and masterclasses to learn more about the craft of theater-making. Theater playwriting contests: Enter contests like the Eugene O'Neill Playwriting Contest or the National Playwrights Conference to share your work with the theater community.

In conclusion, exploring the world of theater podcasts can be an incredibly enriching experience, offering insight into the creative process, and providing valuable insights into the world of live performance. With a rich history of storytelling, both on and off stage, these podcasts celebrate the evolution of theater, from classic performances to innovative new works. Thank you for taking the time to explore the magic of theater with me.
