The Health Benefits of Fermented Foods: Probiotics and Gut Health


As the human body continues to unravel itself in terms of understanding and research, so do the benefits of old and traditional activities. One that stands firm through time banners the health benefits of fermented foods. Foraged by numerous cultures across our beautiful globe, fermentation works as a sort that quite literally adds new life to our ordinary eats.

Let’s dive into some more specific details as to what exactly we’re discussing here. Fermentation is simply the process of “an anaerobic process in which microorganisms…cause foodstuff to impart alcoholic or acidic qualities” [1]. Pretty basic, right? The process uses natural cultures of bacteria and yeasts acting on organic substances. Pretty incredible is more like it.

Looking at the mechanics of this strategy to improve our meals, it shows its might benefit in portions. Firstly and most straightforwardly, there is this growing knowledge about probiotics. Cells in our body, each higher organisms in our own personal microcosm, ensure the wellbeing and general health of us. Sadly, due to the prevalence of processed and antiseptic-residing foods, traditional gut homeostasis is being disrupted. What can we do? Cue probiotics from fermented food–die-hard survivors of our shipwrecked stomachs, they maintain health!

Thus, happy as they are about the taxonomic work done on them, scientists are content to examine their function just as much. The health effects of these males-of-the-science world certainly aren’t negligible. Probiotics improve gut health [2], modulate rates of immunity, such as inflammatory activity, cholesterol and even function of abdominal obesity [3]. Unraveling each thread, I could go on and on; the cloak of benefits they offer are endless.

Fermented foods own another kingdom of benefits–prebiotics! Allow me to let the cat out of the bag: Prebiotics, they’re complicated carbohydrates that humans can’t digest. Why mention them? They navigate the seawaters of our gut untouched until the microscopic good guys barging into the gonfalon and feast luxuriously on it creating yet another world of good health [4].

Let’s talk more of what range of foods we should be consuming. Traditional items from domains such as yogurt, miso, and sauerkraut dominate the high road of food-probiotics. But not stopping there, kimchi, tempeh, and even pickles share the front line [5]. You’ll find more in local cuisine; every culture’s got its vermicelli!

However, there’s a catch - one size doesn’t fit all - that’s right, the oligogenic ingredient of diet is present. The health effects differ according to each person, according to Vrieze, A., et al. [6]. But that shouldn’t be a nail in the coffin. Resounding the importance again, they add to the spirit of heterogeneity. I mean who’d want a monotonous health regime?

The importance of a correctly sited probiotic cannot be overstated either. A positive note to end on, a recent opening by our scientific SIGNORs [7] reports about specific probiotic strains, Lactobacillus reuteri and Bifidobacterium brevis, improving our best friend’s health. Yes, you guessed it right. Pets! Our four-furyed friends benefit from gut health too!

As such, the future of probiotics looks very different. Understandably, we’re not going to stop at fermented foods or the human gut. They’re on a spacecraft taking off toward a new cosmos of health. With studies revolving around its environmental benefits, pharmaceutical-health linkages, and even the very boundaries of space research, I layer with an optimistic outlook.

Rejoice in good health and appeal to the diverse nature of this wonderful world of fermented foods, probiotics, and gut health! If Socrates was around today to embrace his own classics best friend, perhaps, he’d have it with kimchi on the side.
