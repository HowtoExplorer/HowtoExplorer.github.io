ChooseClean: A Guide to Health-Friendly, Non-Toxic Cleaning Practices for Fitness Enthusiasts


Living a clean and healthy lifestyle is important for everyone, but for fitness enthusiasts, it’s especially vital to maintain an environment that supports wellbeing. Regularly indulging in fitness activities can increase your body’s natural detoxification process, making it more susceptible to the effects of toxic cleaning products. In this guide, we’ll explore health-friendly, non-toxic cleaning practices that will help you maintain a fit and clean living space.

safer cleaning alternatives

Traditional household cleaners are usually filled with harsh chemicals that can irritate our respiratory system and contribute to poor indoor air quality. Instead, opt for safer alternatives that are gentle on your body and lungs.

  1. Natural cleaning agents: Baking soda, vinegar, and lemons can replace many harsh chemicals you’d find in commercial cleaning products. Baking soda helps neutralize odors and effectively cleans bathroom and kitchen surfaces. Vinegar cuts through grease, eliminating grime in the kitchen and bathroom without using harsh chemicals. Lemons make a great disinfectant and can be used to combat stubborn odors.

  2. Essential oils: Infuse your cleaning routine with essential oils to not only make your home smell fresh but also pack a punch when it comes to effectiveness. Tea tree oil is a powerful antimicrobial agent that can be used to disinfect surfaces, while lavender and peppermint oils can help deodorize and relax.

  3. Plant-based cleaners: Opting for cleaners based on natural substances like soapnut or coconut can help reduce exposure to chemicals and support a cleaner Earth as well. Essentially, these eco-friendly cleaning agents effectively lift away dirt and leave a pleasant aroma.

  4. Microfiber cloths and mops: While not technically cleaning agents, using microfiber materials is an eco-friendly and efficient way to clean. Microfiber effectively traps dirt and bacteria, reducing the need for harsher chemicals.

everyday cleaning routine

Establishing a regular cleaning routine that focuses on preventing messes is key to a healthier living environment. Here’s a simple approach to cleaning more sustainably:

  1. Dedicate specific cleaning times: Aim to spend 15-20 minutes per day tidying up different areas of your home. This prevents bigger messes that may require more strenuous deep cleaning.

  2. Tackle spills and messes immediately: Act quickly to address any messes that occur during workouts or while preparing meals. Water spots and stains caused by wet surfaces, equipment, and clothing can become arduous to remove if left unattended.

  3. Regularly sanitize high-touch surfaces: High-touch surfaces, such as doorknobs, light switches, televisions, and sinks, can accumulate dirt and bacteria. Sanitizing these surfaces regularly, especially during the winter months when people spend more time indoors, can help prevent illness.

  4. Aerate your living space: Opening windows and utilizing air purifiers can help improve indoor air quality. A well-aerated living space helps reduce the need for toxic air fresheners.

investing in eco-friendly cleaning tools

A healthier cleaning practice not only involves choosing the right cleaning products but also utilizing eco-friendly tools. Here are a few essentials to consider:

  1. Reusable cleaning wipes: Reduce waste while efficiently cleaning by using homemade cleaning wipes made from old t-shirts or towels, infused with natural cleaning agents.

  2. Steam cleaners: Save money and the environment by adjusting the temperature and pressure to tackle tough jobs around the house, including removing pet dander and allergens.

  3. Washing machines with a sanitize cycle: These machines offer an eco-friendly way of cleaning fitness garments and towels, ensuring fresher and healthier workout gear for each session.

choosing clean: making a difference

Making the commitment to a health-friendly, non-toxic cleaning practice may seem like a small step, but it plays a significant role in improving your overall health and well-being. By choosing cleaner alternatives, establishing a dedicated routine, and investing in eco-friendly tools, fitness enthusiasts can contribute to a healthier home environment and community. Remember, you are what you breathe, so make every breath count.
