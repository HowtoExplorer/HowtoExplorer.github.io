Not Just the 'Why Should We Hire You?' Answer: Exploring Diverse Responses


As anyone who’s been through an interview knows, the familiar question, “Why should we hire you?” can be a challenging one. To help job seekers stand out and give a more powerful answer, here, we dive into unique perspectives of some highly successful professionals who have navigated this significant hurdle.

  1. Storytelling: Use Your Experience and Passion

Dan, a marketing expert, recounts his journey from his first entry-level job to where he is now. He shares his passion for the industry, noting that he has grown along with the evolving landscape. Emphasizing personal achievements that align with the company’s goals, such as leading successful marketing campaigns, displays the GAH (gets stuff done) attitude desired in a new hire.

  1. Focused Approach: Industry Insight

Jose, a software engineer, demonstrates his keen awareness of the company’s priorities and projects. By discussing how his skills and experience could contribute to these projects, he aligns his response with the company’s objectives, thus presenting a strong case for why they should choose him.

  1. Powerful Partnership: Highlighting Compatibility

Linda, a potential team member in a consulting company, puts emphasis on the compatibility of her working style with that of the interviewer and the team. Acknowledging her interviewer’s past successes and emphasizing how their combined expertise would lead to future triumphs displays her collaborative spirit and willingness to contribute to the firm’s continued success.

  1. Giving Back: Social Value

Alex, a potential candidate in the nonprofit sector, discusses the positive impact he has made on his previous nonprofits and how the organization he’s interviewing with could benefit from his experience. He builds upon this by emphasizing the growth he has gained from such roles and the value he believes he could bring to the organization.

  1. Show, Don’t Tell: Proof of Performance

Adam, a sales specialist, uses metrics and anecdotes to demonstrate his success: “Last year, I closed $5 million in deals for my previous company. I consistently hit my targets, and you can see from these graphs my incredible track record.” An impressive performance history served as a compelling direct interaction with the question, proving that he’s a secure bet for future achievements.

One of the critical factors these examples have in common is their focus on the impact they would have on the company’s success. Sure, the individuals present their experiences and how they’ll contribute to the company’s growth, but they also explore the realm of possibility alongside the interviewer. In doing so, they create an engaging dialogue that alleviates pressure and paves the way to a professional and satisfying relationship.

The “Why should we hire you?” question creates an opportunity for job seekers to show a deeper understanding of the company’s ethos, culture, and projects. It’s essential to go beyond just listing qualifications. It’s effective to address this question from an original and engaging perspective, allowing interviewers to not only gain an understanding of an individual’s skills but of their potential value to the organization. Remember, an impactful answer sets the stage for a successful hiring experience.
