Chemical Thermodynamics: Understanding Energy Changes


Chemical thermodynamics is the foundation of understanding energy changes in chemical reactions. Knowing the principles and concepts of chemical thermodynamics is crucial for designing and predicting chemical reactions. Below, we will delve into the basic concepts and rules of chemical thermodynamics.

Definition and Types of Energy

In chemical thermodynamics, we discuss two types of energy: internal energy and external energy. Internal energy refers to the energy within a substance, such as the energy of chemical bonds, molecular structure, and stability. External energy refers to the energy exchanged between a substance and its environment, such as thermal energy, light energy, and mechanical energy.

Internal Energy

Internal energy is the energy within a substance, and it is a fundamental concept in chemical thermodynamics. Internal energy can be divided into two parts: chemical internal energy and mechanical internal energy. Chemical internal energy refers to the energy of chemical bonds, which drives chemical reactions. Mechanical internal energy refers to the mechanical energy within a substance, such as the stability of molecular structure.

External Energy

External energy refers to the energy exchanged between a substance and its environment, and it is another fundamental concept in chemical thermodynamics. External energy can be divided into two parts: thermal external energy and mechanical external energy. Thermal external energy refers to the energy exchanged between a substance and its environment through heat transfer, such as heat conduction and radiation. Mechanical external energy refers to the energy exchanged between a substance and its environment through mechanical means, such as pressure and tension.

Law of Energy Conservation

In chemical thermodynamics, the law of energy conservation is the most basic rule. The law of energy conservation states that the total energy remains constant during a chemical reaction, but the energy form may change. This rule can be represented by the following formula:

ΔE = q - w

where ΔE is the change in total energy, q is the external thermal energy, and w is the external mechanical energy.

In this post, we have introduced the basic concepts and rules of chemical thermodynamics, including the definition and types of energy, internal energy, external energy, and the law of energy conservation. Understanding these concepts is crucial for designing and predicting chemical reactions, and it is essential for the development of various fields, such as chemistry, physics, and engineering.
