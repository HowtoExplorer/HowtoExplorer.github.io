From Audience to Dancer: How to Pick Up Your Favorite Dance and Performing Arts


Do you find yourself dancing along to your favorite tunes in front of the mirror? Are you captivated by the grace and artistry of the ballet or the lively energy of breakdance? If you have ever fantasized about getting up on stage and performing your own dance, fear not! It is possible to go from being an audience member to a dancer. This blog post will guide you through the journey of picking up your favorite dance and performing arts, offering tips and advice along the way.

Know Your Dance Style

First and foremost, spend some time to know the various dance styles out there. Exploring different dance forms will help you understand what really resonates with you. Some of the popular styles are:

  1. Ballet: Known for its elegance and technique, ballet is rooted in classical European training.
  2. Jazz: Jazz dance is often dynamic and spirited, incorporating elements of storytelling, improvisation, and personal style.
  3. Hip-hop: Defined by its music and urban style, hip-hop encompasses breakdancing, locking, and popping.
  4. Contemporary: Blending elements of ballet, modern, and jazz, contemporary dance emphasizes the expression of emotions and unrestricted movement.
  5. Tap: Originating in the African American community, tap features rhythmic, staccato-like sounds created by dancers tapping their metal-attached shoes on various surfaces.

After getting familiar with different dance forms, pick your favorite style and consider taking classes to hone your skills. Many local studios and community centers offer classes in various dance styles. Alternatively, you can watch dance tutorials on YouTube or find dance-specific how-to books on Amazon.

Find a Mentor or Instructor

A knowledgeable mentor or instructor can help lay a strong foundation in your dance journey. The importance of a great dance teacher cannot be overstated, as they guide you through proper alignment, technique, and discipline. When selecting a dance instructor, it is essential to choose someone who is experienced, patient, and knowledgeable about the style you wish to learn.

Set Clear Goals

Setting goals is crucial when embarking on a new journey. What do you want to achieve as a dancer? Is it learning new dance moves, performing on stage, or even teaching classes? Having a clear goal will help you stay motivated and focused towards building a dance practice routine that aligns with your objectives.

Practice Makes Perfect

Consistent practice is what sets a dancer apart from an audience member. Commit to regular practice sessions, whether it’s a couple of times a week or every day. Listen to music, study your favorite dancers, and keep refining your skills. Be patient with yourself and remember that everyone starts somewhere.

Create Your Personal Dance Space

Having your personal dance corner or space at home can provide you with a dedicated area for practice and reflection. It could be a tiny corner in your room or even your entire living room. Ensure that your dance space is lively, colorful, and inspiring, with dance-related posters, music, and props if you have them.

Network and Attend Dance Events

Attending dance events and workshops can help enrich your dance experience and expose you to a rich dance culture. Building a network of fellow dancers and attending events can also widen your dance horizon and provide you with opportunities to perform or collaborate.

Build Your Confidence, Build Your Stage Presence

As you start incorporating dance into your daily life, remember that confidence is key. Any stage performance begins with confidence. Fake it till you make it - stand tall, make eye contact, and project your voice. Confidence, in time, will become second nature to you.

Enjoy the Process

Lastly, but most importantly, enjoy your journey from audience to dancer. It is a creative, awe-inspiring, and exciting journey that could lead to some of your life’s most memorable moments. Let your passion drive you and enjoy every step, literally.

Embarking on the journey from audience member to dancer can be an exhilarating experience. It takes time, practice, and dedication, but with a solid plan and persistence, it is definitely possible. So, what are you waiting for? Start dancing, and let your passion be your guiding light!
