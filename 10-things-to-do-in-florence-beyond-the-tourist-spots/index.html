10 Things to Do in Florence Beyond the Tourist Spots


Florence, the birthplace of the Renaissance, is a city that seamlessly blends art, architecture, and history. While most tourists flock to the iconic Duomo, Uffizi Gallery, and Ponte Vecchio, there's a wealth of hidden treasures waiting to be discovered. Venture beyond the crowds and experience the authentic Florence that only a few travelers get to enjoy.

  1. Visit the Museo Galileo Located near the Arno River, the Museo Galileo is a museum dedicated to the life and work of Galileo Galilei. This fascinating museum showcases the scientist's experiments, instruments, and theories that revolutionized the way we understand the world. The museum is set in a 16th-century fortress and features exhibits on astronomy, mathematics, and the scientific method.
  2. Discover the Oltrarno Neighborhood Cross the Ponte alla Carraia and enter the lesser-known Oltrarno neighborhood, where you'll find winding streets, charming piazzas, and a more laid-back atmosphere. Visit the Piazza di Santo Spirito, a tranquil square surrounded by charming cafes, restaurants, and bakeries. Admire the Piazzale degli Uffanzi, a tranquil spot that offers breathtaking views of the Arno River.
  3. Explore the Church of San Marco Ditch the crowds at the Uffizi Gallery and visit the Church of San Marco, which houses a stunning collection of Fra Angelico's frescoes. These beautiful works of art are a testament to the artist's ability to create heavenly beauty in the form of faith and spirituality. Be sure to explore the tranquil monasteries and cloisters surrounding the church.
  4. Visit the Mercato Centrale Get a taste of local life at the Mercato Centrale, a vibrant marketplace filled with fresh produce, meats, and cheeses. Take a peek into the antique stalls selling vintage and handmade goods, or try some traditional Tuscan cuisine at the on-site restaurants. Be sure to browse the stunning hall's frescoes and ornate design.
  5. Walk Along the Passeggiata Lungarno Take a leisurely stroll along the Passeggiata Lungarno, a waterfront promenade that follows the Arno River. Watch the sunset and admire the grand views of the city's rooftops, or snap a selfie with the picturesque riverside accommodations. Stop by the Giardino delle Rose, a tranquil oasis offering respite from the bustling streets.
  6. Dine at Trattoria la Carra Experience authentic Tuscan cuisine at Trattoria la Carra, a cozy restaurant tucked away in the Oltrarno neighborhood. Let the chef guide you through their seasonal offerings, be it a homemade pappardelle or a roasted porchetta. Pair your meal with a glass of Chianti Classico and be immersed in the local culture.
  7. Climb the Torre da Canoncia Ready for a panoramic view of the city? Climb the Torre da Canoncia, a lesser-known tower located in the Santa Croce neighborhood. Admire the stunning views of the terracotta rooftops and majestic Duomo, or observe the sun rising over the rolling hills.
  8. Visit the Giardino Torrigiani Escape the bustling streets and immerse yourself in nature at the Giardino Torrigiani, a serene oasis nestled in the heart of the city. Explore the lush trees, soundproof botanical gardens, and bubbling fountains that make this spot a tranquil retreat from the city chaos.
  9. Browse the Antique Markets Indulge in the peak of antique hunting at Florence's impressive weekend markets. Held every second and fourth Saturday of each month, these markets bring together vendors selling antique goods, from antique furniture to collectible vases and tapestries. Stand out items include restored woodcarvings, rare books, and Venetian glass.
