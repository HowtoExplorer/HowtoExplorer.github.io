The Power of pH Balance: How to Evaluate Your Skincare Routine


pH stands for “Potential Hydrogen” and refers to the measure of acidity or alkalinity of a solution. In the context of skincare, pH level plays a pivotal role on the health and appearance of the skin, often referred to as the skin’s “equilibrium” or “acid mantle.” Consequently, maintaining the optimal pH balance within our skincare routine is crucial to ensuring the absorption of products, prevention of signs of aging, and overall skin health.

A pH value ranges from 0 to 14, with 7 being neutral. The ideal pH balance for healthy skin falls within the range of 4.7 to 5.75, overlapping the slightly acidic boundary – slightly more acidic than water which has a pH of 7. For comparison, the pH of lemon juice is 2.5 while the pH of human tears ranges between 7.5 and 8.

That being said, what happens if our skin pH becomes too high or too low, throwing off this balance? When skin pH is altered, the skin’s protective barrier can weaken, leading to over-exfoliation, inflammation, acne, as well as skin sensitivity and irritation. Thus, understanding and regulating the pH of your skincare routine will contribute to a healthy, glowing complexion.

How to Evaluate Your Skincare Routine: The pH Scale

To evaluate your current skincare routine, it is essential to understand the pH levels of the products you use. Yet, not all products display their pH level on the label for a multitude of reasons, including the lack of regulation on product labeling.

Apart from testing at a pharmacist or skincare specialist, you may do the pH test at home. Using a simple pH strip, you can measure the levels of water and your skincare products. Supermarket pH strips or more precise digital pH meters are available for purchase.

Once you’ve obtained your products’ pH values, consider consulting the scale below for the ideal range:

  • Cleansers: 5.5-6.0
  • Toner/Acidic Serums: 3.5-4.0
  • Moisturizers and Sunscreen: 4.5-5.0

When your cleansing product’s pH value is within the optimal range, it removes excess oils, dirt, and makeup without disrupting your skin’s natural pH balances.

Acidic serums and toners (like those with AHA/BHA or vitamin C) should be pH ranges 3.5-4.0 to be effective. This ensures maximum penetration and efficacy in improving skin texture, stimulating collagen, and brightening.

Lastly, moisturizers and sunscreen should maintain the skin’s natural barrier, keeping the pH levels at around 4.5 to 5.0.

If the products you use fall out of this recommended range, consider searching for alternatives with appropriate pH levels.

Tips to Maintain a Balanced pH in Your Skincare Routine

  1. P::Hydrate: Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily to maintain hydration levels, maintaining skin pH balance. Hydrated skin is less prone to irritation and redness.

  2. Limit Washing: Overwashing your face may disturb the skin’s natural oil balance, causing its pH to drop. Opt for cleansing once or twice per day, not more.

  3. Protect Your Skin Barrier: Sun exposure, harsh weather, chemicals, and loud noises contribute to compromised skin barrier. Keep skin well-moisturized with sun protection in mind.

  4. Patch Test: When trying new skincare products, a patch test can prevent potential dermatitis from products that disrupt your skin’s pH. Apply a small amount of the product onto your forearm, wait 24 to 48 hours to check for possible irritation or negative reaction.

  5. Skip Double Cleansing: Double cleansing might be great to carry off that makeup, but it can be too much for your skin. Consider doing a gentler first cleanse and reserve the deep cleanse for makeup days.

In conclusion, the balance of pH in your skincare routine plays a significant role in maintaining healthy skin that glows. By understanding and adjusting your skincare routine according to your skin’s needs, you can enjoy a well-nourished, radiant skin while preventing potential harm.
