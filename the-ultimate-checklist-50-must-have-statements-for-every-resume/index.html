The Ultimate Checklist: 50 Must-Have Statements for Every Resume


A resume is not just a summary of your professional history; it is a carefully crafted piece of work designed to persuade potential employers that you are the best fit for their organization. With numerous resumes being submitted for every job opening, it’s essential to stand out from the crowd. That’s where the ultimate checklist of must-have statements for every resume comes in. In this blog post, we present 50 of the best and highly effective phrases that will make your resume shine.

1. Action Verbs and Skill-Based Descriptors

Start your resume with dynamic action verbs such as “experienced,” “led,” or “optimized,” followed by specific skills that showcase your qualifications.

2. Customized Contact Information

Ensure that your contact information is current and tailored to the job posting. Include your full name, phone number, email address, and LinkedIn profile link.

3. Strong Keywords

Integrate relevant keywords from the job description throughout your resume to demonstrate your suitability for the role.

4. Clear and Concise Summary

A brief summary statement highlights your professional strengths and the value you bring to an organization. Make sure it is results-oriented.

5. Chronological Work Experience

Organize your work experience in reverse-chronological order, emphasizing major accomplishments and responsibilities.

6. Bulleted Lists

Use bullet points to highlight quantifiable achievements, making them easy to read and digest.

7. Detailed Job Descriptions

When describing your work experience, delve into the tasks you performed, the tools you used, and the challenges you faced.

8. Education in Reverse-Chronological Order

Include your academic background, beginning with the most recent institution attended.

9. Honors, Awards, and Certifications

List relevant accolades, whether academic or professional, to demonstrate your merit and commitment to excellence.

10. Prominent Soft Skills

Identify and emphasize crucial soft skills, such as communication, teamwork, and problem-solving, which are often unquantifiable but equally important in evaluating candidates.

11. Measures of Success

Quantify your accomplishments by providing specific numbers, percentages, and statistics to demonstrate your tangible impact on past employers.

12. Areas of Expertise

Highlight your areas of expertise and how they contribute to the desired role.

13. Professional Memberships

List relevant professional organizations you are a part of.

14. Volunteer Experience

Demonstrate your commitment to the community by including your volunteer work, showing that you take initiative in helping others.

15. Projects and Case Studies

If you have recently completed specific projects, describe the scope and your contributions in detail.

16. Industry Jargon

Integrate industry-specific terms and acronyms to show that you understand the unique landscape of your professional sector.

17. Develop Your Signature Style

Unite all statements with a consistent and concise format that exemplifies your professionalism.

18. Tailor the Language

Despite having a global perspective, your vocabulary must resonate with your target audience, making the connection between your skills and the company’s needs more meaningful.

19. Networking Opportunities

Don’t be afraid to include referrals or relationships that can positively impact your application.

20. Dos and Don’ts

21. Highlight Strong Leadership Skills

Showcase your ability to guide teams through challenges, setting you apart from less experienced candidates.

22. Mention Mentorship Experience

If you have mentored or coached colleagues, employers will appreciate your willingness to share knowledge and help others succeed.

23. Relevant Achievements Beyond Professional Life

Whether it’s winning a sports competition or operating a successful personal project, including relevant achievements can provide context and depth to your professional profile.

24. Strengthen Problem-Solving Skills

Provide examples of situations where you thrived in the face of adversity or complex challenges to demonstrate your resilience.

25. Set Clear Career Goals

Convey your ambition and desire to grow within the organization by stating your future goals and aspirations.

26. Communication Flexibility

Show that you can adeptly communicate with various stakeholders, whether in writing, public speaking, or team interaction, by highlighting your diverse communication skills.

27. Provide Testimonials

Include quotes from colleagues, managers, or clients to reinforce the value you bring to your field.

28. Continuous Learning and Professional Development

Detail your participation in workshops, online courses, or reading materials that enhance your skillset.

29. Keep It Visually Appealing

Use consistent fonts, bullet points, and white space to ensure your resume is easy to scan and visually appealing.

30. Proofread and Edit Thoroughly

Avoid common mistakes such as typos, grammatical errors, or inconsistencies in formatting.

31. Demonstrate Decision-Making Ability

Share instances where you made difficult choices and explain how these decisions positively impacted the organization.

32. Highlight Digital Competence

Mention proficiency in relevant software, tools, or languages that demonstrate your ability to work in a digital environment.

33. Show Adaptability

Emphasize your flexibility and adaptability by providing examples of how you adjusted to rapid change or pivoted amid adversity.

34. Include Volunteering and Non-Professional Achievements

Show diversity by highlighting relevant achievements outside your professional life for a well-rounded image of yourself.

35. Optimize for ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)

Formatting your resume appropriately ensures it passes through Applicant Tracking Systems, which screen resumes based on keywords and programming algorithms.

36. Showcase Cost-Saving Initiatives

If you have saved your company resources or time, it highlights your ability to contribute to the bottom line.

37. Team Collaboration

38. Seek Guidance from a Professional

Consider working with a resume coach or professional who can offer insights into personal strengths and highlight them in your resume.

39. Use Job Descriptions as Guides

Extract keywords from job postings to tailor your resume to the specific role you’re applying for.

40. Reference and Follow-Up

Don’t forget to provide references and follow up on your applications to demonstrate your continued interest in the roles you’re seeking.

41. Emphasize Creativity and Innovation

Provide examples of where you’ve added value through creative problem-solving and innovative ideas.

42. Detail Your Contribution to an Organization’s Culture

Show that you are a good fit for the company by explaining how your work aligns with the organization’s mission and values.

43. Portray Conflict Resolution

When discussing past experiences, describe how you effectively resolved conflicts and maintained team morale.

44. Specify Technological Proficiencies

45. Language Skills

If you’re bilingual or multilingual, include your language skills, considered valuable in today’s global workforce.

46. Commitment to Lifelong Learning

Express your passion for learning by mentioning the courses, certifications, or conferences you’re involved in.

47. Propose Future Professional Growth

Mention how you can grow within the organization and potential areas for future professional development.

48. Use the Present Tense Wisely

When discussing current roles, use the present tense to convey an up-to-date and proactive perspective.

49. Address Big Professional Challenges

Include examples of how you tackled large-scale industry challenges, showcasing leadership and strategic thinking skills.

50. Climate and Sustainability Initiatives

If relevant, highlight any contributions to green initiatives or projects that demonstrate your commitment to environmental sustainability.

In conclusion, a well-crafted resume can indeed open doors to career advancement. Use this ultimate checklist to incorporate must-have statements that showcase your unique value proposition. This comprehensive resume guide has provided you with the tools to stand out from the competition and is a powerful step towards achieving your professional goals.
