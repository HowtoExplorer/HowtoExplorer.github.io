The Art of Building Rapport with Interviewers


When it comes to job interviews, building rapport with the interviewer can make a significant difference in your chances of landing the position. Rapport, defined as a harmonious relationship based on mutual understanding and trust, plays a crucial role in creating a positive impression and showcasing your qualifications effectively. In this blog post, we will explore the art of building rapport with interviewers and provide you with valuable tips to ace your next interview.

1. Prepare and Research

Before stepping into the interview room, take the time to research the company and the interviewer. Familiarize yourself with the organization’s values, mission, and recent achievements. Additionally, try to gather information about the interviewer’s background and professional interests. This preparation will not only demonstrate your enthusiasm but also provide you with valuable conversation starters to establish a connection.

2. Be Authentic and Show Genuine Interest

Authenticity is key when building rapport. During the interview, be yourself and let your true personality shine through. Avoid putting on a facade or pretending to be someone you’re not. Instead, focus on expressing your genuine interest in the position and the company. Actively listen to the interviewer’s questions and respond thoughtfully, demonstrating your enthusiasm and passion for the role.

3. Mirror and Match

One effective technique to build rapport is mirroring and matching the interviewer’s nonverbal cues. Pay attention to their body language, tone of voice, and pace of speech. Subtly adopt similar gestures, posture, and speech patterns. This mirroring technique can create a sense of familiarity and establish rapport on a subconscious level.

4. Use Active Listening

Active listening is a valuable skill when it comes to building rapport. Show genuine interest in what the interviewer is saying by maintaining eye contact, nodding, and providing verbal cues such as “I see,” or “That’s interesting.” Paraphrase and summarize their statements to show that you understand and are engaged in the conversation. By actively listening, you signal that you value their perspective and are invested in the interview process.

5. Find Common Ground

Finding common ground can help break the ice and establish rapport. Look for shared interests, experiences, or hobbies that you can discuss briefly during the interview. This could be anything from a mutual passion for a particular sports team to a shared love for a hobby. However, be mindful not to force connections or overshare personal information. Keep the conversation professional and focused on commonalities that are relevant to the job and the company culture.

6. Ask Thoughtful Questions

Asking thoughtful questions not only demonstrates your interest but also allows you to engage in a meaningful conversation with the interviewer. Prepare a list of insightful questions about the company, the team, or the role itself. This shows that you have done your research and are genuinely interested in the opportunity. Additionally, asking questions gives the interviewer a chance to share their perspective and insights, further strengthening the rapport between you.

7. Follow up with a Thank-You Note

After the interview, send a personalized thank-you note to express your gratitude for the opportunity. This small gesture demonstrates your professionalism and leaves a positive impression. In the note, mention specific points from the interview that resonated with you and reiterate your interest in the position. This follow-up not only reinforces the rapport you built but also keeps you top of mind for the interviewer.

Building rapport with interviewers is an essential skill that can set you apart from other candidates during the job search process. By preparing, being authentic, using active listening, finding common ground, asking thoughtful questions, and following up, you can establish a strong connection with interviewers and increase your chances of success. Remember, building rapport is not about manipulation but about creating a genuine connection based on mutual respect and understanding. So, go ahead and master the art of building rapport to excel in your next interview!
