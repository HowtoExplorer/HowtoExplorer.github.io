The Dos and Don'ts of Running Contests on Social Media


Contests and giveaways have become an integral part of social media marketing, allowing businesses to engage with their audience, increase brand awareness, and drive website traffic. However, running a successful contest requires a thoughtful approach, as it can quickly go wrong if not executed properly. In this post, we will outline the dos and don’ts of running contests on social media, helping you to maximize your chances of success and avoid common pitfalls.

Do: Clearly Define the Contest Rules and Guidelines

Before launching your contest, make sure to clearly define the rules and guidelines. This includes specifying the eligibility criteria, contest duration, and prize details. It is essential to be transparent about the contest rules to avoid confusion and disputes. You can include the contest rules in a separate post or document, and make sure to link to it from your contest announcement.

Do: Make the Contest Relevant to Your Audience

A successful contest is one that resonates with your target audience. Make sure to tailor your contest to your audience’s interests and preferences. This can include asking questions related to your products or services, or hosting a contest that is relevant to a current trend or holiday. By doing so, you can increase engagement and attract new followers.

Do: Utilize Multiple Social Media Platforms

To maximize your reach and engagement, consider running your contest across multiple social media platforms. This can include Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and LinkedIn. By doing so, you can tap into different audiences and increase your chances of success.

Do: Encourage User-Generated Content

User-generated content (UGC) is an excellent way to encourage engagement and increase brand awareness. Consider hosting a contest that encourages followers to share their own content, such as photos or videos, using a specific hashtag. This can help to create a sense of community and increase user engagement.

Do: Monitor and Respond to Contest Entrants

To ensure a successful contest, it is essential to monitor and respond to contest entrants. Respond to comments and messages in a timely and professional manner, and make sure to address any questions or concerns. By doing so, you can build trust and increase engagement.

Don’t: Overcomplicate the Contest Rules

Keep your contest rules simple and easy to understand. Avoid using complex language or jargon, and make sure to clearly outline the eligibility criteria and contest duration. By doing so, you can avoid confusion and disputes.

Don’t: Ignore Contest Entrants

Ignoring contest entrants can lead to a negative experience and damage your brand reputation. Make sure to respond to comments and messages in a timely and professional manner, and address any questions or concerns.

Don’t: Fail to Promote the Contest

To increase your chances of success, it is essential to promote your contest across multiple channels. Consider sharing contest updates and reminders on your social media platforms, and make sure to include a link to the contest rules and guidelines. By doing so, you can increase engagement and attract new followers.

Don’t: Dismiss Contest Entrants

Dismissing contest entrants can lead to a negative experience and damage your brand reputation. Make sure to treat contest entrants with respect and professionalism, and avoid making them feel like they are not important.

Conclusion

Running a successful contest on social media requires a thoughtful approach, as it can quickly go wrong if not executed properly. By following the dos and don’ts outlined in this post, you can increase your chances of success and avoid common pitfalls. Remember to clearly define the contest rules and guidelines, make the contest relevant to your audience, and encourage user-generated content. By doing so, you can create a positive experience for your followers and increase brand awareness.
