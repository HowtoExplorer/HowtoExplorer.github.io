Preparing for Video Interviews: Tips for Looking and Sounding Professional


With the advancement of technology, video interviews have become a popular method for employers to screen candidates before inviting them for in-person interviews. While video interviews offer convenience, they also come with their own set of challenges. In order to make a good impression and increase your chances of success, it’s crucial to prepare yourself both in terms of appearance and communication. In this blog post, we will share valuable tips to help you look and sound professional during video interviews.

1. Dress Professionally

Just because you’re not physically present in an office doesn’t mean you should disregard professional attire. Dressing appropriately shows your respect for the interview process and the company you’re applying to. Opt for attire that aligns with the company’s dress code. If in doubt, it’s better to be slightly overdressed than underdressed. Avoid bright colors or busy patterns as they can be distracting on camera. Stick to neutral tones that won’t clash with your background.

2. Choose a Suitable Background

Ensure that your background is clean, clutter-free, and professional. A messy or distracting background can divert the attention of the interviewer. Ideally, choose a plain wall as your background or set up a simple backdrop. Make sure the lighting is good, and avoid sitting in front of a window as it can create a silhouette effect. Test your setup before the interview to ensure everything looks and functions correctly.

3. Pay Attention to Lighting

Good lighting is crucial for video interviews. Natural light is the best option, so try to position yourself facing a window. If that’s not possible, use a combination of overhead and desk lamps to create a well-lit environment. Avoid harsh or direct lighting that creates unflattering shadows. Test the lighting before the interview to ensure you look clear and professional on camera.

4. Maintain Eye Contact

Maintaining eye contact is important in any interview, including video interviews. Look directly into the camera instead of at your own image on the screen. This will create the impression that you’re making eye contact with the interviewer. Remember to smile and nod occasionally to convey your engagement and interest in the conversation.

5. Check Your Internet Connection

A stable internet connection is crucial for a successful video interview. Test your internet speed and make sure it’s sufficient for a smooth video call. Consider using an ethernet cable to ensure a more reliable connection. If you’re using Wi-Fi, be mindful of potential disruptions and ensure you’re in a location with a strong signal.

6. Practice Active Listening

Active listening is a vital skill during any interview. Pay close attention to the interviewer’s questions and respond thoughtfully. Avoid interrupting and wait for the interviewer to finish speaking before responding. Take notes if necessary, but don’t let them distract you from the conversation. Show genuine interest in what the interviewer has to say and ask relevant questions to demonstrate your engagement.

7. Test Your Equipment

Before the interview, test all your equipment to avoid any last-minute technical difficulties. Check that your microphone and camera are working properly. Ensure that your video conferencing software is up to date and familiarize yourself with its features. It’s also a good idea to have a backup device available, such as a smartphone or tablet, in case of any unexpected issues.

8. Minimize Distractions

During a video interview, it’s important to minimize distractions. Choose a quiet location where you won’t be interrupted. Inform anyone you live with about the interview and ask them to respect your privacy. Close any unnecessary applications or browser tabs on your computer to avoid distractions. Silence your phone and any other devices that may cause disruptions.

9. Practice Makes Perfect

Just like any other interview, practicing beforehand can significantly improve your performance. Conduct mock interviews with a friend or family member to get comfortable with the video interview format. Pay attention to your body language, tone of voice, and overall presentation. This will help you identify areas for improvement and boost your confidence for the actual interview.

10. Follow-Up with a Thank You Note

After the video interview, it’s essential to send a thank you note to express your appreciation for the opportunity. Use this opportunity to reiterate your interest in the position and highlight any relevant points that were discussed during the interview. Keep the note concise, professional, and personalized to leave a lasting positive impression.

Remember, video interviews are becoming increasingly common, so it’s essential to be well-prepared. By following these tips, you can ensure that you look and sound professional, leaving a lasting impression on potential employers. Good luck with your video interviews!
