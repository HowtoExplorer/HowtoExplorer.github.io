Mastering the Art of Answering Competency Interview Questions


Competency-based interviews have become increasingly popular in the hiring process, as they offer employers a more accurate assessment of a candidate’s skills and abilities. These interviews focus on specific competencies or behaviors that are essential for success in a particular role. While they may seem daunting at first, with the right preparation and mindset, you can master the art of answering competency interview questions. In this blog post, we will explore the key strategies and techniques to excel in these interviews.

Understanding Competency Interviews

Before diving into the strategies, it’s important to understand what competency interviews entail. Competency-based questions are designed to assess how you have demonstrated specific skills and behaviors in past experiences. The interviewer wants to understand how you handle real-life situations and whether you possess the necessary competencies for the role.

Research and Preparation

The first step in mastering competency interviews is thorough research and preparation. Start by carefully reading the job description and identify the key competencies required for the role. Take note of any specific skills or experiences mentioned. Then, brainstorm examples from your past experiences that demonstrate your proficiency in these areas.

Next, familiarize yourself with common competency-based questions. These questions often begin with phrases such as “Tell me about a time when…” or “Give me an example of…” By anticipating these questions, you can prepare relevant anecdotes that showcase your skills and accomplishments.

STAR Method

One effective technique for answering competency-based questions is the STAR method:

  • Situation: Start by providing a brief overview of the situation or context in which the competency was demonstrated.
  • Task: Describe the specific task or challenge you were faced with.
  • Action: Explain the actions you took to address the situation. Focus on your individual contribution and highlight any key skills or behaviors.
  • Result: Share the outcome of your actions and emphasize the positive impact you had on the situation. Whenever possible, quantify the results with specific metrics or achievements.

Using the STAR method ensures that your answers are structured, concise, and provide a clear understanding of your abilities.

Tailoring Your Answers

While it’s important to have prepared examples, it’s equally crucial to tailor your answers to the specific question asked. Listen carefully to the question and identify the underlying competency being assessed. Then, select the most relevant example from your repertoire and adapt it to suit the specific question.

Confidence and Authenticity

In competency interviews, it’s essential to showcase your skills and experiences with confidence and authenticity. Be concise, articulate, and maintain a positive attitude throughout the interview. Remember, the interviewer wants to get to know the real you and assess your suitability for the role. Be yourself, and let your true abilities shine through.

Practice, Practice, Practice

As with any skill, practice is key to mastering competency interviews. Conduct mock interviews with friends or family members and ask for feedback. Focus on refining your storytelling abilities, ensuring your examples are concise and compelling.

Additionally, consider recording yourself during practice sessions. This will allow you to analyze your body language, tone of voice, and overall presentation. By identifying areas for improvement, you can enhance your performance in the actual interview.

Final Thoughts

Competency interviews can be intimidating, but with the right approach, they can also be an excellent opportunity to showcase your skills and experiences. By thoroughly researching, preparing, and practicing, you can confidently navigate these interviews and impress potential employers. Remember, mastering the art of answering competency interview questions is a skill that can set you apart in your job search. Good luck!
