Classic Italian Dishes: Recipes to Transport You to Italy


Everyone loves the divine taste of Italian cuisine. The rich and aromatic flavors have long been adored by food connoisseurs worldwide, and the culinary landscape of Italy boasts an incredible array of hearty and flavorful dishes.

In this blog post, we delve into the heart of Italy’s culinary culture, revealing some of the most scrumptious and timeless Italian recipes. Prepared with carefully chosen ingredients and time-honored techniques, these recipes will transport you straight to the sun-drenched hills of Tuscany and the bustling streets of Rome.

Let’s begin our Italian culinary journey by exploring a few of Italy’s cherished and delectable classic dishes!

Spaghetti alla Carbonara

When it comes to Italian dishes, few are as iconic as spaghetti alla carbonara. This classic Italian pasta dish is a savory delight that combines bacon, eggs, and Parmesan cheese to create a pleasurable explosion of flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound spaghetti
  • 6 ounces of pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 cup grated pecorino or Parmesan cheese
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Extra-virgin olive oil

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions.
  2. In a large pan, cook the pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until crispy.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks and grated cheese together.
  5. Drain the spaghetti, reserving 1/2 cup of cooking water.
  6. Add the cooked spaghetti to the pan, and toss with the pancetta and garlic.
  7. Immediately remove the pan from heat and toss the spaghetti with the egg yolk mixture, adding a little reserved cooking water to create a creamy sauce.
  8. Serve with extra cheese and chopped parsley, and enrich your meal with a beautiful glass of Chianti!

Osso Buco alla Milanese

Osso Buco alla Milanese, a staple in Milanese cuisine, is the epitome of rich and soul-satisfying comfort food. This delicious dish consists of slow-cooked veal shanks, braised with tomatoes, aromatic vegetables, and a touch of butter.

Ingredients for Osso Buco:

  • 4-6 veal shanks
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 4-6 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 4-6 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2-3 large onions, chopped
  • 2 cups canned tomatoes
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 200g butter
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Parmesan cheese, grated

Instructions:

  1. Season the veal shanks generously with salt and black pepper.
  2. Dredge the shanks in flour.
  3. Heat the olive oil in a large casserole, and sear the veal shanks on all sides.
  4. In the same pot, sauté the carrot, celery, and onion until soft and fragrant.
  5. Add the canned tomatoes and beef broth, bringing everything to a simmer.
  6. Slowly cook the veal shanks for at least 2 hours, stirring occasionally, until the meat is succulent and tender.
  7. Finish the dish by adding a knob of butter and a pinch of thyme.
  8. Serve the osso buco with a drizzle of the savory sauce, creamy polenta, and a generous helping of grated Parmesan cheese.

Tiramisu

Tiramisu - the heavenly Italian dessert that lifts your spirits and warms your heart. This classic Italian concoction combines the richness of coffee, the velvety smoothness of mascarpone cheese, and the softness of savoiardi ladyfingers to create a delightful symphony of flavors.

Ingredients for Tiramisu:

  • 10-12 ladyfingers
  • 2/3 cup strong coffee (espresso orAmericano), at room temperature
  • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
  • 2/3 cup powdered sugar
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dark cocoa powder
  • Fresh raspberries

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix the mascarpone cheese, powdered sugar, and egg yolks together.
  2. Fold in the vanilla extract.
  3. Brew a pot of strong coffee and allow it to cool to room temperature.
  4. In a shallow dish, evenly coat each ladyfinger in the coffee mixture.
  5. Line the bottom of a serving dish with half of the soaked ladyfingers.
  6. Spread half the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
  7. Repeat the layers with the remaining ladyfingers and mascarpone.
  8. Finally, dust the entire tiramisu with cocoa powder.
  9. Chill for at least 2 hours before serving.
  10. Garnish with fresh raspberries and enjoy a glass of Prosecco with your tiramisu!

Embrace the essence of Italy through these timeless dishes. From the comforting warmth of osso buco, to the iconic carbonara pasta, and the irresistible tiramisu, these culinary gems offer a true taste of the Italian experience.

Buon Appetito!
