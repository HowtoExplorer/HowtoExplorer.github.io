The Best Music Podcasts to Tune Into in 2023


Podcasts have become one of the most dynamic ways to explore and experience music commentary, history, and culture. Whether you’re a devoted audiophile or just someone looking to expand your musical horizons, there’s a podcast for every type of listener. From in-depth discussions on the evolution of genres to artist interviews and the uncovering of musical gems, podcasts offer something truly unique to music enthusiasts.

In 2023, the music podcast landscape continues to thrive with a diverse selection of shows catering to every niche. Below, we’ve rounded up some of the best music podcasts to add to your playlist this year. Dive in and explore audio journeys that will enrich your appreciation for music, inspire your playlists, and connect you with stories behind the songs you love.

Switched on Pop

If you’ve ever wondered about the mechanics behind modern pop music, Switched on Pop is your go-to podcast. Hosted by musicologist Nate Sloan and songwriter Charlie Harding, this podcast dissects popular hits with a combination of wit, intelligence, and genuine curiosity. The duo explores why certain songs stick with us, how they are crafted, and the cultural forces that define today’s music trends.

With episodes featuring interviews with renowned artists, producers, and industry professionals, Switched on Pop goes beyond the surface to give listeners an understanding of the artistry and business of pop music. From chart-topping singles to underground movements, this podcast is an insightful exploration for anyone interested in dissecting what makes music tick.

Song Exploder

Hosted by Hrishikesh Hirway, Song Exploder is a podcast beloved by casual listeners and musicians alike. The concept is simple yet captivating: artists take apart one of their songs, piece by piece, to reveal the raw emotions, inspirations, and meticulous decisions that went into creating it. Listening to this podcast feels like being invited into the studio where magic happens.

Each episode features a wide array of genres and artists, from pop powerhouses to indie legends and hip-hop trailblazers. Recent highlights have included artists like Billie Eilish, Fleetwood Mac, and Run the Jewels. Whether you’re a fan of the featured song or approaching it for the first time, this podcast offers a deeper connection to music through storytelling.

Dissect

For those who crave deep, analytical conversations about music, Dissect is a must-listen. This serialized podcast takes an album-by-album approach, deconstructing every track to study its lyrical themes, production choices, and cultural impact. Host Cole Cuchna adopts a scholarly yet engaging tone, making each season akin to a graduate-level course in music appreciation.

Past seasons have examined groundbreaking works such as Kendrick Lamar’s To Pimp a Butterfly, Beyoncé’s Lemonade, and Frank Ocean’s Channel Orange. By dedicating full episodes or entire seasons to these monumental albums, Dissect provides a rich, immersive experience that music aficionados will undoubtedly appreciate.

Cocaine & Rhinestones

Created by Tyler Mahan Coe, Cocaine & Rhinestones is a journey into the fascinating history of 20th-century country music. Though the podcast specifically focuses on country, its appeal transcends the genre, offering insights into American music history, culture, and artistry.

Each meticulously researched episode peels back the myths and legends surrounding iconic country stars and events, uncovering stories of scandal, triumph, and heartache. Whether you’re a die-hard country fan or just someone curious about the genre’s roots, Cocaine & Rhinestones has the power to pull you in with its gripping storytelling.

No Dogs in Space

For punk rock and alternative aficionados, No Dogs in Space is a podcast to bookmark ASAP. Hosted by Marcus Parks and Carolina Hidalgo, this show delves into the history of iconic punk and post-punk acts while celebrating the rebellious spirit that defined these subcultures.

Rather than sticking to surface-level anecdotes, No Dogs in Space takes a comprehensive approach, often dedicating multiple episodes to the same artist. The hosts bring both humor and historical depth to their narrations, making this an ideal listening experience for anyone eager to learn about punk icons like The Ramones, The Stooges, and others who shaped modern rock.

Questlove Supreme

It’s no surprise that Questlove, drummer of The Roots and an authority on all things music, would host one of the most engaging music podcasts around. Questlove Supreme is a mix of candid interviews, music history, and insightful storytelling, often with a dose of humor.

Each episode features legends from across the entertainment industry, from musicians to actors and writers. Questlove’s encyclopedic understanding of music lets him guide freewheeling discussions that can pivot from personal anecdotes to deep dives into music theory. This podcast is as diverse and eclectic as its host, perfect for anyone eager to soak in the wisdom of industry greats.

Broken Record

Broken Record comes alive thanks to the dynamic hosting team, which includes legendary producer Rick Rubin, author Malcolm Gladwell, former New York Times editor Bruce Headlam, and writer Justin Richmond. Together, they interview some of the biggest names in the music business, from well-established icons to contemporary stars.

This podcast revolves around extended, thoughtful conversations about music creation, storytelling, and the relationship between art and business. You’ll often gain behind-the-scenes perspectives and anecdotes that change how you listen to your favorite songs. The genuine connection between the hosts and their guests makes Broken Record an auditory treat.

The Mothership: Audio Fiction Meets Music

In a unique blend of storytelling and music, The Mothership combines fictional narratives with evocative soundscapes and original compositions. Each episode follows a new short story where music plays a central role, creating an immersive experience unlike anything else in the podcasting world.

Perfect for listeners who enjoy both music and creative storytelling, The Mothership crafts a cinematic journey that will leave you inspired. It’s especially suited to those who crave a podcast that isn’t afraid to merge artistic disciplines in unexpected ways.

Twenty Thousand Hertz

While not exclusively about music, Twenty Thousand Hertz explores the fascinating world of sound. Hosted by Dallas Taylor, this podcast is an insightful deep dive into the auditory experiences that shape our worlds, including music. From the psychology of sound design to the way iconic sounds like auto-tune and synthesizers revolutionized the industry, this podcast opens a whole new way of looking (or listening) to music.

With episodes that are well-researched, expertly produced, and thought-provoking, Twenty Thousand Hertz is perfect for curious minds who want to go beyond just music to explore the science and influence of sound.

Why Music Podcasts Matter More Than Ever

Music podcasts have carved out a unique space in today’s media landscape by offering a blend of education, entertainment, and intimacy. They provide a platform for voices that might not always be heard through traditional media, let fans connect more deeply with their favorite artists, and create a community around shared passions.

The best music podcasts in 2023 highlight the ever-growing desire for thoughtful, creative, and meaningful discussions surrounding music in all its forms. Whether you’re looking to gain insights into the history of a genre, discover how iconic hits were made, or just hear entertaining stories from the people behind the music, there’s a podcast for you.

So, next time you’re searching for audio inspiration, give these podcasts a listen. Your playlists—and your understanding of music—might just change forever.
