Building Rapport with Your Interviewer: Tips for Establishing a Connection


Interviews can be nerve-wracking experiences. The pressure to make a good impression and showcase your skills can sometimes overshadow the importance of building rapport with your interviewer. Establishing a connection with the person sitting across the table from you can be the key to success. So, how can you build rapport and establish a meaningful connection during your interview? Here are some valuable tips to help you navigate this crucial aspect of the job interview process.

1. Do Your Research

Before the interview, take the time to research the company and the person who will be interviewing you. Familiarize yourself with the company’s values, mission, and recent achievements. Look up the interviewer’s background and professional accomplishments. This knowledge will not only impress the interviewer but also provide you with valuable talking points to initiate conversation and demonstrate your interest in the organization.

2. Show Genuine Interest

During the interview, make an effort to engage with the interviewer on a personal level. Show genuine interest in what they have to say and actively listen to their questions and comments. This will help you establish a connection based on mutual respect and understanding. Ask thoughtful follow-up questions and respond with enthusiasm and curiosity. By demonstrating your interest in the conversation, you will create a positive and engaging atmosphere.

3. Mirror Non-Verbal Cues

Non-verbal communication plays a significant role in building rapport. Mirroring the interviewer’s non-verbal cues can create a sense of familiarity and connection. Pay attention to their body language, tone of voice, and facial expressions. Match their level of energy and enthusiasm without appearing insincere. This subtle mirroring technique can help establish a sense of rapport and make the interviewer feel more comfortable in your presence.

4. Share Personal Stories

While it’s important to maintain professionalism during an interview, sharing relevant personal stories can help create a personal connection. When appropriate, sprinkle in anecdotes that demonstrate your skills, problem-solving abilities, or lessons learned from past experiences. These stories will not only showcase your qualifications but also help the interviewer relate to you on a more personal level. Just remember to keep your stories concise and relevant to the conversation.

5. Find Common Ground

Finding common ground with your interviewer can be a powerful way to build rapport. Look for shared interests or connections that you can bring up during the conversation. It could be something as simple as a hobby, a mutual acquaintance, or a shared passion for a particular industry trend. By establishing common ground, you create a bond that goes beyond the professional realm, making the interview more enjoyable and memorable for both parties.

6. Express Gratitude and Follow-Up

After the interview, don’t forget to express your gratitude for the opportunity to meet with the interviewer. Send a personalized thank-you email or note within 24 hours, reiterating your interest in the position and appreciation for their time. This simple act of courtesy will leave a positive impression and further strengthen the connection you established during the interview.

Building rapport with your interviewer is an invaluable skill that can set you apart from other candidates. By doing your research, showing genuine interest, mirroring non-verbal cues, sharing personal stories, finding common ground, and expressing gratitude, you can establish a connection that goes beyond the interview itself. Remember, building rapport is about creating a genuine connection based on mutual respect and understanding. So, the next time you walk into an interview, don’t forget to prioritize building rapport alongside showcasing your skills and qualifications.
