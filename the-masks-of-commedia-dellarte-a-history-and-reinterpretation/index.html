The Masks of Commedia dell'Arte: A History and Reinterpretation


Commedia dell’Arte, or “sealed fossil of European myth” as playwright Dario Fo once called it, is the oldest form of improvised comedy. This unique theater art form originated in Italy during the 16th century, germinating from the country’s rich narrative and improvisational tradition. Commedia dell’Arte shells out various characters, each donning masks that became staple props in the renascent Italian comic scenes.

The Main Masks of Commedia dell’Arte

The masks of Commedia dell’Arte were pivotal protagonists in the performances. They played pivotal roles, speaking neither words nor uttering any sound—heavy on mime, they stood as silent personas of the theatrical dialogues. The four main masks consisted of the Zanni, Pantalone, the Capitano, and Harlequin.

  1. Zanni: Originating from a Turkish troupe, the Zanni are traditionally stereotyped as stubborn, cunning, and goes-to-market peasant or servant characterized by his wine-soaked lifestyle.

  2. Pantalone: Known as the doddering old merchant, the Pantalone character was often an enigma, whose mimicked money-made antics would infuse physical humor into the performances.

  3. Capitano: A fanciful figure to say the least, the Capitano mask depicted a captain, just randy with machismo. The character often played the philanderer in the Italian comedic plotlines.

  4. Harlequin: Also known as Arlecchino, Harlequin is possibly the most celebrated character in Commedia dell’Arte. Dressed colorfully and perpetually needy, the Harlequin played the quintessential comedic role, triggering laughter with his masterful acrobatics and slapstick humor.

The Wearing of the Masks

After their creation, the masks of Commedia dell’Arte played an intriguing role not just as props, but metaphorical identifiers of the characters. The Costumed characters would wear all-encompassing attire, donning their respective masks in sync with the plot situation. For instance, the Harlequin’s mask of merry mischief would differentiate him from the Pantalone’s conservative older man facade.
