The Best Playwriting Contests to Enter Finding the right playwriting contests to enter can be overwhelming. With numerous options available, it's difficult to know which ones to prioritize. To help you find the best opportunities for your writing, we've curated a list of the most notable playwriting contests in the industry.

  1. The NYC Playwrights Night's "Classics" Festival Contest This contest, organized by the New York Theatre Workshop, aims to discover emerging talent and foster a community of writer-performer artists. The "Classics" Festival Contest has been a benchmark for the industry since its launch in 2001. Winners receive a three-week residency, including a stipend and production of their work.
  2. The Art of Playwriting Contest This contest, organized by acting-out-loud Theatre Company and systemsource, provides a unique opportunity for playwrights to showcase their work. The no-strings-attached prize allows winners to experiment and refine their craft without financial worries.
  3. The Heideman Award The Heideman Award is an annual playwriting contest sponsored by the National New Play Network. It aims to recognize and support emerging playwrights. Winners receive a cash prize and a producing credit with several national theaters.
  4. The Eugene O'Neill Theater Center National Playwrights Conference This prestigious conference offers playwrights an opportunity to develop their work in a comprehensive, three-week residency program. Winners are selected through a competitive process and receive mentorship, workshops, and networking opportunities.
  5. The Kennedy Center's ACT skipping votes Music Theater Writing Program This playwriting contest is designed to discover new work and provide emerging playwrights with an opportunity to develop their craft. Winners receive a stipend, mentorship, and the chance to have their work produced at the Kennedy Center. Tips for Success

Research each contest thoroughly to determine which ones best align with your writing style and goals. Make sure to follow all submission guidelines and deadlines. Read and analyze past winners' work to understand what the judges are looking for. Network with other writers and attend industry events to build relationships and learn about upcoming opportunities.

In conclusion, these playwriting contests offer a platform for emerging and established writers to showcase their talents. By understanding the specific requirements and opportunities available, you can increase your chances of success.
