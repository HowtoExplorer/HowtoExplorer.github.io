Interview Stress Relief: Tips to Stay Calm and Collected


Interviews can be a nerve-wracking experience, from the initial sweaty-palm feelings to the seemingly never-ending wait for that long-awaited “You’re hired!” moment. However, with the right mindset and preparation, you can make that journey a bit smoother. Here are some effective tips to help you get through the interview process with your sanity and self-confidence intact.

  1. Prepare, prepare, prepare

Preparation is your best friend. Familiarize yourself with the company and the role you’re applying for. If you’re unsure about the specific questions you might be asked, it’s a good idea to research common interview questions and practice your answers.

  1. Practice mindfulness techniques

Mindfulness techniques can help you in many situations, including during interviews. Take a minute to close your eyes and breathe deeply. Release your tension with each exhalation and focus only on your breath. This quick exercise can make a difference in your focus during the interview.

  1. Get a good night’s rest

You’d be surprised by how much a good night’s rest can impact your performance during an interview. Make sure to get plenty of sleep before the big day. Your brain will thank you!

  1. Visualize success

Visualizing yourself succeeding can positively impact your confidence levels. Picture yourself answering questions fluently and feasting on that interview-winning smile.

  1. Have a positive mindset

A positive mindset can be your secret weapon during the interview process. Train yourself to look at each challenge as an opportunity for growth, and you’ll find the process much less daunting.

  1. Dress for success

First impressions matter, so dressing the part is essential. Dressing professionally not only signals to the employer that you take the role seriously but also boosts your own confidence.

  1. Arrive early

Arriving early gives you ample time to compose yourself, review your notes, and mentally prepare for the interview. Plus, showing up on time or early always makes a great impression.

  1. Stay hydrated

Keeping your body well-hydrated will help you maintain focus during the interview.

  1. Ask open-ended questions

During the interview, asking open-ended questions not only showcases your curiosity and interest in the position, but it can also give you valuable information about the role and company culture.

  1. Follow up

After the interview, be sure to send a follow-up email to express your gratitude for the opportunity to interview and to reiterate your interest in the role. This can set you apart from other candidates and may help with your chances of getting hired.

Remember, interviews can be a learning experience even if you don’t get the job. Each interview brings valuable lessons on how to improve and approach future interviews differently. With the right mindset and some careful preparation, you’ll be able to navigate even the most challenging interview with grace, poise, and confidence. Good luck!
