The Dos and Don'ts of Exercising in Summer Heat: Staying Safe & Healthy


In the warm embrace of summer, maintaining a regular workout routine can seem like a daunting task. The intense heat exudes a challenge of its own, prompting us to ponder over the wisdom of stepping out for a jog, or even a brisk walk. However, exercising during the summer months isn’t just feasible - it’s invigorating and necessary. To ensure a safe and healthy summer exercise routine, it’s essential to learn the dos and don’ts of working out in the heat.

Do Find Your Personal Prime Time

A critical dos of summer exercising is to adapt your workout schedule to align with the cooler parts of the day. This period typically falls in the early morning and late evening. By exercising during these times, you not only ward off the stifling heat, but also benefit from a taste of morning dew or the cool breeze of the evening.

Do Stay Hydrated

Proper hydration is key, especially during summer workouts. Equally essential is consuming the right balance of electrolytes to replace fluids lost through sweating. It’s recommended to sip water throughout your workout and, if necessary, opt for hydrating sports drinks to ensure you’re replenishing electrolytes like sodium, potassium, and magnesium in your system.

Do Invest in High-Quality Clothing

The type of clothes you wear during your summer workouts can significantly impact your comfort level. Opt for breathable, moisture-wicking fabrics that draw sweat away from the body, such as technical synthetic or blends that pair synthetic with natural fibers. Long, lightweight, and light-colored options help your body retain less heat, making workouts more comfortable.

Do Pay Attention to Your Body

Listen to your body - sweat beading on your skin is not a cause for concern, but signs that your body is working hard to cool itself. However, watch out for dehydration symptoms like dizziness or tiredness. Take breaks if needed, but avoid consuming sugary drinks or alcohol, as they can exacerbate dehydration.

Don’t Neglect Sun Protection

The sun’s ultraviolet (UV) radiation can significantly increase your risk of skin cancer, heat exhaustion, and heat stroke. Don’t forget sunblock when you’re outdoors, and protect yourself with a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, and cover-ups if possible.

Don’t Ignore the Weather Forecasts

Constant changes in weather add an element of unpredictability to exercising outdoors. Stay informed about the weather forecast and plan your workout indoors, especially in the event of extreme heatwaves or storms that can make it unsafe to go out.

Don’t Rush Into Extended Workouts

In the summer, your body requires more recovery time than during other seasons. Don’t try to slug through a longer workout routine just because you feel lethargic or spirits dip. Reduce the duration or intensity of your workouts, paying more attention to the quality instead of quantity of your exercise.

Don’t Overlook Post-Workout Cooling

Don’t underestimate the importance of cooling down after a workout in the summer heat. Finish your exercise routine with a light, five-minute stretch that gradually brings your heart rate down to prevent sudden changes in body temperature.

Conclusion

A healthy exercise routine in the summer heat is no different from any other season if you follow these dos and don’ts. Remember to plan ahead, listen to your body, drink plenty of water, and protect yourself from the sun. Staying healthy and active during the warmest months boosts your summer spirit and prepares you for winters too. The time to withstand the heat is now - so don their protective gear, gear up, and embrace the sweat!
