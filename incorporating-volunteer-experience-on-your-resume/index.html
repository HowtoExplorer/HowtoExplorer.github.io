Incorporating Volunteer Experience on Your Resume


Are you looking to enhance your resume and stand out from the competition? One effective way to do so is by incorporating volunteer experience. Volunteer work not only showcases your commitment to making a positive impact on society but also demonstrates valuable skills and qualities that employers value. In this blog post, we will explore how to effectively incorporate volunteer experience on your resume, highlighting its significance and providing practical tips to make your resume shine.

Why Include Volunteer Experience?

Volunteer experience holds immense value when it comes to impressing potential employers. It goes beyond simply listing job titles and responsibilities, allowing you to showcase your character, values, and commitment. Here are a few reasons why you should consider including volunteer experience on your resume:

  1. Demonstrates initiative: By engaging in volunteer work, you demonstrate a proactive approach to making a difference in your community. Employers appreciate individuals who take initiative and show a genuine passion for contributing to society.

  2. Highlights transferable skills: Volunteer work often provides opportunities to develop and enhance a wide range of transferable skills, such as leadership, teamwork, communication, and problem-solving. These skills are highly sought after by employers across various industries.

  3. Shows passion and commitment: Including volunteer experience on your resume reflects your dedication to a cause or organization. It demonstrates your ability to commit to something outside of your professional obligations, which can be seen as a positive attribute by potential employers.

How to Incorporate Volunteer Experience on Your Resume

Now that you understand the importance of volunteer experience, let’s explore how to effectively incorporate it into your resume:

1. Create a dedicated section

Start by creating a dedicated section for volunteer experience on your resume. This will ensure that it receives the attention it deserves and allows employers to easily identify your commitment to community involvement. Place this section after your work experience or education, depending on which is more relevant to the position you are applying for.

2. Provide a concise description

For each volunteer experience, provide a concise description that highlights the organization’s mission, your role, and the impact you made. Focus on quantifiable achievements whenever possible. For example, instead of stating, “Volunteered at a local food bank,” say, “Volunteered 10 hours per week at XYZ Food Bank, serving over 500 meals to those in need.”

3. Emphasize relevant skills

When describing your volunteer experience, emphasize the skills and qualities that are relevant to the position you are applying for. For instance, if you are applying for a leadership role, highlight your experience in leading a team of volunteers or organizing fundraising events. Tailoring your volunteer experience to align with the job requirements will make it more impactful.

4. Include dates and duration

Be sure to include the dates of your volunteer experience and the duration of your involvement. This information provides context and demonstrates your commitment to the cause or organization.

5. Connect volunteer experience to professional goals

When incorporating volunteer experience on your resume, consider how it aligns with your professional goals. Connect the skills and experiences gained through volunteering to the position you are applying for. This will help employers see the relevance and value of your volunteer work in relation to the job at hand.

6. Seek recommendations

If possible, ask for recommendations from the organizations or individuals you volunteered with. Recommendations add credibility to your volunteer experience and provide employers with valuable insights into your character and work ethic.

Final Thoughts

Incorporating volunteer experience on your resume can be a powerful tool to differentiate yourself from other candidates. It showcases your character, values, and commitment while highlighting transferable skills that employers seek. By following the tips provided in this blog post, you can effectively integrate volunteer experience into your resume and increase your chances of landing your dream job.

Remember, your volunteer work is a testament to your dedication and passion. Leverage it to demonstrate your unique qualities and make a lasting impression on potential employers. Good luck!
