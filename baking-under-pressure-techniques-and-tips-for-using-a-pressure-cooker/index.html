Baking Under Pressure: Techniques and Tips for Using a Pressure Cooker


Pressure cookers are a wonderful kitchen tool that allow us to cook food faster and with minimal energy consumption. Often thought of as simply a means for preparing quick stews and beans, the pressure cooker is a highly versatile appliance that can even be used for baking delicious treats like cakes, pastries, and bread. In this blog post, we will explore some techniques and tips to help you bake under pressure and become a master of both the savory and sweet worlds in your kitchen.

Adapting Recipes

Before diving into the technical aspects of baking under pressure, it’s essential to understand that some tested recipes or tried-and-true cookbooks may not have pressure cooker recipes. Fear not – you don’t have to scour the internet for hours or adapt complex formulas to use your appliance. The beauty of baking with a pressure cooker is its simplicity. Basic batters and doughs work beautifully, so simple recipes are your best bet when starting your culinary journey into pressure cooking.

Essential Tips for Baking in a Pressure Cooker

  1. Choose the Right Size: It may seem obvious, but ensure your baking dish fits comfortably in your pressure cooker. Ensure to measure the depth of your chosen dish; it should not obstruct the pressure cooker’s sealing mechanism and must leave enough space for steam to circulate.

  2. Preparation is Key: Cleaning and prepping the pressure cooker is crucial before diving into baking. Preheat your cooker without adding any water to simulate the conditions in a traditional oven. Furthermore, it’s recommended to place the baking dish on a trivet or sling to evenly distribute the heat.

  3. Adjust Cooking Time Accordingly: Baking under pressure significantly reduces cooking time, but it in no way means that all conventional recipes’ cooking times will be halved. Instead, it’s essential to monitor your treats closely and keep an eye on textures, consistency, and doneness. A helpful approach is to use the pressure cooker’s settings to assist, leveraging the “keep warm” function post-cooking to let steam out while maintaining warmth.

  4. Temperature Control: Unlike traditional ovens, pressure cookers can be challenging when it comes to maintaining a specific temperature. Your best bet is to use smaller, shallow dishes that enable the even distribution of heat and steam, ensuring consistency in your baking results.

  5. Ignoring Conventional Wisdom: Certain rules that apply to baking don’t always hold up in a pressure cooker. For example, after melting butter in the pressure cooker pot, you can instantly add your dry ingredients to create a smooth batter. Not having to worry about room temperature ingredients or separating egg yolks and whites introduces a whole new realm of simplicity.

Common Myths About Baking in Pressure Cookers

  1. Overcooking is Impossible: A common misconception might tell you that food overcooks in a pressure cooker, becoming dry or burnt. However, with proper time management and careful supervision, this rubric holds no truth. Instead, baking under pressure can lead to the moist and tender texture that many desire in baked goods.

  2. Restricted Recipe Options: Since traditional mixing bowls, spoons, and whisking are unnecessary, baking in a pressure cooker can seem limiting. Still, a vast array of possibilities exists, ranging from moist cakes to flaky, delightsome pastries, to crusty bread rolls. The only real restriction comes from personal creativity and exploration.

The Final Verdict

Baking under pressure opens a world of culinary possibilities with minimal fuss and mess. It’s all about adapting flavors, techniques, and existing recipes to create scrumptious indulgences in less time and with less effort than ever before. Once you embrace the power of pressure cooking, you’ll see that sugar, flour, and eggs can transform into sweet memories that last a lifetime.

Who says a pressure cooker is only for stews and beans? Now, unleash your inner pastry chef, and let the pressure cooker work its magic on your desserts. Your oven will finally get a break; after all, variety is the spice of life!
