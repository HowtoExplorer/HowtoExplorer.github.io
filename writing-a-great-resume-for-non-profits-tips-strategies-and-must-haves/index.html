Writing a Great Resume for Non-Profits: Tips, Strategies, and Must-Haves


A well-written resume is the cornerstone of any job application process, particularly in the non-profit sector. This is because the demand for highly skilled and compassionate professionals to make a positive impact in the world is greater than ever before. And with the competition fierce, you need to stand out from the crowd. Don’t worry, with the right tips, strategies, and must-haves, you can create the perfect resume tailored specifically for your non-profit job hunt.

  1. Tailor Your Resume to the Position and Organization

It should come as no surprise that tailoring your resume to the specific position and organization is extremely vital for success – especially if you are entering the non-profit world. Prioritize learning about the organization’s mission, values, and culture thoroughly. This is not only an opportunity to see if you align with their beliefs, but it will also provide insight into the preferred qualities and skills employers value.

Incorporate keywords directly from the job description into your resume. With this method, you not only demonstrate your relevancy to the position, but you also increase your chances of passing applicant tracking systems (ATS). Moreover, use keywords throughout your resume that reflect the core competencies required for success in the non-profit industry, such as fundraising, community outreach, and volunteer management.

It’s also important to emphasize how your unique background and experiences align with the organization’s mission. Use concrete examples and anecdotes to showcase how you have previously contributed to similar causes.

  1. Emphasize Your Contributions to the Field

Non-profit organizations are looking for passionate individuals who actively work towards positive change in society. As such, showcase any relevant experience, volunteer work, and leadership positions that exhibit your dedication toward the greater good. No experience in the non-profit field? No worries! Quantify any other related experiences by emphasizing the skills you used in a non-profit setting – how you led projects or fundraisers, managed budgets, or formed and motivated teams.

When listing your achievements, make sure to use action verbs that reflect growth and impact. Providing context on how you contributed to a specific cause will show that you understand the importance of accountability and are a team player. Focus on the measurable outcomes of your work, using numbers and metrics to demonstrate your capabilities.

  1. Clear Sections and a Comprehensive Format

Employers in the non-profit sector will be looking for specific information, so be sure to make it as straightforward as possible. Organize your resume into easy-to-read sections: Personal Information, Professional Experience, Relevant Skills, and Education.

Use clean and easily readable fonts, such as Arial or Calibri, and maintain consistent formatting throughout your resume. Include headers, bullet points for better readability, and parallel structure for consistency in your descriptions.

  1. Highlight Your Relevant Skills

The most sought-after skills in the non-profit industry include strategy and planning, financial management, storytelling and writing, communication, and teamwork. Don’t forget to incorporate these essential skills in your resume. Use both hard skills like proficiency in a specific computer program (e.g., Raiser’s Edge) and soft skills like effective communication and problem-solving to showcase your well-rounded abilities.

  1. Appendix: Keep It Brief and Engaging

An appendix not only showcases additional experiences but also helps to diversify your resume, making it more visually appealing. You can include items such as volunteer work, professional affiliations, professional publications, community service activities, and coursework relevant to the position.

But, be careful not to overcrowd your resume with extracurricular activities. Employers appreciate well-rounded candidates, but they also prioritize relevancy. Focus on the experiences and accomplishments that truly make you a strong candidate for a non-profit role.

Conclusion

When crafting a stellar resume for the non-profit sector, be sure to tailor your resume according to the position and organization, highlight your achievements, maintain a clear and comprehensive format, emphasize your relevant skills, and carefully select the right appendix sections. This combination offers a strong foundation for landing your dream non-profit job, and making a positive impact in the world.
