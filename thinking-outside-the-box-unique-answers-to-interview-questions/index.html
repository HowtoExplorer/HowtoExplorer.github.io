Thinking Outside the Box: Unique Answers to Interview Questions


Interviews can be daunting for anyone, even the most experienced professionals. The classic formula of “Talk about your experience” or “Why should we hire you?” can grow tiresome after a while. In order to separate yourself from the crowd, you need to think outside the box and provide unique answers to these interview questions.

First, before you step into the interview room, practice answering these questions with an unconventional twist. This will help you stay calm and prepared for any question thrown your way. Here are some of the most common interview questions along with unique answers to inspire you.

1. Tell me about yourself.

This is the perfect opportunity to showcase your personality and unique qualities. Instead of the standard “I graduated from XYZ, and I’ve worked in ABC industry for five years,” turn it into a story. Give an example that showcases your skills and experiences.

For example, instead of: “I graduated from XYZ University with a degree in Marketing, and since then, I’ve worked in the automotive industry for the past five years.”

Try: “Growing up, I had this dream to create a racing team with cars that were as fast as my favorite cartoon characters. After graduating from XYZ University, I worked to achieve this dream by establishing a marketing team within the automotive industry. Through hard work and determination, we’ve successfully launched several top-performing car models.”

This answer not only showcases your academic achievements but also your passion for the industry and previous work experience.

2. Why do you want to work for this company?

Instead of just telling the interviewer how cool the company is, demonstrate your interest in the organization by analyzing a business challenge they’ve recently faced.

For example, you could mention a news article you read about the company dealing with a particular challenge, and explain how you could be a part of the solution.

3. Why should we hire you?

This question often generates generic answers about your qualifications and experience. However, take a different approach and describe how you would be an asset to the team.

For instance, “When I was in my previous role, I noticed that there was a disconnected relationship between the marketing and sales departments. After conducting research and developing a comprehensive strategy, we were able to double annual sales. I believe I can use this experience and my problem-solving skills to help your team achieve the same results.”

4. Where do you see yourself in five years?

This is a tricky question that often calls for a generic answer. Instead, use it to explain how your current role aligns with your long-term goals.

For example, “In the next five years, I’d like to continue growing my skills in marketing and contribute to the success of this company. I believe the role at Company XYZ offers me both the challenges and opportunities to help me further develop my profession. I’m eager to utilize my skills and expertise to improve the marketing efforts and participate in the broader company objectives.”

It’s crucial to remember that thinking outside the box during an interview is a skill in itself. It demonstrates your creativity, ability to communicate effectively, and your willingness to approach challenges in a unique way. By doing so, you can set yourself apart from other candidates and impress your future employers. So, the next time you’re preparing for an interview, practice these tips and show them what you’re made of.
