The Best Fashion Documentaries to Watch for Style Inspiration


Fashion is more than just clothes; it’s a reflection of culture, identity, history, and, of course, style. Over the years, countless fashion documentaries have delved into the lives of designers, models, and the evolution of fashion trends. These films give us an intimate look behind the scenes of the fashion world, offering insights that can transform the way we view style and self-expression. If you’re looking for inspiration for your wardrobe or just want to appreciate the artistry of fashion, here are some of the best fashion documentaries that will ignite your creativity and deepen your love for style.

1. The September Issue (2009)

When it comes to fashion, Vogue is often seen as the gold standard, and The September Issue takes you inside the creation of its most important issue of the year: the September edition. This documentary follows Anna Wintour, the legendary editor-in-chief of Vogue, as she navigates the high-stakes world of fashion publishing. It offers a behind-the-scenes look at the editorial process, the pressure of putting together a magazine that sets the tone for the fashion season, and the personalities that define the world of high fashion.

For anyone interested in understanding the business side of fashion and the relationship between design, photography, and styling, The September Issue is a must-watch. It also provides a fascinating insight into Anna Wintour’s leadership and how she has shaped the modern fashion landscape.

What You’ll Learn:

  • The art of curating a fashion magazine
  • The importance of visual storytelling in fashion
  • How style influences trends across industries

2. Dior and I (2014)

If you’ve ever wondered what it’s like to work for one of the most iconic fashion houses in the world, Dior and I offers a unique glimpse into the creation of the first haute couture collection by Raf Simons for Christian Dior. This documentary chronicles the high pressure and creative chaos that goes into designing an haute couture collection for one of the most famous names in fashion. It’s not just about clothes; it’s about vision, artistry, and pushing boundaries.

Simons’ first collection for Dior had to balance tradition with innovation, and the documentary beautifully showcases how he brought his contemporary vision to the house while respecting its storied history. Dior and I is a celebration of craftsmanship, teamwork, and the thrill of seeing your ideas come to life on the runway.

What You’ll Learn:

  • The intricacies of haute couture fashion
  • How designers blend modern aesthetics with tradition
  • The role of teamwork in creating a fashion collection

3. Bill Cunningham New York (2010)

Fashion photography is an art in itself, and Bill Cunningham New York tells the story of the legendary photographer who captured the vibrant fashion scene of New York City for over 40 years. Known for his candid photos of everyday people on the streets, Bill Cunningham didn’t just photograph high-end designers or models; he focused on the real people who made fashion come alive in the streets.

Through the lens of Cunningham’s camera, we get to see how street style can be just as influential as runway trends. His passion for fashion was infectious, and the documentary shows how he turned the streets of New York into a runway of its own. Cunningham’s eye for style is an inspiration for anyone looking to express themselves through clothing, no matter their budget or background.

What You’ll Learn:

  • The importance of street style in shaping fashion trends
  • How to find inspiration from everyday people
  • The intersection of personal style and culture

4. The First Monday in May (2016)

This documentary takes you inside the preparation for the 2015 Met Gala, one of the most extravagant and exclusive events in the fashion world. Hosted by the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the gala is an annual fundraising event that celebrates the opening of the museum’s fashion exhibit. The First Monday in May explores the meticulous planning, the fashion statements, and the cultural significance behind the event’s theme.

Through this film, we see how the Met Gala serves as a platform for designers, celebrities, and artists to push the boundaries of fashion. It’s a grand display of creativity, and the documentary gives viewers a deeper understanding of how fashion, art, and society intertwine.

What You’ll Learn:

  • How themes in fashion exhibitions inspire creative collections
  • The cultural impact of high-profile fashion events
  • The role of fashion as art

5. Advanced Style (2014)

A celebration of personal style and aging, Advanced Style introduces us to a group of New York-based women who refuse to conform to society’s expectations about aging. These women, who range from their 60s to their 90s, have embraced their unique sense of style, proving that fashion doesn’t have an age limit. The documentary focuses on their vibrant personalities, their bold fashion choices, and how they express their individuality through clothing.

This film is a powerful reminder that style is about confidence and creativity, no matter your age. The women featured in Advanced Style are proof that self-expression and fashion don’t stop when you get older. In fact, they thrive with age.

What You’ll Learn:

  • How personal style evolves over time
  • The importance of self-expression in fashion at any age
  • How fashion can empower individuals in their later years

6. McQueen (2018)

The life and career of Alexander McQueen is nothing short of extraordinary, and McQueen is a powerful tribute to the visionary designer who redefined the fashion world. Known for his provocative and darkly theatrical collections, McQueen was a master of blending artistry with fashion. The documentary takes us through his journey from his early days as a tailor in London to his rise as one of the most influential designers of his generation.

The film delves into McQueen’s genius for innovation, his struggles with mental health, and the controversies that surrounded his work. It’s an emotionally charged story that showcases the brilliance and the pain behind some of the most iconic fashion moments in history.

What You’ll Learn:

  • The artistic side of fashion design
  • How fashion can be used as a form of personal expression
  • The impact of mental health on creativity and success

7. The True Cost (2015)

Fashion is not just about aesthetics; it’s also about ethics. The True Cost examines the darker side of the fashion industry, specifically the environmental and social impacts of fast fashion. The documentary exposes the exploitation of workers, the environmental degradation caused by mass production, and the challenges faced by the garment industry in an increasingly globalized world.

While this film may not be focused solely on the glamour of fashion, it offers a critical perspective on how the fashion industry operates and the true cost of our consumption. It serves as a reminder that style and sustainability can go hand in hand, and it encourages viewers to make more conscious choices about how they engage with fashion.

What You’ll Learn:

  • The environmental and ethical challenges of the fashion industry
  • How fashion affects global economies and workers
  • Ways to build a more sustainable wardrobe

8. Halston (2019)

Halston takes us through the glamorous and tumultuous life of the American designer Roy Halston Frowick, whose creations became synonymous with 1970s luxury and modern style. Halston revolutionized American fashion, particularly in his embrace of minimalist elegance and mass production, which made high fashion more accessible to a wider audience.

The documentary explores his rise to fame, his influence on the fashion world, and his eventual downfall as he lost control of his brand. Halston’s story is one of vision, ambition, and the high stakes of building a fashion empire. Watching this documentary will inspire anyone interested in fashion entrepreneurship and the complexities of the designer brand lifecycle.

What You’ll Learn:

  • The intersection of fashion design and brand-building
  • How designers balance creativity and business
  • The impact of a designer’s personal brand on fashion

9. House of Z (2017)

House of Z tells the story of Zac Posen, one of the most talented and influential designers of the 21st century. Known for his sophisticated and feminine designs, Posen’s rise to prominence was rapid, and the documentary offers a candid look at the challenges he faced in maintaining his success in the competitive fashion industry.

This film showcases the creative process behind Posen’s collections, as well as the personal struggles that came with managing a high-profile fashion brand. House of Z is a must-watch for anyone interested in understanding the behind-the-scenes efforts required to build a successful career in fashion design.

What You’ll Learn:

  • The trials and triumphs of building a fashion brand
  • The balance between creativity and business in the fashion world
  • The pressures faced by designers in the industry

Conclusion

Fashion documentaries not only give us access to the minds of designers but also provide a deeper understanding of the industry’s evolution, the artistic process behind each collection, and the social impact of style. Whether you’re a fashion enthusiast, a budding designer, or someone who simply wants to elevate their personal style, these documentaries will provide you with a wealth of knowledge and inspiration.

From the iconic tales of designers like Alexander McQueen and Christian Dior to the street style influences captured by Bill Cunningham, these films offer unique perspectives on the world of fashion. So, grab some popcorn, sit back, and dive into the fascinating world of fashion documentaries – you never know what fresh ideas and styles might inspire you!
