Silence is Golden: Mastering the Pause in Interviews


Have you ever wondered why some interviewers seem to have the magic touch when it comes to conducting interviews? They appear to be able to extract more information from candidates than their counterparts, while seeming to require less effort?

One of the keys to these interviewers’ success may lie in their ability to effectively use silence as a powerful tool. John Keating, the teacher in the film “Dead Poets Society,” famously said, “Carpe diem. Seize the day, boys. Make your lives extraordinary.” But when it comes to conducting interviews, should we aim to seize the silence, too?

The Power of Silence

Silence carries many connotations in different contexts, and interviews are no exception. When used wisely, it can:

  1. Create space for candidates to reflect and provide more well-rounded answers
  2. Break through nervousness or initial hesitance
  3. Give the interviewer an opportunity to observe body language and other non-verbal cues

Mastering the pause in interviews is an art and science that takes practice, observation, and a willingness to let go of the need to fill every moment with sound.

Be Mindful of the Timed Nature of Interviews

Interviewers often feel the need to fill silences to avoid seeming unfriendly, disorganized, or genuinely uncertain of what to ask next. However, this is a mistake. In many cases, candidates appreciate these pauses. It’s essential to remember that interviews are not only about the candidate but also about learning more about the candidate.

When to Use Silence

So when should an interviewer seize the silence?

  1. When the Candidate Needs Time: Give them that space when they seem to be organizing their thoughts or reminiscing deeply on an experience. It shows empathy and understanding, and it may lead to more reflective and insightful answers.
  2. When the Candidate is Embarrassed or Uneasy: Sometimes, a few moments of silence can allow the candidate to regain their composure and feel more comfortable. This might be particularly helpful during a virtual interview setting where non-verbal cues can get lost.
  3. To Establish Authority: Sometimes, well-timed moments of silence can convey confidence and help the interviewer regain control during the interview.

Use Silence Wisely

A key aspect of mastering the pause is knowing when not to use it. Avoid unnecessarily long pauses or prolonged silences, as they might create discomfort or unease in the interviewee or give the impression that the interviewer is unprepared or disinterested. Balance is essential—it is not about awing the candidate with mere pauses.

Active Listening and Observational Skills

While using silence effectively in interviews can lead to better insights, it is crucial to apply it alongside strong active listening skills and the ability to read body language. If you feel that silence might be too intimidating for a particular candidate, try to gauge their reactions and limitations. You can then adjust your approach accordingly.

A Reynolds Number of Strategies

In fluid mechanics, the Reynolds number expresses the relation between various forces in a fluid flow. Interestingly, understanding strategies for pauses in interviews can also require navigating different forces. Like how a basal flow can sometimes lead to a turbulent one, simply giving space may not be enough; you have to be skillful in its execution.

Conclusion

The power of silence in interviews can be an incredibly valuable tool for obtaining better insights and engaging conversations. However, it’s only one piece of the puzzle. Interviewers must also focus on their active listening skills, cultural competency, and the ability to adapt their styles to meet the unique needs of each candidate. Seizing the silence in interviews is a step toward creating better conversations and more comprehensive evaluations. Just remember, as with many things in life, balance is critical. Carpe Diem, but don’t overdo it.
