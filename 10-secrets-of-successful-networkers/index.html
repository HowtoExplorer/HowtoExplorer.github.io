10 Secrets of Successful Networkers


As society continues to evolve technologically, networking remains a critical skill for both personal and professional development. Networkers can establish strong relationships with others, leading to new opportunities, increased business contacts, and the all-important “word-of-mouth” advertising. Here are ten essential secrets to help you navigate the world of networking:

  1. Start by Setting Your Goals

Being clear on what you want to achieve before you start networking is crucial. Are you looking for career advancement, building a business clientele, or new friendships? Clearly defined goals are the foundation of any successful strategy, and networking is no exception.

  1. Choose Your Platforms Wisely

Different people spend time on different platforms - it’s essential to find where your target audience is and invest your networking energy in those locations. LinkedIn is the go-to platform for professional/networking connections, while others may find success on Facebook groups, Twitter chats, or local Meetup groups.

  1. Build a Great Elevator Pitch

Your elevator pitch is an essential tool to introduce yourself to potential connections. A great pitch should explain what you do succinctly and in an interesting way. It’s your ten-second chance to make an impact, so practice, improve, and remember – attention spans shrink when people are introduced to new material.

  1. Offer Help

Offering help is a secret weapon for networkers. By demonstrating genuine interest in their needs and providing help, you can quickly establish trust and long-lasting relationships. It also helps create a reputation as someone reliable, which generates new opportunities.

  1. Be Aware of the Layers

Networking involves various layers, from acquaintances to close friends. It is necessary to be aware of the layers to build strong relationships – people appreciate sincerity and transparency. As relationships develop, it’s important to be conscious of how much information to share and when.

  1. Engage in Meaningful Conversations

Successful networkers listen more than they speak and engage in meaningful conversations, not limited to business subjects. Building trust starts with learning about others, their interests, and their backgrounds. This approach allows for authentic, connected conversations.

  1. Practice Consistency and Persistence

Networking is not a one-time event – it’s an ongoing process. Consistency is key to building relationships, and persistence helps turn connections into opportunity. Reach out to contacts regularly, carving out the time for referrals, follow-ups, or meet-ups.

  1. Seek Out Diverse Perspectives

The more diverse the perspectives, the more significant the learning. Push yourself to meet people that you wouldn’t typically encounter – international colleagues, individuals from different industries, hobby groups. These connections can often lead to the most intriguing opportunities.

  1. Know When to Follow Up

As a successful networker, you’ll have many names, numbers, and emails to store. Knowing when to reach out is crucial. Be considerate of the other person’s schedule, and strike a balance – don’t overlook the opportunity, but also don’t inundate your contacts.

  1. Celebrate Your Progress

Finally, celebrate your progress as you improve your networking skills. Each connection strengthens your network further, remember to acknowledge your achievements - every milestone is a small victory on your networking journey.

By incorporating these ten secrets into your approach, you’ll be well on your way to building a successful and meaningful network. Remember, relationships are the key to unlocking opportunities and opening doors you wouldn’t have thought possible. So go out there, be authentic, and build lasting connections through networking.
