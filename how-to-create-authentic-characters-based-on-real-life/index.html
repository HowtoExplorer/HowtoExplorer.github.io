How to Create Authentic Characters Based on Real Life


Creating characters that feel authentic is a delicate process that requires a deep understanding of the human psyche. Authentic characters are not just well-written, they're multidimensional, relatable, and paradoxical—often mirroring the complexities of real life. While fiction is meant to entertain, its greatest power resides in its ability to evoke empathy through storytelling, forcing readers or viewers to question their own ideologies, feelings, and actions. Knowing the Fundamentals Before diving into the intricacies of character creation, it's crucial to grasp some fundamental principles of psychology and sociology. This involves understanding the spectrum of human emotions, behaviors, and motivations. A person can be happy yet, let's say, socially anxious; their desire for joy conflicts with their need for a sense of belonging. Covering a wide range of these states and emotions can lead to more believable and authentic characters. While not every character has to have every type of motivation, this level of depth goes beyond what might seem necessary, but it truly isn't. Authenticity thrives when characters naturally demonstrate enrichment at every level, lending readers an ethnographic depth to grasp onto. Observing Life For writers to create authentic characters, they need to tap into observations from real life. What they see, hear, and learn from firsthand experiences can fill the gaps of imagination. Everyday Encounters Interacting with people from various backgrounds enriches the understanding of human behavior and emotion. Train conductors, for example, have experiences unmatched by many professionals—interactions with potentially millions of people annually. They know pain, fear, and euphoria—each type taken to extreme through extensive exposure to various situations. Daily conductors witness people cling to seats next to loved ones while boards through scrambling air transport and they observe fleeting romance within chance seat situations.
