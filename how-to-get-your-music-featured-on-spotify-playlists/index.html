How to Get Your Music Featured on Spotify Playlists


Breaking into the music industry has always been a challenge for up-and-coming artists, but with the rise of streaming platforms, the playing field has shifted. Spotify has become a powerhouse for music discovery, and being featured on one of its playlists can be a game-changer for artists looking to gain exposure, grow their audience, and even earn some steady income. But how do you make your way onto a Spotify playlist?

Getting featured is a combination of strategy, understanding the platform, and networking. Below, we break down the steps you can take to maximize your chances of landing a coveted spot on Spotify playlists.

Understand the Different Types of Spotify Playlists

Before diving into the “how,” it’s essential to know the types of playlists available. Spotify playlists typically fall into three categories:

  1. Editorial Playlists
    These are curated by Spotify’s in-house editorial team and focus on specific genres, moods, or events. Examples include “RapCaviar,” “Peaceful Piano,” and “Lorem.” Getting featured on an editorial playlist is a pivotal achievement, as it exposes your music to thousands—or even millions—of listeners.

  2. Algorithmic Playlists
    These playlists, including “Discover Weekly,” “Release Radar,” and “Daily Mix,” are personalized for every user based on their listening habits. While you can’t pitch your music directly for these playlists, your metadata and engagement metrics often determine whether your track gets picked up algorithmically.

  3. User-Curated Playlists
    These playlists are created by users, influencers, or independent curators. They can range from playlists with only a handful of listeners to extremely popular ones with tens or hundreds of thousands of followers.

Each type requires a different approach. A strategic plan should focus on gaining traction on user-curated and algorithmic playlists as stepping stones to being noticed by Spotify’s editorial team.

Perfect Your Music Production and Presentation

The first step to getting featured on any playlist is ensuring your music is high quality. Playlist curators, whether human or algorithmic, gravitate toward polished tracks. Here’s how you can prepare:

  • Professional Sound Quality
    Ensure your track is professionally mixed and mastered. Subpar sound quality could result in curators skipping over your music, regardless of how great the song is.

  • Genre and Mood Matching
    Your song should align closely with the theme, style, or niche of the playlist you’re targeting. Study the playlist’s vibe and listeners, and submit tracks that complement its overall aesthetic.

  • Engaging Visual Assets
    While playlist curators primarily focus on audio, your branding plays a background role. Ensure your album cover is visually appealing, as this could indirectly influence decisions when your track appears beside another in submissions.

  • Compelling Metadata
    Metadata includes your song’s title, description, associated genres, and tags. Use relevant and specific terms to help your track get accurately categorized in Spotify’s system. Avoid using misleading metadata, as it can harm your chances long-term.

Build and Optimize Your Spotify Artist Profile

Your Spotify profile is your digital portfolio. Playlist curators often check it before adding your song to their playlist, so make sure it reflects professionalism and artistry:

  • Update Your Bio
    Write a short, engaging bio that highlights your achievements, genre, and passion for music. Include links to your social media accounts so listeners can find and follow you elsewhere.

  • Add High-Quality Images
    Use professionally photographed images for your profile picture and header. A cohesive visual brand makes you appear serious about your artistry.

  • Leverage Spotify for Artists
    Claim your Spotify for Artists profile to access valuable analytics and tools. Here, you can submit songs for playlist consideration, track streaming metrics, and see which playlists are featuring your music.

  • Add a Canvas (8-Second Visual Loop)
    Use Spotify’s Canvas feature to add short video loops to your tracks. These can improve engagement, as they make your profile visually appealing and encourage listeners to spend more time interacting with your content.

Submit Your Music via Spotify for Artists

Spotify allows artists to directly submit unreleased music for editorial playlist consideration. This is your best chance to catch the attention of Spotify’s editorial team. Here’s how to do it:

  1. Set Up in Advance
    When releasing a new track, schedule it at least 3–4 weeks ahead of the release date. This gives curators enough time to review it.

  2. Detailed Submission Form
    When submitting your track, be as detailed as possible. Spotify asks about the song’s genre, instruments, mood, and links to external promotion (e.g., press releases or blog coverage). Give a clear and accurate description that highlights why your song is unique.

  3. Focus on Unreleased Tracks
    Editorial playlists favor unreleased material submitted through Spotify for Artists. Once the track is live, it’s less likely to be considered.

Network with Playlist Curators

For user-curated playlists, artist-curator relationships significantly influence your likelihood of being featured. Here’s how to build those connections:

  • Identify Key Curators
    Look for prominent user-curated playlists in your niche. Tools like Chartmetric, SubmitHub, and PlaylistSupply can help identify influential curators and evaluate levels of engagement.

  • Reach Out Professionally
    Send personalized emails or messages introducing yourself and your music. Avoid copy-pasting generic pitches. Provide value upfront by explaining how your track fits their playlist’s theme or benefits their audience.

  • Monitor Playlists Regularly
    Stay updated on which playlists are growing and align with your sound. Curators are often open to featuring artists with whom they’ve built connections.

Promote Your Music and Engage Listeners

Ultimately, the exposure your track receives plays a critical role in playlist placements. The more engagement your song generates, the higher the chances of getting noticed by both algorithms and curators. Here’s how to maximize engagement:

  1. Leverage Social Media
    Share your Spotify links across platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube to drive streams and shares. Each interaction signals growing popularity to algorithms.

  2. Collaborate with Influencers
    Reach out to content creators or social media influencers who align with your genre. A viral TikTok trend, for example, can lead to spikes in streams and playlist pickups.

  3. Encourage Saves and Shares
    Encourage fans to save your song, add it to their own playlists, or share it with their friends. These small actions can make a significant impact on Spotify’s algorithmic visibility.

  4. Play Live Shows and Engage Fans
    Real-world interactions often translate into digital engagement. Ask concert attendees to stream your song on Spotify after your set.

Use Data to Refine Your Strategy

The great thing about Spotify is its detailed analytics. Use data to refine your approach and better understand what’s working. Key metrics to focus on include:

  • Stream Count
    Tracks with higher numbers of plays are more attractive for playlist consideration.

  • Listener-to-Stream Ratio
    This metric shows how frequently the same listener is replaying a track, indicating its quality and resonance.

  • Geographic Data
    Playlists often target specific regions. Analyzing your listeners’ locations can give you insight into which curators to approach.

Keep Building a Long-Term Strategy

Getting featured on a Spotify playlist shouldn’t be a one-time goal. Instead, view it as one piece of a broader career-building strategy. Follow these tips for sustained growth:

  • Release Consistently
    Frequent releases keep your profile active and fresh in the eyes of curators and algorithms alike.

  • Stay Relevant
    Be open to collaborations or experimenting with genres that increase your likelihood of gaining playlist traction.

  • Build a Fanbase
    At the end of the day, curators look for tracks with strong fan support. Focus on cultivating an engaged audience who are as passionate about your music as you are.

Conclusion

Getting your music featured on Spotify playlists isn’t an overnight process, but with persistence, careful planning, and strategic execution, you can successfully navigate the platform’s ecosystem. Focus on creating quality music, optimize your presence online, network with curators, and consistently engage your audience.

The journey to playlist placement often starts small, but each step helps build momentum. Over time, even the smallest playlists can snowball into algorithmic recognition and editorial features, helping propel your musical career to new heights. Keep making great music, and never stop pushing forward—you never know when your next release will catch the attention of the right curator.
