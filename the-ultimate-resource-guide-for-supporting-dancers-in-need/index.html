The Ultimate Resource Guide for Supporting Dancers in Need


Dancing is an art form that requires grace, passion, and dedication. Whether amateur or professional, dancers often face physical, emotional, and financial challenges throughout their careers and personal lives. This ultimate resource guide aims to help both dancers and their supporters find the assistance they need to thrive in their pursuits and maintain overall well-being.

Dancer Health and Safety

Dancers are prone to injuries, especially in the feet, ankles, and spine. Thus, maintaining proper health and safety standards is essential. Here are resources for dancers seeking medical assistance, rehabilitation, and injury prevention tips:

  • American Dance Therapy Association (ADTA): ADTA promotes the use of dance and movement therapies to enhance health and well-being. Check out the directory to find certified dance therapists in your area.

  • Dance/USA Health & Wellness Fund: A grant program that helps dancers and choreographers cover medical expenses related to injury or illness.

  • ** dancerfit :** A website providing Pilates and authentic dance-inspired workouts to help prevent injuries and strengthen the body.

  • The DANCENORTH Technique™: An injury-prevention program that increases flexibility, mindfulness, and overall physical health for dancers.

Financial Assistance and Scholarships

For aspiring dancers struggling with college tuition and training fees, numerous scholarships and financial aid programs are available. Support your favorite dancer , or improve your chances to receive these benefits by scouting these resources:

  • Excelity Scholarships: A dance program that offers scholarships to students to pursue higher education in dance or related fields.

  • The Foundation for MRI: Supports dance students with tuition assistance, including scholarships, grants, and career development opportunities.

  • Summer Intensives: Many reputable dance conservatories offer merit-based and need-based scholarships for summer intensives. Be sure to inquire about available programs if you plan to attend one.

  • Carnegie Hall’s “Quest for Freedom” Program: Provides opportunities for young musicians, opera singers, and dancers to secure funding and exposure.

Emotional Support and Resources

Dancers often face immense pressure to maintain a vulnerable balance of physical and emotional demands. They may struggle with perfectionism, self-critical thoughts, eating disorders, and burnout. Check out these resources for maintaining mental well-being:

  • National Association of Anorexia Nervosa and Associated Disorders (ANAD): Provides support, resources, and helpline services for those struggling with eating disorders.

  • Mental Health America (MHA): Offers free and confidential screening tools, educational resources, and advocacy to promote mental health across the country.

  • Minding the Gap: A project by the Dance Advocacy Initiative for Dance/Movement Therapists (DADT) that aims to address and combat mental health issues within the dance community.

  • The Riley Institute: An educational program for dance educators on topics including body image and overall well-being.

Community and Networking Opportunities

Building connections within the dance community can provide invaluable networking opportunities for dancers and dance professionals. Here are some ways to promote connections, mentorship, and collaboration:

  • Dance Informa: An online magazine for dance professionals, students, and enthusiasts, providing news, resources, and community events.

  • DanceUSA: A national service organization offering resources, events, and networking opportunities for dance professionals, including financial assistance and health initiatives.

  • Project Motiv8: Motiv8 inspires and empowers under-resourced performers, integrating dance, theater, and technology.

  • Twitter and Facebook Groups: Join relevant online groups where network with dancers, choreographers, and educators, share resources, find job listings, or collaborate on projects.

This ultimate resource guide aims to support dancers and bring attention to the diverse needs of the dance community. By connecting individuals to valuable resources in health and wellness, financial assistance, emotional support, and networking opportunities, dancers can continue to thrive in their various dance-related endeavors.

Feel free to share this guide with any dancers, educators, or dance enthusiasts you know to create a stronger and healthier dance community together.
