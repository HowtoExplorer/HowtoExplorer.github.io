How to Create a Family Vision Board: A Guide


A family vision board is a powerful tool for bringing loved ones together to visualize, plan, and manifest the collective dreams and aspirations of the family. It’s a fun and meaningful activity that can help each member reflect on their individual goals while also focusing on shared family values and dreams. Whether you’re looking to strengthen family bonds, align your goals, or simply create a more positive family atmosphere, crafting a family vision board can be an enjoyable and transformative experience. This guide will walk you through the process step by step, helping you create a vision board that reflects your family’s vision for the future.

What is a Family Vision Board?

A family vision board is a visual representation of the dreams, values, and goals that a family wishes to achieve. It typically consists of images, words, and symbols cut from magazines, printed from the internet, or hand-drawn, all centered around a common theme. Each member of the family can contribute their own personal aspirations while also adding elements that reflect collective family goals. The vision board serves as a daily reminder of where the family is headed, providing motivation, inspiration, and a clear focus on their path forward.

Why Create a Family Vision Board?

Creating a family vision board offers many benefits. Here are a few reasons why this activity is a great idea for families:

  1. Alignment of Goals: A family vision board helps everyone align their individual goals with the family’s collective aspirations. It fosters unity by ensuring that everyone is working towards a common vision.

  2. Improved Communication: Crafting a vision board together provides an opportunity for open discussion about each family member’s dreams and how they can support one another in achieving them.

  3. Strengthened Bonds: Collaborating on a shared vision fosters a deeper connection between family members, as they work together to build a common future.

  4. Positive Focus: The vision board serves as a constant reminder of the family’s hopes and dreams, helping everyone stay positive and motivated even when times get tough.

  5. Visualization of Success: Visualizing goals can increase the likelihood of achieving them. A family vision board brings these goals to life in a tangible way.

Steps to Create a Family Vision Board

Creating a family vision board can be an enjoyable and creative process that sparks meaningful conversations. Below are the steps you can follow to build your family’s vision board.

Step 1: Set a Family Meeting

Before you begin gathering materials, it’s important to have a family meeting. During this meeting, discuss the purpose of the vision board and what each family member hopes to gain from the process. Ask the following questions:

  • What are your personal goals for this year?
  • What are your family goals?
  • What values do you want to strengthen as a family?
  • What type of experiences would you like to create together?

The key to this meeting is inclusivity—everyone, no matter how young or old, should feel heard and involved. This collaborative brainstorming session will set the tone for the entire project.

Step 2: Gather Materials

Once your family has aligned on the vision, it’s time to gather the necessary materials for the vision board. The supplies you’ll need include:

  • A large poster board or corkboard: Choose a surface large enough for all family members to contribute their ideas.
  • Magazines, newspapers, or printouts: These materials will be used to cut out images, words, and phrases that represent your family’s goals.
  • Scissors and glue: You’ll need these to cut out and affix your selections to the board.
  • Markers, pens, and stickers: Use these to add personal touches or to write specific goals and affirmations.
  • Additional craft supplies (optional): You might want to add glitter, fabric, or other embellishments to personalize your board.

Ensure that everyone has easy access to the materials, as it’s important that everyone is involved in the creative process.

Step 3: Select a Theme for Your Board

Decide on a theme that resonates with the family’s vision. You may want to focus on:

  • Family unity: Centered around improving communication, spending more time together, and strengthening relationships.
  • Adventure and travel: Focus on places you want to visit and experiences you want to share as a family.
  • Personal growth: A theme dedicated to improving personal development, learning new skills, and supporting each other’s individual growth.
  • Financial goals: A vision board to visualize family savings, investments, and financial milestones.
  • Health and wellness: Centered on creating healthy habits, fitness goals, and overall well-being.

The theme you choose will shape the overall vibe of the board and give your family a clear direction as you create it.

Step 4: Brainstorm and Select Images and Words

This step is all about collecting images, words, and phrases that represent your family’s dreams and values. Here’s how to get started:

  1. Individual Reflection: Allow each family member to reflect on their own goals and values. Encourage them to cut out images or words that reflect what they want to achieve.
  2. Family Reflection: Next, brainstorm as a family. Think about shared goals or values, such as love, growth, financial stability, fun, and togetherness. Collect images and words that represent these values.
  3. Diverse Representation: Choose words and images that represent a wide variety of goals, from tangible aspirations like buying a home to intangible ideas like creating more family fun time.

Encourage each family member to contribute something unique. For example, your young child might pick an image of a dream vacation spot, while a teenager might find inspiration in a picture of a college or career path.

Step 5: Arrange and Glue Everything on the Board

Now comes the fun part—arranging the items on the board. As a family, experiment with different layouts and placements until everyone feels satisfied with the arrangement. There are no strict rules here, so feel free to be as creative as you like.

Some families prefer to organize their vision board by sections, with one part dedicated to personal goals, another to family goals, and so on. Others might opt for a more fluid approach, blending everything together.

Once everyone is happy with the layout, begin gluing the images, words, and phrases onto the board. Take your time and enjoy the process. This is a chance for everyone to express themselves visually and create something meaningful.

Step 6: Add Affirmations and Personal Touches

As you glue the images, consider adding empowering affirmations and positive words. These could include:

  • “We are a happy, loving family.”
  • “We will grow together in love and harmony.”
  • “We achieve our dreams by working together.”
  • “We are healthy, strong, and successful.”

These affirmations reinforce the positive mindset and energy you want to bring into your lives. You can write them by hand or use pre-printed words to make them stand out.

Additionally, consider adding some personal touches such as drawings, quotes, or special symbols that represent your family’s unique personality and journey.

Step 7: Place Your Vision Board in a Visible Spot

Once the board is complete, it’s time to display it in a prominent place in your home. The goal is to ensure that it’s visible to everyone so that it serves as a constant reminder of the family’s shared dreams and goals. Popular places to hang the board include the living room, kitchen, or family room—anywhere your family spends a lot of time together.

Step 8: Revisit and Update the Vision Board Regularly

Creating the vision board is just the beginning. It’s important to revisit and update it regularly. You may find that some goals are achieved, while new dreams and aspirations emerge. Updating the board helps keep the energy fresh and ensures that the family’s vision evolves with time.

At least once a year, plan a family meeting to reflect on progress, discuss new goals, and refresh the board. This keeps everyone motivated and engaged in the process.

Final Thoughts

A family vision board is not just an art project—it’s a way to bring your family together, communicate openly, and work towards shared goals. Whether you’re striving for personal growth, financial success, or stronger family connections, a vision board serves as a tangible reminder of your dreams and aspirations. By regularly updating your board and reflecting on your progress, you can stay motivated and inspired as you work towards a brighter future.

Creating a vision board is an opportunity for your family to bond, share values, and manifest their dreams in a creative, positive way. So gather your materials, start brainstorming, and get ready to bring your family’s vision to life!
