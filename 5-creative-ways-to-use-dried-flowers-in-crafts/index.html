5 Creative Ways to Use Dried Flowers in Crafts


Dried flowers are a timeless craft material that brings a touch of nature into your creative projects. Their vibrant colors and unique textures can transform ordinary crafts into stunning masterpieces. Whether you’re a seasoned crafter or just starting out, there are countless ways to incorporate dried flowers into your work. Here, we’ll explore five innovative methods to use dried flowers in your crafts, ensuring that you can showcase their beauty and add a personal touch to your creations.

1. Dried Flower Pressing

One of the most traditional and beloved methods of using dried flowers is through flower pressing. This technique not only preserves the flowers but also allows you to create beautiful art pieces that capture their essence.

How to Press Flowers

  • Select Your Flowers: Choose freshly picked flowers that are in full bloom. Some popular options include pansies, daisies, and lavender. Avoid flowers that are too thick or have a lot of moisture.

  • Prepare for Pressing: Place the flowers between two pieces of absorbent paper, such as blotting paper or parchment paper. This will help absorb the moisture during the pressing process.

  • Pressing Method: Place the absorbent paper with the flowers inside a heavy book or a flower press. If using a book, stack additional heavy books on top to apply pressure. Leave them for at least a week to ensure they are fully dried and flattened.

Creative Uses

Once your flowers are pressed and dried, the possibilities are endless:

  • Framed Art: Arrange the pressed flowers in a shadow box or frame to create a stunning piece of wall art.

  • Greeting Cards: Use pressed flowers to embellish handmade cards for special occasions. Attach them with glue and write a heartfelt message inside.

  • Decorative Bookmarks: Cut cardstock into bookmark shapes and glue pressed flowers onto them for a beautiful and functional craft.

2. Dried Flower Resin Art

Resin art has gained immense popularity for its versatility and the breathtaking results it delivers. Dried flowers can be encapsulated in resin to create stunning pieces that are both beautiful and durable.

Steps to Create Dried Flower Resin Art

  • Gather Your Materials: You will need epoxy resin, a mold (silicone molds work best), dried flowers, gloves, and a mixing stick.

  • Prepare the Resin: Follow the instructions on your resin kit to mix the resin and hardener properly. Make sure to do this in a well-ventilated area.

  • Arrange the Flowers: Before pouring the resin, arrange your dried flowers in the mold. You can create layers by pouring a small amount of resin first, letting it partially cure, then adding more flowers and resin.

  • Pour and Cure: Slowly pour the mixed resin over the flowers, ensuring they are completely covered. Allow it to cure according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

Creative Uses

  • Coasters: Create unique coasters by using a round mold. These can add a pop of color to your coffee table.

  • Jewelry: Smaller molds can be used to create pendants or earrings, giving you a wearable piece of nature.

  • Home Decor: Use larger molds to create decorative trays or wall hangings that will catch the eye of anyone who enters your home.

3. Dried Flower Wreaths

Wreaths are a classic decorative item that can be made seasonally or year-round. Dried flowers bring an earthy, rustic charm, making them perfect for any home decor.

Creating a Dried Flower Wreath

  • Choose Your Base: You can use a vine wreath base, a foam ring, or even a wire frame to create your wreath.

  • Select Your Flowers and Foliage: Combine various dried flowers, such as statice, eucalyptus, and baby’s breath, to create texture and color.

  • Attach the Flowers: Begin by cutting the stems of your dried flowers to the desired length. Use floral wire or a hot glue gun to secure the flowers to the base, starting from the outer edge and working your way inward.

  • Finishing Touches: Once you’ve filled in the wreath, add a ribbon or twine for hanging and any additional embellishments you desire.

Creative Uses

  • Seasonal Decor: Change the flowers according to the season to keep your wreath fresh. Use autumn colors for fall and bright blooms for spring.

  • Door Hanger: Hang your wreath on your front door as a welcoming piece for guests.

  • Table Centerpiece: Place the wreath on a table and add candles in the center for a stunning centerpiece.

4. Dried Flower Candles

Incorporating dried flowers into candles is a creative way to add beauty and fragrance to your home. Not only do they look enchanting, but they also make wonderful gifts.

How to Make Dried Flower Candles

  • Gather Your Supplies: You will need wax (soy or beeswax), a wick, dried flowers, a double boiler, and candle molds or jars.

  • Melt the Wax: Use a double boiler to melt the wax. Once fully melted, let it cool slightly before pouring.

  • Prepare Your Flowers: Position the dried flowers at the bottom of your mold or jar before pouring in the wax.

  • Add the Wick: Secure the wick in the center of the mold or jar before pouring the wax, ensuring it stands upright.

  • Pour the Wax: Slowly pour the melted wax over the flowers, ensuring they are submerged. Allow the candle to cool and harden completely before trimming the wick.

Creative Uses

  • Home Fragrance: Light the candle to fill your home with a delightful, floral aroma, perfect for relaxation or entertaining guests.

  • Decorative Touch: Use colorful dried flowers to match your home decor, creating a cohesive aesthetic.

  • Gift Giving: Handmade dried flower candles make for thoughtful and personalized gifts for friends and family.

5. Dried Flower Potpourri

Creating potpourri is an excellent way to enjoy the scent of dried flowers while also utilizing them in a decorative manner. This craft is simple, and you can customize it to suit your preferences.

Steps to Make Dried Flower Potpourri

  • Collect Dried Flowers: Gather a variety of dried flowers, herbs, and spices. Popular choices include lavender, rose petals, and cinnamon sticks.

  • Mix Your Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the dried flowers and herbs. You can also add essential oils for an extra fragrance boost.

  • Add Color and Texture: Enhance your potpourri by adding colorful dried leaves, citrus peels, or even small pine cones for added texture.

  • Store in a Decorative Container: Place your potpourri mixture in a decorative bowl or jar, allowing it to serve as both a fragrance and decorative element in your home.

Creative Uses

  • Home Fragrance: Place bowls of potpourri around your home for a continuous, pleasant aroma.

  • Gift Bags: Fill small bags with potpourri and tie with a ribbon for charming, homemade gifts.

  • Car Fresheners: Use small sachets of potpourri to keep your car smelling fresh and inviting.

Conclusion

Dried flowers offer a world of creative possibilities in the realm of crafts. From pressed flower art to resin projects, wreaths, candles, and potpourri, there’s no shortage of ways to incorporate these natural beauties into your creations. Experiment with different techniques and let your creativity blossom. Whether you’re crafting for yourself or creating heartfelt gifts, dried flowers are sure to add that special touch to your projects. Embrace the beauty of nature and let your imagination run wild with these five creative ideas!
