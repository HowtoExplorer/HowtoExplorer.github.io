Tips for Traveling with Allergies: Preserving Your usual Hygiene Routine on the Go


As someone with allergies, going on vacation can be a daunting experience. Apart from being aware of attractions that might trigger allergic reactions, ensuring your usual hygiene routine is maintained while traveling is crucial. Here are some tips that will keep your allergy symptoms at bay.

Clear the Air: If you’re unfamiliar with your travel destination, ask for a room with a ventilation system or a window that can be opened before arriving. A well-ventilated space can minimize allergens like dust and pollen.

Embrace Hydration: Drinking water will keep your body hydrated, allowing it to combat allergy-triggering irritants effectively. If you’re prone to allergic reactions from tap water, filter it with a reusable bottle or purchase bottled water for peace of mind.

Do Pack Wisely: When packing, remember to include allergy-relief medications in your carry-on. That way, in case of lost luggage or flight delays, you’ll still have access to medications you need quickly. Don’t forget to pack an extra supply in case of emergencies.

Mess with the Best and Switch to Washable Linens: Where possible, pack washable bed linens such as pillowcases and sheets to minimize exposure to allergens on your journey. Remember to check the bedding provided when traveling - if the hotel allows pets, request for a room without pet-access in advance.

Brush Up on Local Habits: Paying attention to locals can reveal potential hazards or better habits for managing allergies. For example, in some countries, triggering allergies may be more common due to certain practices or environmental factors. Adapting your routine to suit local conditions is crucial.

Shower Power: Showering daily can help remove allergens, such as pollen, that stick to skin and hair. Using hypoallergenic soap and detergent ensures fewer allergens linger on your body.

Paws Off! If you’re traveling with a furry companion, remember to check for fleas, ticks, or shedding regularly. Introducing external allergens from pets into your environment can worsen allergic reactions.

Beware of Food Allergies: Food allergies are as prevalent as environmental allergies, and crossing the globe doesn’t mean abandoning your dietary restrictions. Meal choices affect your overall health and can exacerbate allergy symptoms too. Prepare a list of safe food items when you travel. If you’re unsure about local products, contact a healthcare provider or read food labels to ensure allergens are not present.

Join the Club: Technology exists to aid in your travel experience. Find smartphone apps that offer helpful info about local allergies, pollen counts, weather, and safe food choices. By knowing which activities or foods to avoid before you head out, you’ll better manage your allergies when traveling.

Traveling with allergies may seem like a daunting challenge, but with these tips, you’ll have everything under control. Your allergy symptoms will not have to interrupt or embarrass you on your travels. By preserving your usual hygiene routine, you can continue enjoying cultural and adventurous experiences without fear. Now, pack your bags, remember your medications, and embrace your next adventure!
