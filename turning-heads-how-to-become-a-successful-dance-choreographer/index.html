Turning Heads: How to Become a Successful Dance Choreographer


Dance choreography is an art form that captivates and inspires audiences across the globe. With the right mixture of creativity, technical skills, and commitment, an individual can become a successful dance choreographer. In this post, we will explore the essential steps to becoming a choreographer who’s turning heads.

Step 1: Develop a Deep Understanding of Dance Styles

No matter the genre or style, a choreographer should have knowledge of various dance forms to create unique and captivating performances. Explore and learn different techniques including ballet, hip-hop, contemporary, jazz, tap, and more. Understand the history, cultural significance, and basic principles of each genre. As you grow, you can delve deeper and develop a niche in one or more styles that truly resonate with you.

Step 2: Perfect the Technical Side of Dancing

A successful choreographer needs to have strong personal technique in at least one dance form. This not only allows you to communicate effectively with dancers but also lets you conceptualize your choreography from a practical standpoint. Dedicate time and effort towards, practicing, training, and mastering the fundamental techniques of the dance styles you are interested in.

Step 3: Embrace Creativity and Innovation

While technical prowess is crucial, the essence of choreography lies in creativity and originality. Experiment with different concepts, rhythms, movements, and emotions in your choreography. Recreate stories and evoke emotions through your work. As you grow, establish your unique style based on your interpretation of dance, personal preferences, and artistic flair. Don’t shy away from taking inspiration from other art forms, music, literature, and even everyday life.

Step 4: Collaborate with Other Talented Individuals

Choreography is a communal art form. Work with talented dancers, musicians, and other artists to develop and refine your craft. Collaborations create opportunities for growth and learning, letting you establish a sense of your creative identity. Working with other professionals will also help you make connections in the dance industry, opening doors to new opportunities.

Step 5: Immerse Yourself in the Dance Community

Get involved in the dance community by attending workshops, masterclasses, and dance conventions. These events offer invaluable networking opportunities, allowing you to collaborate with rising and established professionals, learn the latest dance trends and techniques, and participate in dance competitions.

Dance is a form of self-expression and communication, and showcasing your talent can open doors to opportunities you never knew existed. Submit your choreography for festivals, performances, and access talent scouts.

Step 6: Build an Online Presence

In today’s digital age, the internet is a crucial platform for showcasing your work and connecting with existing and potential clients. Create a portfolio website or personal blog, and showcase your choreography, work experience, and any relevant awards or professional achievements. Stay active on online platforms like Instagram and YouTube to promote your work, and network with other professionals and industry insiders.

Step 7: Keep Learning and Evolving

The dance industry is constantly evolving, and it’s essential to stay updated with the latest trends and techniques. Take workshops, masterclasses, and personal training sessions to broaden your skillset and challenge yourself. Keep a growth mindset, never being satisfied fully with your accomplishments. The more you learn, the more you’ll grow as a choreographer.

Step 8: Seek Constructive Criticism

To become a successful choreographer, learn to value the opinions of others and use them to improve your work. Gather feedback from fellow dance professionals, attend choreography workshops, participate in masterclasses, and seek mentorship from established choreographers. Remember, constructive criticism is free, invaluable advice that can lead you down the path of excellence.

Step 9: Establish Your Personal Brand

In the dance world, an identifiable and unique style can be the key to standing out from the crowd. Develop your personal brand by cultivating a distinct style, message, and aesthetic. Your brand should reflect your perspectives and artistic expressions. In a crowded industry, your unique voice will be the thing that draws potential clients to collaborate with you and make your choreography stand out from the rest.

Step 10: Start Charging for Your Work

While building your portfolio, it might be tempting to work pro bono to gain experience, but be mindful of your time and value. As you grow and build recognition for your work, it is only fair to charge clients for your services. Setting your price will not only help you demand respect for your work but will also help you better understand the value of your talent, time, and dedication.

Step 11: Network Like Crazy

In the highly competitive dance world, your network becomes your net worth. Attend dance conferences, sessions, workshops, performances, or events where you can meet other professionals. Socialize with them, exchange ideas, collaborations, and information. Keep in touch with these people and create a strong support system that will help sustain and grow your career.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: How long should it take before I can call myself a choreographer, and start earning money for my work?

A: The time it takes to become a choreographer varies greatly depending on your dedication, talent, and experience. Some may gain recognition faster, while others might take a more extended period to build their skillset and make a name for themselves. When you begin charging for your work depends on the quality of your choreography, your experience, and your network. Building a portfolio and networking is critical before officially establishing yourself as a choreographer.

Q2: Can I become a successful choreographer without having a formal education in dance?

A: It is entirely possible to become a successful choreographer without a formal education in dance. Many successful choreographers are self-taught, and their passion, drive, and creativity have propelled them to the top. However, the benefit of a formal dance education comes in the form of increased discipline, technique, and knowledge of various styles.

Q3: Is it necessary to work for free to gain experience and build a portfolio?

A: There’s nothing inherently wrong with working for experience, as it can provide valuable exposure and opportunities. However, be cautious about offering your services for free for extended periods. Remember that your time and effort have value, and it is perfectly acceptable to charge for your work. Developing a balance between offering your services for free and charging for your work can help build your portfolio while generating income and establishing your worth in the dance industry.

In Conclusion

Becoming a successful dance choreographer is not an easy path. It requires a blend of skill, dedication, and passion. Embrace the journey and continually grow as both a dancer and an artist. Today’s burgeoning dance choreographers are tomorrow’s legends. So, hone your craft, ride the highs, and conquer the lows, for every step will lead you closer to achieving your dreams. Good luck on your journey.
