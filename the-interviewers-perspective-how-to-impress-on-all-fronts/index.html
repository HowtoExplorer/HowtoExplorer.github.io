The Interviewer's Perspective: How to Impress on All Fronts


As an interviewer, one of the most fulfilling aspects of my job is identifying the candidate who stands out among the rest, both professionally and personally. Conversely, the most challenging part of my role is steering clear of the pitfalls that can sabotage the integrity of the hiring process. Throughout my career, I’ve encountered various candidates leaving a lasting impression, and I’ve come to identify specific qualities that set them apart from the rest. In this article, I’ll explore a few essential strategies to impress on all fronts as a job applicant.

  1. Research the Company It’s no secret that we live in a world driven by technology. From resumes submitted to job applications, it’s easy for applicants to quickly apply to multiple openings with minimal effort spent on understanding the employer. Taking the time to research the organization and its mission statement is of utmost importance. Demonstrating this initiative during the interview will highlight your dedication, passion, and good character, impressing the interviewer right off the bat.

  2. Preparation is Key Preparing for an interview can be viewed as a skill in itself. Ensuring that you have gathered all relevant information on the company, position, and associated required skill sets is vital. As an interviewer, I am impressed by candidates who come equipped with thoughtful questions about the company culture, goals, and growth opportunities. This preparation and curiosity show that you’re not afraid to invest time and effort into learning and understanding the organization and its values, making for a potentially successful partnership.

  3. Manage Your Body Language Though all sound and fury, the infamous “first impression is the last impression” rings true in most cases. Body language can either facilitate or hinder an interviewer’s perception of a candidate, often subconsciously. Standing tall, making direct eye contact, and respecting personal space can significantly bolster your chances of making a positive first impression. Responding confidently to questions, even when under pressure, gives the impression that you have the ability to handle tough situations with ease.

  4. Communicate Effectively Effective communication is a fundamental skill in both personal and professional aspects of our lives. Interviewers look for candidates who can articulate their experiences and achievements succinctly and confidently. This requires practicing your answers, critically reflecting on challenges you’ve faced and how you overcame them, ensuring you put your achievements front and center. Engaging in active listening and being mindful of verbal and non-verbal cues will showcase impeccable communication skills.

  5. End the Interview on a High Note Upon concluding the interview, it’s essential to remember a theatrical technique called the “exit line.” Leaving a lasting impression involves thanking the interviewer for their time and beklming confident in your abilities. Furthermore, this is an opportune moment to reiterate your interest in the company and position, and mention any additional skills or experiences that you may not have had the chance to elaborate on during the interview.

Remember, while making a lasting impression can be challenging, following these simple yet impactful steps will have you well on your way to success. As an interviewer, I look forward to meeting candidates who have prepared thoughtfully and have an unwavering commitment to excellence.
