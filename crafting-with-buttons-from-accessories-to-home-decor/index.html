Crafting with Buttons: From Accessories to Home Decor


Buttons are not just small, functional objects that hold our clothes together. They are versatile and can be used in a myriad of craft projects. From accessories to home decor, buttons can add a touch of creativity and uniqueness to your creations. In this blog post, we will explore different ways to incorporate buttons into your crafting endeavors.

Button Jewelry

One of the simplest and most common uses of buttons in crafting is in jewelry-making. Buttons can be transformed into beautiful necklaces, bracelets, and earrings with just a few additional materials. To create a button necklace, simply thread a string or a chain through the buttonholes, and secure it with a clasp. You can also experiment with different sizes and colors of buttons to create a unique and eye-catching piece.

Button Art

Buttons can also be used to create stunning works of art. Whether you choose to create a button mosaic or use buttons as embellishments, the possibilities are endless. To create a button mosaic, start with a blank canvas or a piece of wood. Arrange buttons of different sizes, colors, and shapes to create a design of your choice. Secure the buttons in place with glue, and let your imagination run wild. The end result will be a beautiful and textured piece of art that will add a pop of color to any room.

Button Home Decor

Buttons can also be used to spruce up your home decor. From button-covered vases to button-embellished pillows, there are countless ways to incorporate buttons into your interior design. To create a button-covered vase, simply glue buttons of various sizes onto a plain glass vase. The result will be a unique and eye-catching centerpiece that will add a touch of whimsy to your home.

Button Crafts for Kids

Crafting with buttons is not limited to adults. It can also be a fun and engaging activity for kids. Button crafts can help develop fine motor skills and spark creativity in children. One simple idea is to create button flowers. Cut out flower shapes from colored paper and let your child glue buttons onto the petals. This activity allows children to explore different colors and shapes while creating their own unique artwork.

Button Upcycling

Buttons can also be used in upcycling projects. Instead of throwing away old clothes or accessories with missing buttons, repurpose them by adding new buttons. This simple act can breathe new life into old items and give them a fresh and updated look. You can also use buttons to embellish old picture frames, giving them a unique and personalized touch.

In conclusion, buttons are not just functional objects, they are also a versatile crafting material. From jewelry-making to home decor, buttons can add a touch of creativity and uniqueness to your projects. So the next time you find yourself with a jar of buttons, don’t just see them as mere fasteners, but as a world of possibilities waiting to be explored. Get creative, experiment, and let your imagination run wild. Happy crafting!
