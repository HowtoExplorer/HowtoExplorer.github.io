The Best Ways to Save for a Wedding


Planning a wedding is one of the most exciting times in a couple’s life. However, it can also be one of the most expensive. With so many details to consider, from the venue and catering to the dress and flowers, it’s easy to see how costs can quickly add up. That’s why saving for a wedding is crucial—both to ensure you can afford the day of your dreams and to reduce stress leading up to the big event.

In this guide, we’ll explore the best strategies to save for your wedding. Whether you have a year or just a few months to plan, these tips will help you manage your budget effectively and make your wedding savings goal more achievable.

1. Set a Realistic Budget

The first step in saving for a wedding is to determine how much money you’ll need. Establishing a clear, realistic wedding budget will give you a solid foundation to work from. Start by listing out all the essential expenses you’ll have, such as:

  • Venue
  • Catering
  • Photography and videography
  • Flowers
  • Wedding attire
  • Entertainment
  • Invitations

Once you have a general idea of what each component will cost, it’s time to create a total budget. Make sure to leave room for unexpected expenses, as it’s common for costs to rise unexpectedly during the planning process.

While it’s tempting to opt for extravagant touches, remember that weddings are about celebrating your love. Focus on what’s most important to you and your partner, and don’t feel pressured to include everything you see in Pinterest boards or magazines.

2. Open a Dedicated Wedding Savings Account

Having a separate account dedicated solely to your wedding savings is one of the best ways to stay organized and on track with your financial goals. By opening a high-yield savings account, you can set aside money regularly and earn interest along the way.

Each month, make it a habit to deposit a portion of your income into this account. The more frequently you save, the easier it will be to reach your wedding savings target. Plus, by having a separate account, you’re less likely to dip into the funds for other expenses.

If you want to make saving even easier, consider setting up automatic transfers so that the money is taken out of your main account each time you get paid. This method removes the temptation to skip saving for the month.

3. Create a Timeline for Your Wedding Savings

Saving for a wedding can take anywhere from several months to a couple of years, depending on your wedding date. Creating a timeline for your wedding savings allows you to break down your overall goal into smaller, more manageable monthly targets.

For example, if you’re planning to get married in one year and your wedding budget is $20,000, you’ll need to save roughly $1,667 per month. If your wedding is two years away, you’ll need to save around $834 per month. Knowing how much you need to save each month will keep you focused and motivated.

Make sure to adjust your savings goals if necessary. If you’re able to save more in a particular month, take advantage of the extra funds. Alternatively, if you experience financial setbacks, adjust your timeline so that you’re not stressing over unrealistic savings targets.

4. Cut Back on Non-Essential Expenses

One of the most effective ways to boost your wedding savings is to cut back on unnecessary expenses. Take a close look at your spending habits and identify areas where you can save money without affecting your lifestyle too much. Some common expenses to cut back on include:

  • Dining out: Reduce how often you go out for meals and try cooking more at home.
  • Subscriptions: Cancel unused or unnecessary subscriptions, such as streaming services or magazine subscriptions.
  • Shopping: Limit impulse purchases and unnecessary clothing or gadgets.
  • Entertainment: Choose more budget-friendly activities, like hiking, attending free events, or hosting game nights.

Instead of focusing on what you’re giving up, think of it as temporarily cutting back to achieve a bigger goal. Every dollar you save can be one step closer to making your dream wedding a reality.

5. Look for Extra Sources of Income

While cutting back on your current expenses is a great way to save, another effective method is to find additional sources of income. Consider taking on a part-time job, freelancing, or selling items you no longer need. Some ways to generate extra income include:

  • Freelance work: If you have marketable skills, consider offering freelance services such as graphic design, writing, photography, or social media management.
  • Sell unused items: Go through your closets and sell unwanted items on platforms like eBay, Facebook Marketplace, or Craigslist.
  • Gig economy jobs: Explore gig-based opportunities like driving for Uber, delivering food with DoorDash, or pet-sitting on Rover.
  • Tutoring or teaching: Offer tutoring services in subjects you’re knowledgeable about, or teach a class on a hobby or skill you excel at.

The extra income can be directly deposited into your wedding savings account, accelerating your progress toward your financial goal.

6. Take Advantage of Wedding-Specific Savings Programs

Some banks, financial institutions, or wedding planners offer wedding-specific savings programs designed to help couples save. These programs may offer rewards or bonuses for reaching savings milestones, or they may come with helpful budgeting tools.

Additionally, some credit cards offer rewards points that can be redeemed for travel, gifts, or even cash back, which can be useful for covering wedding-related costs. If you already have a credit card with a rewards program, make sure to use it strategically for your wedding expenses.

Another option is to look into wedding registries that allow guests to contribute money toward the cost of your wedding instead of buying traditional gifts. Websites like Honeyfund or Zola offer wedding registry services that can help you collect funds directly for your wedding expenses.

7. Cut Wedding Costs with DIY Projects

DIY projects can be an excellent way to save money on your wedding without sacrificing style. There are plenty of wedding elements that you can personalize and create yourself, such as:

  • Invitations: Design and print your own invitations or use an online platform to create affordable, beautiful invitations.
  • Decorations: From centerpieces to signage, there are many simple and cost-effective DIY decorations that will still make your venue look stunning.
  • Favors: Create personalized wedding favors for your guests, like homemade candles, bath salts, or cookies.
  • Wedding programs: Design and print your wedding programs at home or through an online service.

You don’t need to be a craft expert to pull off a successful DIY wedding project. Just make sure you start early so you have plenty of time to perfect each item and avoid feeling rushed.

8. Consider a Smaller Guest List

One of the easiest ways to cut down on wedding expenses is to reduce the size of your guest list. While it can be tempting to invite everyone you know, keeping your guest list smaller can significantly lower your costs.

For example, a smaller wedding will likely cost less in terms of catering, venue size, and decorations. It can also make for a more intimate celebration, which many couples find to be more meaningful. Focus on inviting only your closest friends and family, and consider alternatives like live streaming your wedding for those who can’t attend in person.

9. Shop Smart for Wedding Essentials

When it comes to buying wedding essentials, such as the dress, shoes, and accessories, you don’t have to pay full price. There are many ways to find great deals without sacrificing quality:

  • Dress sales: Look for sample sales or consider purchasing a pre-owned wedding dress. Many bridal shops sell off their sample dresses at a fraction of the cost.
  • Second-hand items: Consider buying or renting wedding décor, suits, or other essentials from thrift stores or websites like Poshmark and Rent the Runway.
  • Negotiate with vendors: Don’t be afraid to ask for discounts or inquire about any available promotions. Some vendors may offer a lower price, especially if you’re booking multiple services or paying upfront.
  • Off-season weddings: Consider scheduling your wedding during the off-season. Weddings held during less popular months, such as January or February, can often be more affordable than those held in the summer.

10. Stay Focused and Celebrate the Journey

Saving for a wedding is a big financial undertaking, but remember to stay focused on your end goal and celebrate each milestone along the way. Whether you’ve saved enough to book your dream venue or finally paid off a key component of your wedding, each step is worth celebrating.

Wedding planning is a journey, and the financial aspect is just one part of it. With careful planning, commitment, and these helpful tips, you can make your wedding day everything you’ve dreamed of without breaking the bank.

Remember, the most important part of your wedding day is the celebration of your love and the commitment you’re making to one another. Focus on the joy of the moment and the life you’ll build together.
