How to Write a Resume for a Customer Service Position


When it comes to securing a customer service position, your resume is often the first impression you’ll make on a potential employer. It’s your chance to showcase your skills, experience, and enthusiasm for the role in a way that captures the hiring manager’s attention. A well-crafted resume can be the difference between landing an interview or being passed over. So, how do you create a compelling resume for a customer service position? In this guide, we’ll walk you through every step of writing a resume that highlights your strengths and sets you up for success in the competitive job market.

1. Understand the Role

Before you begin writing your resume, it’s essential to understand what customer service roles typically involve. Customer service professionals act as the face of a company, providing assistance and support to customers through various channels like phone calls, emails, chat, and in-person interactions. Strong communication, problem-solving, patience, and empathy are crucial for success in customer service.

Customer service positions span across many industries, including retail, finance, technology, hospitality, and more. Each sector may require specific technical knowledge, but the core skills remain consistent. When writing your resume, ensure it reflects both the hard and soft skills relevant to the job you’re applying for.

2. Choose the Right Resume Format

The first step in creating your resume is selecting the right format. There are three common resume formats to consider: chronological, functional, and combination.

Chronological Resume

The chronological resume format is the most common and is preferred by most employers. It lists your work experience in reverse chronological order, starting with your most recent job and working backward. This format works well if you have a solid work history and want to emphasize your job progression.

Functional Resume

A functional resume focuses more on your skills and abilities rather than your work history. This format can be beneficial if you’re new to the customer service field or if you’re transitioning from another industry. It allows you to highlight your transferable skills, such as communication, problem-solving, and multitasking.

Combination Resume

A combination resume blends elements of both the chronological and functional formats. It allows you to showcase your skills while also demonstrating your career progression. This format is ideal if you have a combination of relevant experience and strong skills but want to highlight both aspects.

Choose the format that best fits your career trajectory and the specific customer service role you’re applying for.

3. Start with a Strong Resume Header

Your resume header is the first thing the hiring manager will see, so make sure it stands out. Include the following details:

  • Full Name: Use your full legal name as it appears on official documents.
  • Phone Number: Provide a reliable phone number where you can be reached.
  • Email Address: Use a professional email address, ideally one that includes your name (e.g., johndoe@email.com).
  • LinkedIn Profile (optional): If you have a well-established LinkedIn profile, include the link. This can serve as an extension of your resume, showcasing recommendations and additional details.
  • Location: You don’t need to provide your full address, but listing your city and state (or country if you’re applying internationally) gives the employer an idea of your proximity.

Here’s an example of a resume header:

John Doe  
Phone: (123) 456-7890  
Email: johndoe@email.com  
LinkedIn: linkedin.com/in/johndoe  
Location: New York, NY

4. Write a Compelling Resume Summary

The resume summary is a brief section at the top of your resume that highlights your key qualifications and accomplishments. It’s your opportunity to grab the employer’s attention right away. A strong summary should be 2-3 sentences long and should reflect your skills, experience, and passion for customer service.

Here’s how you can structure a customer service resume summary:

  • Start with your job title or the role you’re targeting: For example, “Experienced Customer Service Representative.”
  • Highlight your years of experience: Employers value experience, so mention how many years you’ve spent in the customer service field.
  • Showcase your key strengths: Focus on the skills that are most relevant to the job, such as problem-solving, communication, or conflict resolution.
  • Mention any notable achievements: If you’ve received awards or consistently exceeded performance goals, include this information.

Example:

Experienced Customer Service Representative with over 5 years of experience in the retail and telecommunications industries. Skilled in handling customer inquiries, resolving issues, and ensuring customer satisfaction. Proven ability to meet and exceed service goals in high-pressure environments. Passionate about helping customers and enhancing their experience with the company.

5. Showcase Your Customer Service Skills

Customer service roles require a specific set of skills. These can be broken down into two categories: hard skills and soft skills.

Hard Skills

Hard skills are the technical abilities required for the job. Some examples of hard skills for customer service include:

  • CRM Software: Knowledge of customer relationship management (CRM) tools like Salesforce, Zendesk, or Freshdesk.
  • Technical Support: Providing assistance for technical issues or product-related inquiries.
  • Data Entry: Accurately inputting customer information and service details into databases.
  • Product Knowledge: Understanding the company’s products or services to assist customers effectively.

Soft Skills

Soft skills are personal attributes that help you interact effectively with others. These are especially important in customer service, where you are constantly engaging with people. Some essential soft skills for customer service professionals include:

  • Communication: The ability to explain solutions clearly and listen attentively to customers’ needs.
  • Patience: Handling challenging customers or situations with composure.
  • Problem-Solving: Quickly identifying the root cause of an issue and finding solutions.
  • Empathy: Understanding the customer’s perspective and offering appropriate support.
  • Adaptability: Staying calm and flexible in fast-paced or changing environments.

Be sure to include both hard and soft skills in your resume to give a well-rounded view of your qualifications.

6. Highlight Relevant Experience

The experience section of your resume should demonstrate how you’ve applied your skills in real-world situations. If you have previous customer service experience, list your most recent jobs and describe your responsibilities and achievements in each role.

Use bullet points to make this section easy to read. Start each bullet point with a strong action verb to show how you contributed to the company. Focus on specific achievements or responsibilities that align with the customer service role you’re applying for.

For example:

Customer Service Representative  
XYZ Company | New York, NY | June 2018 – Present  
- Provide support to customers via phone, email, and chat, ensuring timely and accurate responses.  
- Resolve customer complaints by offering solutions and following up to ensure satisfaction.  
- Maintain an average customer satisfaction rating of 95%.  
- Train new team members on customer service protocols and CRM systems.

If you’re new to customer service, focus on transferable skills from previous jobs. For instance, if you’ve worked in retail, hospitality, or even volunteer work, highlight the customer-facing aspects of those roles.

7. Education and Certifications

While customer service positions don’t always require a specific degree, certain educational qualifications can make you stand out. Include your highest level of education and any relevant certifications.

Education

For example:

Bachelor of Arts in Communication  
University of New York | Graduated May 2017

Certifications

While certifications aren’t always required, they can provide a competitive edge. Some relevant customer service certifications include:

  • Customer Service Certification: A general certification that demonstrates your understanding of customer service principles.
  • Certified Customer Service Professional (CCSP): Offered by various organizations, this certification shows that you have a deep knowledge of customer service best practices.
  • Technical Support Certification: If the job requires technical knowledge, having a certification in technical support can be an advantage.

8. Tailor Your Resume for Each Job

While it may be tempting to use the same resume for every application, it’s crucial to tailor your resume for each job you apply to. Carefully review the job description and match your skills and experience to the specific requirements listed by the employer.

For example, if the job emphasizes handling difficult customer interactions, highlight your experience in conflict resolution. If the job requires familiarity with a specific CRM tool, mention your proficiency with that software.

By customizing your resume, you show the hiring manager that you are genuinely interested in the position and have the skills required to succeed in the role.

9. Proofread and Format Your Resume

A well-written resume is important, but it’s equally important to ensure that it’s error-free and properly formatted. Check for spelling and grammar mistakes, as even small errors can make a negative impression. Use clear, concise language and avoid unnecessary jargon.

Formatting your resume properly makes it easier for hiring managers to read and understand. Keep the following in mind:

  • Use a clean, easy-to-read font (e.g., Arial or Calibri) in a size between 10-12 points.
  • Include enough white space to avoid a cluttered look.
  • Use bullet points for easy readability.
  • Keep the resume to one page (two pages if you have extensive experience).

10. Final Thoughts

Writing a resume for a customer service position requires careful attention to detail. By understanding the role, choosing the right resume format, highlighting your skills and experience, and tailoring your resume to each job, you’ll increase your chances of landing the interview. Remember that your resume is a reflection of your professionalism, so invest time and effort into making it the best representation of your abilities.

With a well-crafted resume, you’ll be well on your way to securing the customer service position of your dreams!
