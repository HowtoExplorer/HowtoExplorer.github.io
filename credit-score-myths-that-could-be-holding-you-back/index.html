Credit Score Myths That Could Be Holding You Back


Are you struggling to understand why your credit score isn’t as high as you’d like it to be? Have you fallen prey to common credit score myths that are actually holding you back from achieving your financial goals? In this blog post, we will debunk some of the most prevalent credit score myths and provide you with the truth you need to take control of your credit.

Myth #1: Checking Your Credit Score Lowers It

One of the most widespread credit score myths is the belief that checking your credit score will negatively impact it. This couldn’t be further from the truth. When you check your own credit score, it is considered a “soft inquiry,” which has no effect on your score whatsoever. In fact, regularly monitoring your credit can help you detect and address any errors or fraudulent activities that may be dragging your score down.

Myth #2: Closing Old Credit Card Accounts Improves Your Score

Many people mistakenly believe that closing old credit card accounts will boost their credit score. However, this is a myth that can actually harm your credit. Closing old accounts reduces your overall credit history, which is an important factor in determining your creditworthiness. Additionally, closing accounts can increase your credit utilization ratio, which measures how much of your available credit you are using. It is recommended to keep old accounts open, as long as they are in good standing, to maintain a positive credit history.

Myth #3: Paying Off Collections Removes Them from Your Credit Report

If you have collections accounts on your credit report, you may have heard that paying them off will remove them and improve your score. Unfortunately, that’s not how it works. Paying off collections does not automatically remove them from your credit report. The collection will still be listed, but it may show as “paid” or “settled.” While paying off collections is a responsible step to take, it won’t erase the negative impact they have on your credit. It’s important to address collections, but be aware that they can stay on your report for up to seven years.

Myth #4: Closing Credit Cards with a Balance Will Help Your Score

Another credit score myth is the idea that closing credit cards with a balance will benefit your score. However, this myth can actually backfire and harm your credit. Closing a credit card with a balance means you will lose the available credit limit associated with that card. As a result, your credit utilization ratio will increase, potentially lowering your score. It’s best to pay off the balance while keeping the card open to maintain a healthy credit utilization ratio.

Myth #5: Only Debt Affects Your Credit Score

While it’s true that debt plays a significant role in your credit score, it’s not the only factor. Other factors, such as payment history, credit utilization, credit mix, and length of credit history, also influence your score. It’s important to have a well-rounded understanding of credit scoring to effectively manage your score. By paying bills on time, keeping credit card balances low, and maintaining a diverse credit mix, you can enhance your creditworthiness and improve your credit score.

Conclusion

Don’t let credit score myths hold you back from achieving your financial goals. By debunking these misconceptions and understanding the true factors that affect your credit score, you can take control of your financial future. Remember, it’s essential to regularly monitor your credit, keep old accounts open, address collections responsibly, and maintain a healthy credit utilization ratio. With these steps, you’ll be on your way to a stronger credit score and a brighter financial outlook.
