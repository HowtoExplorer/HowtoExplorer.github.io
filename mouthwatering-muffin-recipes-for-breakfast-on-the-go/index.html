Mouthwatering Muffin Recipes for Breakfast on the Go

3 min · 586 words

Are you tired of skipping breakfast because you don’t have time to prepare a meal in the morning? Look no further! In this blog post, we will share with you some mouthwatering muffin recipes that are perfect for breakfast on the go. These recipes are not only delicious but also packed with nutrients to fuel your day. The best part? You can make them ahead of time and grab one as you head out the door. Let’s dive in!

1. Banana Nut Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, mash the bananas until smooth.
  3. Add the honey, coconut oil, eggs, and vanilla extract to the mashed bananas. Mix well.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  6. Fold in the chopped walnuts.
  7. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  8. Bake for 18-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  9. Allow the muffins to cool for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

2. Blueberry Oat Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or plant-based)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine the rolled oats and milk. Let them soak for 10 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the maple syrup, melted coconut oil, egg, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the wet ingredients to the oat mixture and stir well.
  5. In another bowl, combine the whole wheat flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  7. Gently fold in the blueberries.
  8. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  9. Bake for 20-22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  10. Allow the muffins to cool for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

3. Spinach and Feta Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup baby spinach, chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped sun-dried tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped black olives
  • 1 cup milk (dairy or plant-based)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 eggs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the whole wheat flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, dried oregano, and black pepper.
  3. Add the chopped baby spinach, crumbled feta cheese, chopped sun-dried tomatoes, and chopped black olives to the dry ingredients. Mix well.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, olive oil, and eggs.
  5. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 20-22 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

These mouthwatering muffin recipes are perfect for those busy mornings when you need a quick and nutritious breakfast. They can be made ahead of time and stored in an airtight container for up to a week. Simply grab one on your way out the door and enjoy a delicious breakfast on the go. Give these recipes a try and start your day off right with a satisfying and flavorful muffin!

Remember, the possibilities are endless when it comes to muffin recipes. Feel free to get creative and experiment with different flavors and ingredients. Happy baking!
