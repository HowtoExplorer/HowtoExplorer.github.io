The Best Fantasy Series to Get Lost In


Fantasy literature has always captivated readers with its rich world-building, intricate plots, and deeply complex characters. There’s something uniquely thrilling about diving into another world where magic is real, mythical creatures roam the land, and the fate of kingdoms often hangs in the balance. Whether you’re looking to escape into epic adventures, explore dark and gritty realms, or delve into stories that push the boundaries of imagination, fantasy series offer an incredible variety of experiences.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best fantasy series that are perfect for getting lost in. From established classics to newer releases, these series offer a range of settings, characters, and plots that will captivate any fantasy enthusiast. So, grab your favorite beverage, get comfy, and prepare to lose yourself in these amazing worlds.

1. A Song of Ice and Fire by George R. R. Martin

Perhaps one of the most iconic fantasy series of all time, A Song of Ice and Fire is the inspiration for the wildly popular TV show Game of Thrones. This series, which began with A Game of Thrones in 1996, introduces readers to the complex and brutal world of Westeros. The story is a sweeping epic that involves multiple storylines, rich character development, political intrigue, and wars for power. The struggle for the Iron Throne between rival families such as the Starks, Lannisters, and Targaryens is at the heart of the series, but the stakes are much higher than mere political power. Martin’s series also includes the mysterious White Walkers in the north and the return of dragons, which bring an element of the supernatural to an otherwise political tale.

Martin’s world is known for its morally complex characters. No one is purely good or evil, and the choices that characters make often have consequences that ripple throughout the world. The sheer depth and complexity of this series make it a must-read for anyone looking to get lost in a fantasy universe.

Why You’ll Love It

  • Epic world-building: The detailed setting of Westeros is vast, full of culture, history, and mythology.
  • Complex characters: The multi-faceted personalities keep you engaged as their fates are unpredictable.
  • Political intrigue and moral dilemmas: There are no clear-cut heroes and villains, making every decision feel high-stakes.

2. The Stormlight Archive by Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson is one of the most talented and prolific fantasy writers of modern times, and The Stormlight Archive is considered his magnum opus. Starting with The Way of Kings, this series takes place on the storm-ravaged world of Roshar, where highstorms regularly devastate the land, and the magical powers of the Knights Radiant shape the course of history. The series features multiple characters and perspectives, from the troubled soldier Kaladin Stormblessed to the scholarly but ambitious Shallan Davar, as they navigate a world on the brink of war.

What makes Sanderson’s writing stand out is his remarkable talent for creating intricate magic systems. In the Stormlight Archive, magic is not simply an abstract concept, but a well-defined system with rules and limitations. This allows for high-stakes battles, clever strategic uses of magic, and deeply emotional moments that hit even harder because of the magic’s limitations. With deep character development, complex political intrigue, and beautifully constructed settings, The Stormlight Archive is an immersive experience that will keep readers coming back for more.

Why You’ll Love It

  • Masterful world-building and magic systems: Sanderson’s complex and well-thought-out world will pull you in.
  • Deep character arcs: From personal struggles to moral dilemmas, the characters’ growth is truly engaging.
  • The sense of scale: The world feels vast and full of history, and the looming conflict feels truly epic.

3. The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan

When it comes to long-running fantasy series, The Wheel of Time is a titan. Spanning fourteen books, this series follows the journey of Rand al’Thor, a young man who is revealed to be the Dragon Reborn—someone destined to face the Dark One in an epic final battle that will shape the fate of the world. Along the way, Rand and his companions travel through a richly detailed world filled with magic, political intrigue, and ancient prophecies.

The series stands out for its immersive world-building, with a history that stretches back thousands of years. The magic system, known as the One Power, is also deeply developed, with both male and female magic users possessing distinct abilities. Jordan’s writing is known for its slow burn, where character development is gradual, and the story unfolds with intricate plots that weave together across the series.

After Jordan’s passing, Brandon Sanderson was chosen to finish the series, bringing it to a satisfying conclusion. This is a series that requires patience, but the payoff is well worth it for those who enjoy a deeply immersive world and an epic narrative.

Why You’ll Love It

  • Extensive world-building: The world is vast and ancient, with layers of history that make every discovery feel rewarding.
  • Complex characters: The central characters grow and evolve over the course of the series, with unique perspectives.
  • Epic scale: This is a truly sprawling series with a wide cast of characters and numerous plots that come together in unexpected ways.

4. Mistborn by Brandon Sanderson

Another entry by Brandon Sanderson, Mistborn is a series that blends high fantasy with heist fiction. The first book, Mistborn: The Final Empire, introduces readers to a world where a dark lord, known as the Lord Ruler, has ruled with an iron fist for a thousand years. The central character, Vin, is a street urchin who discovers that she has the rare and powerful ability to manipulate metals—an ability that makes her a key figure in a rebellion aimed at overthrowing the Lord Ruler.

The magic system in Mistborn is unique in that it revolves around “Allomancy,” where users can ingest and “burn” different metals to gain specific abilities, such as increased strength, heightened senses, or the power to manipulate metal objects. The action is fast-paced and full of thrilling heists, while the world-building is rich and immersive. Throughout the series, readers will find themselves constantly surprised by plot twists, making it a thrilling ride from start to finish.

Why You’ll Love It

  • Unique magic system: The concept of Allomancy adds a fresh and exciting element to the fantasy genre.
  • Thrilling heists: The plot revolves around heist-like missions that keep the tension high and the stakes high.
  • Strong character development: Vin’s journey from an uncertain street urchin to a confident leader is one of the most engaging character arcs in fantasy.

5. The Broken Earth Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin

For readers who enjoy more thought-provoking fantasy that also tackles complex themes, The Broken Earth Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin is a must-read. The trilogy begins with The Fifth Season, set in a world where seismic activity regularly devastates the land. In this world, a group of people known as “orogenes” have the ability to control and manipulate seismic activity, but they are also feared and oppressed by society.

Jemisin’s storytelling is unique in its use of narrative structure. The trilogy weaves together multiple points of view, often challenging traditional conventions of fantasy narrative. Jemisin also tackles themes of survival, oppression, power dynamics, and family, all while building a world that feels both familiar and entirely alien. The series is rich in metaphor and meaning, making it an ideal choice for readers who want their fantasy to be more than just a fantastical adventure.

Why You’ll Love It

  • Powerful themes: The series touches on issues of oppression, survival, and the abuse of power.
  • Unique narrative style: Jemisin uses multiple perspectives and structures to tell her story in unconventional ways.
  • Complex world-building: The world of The Broken Earth is intricate and original, with an inventive magic system.

6. The Inheritance Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin

Another powerful series by N.K. Jemisin, The Inheritance Trilogy explores themes of power, politics, and gods. The first book, The Hundred Thousand Kingdoms, introduces a world where gods are enslaved and used as pawns in the power struggles between mortals. The protagonist, Yeine Darr, finds herself thrust into the complex and deadly politics of the ruling family, where she must navigate treacherous alliances and uncover dark secrets.

Jemisin’s writing is rich and complex, and her world-building is both imaginative and thought-provoking. The relationship between gods and mortals, as well as the portrayal of political intrigue, adds layers of depth to the story, making it a compelling read for fans of fantasy with philosophical undercurrents.

Why You’ll Love It

  • Intriguing exploration of gods and power: The relationship between mortals and gods is fascinating and central to the story.
  • Complex political drama: The series delves into the machinations of powerful families and the consequences of their actions.
  • Thought-provoking themes: Jemisin’s work challenges ideas of family, loyalty, and the abuse of power.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a fan of epic sagas, character-driven stories, or dark and gritty adventures, these fantasy series offer something for everyone. Each one is filled with intricate world-building, complex characters, and thought-provoking themes, ensuring that readers will be transported into their fantastical realms for hours on end. So, if you’re looking to escape reality for a while, pick up one of these fantasy books and get ready to lose yourself in a world of magic, adventure, and intrigue.
