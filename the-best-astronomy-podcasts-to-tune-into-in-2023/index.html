The Best Astronomy Podcasts to Tune Into in 2023


Astronomy has captivated humankind for centuries. From the earliest astronomers gazing at the night sky to modern scientists exploring distant galaxies with cutting-edge technology, our fascination with the cosmos is unwavering. One of the most exciting ways to dive into the world of space science is through podcasts. Whether you’re an amateur stargazer, a student, or a seasoned astrophysicist, there’s a podcast for everyone. In 2023, the astronomy podcast scene is thriving, offering a wealth of information and entertainment.

This guide explores the best astronomy podcasts of 2023, covering various topics, from the latest discoveries and space missions to in-depth explorations of the universe’s mysteries.

1. StartUp: Astronomy Edition

While StartUp is primarily a podcast focused on the stories of entrepreneurs, StartUp: Astronomy Edition brings a fascinating twist by diving into the stories of people and companies transforming the space industry. This podcast is ideal for those who are curious about the intersection of business, technology, and space exploration. Hosted by experienced space reporters and entrepreneurs, each episode reveals what goes on behind the scenes of the companies shaping our future in space.

Listeners are treated to inspiring stories from aerospace startups, learning how small teams are developing innovative technologies to unlock the next frontiers of space travel. In addition, StartUp: Astronomy Edition often features interviews with astronauts, engineers, and even executives at companies like SpaceX and Blue Origin, providing insider insights into the challenges and breakthroughs of modern space exploration.

Why Listen?

  • In-depth look at the business side of space exploration.
  • Interviews with innovators shaping the future of space travel.
  • Inspiring stories about entrepreneurship in the space sector.

2. Planetary Radio: Space Exploration and Astronomy News

Produced by The Planetary Society, Planetary Radio is a favorite among those who want to stay up-to-date on the latest happenings in the world of space science and exploration. Hosted by Mat Kaplan, this podcast brings listeners closer to the experts and visionaries who are charting humanity’s course into the cosmos. The podcast features discussions about planetary exploration, telescope discoveries, space missions, and even planetary defense.

Each episode typically includes interviews with leading scientists, engineers, and astronauts, providing behind-the-scenes stories about missions like the Mars rovers, the James Webb Space Telescope, and the upcoming lunar missions under NASA’s Artemis program.

Why Listen?

  • Stay informed about the latest space missions and developments.
  • Engage with fascinating interviews featuring prominent scientists.
  • Discover the wonders of planetary science and space exploration.

3. The Infinite Monkey Cage

Hosted by physicist Brian Cox and comedian Robin Ince, The Infinite Monkey Cage is a delightful blend of science and humor. While the podcast covers a broad range of scientific topics, astronomy and cosmology are frequently at the forefront of the discussion. The show features lively debates, interviews with leading scientists, and fascinating discussions about the universe.

The hosts tackle big questions about the cosmos, such as the nature of black holes, the possibility of life on other planets, and the future of space exploration. What sets this podcast apart is its ability to make complex topics accessible and entertaining, often incorporating humor and quirky anecdotes.

Why Listen?

  • Engaging discussions that make astronomy accessible to all.
  • Humorous take on scientific topics, making learning fun.
  • Frequent involvement of guest experts in space and physics.

4. Orbital Path

If you’re looking for a podcast that takes a deep dive into the science of space, Orbital Path is an excellent choice. Hosted by Dr. Michelle Thaller, an astrophysicist and science communicator, the podcast explores the latest breakthroughs in astrophysics, space exploration, and planetary science. The show features interviews with top scientists, offering listeners the opportunity to learn about topics such as dark matter, exoplanets, the search for extraterrestrial life, and the formation of stars.

Dr. Thaller’s passion for space shines through in every episode, and her ability to explain complex topics in an approachable way makes Orbital Path one of the most educational astronomy podcasts available. Whether you’re a space enthusiast or a seasoned scientist, Orbital Path has something for everyone.

Why Listen?

  • Expert insights into cutting-edge space research.
  • In-depth discussions on a wide variety of astronomy topics.
  • Hosted by a knowledgeable and engaging astrophysicist.

5. Astro 101

For those who are just beginning their journey into the cosmos, Astro 101 offers an excellent starting point. This podcast is designed to introduce listeners to the basics of astronomy, from the origins of the universe to the workings of galaxies, stars, and planets. Hosted by astronomer Dr. Jeffrey Hester, Astro 101 covers fundamental concepts in space science in a straightforward and easy-to-understand way.

Each episode explores essential topics like the solar system, gravity, light, and the life cycle of stars, giving listeners a solid foundation in the key principles of astronomy. Astro 101 is perfect for high school students, beginners, or anyone wanting to brush up on the basics before diving deeper into more advanced space topics.

Why Listen?

  • Perfect for beginners or those interested in learning the basics of astronomy.
  • Simple, easy-to-understand explanations of complex concepts.
  • A great resource for educators and students alike.

6. SpaceTime with Stuart Gary

For those who enjoy staying on the cutting edge of space news, SpaceTime with Stuart Gary is a must-listen. This podcast offers a comprehensive weekly roundup of the latest space science news, including new discoveries, technological advancements, and upcoming missions. Hosted by journalist and space enthusiast Stuart Gary, the show covers everything from breaking space-related news to in-depth analysis of important scientific developments.

What makes SpaceTime stand out is its global perspective, covering not just American and European space agencies, but also highlighting news from the Asia-Pacific region and other space-faring nations. Whether you’re interested in astronomy, space policy, or the future of human space exploration, SpaceTime keeps you well-informed about the vast, ever-evolving world of space.

Why Listen?

  • Comprehensive coverage of space news and discoveries.
  • Expert commentary on space exploration and science.
  • Global perspective on space-related events and missions.

7. The Astronomy Cast

If you’re looking for a podcast that delves deeply into astronomical concepts, The Astronomy Cast is an excellent resource. Hosted by Fraser Cain (from Universe Today) and Dr. Pamela Gay (an astronomer and science communicator), this podcast offers a detailed exploration of various topics in astronomy. Each episode focuses on a specific topic, whether it’s a celestial object, an astronomical phenomenon, or a fundamental concept in space science.

The Astronomy Cast is known for its thorough and structured episodes, which take listeners step-by-step through complex topics. The hosts are incredibly knowledgeable and skilled at breaking down difficult concepts into digestible pieces, making this podcast both informative and engaging. From the birth of stars to the mysteries of dark energy, The Astronomy Cast covers it all.

Why Listen?

  • Deep dive into specific astronomy topics.
  • Hosts are experts in the field of astronomy.
  • Structured format that helps listeners understand complex concepts.

8. The Cosmic Companion

For those who love hearing from a wide range of experts in space science, The Cosmic Companion is a podcast that brings together scientists, researchers, and space enthusiasts from around the world. Hosted by the passionate and knowledgeable James Maynard, The Cosmic Companion explores a variety of topics, from the latest space missions and discoveries to the search for extraterrestrial life.

One of the highlights of The Cosmic Companion is its commitment to bringing fresh perspectives and new voices into the conversation. Whether it’s an interview with a cutting-edge scientist or an exploration of a historical space mission, each episode offers something new and exciting for space lovers.

Why Listen?

  • Wide variety of topics and guest experts.
  • A fresh perspective on current space science and missions.
  • Engaging and informative interviews with experts in the field.

9. SkyFi Radio

If you’re an aspiring space tourist or someone who loves to dream about the future of space travel, SkyFi Radio is the podcast for you. With a focus on space tourism, private space missions, and the commercialization of space, SkyFi Radio explores the future of space exploration from a unique angle. Hosted by space industry experts, the show covers a range of topics, including the development of commercial space stations, space tourism, and the technology that could take humans to Mars and beyond.

SkyFi Radio also features interviews with industry leaders, entrepreneurs, and astronauts, giving listeners an inside look into the companies and individuals shaping the future of space travel.

Why Listen?

  • Focus on the future of commercial space travel.
  • In-depth exploration of space tourism and private space missions.
  • Interviews with industry experts and space pioneers.

10. The Dark Cosmos

For those who are particularly fascinated by the mysteries of the universe, The Dark Cosmos is a captivating podcast that explores the unknown aspects of space. Hosted by cosmologist and astrophysicist Dr. John M. Griggs, the podcast delves into some of the most intriguing topics in cosmology, such as dark matter, dark energy, and the potential for parallel universes.

Dr. Griggs brings a wealth of knowledge and enthusiasm to each episode, offering listeners a deeper understanding of the cosmos and its many mysteries. The Dark Cosmos is ideal for listeners who are not afraid to dive into the more theoretical aspects of space science and cosmology.

Why Listen?

  • Focuses on the mysteries and unknowns of the universe.
  • Hosted by a knowledgeable and passionate cosmologist.
  • Ideal for listeners with a strong interest in theoretical space science.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re an astronomy enthusiast, a student, or someone with a casual interest in space, these podcasts offer a wide variety of perspectives and insights into the universe. The year 2023 is a thrilling time to be involved in the world of astronomy, with new discoveries and exciting missions happening regularly. So, grab your headphones, tune into one of these incredible astronomy podcasts, and continue exploring the cosmos, one episode at a time!
