How to Write Engaging Press Releases for Your Play


As a playwright, you've invested countless hours writing a masterpiece, perfecting the dialogue, and rehearsing the performances. The last hurdle is getting the word out about your production, and that's where a well-crafted press release comes in. A press release can be the key to getting your play seen and noticed by the right people but, let's face it, it's not easy. With a million other playwrights and productions vying for attention, how can you make your press release stand out from the crowd? Start with a Compelling Hook The first sentence of your press release needs to grab the reader's attention, explain what's unique about your play, and make them want to read on. It's the hook that sets the tone and sets the stage for the rest of the release. Think about what makes your play different from others – its themes, characters, or setting – and use that as your starting point. A fresh perspective, an intriguing mystery, or a thought-provoking subtext can all make for a compelling first sentence. Get to Know Your Audience Who is your target audience for this press release? Are they influencers, casting directors, theater enthusiasts, or critics? Understanding who you're writing for will help you tailor the tone, language, and content to speak directly to them. What matters most to them? What are their pain points, interests, and concerns? If you know your audience inside and out, you can craft a press release that resonates with them and addresses their specific needs. Add a Strong Headline The headline of your press release is akin to the title of your play – it sets the tone and grabs attention. A great headline is short, punchy, and informative. It tells the reader what your play is about and why it's worth their attention. Avoid clichés and overly generic titles, as they only lead to indifference. Instead, think outside the box and come up with a headline that sparks curiosity. A Well-Structured Body A press release should be concise, making it easy to scan and understand. Break up the text into paragraphs, each with a clear and specific purpose. Use bullet points and spacing to highlight key information and make it more readable. Avoid padding or using overly complex language that can confuse or bore your reader. Include an Exclusive Statement or News What sets your play apart from others in the same genre? Is there a unique plot twist, a local theme, or a visiting cast or crew member? Provide something meaty that grabs the reader's attention and makes them excited to learn more. Think local or timely news angles that appeal to your target audience. Why Should They Care? Why should your press release generate interest and coverage? Highlight important details like the notable production team, celebrity appearances, or educating stories of bringing this play to life. What makes your production noteworthy? Give readers a reason to cover your story. Social Media Integration You've got more than one channel for spreading the word. Leverage social media to get your press release in front of a larger audience. Share snippets of your release on Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn to drive traffic to your press release. Tweet or post individual key takeaways, a link to the full release, or tease the press release's announcement. Don't forget to include your marketing strategy for getting it covered in media outlets, like press releases.
