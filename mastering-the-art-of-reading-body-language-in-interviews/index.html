Mastering the Art of Reading Body Language in Interviews


Too often, we focus solely on what a person is saying during an interview, while neglecting to pay attention to their body language. Reading body language effectively can give you deeper insights into someone’s strengths, weaknesses, and character. In return, this empowers you to spot inconsistencies in their responses, ask more probing questions, and ultimately make a better hiring decision.

Here are essential tips to help you master the art of reading body language in interviews:

  1. Build Rapport: The first step to effective body language reading is establishing a connection with the interviewee. A positive connection can relax them and help reveal more about their character and communication style.

When meeting the interviewee, greet them warmly and maintain an open posture. Maintain good eye contact, as it shows genuine interest and establishes trust. A sincere and friendly demeanor will help build a rapport that enables effective communication.

  1. Notice Facial Expressions: Facial expressions can reveal a lot about a person’s emotions and thoughts. Closely observe the candidate’s face throughout the interview.

A sincere smile shows warmth and sincerity, while a brief smile might indicate nervousness or dishonesty. Similarly, raised eyebrows and furrowed brows can signify suspicion or disbelief, while a steady, direct gaze often denotes honesty.

  1. Observe Posture and Body Language: The way a person sits, stands, or moves can significantly influence how they feel and communicate. Be alert for these subtle cues.

Hunched shoulders and slumped posture can suggest low confidence or defensiveness. On the other hand, a relaxed, upright posture with maintained eye contact might indicate openness and confidence.

  1. Watch for Microexpressions: Microexpressions are brief, involuntary facial expressions that often reveal genuine emotions underneath a person’s attempted mask of calmness.

To spot microexpressions, look for sudden muscle twitching or quick changes in expression. These neutral, fast microexpressions are often associated with genuine feelings.

  1. Be Mindful of Personal Space: People’s proximity to you in an interview can reveal much about their comfort levels and personalities. Pay attention to how candidates adjust their seats and physical proximity to you.

Candidates who move closer to you might show a desire to connect or assure themselves of your trustworthiness. Conversely, those who maintain a comfortable distancing might prefer to keep things professional and formal.

  1. Use Positive Body Language: In addition to reading body language, using positive body language can enhance your credibility and reveal your interest in the candidate.

Use confident, open body language and posture. Keep an upright stance, uncross your legs, and rest your palms on your lap. These non-verbal cues signal competence and attentiveness, reinforcing your credibility and impacting the candidate’s perception of you.

In conclusion, mastering the art of reading body language is a powerful skill in interviews. By recognizing facial expressions, body posture, and other subtle cues, you can gain deeper insight into the candidate’s true character and communication style. With proper practice and awareness, you will be on your way to becoming an adept interviewer, confident in your ability to make well-founded decisions.
