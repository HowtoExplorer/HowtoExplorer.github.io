In Space, No One Can Hear You Laugh: The Funny Side of Space Exploration


Space, the final frontier, has captured the imagination of humans for generations. From the early days of NASA to the private endeavours of modern-day organizations like SpaceX and Blue Origin, the pursuit of space exploration has been a wild ride of scientific and technological advancements. As we continue to push the boundaries of our understanding of the cosmos, it’s essential to take a moment to appreciate a more lighthearted aspect of this cosmic quest— the humor in space exploration.

We’ve all heard about the triumphs, the discoveries, and the monumental feats accomplished in space. But, sometimes, it’s the unexpected and comical incidents that capture attention and evoke laughter. From the mishaps that happen during launches to the weird science found floating around, let’s take a look at the funny side of space exploration.

Malfunctions and Hiccup Moments

Space exploration is a delicate affair with a margin for error that can’t afford too many slip-ups. But, let’s face it, mishaps and blunders are inevitable. One memorable incident involved the Challenger space shuttle in 1984. After launching, the crew reported a leak in their oxygen tank, jokingly dubbed “the mouse hole.” Now you might be wondering, how could an errant punch hole from a furry airborne creature drastically alter the outcome? Well, as it happens, the crew discovered that an actual mouse had taken up residence inside the space shuttle and chewed through the crucial hoses and wiring, leading to a dramatic 45-minute repair session. Talk about a cosmic rodent run-in!

Then there was the time in 2007 when NASA astronauts Mark Kelly and Rick Mastracchio had a hiccup during a spacewalk. In a burst of well-timed comedy, Kelly said, “I think you owe me a drink for that little maneuver,” after a 20-minute wrestling match with a stubborn camera. And we can’t forget the memorable exchange between astronaut Clay Anderson and Mission Control while he was stranded in the vacuum of space: “Oops, guess I didn’t hold the button quite right. Kind of seems like something you only do in space.”

In addition to these amusing shenanigans, there’s the ever-present fear of spacesuit slip-ups. Remember the unfortunate time when Expedition 37 Flight EngineerWakata had a brief bout of spacesuit failure during a spacewalk? It’s best not to think too much about what could have happened if his suit had given way, but fortunately, everyone managed to keep their cool, and the situation was handled with grace.

Out of This World Science Shenanigans

The vast reaches of space hold many mysteries, and science is yet to figure out some of the most peculiar things found in space. Perhaps the funniest example of this is the impact crater on Bennu, a near-Earth asteroid. After a thorough examination by NASA’s OSIRIS-REx spacecraft, the origin of this seemingly senseless pockmark was determined to have been caused by a rather bitty projectile: a fleck of potato chip! Can you imagine being the scientist assigned to that exciting discovery?

Another riotous revelation is the realization that dark matter in galaxies may have a habit of mimicking the sound of laughter. While dark matter, constituent parts of galaxies, usually remains elusive and undetectable, astrophysicists have found evidence of its influence through the subtle interference of galaxy cluster bullet holes. The “laugh” is really due to the combination of different galaxy cluster interactions, which generate a noise that can only be described as something similar to a cosmic chuckle. It’s as if the universe has its own sense of humor!

The Quirky Names of Spacecraft and Facilities

The cosmos has also gifted us with a plethora of hilarious space-related names. Let’s name a few of these luminaries: the European Space Agency’s ARIEL (Atmospheric Remote-Sensing Infrared Exoplanet Large-survey), the bulbous shape of NASA’s Spitzer Space Telescope dubbed “The Whale,” and the Japanese MDS (Micro debris Surveyor) spacecraft, an actual garbage collector.

Meanwhile, back at ground control, the White Sands Missile Range cleverly located in the Tularosa Basin of New Mexico refers to a structure known as the “Spaceship,” a perfect morale-boosting name for a rather imposing and large facility. And how could we forget the cooperative efforts of a diverse cast of characters at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory – home to the LADEE (Lunar Atmosphere and Dust Environment Explorer), the twin GRAIL (Gravity Recovery and Interior Laboratory) spacecraft, and many other intriguing monikers.

Conclusion

Sure, space is filled with profound mysteries, undeniable beauty, and incredible achievements, but one can’t deny that the humor found in these stories only adds another layer of depth to the incredible universe we call home. Whether it’s astronauts battling tiny airborne rodents or remarkable cosmic discoveries that have us doubling over in laughter, the past, present, and future of space exploration undoubtedly holds many more surprises and comical moments yet to unfold.

As we continue onward in our quest for space knowledge, let’s remember to appreciate the light-heartedness that’s bound to arise from the profound and the abstract. The journey of space exploration has taught us never to underestimate the cosmic quirkiness and humor that makes the pursuit of the unknown, well, simply out of this world!
