The Best Online Resources for Learning New Craft Skills


In a world where creativity knows no bounds, the desire to learn new craft skills has surged. Whether you’re a seasoned artisan or a curious beginner, the internet is brimming with resources that cater to all levels of expertise. This blog post will explore some of the best online platforms, communities, and tools available for anyone looking to expand their crafting abilities. From knitting and sewing to woodworking and digital art, there’s something for everyone.

1. Online Learning Platforms

Skillshare

Skillshare is a popular online learning platform that offers a wide range of classes focused on creative skills. With courses from industry professionals, you can learn everything from watercolor painting to graphic design. The platform allows you to learn at your own pace and offers a subscription model that gives you access to thousands of classes. Each course typically includes video lessons, project assignments, and community feedback, which can be incredibly beneficial for honing your craft.

Udemy

Udemy is another extensive online learning resource that provides a variety of courses on nearly every craft imaginable. With thousands of options, you can find classes on knitting, pottery, photography, and much more. Many courses are self-paced, allowing you to take your time and revisit material as needed. The platform often features discounts and promotions, making it an affordable choice for learners.

Coursera

For those interested in more structured learning, Coursera offers courses from accredited universities and colleges. While it tends to focus on academic subjects, many institutions offer creative courses as well. You can find classes on design, arts, and even specialized crafts. Completing these courses can provide you with a certificate, which may be beneficial for your professional portfolio.

2. YouTube Channels

The Crafty Gemini

The Crafty Gemini, run by Vanessa Vargas Wilson, is a fantastic YouTube channel that offers free tutorials on a variety of crafts, including quilting, sewing, and crafting for kids. Vanessa’s engaging teaching style makes it easy to follow along with projects, and her channel is a treasure trove of inspiration for anyone looking to enhance their sewing skills.

Tasty

While primarily known for cooking, Tasty has expanded its content to include crafting tutorials. Their short, engaging videos cover a range of DIY crafts, including home decor projects, holiday crafts, and more. The visual format is perfect for those who prefer step-by-step instructions and quick learning.

HGTV Handmade

HGTV Handmade is another excellent channel for craft enthusiasts, featuring a variety of DIY projects. From home decor to seasonal crafts, the channel presents content that is both informative and visually appealing. Their videos often include tips and tricks, making it easier for viewers to replicate projects at home.

3. Social Media Platforms

Pinterest

Pinterest is a powerful tool for discovering new craft ideas and tutorials. With countless boards and pins dedicated to various crafting techniques, you can easily explore new projects and save them for later. Pinterest is particularly useful for visual learners, as it showcases photos and infographics that can guide you through the crafting process.

Instagram

Instagram has become a hub for crafters to showcase their work and share tutorials. By following popular craft accounts and hashtags, you can find a wealth of inspiration and connect with fellow craft enthusiasts. Many creators share short video clips or IGTV tutorials, making it easy to learn new skills on the go.

Facebook Groups

Facebook groups dedicated to crafting can be invaluable for learning from others. Joining groups related to your specific interests allows you to ask questions, share your projects, and receive feedback from experienced crafters. These communities foster a sense of camaraderie and support, making the learning process more enjoyable.

4. Blogs and Websites

Craftsy

Craftsy is a website specifically dedicated to crafting, offering classes, tutorials, and patterns across various disciplines. From knitting and sewing to baking and painting, Craftsy provides a wealth of resources that cater to different skill levels. The platform also features a community section where you can connect with other crafters, share your work, and get advice.

The Spruce Crafts

The Spruce Crafts is a comprehensive website that covers a wide array of crafting topics. With articles, tutorials, and project ideas, it serves as a one-stop shop for crafters looking to learn new skills. The site features content on sewing, knitting, paper crafts, and more, along with tips and tricks from experienced crafters.

Handmade Charlotte

Handmade Charlotte is a blog that focuses on family-friendly crafts and DIY projects. With a variety of tutorials ranging from simple crafts for kids to advanced projects for adults, this site is perfect for anyone looking to enhance their crafting repertoire. The blog also emphasizes eco-friendly and sustainable crafting methods, making it a great resource for environmentally conscious crafters.

5. Online Communities and Forums

Reddit

Reddit hosts numerous subreddits dedicated to various crafts, where users share their work, ask for advice, and provide tutorials. Subreddits like r/crafts, r/sewing, and r/knitting are filled with enthusiastic crafters eager to help newcomers and share their expertise. Engaging in these communities can lead to valuable insights and tips that can enhance your crafting skills.

Craftster

Craftster is an online forum designed for crafters to share their projects, ideas, and advice. With a friendly community atmosphere, you can post your work, ask for feedback, and participate in swaps or challenges. The site covers a vast range of crafts, making it an excellent resource for learning and connecting with others.

6. Online Workshops and Webinars

Creativebug

Creativebug is an online platform that offers high-quality video workshops taught by expert instructors. With a subscription model, you gain access to a wide range of craft classes, including knitting, painting, and jewelry making. The platform also features live classes and workshops, allowing you to interact with instructors and fellow crafters in real time.

The Art of Education University

For those interested in teaching art and craft skills, The Art of Education University offers professional development courses specifically for art educators. These courses cover various topics, including innovative techniques and classroom management strategies. Even if you’re not a teacher, the content can provide valuable insights into art and craft instruction.

7. E-Books and Online Resources

Craft Books on Kindle

Amazon’s Kindle Store offers a plethora of e-books dedicated to crafting. From beginner guides to advanced techniques, you can find books on nearly every craft imaginable. Many e-books also include links to additional resources, such as patterns and video tutorials, making them a comprehensive option for learning.

Online Craft Magazines

Various online craft magazines publish articles, tutorials, and project ideas regularly. Subscribing to these magazines can keep you updated on the latest trends and techniques in the crafting world. Popular titles like Mollie Makes and Crafts Beautiful offer a mix of inspiration and practical advice for crafters of all levels.

8. Craft Supply Stores

Michaels

Many craft supply stores, like Michaels, offer online classes and workshops. These classes often cover popular crafting techniques and projects, allowing you to learn new skills while also discovering new products. Additionally, attending in-store classes can provide hands-on experience and the opportunity to ask questions directly to instructors.

Joann Fabrics

Joann Fabrics also provides online tutorials and classes for various crafts. Their website features a plethora of resources, including project ideas, video tutorials, and tips for using their products. Joann’s community events and workshops can also enhance your crafting journey, providing opportunities to connect with other crafters.

Conclusion

In today’s digital age, learning new craft skills has never been more accessible. With a plethora of online resources available, from structured courses and engaging YouTube tutorials to vibrant social media communities and comprehensive blogs, there’s no shortage of opportunities to explore your creative side. Whether you’re looking to master a specific technique or simply try something new, these resources can guide you on your journey to becoming a skilled crafter. Embrace the world of crafting, and let your creativity flourish!
