How to Compete Internally for a Job: Crafting a Winning Resume for Promotion or Lateral Transfer


Looking for a different role within your company can be an exciting and nerve-wracking experience. Competing internally for a job opens up new avenues for growth and could lead to a more fulfilling and enriching career. However, you need to craft the perfect resume to secure that new position. Here are tips for creating a compelling resume that demonstrates your strengths, skills, and experience.

1. Understand the Role

Before diving into crafting a winning resume, make sure you’ve defined the role you’re looking to transition into. Research the job description and requirements thoroughly. This knowledge will serve as the backbone of your resume, ensuring that you highlight appropriate transferrable skills and accomplishments.

2. Tailor Your Resume

Your target job might have entirely different responsibilities and expectations than your current position, which means your resume needs to adapt too. Write a tailored resume that emphasizes skills, experiences, and accomplishments that relate directly to the new role. Use key words from their job description and list your experiences in a way that makes them applicable to the next position.

Here’s a basic resume structure you might consider:

# Job Title

* Company Name

## Achievements and Responsibilities
- [Specific accomplishment or responsibility]
- [Specific accomplishment or responsibility]

3. Quantify Your Accomplishments

Don’t just list your responsibilities; show what you’ve accomplished. Use numbers and metrics to quantify your successes, like:

- Increased monthly revenue by 20% within the first quarter
- Streamlined project management process, reducing turnaround time by 30%

Such concrete examples give employers a clear picture of what you bring to the table and show that you have a track record of successful performance.

4. Demonstrate Growth and Development

If you’re applying for a promotion, it’s essential to show that you’re committed to learning and development. Include any relevant certifications, courses, or professional development opportunities you’ve engaged in. This will demonstrate that you’re not content to just stay in one place but are looking to grow within the company.

5. Create a Visual Structure

While crafting your resume in markdown, pay attention to formatting and structure. Use consistent headings, subheadings, bullet points, and section breaks (like “—” for horizontal lines) to make your resume scannable and easy to read.

For example:

# Project Manager

* Acme Corporation
## Achievements and Responsibilities
---
* Team Lead
---

6. Keep it Concise

Recruiters and hiring managers appreciate brevity. A resume of no more than two pages is usually perfect. Focus on your most relevant accomplishments and eliminate fluff. Be clear and avoid verbose language.

7. Revise and Edit

Carefully proofread your work. Ensure your resume is free of grammatical and spelling errors. If possible, have a trusted colleague or mentor give your resume a once-over as well. The more professional your submission, the more seriously you’ll be taken.

Lastly, if you have the opportunity to apply through an ATS (Applicant Tracking System), ensure your resume is formatted in a way that the ATS will understand.

Remember, the goal of your internal job hunt resume is not just to showcase your past achievements, but also to how how you can apply your experiences and skills to your new position.

When you combine a thorough understanding of the role with a resume tailored specifically to your new goal, you’ll be well on your way to competing for and securing that internal transfer or promotion.

Good luck in your endeavors, and keep shining!
