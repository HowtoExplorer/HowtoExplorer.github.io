Interviewing Techniques for Journalists: Getting the Best Quotes


As a journalist, conducting interviews is a crucial part of your job. It is through interviews that you gather information, insights, and quotes to bring your story to life. However, getting the best quotes from your interviewees can sometimes be a challenge. In this blog post, we will explore some interviewing techniques that will help you elicit compelling quotes from your sources.

1. Prepare Thoroughly

Before you conduct an interview, it is essential to do your homework. Research your interviewee and familiarize yourself with their background, expertise, and previous statements. This will not only help you ask informed questions but also demonstrate your knowledge and earn the interviewee’s trust.

2. Build Rapport

Building rapport with your interviewee is crucial to creating a comfortable and open environment. Start the conversation with some small talk to put them at ease. Show genuine interest in their experiences and opinions, and maintain good eye contact and body language. By establishing a connection, you encourage your interviewee to open up and provide more insightful and authentic quotes.

3. Ask Open-Ended Questions

When conducting an interview, it is essential to ask open-ended questions that cannot be answered with a simple “yes” or “no.” Open-ended questions encourage interviewees to provide detailed and thoughtful responses, which often lead to more compelling quotes. For example, instead of asking, “Did you enjoy your recent success?” you could ask, “How do you feel about the recent success you’ve achieved, and how has it impacted your journey?”

4. Listen Actively

Active listening is a vital skill for journalists. Pay close attention to your interviewee’s responses, and don’t be afraid to ask follow-up questions to delve deeper into their thoughts and experiences. By actively listening, you show respect for their words and demonstrate your commitment to understanding their perspective. This, in turn, can lead to more profound and impactful quotes.

5. Use Silence Effectively

Silence can be a powerful tool during an interview. After your interviewee finishes speaking, allow a moment of silence before moving on to the next question. Sometimes, interviewees feel compelled to fill the silence and may offer additional information or elaborate on their previous response. This can lead to unexpected and impactful quotes that add depth and emotion to your story.

6. Respect Boundaries

While it is essential to push for insightful quotes, it is equally important to respect your interviewee’s boundaries. If they seem uncomfortable or unwilling to answer a particular question, move on to a different topic. Respecting their limits will foster trust and increase the likelihood of them providing meaningful quotes on other aspects of the interview.

7. Follow Up

After the interview, send a thank-you note to your interviewee. Express your appreciation for their time and insights. This simple gesture shows professionalism and may lead to future collaborations. Additionally, if there are any gaps or areas of clarification from the interview, reach out to the interviewee for further discussion. This not only helps in accurate reporting but also strengthens your relationship with them.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of getting the best quotes from your interviewees is a skill that takes time and practice. By preparing thoroughly, building rapport, asking open-ended questions, actively listening, using silence effectively, respecting boundaries, and following up, you can elevate your interviews and create impactful stories that resonate with your audience. Remember, the best quotes are often the ones that evoke emotions, provide unique insights, and capture the essence of your subject’s perspective. So go out there, conduct meaningful interviews, and let the quotes speak for themselves.
