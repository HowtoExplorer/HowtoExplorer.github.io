The Best Craft Resources for Teachers and Educators


As a teacher or educator, you understand the importance of creating engaging and interactive learning experiences for your students. One way to achieve this is by incorporating craft activities into your lesson plans. Not only do craft projects help develop fine motor skills, hand-eye coordination, and creativity, but they also provide an opportunity for students to express themselves and showcase their unique perspectives.

In this post, we will explore some of the best craft resources available to teachers and educators, covering a range of topics and skill levels. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or just starting out, you’ll find something on this list that will inspire and support your teaching practice.

Online Resources

  1. The Art of Education University: This online resource offers a vast collection of art and craft lesson plans, tutorials, and projects for teachers of all grade levels. From painting and drawing to sculpture and printmaking, The Art of Education University has got you covered.
  2. Craftsy: Craftsy is an online learning platform that offers a range of craft-focused courses and tutorials. With classes on topics such as knitting, sewing, and jewelry-making, Craftsy is a great resource for teachers looking to develop their own crafting skills.
  3. The Imagination Tree: The Imagination Tree is a popular blog that offers a wide range of creative activities and crafts for young children. From sensory play to art projects, this site is a treasure trove of inspiration for early years educators.

Print Resources

  1. The Usborne Book of Art: This comprehensive guide to art and craft is perfect for teachers of all ages. With over 200 projects and activities, The Usborne Book of Art is a great resource for developing fine motor skills, creativity, and self-expression.
  2. The Crafter’s Companion: This beautifully illustrated book is a must-have for any crafty teacher. With projects ranging from simple to complex, The Crafter’s Companion is a great resource for developing a range of crafting skills.
  3. The Art of Handmade: This inspiring book showcases the work of over 30 talented crafters, offering a glimpse into the world of handmade art and craft. The Art of Handmade is a great resource for teachers looking to develop their own crafting skills and inspire their students.

Supply Stores

  1. Michaels: Michaels is a popular craft store that offers a wide range of art supplies, from paints and markers to glue and scissors. With locations across the United States and Canada, Michaels is a convenient resource for teachers and educators.
  2. Hobby Lobby: Hobby Lobby is a craft store that offers a wide range of supplies, including fabric, yarn, and paper crafting materials. With a focus on affordable prices and high-quality products, Hobby Lobby is a great resource for teachers on a budget.
  3. Dick Blick Art Materials: Dick Blick is a leading art supply store that offers a wide range of materials, from paints and brushes to canvases and easels. With a focus on high-quality products and expert advice, Dick Blick is a great resource for teachers and educators.

Additional Tips and Resources

  1. Pinterest: Pinterest is a great resource for teachers and educators looking for inspiration and ideas. With millions of pins and boards dedicated to art and craft, Pinterest is a treasure trove of creative ideas and projects.
  2. Local Libraries: Local libraries are a great resource for teachers and educators, offering a wide range of books, magazines, and online resources. Many libraries also offer free or low-cost craft activities and workshops, making them a great place to find inspiration and support.
  3. Collaboration: Finally, don’t forget the importance of collaboration! Working with other teachers, educators, and crafty folk can be a great way to find inspiration, share ideas, and develop new skills. Whether it’s through online communities, local craft groups, or in-school collaborations, working together can be a powerful way to support your teaching practice and inspire your students.

In conclusion, the best craft resources for teachers and educators are those that offer a range of creative activities, inspiring ideas, and high-quality supplies. By incorporating these resources into your teaching practice, you can create engaging and interactive learning experiences that inspire your students and support their development. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or just starting out, these resources will help you to develop your own crafting skills, inspire your students, and create a lifelong love of learning.
