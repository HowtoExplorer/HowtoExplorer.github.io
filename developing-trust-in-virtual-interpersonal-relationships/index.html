Developing Trust in Virtual Interpersonal Relationships


When online communication platforms emerged during the pandemic, they facilitated numerous connections and collaborations. We witnessed millions of people in remote work environments, as well as the growth of social networks and virtual social events. With these advances came increased opportunities for building relationships and friendships online. However, trust in these virtual interpersonal relationships can sometimes be challenging.

Lack of contextual cues, such as face-to-face communication, voice inflection, and body language, can hinder trust development in virtual relationships. To overcome this barrier, we must focus on every opportunity we have to ensure that our virtual interpersonal relationships flourish. This can be challenging but not impossible, as there are various tactics we can use.

Verbal and Written Communication

By having transparent and honest communication, trust can be gradually established in virtual relationships. As our messages and conversations continue, we slowly uncover more information about our interests, values, and even fears or vulnerabilities. This constant disclosure helps build rapport and understanding between individuals, resulting in trust.

Moreover, using a friendly tone, positive body language (even though we don’t see the person physically, we can use emojis and GIFs to convey positivity and friendliness), and showing empathy towards the receiver can help foster trust in virtual relationships.

Consistency in Actions

Trust is built over time, and consistency in actions is a crucial element. By providing the same level of support, commitment, and communication on a continuous basis, we can exhibit our reliability and build trust. Consistency can be achieved through punctuality in replying, following through on promises, and expressing genuine interest and engagement in a relationship.

We must also be open to feedback and adapt our communication style accordingly. This willingness to learn and modify our behavior can be essential in building long-lasting virtual relationships.

Inquiry on Personal Interests and Goals

Exploring interests and goals within a relationship can be pivotal in developing trust. When we understand each other’s motivations and aspirations, we gain insights into their values and life journey. This understanding paves the way for more profound connections.

So, sharing stories and experiences about personal achievements, interests, or hobbies can foster a stronger bond. By asking questions and genuinely inquiring about the individual’s likes and dislikes, we show interest and care for their well-being.

Maintain Privacy and Confidentiality

Maintaining privacy and confidentiality is fundamental to trust. The digital age presents many challenges, as sensitive information can be easily accessible. However, intentionally guarding and preserving the privacy of our virtual relationships is vital for trust, especially if our interactions involve sensitive or personal information.

By maintaining privacy and confidentiality, we send a clear message: our relationship is worth preserving, and we are dedicated to ensuring it thrives.

Partnerships and Opportunities for Collaboration

Lastly, virtual relationships offer fantastic partnership opportunities. By collaborating on projects or sharing ideas, we can create a sense of unity and strengthen trust. When we demonstrate that our goals and values align, we lay the foundation for a more meaningful and trusting relationship.

Trust is the cornerstone of any relationship, be it virtual or in-person. By utilizing the tactics mentioned, we can overcome the challenges and enjoy the benefits of virtual interpersonal relationships. So, let’s embrace the digital world and continue fostering trust online.
