Reading Resolutions for the New Year: Setting Literary Goals


As we enter a fresh new year, now is the perfect time to set our sights on some reading resolutions. Reading not only enriches our minds, but it also allows us to explore new worlds, opinions, and perspectives. Here, we will explore a variety of literary goals to consider for the upcoming year.

Diversify Genres

One great goal for the year is to challenge oneself and explore new literary territories. This might include picking up a genre you have never attempted before, such as science fiction, romance, or historical fiction. Give yourself permission to wander off your typical reading path and experience the world through different eyes.

Read More Classics

Make a point to revisit some of the classic works that have stood the test of time. Literary works like Fyodor Dostoevsky’s “Crime and Punishment,” Jane Austen’s “Pride and Prejudice,” or Mary Shelley’s “Frankenstein” offer timeless insights and memorable characters. In doing so, we can gain a deeper appreciation of literary history and the many that came before us.

Broaden the horizons with International Literature

Another exciting resolution could be to read literature from different countries and cultures. From Haruki Murakami’s “Kafka on the Shore” to Isabel Allende’s “The House of the Spirits,” international literature offers fresh perspectives and unique storytelling. Embrace the chance to dive deep into an unfamiliar culture and enhance your understanding of the world.

Set a “Goodreads” Goal

Goodreads, an online library catalog and book-shelf management platform, makes it easy to track reading progress throughout the year. Set a reading goal for the year, and watch yourself succeed! Reading goals, such as aiming to read 12 books, or setting a specific number per month, can provide a sense of accomplishment, motivation, and anticipation.

Join a Book Club

Another way to stay accountable is to join or form a book club. Being part of a group offers the chance to discuss and share thoughts and opinions on the books you read and creates a collective wealth of knowledge and experience. Book clubs also provide a journey of self-growth and friendship, proving an enriching investment in the new year.

Make Reading a Social Activity

Ensure to share your reading experience on social media platforms like Twitter or Instagram. Reading challenges can generate friendly competition, and book reviews posted on platforms can inspire others to expand their literary worlds. For instance, tweet your progress on a particular book, or discuss a thought-provoking passage from a recent read.

Keep Track of Your Progress

Maintain a journal or reading log for the year and make note of your impressions, thoughts on particular works, and highlights. These logs can serve as cherished reminders of literary journeys and provide insights into personal growth and preferences.

By setting literary goals and working towards them, we can deepen our understanding of the world, ourselves, and the beauty of literature. Embrace the power of the written word, and see where your reading resolutions take you in the new year.
