Understanding Aquatic Ecosystems: The Balance of Life


Aquatic ecosystems cover more than 70% of the Earth's surface, harboring a staggering array of life forms and playing a crucial role in maintaining the health of our planet. These ecosystems, which range from shallow saltwater estuaries to the deep ocean's furthest reaches, are incredibly diverse and interconnected, supporting complex relationships between plants and animals that have evolved over millions of years. The Sands of the Seabed and the Grasses of the Shore Aquatic systems can be as shallow as a tide pool, where sea lilies sway gently in the current, their delicate filaments swaying in synchronization with the gentle lapping of the waves.
