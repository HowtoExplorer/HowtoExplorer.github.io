Michelangelo's Secret to Solving Problems: Defining versus Solving


In the heart of the Renaissance, an Italian artist named Michelangelo emerged as one of the most celebrated and influential figures in the art world. Michelangelo’s innovative approach to problem-solving is bound to give us a fresh perspective on tackling our obstacles in a similar way. His artistic mastery and far-sighted vision have set the foundation for the way we perceive art today. Let us delve deeper into the genius of Michelangelo by exploring his approach to defining problems versus solving them.

When Michelangelo ventured into creating the acheiropoieta equivalent of his era, the iconic frescoes in the Sistine Chapel, he had a profound understanding of problem-solving. As opposed to traditional methods of painting, he approached the project by meticulously defining his problem instead of merely seeking solutions. At first glance, his approach might seem counterintuitive, but consider the magnitude of detail and ambition in his masterpiece. Michelangelo encountered the vast ceiling of the chapel with a width of twenty-four meters and transformed it into a thriving universe that can still ignite our imaginations today.

Michelangelo’s secret was his intuition to focus sharply on defining the problem before engaging in their resolution. This approach has been expanded into a problem-solving model known as the Problem Definition Model.

The Problem Definition Model

  1. Identify the Problem: Acknowledge the problem at hand. This step might sound like the most obvious component in problem-solving, but it’s a step that often gets overlooked. By engaging in introspective, critical thinking, we enable ourselves to have a clearer understanding of what we’re dealing with.

  2. Clarify and Focus: Ensure that you have a clear understanding of the problem’s reach and its core issues. In our Sistine Chapel example, Michelangelo didn’t just intend to create a beautiful painting. He sought to give context to the biblical narratives, instilling them as lessons for all who would gaze upon them in reverence.

  3. Analyze and Breakdown: Michelangelo’s analytical prowess led him to zoom in further, breaking down his project into manageable tasks. He drew his sketches, refined them and continuously learned from the process. This stage is essential to prevent getting overwhelmed by the magnitude of the task or the problem we are confronting.

  4. Define the Specific Goals: Michelangelo was clear about his objectives while working on the Sistine Chapel project. He had a vision to create a celestial masterpiece that embodied the divine connection between human beings and divine entities. This was his goal, and he set forth to achieve it.

  5. Veering Away from Alternative Approach: Another secret Michelangelo had was to avoid falling into the trap of jumping into solutions early. This tendency often leads us to invest in inadequate solutions or changes of direction in our work. By defining the problem and the specific goals, he could discern whether a proposed solution would indeed lead to the desired outcome.

  6. Develop a Method to Assess and Evaluate: Michelangelo had a unique method when it came to perfecting his creations. By meticulously analyzing his sculptures and paintings, he could adapt and improve until he achieved greatness that would leave a lasting impression on society.

In conclusion, Michelangelo’s secret in solving problems was centered around the act of definition. By meticulously defining his problems, he could allocate his resources effectively, meticulously plan his approach, and ultimately achieve what seemed impossible during his time. By following his problem-solving model, we can achieve similar success in our respective fields and overcome the obstacles that lie in our paths.

As we challenge ourselves to look at Michelangelo’s work and study his techniques, we can apply his problem definition approach to problem-solving in our own creative and professional endeavors. His legacy challenges us to redefine the conventional approach to tackling problems and to embrace the power of defining.

Understanding Michelangelo’s secret might just be the differentiator you need to overcome the obstacles in your path. Nurture your problem-solving abilities and adopt the Problem Definition Model inspired by the great Michelangelo himself.
