The Dos and Don'ts of Using Presentation Slides


Creating and delivering a compelling presentation requires more than just good content and speaking skills. The use of presentation slides can greatly enhance the audience’s understanding and engagement, but when used incorrectly, they can also detract from the overall impact of the presentation. In this guide, we’ll explore the dos and don’ts of using presentation slides to help you create visually appealing and effective presentations.

The Dos

1. Keep It Simple

When designing presentation slides, remember that less is often more. Use clean and simple designs with minimal text to avoid overwhelming your audience. Focus on using visuals, such as images, charts, and graphs, to convey your message effectively.

2. Use Consistent Formatting

Maintain a consistent formatting style throughout your presentation slides. This includes using the same font, color palette, and layout to create a cohesive visual identity. Consistency helps establish a professional and polished look for your presentation.

3. Highlight Key Points

Use presentation slides to emphasize key points and takeaways. Bold or italicize important text, use bullet points for lists, and employ contrasting colors to draw attention to critical information. This will help your audience focus on the most crucial aspects of your presentation.

4. Practice Visual Hierarchy

Arrange content on your slides in a way that guides the audience’s attention. Utilize larger fonts for headings, smaller fonts for supporting text, and strategic placement of visuals to create a clear visual hierarchy. This ensures that the audience can easily follow the flow of your presentation.

5. Use High-Quality Images

Incorporate high-quality images that are relevant to your content. Avoid using generic stock photos and opt for original or purposeful visuals that add value to your message. Compelling images can help create an emotional connection with your audience and make your presentation more memorable.

The Don’ts

1. Overcrowd Slides with Text

Avoid overcrowding your slides with excessive text. Long paragraphs or too many bullet points can overwhelm your audience and make it challenging for them to focus on your message. Instead, use concise phrases and visual aids to convey information more effectively.

2. Use Distracting Transitions and Animations

While some transitions and animations can enhance your presentation, using them excessively or inappropriately can be distracting. Stick to subtle and professional transitions to maintain the focus on your content rather than the visual effects.

3. Rely Solely on Text

Resist the temptation to rely solely on text to convey your message. Visual aids are meant to complement your spoken words and provide visual support for your audience. Incorporate relevant images, charts, and diagrams to enhance understanding and retention of information.

4. Neglect Proofreading

Always proofread your presentation slides for spelling, grammar, and formatting errors. Mistakes can detract from your credibility and professionalism. Double-check all content to ensure accuracy and coherence.

5. Use Cluttered or Unappealing Designs

Steer clear of cluttered or unappealing slide designs. Busy backgrounds, mismatched fonts, and excessive use of colors can make your presentation slides look unprofessional. Opt for clean and visually pleasing designs that contribute to a positive audience experience.

By adhering to these dos and don’ts, you can elevate your presentation slides and deliver impactful presentations that resonate with your audience. Remember that effective use of presentation slides enhances your message and aids in conveying information in a memorable and engaging manner. Whether you’re presenting to clients, colleagues, or a larger audience, thoughtful and purposeful slide design can make a significant difference in the overall success of your presentation.

Now that you have a comprehensive understanding of the dos and don’ts of using presentation slides, it’s time to put these principles into practice and create compelling visuals that complement your message effectively. With these guidelines in mind, you can confidently craft visually appealing and impactful presentations that leave a lasting impression on your audience.
