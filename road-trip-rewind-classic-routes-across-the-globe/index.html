Road Trip Rewind: Classic Routes Across the Globe


There’s something truly magnificent about embarking on a road trip – the open road stretching out ahead, the fresh air blowing through your hair, and every stopover offering a new adventure. The allure of these journeys has persisted throughout history, leading to the development of some classic routes that have gained legendary status. From creeping through the ancient highways of India to cruising the highways of America’s West, the world truly offers endless opportunities for an awe-inspiring road trip. So, buckle up and let’s explore some of these time-honored routes across the globe and the stories that go along with them.

The Silk Road

The Silk Road, connecting the East and West, has long held the fascination of countless travelers. Spanning over 4,000 miles from China to the Mediterranean Sea, this ancient network of trade routes gained prominence between 130 B.C. and A.D. 1453, an era spanning the Han Dynasty of China and the fall of the Roman Empire. It was, without a doubt, one of the most important trading routes in the world, facilitating the exchange of goods, culture, ideas, and religions between the East and West, as well as between the civilizations of North Africa, the Middle East, and Europe.

Today, adventurers can embark on the reverse of this ancient route towards Xi’an, China, the end of the Silk Road, and gaze upon the famous Terracotta Warriors that guarded Emperor Qin Shi Huang’s tomb. Traversing the sun-drenched deserts, dusty mountain passes, and historic cities – Samarkand, Bukhara, and Khiva in Uzbekistan – the modern merchant can experience the rich tapestry of history that comes alive as he weaves his way through this storied path.

The Appian Way

The Appian Way, known as Regina Viarum or Regina Laudis, harks back to 312 B.C. as the epitome of an ancient Roman road trip experience. Stretching over 350 miles southeast from Rome to the ancient port of Brindisi, it was the world’s first superhighway – not only the longest but also one of the best-paved, engineered, and maintained roads of its time. This route, as vital to the Roman Republic and Empire as the interstate highways are to modern America, has endured centuries and embraced countless tales woven into its very foundation.

To this day, the Appian Way offers a tangible experience that connects ancient Roman history with contemporary Italy, allowing travelers to literally walk in the footsteps of those who built an empire. The remnants of this historic path don’t only embrace the remnants of Rome’s golden age but also cater to vibrant ruins sprinkled all along its route, such as the Aqueducts of Casal Lumbroso, the Circus Maximus, and the Entrance Arch of Gallienus.

The Pan-American Highway

The idea behind the Pan-American Highway was to provide a link between the Americas in the early 20th century. Spanning over 19,000 miles from Prudhoe Bay in Alaska to Tierra del Fuego in Argentina, this route remains the longest continuous highway in the world. Driving the Pan-American Highway means delving into the heart of North, Central, and South America – experiencing the cultural diversity, breathtaking natural panoramas, and diverse ecosystems ranging from Arctic tundras to Amazon rainforests.

Moreover, along the way, you’ll lap up the charms of characters that often lie off the well-beaten track – the indigenous people in Peru, the Mexican Day of Dead festival, or the dazzling sights of the Colombian coffee region. The best part? The Pan-American road trip can be customized and tailored to suit every taste – from adventurers hunting for the perfect wave in Nicaragua to skiers looking for snow-capped mountain ranges in Argentina.

The Kumbh Mela Route

One of the most significant Hindu pilgrimages, the Kumbh Mela, unfolds in rotational cycles every 12 years at four different locations in India – Allahabad, Haridwar, Nashik, and Ujjain. The epic journey reflects the faith and devotion of millions of Hindus who seek the divine nectar, or ‘Amrit,’ associated with the celestial pitcher ‘Kumbh.’

Along the Kumbh Mela routes, explorers encounter a variety of experiences – from the serene and sacred surroundings of the Ganges and Yamuna Rivers to the buzzing chaos of hubs like Allahabad and Haridwar. As one of the world’s largest gatherings, the Mela takes on more significance beyond its religious resonance – its sheer scale presents a logistical and cultural spectacle tough to witness anywhere else.

Route 66

As one of America’s most iconic road trip experiences, Route 66, the “Mother Road,” weaves through the heart of America, stretching for over 2,200 miles from Chicago to Los Angeles. Aside from its modern allure, Route 66 has long evoked the spirit of the American dream – the prospect of adventure, opportunity, and a journey anchored in the pursuit of freedom and possibility.

Along this classic American road trip, you’ll get greeted by a string of picturesque small towns, lively roadside attractions, diners, and motels that breathe the spirit of yesteryears. Travelers become a part of folklore with each stop – perhapslock lips with a iconic “Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas” sign or shed a few climactic tears in front of the former home of the Joad family in John Steinbeck’s ‘The Grapes of Wrath.’

These memorable road trips illustrated above are by no means exhaustive. The world abounds with other classic routes shaping unique stories and offering distinct experiences, from Australia’s Great Ocean Road to Europe’s “Blue Route” that unfolds the spectacular beauty of the African continent.

The roads that connect the far-off corners of our world often become the ultimate corridors of our memories – cobbled or paved, winding or straight, these highways unspool a personal narrative defined by horizons explored, carved new memories, or revitalized by the timeless past. A road trip, after all, presents the gift of a front-row seat to unfolding vistas while presenting the storyteller with ever-growing pages of their tale. It’s a journey not just of inches, but of landscapes and perspectives that shapeshift as the horizon beckons – a beckoning worth heeding, time and time again. So, reach out and touch that road. It awaits your adventure.
