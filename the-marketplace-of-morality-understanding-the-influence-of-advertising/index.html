The Marketplace of Morality: Understanding the Influence of Advertising


In today’s world, advertising has become an indispensable part of our lives. From the moment we wake up to the time we go to sleep, we are inundated with various forms of advertisements (ads for short) – on billboards, in magazines, on the radio, on television, and most importantly on the internet. In this blog post, we will delve into the realm of advertising and its immense power in shaping our beliefs, desires, and actions. We’ll discuss how ads persuade us to buy products and services, influence our values and behaviors, and mold our perception of what is considered “normal” or desirable in society.

The Art of Advertising

At its core, advertising is a form of persuasive communication that uses various elements (such as text, visuals, and audio) to convey a message about a product, service, or idea. To create effective ads, advertisers employ the expertise of skilled professionals (copywriters, graphic designers, directors, and more) who work tirelessly to captivate the target audience’s senses, attention, and emotions. One such iconic ad campaign is Dove’s “Real Beauty” campaign, which replaced the idealized norms of beauty with more realistic, human models – a campaign that undeniably struck a chord with women worldwide, evoking an emotional response and fostering a sense of camaraderie and appreciation.

Influencing Values and Behaviors

Through the power of captivating storytelling, relatable characters, and striking visuals, advertisements often communicate more than just the straightforward benefits of a product or service. They shape how we perceive ourselves, our relationships, and the world around us. For example, an ad for a luxury car may not only showcase features like advanced technology or horsepower but also associate the car with success, prestige, and power – values that are commonly held in today’s society. By extension, viewers can perceive themselves as successful or powerful if they purchase the product being advertised.

Ads also have the potential to influence our behavior and decision-making processes. For instance, they appeal to our emotions (e.g., fear of missing out, desire for social acceptance) or our cognitive processes (e.g., by emphasizing the benefits of a product or subtly undermining the competition). In one study, researchers found that the wording of restaurant ads could significantly influence consumers’ evaluations of menu items. Ads describing menu items in terms of their health benefits (e.g., “low in calories”) were associated with lower calorie perceptions and, conversely, higher calorie perceptions when described in indulgent terms (e.g., “creamy”).

Settling for the Marketplace’s Morality Standard

By consistently exposing us to certain images, ideas, and products, advertisements shape our perception of what is “normal” or acceptable within society. Consequently, we start to judge our own values and those of others based on whether they align with those presented in ads. This phenomenon can even influence our ethics or moral compass. For instance, research has shown that those exposed to ads portraying a casual attitude towards substance use were more likely to hold lenient attitudes towards the use of marijuana, regardless of their initial beliefs.

Moreover, without realizing it, we can develop certain feelings of entitlement, expecting that our lives should resemble those depicted in advertisements – comparing ourselves to perfect, flawless versions of people and constantly striving to keep up with the latest trends. In eroding our self-esteem, the crossroads of advertising and morality can lead us to make poor life decisions or deprive ourselves of genuine joy and satisfaction in the pursuit of an unattainable ideal.

Striving for a Healthier Balance

In the pursuit of self-awareness, being mindful of the influence that advertising has on our lives is the first step towards resisting its power. Here are some ways to maintain a healthier balance and preserve our sense of self:

  1. Critical thinking: Question and analyze the message of an advertisement, determine the emotion and need it seeks to exploit, and be more aware of its underlying motives.

  2. Mindful exposure: Reduce the time spent on platforms saturated with ads (e.g., social media) and consciously choose what content you engage with.

  3. Different perspectives: Engage with a variety of content that presents alternative viewpoints to the dominant narrative, starkly different from the mass-produced ads.

  4. Self-identity and values: Constantly remind yourself of your core values, strengths, and passions, and seek fulfillment in activities and achievements that align with them.

By acknowledging the significant role that advertising plays in shaping our moral landscape and maintaining a critical and mindful perspective, we can gain greater control over our lives and the direction of our values and actions.

Final Thoughts

Advertising is an unavoidable part of modern life, as much as we would like to believe that we possess the power to ignore it. But understanding its role in molding our opinions, desires, and actions can equip us with the awareness to resist its pull, make more informed decisions, and define our sense of self independently.

As we navigate the marketplace of morality, the journey to maintain and strengthen our true values lies not only in taking a closer look at the dazzling ads but in being courageous enough to define our lives beyond the perceived norm and societal expectations.
