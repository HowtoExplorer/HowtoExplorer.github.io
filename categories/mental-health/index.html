Understanding and Managing Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder, previously known as manic depression, is a mental health condition characterized by extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression). These mood swings can be intense and disruptive, affecting an individual’s ability to function in everyday life. Understanding the nature of bipolar disorder and learning how to manage it is essential for individuals diagnosed with this condition and their loved ones. What is Bipolar Disorder? Bipolar disorder is a complex condition that involves changes in mood, energy, and activity levels. It is categorized into several types, including bipolar I disorder, bipolar II disorder, and cyclothymic disorder. Bipolar I disorder involves manic episodes that last for at least seven days or are severe enough to require immediate hospital care. Depressive episodes typically follow the manic episodes. On the other hand, bipolar II disorder is characterized by a pattern of depressive episodes and hypomanic episodes, which are less severe than full-blown manic episodes. ...
