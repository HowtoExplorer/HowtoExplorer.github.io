Fortunately, there’s a simple yet powerful solution that can help improve time management and mental clarity: the Pomodoro Technique. What is the Pomodoro Technique? The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The technique is named after the Italian word for “tomato” because Cirillo initially used a tomato-shaped kitchen timer as a university student. The method is based on the concept of breaking work into focused time intervals, usually 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks. These intervals are referred to as “Pomodoros,” and after completing four Pomodoros, a longer break of 15-30 minutes is taken. ...
