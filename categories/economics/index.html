When we talk about market failures in economics, we often refer to situations where the allocation of goods and services by a free market is not efficient. There are various reasons for market failures, and two significant contributors to this phenomenon are externalities and public goods. Externalities Externalities are the side effects or consequences of economic activities that affect individuals or entities who are not directly involved in the activity. These effects can be either positive or negative. For example, when a factory pollutes a nearby river, it imposes a negative externality on the people living downstream who rely on the river for drinking water and irrigation. On the other hand, when a beekeeper sets up hives near an apple orchard, the bees provide a positive externality by pollinating the apple trees, benefiting both the beekeeper and the orchard owner. ...
