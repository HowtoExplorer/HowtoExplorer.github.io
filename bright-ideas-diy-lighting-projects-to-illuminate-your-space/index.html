Bright Ideas: DIY Lighting Projects to Illuminate Your Space


As the sun sets on another day, there’s something particularly satisfying about transforming a room with a well-placed lamp or light fixture. Here are some DIY lighting ideas that can illuminate your space and show off your creative side.

  1. Repurposed Pendant Lights Who says a pendant light has to be a pendant? These nifty little lamps can be made from just about any spherical object: mason jars, wine bottles, or repurposed light bulbs. Simply string some wire and attach your vessel of choice, and voilà – a unique hanging lamp that casts a warm, inviting glow into any room.

  2. Solar-Powered Lights for Outdoor Spaces Nothing beats the convenience of solar-powered lighting for an easy-to-implement backyard project. Create solar light stakes by attaching a clear sticky-note-size solar panel to a bamboo skewer or chopstick, then encase the bottom in a waterproof container half-filled with pebbles or sand. Place your solar-lit stake anywhere you need a bit more illumination: garden paths, patio spaces, or branches in a tree.

  3. Upcycled Industrial Table Lamps Materials: Empty soda cans, duct tape, LED tea lights, and a bit of creativity.

Transform your old soda cans into chic industrial lamps with this simple hack. Using duct tape, affix a LED tea light to an empty soda can base, making sure that the LED is secure and centered. Watch as the soft, warm light casts industrial-chic shadows on your tables and shelves.

  1. Wall Lamplike Hanging Art Go beyond the picture frame with this easy DIY project—affordable and adaptable to any space decor—and bring personality to your lighting. All you need is a wine bottle, some LED fairy lights, and a way to hang your bottle (wall hooks, for example). Run your lights up the bottle, winding them from the base to the neck, and voilà—a custom light up or down the wall.

  2. Globe Lights to Illuminate Bookshelves (or Window Spaces) Leftover Christmas ideas, here we come! For this idea, you will need miniature white lights or perhaps LED string lights, and a string of glass, plastic, or Terracotta globes. Hand thread these translucent orbs onto the wire, working in symphony with the light inside the bulbs. Place the globe and light on a bookshelf, window sill, or any space that needs a soft, gentle touch of light.

  3. Make an Instant Hanging Lantern with Jars The best part of this trick? No sewing required. All you’ve got to do is repurpose an old sheet or curtain to create a hanging shade. Cut a square that’s larger than the top opening of your mason jar, gather fabric up at the base, and secure it with a rubber band or twine to create a wide brim. Hang your makeshift lantern indoors or outside – the lantern effect shines through, no matter how jury-rigged it may be.

Embrace Your Inner Maker

With these DIY lighting ideas, you have no excuse not to brighten up (or darken, depending on the project) any space you desire. From repurposed pendant lights to mason jar illuminance, think of these projects as more than meets the eye, and let your creativity take center stage.
