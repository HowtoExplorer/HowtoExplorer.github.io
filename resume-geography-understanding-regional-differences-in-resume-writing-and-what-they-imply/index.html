Resume Geography: Understanding Regional Differences in Resume Writing and What They Imply


Resume writing is an art that can be significantly influenced by geographic location. Much like language, culture, and etiquette vary from place to place, the conventions surrounding resume creation also have regional nuances. Here, we will delve into this fascinating aspect of ‘resume geography’, exploring how regional differences impact resume writing and what these implications may be.

To begin with, let’s look at the United States, a melting pot of various cultural influences that can impact the way resumes are written.

The USA: Land of Opportunity or Land of Different Resume Writing Styles?

In the USA, different regions have their unique resume writing style. For example, resumes in the Northeast are generally more conservative, concise, and traditional. They often follow the chronological format and focus on work experience over education. Keywords here are minimal, and achievements are subtly incorporated.

Midwestern resumes, on the other hand, are known for their honesty, down-to-earth tone, and focus on teamwork. They tend to emphasize community involvement and share a strong connection to local networks. Expect modesty and a low-key approach in your Midwestern resume.

In contrast, resumes written in the West are often more creative, expressive, and bold. The emphasis on individuality and innovation can make these resumes stand out, often including a summary of qualifications and highlighting unique skills or achievements.

Southern resumes can be a blend of all the above styles, yet they often showcase a sophisticated balance of politeness and directness, not to mention a keen sense of communication – an essential quality in a resume.

When considering international job markets, the UK presents another unique landscape:

The United Kingdom: A Sea of Differences

British resume writing contrasts sharply from the American style. The first major difference is the lack of an ‘Objective’ section – resume all you want, but don’t aim too high, so the saying goes. Instead, a traditional British resume starts with a personal statement, summarizing both your professional goals and achievements.

British resumes also featureObjectName(indent(1), title(“References”), “References\n”): British resumes are less detailed about education and include a personal statement instead. Companies in the UK often requestreferences separately, so this section is optional or replaced by ‘References on Request’ at the end.

On a similar vein, Canadian resume writing bears close resemblance to the British approach. The personal statement reigns supreme, and so does keeping the resume concise, reflecting the polite and efficient ethos of the country.

Transitioning to the landscape of Asia, the resume-writing practices of China demonstrate a blend of European and American influences:

China: Embracing a Fusion of Styles

Chinese resumes often follow a chronological pattern, similar to the American style. However, they allocate more space to education and include a detailed work history. Moreover, Chinese resumes use extensive networks and connections, a vital component of recruitment in the country.

In Japan, resumes, called “ryaku,” take on a more organized structure, placing education before work experience. Due to the high emphasis on collectivism, long-term career goals, team achievements, and personal characteristics are common elements in a Japanese resume.

The Far East: A League of Their Own

South Korean and Hong Kong resumes also have their idiosyncrasies. For instance, South Korean resumes often begin with a brief description of your professional objectives followed by qualifications and work experiences. In Hong Kong, however, locals often combine Western formats with Asian elements, such as heavy description of educational background and explicit mention of language skills.

Conclusion: The World is Your Oyster

Understanding the regional differences in resume writing can be crucial for anyone aspiring to work across borders. Although there may not be a one-size-fits-all approach to crafting the perfect resume, being aware of the nuances helps one navigate the various job markets better. So, when preparing for your next global job hunt, remember to research and tailor your resume to the geographic location, because remember, every country has its own unique resume piece de résistance.
