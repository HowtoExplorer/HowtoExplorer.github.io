The 5-Minute Reboot: How to Impress Interviewers With Your Closing Statement


As the clock winds down, and your interview is nearing its completion, you’ve got only one more chance to leave a lasting impression - the closing statement. This is the moment when you can shine and set yourself apart from other candidates. Follow this five-step process to reboot your interview and create a memorable closing statement.

1. Summarize Your Key Points

Before diving into your closing statement, take a few moments to collect your thoughts and reflect on the interview. Briefly summarize the key points you’ve made throughout the conversation. This helps to reinforce your qualifications and reminds the interviewer of your strengths.

  • “To recap my qualifications, I have extensive experience in [industry or role] and a strong track record of [relevant achievements].”

  • “If I may, I’d also like to address the Q about [specific concern or question] I think I can provide valuable insights on that topic.”

Remember to keep your summary concise and avoid repeating everything you’ve said. The goal is to remind the interviewer why you’re the ideal candidate without wasting their time.

2. Offer Additional Value

Suggest how you can contribute to the organization beyond what you’ve already discussed. Show your enthusiasm for the position and the company.

  • “I’m excited about the prospect of contributing to [company mission or goal] and would relish the opportunity to work with such a talented team.”

  • “In addition to my professional skills, I believe my [relevant personal quality or experience] would further enhance our team dynamics.”

By demonstrating your eagerness to grow and contribute, you demonstrate your commitment and passion for the role.

3. Express Gratitude

Express your appreciation to the interviewer for their time and consideration. This simple gesture helps to leave a positive impression and highlights your professionalism.

  • “I would like to thank you for the opportunity to discuss my qualifications. Your questions have given me the chance to elaborate on my experiences.”

  • “I appreciate the time you’ve taken to meet with me today and learn more about my background.”

Gratitude can set you apart from other candidates and leaves a lasting impression.

4. Request Feedback

At the end of your closing statement, ask if it’s okay to provide feedback on the interview process. This shows your genuine interest in the role and prompts the interviewer to offer constructive criticism.

  • “Is it all right if I provide some feedback on how this interview has gone? I believe it can help me improve and grow in future opportunities.”

  • “I do have a few questions about the next steps in the interview process, if that’s okay?”

By requesting feedback, you signal your dedication to continuous improvement and a willingness to learn.

5. Show Enthusiasm for Following Up

Assure the interviewer that you’re open to proceeding to the next steps in the process and that you’re genuinely enthusiastic about the opportunity.

  • “I am excited to receive more information on the next steps in the process. If you need any further information from me, please don’t hesitate to reach out.”

  • “I look forward to hearing from you about the next steps and learning more about this exciting opportunity.”

By expressing your enthusiasm for moving forward, you show genuine interest in the position and the organization.

Closing statements can be your footprints out of the interview arena. Give it a reboot using these five strategies and prove that you deserve the role. Your next dream job may be just a few well-chosen words away.
