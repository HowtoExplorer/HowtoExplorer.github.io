The Ultimate Guide to Vegan Baking: Substitutes, Techniques, and Recipes


Vegan baking is more popular than ever before, with dietary restrictions and lifestyle choices spurring people to look for plant-based alternatives. If you’re looking to make the switch to vegan baking or simply want to experiment with new ingredients, this guide is for you! We’ll explore some fantastic substitutes, handy techniques, and delicious recipes to help you add a vegan twist to your usual baking repertoire.

The Basics of Vegan Baking

Before diving into recipes and techniques, it’s essential to familiarize yourself with the fundamentals of vegan baking. The main goal is to substitute animal-derived ingredients with plant-based alternatives. Common items you may be tempted to leave in your pantry include eggs, dairy, and honey. Don’t worry; there are plenty of alternatives to ensure your baked goods are just as deliciously irresistible as their traditional counterparts!

Egg Substitutes

Eggs play a key role in baking, acting as binders, leavening agents, and emulsifiers. Fortunately, there are several healthy alternatives to choose from:

  1. Flaxseeds and Chia Seeds: Makes a fantastic egg substitute. Combine 1 tbsp of ground flaxseeds or chia seeds with 3 tbsp of water and let it sit for a few minutes to create a “flax egg.” This gel-like substance mimics the binding and emulsifying properties of eggs.

  2. Bananas and Applesauce: These moist and subtly sweet fruits can add both moisture and sweetness to your baked goods while offering binding power. Use half a mashed banana or 1/4 cup of unsweetened applesauce to replace one egg.

  3. Silken Tofu: This creamy ingredient works well in vegan cakes and muffins. Add 1/4 cup of blended silken tofu for every egg you’re replacing.

  4. Vinegar and Baking Powder: Combine 1 tbsp of vinegar (apple cider or white) with 1 tsp of baking powder to replace one egg.

Dairy Substitutes

Finding dairy-free alternatives for milk, yogurt, and butter makes the transition to vegan baking a breeze. Here’s what you can use:

  1. Plant-Based Milks: Unsweetened almond, soy, oat, and coconut milks are great milk alternatives in baking. Choose your favorite to replace cow’s milk on a 1:1 basis.

  2. Vegan Yogurt: Use store-bought or homemade vegan yogurt to replace traditional dairy yogurt. Cashew yogurt is a favorite for its creamy texture.

  3. Vegan Butter: Brands like Earth Balance and Miyoko’s Kitchen offer vegan butter alternatives that can be substituted for dairy butter at a 1:1 ratio.

Sweeteners and Additives

Traditional baked goods often rely on ingredients like honey, sugar, and gelatin.

  1. Maple Syrup and Agave Nectar: Both make excellent vegan sweeteners, offering a nuanced sweetness and a hint of flavor that goes well in a variety of recipes.

  2. Coconut Sugar: A slightly nutritious substitute for white sugar, coconut sugar boasts a low glycemic index and lends itself well to caramelization in baking.

  3. Aquafaba: This might sound unfamiliar, but aquafaba (the liquid from canned beans) is a fantastic substitute for egg whites. It’s perfect for meringues, macarons, and marshmallows.

Techniques to Achieve Vegan Perfection

With the proper ingredients on hand, you can incorporate these techniques to ensure your vegan baked goods turn out just as expected:

  1. Increasing Moisture: Because egg yolks help retain moisture, replace each egg with extra wet ingredients like mashed bananas or applesauce.

  2. Using Leavening Agents: To help your vegan baked goods rise, increase the amount of baking powder or baking soda in your recipes. Be weary of over-leavening, as this can result in dry or crumbly cakes.

  3. Blending Flours: Gluten-free alternatives like oat flour and almond flour can be blended with regular flour to achieve the perfect texture.

  4. Mix your Dry Ingredients Separately: To prevent lumpiness in your final product, mix dry ingredients like flour, sugar, and leavening agents in a separate bowl before folding into the wet ingredients.

Vegan Baking Recipes to Try at Home

Now that you’re equipped with substitutes and techniques, it’s time to dive into some scrumptious vegan recipes:

1. Vegan Blueberry Muffins

This versatile recipe is a great starting point for your vegan baking journey. Blueberries lend themselves well to the chewiness of these muffins, while vegan yogurt and applesauce keep them delightfully moist.

  • Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 1/2 tsp baking powder, 1/2 cup vegan yogurt, 1/2 cup unsweetened applesauce, 1/2 cup unsweetened almond milk, 1/2 cup maple syrup, 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted, 1 tsp vanilla extract, 1/2 cup fresh or frozen blueberries.

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a 12-cup muffin tin.
    2. In a large mixing bowl, combine dry ingredients (flour, baking powder).
    3. In a separate bowl, whisk together wet ingredients (yogurt, applesauce, almond milk, maple syrup, melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract).
    4. Fold the wet mixture into the dry mixture until well combined. Gently fold in the blueberries.
    5. Divide batter evenly amongst the muffin cups and bake for 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow muffins to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

2. Vegan Carrot Cake

This spiced, moist cake showcases lovely swirls of carrot ribbons and features a dairy-free cream cheese frosting. It’s sure to impress both vegans and non-vegans alike.

  • Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour, 2 tsp baking soda, 1 tsp baking powder, 1 tsp salt, 1 tsp cinnamon, 1/2 tsp ginger, 1/2 tsp nutmeg, 2 cups grated carrot (about 4 medium carrots), 1/2 cup vegan butter, melted, 3/4 cup unsweetened applesauce, 3/4 cup maple syrup, 2 tsp vanilla extract, 1 cup unsweetened almond milk, vegan cream cheese frosting.

  • Instructions:

    1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C) and lightly grease a 9x13-inch baking pan.
    2. In a large mixing bowl, combine dry ingredients (flour, baking soda, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg).
    3. In a separate bowl, mix together grated carrot, melted vegan butter, applesauce, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and almond milk.
    4. Fold the carrot mixture into the dry mixture and blend well.
    5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread evenly.
    6. Bake for 35-40 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cake to cool completely in the pan.
    7. Once the cake is cooled, make your vegan cream cheese frosting by combining 8 oz of creamed vegan cream cheese, 1 cup of powdered sugar, and 1-2 tbsp of almond milk, then frost the top of your carrot cake.

Conclusion

With the right tools and ingredients, you too can create delightful vegan baked goods. Substitutes, techniques, and recipes have made it simple to transition into vegan baking. Get excited to explore and expand your baking repertoire by experimenting with these factors. It only takes one recipe to embark on a plant-powered journey of delightful indulgence. Sweeten your world with new vegan baking adventures!
