Handling Competency-Based Questions in a Remote Interview


Remote interviews have become increasingly popular in today’s digital world, allowing employers to connect with candidates from anywhere in the world. While the convenience of remote interviews is undeniable, it also presents unique challenges, especially when it comes to handling competency-based questions. In this blog post, we will explore effective strategies to tackle competency-based questions in a remote interview setting, ensuring you make a lasting impression on your potential employer.

Understand the Competency Framework

Before delving into the specifics of handling competency-based questions, it is crucial to have a clear understanding of the competency framework used by the organization. Competencies are a set of skills, behaviors, and attributes that are necessary for success in a particular role. Familiarize yourself with the key competencies required for the position you are applying for, as this will help you tailor your answers accordingly.

Prepare with STAR Method

The STAR method is a highly effective technique for structuring your responses to competency-based questions. STAR stands for Situation, Task, Action, and Result. When faced with a question, first, provide a brief overview of the situation or task you encountered. Then, explain the actions you took to address the situation, highlighting the specific skills and competencies you utilized. Finally, discuss the result or outcome of your actions, emphasizing the positive impact you made.

Practice, Practice, Practice

As with any interview, practice is key to success. Take the time to familiarize yourself with common competency-based questions and craft thoughtful, well-structured responses. Practice delivering your answers in a clear, concise manner, ensuring you highlight the relevant competencies and provide concrete examples to support your claims. Recording yourself during practice sessions can also be helpful in identifying areas for improvement.

Create a Professional Remote Interview Setup

A professional and distraction-free remote interview setup is essential to make a positive impression on your potential employer. Ensure you have a well-lit and quiet space for the interview. Test your audio and video equipment in advance to avoid any technical difficulties. Dress professionally, as you would for an in-person interview, to convey your seriousness and commitment to the opportunity.

Establish Rapport and Active Listening

In a remote interview, establishing rapport can be challenging, as you don’t have the advantage of in-person interaction. However, it is important to build a connection with your interviewer. Begin the interview by greeting them warmly and maintaining a friendly tone throughout. Demonstrate active listening by paraphrasing their questions and seeking clarification when necessary. This will show your genuine interest and engagement in the conversation.

Demonstrate Adaptability and Remote Work Skills

Competency-based questions are often designed to assess your ability to adapt to different situations and work effectively in a remote environment. Showcase your adaptability by highlighting past experiences where you successfully navigated challenges and embraced change. Discuss your remote work skills, such as effective communication, time management, and self-motivation, to demonstrate your readiness for remote work.

Conclusion

Handling competency-based questions in a remote interview requires preparation, practice, and a clear understanding of the organization’s competency framework. By utilizing the STAR method, creating a professional remote interview setup, establishing rapport, and showcasing adaptability and remote work skills, you can confidently navigate competency-based questions and impress your potential employer. Remember to remain calm, focused, and authentic throughout the interview process. Good luck!
