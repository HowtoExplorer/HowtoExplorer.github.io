How to Write a Feature Article: A Step-by-Step Guide


Writing a compelling feature article is both an art and a skill. It offers a unique opportunity to explore topics in depth, weaving together facts, expertise, and stories to engage readers. However, crafting a feature article requires more than just a good idea—it demands structure, research, and creativity. Whether you’re an aspiring journalist, a blogger, or a content marketer, mastering the art of feature writing can help you captivate your audience. In this guide, we’ll break down the process step by step, guiding you from brainstorming to publishing.

Step 1: Understand the Purpose of a Feature Article

A feature article differs from a standard news report or opinion piece. Its main goal is to offer readers insight into a subject while holding their attention from start to finish. While news articles are based on immediacy and facts, feature articles focus on storytelling, analysis, and emotional resonance.

When you write a feature article, you should aim to:

  • Inform your readers by providing well-researched content.
  • Entertain by presenting the information in a relatable and engaging way.
  • Inspire, provoke thought, or elicit an emotional response.

Understanding this purpose will help frame the way you approach your writing.

Step 2: Choose a Compelling Topic

Your topic is the foundation of your feature article. To ensure that your piece draws readers, choose a subject that is timely, impactful, or deeply interesting. Great feature article topics often explore human interest stories, cultural trends, industry insights, or social issues.

Consider the following when brainstorming ideas:

  • What subjects are people talking about right now?
  • Which topics resonate with your target audience?
  • What personal experiences or connections can you draw from?
  • Is there a unique angle or underreported aspect of a popular topic?

For example, instead of a generic piece about climate change, you could focus on the inspiring efforts of a specific activist or highlight innovative solutions being implemented in certain communities.

Step 3: Define Your Angle

Once you settle on a topic, the next step is to refine your angle. Your angle is the perspective or approach you’ll take on the subject. It determines how you’ll structure your narrative, select your sources, and engage your readers.

To identify your angle, ask yourself questions like:

  • What makes this story unique?
  • What emotions, insights, or questions do I want my readers to walk away with?
  • How does this story connect with the bigger picture?

For instance, if your topic is about the benefits of remote work, your angle might focus on how remote work is transforming small-town economies. By narrowing your focus, you can offer depth rather than simple breadth.

Step 4: Research Thoroughly

Research is the backbone of every well-written feature article. Strong research ensures your article is accurate, credible, and comprehensive. Take the time to gather information from a variety of sources, including:

  • Books, journals, and academic studies.
  • Industry reports or survey data.
  • Interviews with subject matter experts or individuals with personal stories.

Additionally, look into contrasting perspectives to enrich your content. A well-balanced feature highlights multiple sides of a topic without losing focus on your chosen angle.

While researching:

  • Keep detailed notes, paying attention to direct quotes and statistics.
  • Bookmark or organize your research, ensuring you can cite your sources.
  • Stay critical and cross-check your facts to avoid misinformation.

Step 5: Plan Your Structure

Feature articles thrive on a coherent structure that takes readers on a journey. A haphazard presentation of facts can quickly lose your audience’s interest, no matter how intriguing the topic is. To avoid this, plan the framework of your article before diving into the first draft.

A typical structure for a feature article includes:

1. The Lead

The lead, or opening, is your hook. Whether it’s an anecdote, shocking statistic, or intriguing question, your introduction should immediately grab the reader’s attention and set the tone for the article. For example:
“For years, community libraries faced the shadow of obsolescence. But in a remote Alaskan town, a tiny bookmobile is changing lives—one story at a time.”

2. The Nut Graph

Following the lead, the nut graph explains why this story matters. In one to two paragraphs, outline the article’s purpose, context, and main points. This section serves as your roadmap, helping readers understand what they’ll gain from reading further.

3. The Body

The body is where your narrative unfolds. You can break this section into thematic subsections, each exploring a key point or angle. Use vivid descriptions, real-life examples, and data to build your story. Keep sentences and paragraphs concise, ensuring every detail adds value.

4. The Conclusion

A strong conclusion solidifies your key takeaway, offering readers a sense of closure or action. Whether it’s a thoughtful reflection, a call to action, or a hint of optimism, ensure your conclusion leaves a lasting impression.

Step 6: Develop a Unique Writing Style

Feature articles are creative pieces, and your writing style significantly impacts how readers perceive your work. To make your article stand out:

  • Use storytelling techniques like vivid imagery, metaphors, and anecdotes to bring your narrative to life.
  • Write in an active voice, avoiding overly complex or formal phrases.
  • Balance professionalism with a conversational tone to connect with your audience.
  • Avoid jargon unless your audience is industry-specific, and simplify concepts without losing nuance.

As you draft, don’t underestimate the power of pacing. Vary your sentence lengths to create rhythm, emphasizing key points with shorter statements.

Step 7: Conduct Interviews

Interviews are an essential component of feature articles, as they add authenticity and depth to your narrative. While research provides a factual base, interviews give your article a human touch, offering new perspectives and insights.

Here are some interview tips:

  • Prepare thoughtful, open-ended questions that encourage detailed answers.
  • Record the conversation (with permission) to ensure you capture quotes accurately.
  • Incorporate direct quotes carefully into your article, ensuring they add value and complement your narrative.

Remember, interviews aren’t just about collecting information—they’re about discovering stories and voices that resonate.

Step 8: Edit and Refine

Once your first draft is complete, it’s time to edit. Writing is rewriting, and this is where your article truly takes shape. During the editing process:

  • Check for clarity and coherence. Does your narrative flow? Are your ideas presented logically?
  • Eliminate redundancies and tighten your sentences.
  • Ensure your facts, data, and quotes are accurate.
  • Proofread for spelling, grammar, and punctuation errors.
  • Get feedback from peers, mentors, or editors to identify areas for improvement.

Polish your article until every word serves a purpose and contributes to the story.

Step 9: Develop an Engaging Headline and Subheadings

Your headline and subheadings are crucial for grabbing reader attention and improving the article’s readability. A compelling headline should be concise, intriguing, and reflective of your article’s angle. Subheadings, on the other hand, break the article into digestible sections, guiding readers through the content.

For example:

  • Headline: “The Silent Revolution: How Remote Work is Reviving America’s Small Towns”
  • Subheadings: “From Bustling Cities to Quiet Suburbs,” “A Catalyst for Economic Growth,” “Challenges Facing Remote Communities.”

Step 10: Optimize for Readability and Web Performance

If your feature article is intended for an online platform, optimizing it for search engines and readability is essential. Incorporate relevant keywords naturally throughout the article, use a clean and scannable layout, and include relevant links to reputable sources.

Some tips for enhancing SEO and user engagement:

  • Include short paragraphs and clear subheadings.
  • Use bullet points or numbered lists where appropriate.
  • Add hyperlinks to related content or credible references.
  • End with a call to action that invites engagement, whether it’s a social share, comment, or further reading.

Step 11: Publish and Promote

Once your feature article is finalized, it’s time to publish and share it with the world. Don’t just rely on the platform to generate traffic—promote your article actively. Share it on social media, email newsletters, or relevant online communities. Engage with readers by responding to comments or questions.

Final Thoughts

Writing a feature article can be a rewarding endeavor that allows you to explore fascinating topics while connecting with readers on a deeper level. By following these steps—choosing a compelling topic, refining your angle, conducting thorough research, and crafting a well-structured narrative—you can develop a piece that captivates and informs. Remember, the key to a successful feature lies in its ability to tell a story that lingers in the minds of your audience long after they’ve read the final word. Keep practicing, experimenting, and perfecting your craft, and your articles will leave an enduring impact.
