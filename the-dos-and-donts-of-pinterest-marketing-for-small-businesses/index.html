The Dos and Don'ts of Pinterest Marketing for Small Businesses


Pinterest has emerged as a powerful marketing platform for small businesses, allowing them to showcase their products and services to a vast audience. With over 300 million users worldwide, Pinterest offers a unique opportunity to connect with potential customers and drive traffic to your website. However, like any marketing strategy, there are certain dos and don’ts that small businesses should keep in mind to make the most of their Pinterest marketing efforts. In this article, we will explore the best practices and pitfalls to avoid when using Pinterest for small business marketing.

The Dos of Pinterest Marketing

1. Create a business account

To effectively market your small business on Pinterest, it’s crucial to create a dedicated business account. This will provide you with access to valuable analytics and advertising features, allowing you to track the performance of your pins and make data-driven decisions. Additionally, a business account enables you to showcase your brand’s logo and website, lending credibility to your profile.

2. Optimize your profile

Make sure your Pinterest profile is fully optimized to attract your target audience. Use relevant keywords in your profile description and include a link to your website. Use a high-quality, visually appealing profile picture that represents your brand. Remember, first impressions matter, and a well-optimized profile can significantly increase your chances of attracting followers and potential customers.

3. Create compelling visuals

Pinterest is a visual platform, so it’s essential to create eye-catching and engaging visuals for your pins. Use high-resolution images that reflect your brand’s aesthetic and style. Incorporate your brand colors and logo to maintain consistency across your pins. Additionally, consider creating infographics, step-by-step guides, and tutorials to provide value to your audience. The more visually appealing and informative your pins are, the more likely they are to be saved and shared.

4. Pin regularly and consistently

Consistency is key when it comes to Pinterest marketing. Aim to pin regularly to keep your profile active and maintain engagement with your audience. Develop a content schedule and plan ahead to ensure a steady stream of fresh pins. However, avoid spamming your followers with too many pins at once. Instead, spread them out throughout the day or week to maximize visibility and reach.

5. Join group boards and engage with others

Pinterest group boards can be a valuable tool for small businesses to expand their reach and gain exposure to a wider audience. Look for relevant group boards in your niche and request to join them. Once you’re a member, actively engage with others by repinning and commenting on their content. Building relationships with fellow pinners can increase your visibility and attract new followers to your profile.

The Don’ts of Pinterest Marketing

1. Don’t neglect Pinterest SEO

Optimizing your pins for search engines is crucial for increasing their visibility and driving organic traffic to your website. Use relevant keywords in your pin titles, descriptions, and alt text. Research popular keywords in your industry and incorporate them naturally into your content. However, avoid keyword stuffing, as it can negatively impact your SEO efforts.

2. Don’t ignore Pinterest analytics

Pinterest provides valuable analytics that can help you understand your audience, track the performance of your pins, and identify trends. Regularly review your analytics to gain insights into which pins are resonating with your audience and adjust your strategy accordingly. Use this data to refine your content and make informed decisions about future pinning.

3. Don’t disregard Pinterest etiquette

Pinterest has its own set of etiquette rules that users are expected to follow. Avoid spammy behavior such as repeatedly pinning the same content or mass following and unfollowing other users. Respect copyright laws and always give credit to the original source of the content you pin. Engage with other pinners in a genuine and meaningful way, focusing on building relationships rather than just self-promotion.

4. Don’t overlook Pinterest trends

Stay up-to-date with the latest Pinterest trends to capitalize on popular topics and maximize your reach. Monitor popular categories and keywords to identify emerging trends that align with your brand. Creating content around trending topics can help you attract a larger audience and increase the chances of your pins being discovered and shared.

5. Don’t underestimate the power of Pinterest advertising

While organic reach is essential on Pinterest, don’t overlook the benefits of Pinterest advertising. Promoted pins can help you reach a larger audience and drive targeted traffic to your website. Experiment with different ad formats and targeting options to find the strategies that work best for your small business.

In conclusion, Pinterest marketing can be a game-changer for small businesses looking to increase brand visibility and drive website traffic. By following the dos and avoiding the don’ts outlined in this article, you can maximize the effectiveness of your Pinterest marketing efforts and achieve tangible results. Remember to stay consistent, engage with your audience, and keep up with the latest trends to stay ahead of the competition. Happy pinning!
