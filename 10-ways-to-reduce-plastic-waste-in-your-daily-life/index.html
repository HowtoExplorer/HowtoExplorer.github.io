10 Ways to Reduce Plastic Waste in Your Daily Life


We’re all aware of the plastic problem we face today. From the the beaches to the oceans, every nook and cranny, plastic has infiltrated every aspect of our lives. It is a material that is both versatile and indomitable; it is found in packaging, bottles, bags, and much more. The average person uses about 169 pounds of plastic per year, which ends up in our ecosystems, causing harm to wildlife, polluting the natural world. It’s high time we took charge and reduced our plastic waste! Let’s discuss 10 ways you can personally decrease the amount of plastic you use.

  1. Choose reusable bags Whenever you go shopping, avoid single-use plastic bags. Keep a collection of reusable bags in your car, by the door, or wherever you usually pick up shopping bags. Not only do they come in various attractive designs, but they can also carry much more weight than their single-use counterparts. A good reusable bag can last for years, negating the need for hundreds of plastic bags.

  2. Say no to plastic straws and cutlery In drinking establishments, straws and cutlery are often provided with no thought for the future. If you’re heading to a restaurant or bar, ask for a paper or bamboo straw instead. If you’re at home, replace your plastic cutlery with bamboo or stainless steel. These items are just as functional as plastic, but can be washed, reused, and in some cases, recycled.

  3. Use a refillable bottle Disposing of plastic water bottles is a massive problem. Carrying your own reusable bottle saves money, keeps plastic out of landfills, and ensures that you always have fresh, clean water. Stainless steel and glass bottles are ideal. Remember to clean them regularly to maintain hygiene.

  4. Pack your lunch in reusable containers If you’re taking lunch to work or school, swap your plastic containers and ziplock bags for glass, stainless steel, or BPA-free alternatives. These containers are more durable, reusable, and less likely to break and send plastic to the landfill.

  5. Choose natural cleaners You may not think about your cleaning products, but many contain plastic microbeads that are dangerous to the environment. Use natural alternatives instead. Baking soda and vinegar can clean almost anything and are completely safe for the environment.

  6. Buy in bulk Buying in bulk saves you money and helps the environment. When you purchase what you need from the bulk bins, you are avoiding the excess packaging of pre-packaged goods. Consider shopping at a store that allows you to bring your own containers for items like nuts, grains, and spices.

  7. Use bars of soap and shampoo Bar soap and shampoo are environmentally friendly alternatives to liquid soap or body wash. Not only do they eliminate the need for plastic bottles, but they require less energy to produce as well.

  8. Choose non-plastic packaged foods Many snacks now come in paper or biodegradable packaging. Opt for these snacks over their plastic-covered counterparts. When possible, buy locally made snacks since they often have less packaging than large corporation snacks.

  9. Recycle as much as possible Even with our best efforts, some plastic is inevitable. Proper recycling helps alleviate some of the damage. Learn your local recycling guidelines and abide by them. Some plastics cannot be recycled but others, like bottles, are a great start.

  10. Support environmentally-friendly legislation It’s not up to your personal action alone to completely cure our plastic addiction. Support legislation that advocates for the reduction of plastic waste, such as bans on plastic bags and single-use items. When washed out, these gains can result in meaningful change.

Conclusion: Our environment needs our all-out effort to preserve it, and this includes reducing our plastic waste. Adopting a few easy changes to our daily lives can significantly impact the environment. Remember to stick to the changes you’ve made as they can cause a Copernican revolution in efforts to save our planet. Together we can turn the Earth into a greener, cleaner place for all life.
