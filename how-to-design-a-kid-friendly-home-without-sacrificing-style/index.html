How to Design a Kid-Friendly Home Without Sacrificing Style


Creating a home that is both functional and visually appealing is a dueling act, especially when bringing up kids. You want to strike a balance between design aesthetics and practicality. This paradox is a universal challenge faced by many parents. To satisfy both young home dwellers and design enthusiasts, consider investing in timeless design pieces and materials that grow with your family. By combining these fundamentals with gentle decorating principles, you can carve out a space that fits multiple personalities and ages. Building Blocks of Kid-Friendly Design To create a space that effectively combines both style and functionality for kids, several components must be in place. Understanding these components will guide your decisions as you embark on designing your space. Separate Living Spaces Opting for separate living spaces allows for individualized environments within a broader shared space. This configuration reduces competition and the inevitable stress associated with speaking areas of residence. Homeowners can accomplish this by purchasing an additional living area, subdividing existing ones, or compose architectural styles with separate, quieter areas.
