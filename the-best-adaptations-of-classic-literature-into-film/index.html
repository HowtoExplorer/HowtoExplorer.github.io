The epic musical, starring Hugh Jackman, Anne Hathaway, and Russell Crowe, transported viewers to 19th-century France, where the struggle for redemption and hope unfolds. The film's universality and grandeur brought Victor Hugo's sprawling novel to a new generation, emphasizing the importance of fighting for justice and compassion in the face of adversity. Hugo's revolutionary themes of social justice and revolution are timeless, resonating with contemporary audiences. The cinematic adaptation beautifully captures the drama, heartbreak, and triumph of the original novel, elevating it to new heights. This version stands as a testament to the timelessness of Hugo's masterpiece, as well as the power of collective movement and resistance.
