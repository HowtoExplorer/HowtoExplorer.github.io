First Resume: The Ultimate Guide to Starting Your Job Application Journey


Starting your job application journey with your very first resume can be an intimidating task. Perhaps you feel overwhelmed by the prospect of presenting the unique blend of your skills, experiences, and aspirations to potential employers. Fear not, for we’re here to illuminate the process for you in creating the ultimate first resume. Let’s dive in!

The Basics:structuring your resume

1. Contact Information

First and foremost, every resume should begin with the applicant’s contact information. This includes your full name, phone number, e-mail address, and (optionally) your city and state. Keep your contact information up-to-date and professional, as this gives hiring managers a first impression of you.

2. Objective or Summary

For a first resume, it can be effective to include a brief objective statement at the top. This statement should clearly outline your professional goals, aligning with your target job. Alternatively, a summary can present your skills and experiences concisely. Remember to tailor your objective or summary to the specific job or industry you’re interested in.

3. Experience

Organize your work experience in reverse chronological order, starting with the most recent. For first-time job seekers, this might only encompass volunteer work, shadowing experiences, internships, or after-school jobs. Even in these cases, focus on describing your responsibilities and the impact you made. Use action verbs and try to quantify achievements where possible.

4. Education

List your highest degree first, starting with the most recent. Clearly state the degree, institution, and graduation year. For high school students or recent graduates, this might include your GPA, class rank, and any relevant honors. Showcase your academic achievements, such as academic clubs, research projects, or awards.

5. Skills and Achievements

Highlight any relevant skills that you’ve acquired through employment, volunteering, education, or personal interests. This can include technical or language skills, proficiency in software or computer programs, and any knowledge of foreign languages.Tailor your skill list to match the job you’re applying for.

6. Hobbies and Interests

While not essential for a first resume, including a section for hobbies and interests can help to demonstrate a well-rounded candidate. Choose activities that complement your professional goals or showcase transferable skills. For example, if you attained leadership experience in a club, consider including this information.

Additional Tips for That Winning Resume

1. Use a Professional Design

Your resume should be clear, concise and professional looking. Did you know that employers spend an average of six seconds scanning a resume? This means your design should make important details stand out. Stick to a clean and consistent formatting throughout the document. Use bullet points to separate experiences and skills, and avoid fancy fonts or quirky color schemes.

2. Keep It Brief

When crafting your first resume, aim to keep it to a single page. Highlight essential information and avoid going into excessive detail on each experience. This rule can be flexible as you gain more experience, but initially, focus on presenting a straightforward and digestible resume.

3. Customization is Key

When you’re just starting out, you might have a broader range of job interests, but it’s still important to customize your resume for each job application. Tailor the skills, experiences, and objectives and summarises áreas to the specific job posting. Employers value a candidate who demonstrates knowledge of their company and industry.

4. Proofread and Edit

Typographical errors and grammatical mistakes can make a bad first impression. So, proofread your resume at least twice, and ideally ask someone else to review it as well. Sometimes, taking a break for some time and then coming back to it ensures you have fresh eyes for editing.

The Next Steps in Your Job Application Journey

Congratulations! You’ve now got the ultimate guide for creating a first resume. Now, we suggest experimenting by creating several versions tailored to different industries or jobs. Analyze each one to gauge which one gets the best results upon submitting it. Building a strong resume is a necessity, but so too is adapting and learning from each experience.

Beyond resume targets, remember to utilize LinkedIn, conduct informational interviews, and research Industrial-Organizational Psychology. Keep learning about various industries to figure out the best roles that resonate with your skills and interests.

Indeed, the First Resume is just the beginning, but it can significantly lay out your future growth. So go out there, and paint an engaging picture of the professional you with your first resume!
