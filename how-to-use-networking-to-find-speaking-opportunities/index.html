How to Use Networking to Find Speaking Opportunities


Networking is an essential skill for personal and professional growth. Whether you’re an established expert or just starting in your career, building a solid network can open doors to exciting opportunities, including speaking engagements. If you’ve been dreaming of taking the stage at conferences, webinars, or workshops, leveraging networking can be one of the most effective ways to unlock these speaking opportunities.

In this guide, we’ll walk through how you can use networking to find speaking engagements and make your voice heard.

Why Networking Matters for Speaking Opportunities

Networking is more than just collecting business cards or connecting on LinkedIn. It’s about building meaningful relationships and leveraging those connections to create opportunities. Speaking engagements often come from trusted recommendations, insider information, and relationships that go beyond the surface level.

Many speaking opportunities are not advertised publicly. Instead, event organizers and companies often rely on their networks to find speakers they can trust. The more people you know, and the more you engage with them, the better your chances are of being invited to speak.

The Power of Personal Connections

When someone in your network recommends you as a speaker, it carries more weight than simply applying to an open call for speakers. People are more likely to recommend someone they know and trust. By networking effectively, you can ensure that you’re on the radar of the people who can open those doors for you.

Steps to Leverage Networking for Speaking Opportunities

Now that we understand why networking is important, let’s break down the steps you can take to start using your network to find speaking opportunities.

1. Attend Industry Events

The most obvious way to begin networking for speaking opportunities is by attending industry events. Whether it’s conferences, conventions, or smaller networking meetups, being present at these events allows you to meet key people in your field.

Take the time to introduce yourself to event organizers, speakers, and other attendees. Be genuinely interested in their work and create a lasting impression. It’s not enough to just exchange pleasantries. Engage in meaningful conversations about your expertise and express your interest in speaking at future events.

2. Use Social Media to Build Connections

Social media is one of the most powerful tools for networking in today’s world. Platforms like LinkedIn, Twitter, and Facebook can connect you with a wide audience of professionals, event organizers, and fellow speakers. But it’s important to use these platforms strategically.

Here’s how to use social media for speaking opportunities:

  • Create valuable content: Share articles, videos, and insights related to your area of expertise. When you post regularly, people begin to see you as a thought leader in your field, which can attract speaking invites.
  • Engage with others: Comment on posts, join relevant groups, and actively engage in conversations. When people see you interacting with other influencers and speakers, they’ll be more likely to think of you for future opportunities.
  • Reach out directly: Don’t be afraid to message event organizers and conference planners. Send a personalized message expressing your interest in speaking at their event. Keep it brief but highlight why you’re the right person for the job.

3. Join Professional Associations

Being a member of industry-specific associations is a great way to expand your network and increase your visibility. These associations often have connections to conferences, workshops, and other events where speakers are needed.

Associations frequently hold events where members can present their ideas or expertise. Get involved with these organizations, attend meetings, and volunteer for committees. By being an active member, you can position yourself as a potential speaker for their upcoming events.

4. Volunteer for Speaking Roles

One way to get your foot in the door is by volunteering to speak at local events, schools, or non-profit organizations. Even if these aren’t high-profile events, they provide excellent opportunities to practice speaking in front of an audience and build your portfolio.

When you volunteer, you’ll gain valuable experience and also develop connections with those who may recommend you for higher-profile speaking engagements down the line. People often remember those who contribute without expecting anything in return.

5. Build Relationships with Event Organizers

Event organizers are key players in the speaking engagement process. By networking with them, you’ll gain insight into how they choose speakers and what types of topics they’re looking for.

Be patient and approach event organizers in a way that demonstrates your value. You might start by attending their events and offering feedback or assisting with logistics. Over time, as you build a relationship, you can express your interest in speaking at future events.

6. Join Online Communities and Forums

The digital age has made it easier than ever to network from the comfort of your own home. Join online communities and forums where professionals in your field gather. Websites like Reddit, Quora, and specialized forums for your industry can be great places to interact with others, answer questions, and share your knowledge.

By becoming an active member in these online spaces, you’ll position yourself as an authority in your field. Organizers who are looking for speakers often browse these communities to find experts who are engaged and knowledgeable.

7. Speak at Smaller Events to Build Momentum

You don’t need to start with big conferences. Look for smaller events, such as local meetups or webinars, where you can speak. These events offer a low-pressure environment for honing your speaking skills while still allowing you to build a solid reputation.

Additionally, smaller events are often more accessible in terms of networking opportunities. You may meet other speakers and organizers who can connect you with larger events in the future.

8. Create a Speaker Portfolio

Having a professional speaker portfolio is essential when you’re looking to book speaking engagements. This portfolio should include:

  • A bio: A short, engaging biography that highlights your expertise and experience.
  • Past speaking engagements: If you’ve spoken at events before, include links to videos or recordings. If you haven’t spoken yet, create a demo video to showcase your speaking abilities.
  • Testimonials: If possible, include testimonials from past events or colleagues who can vouch for your speaking abilities.
  • Topics you speak on: List the areas you can cover in your speaking engagements. Be specific about the topics you’re most passionate about and most knowledgeable in.

Having a polished speaker portfolio ready will help you appear more professional when networking and reaching out to event organizers.

9. Ask for Referrals

Once you’ve started speaking at a few events and making a name for yourself, don’t hesitate to ask for referrals. After an event, reach out to the event organizer and thank them for the opportunity. You can also ask them if they know anyone who might be looking for a speaker on your topic.

Word-of-mouth referrals are one of the best ways to expand your network and land more speaking gigs. People trust recommendations from their colleagues and peers, so asking for referrals can dramatically increase your chances of landing future opportunities.

10. Follow Up and Stay in Touch

Building relationships takes time, so it’s important to follow up after meeting new contacts and event organizers. Send a thank-you message, connect on LinkedIn, and stay engaged with their content. By staying in touch, you ensure that you remain top-of-mind when new speaking opportunities arise.

Regular follow-ups are a great way to maintain connections and reinforce your desire to speak at future events. Don’t be too pushy, but show genuine interest in their work and continue to engage with them.

11. Leverage Existing Relationships

Don’t overlook the power of your existing network. Many people you already know can help you land speaking engagements. Your friends, colleagues, mentors, and clients may have connections to event organizers or may be able to refer you to speaking opportunities in their own networks.

Be vocal about your desire to speak, and make it easy for your existing contacts to refer you. Keep them informed about the topics you’re passionate about and any recent speaking engagements you’ve had.

12. Stay Consistent

Networking takes time, so don’t expect instant results. Stay consistent in your efforts to network and build relationships. Continue attending events, engaging online, and following up with potential connections. Over time, your reputation will grow, and more speaking opportunities will come your way.

Final Thoughts

Using networking to find speaking opportunities is a gradual process, but it’s one of the most effective ways to build your public speaking career. By attending industry events, engaging on social media, joining professional organizations, and volunteering your time, you can increase your visibility and get your name on the radar of key decision-makers.

Remember, networking isn’t just about asking for something—it’s about building genuine relationships that provide mutual value. With patience and persistence, your network will help you find the speaking opportunities you’ve been seeking.

So, go ahead—start building your network today and watch as those speaking opportunities start to come your way!
