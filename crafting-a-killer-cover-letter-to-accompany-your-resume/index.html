Crafting a Killer Cover Letter to Accompany Your Resume


A cover letter is a crucial accessory to your resume, delving deeper into your capabilities as a professional and emphasizing your highly transferable soft skills. Equally important as a resume, the cover letter unfolds your industry experience, career goals, and other relevant details, setting you ahead of the competition.

The New Age Cover Letter: Unleash Your Creativity!

When it comes to creating the much-coveted cover letter, injecting creativity in your language is an eye-catching method. Instead of presenting mundane details like “I have worked in a similar role,” explore innovative linguistic techniques, such as “I effortlessly made pivotal contributions in a strikingly similar role.”

Employers are always on the lookout for creative problem-solvers, and adding some creative temperament to your cover letter screams, “I am that person!” Remember to balance this creativity, though - you don’t want to venture too far into the wacky or unprofessional.

Crafting the Perfect Opening Line

Your cover letter holds an opportunity to present your introductions in a more profound and personal manner. Skim through the employer’s company website for key phrases, and weave them seamlessly into your opening line.

For example:

“Your vegan bakery, [Company Name], caught my attention through the delicious displays of your website, and I’m excited to apply for the [Job Title] position.”

Or:

“As a fellow advocate for a more sustainable world, I was compelled to apply for the [Job Title] role at [Company Name], having seen the company’s drive toward renewable energy.”

By starting with a personal, industry-relevant statement, you set the stage for a custom-tailored application.

Tailor Your Letter to Address Each Employer’s Needs

While sending a generalized letter, peppered with universal phrases, to every employer, may allow you to multi-task, it seldom wins favor. Instead, address each employer’s specific needs in your cover letter.

When you understand the particular each company seeks, tailor your communication in identical fashion - this includes scanning each job listing for unique buzzwords and incorporating them bespoke-fit into your cover letter. This not only showcases understanding but also adds dedication.

Highlight Your Relevant Accomplishments

Remember, your cover letter acts as a route to display not merely your work history, but your achievements. Emphasize on your relevant accomplishments more than past work positions.

From saving the company funds, to achieving challenging targets, be sure to sprinkle your cover letter with accomplishment-heavy details. These statement should resonate with the position you are applying for and the company’s industry goals.

End With a Call-to-Action

Concluding your cover letter on a strong note with an invitation to engage further, such as indicating your upcoming phone call or readying for an in-person interview, can create a more personal impact.

Say something like, “I look forward to discussing my candidacy with you further and hope to hear from you soon for a phone or in-person interview.” This not only prompts the employer, but also conveys confidence and assertiveness, two traits that employers often find attractive.

In conclusion, crafting a killer cover letter accompanies your resume is an opportunity to stand out in a sea of applications. Don’t be afraid to show your personality and tailor your approach to the specific employer and job. Inject creativity into your writing, highlight your accomplishments, and end with a strong call-to-action. By doing so, your cover letter will provide a more personal, comprehensive insight into your professional capabilities, setting you apart in the job market.
