The Best Fitness Challenges to Try This Year


As the new year unfolds, many of us are looking for fresh ways to invigorate our fitness routines. Whether you’re a seasoned athlete or just starting your fitness journey, participating in fitness challenges can provide motivation, structure, and a sense of community. This year, we’ve compiled a list of the best fitness challenges to try, each designed to push your limits, enhance your skills, and keep you engaged.

1. 30-Day Plank Challenge

The plank is a fundamental exercise that strengthens your core, improves posture, and enhances overall stability. The 30-day plank challenge gradually increases the duration of your plank holds, starting from 20 seconds and culminating in a 5-minute hold by the end of the month.

How to Get Started:

  • Week 1: Hold a plank for 20 seconds, rest for 10 seconds, and repeat for 5 sets.
  • Week 2: Increase to 30 seconds, then 40 seconds by the end of the week.
  • Week 3: Progress to 1 minute, then 1 minute and 30 seconds.
  • Week 4: Aim for 2 minutes, 3 minutes, and finally 5 minutes.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens core muscles
  • Improves balance and stability
  • Enhances endurance

2. Couch to 5K

If you’re looking to get into running, the Couch to 5K program is a fantastic way to start. This challenge is designed for beginners and gradually builds your running stamina over the course of nine weeks.

How to Get Started:

  • Week 1: Alternate between 60 seconds of running and 90 seconds of walking for 20 minutes.
  • Week 2: Increase running time to 90 seconds and walking to 2 minutes.
  • Continue to increase running intervals until you can run for 30 minutes straight by the end of week 9.

Benefits:

  • Builds cardiovascular endurance
  • Promotes weight loss
  • Boosts mental health through endorphin release

3. 100 Push-Ups Challenge

The 100 push-ups challenge is a great way to build upper body strength and endurance. This challenge can be tailored to your fitness level, whether you can do one push-up or a hundred.

How to Get Started:

  • Week 1: Test your maximum push-ups in one go. Use this number to determine your daily goal.
  • Week 2: Increase your daily push-up count by 10% each day.
  • Week 3: Incorporate variations like incline or decline push-ups to target different muscle groups.
  • Week 4: Aim to complete 100 push-ups in one session by the end of the month.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens chest, shoulders, and triceps
  • Improves core stability
  • Enhances overall body strength

4. 30-Day Yoga Challenge

Yoga is not only a fantastic way to improve flexibility and strength, but it also promotes mental clarity and relaxation. A 30-day yoga challenge can help you establish a consistent practice and explore different styles.

How to Get Started:

  • Week 1: Start with 10-15 minutes of basic poses each day.
  • Week 2: Increase to 20-30 minutes, incorporating sun salutations and balance poses.
  • Week 3: Explore different styles such as Vinyasa, Hatha, or Yin yoga.
  • Week 4: Aim for a full hour of practice, focusing on breath and mindfulness.

Benefits:

  • Increases flexibility and balance
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Enhances mental focus

5. 21-Day Sugar Detox

While not a traditional fitness challenge, a sugar detox can significantly impact your overall health and fitness goals. This challenge focuses on eliminating added sugars from your diet for 21 days, helping you reset your cravings and improve your energy levels.

How to Get Started:

  • Week 1: Eliminate all sugary snacks and beverages. Focus on whole foods.
  • Week 2: Cut out processed foods and read labels to avoid hidden sugars.
  • Week 3: Experiment with natural sweeteners like stevia or monk fruit in moderation.

Benefits:

  • Reduces cravings for sugary foods
  • Improves energy levels and mood
  • Aids in weight loss and better digestion
