The Best Fitness Challenges to Try This Year


Every year brings fresh opportunities to prioritize health, upgrade routines, and discover new ways to stay motivated on the fitness journey. Whether you’re a seasoned athlete, a regular gym-goer, or just someone looking to kickstart a healthier lifestyle, fitness challenges can be a fun, effective structure to achieve your goals.

Fitness challenges not only help you set precise objectives but also create a sense of accomplishment when completed. They are designed to push boundaries, break monotony, and introduce excitement into your regimen. This year, why not shake things up and try a fitness challenge that will sharpen your mind, strengthen your body, and transform your habits? From strength-building tests to flexibility and mental discipline workouts, there’s something for everyone. Here are some of the best fitness challenges to try this year.

1. 30-Day Strength Training Challenge

Building muscle mass, improving stamina, and increasing overall strength can bring numerous benefits, from better posture to enhanced metabolism. A 30-day strength training challenge provides a structured plan to help target muscles across your entire body.

The key to this challenge is progression. It might start with simple bodyweight exercises like push-ups, planks, and squats, then gradually introduce weights, resistance bands, or more complex compound movements like deadlifts or snatches. Many challenges also include plenty of rest days, ensuring recovery and minimizing the risk of injury. What makes this so appealing is that it can be tailored to individual ability levels—beginners and advanced lifters alike can participate.

Make sure to include warm-ups and cool-downs, and keep track of your progress. Whether you’re working toward total pull-ups, bench press efficiency, or full-body strength, this challenge is an excellent place to begin.

2. Mindful Movement Challenge

Fitness isn’t just about physical endurance—it’s about connecting the mind and body. A mindful movement challenge blends physical activity with mindfulness techniques to help improve stress responses, enhance flexibility, and build concentration.

In this challenge, you might dedicate 20 to 30 minutes per day to yoga sequences, tai chi sessions, or other mind-body practices like Pilates. Regularly checking in with your breath and practicing gratitude during the workouts can reinforce mindfulness skills.

The beauty of this challenge is its adaptability. It can be catered toward anyone’s pace, making it perfect for those who have limited time but want to fit in grounded, intentional workouts into their routine.

3. Couch-to-5K Program

Running challenges are no longer reserved for marathoners or seasoned sprinters. The couch-to-5K program is arguably one of the most inclusive fitness endeavors available. This low-pressure plan is designed to help anyone—even those with zero running experience—comfortably complete a 5K race by the end of the program.

The progression typically spans 8-9 weeks, mixing walking with running to gradually build stamina. Most programs only require 20-30 minutes of activity three times a week, making it an excellent challenge if you have a busy schedule. And once you cross that 5K finish line, you’ll feel an incredible sense of personal achievement.

To spice things up, consider joining a community event or virtual race. The social component of running challenges often creates accountability while injecting some fun competition into the experience.

4. 75 Hard Challenge

If you’re searching for something that addresses both physical and mental toughness, the 75 Hard Challenge is a popular choice. However, it’s not for the faint of heart—it’s an intense program which tests your discipline across multiple areas of life over the course of 75 days.

The rules are strict: complete two 45-minute workouts a day (one must be outdoors), follow a nutrition plan, drink a gallon of water daily, read 10 pages of a personal development book, and take progress pictures daily. Slip-ups mean starting from Day 1.

While extreme, many participants swear by the transformational results. This challenge not only builds physical endurance but also sharpens mental resilience and cultivates self-discipline, making it powerful for personal growth.

5. Plank Challenge

Core strength is the foundation of many physical activities, helping improve balance, posture, and athletic performance. The plank challenge is a simple, effective way to build this vital area of fitness over a set period.

Typically lasting 30 days, the challenge involves holding a plank position for progressively longer time durations each day. Start with 20-30 seconds if you’re a beginner, and work your way up to achieving milestones of 2-3 minutes by the end. Advanced participants can incorporate variations like side planks, forearm planks, or plank push-ups.

Minimal equipment is needed, so this challenge is ideal for beginners or anyone looking for an easy, at-home core routine with maximum benefits.

6. Flexibility and Mobility Challenge

Flexibility challenges often get overlooked in favor of high-intensity or strength-focused goals, but they’re vital for overall fitness. Improved flexibility prevents injuries, reduces soreness, and supports functional movement.

A flexibility or mobility challenge might involve daily stretching, foam rolling, or short yoga sessions. Goals could include touching your toes, holding advanced poses, or even improving your squat depth. Apps and online programs with guided routines make this type of challenge accessible to anyone with a yoga mat.

Make it even more engaging by tracking progress with mobility benchmarks such as testing your functional range of motion every week. The improvements will show up not just during workouts but in everyday movements as well.

7. Outdoor Activity Streak

Nature is a powerful tool for enhancing mental and physical well-being, which is why an outdoor activity streak can be just what you need to reignite your passion for fitness. The idea is simple: spend a set amount of time outside every single day engaging in some form of physical activity.

Whether it’s a brisk walk in a nearby park, trail running, cycling, or even beach volleyball, this challenge adds variety while exposing you to fresh air and sunshine. The time requirement can be flexible based on individual schedules, with common goals ranging from 20 to 60 minutes daily.

By combining fitness with exposure to the outdoors, participants often find that their mood and energy levels consistently improve over time—two added bonuses beyond physical progress.

8. Step Count Challenge

The step count challenge is perfect for those who want to ease into fitness or stay active without committing to high-intensity routines. This challenge involves hitting a specific daily step goal, such as 10,000 steps, often tracked using smartwatches, phone apps, or pedometers.

What’s great about this program is that it motivates movement throughout the day instead of solely relying on structured workouts. Simple changes like taking the stairs, walking during lunch breaks, or pacing while on phone calls can help you hit goals. Many people also find friendly competition with peers or coworkers a compelling way to stay consistent.

9. 10-Minute HIIT Challenge

Short on time? A 10-minute high-intensity interval training (HIIT) challenge might be exactly what fits into your lifestyle. Known for its efficiency, HIIT alternates bursts of intense effort with rest or active recovery, providing a highly effective workout in minimal time.

This challenge might involve exercises like jumping jacks, burpees, or kettlebell swings in quick circuits, three to five times a week. Gradually increasing interval intensity or trying new moves can prevent plateaus.

It’s a myth that workouts must be lengthy to produce results. 10 minutes of focused effort can burn calories, build endurance, and even improve cardiovascular health.

Final Thoughts

The perfect fitness challenge isn’t one-size-fits-all—it’s about finding what excites and motivates you. Whether you’re trying to build strength, improve flexibility, or simply make exercise a consistent habit, there’s a challenge waiting for you. By stepping outside your comfort zone and committing to a structured plan, you’ll tap into renewed energy and witness your progress, both physically and mentally.

Fitness is a journey, not a sprint—so whichever challenge you choose, remember to listen to your body, respect your limits, and celebrate your victories, big and small. Dive in, commit, and make this year your strongest, healthiest yet.
