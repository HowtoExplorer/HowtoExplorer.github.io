Top 5 Sports to Try This Summer


Trying out new sports during the summer is a great way to stay active, enjoy the outdoors, and even discover hidden talents or interests. The long days and warm weather provide the perfect setting to explore activities that boost fitness, foster social connections, and offer a refreshing break from the usual routine. Whether you’re looking for a team sport, something adventurous, or an activity that provides tranquility, there’s an option to suit every interest. Here are the top five sports to consider trying this summer.

1. Stand-Up Paddleboarding (SUP)

Stand-up paddleboarding has quickly become one of the most popular water sports due to its versatility and accessibility. It’s a sport that can be as relaxing or as challenging as you want it to be.

Paddleboarding strengthens your core muscles as you balance on the board and engage your arms, shoulders, and back while paddling. It’s also an excellent cardiovascular workout, especially if you start picking up speed. For those looking for a peaceful connection with nature, SUP yoga is an emerging trend that combines the art of balancing with the calming benefits of yoga.

To get started, you’ll need a paddleboard, paddle, and a life jacket. Many rental places at lakes, rivers, and beaches offer beginner lessons. Whether cruising along tranquil waters or navigating small waves, paddleboarding allows you to explore new surroundings while getting a great full-body workout.

Pro Tip:

Try going out early in the morning or late in the evening when the water is calmer, especially if you’re a beginner. These times also tend to be less crowded, giving you a more enjoyable experience.

2. Beach Volleyball

Beach volleyball combines the thrill of an intense workout with the joy of playing under the sun on sandy shores. It’s one of the most loved sports globally and thrives during the summer.

This dynamic sport requires teamwork, agility, and quick reflexes. Playing in the sand adds an unexpected challenge—it’s harder to sprint or jump compared to indoor volleyball, so you’ll burn more calories while strengthening your lower body muscles. Plus, unlike some sports that require specialized training, beach volleyball is easy to pick up for beginners.

To start, all you need is a net, a volleyball, and a willing group of friends or family. Many public beaches also have set-up volleyball courts for impromptu games. It’s a friendly, engaging way to bond with others, but it also tests your strategic thinking and coordination.

Pro Tip:

Apply sunscreen generously and wear proper beach attire to stay protected from the sun, as matches can be long and intense.

3. Cycling

Cycling is a timeless activity that offers a variety of benefits for individuals of all ages and fitness levels. Whether you’re cruising along paved trails or tackling rugged mountain terrain, biking is a fantastic way to explore your local area or discover scenic routes you’ve never seen before.

Long cycling rides improve cardiovascular health, strengthen lower body muscles, and enhance stamina. It’s also an eco-friendly and low-impact exercise that’s gentle on the joints. For those seeking an added challenge, mountain biking provides an adrenaline rush as you navigate rough dirt trails, twisting descents, and steep climbs.

If you’re new to cycling, start with shorter rides on flat surfaces and gradually build your endurance. Invest in a good-quality helmet, and consider gloves for better grip and padded shorts for added comfort. With the growing number of bike paths and countryside trails, now is the perfect time to join the cycling community.

Pro Tip:

Plan your route in advance and stay hydrated by carrying a water bottle or hydration pack. Many popular cycling apps can help you track your route and progress.

4. Tennis

Tennis is a classic summer sport that combines physical exertion with strategic gameplay. Played both recreationally and competitively, it’s a great way to enjoy a fast-paced workout while working on agility, strength, and hand-eye coordination.

Tennis engages multiple muscle groups, including your core, arms, legs, and shoulders, while also improving balance and mental focus. It builds endurance and spontaneity as you pivot to meet the ball and strategize your next move against your opponent. For beginners, lessons from a coach or online tutorials can help you master the techniques of serving, volleying, and groundstrokes.

All you’ll need are a tennis racket, a few balls, and access to a court. Many public parks have free or affordable courts, making it easy to start. Whether played in singles or doubles, tennis is perfect for fostering friendly competition and can be enjoyed at nearly any age.

Pro Tip:

Wear supportive shoes designed for court sports to help protect your ankles and provide optimal grip during quick sprints or direction changes.

5. Trail Running

For those who love to combine fitness with adventure, trail running offers an invigorating escape from ordinary jogging routes. This outdoor sport is about more than just running—it’s experiencing the untouched beauty of nature while challenging your body on varied terrains.

Unlike jogging on flat surfaces, trail running involves navigating hills, rocky paths, and uneven ground, which activates stabilizing muscles you may not use as often. It enhances cardiovascular endurance, strengthens your lower body, and improves mental clarity as you stay attuned to the trail ahead.

To get started, choose trails that match your fitness level and build up to more challenging routes over time. All you need is a pair of trail-running shoes designed with extra grip and support. This is an excellent sport for those who enjoy solitude or meditative workouts, but it can also be a fun group activity if you bring along fellow adventurers.

Pro Tip:

Be aware of your surroundings and check weather conditions beforehand. A lightweight backpack with snacks, water, and a first-aid kit is always a smart addition for longer trails.

Wrapping It Up

Summer is an ideal time to embrace new sports that promote an active, healthy lifestyle while making the most of the beautiful weather. Incorporating activities like stand-up paddleboarding, beach volleyball, cycling, tennis, and trail running can help you effectively mix fitness with enjoyment. Beyond the physical benefits, each of these sports has its unique way of enriching your summer through social interaction, time spent outdoors, and the thrill of learning something new.

This summer, step outside your comfort zone, pick up some gear, and give one—or all—of these sports a try. It’s not just about staying active; it’s about creating lasting memories and discovering new passions that might stick with you long after the summer sun has set.
