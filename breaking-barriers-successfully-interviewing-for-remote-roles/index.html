Breaking Barriers: Successfully Interviewing for Remote Roles


Searching for remote jobs has actually never been easier. It is inspiring to see more and more organizations acknowledging the versatility of remote work and their employees. But, securing a remote role still calls for a serious set of skills. It requires job seekers to ace not one but multiple interviews, often in the comfort of their own homes. How, then, does one overcome such challenges and rise above with flying colors? Let’s dive into some guidelines that can help you break barriers while interviewing for remote roles.

Practicing Good Communication Is Key

Communication is the lifeline of any remote team, and it’s crucial in interviews too. While written communication is an important skill to master, a knack for effective verbal communication can take you a long way. Formerly, interviews were about showing how well you know the job and the team dynamics, but now, how effectively you communicate becomes equally essential.

Eye Contact and Body Language

We are programmed to associate interviews with face-to-face interactions, and in this case, that means regular video calls. Virtual communication removes the need for maintaining physical presence, but you’ll need to develop skills for effective video communication. Consciously or subconsciously, our body language and eye contact can convey confidence, engagement, and interest.

Choosing the Right Environment

Choosing the right environment is a part of nailing the technicalities of remote interviewing. A peaceful and well-lit environment can significantly calm your nerves, smoke away distractions and help you perform your best. Also, it can be very beneficial to have all relevant documents, including the job description and company profile, within reach.

Showing Your Relevant Experience

Though remote jobs have blurred the physical boundaries, they’ve created new ‘digital’ boundaries that require new rules to break. Start by preparing yourself to demonstrate your previous experience that matches the role, and be ready to explain how your skills can fit the new role.

Consistently Following Up

Consistent follow-up is another important aspect, as it shows your initiative and can help break barriers. When you show proactiveness by following up with the interviewer, you demonstrate your commitment towards the role, even before you’ve stepped into it.

Converting Your Resume to a Virtual Profile

Last but not the least, the traditional resume has converted into a virtual profile in the digital world. Having an online presence in the form of a LinkedIn profile, including work samples, portfolio and the ever-versatile video introductions can help you stand out during the virtual interviews.

Wrapping up, let’s remember that virtually interviewing for remote roles is a contemporary part of the job selection process these days. Tackling each of these steps will help you gradually get more comfortable and increase your chances of getting selected for the role.

Successful remote interviewing depends on your ability to adapt and overcome challenges thrown by this unique scenario. By following these guidelines, you’ll be well on your way to overcoming these challenges and securing your dream remote job. Good luck!
