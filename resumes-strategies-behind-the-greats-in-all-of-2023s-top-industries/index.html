Resumes: Strategies Behind the Greats (in all of 2023's Top Industries)


Resumes are the gateway to your dream job. These critical documents showcase your skills, experience, and unique story to potential employers in just a few pages. With the job market becoming ever more competitive, crafting an impressive and effective resume has become more challenging than ever. But no need to despair! With the right strategies in place, it’s possible to create a stand-out resume that will catch the eye of hiring managers in any industry.

Below, we’ve compiled a list of top strategies, relevant across a broad range of industries in 2023, that can propel your resume to the top of the pile.

1. Tailor Your Resume to the Industry

One of the major keys to a great resume is customization. Don’t use a one-size-fits-all approach; instead, personalize your resume to match the industry you’re targeting. This means researching industry-specific language, key skills, and core values that employers are looking for and integrating them into your resume.

For example, a software engineer might focus on technical proficiency and projects in their resume, while a creative designer would highlight their portfolio, creativity, and visual aesthetic.

2. Highlight Your Achievements and Results

In a sea of applicants, it’s crucial to differentiate yourself from the competition. Instead of simply listing your responsibilities from previous jobs, shift your focus to your accomplishments and the results they delivered. Quantify your achievements whenever possible, using numbers and other data to support your claims.

A specific example: instead of stating “Managed a team of employees,” say you “managed a team of 15 employees, resulting in a 20% increase in productivity.”

3. Keep It Concise and Clear

Your resume is your first line of defense in making a great first impression. A well-formatted, easy-to-read document with a clean design will give you a better opportunity to get noticed. Avoid walls of text by using bullet points and maintaining a consistent, professional format throughout. Keep track of the most relevant information by sticking to one or two pages if possible.

4. Utilize Keywords

Searching for keywords is a common practice among recruiters and applicant tracking systems (ATS) to validate the relevance and quality of your resume. For optimal SEO and improved pass-through rates, strategically include relevant industry and company-specific keywords throughout your document. These can include job titles, technologies, skills, and accomplishments that pertain to the position and industry you’re targeting.

5. Optimize for SEO and Applicant Tracking Systems

IRS, applicant tracking systems (ATS) have become another crucial gatekeeper in the resume submission process. To maximize your resume’s chances of passing through these systems, optimize it for both SEO and ATS. Use consistent formats, clear and organized sections, and incorporate targeted keywords that closely match the job posting. Remember, recruiters may use specialized software designed to analyze CVs, so ensure your document conforms to their standards.

6. Incorporate Action Verbs and Industry Jargon

When describing your qualifications, it’s essential to use precise and relevant language. Individualize your verb choices to effectively showcase your experience and enhance readability. While common verbs like “managed,” “collaborated,” or “led” are useful, consider investing in a thesaurus to avoid repetition and bring more interest to your resume.

Additionally, sprinkling in relevant industry terms will convey your familiarity with the specific field and make it easier for recruiters to spot critical qualifications.

7. Strong Objective, Summary, or Professional Statement

A well-written objective, summary, or professional statement at the beginning of your resume is essential to grab the attention of a potential employer. This section should set the tone for your resume and communicate significant information about your qualifications, ambitions, and experience.

Showcase your expertise and passion for the industry, and emphasize how you can contribute to the organization’s success. Though tailored to your specific industry, this statement offers a sneak peek of what’s to come within the body of your resume, enticing the reader to continue on.

8. Provide a Career Timeline

Your resume should be organized chronologically to display your career trajectory. Listing your work history in reverse-chronological order enables the reader to follow your career path over time and assess your growth and professional development.

When detailing your previous positions, include the company name, your title, dates of employment, and a brief description of your key accomplishments or responsibilities.

9. Demonstrate Adaptability and Continuous Learning

As the job market evolves, employers are often looking for individuals capable of adapting to new situations and staying on top of the latest developments in their industry. Emphasize your ability to learn, adapt, and grow, by including information about professional development and certifications relevant to the industry you’re pursuing.

10. Proofread and Edit

No matter how stellar the content of your resume is, a single typo or grammatical error can quickly disqualify you from consideration. Devote ample time to proofreading and editing your resume for errors before sending it out. Consider having a trusted colleague or mentor review it as well to catch any errors you may have overlooked.

Remember that creating a great resume takes time, effort, and attention to detail, but the payoff is worth it! By integrating these impressive strategies into your resume, you will be well on your way to landing that dream job in any industry.

Stay updated on the latest industry trends, and make your resume a true reflection of your unique value proposition. Good luck!
