How to Choose the Right Sunglasses for Your Face Shape


Sunglasses are more than just a tool to protect your eyes from the sun. They are an essential fashion accessory that can enhance your look and help express your personality. However, finding the right pair of sunglasses isn’t as simple as picking any style off the shelf. The key to selecting the perfect pair lies in understanding how sunglasses interact with your face shape. Just like choosing the right clothing or hairstyle, the sunglasses you wear should complement your face’s natural contours.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of choosing the right sunglasses for your face shape, with tips and advice that will help you look your best while keeping your eyes protected.

Understanding Face Shapes

Before you can choose the perfect pair of sunglasses, it’s essential to determine your face shape. Here are the most common face shapes to consider:

1. Oval Face Shape

An oval face is characterized by balanced proportions, with a gently rounded jawline and slightly narrower cheekbones. People with oval faces typically have high foreheads and a face length that’s longer than the width. Oval faces are versatile, and most sunglasses will suit this shape.

2. Round Face Shape

Round faces tend to have full cheeks and a rounded chin with equal width and height. The goal for people with round faces is to create balance by choosing sunglasses that add angles and structure to the face.

3. Square Face Shape

Square faces have strong, angular features, including a wide forehead, strong jawline, and wide cheekbones. The goal for square faces is to soften the angular lines and add more curves to the overall look.

4. Heart Face Shape

Heart-shaped faces are wider at the forehead and cheekbones, narrowing to a pointed chin. This face shape requires sunglasses that help balance the proportions by adding width around the lower part of the face.

5. Diamond Face Shape

Diamond-shaped faces are characterized by high cheekbones, a narrow forehead, and a narrow chin. People with diamond-shaped faces have angular features and should look for sunglasses that soften the lines and provide some balance.

6. Rectangle Face Shape

Rectangle faces, also known as oblong faces, are longer than they are wide, with a strong jawline and high cheekbones. The goal is to make the face appear shorter and add volume to the sides of the face.

Sunglasses for Oval Faces

Best Styles for Oval Faces

If you have an oval face, consider yourself lucky because you can wear almost any sunglasses style. Oval faces are well-balanced, so the focus should be on enhancing these proportions.

  • Aviators: Classic aviators are an excellent option for oval faces because they add structure and definition without overwhelming your features.
  • Wayfarers: The iconic wayfarer shape works well for oval faces as they bring boldness and contrast, complementing the soft lines of the face.
  • Round Sunglasses: Round sunglasses can create an interesting contrast with the natural curves of an oval face, providing a unique and fashionable look.
  • Cat-Eye Sunglasses: These sunglasses will draw attention to the eyes while providing a lift to the upper face, adding a fun and retro vibe to your style.

What to Avoid

While most styles work for oval faces, it’s best to avoid sunglasses that are too large or too small. Extremely oversized glasses can overwhelm your balanced features, while tiny sunglasses may look disproportionate.

Sunglasses for Round Faces

Best Styles for Round Faces

Round faces are characterized by their equal width and height, so the goal is to elongate the face and add some definition to the cheeks and jawline. To achieve this, you’ll want sunglasses with angular frames.

  • Square or Rectangular Sunglasses: These styles add sharp lines and angles, which can help elongate your face and create the appearance of a more angular jawline.
  • Cat-Eye Sunglasses: The upswept design of cat-eye sunglasses can help lift the face and add definition to the cheekbones and jawline.
  • Browline Sunglasses: Sunglasses with a thicker top frame can draw attention to the upper half of your face, helping to balance out the roundness of your cheeks.
  • Wayfarers: Wayfarers are also a good choice for round faces, as their strong geometric shape contrasts with the softness of the face.

What to Avoid

Avoid round sunglasses, as they can emphasize the roundness of your face. Also, steer clear of overly large frames, which can make your face appear even rounder. Instead, go for frames that are proportional to the size of your face.

Sunglasses for Square Faces

Best Styles for Square Faces

Square faces are known for their strong, angular features. The key when choosing sunglasses for square faces is to soften the angles and create balance. Opt for frames that have rounded edges or a more fluid shape.

  • Round Sunglasses: Round sunglasses can help soften the sharp angles of a square face by introducing curves. These sunglasses balance the angularity of the face and add a touch of sophistication.
  • Oval Sunglasses: Similar to round sunglasses, oval frames can also help create a contrast with the straight lines of a square face, adding harmony and balance.
  • Aviators: Aviator sunglasses with a teardrop shape are another excellent option for square faces, as they draw attention to the center of the face and soften the sharp angles of the jawline.
  • Soft Cat-Eye Sunglasses: Cat-eye sunglasses with gentle curves can also help soften the edges of a square face while adding an element of elegance.

What to Avoid

Avoid angular sunglasses, such as square or rectangular frames, as they may accentuate the harsh lines of a square face. Steer clear of oversized sunglasses as well, as they may overwhelm your facial features.

Sunglasses for Heart-Shaped Faces

Best Styles for Heart-Shaped Faces

Heart-shaped faces are wider at the forehead and narrow toward the chin, so sunglasses that balance the proportions of the face are the best choice. Look for styles that add width to the lower half of your face and draw attention to your eyes.

  • Round or Oval Sunglasses: Round or oval sunglasses will balance the width of your forehead and create a more proportionate look. The gentle curves also help soften the angularity of your chin.
  • Cat-Eye Sunglasses: The upward-turned shape of cat-eye sunglasses works well with heart-shaped faces, as it emphasizes the eyes while minimizing the width of the forehead.
  • Browline Sunglasses: Browline sunglasses draw attention to the upper half of the face, which can balance the wider forehead and narrow chin.
  • Wayfarers: Classic wayfarers add definition to the jawline, making them a great option for heart-shaped faces.

What to Avoid

Avoid sunglasses with a heavy frame at the top, as they may draw too much attention to the widest part of your face—the forehead. Also, steer clear of overly large or oversized sunglasses, which may overwhelm the lower part of your face.

Sunglasses for Diamond Faces

Best Styles for Diamond Faces

Diamond-shaped faces have high cheekbones and a narrow forehead and chin. The goal is to create balance and emphasize the eyes while softening the angular features. Sunglasses that provide width to the forehead and chin are ideal.

  • Oval Sunglasses: Oval sunglasses help soften the sharp angles of a diamond face and provide balance to the overall features.
  • Round Sunglasses: Round frames also work well by softening the lines of the face and highlighting the eyes.
  • Browline Sunglasses: These sunglasses add width to the upper part of the face, creating more balance with the narrower chin.
  • Wayfarers: Wayfarers are a good choice for diamond-shaped faces because they offer structure and contrast while balancing the proportions of the face.

What to Avoid

Avoid angular or geometric sunglasses, such as square or rectangular frames, as they can emphasize the sharp features of a diamond-shaped face. Instead, opt for softer shapes that provide balance and harmony.

Sunglasses for Rectangle Faces

Best Styles for Rectangle Faces

Rectangle faces are characterized by their longer-than-wide proportions, so the key to choosing sunglasses for this face shape is to add volume to the sides of the face and create the illusion of a shorter face.

  • Round or Oval Sunglasses: These sunglasses help soften the long lines of a rectangle face and add a sense of roundness and balance.
  • Wayfarers: The bold lines of wayfarers help shorten the length of the face, creating a more balanced look.
  • Browline Sunglasses: Browline sunglasses add width to the top of the face, balancing the length of a rectangle-shaped face and bringing attention to the eyes.
  • Aviators: Aviators can create a balanced appearance by adding width and structure to the sides of the face, providing a sense of proportion.

What to Avoid

Avoid narrow sunglasses, as they will only make your face appear longer. Also, steer clear of oversized frames that can exaggerate the length of your face. Opt for sunglasses that add width and create balance.

Conclusion

Choosing the right pair of sunglasses for your face shape doesn’t have to be complicated. By understanding the key features of your face and selecting sunglasses that complement and enhance your natural contours, you can find a style that suits you perfectly. Whether you have a round, square, oval, heart, diamond, or rectangle face shape, there are sunglasses out there that will not only protect your eyes but also enhance your look.

Remember that sunglasses are an accessory that reflects your personality and style, so take your time when selecting the perfect pair. With the right sunglasses, you can make a bold statement while keeping your eyes shielded from the sun.
