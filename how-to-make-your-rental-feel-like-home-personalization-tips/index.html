How to Make Your Rental Feel Like Home: Personalization Tips


When you move into a new rental, it can sometimes feel like you’re living in a temporary space rather than your true home. The walls may be a neutral color, the floors standard laminate or carpet, and the furniture may feel uninspired. However, even though you might not own the property, there are plenty of ways to make your rental feel personal, cozy, and uniquely yours. In this guide, we’ll explore a range of strategies to help you transform your rental into a space that feels like home.

1. Start with a Deep Clean

Before you begin personalizing your rental, it’s important to start with a clean slate. A deep clean not only ensures that your space feels fresh and inviting, but it can also help you assess the area for any changes or updates you might want to make.

  • Dust and wipe down surfaces, especially if you’re moving into a place where previous tenants left their mark.
  • Deep clean the carpets and floors to remove any dirt and stains that might have accumulated.
  • Pay attention to the bathrooms and kitchen—areas that can easily collect grime over time.

A clean home sets the tone for your creative and personalization efforts, so give it the attention it deserves.

2. Play with Color

Color is one of the easiest ways to personalize any space, and it can dramatically change the mood of a room. While you may not be able to repaint the walls of your rental, there are many ways to bring color into the space through accessories and décor.

  • Use colorful textiles: Throw pillows, blankets, and rugs can bring in vibrant pops of color that make your space feel more inviting and personal.
  • Curtains and blinds: Switch out any plain window coverings for something that matches your style. Whether it’s a neutral tone for a calming effect or a bold print for a statement piece, window treatments can easily refresh a room.
  • Art and wall décor: Fill empty walls with art that resonates with you. Framed prints, paintings, or even a gallery wall of personal photographs can add personality to any space.

When selecting colors, choose hues that reflect your personal style and create a vibe you enjoy. Bright and bold tones energize a space, while soft and muted tones create a calm atmosphere.

3. Invest in Comfortable Furniture

While you may not be able to overhaul the entire rental, one of the best ways to make a space feel like home is by having furniture that suits your needs and adds comfort. Think about the pieces that you spend the most time with, and prioritize those.

  • Couches and chairs: Invest in cozy seating that suits your style. Whether you go for a plush sectional or a sleek modern chair, make sure it’s something you’ll love spending time in.
  • Comfortable bed: Since sleep is so important, a comfortable mattress and high-quality bedding are essential. Choose sheets, comforters, and pillows that make you feel relaxed and rested.
  • Storage solutions: Look for stylish storage furniture like bookshelves, cabinets, or storage ottomans that help keep the space organized while contributing to the room’s overall look.

The furniture you select should feel good and be functional, but also work to reflect your personality.

4. Incorporate Personal Touches

Nothing makes a place feel like home more than your own personal items. Whether it’s cherished mementos, heirlooms, or items you’ve collected over time, incorporating personal touches helps create a unique and inviting space.

  • Photographs: Displaying family photos or snapshots from your travels brings warmth to your rental. Use frames that match the tone of your décor, whether minimalist or more eclectic.
  • Travel souvenirs: If you’ve traveled extensively, showcase your souvenirs in a way that adds character to the space. Place them on shelves, walls, or coffee tables as conversational pieces.
  • Books: A collection of books can give your rental a lived-in and personal feel. Group them together on bookshelves or stack them artfully on tables.

Your personal items should not only be meaningful to you but also contribute to the space’s overall aesthetic.

5. Add Greenery and Plants

Bringing the outdoors inside can significantly transform a space. Indoor plants can improve the air quality and add a sense of tranquility to any room. Whether you choose low-maintenance succulents, large statement plants, or hanging greenery, plants are a great way to inject life into your rental.

  • Small potted plants: These can be placed on tables, shelves, or windowsills.
  • Larger plants: If you have the space, larger plants like fiddle-leaf figs or snake plants can make a striking statement in corners of rooms.
  • Hanging plants: If floor space is limited, consider hanging plants in stylish planters from the ceiling.

The greenery will give your rental a fresh, natural vibe and make it feel more like your own personal oasis.

6. Focus on Lighting

Lighting plays a critical role in setting the tone of a room. Proper lighting can make a space feel cozy, bright, or even romantic. You may have basic overhead lighting in your rental, but adding extra layers of light through lamps and fixtures will make your space feel more dynamic.

  • Table and floor lamps: These provide soft, ambient lighting and can be placed strategically to highlight certain areas of the room.
  • String lights or fairy lights: These can add a magical or whimsical touch, especially in living rooms or bedrooms.
  • Task lighting: If you like to read, cook, or work from home, consider adding task lighting to help with specific activities.

The right lighting can enhance the mood of your space and give it a cozy, inviting feeling.

7. Create Defined Spaces

If your rental is a studio or open-concept space, creating defined areas within the room will help make it feel more organized and comfortable. You don’t need to erect walls to divide the space—there are plenty of other ways to define different zones.

  • Rugs: Use rugs to define specific areas like the living room or dining area. A rug can anchor the furniture and add warmth to any room.
  • Screens or room dividers: A stylish screen or room divider can help separate areas without needing major construction. It’s a great way to create a little privacy in shared spaces.
  • Furniture arrangement: Play around with the layout of your furniture. Group furniture in a way that makes sense for the space, with a focus on creating a sense of flow and separation.

By dividing up your space thoughtfully, you can create areas for rest, work, and relaxation—just like you would in a permanent home.

8. Customize Your Kitchen and Bathroom

While the kitchen and bathroom may seem like utilitarian spaces, they are also prime areas for personalization. Even small updates or decorative touches can make these rooms feel more comfortable and inviting.

  • Organize your kitchen: Use stylish containers and baskets to keep your kitchen neat. Label jars or spice racks for an organized yet visually pleasing look. You can also add colorful towels, trivets, and placemats to brighten the space.
  • Upgrade bathroom accessories: Replace any old or boring bath mats, shower curtains, and towels with ones that reflect your style. Adding simple décor like candles, mirrors, or storage baskets can also elevate the room.

Even in small spaces, customizing the kitchen and bathroom will make them more enjoyable to use.

9. Add Unique Decorative Items

Decorative items, such as candles, vases, mirrors, and sculptures, can make a world of difference in personalizing your rental. These items allow you to bring in your own sense of style, whether that’s modern, vintage, rustic, or eclectic.

  • Mirrors: A stylish mirror can open up the space, making it feel larger and brighter.
  • Candles: The soft glow of candles creates a cozy and intimate atmosphere. Scented candles can also add pleasant fragrances to the air.
  • Artisan décor: Add character to your space with unique pieces like handmade ceramics, woven baskets, or vintage finds. These items not only reflect your personality but also make the space feel more lived in.

Decorative touches go a long way in adding personality and depth to any room.

10. Make It Smell Like Home

One of the most overlooked ways to personalize a space is through scent. A homey, welcoming smell can enhance your space and create a sense of comfort.

  • Scented candles: Choose fragrances that make you feel relaxed or energized, such as lavender, vanilla, or citrus.
  • Essential oils: Use a diffuser to fill your space with soothing scents. Essential oils like eucalyptus or chamomile can make the atmosphere calming.
  • Baking scents: Even if you don’t bake often, the scent of cookies or fresh bread in the oven can give your rental a homely feel. Try a store-bought option or use a candle that mimics those smells.

Scent is a powerful tool for creating a sense of home in any space.

11. Be Mindful of the Small Details

Finally, it’s the small details that really make a rental feel like home. These often-overlooked touches can tie your entire space together.

  • Unique door hardware: Swap out basic door handles for something more decorative.
  • Personalized doormats: Welcome guests into your home with a doormat that reflects your personality or a fun message.
  • Decorative trays and bowls: Place them on coffee tables or countertops to hold keys, coins, or even small plants.

These small but thoughtful details help create a space that feels unique and personal.

Conclusion

Making a rental feel like home doesn’t require major renovations or expensive furniture. By focusing on the elements that matter most to you—whether it’s color, comfort, or personalization—you can transform any space into a home. Experiment with different ideas, and remember that it’s the details that make a house feel like a true home. Your rental can reflect your personality and provide the comfort you need to feel truly settled, no matter how long you plan to stay.
