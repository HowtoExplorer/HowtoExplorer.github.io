Crafting a Resume for International Development and NGOs


Crafting a resume tailored for international development and non-governmental organizations (NGOs) requires a unique approach to highlight your skills, experiences, and passion for making a difference in the world. Whether you are a seasoned professional or just starting your career in this field, a well-crafted resume can be your ticket to opportunities that align with your values and goals. In this guide, we will walk you through the essential steps to create a compelling resume that stands out in the competitive world of international development and NGOs.

Understanding the Sector

Before diving into resume writing, it’s crucial to understand the intricacies of the international development sector and NGOs. These organizations often look for candidates who not only possess the required technical skills but also demonstrate a deep commitment to social impact, cultural sensitivity, and adaptability to diverse environments. Highlighting your understanding of global issues, cross-cultural communication, and project management can greatly enhance your resume’s effectiveness.

Structuring Your Resume

1. Header Section

  • Start your resume with a clear header containing your name, contact information, and professional title.

2. Summary or Objective Statement

  • Craft a concise summary or objective statement that captures your professional background, key skills, and your passion for international development work.

3. Skills Section

  • List relevant technical skills such as project management, data analysis, monitoring, and evaluation, as well as soft skills like cross-cultural communication, teamwork, and adaptability.

4. Experience Section

  • Detail your work experience in reverse chronological order, emphasizing roles and projects that showcase your impact, leadership, and collaboration in multicultural settings.

5. Education Section

  • Include your educational qualifications, relevant certifications, and any specialized training related to international development or NGO work.

6. Volunteer Experience

  • Highlight any volunteer work or internships that demonstrate your commitment to social causes and community engagement.

7. Language Proficiency

  • If you are proficient in multiple languages, especially those relevant to the regions where NGOs operate, make sure to mention them.

Tailoring Your Resume

1. Research the Organization

  • Before applying to an NGO or international development agency, research their mission, values, and current projects to tailor your resume to their specific needs.

2. Use Action Verbs

  • Start each bullet point describing your work experience with strong action verbs to showcase your achievements and contributions.

3. Quantify Your Achievements

  • Where possible, quantify your accomplishments with numbers and percentages to demonstrate the impact of your work.

4. Highlight Relevant Experience

  • Focus on experiences that directly relate to the job requirements, such as fieldwork, grant writing, policy analysis, or community development initiatives.

5. Customize for Each Application

  • Avoid sending out generic resumes. Customize each application to align with the job description and organization you are applying to.

Additional Tips

  • Professional Formatting: Keep your resume clean, well-organized, and easy to read. Use bullet points and headings to structure information effectively.

  • Proofread: Avoid typos and grammatical errors. Proofread your resume multiple times or ask a friend to review it for you.

  • Seek Feedback: Get feedback from mentors, career counselors, or professionals in the field to improve your resume further.

Crafting a resume for international development and NGOs is not just about listing your qualifications; it’s about telling a compelling story of your commitment to creating positive change in the world. By following these guidelines and tailoring your resume to showcase your unique strengths and experiences, you can increase your chances of landing your dream job in the rewarding field of international development. Remember, your resume is your first impression – make it count!

End your resume with a strong call-to-action, encouraging recruiters to reach out and discuss how your skills and passion can contribute to their organization’s mission.

Remember, your resume is your marketing tool – make sure it represents the best version of you!

By following these steps, you can create a resume that not only impresses potential employers but also effectively communicates your dedication to international development and NGOs. Good luck with your job search, and may your career journey be filled with meaningful opportunities to make a difference in the world.
