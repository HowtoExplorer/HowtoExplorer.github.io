Math Talk: The Connection Between Language and Mathematics


Once upon a time, humans believed the universe was a static and unchanging place. Mathematics was seen as a universal language, carrying deep truths about the world. Over time, this belief was shattered as humanity learned the world was ever-changing and that mathematical principles evolved. This observation led to the realization that language and mathematics are fundamentally intertwined.

The Connection Between Language and Math

Language as a tool for math communication Language is the seed that nurtures mathematical ideas. Our ancestors communicated simple ideas such as counting, measurements, and inventory management through the spoken word, in their respective languages. This early communication allowed them to share and comprehend simple mathematical concepts, using words. Thus, the roots of mathematics were planted early on, as language served to carry the seeds of mathematical ideas into the world.

Symbolic representation in mathematics As mathematics evolved, words evolved into symbols to represent numbers, formulas, and equations. This shift allowed for more efficient communication and expanded the realm of mathematical discoveries. Much like how alphabets emerged from simple pictographs, mathematical symbols represent numbers, operation, and formulas, streamlining communication in the mathematical community.

The connection becomes omnipresent Mathematics has co-evolved with human culture, advancing alongside human languages. This connection is highlighted in languages’ unique takes on mathematical concepts. For instance, some languages have developed special color terminology to better speak about the concepts in color theory; meanwhile, Mandarin Chinese uses a pictorial writing system based on symbols, which heavily influences how Far Eastern mathematics has traditionally been represented and shared.

The language of music as an extension of math Beyond spoken language and written symbols, music represents an intriguing intersection of mathematics and human expression. Music Theory relies heavily on mathematical concepts such as rhythm, pitch, intervals, and scales. The symbolic language of music permits musicians to share these mathematical ideas, just as written language allows mathematicians to share their ideas.

The Twist of Lexical Memory and Math Comprehension

How our brain makes the connection

Neuroscience and psychology have been entertaining a fruitful discussion on how humans process numbers and mathematical concepts. It appears that number processing begins in Broca’s area and Wernicke’s area, both brain centers associated with language processing. Thus, the brain applies the same tools that enable us to understand spoken language to comprehend mathematical concepts.

The path from math to language and back

Mathematical concepts and language are fundamentally intertwined. The comprehension of one heavily relies on the understanding of the other. For example, when thinking of a long string of numbers like “one hundred forty-five billion, seven hundred ninety-one million, four hundred thousand,” people understand the concept by breaking it down into smaller numbers, applying language to simplify math concepts in spoken form. This same concept applies to written or symbolic form.

Math Speak: Communicating Complexity

Beyond the Language Classroom

Understanding the connection between language and math benefits our understanding not only of numbers and formulas, but also of the world around us. Analyzing data, following scientific methods, understanding patterns: these abilities depend on applying both languages and math together. Furthermore, in an increasingly technological world, a deeper understanding of how language brings life to mathematical concepts enriches human connections, from fostering better communication about the abstract, to delivering a more profound ability to understand and appreciate the beauty of the universe itself.

In conclusion, the connection between language and mathematics has opened vast possibilities for human understanding of the world. This relationship has been enriched over time, involving significant advancements in how we communicate abstract thoughts. As language and mathematics continue to evolve, it is crucial that we appreciate this remarkable bond and the profound impact it has on our lives.
