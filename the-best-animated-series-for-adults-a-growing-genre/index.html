The Best Animated Series for Adults: A Growing Genre


In recent years, animated series for adults have surged in popularity, capturing the attention of viewers who seek storytelling that resonates with mature themes, complex characters, and sophisticated humor. This trend marks a significant evolution in animation, a medium once predominantly associated with children’s programming. Today, we dive into the best animated series for adults that have not only entertained but also pushed the boundaries of storytelling.

A Brief History of Adult Animation

The roots of adult animation can be traced back to the late 20th century with groundbreaking shows like “The Simpsons,” which debuted in 1989. This series paved the way for animated programs that tackled social issues and adult humor. However, it wasn’t until the 2000s and 2010s that the genre truly flourished, with the emergence of numerous series that explored darker themes and complex narratives. Today, adult animated series span a variety of genres, including comedy, drama, science fiction, and fantasy, showcasing the versatility of animation as a storytelling medium.

The Appeal of Animated Series for Adults

One of the primary reasons animated series for adults have gained traction is their ability to explore themes that resonate with mature audiences. Animation allows creators to depict scenarios that may be difficult or impossible to portray in live-action formats. This freedom leads to innovative storytelling, where the absurd blends seamlessly with poignant social commentary.

Moreover, the visual medium of animation provides unique opportunities for creativity. From vibrant color palettes to surreal landscapes, animated series can create entire worlds that reflect the inner workings of the characters’ minds. This imaginative aspect attracts viewers who appreciate both the artistry and the narrative depth.

The Best Animated Series for Adults

1. BoJack Horseman

“BoJack Horseman” is often hailed as a masterpiece in adult animation. The series follows the life of BoJack, a washed-up actor who struggles with addiction, depression, and his quest for redemption. What sets this series apart is its raw and honest portrayal of mental health issues, relationships, and the impact of fame. The blend of humor and heartache is executed flawlessly, making it a must-watch for anyone seeking a deeply resonant narrative.

2. Rick and Morty

“Rick and Morty” follows the misadventures of an eccentric scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his good-hearted but fretful grandson, Morty. This series expertly combines science fiction with dark humor, exploring complex themes such as existentialism, family dynamics, and moral ambiguity. Its clever writing and unique animation style have garnered a dedicated fanbase, making it a significant player in the adult animation landscape.

3. Futurama

Created by Matt Groening, “Futurama” takes viewers on a comedic journey through the future. The series is renowned for its witty satire, clever cultural references, and its ability to tackle serious issues while maintaining a lighthearted tone. The character development, particularly of Fry, Leela, and Bender, adds depth to the narrative, making it a beloved classic among adult animated series.

4. Big Mouth

“Big Mouth” is a coming-of-age series that addresses the awkwardness of adolescence with unflinching honesty. Through the lens of animated characters, it explores topics such as puberty, sexuality, and mental health. The series is both hilarious and educational, providing viewers with relatable experiences while breaking down societal taboos.

5. Archer

“Archer” is a spy comedy that follows the misadventures of Sterling Archer and his dysfunctional team of secret agents. The series is known for its sharp wit, absurd humor, and unique animation style. It cleverly parodies the spy genre while delving into the complexities of relationships and workplace dynamics, making it a standout in adult animation.

6. The Boondocks

Based on the comic strip by Aaron McGruder, “The Boondocks” addresses social and political issues through the lens of two young boys, Huey and Riley Freeman, who move to a predominantly white suburb. The series is acclaimed for its sharp commentary on race, culture, and identity, making it a powerful voice in adult animation. Its thought-provoking narratives and distinct animation style have left a lasting impact on the genre.

7. Solar Opposites

From one of the creators of “Rick and Morty,” “Solar Opposites” tells the story of a family of aliens who crash-land on Earth. The series cleverly juxtaposes sci-fi elements with everyday life, exploring themes of culture shock and the human experience. Its irreverent humor and unique storytelling approach make it a refreshing addition to the adult animation landscape.

8. Tuca & Bertie

“Tuca & Bertie” is a whimsical exploration of female friendship, mental health, and the complexities of adulthood. The animated series features two bird roommates navigating their twenties in a vibrant and surreal world. Its artistic animation style and heartfelt storytelling contribute to its charm, offering a unique perspective on relationships and personal growth.

9. Undone

“Undone” is a groundbreaking series that blends live-action and animation to create a visually stunning narrative. The story follows Alma, a young woman grappling with her mental health and the discovery of her ability to manipulate time. The innovative animation technique and thought-provoking themes make “Undone” a standout, pushing the boundaries of traditional storytelling in adult animation.

10. Castlevania

Based on the popular video game series, “Castlevania” is a dark fantasy animated series that follows the last surviving member of the Belmont clan as he battles Dracula and his minions. The series is praised for its stunning animation, complex characters, and intricate storytelling. It successfully combines action, horror, and fantasy elements, appealing to both gamers and animation enthusiasts.

The Future of Adult Animation

As the demand for diverse storytelling continues to grow, the future of adult animation looks promising. With streaming platforms investing heavily in original content, we can expect to see even more innovative series that challenge conventions and explore new themes. In addition, the rise of independent creators and smaller studios is diversifying the landscape, offering fresh perspectives and unique narratives.

Moreover, the increasing acceptance of animation as a legitimate storytelling medium for adults is encouraging more creators to venture into this space. As audiences become more receptive to bold and unconventional narratives, the genre will continue to evolve, pushing the boundaries of creativity and imagination.

Conclusion

The best animated series for adults represent a remarkable evolution in the genre, showcasing the potential of animation to tell compelling and meaningful stories. From the poignant explorations of mental health in “BoJack Horseman” to the absurd humor of “Rick and Morty,” these series have proven that animation is not just for children but can resonate deeply with adults as well.

As we look to the future, the growth of adult animation signals a shift in the entertainment landscape, inviting viewers to embrace new narratives and imaginative worlds. With each new series, the genre continues to expand, offering a diverse array of stories that challenge, entertain, and inspire. Whether you’re a long-time fan or new to the genre, there’s never been a better time to immerse yourself in the best animated series for adults.
