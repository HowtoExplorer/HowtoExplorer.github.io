The Dos and Don'ts of Resume Formatting


A well-formatted resume can significantly impact whether a hiring manager decides to consider you for the job or not. Ditch the one-size-fits-all mindset and customize your resume for each job application. Here are some essential dos and don’ts to follow for perfect resume formatting.

Dos

1. Stick to a clean and easy-to-read layout

Professional and clean layouts are not only visually appealing but also easier to skim through for hiring managers. Avoid overly complex formats with too much color or animation.

2. Highlight relevant skills and achievements

Tailor your resume to match the job requirements by emphasizing your most relevant skills and achievements. Provide concrete examples, such as accomplishments and keywords that match the position description.

3. Use a clear and legible font

Stick to universally legible fonts like Times New Roman or Arial. Avoid excessively decorative fonts that might be challenging to read.

4. Opt for reverse chronological ordering

Employers generally prefer to see your most recent work experience first. Organize your work experience section in reverse chronological order, with your current or most recent job at the top.

5. Include a summary statement

A summary statement is a brief, two to three sentence summary that highlights your most relevant skills and experiences. This section is crucial because it acts as an introduction to your resume and can help match your qualifications to the job requirements.

6. List your education

Include your degrees, majors, and any relevant coursework. If you have multiple degrees, list these in order of importance.

7. Proofread before submitting

No one wants to read a resume riddled with typos and grammatical errors. Proofread your resume carefully or ask a friend to help you.

Don’ts

1. Overload on fonts, colors, and images

Avoid using gimmicky elements like multiple fonts, colors, and images that can distract the reader from your qualifications.

2. Focus solely on your resume

Instead of looking at the job requirements, applicants often write their resumes without considering the employer’s needs. Your resume should be tailored to the job description.

3. Exclude a skills section

Including a skills section highlighting your most relevant skills can showcase your qualifications without repetitively listing them under each job experience.

4. Forget to mention gaps in employment

If you have any employment gaps or periods of unemployment, be transparent and explain why you were unemployed during that period. For example, if you took time off to travel, you could write, “Traveled through Southeast Asia, experiencing new cultures, languages, and cuisines, January-June 2018.”

5. Compress your resume to fit one page

While a one-page resume might be ideal, some positions and roles require more space to detail your experiences and achievements. Don’t hesitate to expand your resume to two pages if you need the extra space.

6. Neglect to review employer feedback

When getting feedback on your resume, always review the suggestions and make changes accordingly.

Resume formatting is a critical aspect of job applications. Following these dos and don’ts will help you create a professional, concise, and well-structured resume that can boost your chances of landing your desired job. Don’t forget to proofread and personalize your resume for each application. Happy job searching!
