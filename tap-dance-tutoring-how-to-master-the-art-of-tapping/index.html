Tap Dance Tutoring: How to Master the Art of Tapping


Tap dancing is an enchanting and energetic form of dance that has been captivating audiences for over a century. It showcases rhythm, creativity, and technique as the dancer’s feet expertly produce a delightful percussive sound. Eager to elevate your tap dance skills? Here’s our comprehensive guide on how to master the art of tapping.

Finding the Right Tap Dance Tutoring

Before embarking on your tap journey, you’ll need to find a skilled and supportive tap dance tutor. Look for qualified instructors at dance studios, or even consider taking online lessons. Request recommendations from friends or fellow dancers who might have experience in tap. Scour the internet for top-rated tap dance instructors. As you evaluate potential tutors, consider their background, passion, and teaching techniques. A seasoned tap dancer will possess not only expert skills but also the ability to teach and nurture students of all ages and experience levels.

Basic Steps to Build a Strong Foundation

The first steps in mastering tap dance are the foundation upon which all advanced techniques will build. Let’s briefly cover some basic tap dance steps you should master:

1. Stomp

A stomp is the starting point for beginners. It’s an open-toed step where you lift the back of your shoe from the floor while making a sound. To do this, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, straighten your legs, and apply modest pressure while lifting your heel to create sound. Stomps can be executed in various rhythms and will form the base of the majority of tap dance combinations.

2. Flap

Flaps involve “flapping” your feet alternately, similar to the way a bird’s wings flap. To create this sound, press your right foot from the heel to the toes against the floor and release it, then do the same with the left foot. This produces a distinct slap sound with each flap.

3. Shim Sham

The Shim Sham is a well-known tap dance routine. It’ll help you learn coordination, timing, and fluidity of multiple steps. You’ll find numerous instructional videos online to help you master this tap routine, making it a perfect next step after you’ve gotten a handle on your basic steps.

Developing Advanced Tap Dance Skills

Once you’ve learned the basics and feel comfortable executing them, the sky’s the limit with tap dance tutoring. Here are some advanced tap dance techniques to help you elevate your skillset:

1. Buffalo

The Buffalo is an intricate tap dance step that allows you to showcase your rhythm and coordination. To perform this move, stomp your right foot back while brushing your left foot’s toe forward. Retrace these movements, beginning with a brush of the left foot’s toe and following with a stomp of your right foot forward. Practice the buffalo alongside a rhythmic accompaniment to fully engage your sense of timing.

2. Time Steps

Time steps are energetic and rhythmic, making them excellent for incorporating swifter footwork into your tap dance repertoire. Start by doing two hops on the balls of your feet followed by two grazes of your heels across the balls of your feet. Add this step to your routines for an instant upgrade in both style and skill.

3. Weave

The Weave is an advanced tap dance step that combines both the Buffalo and the Time Step. Begin with a two-hopped time step to the right and end with two hops to the left. As you do this, execute a brushing motion with your right foot before making contact on the balls of your feet. Practice this move with a consistent beat to improve your timing and speed.

Enhancing Tap Dance Creativity

With a solid foundation, you’re ready to explore your creativity and individuality in tap dance. Here are some tips to outshine the crowd:

  1. Experiment with different rhythms and sounds: Tap dancing is a musical percussion instrument, so take advantage and let your creativity flow. Try unique rhythms, assist in improvisations, or work on integrating non-traditional melodies.

  2. Watch diverse tap dance performances: Whether it’s on YouTube, live theater, or in films, gaining a variety of exposure to tap dance performances enhances your understanding of rhythm, timing, and creativity.

  3. Collaborate with others: Collaboration is an excellent way to push yourself out of your comfort zone and develop chemistry in your dancing. Try practicing duets, performing with live musicians, or creating smaller group routines specially choreographed by your tap dance tutor.

Mastering the art of tap dancing is a lengthy process. Be patient with yourself and remember that regular practice, combined with effective tutoring, goes a long way in improving your skills and enhancing your overall tap dance performance. Keep working on your foundation steps, explore advanced techniques and don’t be afraid to experiment and showcase your unique style. Happy tapping!
