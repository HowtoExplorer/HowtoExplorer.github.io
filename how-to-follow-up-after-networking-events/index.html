How to Follow Up After Networking Events


Networking events offer a unique opportunity to meet new people, exchange ideas, and build professional relationships. But for many, the true challenge lies not in attending the event itself, but in the crucial follow-up process that occurs afterward. How you follow up after networking events can make or break the potential for long-term professional connections.

In this guide, we will walk you through everything you need to know about how to follow up effectively after a networking event, ensuring that your efforts lead to meaningful connections that help advance your career.

Why Following Up is Crucial

Following up after networking events is critical because it keeps the momentum going and solidifies the relationships you started to build during the event. Meeting someone in person, exchanging business cards, and having a brief conversation is just the first step. Without proper follow-up, the connection may be forgotten or fade over time.

Here are a few key reasons why following up is essential:

  • Reinforce your initial interaction: People meet many individuals at networking events, and it’s easy to forget faces and details. A follow-up message reminds the other person who you are and why you’re reaching out.
  • Show professionalism: Taking the time to send a thoughtful follow-up shows that you’re organized, professional, and interested in cultivating a meaningful relationship.
  • Create opportunities: By following up, you might unlock doors to collaborations, mentorship, job opportunities, or partnerships.
  • Build rapport: Regular follow-up interactions help you stay top of mind, making it easier for others to remember you and refer you when the opportunity arises.

The Timing of Your Follow-Up

Timing plays a significant role in the success of your follow-up. If you wait too long, the connection may lose its relevance, and the person may not remember you well. Conversely, if you follow up too quickly, it may come across as overzealous or pushy.

The general rule of thumb is to send a follow-up message within 24 to 48 hours after the event. This allows the interaction to be fresh in both your and the other person’s mind. Additionally, it gives you enough time to personalize your message, reflect on the conversation, and craft a meaningful note.

How to Craft an Effective Follow-Up Message

The key to a successful follow-up message is to keep it personalized, professional, and concise. Your goal is to remind the person of who you are, express your appreciation for the connection, and suggest a potential next step. Here’s a step-by-step breakdown of how to craft the perfect follow-up message:

1. Subject Line: Keep It Relevant and Simple

If you’re following up via email, your subject line should be clear and to the point. Avoid using generic or overly catchy phrases. Instead, use something that relates directly to your meeting and lets the recipient know why you’re reaching out.

Examples:

  • “Great to Meet You at [Event Name]”
  • “Following Up After [Event Name]”
  • “Enjoyed Our Conversation at [Event Name]”

2. Personalize the Greeting

Start by addressing the person by their first name. Avoid generic greetings like “Dear Sir/Madam” or “To Whom It May Concern.” Personalization shows that you value the individual and remember the conversation.

Example:
“Hi [First Name],”

3. Express Gratitude

Begin the body of your message by expressing appreciation for the time spent during the event. Acknowledge the conversation you had and reference a specific topic, if possible, to make your message more memorable.

Example:
“It was a pleasure meeting you at [Event Name] yesterday. I really enjoyed our conversation about [topic you discussed], and I appreciated hearing your insights on [specific detail].”

4. Remind Them of Who You Are

While you may have already introduced yourself, a quick reminder of who you are, what you do, and how you relate to the person is always helpful. Mention something that ties back to your conversation or a common interest you shared.

Example:
“I’m [Your Name], and I work as a [Your Job Title] at [Your Company]. We discussed [shared interest or goal], and I believe there could be potential for us to collaborate.”

5. Propose a Next Step

If your goal is to continue the conversation or explore opportunities together, suggest a clear next step. This might involve scheduling a coffee meeting, setting up a call, or connecting on LinkedIn.

Example:
“I would love to continue our conversation and explore how we might work together. Would you be open to scheduling a quick call sometime next week? I’m happy to accommodate your schedule.”

6. Keep It Short and Sweet

Your follow-up message should be concise and to the point. Avoid writing lengthy paragraphs or overwhelming the recipient with too much information. Aim for a message that’s around 150-200 words.

7. End with a Call to Action

Finish your message by encouraging a response. Let the recipient know that you’re eager to hear back and are looking forward to next steps.

Example:
“I look forward to hearing from you soon and hope we can connect further. Thanks again for your time!”

8. Sign Off Professionally

Close with a polite and professional sign-off. Common options include “Best regards,” “Sincerely,” or “Best wishes,” followed by your full name and contact information.

Example:
“Best regards,
[Your Full Name]
[Your Job Title]
[Your LinkedIn Profile or Contact Information]”

Different Ways to Follow Up

While email is the most common method of following up after a networking event, there are other avenues you can consider based on the context and your connection.

1. Follow-Up on LinkedIn

LinkedIn is a powerful platform for networking, and following up there can help solidify your professional connection. After meeting someone at a networking event, send them a personalized connection request.

In your message, briefly mention how you met and why you’d like to connect. This creates an opportunity for you to engage further and keep the conversation going.

Example:
“Hi [First Name],
It was great meeting you at [Event Name] yesterday. I really enjoyed our conversation about [topic discussed] and would love to stay connected. I look forward to continuing our conversation here on LinkedIn. Best, [Your Name].”

2. Send a Handwritten Note

In a world dominated by digital communication, a handwritten note can stand out and leave a lasting impression. If the person you met at the networking event is someone you’d like to build a more personal relationship with, consider sending them a brief, handwritten note. Express your gratitude for their time and mention something specific from your conversation.

This thoughtful gesture can be especially impactful for high-level professionals or potential mentors.

3. Follow Up Via Phone or Video Call

In some cases, following up via a phone call or video conference can be an effective way to deepen the connection. This is especially true if you had an engaging conversation during the event and are looking to discuss potential collaborations or job opportunities.

Keep your tone friendly and professional, and be respectful of the other person’s time. Schedule a call in advance and be prepared with specific questions or ideas to discuss.

Best Practices for Networking Follow-Up

While every networking situation is unique, there are a few best practices that can help you stand out and increase the chances of building lasting professional relationships.

1. Be Prompt, but Not Overwhelming

As mentioned earlier, aim to follow up within 24-48 hours of the event. However, don’t send multiple messages or check-ins too soon, as this can come across as pushy. A single, thoughtful follow-up is enough.

2. Add Value in Your Follow-Up

In your follow-up message, think about how you can add value to the other person. Offer to share relevant resources, introduce them to someone in your network, or provide additional insights based on your conversation. This approach demonstrates that you’re not just thinking about what you can get out of the relationship, but also what you can give.

3. Be Specific in Your Request

If you’re hoping to meet up or discuss something further, be specific about what you’d like to happen next. Propose a time and date for a follow-up meeting or call, and make it clear how that next step aligns with your goals.

4. Don’t Be Discouraged by Silence

Not everyone will respond to your follow-up message right away—or at all. Don’t take it personally. People are busy, and your message might have gotten lost in a crowded inbox. If you haven’t heard back after a week or two, you can follow up again with a gentle nudge, reiterating your interest in staying in touch.

5. Keep Track of Your Connections

To stay organized, keep track of your networking interactions. Use a CRM tool or even a simple spreadsheet to record the people you’ve met, the events you attended, and any follow-up actions. This ensures that no one falls through the cracks, and you stay on top of all your connections.

Conclusion

Following up after networking events is more than just a polite gesture—it’s an essential part of building and maintaining professional relationships. Whether you’re hoping to collaborate with someone, land a job opportunity, or simply grow your network, a well-crafted follow-up message can make all the difference.

By personalizing your communication, being timely, and focusing on adding value, you can ensure that your networking efforts pay off and lead to lasting connections that benefit both you and the people you meet.
