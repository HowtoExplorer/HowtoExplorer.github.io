How to Follow Up After Networking Events


Networking events are a great way to expand your professional connections and explore new opportunities. But attending these events is just the first step. To truly make the most of your networking efforts, it’s crucial to follow up with the people you meet. In this blog post, we’ll explore some effective strategies for following up after networking events.

1. Personalize your follow-up message

When reaching out to someone you met at a networking event, it’s important to personalize your message. Avoid generic templates or automated responses. Take the time to mention something specific you discussed or a memorable moment from the event. This shows that you genuinely paid attention and establishes a stronger connection.

2. Send your follow-up message promptly

Timing is key when it comes to following up after networking events. Aim to send your follow-up message within 24-48 hours of the event. This ensures that your interaction is still fresh in the recipient’s mind. Delaying your follow-up can make it seem less genuine or important.

3. Express gratitude

Start your follow-up message by expressing gratitude for the opportunity to connect. Thank the person for their time and for sharing their insights or advice. Showing appreciation demonstrates professionalism and leaves a positive impression.

4. Reference your conversation

In your follow-up message, refer to a specific conversation or topic you discussed at the event. This helps jog the person’s memory and shows that you were actively engaged in the conversation. It also provides a starting point for further discussion or collaboration.

5. Offer value

When following up, try to offer something of value to the person you’re reaching out to. This could be sharing an article or resource related to a topic you discussed, introducing them to a contact who might be beneficial to them, or offering your assistance in some way. Adding value to the relationship strengthens your connection and increases the likelihood of future collaborations.

6. Suggest a follow-up meeting

If you had a particularly engaging conversation with someone at the networking event, consider suggesting a follow-up meeting. This could be a coffee chat, a lunch meeting, or even a phone call. Taking the initiative to schedule a follow-up meeting shows your genuine interest in continuing the conversation and building a professional relationship.

7. Stay organized

As you meet more people at networking events, it’s important to stay organized and keep track of your connections. Consider using a CRM (Customer Relationship Management) tool or a simple spreadsheet to record contact information, notes from your conversations, and any follow-up actions you need to take. Staying organized ensures that you don’t miss out on valuable opportunities.

Conclusion

Attending networking events is just the first step in building professional relationships. Following up effectively is what truly sets you apart. By personalizing your messages, sending them promptly, expressing gratitude, referencing your conversations, offering value, suggesting follow-up meetings, and staying organized, you can maximize the benefits of networking events and create meaningful connections that can help advance your career or business.

Remember, networking is not just about collecting business cards; it’s about building relationships. So, put these strategies into practice and watch your network grow and flourish. Happy networking!
