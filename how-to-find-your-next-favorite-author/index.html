How to Find Your Next Favorite Author


Discovering your next favorite author can feel like an adventure, a treasure hunt where each turn of the page offers an opportunity to uncover a new voice that resonates deeply with you. With so many authors producing incredible works across different genres and formats, finding the one that speaks to your tastes might seem daunting at first. However, by leveraging a combination of strategies, exploration, and reflection, you can open the door to discovering authors who leave you eagerly anticipating their next book. Here’s how to embark on this fulfilling journey.

Reflect on What You Like Reading

The first step to finding a new favorite author is understanding your current reading preferences. Take a moment to reflect on past books you’ve enjoyed. What drew you to those stories? Was it the gripping plot twists, the relatable characters, the beautiful prose, or the vivid world-building? Pinpointing what resonates with you is instrumental in narrowing down your search.

Consider genre as well. Do you gravitate toward science fiction, fantasy, literary fiction, mystery, or romance? Or perhaps you enjoy non-fiction topics like history, self-improvement, or biographies? Knowing your favorite themes and literary styles can help filter choices. For instance, if you adored the thought-provoking world-building of Ursula K. Le Guin, you might enjoy authors like N.K. Jemisin or Ann Leckie. Acknowledging the patterns in your preferences gives you a solid foundation for your search.

Utilize Online Communities and Platforms

The internet offers a goldmine of resources for readers looking to discover their next literary love. From book review sites to social media platforms and reading communities, here’s how to tap into the collective wisdom of fellow readers:

Book Recommendation Websites

Websites like Goodreads and LibraryThing allow users to share reviews, create reading lists, and join discussion groups. Goodreads, in particular, offers personalized recommendations based on your previous ratings. Additionally, the platform’s user-created Listopia rankings can guide you toward books in specific niches or genres. For instance, a list like “Best Cozy Mysteries of the Year” might point you toward your next favorite mystery author.

Subreddits and Forums

Platforms like Reddit host vibrant communities such as r/books or genre-specific forums like r/Fantasy and r/SuggestMeABook. Post a request for recommendations detailing what you’ve enjoyed in the past, and you’re almost certain to receive personalized and thoughtful suggestions from a wide spectrum of readers.

BookTube and BookTok

On platforms like YouTube and TikTok, creators dedicated to the love of books offer reviews, reading vlogs, and curated recommendation lists. The energy and enthusiasm of these creators often provide new insights into books and authors you might not have otherwise considered. Look for creators who align with your literary preferences for targeted advice.

Explore Independent Bookstores and Libraries

Sometimes the best way to find a new favorite author is by stepping away from algorithms and immersing yourself in the physical world of books. Independent bookstores, in particular, often house curated collections that highlight lesser-known authors. Visiting one offers the added bonus of interacting with knowledgeable staff who are enthusiastic about connecting readers with the right books.

Libraries are another treasure trove. Librarians can help identify authors that match your taste, and many libraries host book clubs or author talks that broaden your literary horizons. Borrowing books also gives you the freedom to try new authors without financial commitment.

Don’t overlook the joy of serendipity. Flipping through random books on a shelf and stumbling upon a story that captures your imagination can be one of the most satisfying ways to discover new authors.

Take Advantage of Technology

In addition to platforms like Goodreads, several technology-driven tools and services excel at helping readers discover authors.

Book Recommendation Algorithms

Services like Google Books and Amazon’s book recommendation engine use sophisticated algorithms to suggest titles based on your purchase or viewing history. Although these suggestions might lack the personal touch, they often introduce authors you wouldn’t have otherwise encountered.

Apps for Readers

Apps like The StoryGraph have gained popularity for their tailored recommendations. With its unique focus on tracking your reading mood and preferences, The StoryGraph can guide you to authors with similar styles or themes to books you’ve loved in the past.

Revisit the Classics

Sometimes discovering your next favorite author doesn’t mean exploring just contemporary writing. Checking out classic literature can reveal timeless voices that still resonate today. Reading authors like James Baldwin, Jane Austen, Gabriel García Márquez, or Zora Neale Hurston could introduce you to stories that strike an unexpected chord.

Exploring the classics often serves as a gateway to discovering modern writers who’ve drawn inspiration from them. For example, after enjoying Charlotte Brontë or Mary Shelley, you might appreciate the modern reinterpretations of gothic elements in Sarah Perry’s or Silvia Moreno-Garcia’s works.

Follow the Rabbit Hole from One Favorite Author

A great way to find new authors is to explore the influences, peers, or contemporaries of your current favorites. Did your favorite author frequently reference another writer in interviews or essays? Did they recommend specific books to their readers? These breadcrumbs can lead you directly to authors who share a similar ethos or storytelling style.

Additionally, anthology collections can introduce you to multiple authors within a single volume. Reading short stories or flash fiction anthologies in your favorite genre is a fantastic way to discover new voices without committing to a full novel.

Embrace Literary Awards and Critically-Acclaimed Works

Prestigious literary prizes—such as the Booker Prize, the National Book Award, or the Hugo Awards for science fiction—offer curated lists of outstanding works by exceptional authors. Reviewer-driven awards like the Goodreads Choice Awards can also guide you to authors who appeal to both critics and the public.

Awards can introduce you to international authors as well, broadening your perspective and giving you access to fresh narratives and writing styles. Writers like Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie, Haruki Murakami, or Elena Ferrante have gained global recognition due to their unique stories and voices.

Try Different Formats

Sometimes what you need is a change of pace. If you typically stick to novels, consider exploring other formats like poetry, short stories, graphic novels, or audiobooks. This diversification of reading material can lead you to authors outside your usual realm of discovery.

For instance, if you enjoy introspective and lyrical writing, trying poetry collections might introduce you to a new favorite wordsmith. Likewise, graphic novels often blend stunning visuals with compelling narratives, showcasing unique storytelling by creators like Neil Gaiman or Marjane Satrapi.

Ask Friends and Family

Your social circle likely includes avid readers. Engaging in conversations about books and authors can often yield unexpected recommendations. Share your favorite reads with friends or family and ask about their favorites—especially those who share similar literary tastes.

Better yet, join a local book club or an online group where members frequently share their latest discoveries. Discussions about books almost always lead to enthusiastic suggestions of authors worth exploring.

Stay Open to Experimentation

Finally, keep an open mind. Sometimes your favorite author might not write in the genre or style you typically read. Stepping out of your comfort zone and giving new writing styles or genres a chance often leads to delightful surprises.

Perhaps you’re a fan of hard science fiction but never thought you’d enjoy historical fiction—and then you try an author like Hilary Mantel or Ken Follett and become captivated. Or, a humorous memoir by someone like David Sedaris might unexpectedly resonate even if you don’t normally read non-fiction.

The Journey is the Reward

Finding your next favorite author isn’t just about the destination—it’s about the process of discovery. Every book introduces you to different perspectives, styles, and worlds, enriching your reading life along the way. By reflecting on your taste, utilizing resources, and remaining open to new possibilities, you’ll eventually stumble upon an author whose works feel like they were written just for you.

So go ahead, explore, and enjoy the journey. Your next favorite author is waiting for you.
