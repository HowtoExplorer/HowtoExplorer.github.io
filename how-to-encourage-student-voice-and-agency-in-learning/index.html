How to Encourage Student Voice and Agency in Learning


In today's education system, learners' voices are often diminished, overlooked, or merely given a platform to assert opinions rather than bringing genuine impact on their learning experiences. However, the power of student voice and agency can revolutionize the way we think about education. When students are given the autonomy to take ownership of their learning, they become more engaged, motivated, and invested in their educational journeys. Student voice and agency are interconnected concepts that together foster a sense of empowerment, emotional intelligence, and critical thinking in learners. Student voice is about expressing one's thoughts, feelings, and opinions, whereas agency gives students the opportunity to make informed choices that directly impact their learning. By embracing these concepts, educators can unlock the full potential of their students and create an engaging, learner-centered educational environment. Why is Student Voice and Agency Important?

Students who express their thoughts and opinions have higher levels of motivation, resilience, and long-term engagement with their learning. When students are involved in designing their learning experiences, they develop problem-solving skills, creativity, and a further voice A student-centered approach to learning encourages diverse perspectives, inclusivity, and social cohesion by effectively acknowledging and appreciating all voices within the classroom.
