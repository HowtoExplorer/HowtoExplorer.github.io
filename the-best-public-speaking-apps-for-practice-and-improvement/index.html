The Best Public Speaking Apps for Practice and Improvement


Many people find public speaking intimidating, whether it’s addressing a conference room full of colleagues, giving a toast at a wedding, or delivering a presentation to an audience. Public speaking is a skill that requires confidence, preparation, and consistent practice to master, and thanks to technology, there are now innovative tools that can help you level up your skills from the comfort of your home. Public speaking apps are designed to provide personalized feedback, powerful strategies, and essential tools to help individuals overcome stage fright, hone their communication skills, and become more impactful speakers. In this article, we’ll explore some of the best and most effective apps available for improving public speaking skills.

Why Use Apps for Practicing Public Speaking?

Public speaking apps have revolutionized how individuals prepare for speeches, presentations, or impromptu talks. They provide on-demand resources such as virtual coaching, metrics to track improvement, and interactive features that simulate real-life speaking situations. By integrating apps into your practice routine, you’ll enjoy the following benefits:

  • Accessibility: A speech coach or speaking class may not always be accessible or affordable. Apps bridge this gap, offering guidance anytime, anywhere.
  • Custom Feedback: Many apps provide AI-driven insights into your tone, pace, clarity, and even filler word usage, enabling targeted improvements.
  • Convenience: Apps can seamlessly fit into your schedule, allowing you to practice regularly without disrupting other commitments.
  • Interactive Tools: Features such as speech timing, audience simulation, and pronunciation checks make practicing more engaging and effective.

Now, let’s dive into the best public speaking apps for enhancing your communication skills.

1. Orai: Your Virtual Speaking Coach

Orai is an extremely popular public speaking app designed to build confidence and refine speaking skills in bite-sized, actionable ways. Known for its user-friendly interface and advanced AI analysis tools, Orai provides constructive feedback on various aspects of speaking.

Key Features:

  • Personalized Feedback: Evaluate factors like pacing, tone variation, word clarity, and pronunciation.
  • Filler Word Detection: Helps eliminate words such as “um” or “uh” from your speech.
  • Interactive Practice Modules: Gamified exercises make learning more engaging.
  • Overall Delivery Score: Track progress over time to see measurable improvements.

Orai is particularly useful for anyone looking to prepare for professional presentations or speeches in a structured yet flexible manner.

2. Toastmasters International App

Toastmasters is a widely recognized organization for public speaking and leadership development. Their app brings the principles of their programs into the digital realm, making it easier for members or even non-members to practice and refine their speech skills.

Key Features:

  • Speech Timer: Practice managing time effectively while speaking.
  • Meeting Tracker: For Toastmasters members, track meeting roles and attendance.
  • Access to Resources: Browse educational materials and guidelines for crafting excellent speeches.

While the Toastmasters app is a part of a larger community, it’s still helpful on its own for users looking to improve their organization and speech timing.

3. PromptSmart Pro

PromptSmart Pro is a powerful app for anxiety-free speaking. It uses advanced voice recognition to follow your pace as you read a speech, ensuring no missed cues and perfectly timed delivery. It’s an excellent resource for those who use scripts or outlines when delivering talks.

Key Features:

  • Voice-Tracked Teleprompter: Voice recognition adjusts the scrolling script to your speaking pace.
  • Customizable Prompts: Organize speeches or notes for seamless delivery.
  • Offline Functionality: Perfect for areas without internet access.
  • Mobile-Friendly: Available for both iOS and Android.

This app is ideal for anyone relying on prepared scripts, whether for business presentations, lectures, or performances.

4. Ummo

Ummo is specifically designed to help speakers reduce filler words and maximize speech clarity. Its simple yet powerful feedback system motivates users to refine one of the most important aspects of public speaking: fluency.

Key Features:

  • Real-Time Analysis: Tracks your speech as you practice and identifies areas needing improvement.
  • Filler Word Counter: Provides statistics for “ums,” “uhs,” and other filler words.
  • Speech Pace Metrics: Balance between speaking too fast or too slow.
  • Customizable Goals: Focus on areas most pertinent to you, whether tone or word choice.

Ummo is an excellent tool for both beginners and seasoned speakers looking to clean up their delivery style and project confidence.

5. Speeko

Speeko is an app that makes public speaking approachable, combining science-backed techniques with engaging practice tools. It provides professional coaching-level insights tailored to modern users.

Key Features:

  • Voice Analytics: Evaluate your pitch, pacing, and articulation.
  • Targeted Training Pathways: Focus on storytelling, impromptu speaking, or persuasion skills.
  • Speech Confidence Tracking: Monitors improvement and encourages frequent practice.
  • Learning Library: Access tips, tricks, and guides to professional speaking.

Speeko is designed to be a well-rounded speaking companion, enabling users to grow at their own pace while consistently measuring improvement.

6. Virtual Speech

Virtual Speech takes storytelling to the next level by combining public speaking exercises with immersive virtual reality environments. This app allows users to practice speeches in a simulated setting, making it feel close to the real-world experience.

Key Features:

  • Immersive VR Practices: Simulate boardrooms, conferences, and auditoriums using VR technology.
  • Interactive Scenarios: Practice impromptu speeches or responses to questions.
  • Feedback Tracking: Provides insights into delivery style to assess progress.
  • Skill Courses: Access detailed modules on communication and presentation skills.

Virtual Speech is perfect for people looking to overcome stage fright and adapt confidently to varied speaking scenarios.

7. Sounding Board

Sounding Board is a unique app allowing users to upload their speeches and invite colleagues, mentors, or friends to provide personalized feedback. This collaborative tool emphasizes constructive criticism from real audiences.

Key Features:

  • Collaborative Environment: Share speeches and receive feedback from your trusted circle.
  • Content Structuring: Helps organize talk notes and ensures logical flow.
  • Audience Simulations: Receive additional feedback on audience engagement.

Sounding Board is an excellent app for those who prefer feedback from familiar individuals rather than automated systems.

Other Useful Apps to Consider

While the above apps shine for their targeted public speaking features, there are other platforms worth exploring for specific use cases:

  • TED: Allows users to study and learn from brilliant speakers across industries.
  • Canva: Helps design impactful visual aids to complement speeches.
  • Auditory-specific Apps: Apps like Audacity can help fine-tune voice recordings for clarity.

How to Maximize Your Experience With These Apps

Using public speaking apps effectively is important to see real progress. Follow these best practices for maximizing their value:

  • Set Goals: Start small by targeting one area of improvement, such as pacing or clarity, before expanding to other facets.
  • Consistency: Make practice a regular habit to gradually build confidence and skills.
  • Combine Resources: Pair app insights with real-life practice, such as attending public speaking events.
  • Track Progress: Leverage performance metrics to ensure measurable growth.

Conclusion

Public speaking is a craft that requires dedication, practice, and the right resources to master. Whether you’re a novice looking to build foundational confidence or a seasoned professional preparing for the next big presentation, these public speaking apps provide the tools necessary for continuous improvement. By integrating them into your practice routine and embracing feedback, anyone can grow into a polished, confident speaker ready to impress any audience.
