Credit Score Myths Debunked


A good credit score can be a powerful tool. Not only can it help you secure loans with favorable interest rates, but it can also affect your ability to rent a house, land a job, and even obtain cell phone contracts. With so much on the line, it’s easy to buy into the various myths that float around about credit scores. Let’s debunk some of these common myths to give you the clear facts you need to make the most of your credit.

Myth #1: Closing Credit Card Accounts Will Boost Your Credit Score


It's natural to think that reducing your exposure to credit by closing unused credit card accounts would be good for your credit. However, this is actually a common myth. Closing accounts will not necessarily improve your score. In fact, it can negatively impact your credit utilization ratio—the percentage of your available credit that you've used—which plays a significant role in your credit score.

Instead of closing accounts, consider keeping them open and utilizing them responsibly.

Myth #2: Only Late Payments Hurt Your Credit
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

While late payments can create problems with your credit score, it's not the only factor that can damage your credit. Missing a single payment or not using your credit card responsibly can lead to a decline in your score.

It's essential to create a habit of paying all your bills on time and using credit judiciously to maintain a healthy credit rating.

Myth #3: All Credit Scores Range from 300 to 850
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

This is an easily misunderstood topic. The widely accepted range for credit scores is 300 to 850. However, each credit bureau assigns scores slightly differently, which can result in slight variations.

For instance, Equifax uses a different scoring model than the other two major credit bureaus, Experian and TransUnion. As a result, your Equifax credit score may be a bit different from your Experian or TransUnion scores.

Myth #4: Checking Your Credit Report Damages Your Score
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

One of the most widespread myths about credit scores is that checking your credit report negatively impacts your score. However, it's actually the credit inquiries made by lenders when you apply for credit that can temporarily harm your score.

Where this myth comes from is the existence of hard and soft credit inquiries. A hard inquiry arises when a lender checks your credit as part of an application, while a soft inquiry results from checking your credit report by yourself or postmarked to you by a cell phone company or potential employer.

Hard inquiries have the possibility of lowering your credit scores temporarily, but they're usually small and fade over time. Soft inquiries, on the other hand, don't affect your scores at all.

Myth #5: Paying Off Debts in Full Saves Your Credit
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

While it's no secret that paying off debts can help improve your credit score, your score will not suffer if you don't pay the exact amount off each month. Whether you pay off your debts in full or continue making on-time minimum payments, your score remains largely unaffected.

That being said, eliminating high-interest debt as quickly as possible can help boost your cash flow and make it easier to meet your necessary financial obligations.

Conclusion
~~~~~~~~~~

Understanding your credit can be intimidating, but it doesn't have to be. By debunking these common myths, you can confidently make decisions about managing your credit for a better financial future. Don't fall for the misconceptions that have no substantial basis. Stay well-informed, and trust in your knowledge to make the right choices for your financial well-being. Remember, managing credit responsibly is vital for obtaining loans and financial accommodations with favorable terms. Wrapping it up, do not hesitate to check your credit report regularly and maintain an excellent credit reputation.
