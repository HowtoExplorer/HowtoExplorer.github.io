How to Make Homemade Bagels: Easy and Fun


Bagels have a unique charm that sets them apart from other baked goods. Their chewy texture, glossy exterior, and the ability to customize with different toppings make them a favorite for breakfast, brunch, or a snack anytime. If you’ve ever wanted to try your hand at making bagels from scratch, you’re in for a delightful culinary experience! This guide will take you through the entire process, step by step, ensuring it’s both easy and fun.

The Joy of Making Bagels at Home

Creating homemade bagels is not just about baking; it’s about the satisfaction of crafting something delicious from scratch. The aroma of freshly baked bagels wafting through your kitchen is unmatched, and the taste of a warm, chewy bagel topped with your favorite cream cheese or lox is simply divine. Plus, making bagels at home allows you to experiment with flavors and toppings, tailoring each batch to your preferences.

Ingredients You’ll Need

To make bagels, you’ll need a few essential ingredients that are likely already in your pantry. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 4 cups of bread flour: Bread flour has a higher protein content, which contributes to the bagel’s chewy texture.
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) of active dry yeast: This is what helps your dough rise.
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar: This will feed the yeast and add a hint of sweetness.
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of salt: Essential for flavor.
  • 1 ½ cups of warm water: Helps activate the yeast.
  • Optional toppings: Sesame seeds, poppy seeds, everything bagel seasoning, or coarse salt.

Equipment You’ll Need

Before you start, gather the necessary equipment:

  • Mixing bowl
  • Wooden spoon or dough whisk
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • A clean surface for kneading
  • Large pot for boiling
  • Slotted spoon
  • Baking sheet
  • Parchment paper

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Activate the Yeast

Start by activating your yeast. In a small bowl, combine the warm water and sugar. Sprinkle the yeast over the top and let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This indicates that your yeast is alive and ready to work.

Step 2: Prepare the Dough

In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture. Stir with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms. If the dough is too sticky, you can add a little more flour, but be careful not to overdo it.

Step 3: Knead the Dough

Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. This step is crucial for developing the gluten, which gives bagels their characteristic chewiness.

Step 4: First Rise

Place the kneaded dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm spot for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size. This is your dough’s chance to develop flavor and texture.

Step 5: Shape the Bagels

Once your dough has risen, punch it down to release any air bubbles. Divide the dough into 8 equal pieces for standard-sized bagels or 12 pieces for smaller ones. Roll each piece into a ball and then poke a hole in the center with your finger, stretching it out to create the classic bagel shape. Make sure the hole is large enough, as it will shrink during boiling and baking.

Step 6: Prepare for Boiling

Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). While the oven is heating, bring a large pot of water to a boil. You can add a tablespoon of sugar or honey to the water for extra flavor and sheen.

Step 7: Boil the Bagels

Once the water is boiling, carefully drop in a few bagels at a time. Boil them for about 1-2 minutes on each side. This step is crucial as it helps form the bagel’s crust, giving it that signature glossy appearance. Use a slotted spoon to remove them from the water and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.

Step 8: Add Toppings

Before baking, it’s time to add your favorite toppings. While the bagels are still wet from the boiling, sprinkle on sesame seeds, poppy seeds, everything bagel seasoning, or coarse salt. This is where you can get creative!

Step 9: Bake

Place the bagels in the preheated oven and bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until they are golden brown. The smell of baking bagels will fill your kitchen, promising a delicious treat ahead.

Step 10: Cool and Enjoy

Once baked, remove the bagels from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack. This cooling process allows them to set properly. Once they’re slightly cooled, slice them open, toast to your liking, and enjoy with cream cheese, avocado, or your favorite spreads.

Tips for the Perfect Bagel

  • Experiment with Flavors: Mix in spices or herbs to the dough for a unique flavor. Try adding garlic powder, onion powder, or even cheese for a savory twist.
  • Store Properly: Bagels are best enjoyed fresh. If you have leftovers, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for a couple of days or freeze them for longer storage.
  • Customize Your Boil: Some people like to add baking soda or malt syrup to the boiling water for an even richer flavor and color.

Common Challenges and Solutions

Problem: Bagels are too dense. Solution: Ensure that you are using bread flour, as all-purpose flour may not provide the same chewiness. Also, kneading the dough well is essential for developing gluten.

Problem: Bagels won’t hold their shape. Solution: Make sure to stretch the holes wide enough during shaping, as they tend to shrink during boiling and baking.

Problem: Bagels too chewy or tough. Solution: Over-kneading can create a very tough texture. Knead just until smooth and elastic; a little stickiness is okay!

The Bagel Tradition: A Brief History

Bagels have a fascinating history that dates back to the Jewish communities in Poland in the 17th century. They have evolved from being a simple bread into a beloved staple around the world. The classic New York-style bagel, known for its chewy texture and shiny crust, became popular in the early 20th century as Jewish immigrants brought their recipes to America.

Conclusion: A Fun Culinary Adventure

Making homemade bagels is not only a rewarding experience but also a fun activity to share with family and friends. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, the process is straightforward and allows for creativity. So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and get ready to enjoy the delightful experience of crafting your very own bagels at home!

Embrace the joy of baking, and don’t forget to share your delicious creations with loved ones. Happy bagel-making!
