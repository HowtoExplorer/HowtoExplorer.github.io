10 Creative Uses for Leftover Craft Supplies


As crafters, we’ve all been there - staring at a stash of leftover supplies, wondering what to do with them. Whether it’s a half-used pack of scrapbook paper, a few stray buttons, or a roll of leftover ribbon, it can be frustrating to see perfectly good materials going to waste. But fear not! With a little creativity, those leftover supplies can be transformed into something truly special.

1. Make a Memory Book for a Loved One

Take those leftover scrapbook paper scraps and turn them into a one-of-a-kind memory book for a loved one. Cut out shapes and designs to create a unique and personalized cover, then fill the pages with photos, quotes, and mementos from special moments you’ve shared together. Add some decorative tape, stickers, and other embellishments to make it truly special.

2. Upcycle Old CDs into Wind Chimes

Who needs boring old wind chimes when you can create your own using leftover CDs? Simply drill a hole in the center of each CD, then thread some twine or string through to create a makeshift hanger. Add some small objects like beads, keys, or washers to create a unique sound, then hang them up in a window or from a tree branch.

3. Create a DIY Pom-Pom Garland

Those leftover yarn scraps can be transformed into a colorful and playful pom-pom garland. Simply wrap the yarn around a fork to create individual pom-poms, then thread them onto a piece of string or twine. Hang them up in a child’s bedroom, living room, or even outdoor space to add a pop of color and whimsy.

4. Make a Customized Phone Case

Take those leftover fabric scraps and turn them into a one-of-a-kind phone case. Cut out a piece of fabric to fit your phone, then glue it onto a pre-made case or a piece of cardboard. Add some decorative stitching, buttons, or other embellishments to make it truly unique.

5. Upcycle Old T-Shirts into Reusable Bags

Who needs plastic bags when you can create your own reusable bags using leftover t-shirts? Simply cut out the front and back of the t-shirt, then sew or glue the sides together to create a simple bag. Add some decorative stitching, ribbons, or other embellishments to make it truly special.

6. Create a DIY Terrarium

Those leftover glass containers can be transformed into a beautiful and self-sustaining terrarium. Simply fill the container with a layer of small rocks, then add a layer of soil and your choice of plants. Top it off with a layer of small pebbles or decorative stones, then add a few decorative objects like seashells or small figurines.

7. Make a Customized Keychain

Take those leftover beads and turn them into a one-of-a-kind keychain. Simply string the beads onto a piece of twine or string, then attach a small keyring or clip to the end. Add some decorative stitching, buttons, or other embellishments to make it truly unique.

8. Upcycle Old Cardboard into Planters

Who needs boring old planters when you can create your own using leftover cardboard? Simply cut out a shape or design from the cardboard, then paint or decorate it with markers. Add some soil and your choice of plants, then place it on a windowsill or outdoor space.

9. Create a DIY Paper Mache Bowl

Those leftover paper scraps can be transformed into a beautiful and unique paper mache bowl. Simply tear the paper into small pieces, then soak them in water to create a pulp. Mix the pulp with some glue and water, then apply it to a balloon or bowl shape. Let it dry, then pop the balloon and sand the bowl to create a smooth finish.

10. Make a Customized Zipper Pouch

Take those leftover fabric scraps and turn them into a one-of-a-kind zipper pouch. Simply cut out a piece of fabric to fit your desired pouch size, then sew or glue the sides together to create a simple pouch. Add some decorative stitching, buttons, or other embellishments to make it truly unique.

As you can see, there are countless ways to repurpose leftover craft supplies. With a little creativity and imagination, even the most mundane materials can be transformed into something truly special. So next time you’re staring at a stash of leftover supplies, don’t be afraid to think outside the box and get creative!
