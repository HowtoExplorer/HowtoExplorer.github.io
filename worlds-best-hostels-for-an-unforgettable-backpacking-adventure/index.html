World's Best Hostels for An Unforgettable Backpacking Adventure


Backpacking across the globe is an experience like no other. Nestled deep within the heart of this magnificent adventure is the search for the perfect hostel to call home, even if only for a night. To ensure a truly unforgettable backpacking experience, look no further than these top hostels around the world.

1. The перебити Peanut in Bangkok, Thailand

The first hostel on our list is Theーヌ Peanut in Bangkok, Thailand. This unique hostel is an artistic paradise with murals on every wall, perfect for capturing that Instagram-worthy shot. Although the location is a bit off the beaten path, this hostel makes up for it with its eclectic charm and vibrant atmosphere.

The room layout caters explicitly to the solo backpacker, with twelve-bed mixed dormitories, complete with privacy curtains, power outlets, and lockers for your peace of mind. And let’s not forget the rooftop terrace, offering the perfect respite after a long day of sightseeing.

Be adventurous and indulge in a game of foosball or pool, or challenge fellow travelers to a competitive round of table tennis. If you’re feeling particularly brave, join in on the hostel’s km zero bar crawl, making unforgettable memories with new friends.

2. Eat Love Burger in Osaka, Japan

When exploring the bustling city of Osaka, Japan, a stay at the Eat Love Burger hostel is a must. Winner of the 2017 Hostelworld Award for top-rated hostel in Japan, this quirky hostel embraces its love for burgers, with a delicious burger-themed cafe right on the premises.

Each dormitory room boasts its own unique burger artwork and color scheme, ensuring that your stay at Eat Love Burger is anything but mundane. The large common area and public spaces offer ample opportunities for meeting fellow travelers, swapping stories and forming unlikely friendships.

Enjoy hand-painted wooden tables with artistic flair, powered by free Wi-Fi, and indulge in some mouth-watering Japanese cuisine at the onsite eatery.

3. El Padrino in Cusco, Peru

Amidst the mystical mountains of Cusco, Peru, lies the enchanting hostel, El Padrino. Once a tranquil antique shop, this converted hostel now houses historical artifacts within its walls, adding an extra element of intrigue to this already unique stay.

Boasting a generous rooftop terrace with incredible views of the night sky, this hostel is the perfect spot for stargazing. The beds are adorned with cozy linen and plush pillows offering an unparalleled sleeping experience that rivals even the most luxurious hotels.

Dive into the inviting cocktail lounge area and mix and mingle with fellow backpackers, swapping stories about your adventures in the Andean mountains.

4. Hostel Bowl in Bali, Indonesia

The vibrant and artistic passion of the Hostel Bowl in Bali, Indonesia, brightens your trip from start to finish. With a striking collection of bowls outside the entrance, you can be assured of an assortment of cuisines influenced by the host of cultures found in this region.

Groups and solo travelers alike find friendship and camaraderie in the communal areas, with each floor hosting multiple social hubs with individual seating, tables, and charging stations. Dormitories are spacious and airy, offering individual lighting and storage for those most personal of belongings.

Explore the lush landscapes and terraces that paint this wondrous island, then return to Hostel Bowl to bask in the warmth of a crackling crackling fireplace or a game of foosball with new found friends.

5. Teddy & The Fox in Copenhagen, Denmark

The Teddy & The Fox hostel is housed within a bohemian-chic converted warehouse in the heart of Copenhagen, Denmark. This hostel caters to the tasteful traveler, with each dormitory designed with art installations and unique tapestries adorning the walls.

With an in-house coffee and cocktail bar, and an impressive, immersive library, you’ll find it near impossible to resist delving into literature by candlelight or exploring the Scandinavian flavors on offer at Teddy & The Fox.

Take in the serenity of this thoughtfully designed hostel, where the privacy of modular pod-style beds offers a more intimate night’s rest, away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

Remember, the heart of a backpacking adventure lies not only within the vast landscapes and historical landmarks you explore but within the friendships and memories created in the world’s best hostels. Begin your unforgettable journey into the heart of global culture and experience the warmth of other travelers at these top hostel destinations.
