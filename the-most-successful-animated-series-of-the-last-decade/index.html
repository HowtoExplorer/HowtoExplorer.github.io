The Most Successful Animated Series of the Last Decade


Animated series have long held a unique place in entertainment, captivating audiences of all ages with their imaginative storytelling, vibrant visuals, and memorable characters. Over the past decade, a select group of animated shows have not only entertained viewers but have also achieved remarkable success and cultural significance. This blog post delves into the most successful animated series of the last decade, exploring what made them stand out and their impact on both audiences and the industry.

1. Rick and Morty

Premiering in 2013, Rick and Morty quickly became a cultural phenomenon. Created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon, this adult animated series blends science fiction with dark humor, following the misadventures of an eccentric scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his good-hearted but fretful grandson, Morty Smith.

The show’s success can be attributed to its clever writing, intricate plotlines, and the ability to tackle complex themes such as existentialism, family dynamics, and the multiverse. The unique animation style, combined with a deep understanding of science fiction tropes, attracted a dedicated fanbase and sparked countless memes, making it a staple of internet culture.

Impact and Reception

Rick and Morty has received critical acclaim, winning multiple awards, including the Critics’ Choice Television Award for Best Animated Series. Its influence extends beyond television; the series has inspired video games, merchandise, and even a dedicated fan convention, demonstrating its significant cultural impact.

2. BoJack Horseman

BoJack Horseman, which debuted on Netflix in 2014, is another standout animated series that has reshaped the landscape of adult animation. Created by Raphael Bob-Waksberg, the show follows the life of BoJack, a washed-up actor who struggles with addiction, self-identity, and the consequences of fame.

Unlike many traditional comedies, BoJack Horseman delves into serious themes such as mental health, depression, and the complexities of relationships. Its unique blend of humor and poignancy resonates deeply with viewers, making it a critical darling.

Cultural Significance

The series has been praised for its honest portrayal of mental health issues and its ability to blend humor with heavy topics. Its unique animation style and character-driven storytelling have set a new standard for animated series, influencing many creators in the industry.

3. Adventure Time

Although it concluded in 2018, Adventure Time has left an indelible mark on the animated landscape. Created by Pendleton Ward, the show follows the adventures of a young boy named Finn and his magical dog, Jake, in the Land of Ooo.

Adventure Time is known for its surreal humor, rich world-building, and emotional storytelling. Over its ten seasons, it explored themes of friendship, love, loss, and the complexity of growing up, appealing to both children and adults.

Legacy and Influence

The series has garnered numerous awards, including eight Primetime Emmy Awards. Its legacy continues through spin-offs like Fionna and Cake and the ongoing influence it has on new animated series. Adventure Time has inspired a generation of animators and writers, showcasing the potential for animated storytelling to touch on profound themes.

4. Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Released in 2018, Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse is a groundbreaking animated film that redefined superhero storytelling. Directed by Bob Persichetti, Peter Ramsey, and Rodney Rothman, this film introduced audiences to a multiverse of Spider-People, centering on the teenage Miles Morales.

The film received widespread acclaim for its innovative animation style, engaging narrative, and representation of diverse characters. It successfully brought a new perspective to the Spider-Man franchise, showcasing that anyone can wear the mask.

Awards and Recognition

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature, among numerous other accolades. Its success has paved the way for future animated superhero films, emphasizing the importance of representation and creativity in storytelling.

5. The Dragon Prince

Debuting in 2018 on Netflix, The Dragon Prince is a fantasy animated series created by Aaron Ehasz and Justin Richmond. The show follows two human princes and an elven assassin who must work together to restore peace in their war-torn lands.

With its rich world-building, deep character development, and themes of friendship and redemption, The Dragon Prince has quickly gained a loyal fanbase. The animation style, which blends 3D and 2D techniques, lends a unique aesthetic that sets it apart from other animated series.

Fan Engagement and Future Prospects

The series has garnered praise for its representation of LGBTQ+ characters and its intricate plotlines. Its active fan community and ongoing story arcs suggest that The Dragon Prince will continue to thrive in the coming years.

6. Big Mouth

Big Mouth, created by Nick Kroll, Andrew Goldberg, Mark Levin, and Jennifer Flackett, premiered on Netflix in 2017 and has since gained a reputation for its bold and unapologetic exploration of adolescence. The series follows a group of middle school friends as they navigate the tumultuous journey of puberty, all while being guided by hormone monsters.

The humor in Big Mouth is both raunchy and insightful, tackling topics such as sexual awakening, body image, and mental health. Its candid approach to the challenges of growing up resonates with many viewers, making it a relevant and necessary series in the landscape of animated shows.

Cultural Impact and Reception

Big Mouth has sparked conversations around puberty and mental health, making it a significant player in the realm of adult animation. Its success has led to spin-offs and additional seasons, solidifying its place in modern pop culture.

7. Avatar: The Last Airbender (Netflix Live-Action)

Although originally aired in the mid-2000s, the enduring popularity of Avatar: The Last Airbender has been revitalized with the announcement of a live-action adaptation by Netflix, set to premiere in the near future. Created by Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko, the original animated series follows Aang, the last Airbender, as he embarks on a quest to bring peace to a world divided by elemental nations.

The series is celebrated for its intricate storytelling, character development, and exploration of themes such as war, friendship, and responsibility. Its ability to blend humor with serious themes has endeared it to audiences of all ages.

Lasting Legacy

Avatar: The Last Airbender remains a touchstone in animation, inspiring countless fans and creators alike. The highly anticipated live-action adaptation highlights the lasting impact of the original series, showcasing its relevance in today’s entertainment landscape.

Conclusion

The last decade has seen a remarkable evolution in animated series, with a diverse array of shows captivating audiences and pushing the boundaries of storytelling. From the dark humor of Rick and Morty to the heartfelt narratives of BoJack Horseman, these series have explored complex themes and characters, resonating with viewers on multiple levels.

As the landscape of animated series continues to evolve, these successful shows have set the standard for creativity, representation, and innovation. Whether through groundbreaking animation techniques or bold storytelling, the most successful animated series of the last decade have left an indelible mark on the industry, inspiring future generations of animators and storytellers. As we look forward, it’s exciting to consider what new narratives and artistic visions will emerge in the world of animation.
