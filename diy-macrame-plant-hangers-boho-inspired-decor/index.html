DIY Macrame Plant Hangers: Boho-Inspired Decor


Are you looking for a creative way to add a touch of bohemian style to your home? Look no further than DIY macrame plant hangers! These beautiful and intricate designs can bring a sense of charm and elegance to any space. In this blog post, we’ll guide you through the process of creating your own macrame plant hangers, step by step.

Materials You’ll Need

To get started, gather the following materials:

  • Macrame cord: Choose a sturdy and durable cord that can hold the weight of your plants. Natural cotton cord is a popular choice.

  • Scissors: You’ll need a sharp pair of scissors to cut the cord and trim any excess.

  • A plant pot: Select a pot that complements the overall aesthetic you’re aiming for.

  • A hook or ring: This will be used to hang your macrame plant hanger.

Step 1: Measure and Cut the Cord

Begin by measuring and cutting eight equal lengths of macrame cord. The length will depend on how long you want your plant hanger to be, so consider the height of the space where you plan to hang it. A good starting point is around four feet (120 cm) for each piece of cord.

Step 2: Create the Base Knot

Take all eight cords and fold them in half. This will give you sixteen strands to work with. Now, create a loop at the top and secure it with a knot. This loop will be used to hang your plant hanger.

Step 3: Start the Macrame Knots

Divide the sixteen strands into four groups of four. Take the first group and create a square knot by crossing the left two cords over the middle two cords. Then, pass the right two cords over the crossed cords and through the loop created on the left. Pull tight to secure the knot.

Repeat this process with the remaining three groups of cords, creating a total of four square knots.

Step 4: Form the Body of the Plant Hanger

Now, take one cord from the first group and pair it with a cord from the adjacent group. Tie a square knot using these two cords. Repeat this process with the remaining cords until you have created a row of square knots.

Continue this pattern, alternating the cords from each group, until you’ve reached the desired length for your plant hanger. Remember to leave enough space at the bottom for your plant pot.

Step 5: Finish with a Gathering Knot

To finish your macrame plant hanger, gather all the cords together and tie a secure knot at the bottom. This will hold your plant pot in place.

Step 6: Hang and Enjoy!

Attach a hook or ring to the loop at the top of your plant hanger and hang it in your desired location. Carefully place your plant pot inside, ensuring it is secure. Step back and admire your beautiful DIY macrame plant hanger!

Conclusion

DIY macrame plant hangers are a fantastic way to add a boho-inspired touch to your home decor. With just a few simple materials and some basic knotting techniques, you can create stunning and unique plant hangers that will elevate the style of any room. So why not unleash your creativity and give it a try? Get started on your own macrame plant hanger today and watch as your home comes alive with natural beauty and bohemian flair.
