Incredibly Bizarre: Mysterious Landmarks That Will Leave You Stunned


Image, if you may, the skyline of London with its iconic brick buildings and Edwardian marvels. A significant focal point amidst the concrete jungle - stands the Willis Towers Watson Building. Though seemingly unremarkable, the building is best known for its bizarre square-cut glass faḃade. Each of the 1500+ panes is intricately designed to follow a specific pattern that appears simply random when viewed from ground level. Add the building’s curved outer layer, and you’ve got yourself a perplexing bee-hive-like structure that has been described by some as an ‘understated’ Bauhaus masterpiece.

The Crooked House, Sopot, Poland

Traveling further into architectural curiosities, you’ll find the Crooked House in Sopot, a seaside resort in northern Poland. The edifice is undeniably quirky in its façade - its frame twisted, spiraling, and at times, even leaning. Conjured by architect Danuta Mlynarska and inspired by Hansel and Gretel’s gingerbread house from a famous fairy tale, the Crooked House has earned its place amongst the list of bizarre landmarks. Wander around the interiors, and you’ll find that the distortion does not just lie outside. Every room appears as if it belongs to an entirely different world - and trust me, it thoroughly captivates.

The Meanders, Prague, Czech Republic

An ode to present-day worldliness, the Meanders is another architectural marvel in the heart of Prague. A collection of serpentine tunnels hand-carved meticulously to create an awe-inspiring residential complex, it’s no wonder that this structure has intrigued many visitors. Completed in the 13th century, these tunnels boast of massive looping pathways that are rumored to provide residents with a secure passage to the castle above. Dare to take a tour, and you’ll soon find yourself roped into chasing whispers of secret passageways and untold legends.

Statue of Liberty, Torres Strait Islands, Australia

In the farthest reaches of the Torres Strait Islands in Australia lies the ‘Statue of Liberty.’ No, not the famed NYC harbor statue nor a miniature replica - but a 4.9-meter tall stone monolith that reigns over the island. Although unknown to the rest of the world until 2006, Islanders claim their ‘Statue’ holds a spiritual significance, with the figure believed to ward off evil spirits. And while the real deal statue’s enigma revolves around its origin, it’s certainly endearing to think that this island’s ‘Statue’ might hold a unique legacy of its own.

Moa’s Grave, Otago Peninsula, New Zealand

Stepping into poetic history territory is Moa’s Grave. While not an actual grave, this mysterious landmark does pay homage to the ancient New Zealand bird - Moa. Standing 24 meters tall, this pseudo-henge comprises of limestone pillars carved like tree trunks that stretch into the sky. Installed in the 1990s by poet and environmentalist artist, Min Kim, Moa’s Grave acts as both a memorial and protectorate for the extinct bird, inviting those who wander its path to partake in a moment’s silence, and to remember the ancient creature that once roamed New Zealand.

Kaplica Czaszek, Poland

Beyond description, Kaplica Czaszek is undoubtedly a sight to behold. Literally translating to ‘Chapel of Bones,’ this underground crypt boasts a peculiar arrangement of over 21,000 human skulls and bones! Artistically placed in ornate disgustingness upon the walls, floor, and ceiling, this chilling chapel in the town of Czestochowa is often associated with mystical symbolism, guiding one’s soul towards the passage to heaven.

While the aforementioned landmarks could undoubtedly serve as strange, sinister narratives for a horror film, there’s no denying their ability to pique curiosity. Each site approaches the ordinary from an extraordinary standpoint, lending itself to the unimaginable and the sublime. Whether it’s through an architects’ whims, spiritual sentiment, or natural oddity, these bizarre landmarks stun you into realizing the true beauty of existence – how simplistic concepts of reality are, indeed, a maze of perplexity.
