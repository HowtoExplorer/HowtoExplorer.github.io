The Pomodoro Technique: Boosting Productivity Through Time Management

The Pomodoro Technique is a simple yet powerful productivity hack developed in the late 1980s by Francesco Cirillo. Originally conceived as a time-management system for students, it has since become popular among professionals and all those seeking to boost their productivity. The name ‘Pomodoro’ is derived from the Italian word for ’tomato,’ as Cirillo used a tomato-shaped kitchen timer during the early stages of his technique. The essence of the Pomodoro Technique lies in its intuitive and straightforward method of breaking down work into manageable, focused intervals, separated by brief breaks. This system aims to eliminate distractions, maintain focus, and reduce anxiety associated with extended periods of work. ...
