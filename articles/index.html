A deductible is the amount of money you pay out of pocket before your insurance policy kicks in to cover repairs or replacements of damaged or lost property. With so many different options available, it can be overwhelming to determine which deductible is right for you. Understanding Deductible Options Most home insurance policies offer one or more deductible options, which can range from 0% to 5% or more of the policy's total value. For example, if your home is insured for $200,000 and your deductible is 2% of the policy's total value, you would have to pay $4,000 out of pocket before your insurer would start covering repairs. In exchange for a higher deductible, policyholders typically enjoy lower premiums. This can be a great option for those who feel they can afford to absorb more of the risk.
