SPF, or Sun Protection Factor, is a measure of how well a sunscreen can protect your skin from the damaging effects of the sun’s ultraviolet (UV) radiation. It indicates how long you can stay in the sun without getting sunburned compared to the time it would take for your skin to burn without any sunscreen.
