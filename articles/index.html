Deserts are formed through a combination of geological and climatic factors. In some cases, deserts are created by the movement of tectonic plates, which can push up mountains and create dry riverbeds. In other cases, deserts are the result of a shift in global climate patterns, such as the cooling of the Earth's atmosphere, which can lead to the formation of cold deserts. Types of Deserts There are several types of deserts, each with its unique characteristics. Hot deserts, such as the Sahara and the Mojave, are characterized by high temperatures and low humidity. Coastal deserts, such as the Atacama in Chile, are formed by the fog and mist that blows in from the ocean, creating a unique microclimate.
