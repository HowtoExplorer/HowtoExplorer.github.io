How Literature Reflects Societal Changes


Literature has long been a window into the human experience, offering a unique lens through which to examine the complexities of society. From the tumultuous social commentary of the Victorian era to the dystopian warnings of the climate crisis, literature has consistently reflected the aspirations, fears, and concerns of the time. This essay will explore the ways in which literature has mirrored the transformations of society, from the emergence of superstition in the 17th century to the exploration of technology-facilitated identity in the present day.
