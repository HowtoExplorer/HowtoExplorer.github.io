How to Write a Resume That Gets You Noticed


Creating a resume that stands out is one of the most important steps in securing a job interview. In a competitive job market, hiring managers often receive dozens, if not hundreds, of resumes for each position they post. This means you only have a brief moment to make a strong impression. A well-crafted resume doesn’t just list your qualifications; it showcases your skills, achievements, and potential value to the employer.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the essential steps to writing a resume that grabs attention, highlights your strengths, and increases your chances of landing your desired job.

1. Understand the Purpose of a Resume

Before diving into the specifics, it’s important to understand the primary goal of a resume: to present a snapshot of your professional background and skills to potential employers. Your resume should make it easy for a hiring manager to quickly determine if you’re a good fit for the position. It is not a comprehensive life history but a marketing tool designed to showcase why you’re the best candidate for the job.

The first step to writing an impactful resume is to focus on what employers are really looking for: relevant skills, experience, and qualifications that match the job description. Tailor your resume to fit the specific role you are applying for.

2. Choose the Right Resume Format

The format of your resume can greatly influence how your qualifications are perceived. There are several common resume formats, each with its benefits. The three most popular formats are:

2.1 Chronological Resume Format

The chronological format is the most traditional and widely used. It focuses on your work experience and presents your professional history in reverse chronological order (most recent experience first). This format works well for those with a steady career progression in one field.

Pros:

  • Clear and easy to follow.
  • Ideal for those with consistent work experience.
  • Demonstrates career growth.

Cons:

  • Not suitable for job seekers with gaps in their employment history or frequent job changes.

2.2 Functional Resume Format

The functional format focuses on your skills and qualifications rather than the timeline of your work history. This format is best for people changing careers, re-entering the workforce, or those with gaps in their employment.

Pros:

  • Highlights skills and accomplishments over work history.
  • Suitable for career changers or those with gaps in employment.

Cons:

  • Some hiring managers may find it harder to follow.
  • Can raise red flags if not executed properly.

2.3 Combination Resume Format

As the name suggests, the combination format blends both the chronological and functional styles. It allows you to highlight your skills at the top of the resume and then showcase your employment history.

Pros:

  • Provides a comprehensive view of your qualifications and experience.
  • Flexible enough to emphasize skills or employment history depending on your background.

Cons:

  • Can become lengthy if not carefully structured.
  • May be overwhelming if there’s too much information.

Each format has its advantages, but choosing the right one depends on your experience, career goals, and how you want to present yourself to employers.

3. Start with a Strong Header

Your resume’s header is the first thing a hiring manager will see, so make sure it’s clear, professional, and easy to find. Your header should include the following information:

  • Full Name: Use your full legal name (no nicknames).
  • Phone Number: Make sure your voicemail is professional.
  • Email Address: Use a professional email address, ideally one with your name.
  • LinkedIn Profile: Include your LinkedIn URL if it’s up to date and reflects your professional brand.
  • Location: You don’t need to list your full address, but including your city and state can be helpful.

If you have a personal website or portfolio, consider including that in the header as well. Just be sure it’s relevant to the job you’re applying for.

4. Write a Compelling Summary or Objective Statement

A resume summary or objective statement is your opportunity to make a great first impression. It should be concise and provide a quick overview of who you are as a professional.

4.1 Resume Summary

A resume summary is ideal for candidates with significant experience. It highlights your key skills, accomplishments, and professional identity.

Example: “Results-oriented marketing professional with over 10 years of experience in developing digital marketing strategies and driving brand awareness. Proven track record in leading successful campaigns that increase revenue and engage customers.”

4.2 Resume Objective

A resume objective is often used by those early in their careers or those changing industries. It focuses on the skills you want to develop or the type of role you’re seeking.

Example: “Motivated recent graduate seeking a challenging position in software development where I can utilize my programming skills and contribute to innovative projects.”

Keep your summary or objective short, no more than 2-4 lines, and be sure to focus on what you can bring to the table for the employer.

5. Highlight Your Skills and Achievements

Employers are interested in what you can do for them. Therefore, your resume should emphasize the skills and achievements that make you a great fit for the role.

5.1 Skills Section

A skills section is essential for quickly demonstrating your abilities. Be sure to list both hard skills (technical skills specific to your job) and soft skills (personal attributes that help you work well with others). Tailor this section to match the skills outlined in the job description.

Examples of Hard Skills:

  • Data analysis
  • Graphic design software (Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator)
  • Coding languages (Java, Python)

Examples of Soft Skills:

  • Team collaboration
  • Time management
  • Problem-solving

5.2 Achievements and Accomplishments

Instead of simply listing your responsibilities, focus on specific accomplishments that demonstrate your ability to produce results. Use quantifiable metrics whenever possible to highlight your impact.

Example:

  • Managed a team of 10 marketers to increase lead generation by 25% over six months.
  • Developed a new customer service strategy that resulted in a 15% decrease in customer complaints.

If you’ve received awards or recognitions, include them in this section as well. Be specific and use numbers to make your accomplishments stand out.

6. Showcase Your Professional Experience

Your professional experience section is the heart of your resume. This section should detail your past roles and responsibilities, focusing on those that are most relevant to the job you’re applying for. List your previous positions in reverse chronological order.

Each entry should include the following:

  • Job Title: Clearly state the position you held.
  • Company Name: Include the name of the company and its location (city, state).
  • Dates of Employment: Include the month and year you started and ended each role.
  • Key Responsibilities and Achievements: Highlight your main tasks and specific accomplishments.

Use bullet points to make this section easy to read, and focus on results rather than just listing tasks. When possible, quantify your achievements to demonstrate your impact.

7. Include Your Education

If you have a degree or relevant certifications, include them on your resume. List your education in reverse chronological order as well, including the following:

  • Degree: Specify the degree you earned (e.g., Bachelor of Arts in Business Administration).
  • Institution: Name the college or university you attended.
  • Graduation Date: Include the month and year you graduated or expect to graduate.
  • Relevant Coursework (optional): If you’re a recent graduate or changing careers, you can include relevant coursework that supports your application.

If you have certifications, include them in a separate section. Some fields require specific certifications (e.g., Project Management Professional, Certified Public Accountant), so be sure to list those.

8. Add Additional Sections as Needed

Depending on your background and the role you’re applying for, you may want to include additional sections such as:

  • Volunteer Work: Include any volunteer experiences that highlight transferable skills.
  • Awards and Honors: Recognize any accolades or awards you’ve received.
  • Languages: If you speak multiple languages, list them.
  • Publications: For academic or research-focused positions, include any publications or research projects.
  • Professional Memberships: List any relevant professional organizations you belong to.

Make sure these sections are relevant to the position you’re applying for and don’t overwhelm the resume with unnecessary information.

9. Tailor Your Resume for Each Job Application

One of the most important tips for writing a standout resume is to tailor it for each job you apply to. This means reviewing the job description carefully and adjusting your resume to highlight the skills and experience that are most relevant to the role.

  • Use keywords from the job description to help your resume pass through Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).
  • Emphasize your most relevant experiences and skills based on what the employer is looking for.

Tailoring your resume shows that you’ve taken the time to understand the role and that you are genuinely interested in the position.

10. Proofread and Edit

Before submitting your resume, it’s crucial to proofread it thoroughly. A resume with spelling errors, grammatical mistakes, or inconsistencies can create a negative impression. Take the time to carefully review your resume and consider asking someone else to proofread it as well.

Check for:

  • Consistent formatting (font size, bullet points, spacing).
  • Accurate dates and company names.
  • Proper grammar and punctuation.
  • Clear and concise language.

Conclusion

Writing a resume that gets you noticed takes time, effort, and attention to detail. By following the tips and strategies outlined in this guide, you can create a resume that stands out to hiring managers, highlights your qualifications, and increases your chances of landing an interview.

Remember, your resume is a marketing document designed to sell your skills and experience to potential employers. Keep it focused, tailored, and results-oriented, and you’ll be well on your way to securing the job of your dreams.
