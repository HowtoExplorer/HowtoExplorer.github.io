Resume Tips for Military Veterans


Are you a military veteran transitioning into civilian life and looking to create a powerful resume that highlights your unique skills and experiences? Crafting a resume that effectively communicates your military background to potential employers can be a challenging task. However, with the right guidance and strategies, you can create a compelling resume that grabs the attention of hiring managers. In this blog post, we will provide you with valuable resume tips specifically tailored to military veterans to help you land your desired job in the civilian workforce.

1. Translate Military Jargon

One of the most crucial aspects of creating a resume as a military veteran is to translate your military jargon into civilian terms. While military acronyms and terminology are well-understood within the military community, they may be confusing to civilian employers. Make sure to clearly explain your military roles, responsibilities, and achievements in a way that is easily understandable to individuals outside the military.

2. Highlight Transferable Skills

Military veterans possess a wide range of transferable skills that are highly valuable in the civilian workplace. Skills such as leadership, teamwork, problem-solving, adaptability, and discipline are all qualities that employers look for in potential candidates. Be sure to highlight these transferable skills prominently in your resume to showcase how your military experience makes you a strong candidate for the job.

3. Quantify Your Achievements

When describing your accomplishments in the military on your resume, try to quantify them whenever possible. Whether it’s the number of team members you led, the percentage of improvement you achieved, or the amount of budget you managed, providing quantifiable metrics adds credibility to your achievements and demonstrates the impact you had in your military roles.

4. Customize for Each Job Application

While it may be tempting to create a generic resume to send out to multiple employers, customizing your resume for each job application can significantly increase your chances of success. Tailor your resume to highlight the specific skills and experiences that align with the job requirements of each position you apply for. This shows employers that you have taken the time to understand their needs and are genuinely interested in the role.

5. Seek Support and Feedback

Don’t hesitate to seek support and feedback on your resume from fellow veterans, career counselors, or professional resume writers. Getting a second opinion can provide valuable insights and help you identify areas for improvement in your resume. Additionally, reaching out to veteran support organizations or career transition programs can connect you with resources specifically designed to assist military veterans in their job search.

6. Use a Professional Format

Ensure that your resume follows a clean and professional format that is easy to read and navigate. Use clear headings, bullet points, and concise language to present your information effectively. Remember that hiring managers often have limited time to review each resume, so make it easy for them to quickly identify your key qualifications and experiences.

7. Showcase Continuous Learning

Demonstrate your commitment to continuous learning and professional development by including any relevant certifications, training programs, or educational courses you have completed. Highlighting your efforts to expand your skills outside of your military duties shows employers that you are proactive, motivated, and dedicated to personal growth.

By implementing these resume tips tailored specifically for military veterans, you can create a compelling resume that effectively communicates your unique strengths and experiences to potential employers. Remember that transitioning from a military career to a civilian job market can be a challenging process, but with the right approach and a well-crafted resume, you can successfully navigate this transition and secure fulfilling employment opportunities.

Best of luck with your job search, and thank you for your service!
