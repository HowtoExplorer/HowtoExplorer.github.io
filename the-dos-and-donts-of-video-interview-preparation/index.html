The Dos and Don'ts of Video Interview Preparation


In today’s digital age, video interviews have become a common part of the job application process. Whether you’re a seasoned professional or a recent graduate, mastering the art of video interview preparation is essential for securing your dream job. However, many candidates underestimate the nuances of presenting themselves effectively in a virtual setting. To help you navigate this modern form of interviewing, here are the dos and don’ts of video interview preparation.

The Dos

1. Test Your Technology

Before the interview, ensure that your internet connection, webcam, and microphone are all functioning properly. Conduct a test call with a friend or family member to check the audio and video quality. Being technologically prepared will minimize the risk of technical glitches during the actual interview.

2. Choose a Professional Setting

Select a quiet, well-lit space for your video interview. Consider using a neutral background to minimize distractions. Ensure that the area is tidy and free from clutter. A professional setting will convey a sense of competence and attention to detail.

3. Dress Appropriately

Dress as you would for an in-person interview, from head to toe. Even though the interviewer may only see the top half of your outfit, dressing professionally from head to toe will help you feel confident and composed. Avoid wearing distracting patterns or overly bright colors that may not translate well on camera.

4. Maintain Eye Contact

During the interview, look directly at the camera to create the impression of eye contact. This will establish a connection with the interviewer and demonstrate your engagement. Practice speaking naturally while maintaining eye contact with the camera.

5. Prepare Your Environment

Have a copy of your resume, notes, and any relevant documents within reach. Be ready to discuss your qualifications and experiences confidently. Take advantage of the digital format by having key points or examples visible on your screen to reference during the interview.

6. Practice, Practice, Practice

Familiarize yourself with common video interview platforms and practice answering potential questions. Record yourself answering typical interview questions and review the footage to identify areas for improvement. Practicing will help you appear more polished and composed during the actual interview.

The Don’ts

1. Don’t Underestimate the Setup

Avoid leaving the technical setup to the last minute. Rushing to troubleshoot technical issues right before the interview can create unnecessary stress. Plan ahead and test your setup well in advance to avoid any last-minute complications.

2. Don’t Neglect Body Language

Be mindful of your body language throughout the interview. Avoid slouching, fidgeting, or making distracting movements. Sit up straight, maintain a relaxed yet professional posture, and use expressive gestures to convey your enthusiasm.

3. Don’t Forget About Timing

Respect the interviewer’s time by being punctual. Log in to the video interview platform a few minutes early to ensure everything is functioning correctly. Avoid scheduling other appointments or commitments that may cause you to rush through the interview.

4. Don’t Overlook Distractions

Minimize potential distractions by informing those around you of the interview and asking for privacy. Silence or turn off any notifications on your computer or phone. Distractions can disrupt your flow of thought and give the impression of disinterest.

5. Don’t Wing It

While spontaneity is valuable, treating a video interview as an improvised conversation can lead to rambling or unclear responses. Prepare thoughtful answers to common interview questions and conduct thorough research on the company and position beforehand.

6. Don’t Neglect Follow-Up

After the interview, send a personalized thank-you email to express your appreciation for the opportunity. Emphasize your interest in the position and briefly reiterate your qualifications. A well-crafted follow-up demonstrates your professionalism and enthusiasm.

Mastering the art of video interview preparation requires attention to detail, practice, and a thorough understanding of the virtual environment. By following these dos and don’ts, you can confidently navigate the world of video interviews and make a lasting impression on potential employers.

Remember, preparation is the key to success in any interview, and the virtual realm is no exception. Embrace the opportunity to showcase your skills and expertise in a digital format, and let your professionalism and confidence shine through the screen. Good luck!
