How to Make Your Own Wall Hangings: DIY Ideas


Wall hangings are a timeless and stylish way to add character, warmth, and creativity to your space. They can instantly elevate any room, making even the simplest walls come alive with personality. Instead of buying premade décor, creating your own wall hangings offers a unique opportunity to express yourself while also saving some money. Crafting your own pieces transforms your home into an extension of your creativity, and it’s easier than you might think! Let’s dive into some DIY ideas, materials, and techniques to help you design stunning wall art.

The Benefits of DIY Wall Hangings

Before we jump into the how-to’s, it’s worth exploring why DIY wall art is an excellent choice:

  1. Personalization: Creating your own wall art ensures every item is tailored to your taste, style, and color scheme.
  2. Affordability: Crafting at home is often much cheaper than purchasing from a store, especially for large or custom pieces.
  3. Sustainability: Utilize materials you already have or repurpose old items for a sustainable touch.
  4. Therapeutic Creativity: DIY projects can be a relaxing hobby, allowing you to destress and channel your imagination into something tangible.

Essential Materials and Tools

The materials required for DIY wall hangings depend on the design you choose, but these are some versatile basics to have on hand:

  • Textiles (yarn, fabric, twine, or rope)
  • Wooden dowels, embroidery hoops, or sticks
  • Scissors, craft glue, and measuring tape
  • Paints, brushes, and markers
  • Natural elements such as driftwood, feathers, beads, or dried flowers
  • Sewing needles, thread, or a hot glue gun for construction
  • Baskets, old picture frames, or reclaimed items for repurposing

With these basics in your arsenal, you’re ready to tackle a variety of fun projects. Let’s walk through some of the most popular DIY wall hanging ideas that you can try.

1. Macrame Wall Art

One of the most popular forms of textile wall art, macrame brings a boho-chic vibe to any home. It involves knotting cords into intricate designs and patterns, and the results can range from simple geometric hangings to elaborate cascades.

How to Make It:

  • Start by cutting several pieces of cotton cord or yarn to your desired length (longer cords will allow for more complex designs).
  • Attach the cords to a wooden dowel, hoop, or stick using the “lark’s head knot.”
  • Use common macrame knots like the square knot, half hitch, and spiral knot to create your design. There are countless tutorials online for beginners.
  • Once your piece is finished, trim the cords evenly or leave them in a fringe style for texture.

Customization Tip:

Experiment with adding beads, tassels, or dip-dying the cords for extra flair. You can also use colorful yarns if you’re looking for something more playful.

2. Upcycled Fabric Weaving

For a more textured and rustic look, consider making a woven wall hanging using scraps of fabric or old clothes. Weaving allows you to incorporate bold patterns and beautiful textures, making it a great addition to a cozy interior.

How to Make It:

  • Use a wooden frame or large embroidery hoop as your weaving base. Attach parallel strings to mimic a loom.
  • Cut fabric into strips and weave them over and under the strings in your desired pattern.
  • Alternate colors, textures, and thicknesses to add dimension.
  • When finished, secure the fabric edges and attach the frame to the wall.

Customization Tip:

Incorporate different fabrics like denim, linen, or even metallic accents for an eclectic mix of textures.

3. Nature-Inspired Wall Hangings

Bring an element of the outdoors into your home by using natural materials. Driftwood, dried flowers, feathers, and even small pieces of bark or stones can create beautiful, organic designs.

How to Make It:

  • Collect natural materials like sticks, shells, or dried branches during your next outdoor adventure.
  • Assemble the items on a sturdy stick or piece of driftwood. You can tie them with twine, glue them, or use metal clamps for a more modern look.
  • If you’re adding dried flowers, consider arranging them in small bundles and securing them with string.
  • Add a twine loop for hanging.

Customization Tip:

Paint the driftwood gold or white for a chic, polished look, or leave it raw for a more rustic, earthy appeal.

4. Geometric Wooden Wall Art

For a modern, minimalistic vibe, create a geometric wall hanging using wooden dowels, blocks, or embroidery hoops. This style is great for adding symmetry and understated elegance to your walls.

How to Make It:

  • Arrange wooden dowels or blocks into your preferred geometric shape (triangles, squares, hexagons, etc.).
  • Use hot glue, nails, or string to hold the pieces together.
  • Paint the wood in solid colors, gradients, or leave it unfinished for a natural tone.
  • Attach a string at the top for hanging.

Customization Tip:

Mix in metals, such as copper pipes, with the wood for a trendy industrial-meets-modern design.

5. Canvas Art with a Twist

If you enjoy painting but want something a little different from traditional framed artwork, consider painting directly onto a stretched canvas or experimenting with mixed media.

How to Make It:

  • Start with a blank canvas and use acrylic or watercolor paints to create your design.
  • Add embellishments like dried leaves, cut-out paper shapes, or textured fabric elements to layer your design.
  • For an abstract look, create splatter effects with paint, or use a palette knife to apply thick strokes.

Customization Tip:

Incorporate metallic paint or gold foil for an eye-catching shimmer.

6. Embroidery Hoop Creations

Embroidery hoops are inexpensive, versatile, and perfect for making lightweight wall hangings. These circular frames offer near-infinite possibilities for crafting.

How to Make It:

  • Stretch colorful fabric or gauze into the embroidery hoop and tighten it.
  • Embroider simple designs or stitch patterns onto the fabric using contrasting threads.
  • Alternatively, glue on lightweight items like pressed flowers, small beads, or other decorative accents.

Customization Tip:

Create a grouping with different-sized hoops for a unique statement wall.

7. String Art Masterpieces

String art is a playful yet precise craft that creates bold outlines or intricate patterns on wooden backdrops.

How to Make It:

  • Start with a piece of wood or similarly sturdy base.
  • Hammer nails into the wood in the shape of your desired design (e.g., a heart, an animal, or a geometric pattern).
  • Wrap colored string or yarn around the nails to fill in the design or accentuate the outline.

Customization Tip:

Paint the wood base before adding the string for an additional contrast.

8. Paper-Based Wall Hangings

For a lightweight option, paper wall hangings are a quick and budget-friendly way to add charm to your décor.

How to Make It:

  • Cut stiff paper or cardstock into decorative shapes, such as geometric designs, flowers, or abstract patterns.
  • Layer the papers to create a three-dimensional look.
  • Attach them to string or a dowel and hang.

Customization Tip:

Invest in patterned or metallic paper for extra elegance, or use recycled paper for an eco-friendly approach.

Tips for Hanging Your Creations

Once you’ve completed your DIY wall hangings, it’s important to display them properly to maximize their impact:

  • Choose the Right Spot: Hang your art in a location that complements the rest of your décor without overwhelming the room.
  • Height Matters: A general rule is to hang wall art at eye level, but consider the proportions of your space. Larger pieces may need to go slightly higher to match the room’s balance.
  • Group Strategically: When creating a gallery wall or grouping hangings together, experiment with arrangements on the floor before committing to nails or adhesives.

Making your own wall hangings is not only a rewarding creative project but also a deliberate way to personalize your environment. By exploring different styles, materials, and designs, you can craft art pieces that are as unique as you are. So, gather your supplies, let your imagination soar, and start transforming those empty walls into stunning focal points that reflect your personality and creativity!
