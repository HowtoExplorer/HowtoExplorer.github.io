10 Must-See UNESCO World Heritage Sites in South America


South America is a treasure trove of rich history, diverse cultures, and awe-inspiring natural beauty. Home to an incredible 30 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, the continent boasts an array of incredible landscapes, ancient ruins, and vibrant cities that are a testament to human ingenuity and creativity. From the majestic Iguazu Falls to the historic city of Cartagena, we delve into the top 10 must-see UNESCO World Heritage Sites in South America that will leave you in awe.

  1. Galapagos Islands, Ecuador Located off the coast of Ecuador, the Galapagos Islands are a living laboratory of evolution and biodiversity. This unique ecosystem has been largely untouched by human activity, allowing visitors to experience the unspoiled beauty of nature. The islands are home to an incredible array of wildlife, including giant tortoises, marine iguanas, and blue-footed boobies, making them a must-visit destination for any nature lover.
  2. Cuzco and Machu Picchu, Peru In the heart of the Andes lies the ancient Inca city of Cuzco, a testament to the engineering prowess of the Incas. This sprawling city was once the capital of the Inca Empire and features impressive stonework, intricate carvings, and stunning architecture. From Cuzco, visitors can hike the famous Inca Trail to Machu Picchu, one of the Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  3. Iguaçu National Park, Argentina/Brazil Situated on the border of Argentina and Brazil, Iguaçu National Park is home to the breathtaking Iguazu Falls, one of the largest waterfalls in the world. Consisting of over 275 individual cascades, the falls create a misty veil that rises into the air, shrouding the surrounding area in a mystical beauty. Visitors can hike, picnic, or simply marvel at the sheer power and majesty of these stunning falls.
  4. Chan Chan, Peru Chan Chan is the largest mud city in the world and was once the capital of the Chimú Empire. Constructed in the 9th century, the city boasts an impressive array of mud-brick architecture, including grand temples, palaces, and fortifications. Visitors can explore the city's ruins, including the elaborate decorations and intricate stone carvings that adorn the buildings.
  5. San Miguel de Allende, Mexico This charming colonial town in central Mexico is a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its well-preserved historic center and picturesque streets. Visitors can wander through the cobblestone streets, admiring the colorful buildings, beautifully decorated churches, and lively town square. San Miguel de Allende is also a popular destination for art lovers, with its many galleries and museums showcasing the work of local and international artists.
  6. Cartagena, Colombia Founded by the Spanish in the 16th century, Cartagena is one of the most historic cities in South America. Its ancient walled city is a maze of colorful buildings, bustling markets, and grand churches. Visitors can explore the city's many fortifications, including the imposing Castillo San Felipe de Barajas, or wander through the winding streets, taking in the vibrant atmosphere and bustling energy of this incredible city.
  7. Cunitas de Úmbria, Brazil The Cunitas de Umbria are a collection of ancient stone churches that were built into the sides of hills in the 17th and 18th centuries. Located in the state of Minas Gerais, these churches are a testament to the determination and creativity of the Portuguese colonizers, who built these structures with local materials and techniques.
  8. Sucre, Bolivia The historic city of Sucre was once the capital of Bolivia and features a stunning array of colonial architecture, including the Basilica of San Francisco, the Cathedral of Sucre, and the City Hall. Visitors can explore the city's many museums and art galleries, showcasing the work of local artists and the rich cultural heritage of Bolivia.
  9. Iñapari National Park, Peru Located in the Amazon rainforest, the Iñapari National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site due to its incredible biodiversity and unique geology. Visitors can explore the park's many trails, including the famous Gran Pajonal Trail, or visit the stunning Iñapari Falls, which cascade through the lush rainforest.
  10. Qhapaq Ñan, Argentina/Chile/Bolivia/Peru The Qhapaq Ñan, also known as the Inca Road, is the largest and most impressive network of stone trails and paths ever built in South America. Stretching over 25,000 kilometers from modern-day Colombia to Chile, this ancient road was used by the Incas for trade, communication, and cultural exchange. Visitors can hike sections of the trail, experience the stunning landscapes and unique geology, and gain a deeper understanding of the history and culture of the Inca Empire. These 10 must-see UNESCO World Heritage Sites in South America showcase the incredible diversity, beauty, and cultural richness of the continent. From ancient ruins to breathtaking natural wonders, each site offers a unique insight into the history, culture, and geography of this incredible region. Whether you're interested in history, art, nature, or simply exploring new destinations, South America has something to offer every traveler.
