A Literary Love Triangle: Must-Read Books Featuring Love Triangles


Love triangles have captivated readers for centuries, with these tantalizing tales of romance playing out on the pages of beautifully crafted novels. These storylines seem to effortlessly weave their way into many literary classics and contemporary fiction alike. In this blog post, we’ll delve into some of the best must-read books that feature love triangles, offering a mix of classics, contemporary reads, and lesser-known gems.

  1. Edith Wharton’s “Ethan Frome” (1911)

Our journey begins with one of the earliest examples of a literary love triangle in the form of Edith Wharton’s Ethan Frome. Set in the cold, stark hills of New England, this novella centers on Ethan, who is hopelessly enamored with his wife’s cousin, Mattie. Torn between his allegiance to his wife and his growing feelings for Mattie, Ethan finds himself in a relentlessly enthralling and tragic predicament. Wharton’s masterful storytelling and keen insight into human emotions make her novel an absolute must-read for any aficionado of gripping love triangles.

  1. Daphne du Maurier’s “Rebecca” (1938)

Daphne du Maurier’s hauntingly enchanting novel, Rebecca, offers readers a suspenseful and ethereal love triangle. The story unfolds through the eyes of the second Mrs. de Winter as she competes for the affection of her enigmatic husband, Maxim, who is still haunted by the enigmatic memory of his first wife, Rebecca. As the lines blur between reality and lingering illusions, this psychological thriller successfully highlights the peril, intrigue, and complexity of love triangles.

  1. J.D. Salinger’s “The Catcher in the Rye” (1951)

In J.D. Salinger’s classic coming-of-age novel, The Catcher in the Rye, the prototypical love triangle unfolds in the form of the narrator, Holden Caulfield, who is infatuated with his ex-girlfriend, Sally Hayes. His growing obsession with her leads him into an uncomfortable web of unrequited love and frustration. Although the love triangle format is fundamentally different from those mentioned above, this poignant and insightful novel shines a light on the universal experiences of heartache, emotional turmoil, and longing.

  1. Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie’s “Half of a Yellow Sun” (2006)

Set against the backdrop of the Nigerian Civil War, Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie’s Half of a Yellow Sun offers a more grounded perspective on the dynamics of love triangles. The novel predominantly follows the interwoven relationships of a group of friends, with one woman caught in the throes of a passionate love affair with a man who remains attached to his childhood sweetheart. Adichie provides a brilliant exploration of love, loyalty, and the fraught nature of love triangles in this beautifully crafted novel.

  1. E. Lockhart’s “We Were Liars” (2014)

Finally, we pay tribute to the modern literary love triangle found in E. Lockhart’s We Were Liars. The story revolves around Cadence, a teenage girl who finds herself facing a heartbreaking dilemma when her beloved stepbrother, Gat, starts pursuing another girl from their family’s wealthy and close-knit community. With carefully crafted narration, readers must unravel the intricacies of the love triangle while piecing together the puzzle of a sinister accident that occurred during one fateful summer.

To sum it up, love triangles continue to be a staple in the world of literature. Each of these novels demonstrates that there’s more than one way to create and play out the classic love triangle, whether it takes place throughout centuries or unfolds in a single summer. With captivating characters and exhilarating plotlines, these books are an essential read for anyone who simply cannot resist a long-lasting literary love affair!
