How to Handle Illegal or Inappropriate Interview Questions


When you’re preparing for a job interview, you likely spend a lot of time perfecting your resume, practicing your answers to common interview questions, and researching the company. However, what do you do when you’re faced with illegal or inappropriate interview questions? It’s important to know your rights as a job candidate and how to handle such situations with professionalism and confidence. In this blog post, we’ll discuss what constitutes illegal or inappropriate interview questions, how to respond to them, and steps you can take if you encounter them during the interview process.

Understanding Illegal or Inappropriate Interview Questions

Illegal or inappropriate interview questions are those that touch on protected characteristics such as race, gender, age, marital status, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or national origin. These questions are prohibited under anti-discrimination laws, including Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 in the United States. Examples of illegal or inappropriate interview questions include inquiries about your age, plans for starting a family, religious beliefs, or health status.

How to Respond

  1. Stay Calm and Professional: If you’re asked an illegal or inappropriate question during an interview, it’s essential to remain calm and composed. Remember that the interviewer may not be aware that the question is inappropriate, so it’s important to handle the situation with professionalism.

  2. Politely Decline to Answer: You have the right to politely decline to answer the question. For example, if asked about your marital status, you can respond by saying, “I don’t believe my marital status is relevant to my ability to perform in this role.”

  3. Redirect the Conversation: Politely steer the conversation back to your qualifications and the value you can bring to the role. For instance, if asked about your age, you can respond with, “I’m confident that my experience and skills make me a strong candidate for this position.”

  4. Educate the Interviewer: If you feel comfortable, you can gently educate the interviewer about the illegality of the question. For instance, you might say, “I believe that question is not permissible under anti-discrimination laws.”

  5. Follow-Up After the Interview: If you’re uncomfortable with the questions asked during the interview, consider following up with the hiring manager or HR department to express your concerns in a diplomatic manner.

Steps to Take If You Encounter Illegal or Inappropriate Interview Questions

If you believe you’ve been asked illegal or inappropriate interview questions, here are some steps you can take:

  1. Document the Questions: Make a note of the questions asked, including the date, time, and individuals present during the interview.

  2. Seek Legal Advice: If you feel that the questions were discriminatory, consider seeking legal advice from an employment attorney to understand your rights and potential courses of action.

  3. Provide Feedback to the Company: If you’re comfortable doing so, consider providing feedback to the company about your interview experience. This can help raise awareness and prevent similar incidents in the future.

  4. Consider Your Options: Depending on the severity of the situation, you may need to evaluate whether you still wish to pursue employment with the company.

Conclusion

Handling illegal or inappropriate interview questions requires tact, confidence, and an understanding of your rights as a job candidate. By staying composed, politely declining to answer, and taking appropriate follow-up actions, you can navigate these challenging situations with professionalism. Remember that you have the right to a fair and respectful interview process, and don’t hesitate to seek support or guidance if you encounter discriminatory practices during your job search.
