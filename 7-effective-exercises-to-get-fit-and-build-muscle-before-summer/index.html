7 Effective Exercises to Get Fit and Build Muscle Before Summer


A new season is approaching, and along with it, the scorching summer heat. Who wouldn’t want to look and feel their best during these sunny months? Getting fit and building muscle not only helps us sport a more toned appearance but can also improve our overall health and well-being. Today, we’ve scoured various workout routines and consulted with fitness experts to provide you with an outstanding selection of seven effective exercises that will help jumpstart your summer fitness journey.

  1. Dumbbell Deadlifts

Starting our list are the humble yet powerful dumbbell deadlifts. This compound exercise works multiple muscle groups simultaneously - calves, glutes, hamstrings, traps, and latissimus dorsi to name a few!

Instructions:

  • And stay clear of swinging, and keep a neutral spine throughout the movement.
  • Drive through your heels to stand tall, squeeze your glutes, and lower the weights back to the floor.
  1. Shoulder Press

Ah, the classic shoulder press! This exercise is perfect for working your shoulders, triceps, and upper chest - an absolute must for those aiming for a chiselled upper body.

Instructions:

  • Start by holding dumbbells at shoulder level with palms facing forward, then press the weights overhead until your arms are fully extended.
  • Pause, then lower the weights back to your shoulders.
  1. Squats

The ultimate lower body workout, squats build strength and power in your quads, hamstrings, and glutes. Aim for a set of 10 to 12 reps, ensuring you maintain proper form to avoid injury.

Instructions:

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart and hold a weight or a kettlebell in front of your chest, thighs parallel to the ground before pushing through your heels to stand back up.
  1. Bulgarian Split Squats

This exercise is an advanced variation of the traditional squat, designed to intensely target your quads and glutes. Struggling to find the perfect balance? Use a resistance band around your knees for unbeatable support.

Instructions:

  • Stand a couple of feet away from a bench, place one foot on the bench behind you, and hold a weight in both hands at your chest.
  • Bend both knees to lower your hips until your front thigh is parallel to the floor, then press back up to the starting position.
  1. Plank

A workout that doesn’t involve any weights? You heard that right! Planks are brilliant for honing in on your core, building stability, and preventing back pain.

Instructions:

  • Start on a yoga mat, supporting your weight on your forearms and toes with your body forming a straight line from your head to your heels. Make sure to squeeze those glutes to avoid sagging in the hips.
  1. Bent-Over Rows

When it comes to building muscle along your upper back and arms, nothing beats a good row. This exercise targets your lats, traps, and biceps simultaneously!

Instructions:

  • Start by setting up a barbell across your lap or holding onto a sturdy handle. Be sure to hinge forward at the hips, maintaining a flat back and keeping your core tight.
  • Pull the weight to your lower abs while squeezing your shoulder blades together, then slowly lower it back to the starting position.
  1. Pull-Ups or Assisted Pull-Ups

The hardest yet most rewarding exercise on our list - the pull-up! If unassisted pull-ups are too much to handle, opt for an assisted version (using a resistance band or an assisted machine) to build your upper body strength.

Instructions:

  • To start, grip a pull-up bar with palms facing away, arms extended. Engage your core and, with a straight body, pull your body upward, pulling elbows down towards your hips.

Conclusion

Now that you’re equipped with an arsenal of exercises to kickstart your summer fitness journey, there’s no stopping you from achieving your ideal body! Remember to never neglect proper form and gradually increase the weight or resistance over time. Consistency and dedication will surely make a difference - look forward to rocking that swimwear with confidence this summer!
