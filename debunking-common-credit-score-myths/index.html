Debunking Common Credit Score Myths


Credit scores play a crucial role in our financial lives. They determine our ability to secure loans, obtain favorable interest rates, and even impact our job prospects. However, credit scores are often misunderstood, giving rise to various myths and misconceptions. In this blog post, we will debunk some of the most common credit score myths, providing you with accurate information to help you make informed financial decisions.

Myth #1: Checking Your Credit Score Lowers It

One of the biggest misconceptions surrounding credit scores is the belief that checking it will negatively impact your score. In reality, checking your own credit score, also known as a “soft inquiry,” has no effect on your score whatsoever. It is considered a responsible financial practice to regularly monitor your credit score to ensure its accuracy and detect any fraudulent activity. So, feel free to check your credit score as often as you like without any worries.

Myth #2: Closing Old Credit Accounts Improves Your Credit Score

Another myth that many people believe is that closing old credit accounts will increase their credit score. However, the opposite is true. Closing old credit accounts can actually harm your score because it reduces your overall credit history and increases your credit utilization ratio. Keeping old credit accounts open, especially if they have a positive payment history, can have a positive impact on your credit score.

Myth #3: Carrying a Small Balance on Your Credit Card Boosts Your Score

Contrary to popular belief, carrying a small balance on your credit card does not enhance your credit score. In fact, it can potentially harm your score. The key factor that influences your credit score is your credit utilization ratio, which is the percentage of your available credit that you are using. It is advisable to keep your credit utilization ratio below 30% to maintain a healthy credit score. Paying off your credit card balance in full and on time each month is the best strategy to improve and maintain a good credit score.

Myth #4: Closing Credit Cards with High Interest Rates is Beneficial

Many individuals believe that closing credit cards with high interest rates will improve their credit score. However, this is not the case. Closing credit cards, regardless of their interest rates, can negatively impact your credit score. As mentioned earlier, closing accounts reduces your overall credit limit, which in turn increases your credit utilization ratio. Instead of closing high-interest credit cards, consider paying off the balances or transferring them to cards with lower interest rates.

Myth #5: You Only Have One Credit Score

Contrary to popular belief, you don’t have just one credit score. In fact, there are multiple credit scoring models used by different lenders and credit bureaus. The most commonly used credit scoring model is FICO, but there are also VantageScore and other proprietary models. Each scoring model may generate a slightly different credit score based on the information available to them. It’s important to understand that your credit score can vary depending on the scoring model used.

Conclusion

Understanding credit scores is essential for making sound financial decisions. By debunking these common credit score myths, we hope to have provided you with accurate information that will empower you to take control of your financial health. Remember, regularly monitoring your credit score, keeping old credit accounts open, paying off credit card balances in full, and being aware of the different credit scoring models will help you maintain a healthy credit score.
