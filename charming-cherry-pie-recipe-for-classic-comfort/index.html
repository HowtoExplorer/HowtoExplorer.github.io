Charming Cherry Pie Recipe for Classic Comfort

3 min · 550 words

Nothing beats the classic comfort of a homemade cherry pie. The rich, tangy filling combined with a flaky, buttery crust is a timeless dessert that never fails to please. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a novice in the kitchen, this charming cherry pie recipe is sure to impress your family and friends. So roll up your sleeves, tie on your apron, and let’s get baking!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh or frozen cherries
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 package store-bought pie crust (or homemade if you prefer)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a medium-sized saucepan, combine the cherries, sugar, and cornstarch. Stir well to ensure the cherries are evenly coated.
  3. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook the cherry mixture until it starts to bubble and thicken, about 5-7 minutes. Stir frequently to prevent sticking.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the almond extract. Set aside to cool slightly.
  5. While the cherry filling cools, roll out one of the pie crusts and carefully transfer it to a 9-inch pie dish. Press the crust gently against the sides of the dish.
  6. Pour the cherry filling into the pie crust and dot the top with small pieces of butter.
  7. Roll out the second pie crust and place it on top of the filling. Seal the edges by crimping them with a fork or your fingers.
  8. Optional: You can create a lattice crust by cutting the second pie crust into strips and weaving them together on top of the cherry filling.
  9. Use a sharp knife to cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.
  10. Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake in the preheated oven for 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  11. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours before serving. This will allow the filling to set and ensure clean slices.

Serving Suggestions

Serve your charming cherry pie warm or at room temperature, with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream on top. The combination of the sweet cherry filling and the creamy accompaniment is simply divine.

Tips and Tricks

  • If you’re using frozen cherries, be sure to thaw them completely before making the filling.
  • If you prefer a sweeter pie, you can increase the amount of sugar to suit your taste.
  • To prevent the edges of the crust from browning too quickly, you can cover them with strips of foil during the last 15 minutes of baking.
  • Leftovers can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Simply reheat individual slices in the microwave or enjoy them cold.

Conclusion

There’s something magical about a slice of homemade cherry pie. The combination of the sweet, tart cherries and the buttery, flaky crust creates a dessert that is both comforting and indulgent. So why not channel your inner baker and give this charming cherry pie recipe a try? It’s guaranteed to bring smiles and warmth to your kitchen, and create lasting memories with your loved ones. Happy baking!
