How to Write a Nonprofit Press Release


Writing a press release for a nonprofit organization is an essential skill for effectively sharing news and updates with the public, media outlets, and stakeholders. Press releases can help raise awareness for causes, promote fundraising events, highlight partnerships, or announce significant achievements. A well-crafted press release can generate buzz, attract media coverage, and help your nonprofit reach a wider audience.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore how to write a nonprofit press release, covering everything from the essential components to tips for ensuring your release is both informative and impactful. By the end, you’ll have a solid understanding of what makes a great press release and how to use it strategically to benefit your nonprofit.

What is a Nonprofit Press Release?

A nonprofit press release is a brief, informative document used to announce something newsworthy related to the nonprofit sector. These releases are typically distributed to journalists, media outlets, and online platforms to get coverage for the organization’s events, initiatives, or milestones.

Whether it’s announcing a charity event, a new partnership, or the launch of a new program, a nonprofit press release serves as an official way to communicate important news with a broader audience.

It’s important to keep in mind that press releases are not advertisements. They are journalistic pieces meant to convey valuable, newsworthy information in a clear, objective, and concise manner.

Key Components of a Nonprofit Press Release

Before diving into the steps of writing a nonprofit press release, it’s essential to understand the key components that should be included. Every press release should be formatted correctly and include certain elements to make it effective.

1. Headline

The headline of a press release is one of the most important aspects, as it grabs the reader’s attention and conveys the main message. A good headline should be clear, concise, and compelling. It should answer the basic question, “What’s happening?” in a way that makes the reader want to continue reading.

Example:
“Local Nonprofit Raises $50,000 for Homeless Shelter Expansion”

2. Subheadline

A subheadline is an optional, secondary sentence that provides further context to the headline. It often expands on the key details and offers more information to clarify the message. The subheadline should not be redundant but instead complement the headline.

Example:
“Fundraiser exceeds expectations, bringing the community together to support the cause.”

3. Dateline

The dateline is the section that includes the location and date of the release. It typically starts with the city or town where the nonprofit is located, followed by the date the press release is being issued. This is important for establishing when and where the news is happening.

Example:
“Chicago, IL – February 20, 2025”

4. Introduction

The introduction, or lead paragraph, should summarize the most important details of the news. It should answer the “Who, What, When, Where, Why, and How” questions. The introduction is where you highlight the most compelling aspect of your press release and set the tone for the rest of the document.

Example:
“The Helping Hands Foundation, a nonprofit dedicated to providing affordable housing to low-income families, raised $50,000 at its annual fundraising gala on Saturday. The funds will support the expansion of the local homeless shelter, providing more beds and services to those in need.”

5. Body Paragraphs

The body of the press release is where you provide additional details about the news. This section should include more specific information, such as quotes from key individuals involved, background information about the nonprofit or initiative, and relevant statistics. It’s essential to focus on the key points while keeping the content relevant and to the point.

Each paragraph should transition smoothly into the next, creating a logical flow of information. Avoid including unnecessary jargon or long-winded explanations. If your press release includes quotes, make sure they are from credible sources such as nonprofit leaders, donors, or beneficiaries of your program.

Example:
“The gala, which was attended by over 300 guests, featured live entertainment and a silent auction. ‘We are overwhelmed by the support of our community,’ said Jane Smith, Executive Director of Helping Hands Foundation. ‘This generous donation will help us provide critical services to those who need them most.’”

6. Call to Action (CTA)

The call to action (CTA) is where you prompt your audience to take the next step. Whether it’s asking them to donate, attend an event, volunteer, or visit your website, the CTA should be clear and direct. This is your opportunity to engage the reader and encourage them to act.

Example:
“To support the Helping Hands Foundation’s mission, visit [website link] to donate or learn more about upcoming events.”

7. Boilerplate

The boilerplate is a brief paragraph at the end of the press release that provides background information about the nonprofit organization. It typically includes the organization’s mission statement, history, and contact details. The boilerplate should be concise and informative.

Example:
“The Helping Hands Foundation is a nonprofit organization dedicated to alleviating homelessness in the Chicago area. Since 2005, the foundation has provided housing, healthcare, and job training services to over 10,000 individuals in need. For more information, visit [website link].”

8. Contact Information

At the very end of the press release, you should include the contact information of the person who can provide more details about the story. This often includes the name, phone number, email address, and social media handles of your media contact.

Example:
“For more information, please contact:
John Doe
Communications Manager
Helping Hands Foundation
Phone: (555) 123-4567
Email: john.doe@helpinghands.org”

Step-by-Step Guide to Writing a Nonprofit Press Release

Step 1: Identify the Newsworthiness

Before writing a press release, it’s essential to ask whether the news you’re sharing is truly newsworthy. Ask yourself the following questions:

  • Is this something that the public would care about?
  • Will the media find this information valuable?
  • Does this update have broader significance, such as contributing to the mission of your nonprofit?

If the news doesn’t feel compelling, it may not be the right time for a press release. Focus on sharing updates that have tangible impact, such as major donations, successful events, new partnerships, or impactful programs.

Step 2: Craft a Compelling Headline

As mentioned earlier, the headline is critical for grabbing attention. Make sure your headline is straightforward and informative, but also compelling enough to make people want to learn more. Use strong action words and be as specific as possible.

Avoid vague headlines like “Nonprofit Announces New Initiative.” Instead, focus on the outcome, such as “Nonprofit Launches Initiative to Provide 1,000 Meals for the Homeless.”

Step 3: Write a Strong Lead

The introduction should immediately capture attention by providing the key facts in a concise manner. Avoid fluff or unnecessary background details. Your goal is to answer the key questions in the first few sentences. A strong lead helps journalists understand the main message and decide whether to cover the story.

Step 4: Provide Key Details in the Body

In the body of the press release, break down the details into easy-to-read paragraphs. Ensure each section provides value, whether it’s background information on the nonprofit, details about the event, or quotes from key stakeholders. Be sure to avoid overwhelming the reader with too much information.

Use direct quotes from those involved to add a human element to the release. When quoting someone, make sure the quote adds substance and credibility to the story.

Step 5: Keep it Brief and Focused

While it may be tempting to include every detail, keep in mind that press releases should be concise. Aim for one page (roughly 400-500 words) unless the story is exceptionally complex. Journalists and readers don’t have time for lengthy releases, so stick to the essentials.

Step 6: End with a Clear Call to Action

After providing all the details, close the press release with a call to action. Encourage the reader to take the next step, whether it’s donating, attending an event, or getting involved in another way. Be direct but polite, and make it easy for them to take action.

Step 7: Proofread and Edit

Before sending out your press release, it’s essential to proofread and edit it for clarity, grammar, and accuracy. Ensure that the facts are correct and that the tone is professional and informative. A well-polished press release will make a stronger impact on the media and your audience.

Tips for Distributing Your Nonprofit Press Release

Once your press release is ready, it’s time to distribute it. Here are some distribution strategies to maximize its reach:

  • Send to relevant media outlets: Make a list of journalists and media outlets that cover nonprofit news or stories similar to yours. Tailor your pitch to the specific interests of each outlet.
  • Use press release distribution services: Platforms like PR Newswire or Business Wire can help distribute your press release to a wider audience, including journalists, bloggers, and media outlets.
  • Post on your website and social media: In addition to sending your press release to the media, post it on your nonprofit’s website and share it across your social media channels. This ensures your supporters and followers are in the loop.
  • Build relationships with journalists: Foster relationships with journalists and reporters who are interested in your nonprofit’s mission. Cultivating long-term connections can increase the chances of media coverage for future events.

Conclusion

A nonprofit press release is an essential tool for communicating important news and updates to the public and media outlets. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you can craft a compelling and effective press release that helps your nonprofit gain visibility, build relationships, and advance its mission.

Remember, a strong press release is clear, concise, and newsworthy. Focus on the impact of your news, use strong headlines and leads, and provide all the necessary details in an engaging format. When done well, a nonprofit press release can be a powerful tool for amplifying your organization’s voice and reaching a broader audience.
