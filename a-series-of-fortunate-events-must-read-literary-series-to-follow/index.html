A Series of Fortunate Events: Must-Read Literary Series to Follow


In the vast realm of literature, there exists a particular subset that has gained immense traction among avid readers: the literary series. These collections of novels have the power to draw us into worlds and stories that stay with us long after we’ve turned the last page. From the depths of dragons to the hustle and bustle of British domestic life, and everything in between, these series leave us on the edge of our seats and begging for more. In today’s blog, we’ll dive into a collection of literary series that ABSOLUTELY deserve to have their spot on your bookshelf.

Harry Potter by J.K. Rowling

It goes without saying that the wizarding world created by J.K. Rowling has captured the hearts and imaginations of millions. With seven core novels and countless companion pieces, there’s never a dull moment in Harry Potter. This must-read series follows the journey of a young wizard, Harry Potter, as he navigates through the magical world that lies behind a mobilized cupboard under the stairs. As he attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, friendships are formed, enemies made, and epic battles are fought. The series is steeped in magic, adventure, and themes that resonate with readers of all ages. Not only will you be sucked into an unforgettable world, but you’ll find life lessons and enjoyment around every page.

A Song of Ice and Fire by George R.R. Martin

When the world fell in love with Game of Thrones, it all started with George R.R. Martin’s legendary A Song of Ice and Fire series (commonly known as ASOIAF). The series features a myriad of characters from various noble families fighting for power in the Seven Kingdoms. Martin’s immersive storytelling and masterful character development bring an authenticity and depth to the series that fans can’t seem to get enough of. Political intrigue and epic battles dominate this world, and fans can’t get enough of the page-turning, heart-wrenching story.

Outlander by Diana Gabaldon

Filled with action, adventure, and love, Outlander brings readers along on an epic journey through time. The story follows Claire Randall, a World War II nurse who finds herself mysteriously transported to 18th-century Scotland. Falling in love with a dashing Highlander named Jamie Fraser, Claire must navigate the dangerous world of the Scottish clans while also grappling with the ramifications of her time travel. Gabaldon’s incredible research and vivid world-building make this series a must-read for any lover of history, romance, and adventure.

The Chronicles of Narnia by C.S. Lewis

The Narnia series by C.S. Lewis is an unapologetic exploration of faith, morality, and adventure. Set in a realm of magical creatures, heroic battles, and spellbinding allegories, these books captivate readers of all ages. The journey through Narnia allows readers to experience the enchanting adventures and life lessons of the Pevensie siblings, telling a powerful story like no other.

The Discworld series by Terry Pratchett

Terry Pratchett’s Discworld novels are renowned for their humor, wit, and satirical commentary on our world. Set in a flat planet balanced on the backs of four elephants who themselves stand on the back of a giant turtle, Discworld is teeming with quirky characters and imaginative plotlines. With over 40 novels under the Discworld umbrella, there’s plenty of magic, mayhem, and laughter to be found in this unique and engaging series.

The ability to disappear into a meticulously crafted world and stay there for an indefinite amount of time is one of the greatest gifts literature can offer us. These five series offer just that, each with its own flavor and charm that will stay etched in your memory long after the last page is turned. So, grab a cup of tea (or whisky), remember to suspend your disbelief, and immerse yourself in these tales of fortune and misfortune. Remember, what abyss does not know, you’ll be consumed by it. Or you’ll find the secret to time travel, depending on which series you choose!
