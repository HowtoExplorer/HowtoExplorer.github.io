The Importance of Feedback in Communication


Feedback is an essential element in communication, playing a significant role in how we interact and connect with each other. When we communicate effectively, we do not only send messages but also receive and send feedback. Without feedback, a conversation is only a one-way street, offering little room for growth, clarity, or success. Feedback forms the backbone of effective communication by allowing individuals to understand each other's perspectives and respond accordingly. In this piece, we explore the importance of feedback in our daily interactions. The Mechanism of Feedback in Communication Feedback is part of a give-and-take process. Initially, one individual sends a message, and this message is meant to be perceived and interpreted by the recipient in a certain manner. The goal of communication in this context would be to spark a reaction and elicit the desired response or action. What happens next plays a crucial part in determining how successful the entire communication process would be. For instance, assume you're providing instructions to your colleague on the best way to complete a job. Your instruction is a means of sending your message, yet it is nothing without the input from your coworker, explaining whether they follow your guidance seamlessly or need guidance on how best to approach each step. In this instance, the colleague gives you feedback to clarify their misunderstanding or highlight confusion. Your part in the situation is to recognize the input as feedback and ask questions or suggest alternatives if more clarification is necessary. The same principle applies even in informal and casual conversations like those between parents and children. The exchange goes both ways where the parent transmits a point, and this is received with feedback in various forms like "I don't understand," or "That isn't right, let me say it this way." Enhancing Clarity and Reducing Misunderstanding Feedback in all its forms increases the likelihood that the message that was sent successfully reaches the targeted audience. Sometimes, when delivering a message orally, we expect the recipient to grasp it straight away. There are times this is the case, but very often, confusion arises due to the absence or lack of precise feedback. Suppose you are assigned a task on a project without clear guidelines of what the target is. On your part, you do all you can in your understanding. However, should you receive insufficient feedback from project leaders, things can go askew. Moreover, in workplace settings, regular feedback helps eliminate misunderstandings regarding expectations and encourages employees to engage more with a project. Consequently, when this is lacking and an employee misunderstands their requirements, they make mistakes or might not even have the opportunity to correct their way of working quickly. It implies that effective use of feedback at the right points in communication guarantees that both speaker and listener interact on the exact same level – a fundamental part of the relationship between two engaged parties. The Impact on Relationships and Understanding Feedback not just helps clarify objectives and reduce possible errors; also, it enables individuals to recognize each other’s perspectives and understanding. Feedback within relationships allows understanding of how two individuals connect through communication. To illustrate, there are instances we may unintentionally hurt or give offense to somebody. Without being able to listen to the negative feedback that can be received if we only stick to our perceptions, it often becomes impossible for us to connect with that other person again due to our different perspectives.
