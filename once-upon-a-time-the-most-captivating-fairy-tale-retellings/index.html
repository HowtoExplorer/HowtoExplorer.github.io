Once Upon a Time: The Most Captivating Fairy Tale Retellings


Fairy tales have been a staple of storytelling for centuries, enchanting generations of readers and viewers with their whimsical characters, magical adventures, and timeless lessons. However, as time passed, authors and screenwriters have taken these classic tales and given them a fresh, modern twist in what we now know as fairy tale retellings. These retellings transport us back to the worlds of our childhood dreams while simultaneously exploring deeper themes and challenging societal norms. In this blog post, we shall explore the most captivating fairy tale retellings that have left readers spellbound.

Cinder: The Once Upon an Android Series

Cinder, the first book in Marissa Meyer’s Lunar Chronicles, breathed new life into the classic tale of Cinderella. As technology advanced and androids became more enmeshed in society, Cinder, our main character, operated as a cyborg mechanic. But beneath her robotic exterior, she harbored a powerful secret – she is also a delicate young lady destined to fulfill an age-old prophecy.

Meyer masterfully weaved elements from classic fairy tales, such as Cinderella, Little Red Riding Hood, and Snow White, into an intricate retelling that forces readers to question the traditional interpretations of these stories. By placing her characters in a futuristic setting with advanced technology and political intrigue, Meyer adds an exciting, modern edge to these timeless tales.

Beauty and the Beast: The Beastly Tales Series

In Tara Crewe’s Beastly Tales series, a unique spin is put on the beloved classic Beauty and the Beast. While still retaining the magical themes of love and transformation, Crewe delves deeper into the perspective of the beast. The series centers around Henri, a noble beast, who has lost all his humanity due to an ancient curse. His shapeshifting companion, Helena, embarks on a journey to break the curse by bringing love and compassion back into Henri’s heart.

Crewe’s innovative retelling of Beauty and the Beast reshapes our understanding of the beast’s role in the original story and invites readers to explore the intricate emotions and motivations of such a misunderstood character.

Snow White: The Black Clover Manga

The Black Clover manga series, created by Yūki Tabata, is a dark, action-packed reimagining of the classic Snow White tale set in a world where magic is the basis of life. The story centers around a young boy named Asta who, despite being born without magical abilities in a world where magic is the norm, dreams of becoming the greatest magician in the kingdom. Upon meeting a mysterious cloaked figure, Asta unearths a hidden power that binds him to the spirit of the true/forgotten magic sword.

Delving into the themes of power, identity, and destiny, this epic retelling of Snow White draws readers into a captivating fantasy world filled with adversity, loyalty, and, above all, hope.

Little Red Riding Hood: The Lottery

In The Lottery by Shirley Jackson, we witness an unnerving retelling of Little Red Riding Hood, set in an eerily ordinary American town. The story focuses on a village that yearly participates in a lottery, where one individual is selected for a seemingly unfair and brutal punishment. Through this chilling nod to the classic tale, Jackson teases the darker, twisted side of humanity and draws an important question: would we dare defy societal norms when they demand sacrifice?

This gripping retelling invites readers to delve into the psyche of their neighbors and confront the possibility that our most tragic stories might not originate from distant lands but from our very own communities.

In Conclusion:

These four retellings just scratch the surface of the multitude of captivating fairy tale retellings out there, each offering a unique perspective on the well-worn stories we’ve come to know and love. Whether it’s reimagining a classic tale set in a futuristic world, flipping the script on a notorious villain, or reshaping our view of the darker side of human nature, these retellings provide endless opportunities for storytelling and enchantment.

While these tales may take us back to the original spirit and charm of the fairy tales we so dearly love, they also invite us to venture into uncharted territory and question the boundaries and norms that define our understanding of these classic stories. So, let us continue exploring the most captivating fairy tale retellings, and who knows what other enchanting stories await us?
