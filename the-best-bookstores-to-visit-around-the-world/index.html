The Best Bookstores to Visit Around the World


For book lovers, bookstores are not just places to buy books; they are sanctuaries of knowledge, creativity, and inspiration. Across the globe, some bookstores have become iconic destinations, each with its unique charm and character. Whether you are a casual reader or a passionate bibliophile, visiting these bookstores can be a transformative experience. In this post, we will explore some of the best bookstores to visit around the world, each offering a distinct atmosphere and a treasure trove of literary delights.

1. Shakespeare and Company, Paris, France

Nestled in the heart of Paris, Shakespeare and Company is more than a bookstore; it is a literary institution. Established in 1919 by Sylvia Beach, it became a gathering place for renowned writers such as Ernest Hemingway, F. Scott Fitzgerald, and James Joyce. The store’s eclectic collection of new and used books, along with its cozy reading nooks, invites visitors to spend hours lost in the pages of literature.

The charming, bohemian atmosphere of Shakespeare and Company is accentuated by its beautiful wooden shelves, creaky floorboards, and the presence of resident cats that add to the store’s welcoming vibe. The bookstore also hosts various literary events, including readings and discussions, making it a vibrant hub for the literary community.

Why Visit? Experience the literary history, browse through a carefully curated selection, and enjoy a cup of coffee in a space that has inspired countless writers.

2. El Ateneo Grand Splendid, Buenos Aires, Argentina

Once a grand theater, El Ateneo Grand Splendid has been transformed into one of the most stunning bookstores in the world. Located in the Recoleta district of Buenos Aires, this architectural marvel retains much of its original grandeur, complete with ornate ceilings, plush red curtains, and gilded balconies.

As you wander through the aisles, you’ll find books in various languages spanning all genres. The café, situated on the former stage, allows visitors to sip coffee while enjoying breathtaking views of the elaborate interior. The atmosphere is both serene and inspiring, making it an ideal place for book lovers to relax and immerse themselves in literature.

Why Visit? Marvel at the blend of history and literature, and enjoy the unique experience of sipping coffee in a former theater surrounded by thousands of books.

3. Powell’s City of Books, Portland, Oregon, USA

Powell’s City of Books is a book lover’s paradise, claiming to be the largest independent bookstore in the world. Covering an entire city block, this bookstore offers over a million new and used books across numerous genres. Its vibrant orange exterior and eclectic interior design create an inviting atmosphere for readers of all ages.

Inside Powell’s, you can easily get lost among the countless aisles, each dedicated to different genres and categories. The store also features a café and hosts regular author events, signings, and readings. Powell’s commitment to promoting literature and supporting local authors has solidified its place as a cornerstone of the Portland literary scene.

Why Visit? Explore an extensive collection of books, participate in engaging events, and enjoy the sense of community that Powell’s fosters.

4. Bookworm, Beijing, China

Bookworm in Beijing is more than just a bookstore; it is a cultural hub that connects readers, writers, and thinkers. This unique establishment offers a diverse selection of English-language books alongside an array of Chinese literature, making it a vital resource for expatriates and locals alike.

The cozy café serves delicious food and drinks, making it a perfect spot to relax with a book. Bookworm frequently hosts literary events, film screenings, and discussions, providing a platform for dialogue and cultural exchange. The store’s commitment to fostering a love for reading and literature makes it a must-visit for anyone in Beijing.

Why Visit? Enjoy a multicultural literary experience while savoring delicious meals and engaging in thought-provoking events.

5. Livraria Lello, Porto, Portugal

Often referred to as the most beautiful bookstore in the world, Livraria Lello is a stunning architectural gem located in Porto, Portugal. The bookstore boasts a neo-Gothic façade and a breathtaking interior, complete with a grand wooden staircase and intricate stained glass windows.

Livraria Lello is not only a visual delight but also a haven for book lovers, offering a wide selection of Portuguese and international titles. The bookstore has gained popularity among tourists, partly due to its rumored inspiration for J.K. Rowling’s Hogwarts in the Harry Potter series. The magical atmosphere, combined with the rich literary history of Porto, makes it an unforgettable stop for any traveler.

Why Visit? Experience the breathtaking beauty of the architecture while browsing through a carefully curated selection of books.

6. The Last Bookstore, Los Angeles, California, USA

The Last Bookstore is a unique blend of a traditional bookstore and an art installation. Located in downtown Los Angeles, this quirky space features an impressive collection of new and used books, along with an extensive vinyl record section. The bookstore’s whimsical design, including book tunnels and creative shelving, creates a playful environment that sparks the imagination.

In addition to its vast inventory, The Last Bookstore regularly hosts events such as readings, poetry slams, and art exhibitions, making it a dynamic space for creativity and community engagement. The store’s commitment to promoting local artists and authors has made it a beloved fixture in the Los Angeles literary scene.

Why Visit? Explore an imaginative bookstore design, discover unique treasures, and engage in the vibrant local arts community.

7. Daunt Books, London, England

Daunt Books is an Edwardian bookshop located in the heart of London, known for its stunning oak galleries and skylights. The bookstore specializes in travel literature, with books carefully organized by country, allowing readers to explore destinations through literature.

In addition to travel books, Daunt offers a diverse selection of fiction, non-fiction, and children’s books, all curated with a discerning eye. The cozy reading areas invite visitors to linger and discover new titles, while the knowledgeable staff is always on hand to provide recommendations.

Why Visit? Experience the elegance of a historic bookstore while exploring a carefully curated selection of travel literature and more.

8. The Strand, New York City, New York, USA

The Strand is an iconic bookstore located in Manhattan, famous for its “18 miles of books.” This independent bookstore has been a beloved destination for bibliophiles since 1927, offering an unparalleled selection of new, used, and rare books. The store’s distinctive red awning and eclectic inventory make it a must-visit for anyone in New York City.

The Strand also hosts a variety of events, including author signings, readings, and book launches, creating a vibrant atmosphere for literary enthusiasts. The store’s commitment to supporting the literary community and preserving the joy of reading has solidified its status as a cultural landmark.

Why Visit? Browse an extensive collection of books in a historic bookstore that has become a symbol of literary culture in New York City.

9. Waterstones, Edinburgh, Scotland

Situated in the historic West End of Edinburgh, Waterstones is a stunning bookstore housed in a former bank building. The grand architecture features high ceilings, ornate details, and a beautiful staircase that invites visitors to explore multiple floors of books.

Waterstones offers a wide range of titles, including Scottish literature, fiction, non-fiction, and children’s books. The store regularly hosts author events, book clubs, and literary festivals, making it a vibrant part of the Edinburgh literary scene. The café on-site provides a cozy spot to enjoy a cup of tea while surrounded by books.

Why Visit? Experience the charm of a historic building while enjoying a diverse selection of literature in the heart of Scotland’s capital.

10. The Bookstore at the End of the World, Ushuaia, Argentina

Located in the southernmost city in the world, The Bookstore at the End of the World is a unique destination for travelers and book lovers alike. This charming bookstore specializes in literature about Patagonia, adventure, and nature, making it a perfect stop for those exploring the stunning landscapes of the region.

The cozy atmosphere invites visitors to browse through a carefully curated selection of books while enjoying breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and sea. The bookstore also hosts workshops, readings, and discussions that celebrate the local literary community and the rich culture of Patagonia.

Why Visit? Discover the literary treasures of Patagonia while enjoying a unique atmosphere in one of the most remote places on Earth.

Conclusion

Bookstores are more than just retail spaces; they are cultural landmarks that foster a love for literature and creativity. Whether you are exploring the historic streets of Paris, the vibrant neighborhoods of New York, or the serene landscapes of Patagonia, these bookstores offer a unique glimpse into the heart of their respective cities. Make it a point to visit these enchanting bookstores and immerse yourself in the beauty of literature, culture, and community. Each visit may inspire your next great adventure, both in reading and in life.
