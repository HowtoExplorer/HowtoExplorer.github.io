Writing a Resume for Nonprofit Organizations: Best Practices


When it comes to applying for a position at a nonprofit organization, your resume plays a crucial role in showcasing your skills and experience. Nonprofits have unique missions and values, and they look for candidates who align with their goals. In this blog post, we will discuss the best practices for writing a resume that stands out to nonprofit organizations.

1. Start with a compelling summary statement

Your resume should begin with a powerful summary statement that captures the attention of the reader. Highlight your passion for making a difference and emphasize any relevant experience or skills you possess. Use keywords such as “nonprofit,” “impact,” and “community” to showcase your commitment to the sector.

2. Tailor your resume to the organization

Research the nonprofit organization you are applying to and tailor your resume accordingly. Nonprofits often have specific requirements and preferences, so make sure to highlight the skills and experiences that are most relevant to the position. Use the organization’s mission statement and values as a guide to align your resume with their goals.

3. Showcase your volunteer experience

Nonprofit organizations value candidates who have a genuine interest in their cause. If you have volunteered for similar organizations or have relevant volunteer experience, make sure to include it in your resume. Highlight the skills you have developed through volunteering, such as teamwork, leadership, and problem-solving.

4. Highlight your transferable skills

Even if you don’t have direct nonprofit experience, you may still possess transferable skills that are valuable to nonprofit organizations. Skills such as project management, fundraising, event planning, and grant writing can be applicable in the nonprofit sector. Showcase these skills in your resume, demonstrating how they can contribute to the organization’s goals.

5. Include measurable achievements

Quantify your accomplishments to demonstrate the impact you have made in your previous roles. Nonprofit organizations are results-oriented, so providing concrete examples of your achievements will make your resume stand out. Use metrics, such as the number of funds raised, the number of beneficiaries served, or the percentage increase in volunteer engagement.

6. Emphasize your passion and commitment

Nonprofit organizations are looking for individuals who are genuinely passionate about their cause. Use your resume to convey your dedication and commitment to making a difference. Share any personal stories or experiences that have shaped your interest in the nonprofit sector. This will help the organization understand your motivation and connection to their mission.

7. Proofread and format your resume

A well-written and error-free resume is essential when applying to nonprofit organizations. Ensure that your resume is free from typos, grammatical errors, and inconsistencies. Use a clean and professional format, making it easy for the reader to navigate and find relevant information. Consider using bullet points to highlight your key achievements and skills.

8. Include relevant certifications and training

If you have any certifications or training that are relevant to the nonprofit sector, include them in your resume. This could include courses in fundraising, nonprofit management, grant writing, or volunteer management. These certifications demonstrate your commitment to professional development and can make your resume more appealing to nonprofit organizations.

9. Network and seek referrals

In addition to submitting your resume, networking and seeking referrals can significantly increase your chances of landing a position at a nonprofit organization. Attend industry events, connect with professionals in the sector, and ask for referrals from individuals who have connections within nonprofit organizations. Personal recommendations can carry a lot of weight in the nonprofit sector.

10. Follow up after submitting your resume

After submitting your resume, it’s essential to follow up with the organization to express your continued interest and inquire about the status of your application. This demonstrates your proactive approach and genuine enthusiasm for the position. Send a professional and concise email or make a phone call to follow up, thanking the organization for considering your application.

In conclusion, writing a resume for nonprofit organizations requires a thoughtful approach that showcases your passion, skills, and commitment to making a difference. By tailoring your resume to the organization, highlighting your relevant experience, and emphasizing your achievements, you can increase your chances of standing out to nonprofit organizations. Remember to proofread and format your resume carefully and follow up after submitting your application. Good luck with your nonprofit job search!
