Resume Cultural Adjustment: How to Showcase Relevant International Experience


The world becomes increasingly interconnected every day, creating an eagerness to work, travel, and live abroad. Whether you’re an expat, a global nomad, or a frequent traveler with international experience, knowing how to present this experience on your resume can make a significant difference in your job search process.

In the modern global job market, recruiters and hiring managers recognize the benefits of hiring candidates with international experience. Not only do they understand different cultures and working styles, but they also adapt easily to new environments and exhibit strong problem-solving skills.

So, how can you maximize your international experience on your resume? Here are a few tips to help you showcase your unique story effectively:

  1. Use a functional format

A functional format resume highlights your skills and achievements rather than focusing on your work history in a chronological order. This format is ideal for candidates with international exposure as it allows you to emphasize the transferable skills you have acquired during your travels and placements abroad.

  1. Customize your sections

When presenting your resume, it’s essential to customize the sections depending on the position or company you’re applying to. As you have international experience, focusing on your multicultural skills, language proficiency, and cross-cultural experiences may be more relevant than emphasizing on your work history.

Instead of listing work experiences in chronological order, group them under relevant sections, like “International Projects” or “Work Abroad.” Write a compelling summarized description that highlights the skills and expertise you’ve gained during your expat journey.

  1. Highlight language skills

A significant part of international experience is communication, and language skills are essential in this context. Make sure you demonstrate your linguistic capabilities by listing all the languages you are proficient in. Include the level of proficiency (like Basic, Intermediate, Advanced, and Proficient) and mention any language certificates you may have.

  1. Mention cross-cultural experiences

Recruiters are highly interested in understanding how well a candidate’s international exposure has prepared him/her for diverse cultural environments. You can subtly mention your cross-cultural experiences by highlighting soft skills like empathy, adaptability, and emotional intelligence. Incorporate examples of how these skills have been beneficial in your previous roles.

  1. Incorporate multicultural skills

Demonstrate how your international experience has honed specific skills, such as teamwork, leadership, and problem-solving in a multicultural setting. If you have led multicultural teams or managed diverse departments, make sure to highlight these experiences on your resume. Using country-specific examples will add a touch of authenticity and showcase your adaptability and cultural awareness.

  1. Quantify your achievements

Numbers and percentages help to sell your accomplishments. For example, if you have expanded a company’s presence into a new market, specify how much revenue it generated, or the growth it achieved under your guidance. Quantifying your achievements takes your international experience to the next level and makes you stand out as a candidate with measurable results.

  1. Use keywords specific to the job position

As you customize your resume for each application, make sure to use industry-specific keywords in your summary or objective statement. Tailoring your language to fit the specific job posting demonstrates your understanding of the desired job qualifications and makes your resume stand out in the application pool.

  1. Relate foreign experience to the job

Identify the cross-functional hard and soft skills from your international experience that are closely aligned with the job role you’re applying to. Meticulously describe how your time abroad has equipped you uniquely for the job, and what specific skills you have acquired that will benefit the company.

In conclusion, employers consider international work experience favorably because it demonstrates the ability to adapt to new environments and overcome cultural differences. Present your cultural adjustment tale effectively by focusing on transferable skills, quantifying accomplishments, and making concise connections between your experience and the desired job.

Customizing and highlighting your international experience will give you an edge in your job search, paving the path to landing your desired role, whether you’re an expat, global nomad, or frequent traveler. Happy job hunting!
