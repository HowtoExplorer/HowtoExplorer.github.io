Cultural Geography: How Place Influences Identity


Geography is often seen as a fixed and static concept, simply referring to the physical environment in which we live. However, cultural geography delves deeper into the intricate relationships between people, place, and identity. The notion that our surroundings shape who we are and how we think is rooted in the concept of environmental determinism, which suggests that the environment has a direct impact on human behavior and culture. While this idea has evolved significantly over time, its core principle remains the same: place matters, and it matters deeply. Ties to the Land: The Communal Aspect of Identity In the realm of geography, it's not uncommon to come across studies that focus on urban planning, transportation systems, or the curing of poverty. While all these topics are crucial to our understanding of how geography shapes society, one key area that is rarely explored is the role of place in shaping identity. Our connection to the land can be derived from Indigenous, agrarian, or coastal communities, all of which reflect a common thread - a deep-rooted love and commitment to the land that forms the foundation of identity. These group-based efforts can foster a profound sense of belonging, shaping individuals into key components of communal life.
