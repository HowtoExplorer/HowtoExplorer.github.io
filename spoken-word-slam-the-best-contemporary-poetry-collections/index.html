Spoken Word Slam: The Best Contemporary Poetry Collections


In recent years, spoken word poetry has gained considerable traction, transcending the pages of books and into the realm of live performances, TV shows, and even master classes. From The TODAY Show to HBO’s Def Poetry Jam, the world stages have given birth to resilient spoken word slams and a multitude of contemporary poetry collections. To embark on this poetic journey, we must first delve into the world of spoken word poetry, explore its history, and look at some of the most gripping poetry collections that have graced the modern literary scene.

Spoken word poetry blends together the artistic realms of poetry and performance, oftentimes employing rhythm, voice inflection, and the performer’s body language to convey the true essence of the words on the page. The art form has its roots in the African American and Hip Hop culture of the 1980s and 1990s through the Beats, Black Arts, and neo-Black Arts movements. In the late 20th century, the Slamdance Film Festival organized poetry slams which led to the birth of the National Poetry Slam, galvanizing spoken word’s relevance in contemporary society. With slam poetry, the intimacy and immediacy of the live audience creates a palpable connection as the voice and the words intertwine to weave the fabric of powerful stories that resonate with listeners.

The spoken word scene boasts a plethora of captivating poetry collections that will stir your soul and leave you breathless. Ranging from heartfelt memoirs to brutal truth, here are some of the finest contemporary poetry collections to explore.

  1. The BreakBeat Poets Vol. 1: New American Poetry in the Age of Hip-Hop – Edited by Sauublé, Quran Brantley, and Tommy ‘TayDaINTEGER’ Pico This anthology features the daring poetry of the newly emergent Hip-Hop generation. It breaks the norm of traditional poetry and exposes fresh, authentic, and provocative voices that embody the unfiltered spirit of spoken word poetry. The diverse range of poets showcases an energetic blend of rhythms and styles that move beyond the notion of genre.

  2. Broke in Philly by M. Lynx Sagara M. Lynx Sagara brings you a rousing and transformative journey through love, loss, and resilience. In the heart of Philadelphia, this poetry collection braids the unspoken tales of pain and beauty, revealing the raw emotions of a Philadelphia native. Each piece resonates with the power of spoken word poetry, resulting in a passionate compilation of life’s fragility and strength.

  3. Land of the Giants by Armin Moradpour Land of the Giants conveys the transformative power of spoken word poetry as it travels through the life of migration, culture, and identity. The courage, love, and humor found throughout the poems enhance the sense of adventure and wanderlust, inviting readers to be a part of the migration experience, celebrated with sheer intensity and devotion.

  4. Hip Hop Speaks to Children: A Celebration of Poetry with a Beat by Nikki Grimes Renowned and respected poet Nikki Grimes offers a unique tapestry of spoken word masterpieces set to a hip-hop rhythm. This collection highlights the importance of providing a space for children to express themselves and appreciate the artistry of the spoken word. It beautifully conveys the power and creativity infused into the soul of poetry.

  5. Wilderness of One: Uncollected Poems 1984-2003 by Arthur Sze Wilderness of One is Arthur Sze’s collection of uncensored, unfiltered spoken word poetry reflecting a myriad of perspectives. Criss-crossing landscapes and cultures, the imagery weaved into these poems invigorates the fusion of language and the natural world, resulting in a mosaic of stunning and poignant artistry.

In conclusion, spoken word slams are ever-evolving expressions of humanity, witnessing a human experience brought to life through the enchanting pulsations of words. Marking the depths of the human condition, the collections mentioned above are rich vibrational experiences aimed at enlightening and unearthing the anxieties, joys, and sorrows that listeners can relate to in their own lives. Whether it be in the concert halls, streets, or open mics, spoken word slam poetry maintains the extraordinary potential to captivate and build bridges between humanity through the artform’s immersive spoken language.
