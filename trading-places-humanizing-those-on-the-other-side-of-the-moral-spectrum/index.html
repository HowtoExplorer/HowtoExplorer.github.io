Trading Places: Humanizing Those on the Other Side of the Moral Spectrum


When we stumble upon someone whose beliefs or actions seem radically different from our own, it’s only natural to feel a sense of discomfort. In today’s polarized world, our first instinct may be to dismiss or demonize those we deem as ‘others.’ However, by delving beneath the surface and approaching these individuals with empathy and respect, we may uncover meaningful commonalities. We have more in common with one another than we perceive; it just takes a dose of understanding.

One way of fostering this connection is by applying the concept of perspective trading, an idea derived from the classic film, “Trading Places.” In this movie, a wealthy upper-class trader and a streetwise hustler trade places in order to prove to each other the futility of their conventional beliefs about low-class people and high-class people, respectively. Through this arrangement, they both end up discovering the complex realities beyond their preconceptions.

The goal here is not to argue for the validity of either side nor to impose one’s values on others. Rather, it’s about opening a dialogue that allows us to learn from one another, appreciate different experiences, and work towards understanding the viewpoints of those we consider different.

To begin this trading process, we must first set aside our assumptions and be genuinely curious about those we are connecting with. Ask questions, listen actively, and seek to understand their experiences. Humanizing the other side of the moral spectrum isn’t about equating all viewpoints but valuing individual perspectives while recognizing the shared human experiences that bind us.

For example, let’s consider someone who holds strong anti-abortion views. We might initially dismiss these views as insensitive or backward. However, once we engage in empathic dialogue, we could discover that their stances are deeply rooted in their personal experiences or belief that every child has the potential to contribute positively to society. While this perspective may not align with ours, understanding the motives behind it can lead to more productive discourse.

The same goes for individuals on the other end of the spectrum – those who fervently support a pro-choice stance. We must extend the same courtesy of understanding to them, acknowledging that their beliefs may stem from validating a woman’s right to autonomy and prioritizing her well-being.

Expanding on this idea, consider debates on climate change, capitalism, or political ideologies. By implementing the principle of perspective trading, our dialogue shifts from a place of opposition to one of mutual respect and, more importantly, a shared sense of responsibility to address the challenges we all face.

Respectfully confronting the other side of the moral spectrum can unveil the complexities of each individual’s opinions and experiences. Moreover, it grants us a broader and more inclusive perspective on social issues. By learning from those we tend to disagree with, we can eventually bridge the gaps that divide us and foster understanding.

Perhaps one of the most difficult aspects of humanizing the other side is resisting the knee-jerk reactions that arise when faced with conflicting views. Logical fallacies, ad hominem attacks, and moral imperatives might urge us to demonize rather than understand. Ultimately, we need to remember that we’re all on the same team, and our true adversary is the ignorance that allows us to maintain an ‘us vs. them’ mentality.

Trying to truly understand the moral spectrum through perspective trading can have far-reaching effects, contributing to social progress and fostering more harmonious communities. While it might be uncomfortable at first, widening the aperture through which we view the world around us can reveal a rich tapestry of human experiences and values. Sincerely attentive listening and open dialogue can lead us to mutual understanding and empathy – a first step toward healing divided hearts and minds. So, let’s engage in perspective trading, allow ourselves to remain humble and open-minded enough to humanize the other side of the moral spectrum.

By embracing such a trading strategy, we can redefine ‘opposing’ sides as individuals who carry intersecting experiences and have something meaningful to offer one another. Let us venture beyond our comfort zones, challenge our convictions, and cultivate a genuine curiosity for the world around us. Only then can our conversation address much deeper and more nuanced issues that not only acknowledge our differences but also embrace our shared humanity.
