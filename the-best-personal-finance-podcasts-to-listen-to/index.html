The Best Personal Finance Podcasts to Listen To


In the ever-evolving world of personal finance, staying informed is crucial to making smart financial decisions. With the rise of podcasts, there is now a wealth of information available at the tip of your fingers. Whether you’re looking to get out of debt, learn how to invest, or plan for retirement, there’s a podcast that can help guide you on your journey to financial literacy. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best personal finance podcasts that provide valuable insights, expert advice, and actionable steps for improving your financial situation.

1. The Dave Ramsey Show

Dave Ramsey is a household name when it comes to personal finance, and his podcast is a must-listen for anyone seeking to take control of their money. The Dave Ramsey Show focuses on debt management, budgeting, and creating a plan for financial freedom. Ramsey’s straightforward approach, along with his 7 Baby Steps system, has helped millions of people pay off debt, save for emergencies, and build wealth.

What sets this podcast apart is the call-in format. Listeners can call in to share their financial struggles and get personalized advice from Dave. Whether you’re drowning in debt or looking for ways to improve your budgeting skills, this podcast offers practical tips and motivational stories that inspire action.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Practical advice for getting out of debt
  • Easy-to-follow budgeting strategies
  • Real-life stories and examples from listeners
  • Clear, actionable steps for financial independence

2. The Smart Passive Income Podcast

If you’re looking to generate multiple income streams or transition from a traditional 9-to-5 job to more entrepreneurial ventures, the Smart Passive Income Podcast by Pat Flynn is an essential resource. Flynn, an expert in online business and passive income, dives deep into the strategies and tools that can help you build sustainable income outside of a traditional job.

Episodes cover a variety of topics, from creating and marketing an online course to starting a profitable blog or podcast. He interviews successful entrepreneurs, shares his own journey, and provides actionable advice on how to start and grow a passive income business. With a focus on both the mindset and the practical steps needed for success, this podcast is perfect for anyone looking to improve their financial situation through entrepreneurship.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Insights on building passive income
  • Strategies for starting an online business
  • Expert interviews with successful entrepreneurs
  • Actionable steps for creating sustainable wealth

3. BiggerPockets Podcast

For those interested in real estate investing, the BiggerPockets Podcast is a treasure trove of information. Hosted by Brandon Turner and David Greene, this podcast is tailored to individuals who want to learn about real estate investing, from beginners to seasoned pros. The BiggerPockets team provides actionable advice on house flipping, rental properties, and building wealth through real estate.

The podcast features interviews with real estate investors who share their stories, strategies, and mistakes. It also covers broader financial topics related to building wealth, such as managing risk, improving credit scores, and understanding tax implications. Whether you’re interested in flipping homes or building a rental portfolio, the BiggerPockets Podcast is one of the best resources for anyone serious about real estate investing.

Why You Should Listen:

  • In-depth coverage of real estate investing
  • Expert interviews with top investors
  • Tips on managing rental properties and house flipping
  • Broader wealth-building strategies beyond real estate

4. The Financial Independence Podcast

Achieving financial independence (FI) is a goal for many, and the Financial Independence Podcast is a must-listen for anyone looking to retire early or build wealth over the long term. Hosted by Joe Udo and others, this podcast explores topics related to the FIRE movement (Financial Independence, Retire Early). It provides practical advice on how to save aggressively, invest wisely, and design a life of freedom and choice.

The podcast covers a variety of topics, including budgeting, investing, minimizing expenses, and living frugally. It also features interviews with individuals who have achieved financial independence and are living their dreams. Whether you’re just starting to plan for your financial future or are already on the path to financial independence, this podcast is a great resource for actionable advice and inspiration.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Learn how to achieve financial independence
  • Strategies for saving and investing aggressively
  • Inspirational interviews with those who have reached FI
  • Practical tips on budgeting and living frugally

5. Afford Anything Podcast

The Afford Anything Podcast, hosted by Paula Pant, is a great resource for those interested in achieving financial freedom and living a life by design. Paula takes a holistic approach to personal finance, covering topics like real estate investing, early retirement, and the psychology behind financial decision-making.

What makes this podcast unique is its focus on the intersection of money and life choices. Paula helps listeners reframe how they think about money and gives practical advice for making better financial decisions. She also interviews a wide range of guests, from financial experts to everyday people who have achieved financial independence in unconventional ways.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Focus on money as a tool for life design
  • In-depth discussions on investing and financial freedom
  • Practical tips on decision-making and goal setting
  • Inspiring guest interviews

6. The Stacking Benjamins Show

If you want a lighthearted, entertaining take on personal finance, the Stacking Benjamins Show is perfect for you. Hosted by Joe Saul-Sehy and Josh Bannerman, this podcast offers a fun, conversational approach to financial topics. It covers a wide variety of personal finance subjects, from budgeting and saving to investing and planning for retirement.

While the show is known for its humorous tone, it’s also packed with valuable insights and expert advice. The hosts often bring in industry experts and financial advisors to share their wisdom, making it both entertaining and educational. The Stacking Benjamins Show is ideal for those who want to learn about personal finance in a way that doesn’t feel like a lecture.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Fun, conversational approach to finance
  • Broad range of topics covered
  • Expert advice mixed with humor and entertainment
  • Great for people looking for an engaging way to learn about finance

7. The Mad Fientist Financial Independence Podcast

The Mad Fientist Financial Independence Podcast focuses on advanced financial strategies for achieving financial independence. Hosted by Brandon, the Mad Fientist, this podcast is perfect for those who already have a basic understanding of personal finance and are looking to dive deeper into more complex topics. The show covers a wide range of subjects, from tax strategies to advanced investing techniques, all aimed at helping you reach financial independence faster.

Brandon often interviews financial experts and offers in-depth analysis on specific strategies that can help listeners maximize their savings and investments. If you’re serious about reaching FI and want to optimize your financial plan, this podcast is a valuable resource.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Focus on advanced financial strategies for achieving FI
  • Expert interviews with financial planners and experts
  • In-depth analysis of tax strategies, investing, and wealth management
  • Perfect for those who want to take their financial planning to the next level

8. The Motley Fool Money Show

The Motley Fool Money Show is an excellent podcast for anyone interested in learning more about investing and the stock market. Hosted by Chris Hill and a team of Motley Fool analysts, this podcast provides timely updates on the stock market, investment strategies, and the latest business news.

The show also features interviews with top investors, business leaders, and industry experts who share their insights on market trends and how to build a long-term investment strategy. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced investor, The Motley Fool Money Show provides valuable information to help you navigate the world of investing with confidence.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Expert analysis of the stock market and investment strategies
  • Regular interviews with top investors and business leaders
  • Actionable tips for building a diverse investment portfolio
  • A great resource for both beginners and experienced investors

9. The Money Tree Investing Podcast

The Money Tree Investing Podcast is a fantastic resource for individuals interested in building wealth through investing. Hosted by a group of experienced financial advisors, this podcast covers topics such as stock market investing, real estate, retirement planning, and more. Each episode dives into different investing strategies, tools, and trends that can help you make better decisions with your money.

What sets this podcast apart is its emphasis on long-term investing strategies and the importance of understanding risk. The hosts offer a balanced approach, providing both the benefits and potential pitfalls of various investment options. If you’re looking for a comprehensive guide to investing, this podcast is definitely worth checking out.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Focus on long-term investing strategies
  • Expert insights on various types of investments
  • Actionable advice on minimizing risk and maximizing returns
  • Perfect for those looking to build a diverse portfolio

10. Real Wealth Show

The Real Wealth Show, hosted by Kathy Fettke, focuses on real estate investing and wealth-building strategies. Kathy is a seasoned real estate investor and founder of Real Wealth Network, a company dedicated to helping people build wealth through real estate. The podcast covers a wide range of topics, from passive income strategies to market trends and investment opportunities.

What makes this podcast unique is its focus on real estate investing for beginners and experienced investors alike. Kathy often interviews real estate professionals, offering listeners a behind-the-scenes look at successful investment strategies. Whether you’re just getting started or you’re looking to expand your portfolio, the Real Wealth Show is an excellent resource.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Focus on building wealth through real estate
  • Interviews with top real estate professionals
  • Tips for both beginners and experienced investors
  • Comprehensive coverage of the real estate market

Conclusion

Personal finance podcasts are an invaluable resource for anyone looking to improve their financial literacy, make informed decisions, and ultimately achieve financial freedom. The podcasts listed above offer a wealth of knowledge, expert advice, and actionable steps for tackling everything from debt management to investing and retirement planning.

Whether you’re just starting out or are already well on your way to financial independence, there is a podcast on this list that can help you take your financial journey to the next level. So, put on your headphones, hit play, and start learning how to take control of your financial future today!
