10 Untranslatable Words from Around the World


Language is a fascinating reflection of culture, and sometimes, there are words in one language that simply have no direct equivalent in another. These untranslatable words often encapsulate unique feelings, experiences, or phenomena that are not easily expressed in other languages. Here are 10 intriguing untranslatable words from around the world that offer a glimpse into the rich tapestry of human emotions and experiences.

1. Mamihlapinatapei (Yaghan, Chile)

This word refers to a meaningful yet unspoken look shared by two people who both desire to initiate something but are both reluctant to start. It captures the essence of a profound moment of mutual understanding and unspoken empathy.

2. Komorebi (Japanese)

Komorebi describes the interplay of light and leaves when sunlight filters through the trees. This word beautifully captures the serene and enchanting beauty of nature, a concept that many languages lack a specific term for.

3. Tingo (Pascuense, Easter Island)

In the Pascuense language spoken on Easter Island, “tingo” means to borrow objects from a friend’s house, one by one, until there is nothing left. This word reflects a unique cultural practice and the trust and generosity embedded within it.

4. Saudade (Portuguese)

Saudade is a deep emotional state of nostalgic longing for something or someone that one loves and that is lost. It’s a complex mix of emotions that encompasses yearning, melancholy, and a bittersweet sense of memory and absence.

5. Schadenfreude (German)

This word, although widely known, has no direct equivalent in English. It refers to the pleasure derived from the misfortunes of others, capturing a complex and often taboo emotion that is recognized and named in German culture.

6. Jayus (Indonesian)

In Indonesia, “jayus” refers to a joke so poorly told and so unfunny that one cannot help but laugh. This term humorously captures the awkwardness of finding something amusing despite its lack of comedic value.

7. Fernweh (German)

Fernweh describes a deep ache for distant places, a homesickness for the unknown. While “wanderlust” is often used in English, fernweh encapsulates a specific yearning for far-off lands, tinged with a sense of romantic longing.

8. Gigil (Filipino)

This Filipino word refers to the irresistible urge to squeeze or pinch something that is incredibly cute, often a baby or a small animal. The lack of a direct translation in English makes this word a charming addition to the lexicon of untranslatable words.

9. Pochemuchka (Russian)

A “pochemuchka” is someone who asks a lot of questions. However, the word goes beyond that simple definition to convey a sense of curiosity and inquisitiveness that is often seen as endearing in Russian culture.

10. L’appel du vide (French)

Literally translating to “the call of the void,” this phrase captures the inexplicable urge to jump when standing at a great height. It encapsulates the paradoxical attraction to danger and the fleeting thought of self-destruction.

These untranslatable words offer a glimpse into the rich and diverse range of human experiences and emotions that are uniquely captured by different cultures and languages. They remind us of the intricacies and nuances of human expression and the beauty of linguistic diversity.

Language is a living, breathing entity that evolves alongside its speakers, and these untranslatable words provide a window into the complex emotions and experiences that shape our lives. As we continue to explore and appreciate the diverse linguistic landscape of the world, we gain a deeper understanding of the human experience, one untranslatable word at a time.

Whether it’s the yearning for distant lands, the bittersweet nostalgia for a lost love, or the inexplicable urge to laugh at a terrible joke, these words offer a unique lens through which to view the world and the depths of human emotion. Embracing the untranslatable allows us to celebrate the richness of each culture and the depth of human experience that transcends language barriers.
