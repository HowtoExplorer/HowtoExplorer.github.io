Planting by the Moon: Lunar Gardening Techniques


As an ancient agricultural practice, lunar gardening is gradually gaining popularity globally. This method of planting, popularly known as ‘planting by the moon,’ is linked with the lunar cycle as moon phases are thought to influence plant growth. Cultivators have for centuries claimed that aligning with the phases of the moon can lead to substantial improvements in crop production and yields.

Lunar gardening leverages gravitational forces believed to stimulate plant growth, water absorption, and seed germination at different lunar phases. Although scientific evidence confirming the effectiveness of these techniques remains limited, many gardeners remain deeply committed to incorporating lunar gardening into their cultivation practices. In this blog, we unveil the secrets of lunar gardening techniques as we embark on an exploration of planting by the moon.

Starting with Moonflowers

Moonflowers represent a perfect entry point for lunar gardening. They grow well during the full moon when there’s ample light. Be sure to sow the seeds about four weeks before the first frost. To further maximize lunar energy, sow the seeds during a waxing moon. As the moon starts to wane, begin the transplantation process.

No-Fail Garlic Growing

Garlic is well-known for its incredible health benefits. If you plan on growing garlic, consider aligning with the planting cycle of the new moon. This helps to promote root growth, ensuring your garlic plants get a strong foundation. As your plants begin to establish themselves during the waxing moon phase, you can exercise invigorating companion planting with marigolds, tomatoes, and basil.

Give Your Strawberries the Moon Treatment

Strawberries, too, respond positively to the lunar cycle. Plant during the new moon for balanced and healthy growth. Keep the planting beds moist through the entire moon cycle for maximum benefits.

Woodland Indigo Bush and the Moon

Woodland Indigo is a special perennial plant with various uses. Full moon planting is recommended for this plant, which grows exceptionally well under highly fertile soils. Indigo is known to enhance the energy of your garden and make it more productive once planted using this lunar gardening technique.

The Importance of Moon Phases

The lunar cycle can be divided into four significant phases: the new moon, waxing moon, full moon, and waning moon. Here’s how to align with each phase for best results:

  1. New Moon: This phase is ideal for planting root crops like onions, carrots, and radishes. New moon energy is also beneficial for setting seeds for annuals and perennials.

  2. Waxing Moon: This moon phase fosters growth, so it’s perfect for actions that encourage blooming, fruiting, and harvesting. During this time, sow seeds that grow above ground like leafy greens, herbs, or直接gourd seeds.

  3. Full Moon: This phase provides optimal growth, making it a great time to grasp weeds, deadhead plants, and perform any pruning.

  4. Waning Moon: When the moon starts to fade, it’s an excellent time to harvest items. Also, use this period to influence the growth of plants by staking or pruning them in your garden.

Incorporating lunar gardening techniques can be a fun and rewarding addition to your gardening plans. By understanding the connection between the moon’s phases and the growth of plants, you can harness the energy of the moon to guide your planting, germination, and growth cycles. With time, you’ll find that lunar gardening opens up a fascinating partnership between gardener, plant, and the moon. Embrace the magic of the moon and watch your garden flourish!
