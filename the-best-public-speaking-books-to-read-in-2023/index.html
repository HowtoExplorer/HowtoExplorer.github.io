The Best Public Speaking Books to Read in 2023


Public speaking has long been considered one of the most valuable skills a person can learn. Whether you’re leading a team presentation at work, giving a toast at a wedding, or trying to grow as an influencer, effective communication can set you apart. For those looking to improve their skills, the right books can serve as an affordable and empowering tool for transformation.

In this post, we’ve curated some of the best books on public speaking to read in 2023. These books range from classics that provide timeless advice to contemporary works that reflect the latest techniques. Each one has something unique to offer for individuals at every stage of their public speaking journey.

1. “Talk Like TED” by Carmine Gallo

This book explores the art of captivating an audience, inspired by some of the most viewed TED Talks of all time. Carmine Gallo distills years of research and observation into concise principles that anyone can follow. The book breaks down what makes TED speakers so engaging, whether it’s emotional storytelling, simplicity in messaging, or mastering body language.

What makes “Talk Like TED” stand out is its focus on blending data and storytelling, teaching readers how to create emotional connections while delivering valuable information. This balance is essential for modern public speakers who want to resonate with diverse audiences.

2. “The Art of Public Speaking” by Dale Carnegie and J. Berg Esenwein

A quintessential guide for aspiring speakers, this updated version of Dale Carnegie’s classic work continues to help readers master the fundamentals of public speaking. Carnegie covers key principles such as overcoming stage fright, using vocal variety, and organizing your thoughts for maximum impact.

Its longevity is a testament to its value, proving that many timeless techniques in public speaking are still relevant today. Whether you’re new to public speaking or need a refresher, this book remains a must-read for its practical advice packed in an easy-to-understand format.

3. “You’re Speaking, But Are You Being Heard?” by Dr. Erik Palmer

Dr. Erik Palmer emphasizes that speaking isn’t just about saying words but ensuring those words are understood and remembered by your audience. This book dives into how to connect with any audience through clear, compelling communication strategies. It provides frameworks for structuring dynamic presentations and becoming more persuasive.

One particularly valuable aspect of Palmer’s book is his take on how modern communication tools—like slides and virtual conferencing—impact our interactions. The book speaks directly to today’s professional world while helping readers master nuances of delivery and engagement.

4. “Pitch Perfect” by Bill McGowan

For professionals in business and sales, “Pitch Perfect” is one of the most practical public speaking books you can find. Bill McGowan, a media coach with decades of experience, teaches the skills needed to present ideas in a crisp, clear, and compelling way.

What sets this book apart is its emphasis on curating the perfect tone for the right setting, whether you’re pitching to investors or conducting a team meeting. It’s full of actionable tips that both novice and experienced speakers can integrate right away. If you speak professionally, this book is a treasure trove of insight.

5. “Presence” by Amy Cuddy

Technically not just about public speaking, Amy Cuddy’s “Presence” focuses on how body language influences both you and your audience during communication. Known for her TED Talk on power poses, Cuddy’s book dives deeper into how confidence and authenticity shape a person’s ability to connect.

This book’s strength lies in its psychological foundation. Cuddy’s writing encourages readers to harness their unique qualities to build presence regardless of setting—whether on stage, in meetings, or even during one-on-one interactions. If you struggle with anxiety or imposter syndrome, “Presence” offers strategic advice to elevate your speaking game.

6. “Confessions of a Public Speaker” by Scott Berkun

For those who enjoy a more entertaining take on public speaking, Scott Berkun’s “Confessions of a Public Speaker” offers humor alongside powerful lessons learned from his extensive speaking career. The book stands out for its candid stories about what worked, what failed, and how he adapted along the way.

Berkun demystifies public speaking by tackling common fears and misconceptions head-on. His approachable style makes even daunting concepts like speech preparation or audience management feel surprisingly accessible. It’s a refreshing read for those intimidated by more technical or formal public speaking guides.

7. “Steal the Show” by Michael Port

If you see public speaking as a performance, “Steal the Show” will resonate deeply. Michael Port takes principles from acting and applies them seamlessly to other speaking environments. His methodology encourages speakers to view their roles from the audience’s perspective, focusing on delivering memorable experiences.

Port’s book addresses everything from crafting narratives to managing anxiety. It’s great for individuals looking to hone both soft presentation skills and technical aspects like stage presence and vocal delivery.

8. “The Quick and Easy Way to Effective Speaking” by Dale Carnegie

A follow-up to his legendary work, this book narrows in on specific methods for effective speaking. Carnegie details actionable steps for handling nerves, engaging audiences, and expressing ideas naturally. Its conversational tone makes it ideal for beginners without overwhelming them.

What stands out about this book is its practical approach to mastering the basics. Using techniques that worked for Carnegie’s students over decades, it’s a confidence-building guide for those just beginning their public speaking journey.

9. “Speak with No Fear” by Mike Acker

If one of your primary challenges is overcoming fear, Mike Acker’s “Speak with No Fear” provides a solid game plan. The book gives practical recommendations for addressing the root causes of stage fright.

Acker divides his methodology into seven steps that empower readers to face their fears and develop confidence over time. His wisdom is especially useful for introverts or those who consider themselves “poor speakers.” The tone throughout the book is encouraging and rooted in practice over theory.

10. “Speak Up, Dammit!” by Hope Timberlake

Hope Timberlake’s book challenges readers to unapologetically own their voice in any public speaking or presentation setting. She explores how to advocate for yourself without sounding pushy and how to combat internal self-doubt.

Special focus is given to spontaneous speaking moments—like answering on-the-spot questions or participating in team discussions—making this an incredibly relevant addition to the list.

Why Public Speaking Books Are Worth Your Time

Whether you’re a seasoned speaker or just starting out, the right book can reshape how you approach communication. These authors bring diverse expertise, ranging from psychology to business to storytelling, ensuring a well-rounded toolkit for improvement. The lessons in these books not only improve your public speaking skills but also help in personal branding, career advancement, and social confidence.

By exploring one or more of the books listed here, you’ll find yourself equipped to handle high-stakes presentations with poise, deliver memorable speeches, and leave a lasting impression. 2023 is the perfect time to invest in your skillset—so take the first step and choose your read today.
