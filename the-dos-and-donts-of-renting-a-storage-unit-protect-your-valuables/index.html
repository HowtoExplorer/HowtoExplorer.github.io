The Dos and Don'ts of Renting a Storage Unit: Protect Your Valuables


Renting a storage unit can be a convenient solution for decluttering your home, storing seasonal items, or keeping valuable belongings safe during a move. However, it’s important to approach the process with caution to ensure the protection of your valuables. In this blog post, we will discuss the dos and don’ts of renting a storage unit, providing you with valuable insights to safeguard your belongings.

The Dos

1. Research Different Storage Facilities

Before committing to a storage unit, take the time to research different facilities in your area. Look for reputable companies with good reviews and a track record of security. Choose a facility that offers features like 24/7 surveillance, well-lit premises, and secure access control to provide maximum protection for your valuables.

2. Determine the Right Size

Evaluate your storage needs carefully to determine the appropriate unit size. It’s better to opt for a slightly larger unit than you initially think you need, as this will give you room to access your belongings comfortably and prevent overcrowding. Consider using online tools or speaking with facility staff to assist you in determining the ideal unit size.

3. Use High-Quality Locks

Invest in high-quality, tamper-resistant locks to secure your storage unit. Avoid using cheap padlocks that can easily be cut or manipulated. A sturdy lock acts as a deterrent to potential thieves and adds an extra layer of protection to your belongings. Combination locks or disc locks are recommended for their durability and resistance to picking.

4. Pack and Label Your Belongings Strategically

When packing your belongings for storage, it’s crucial to do so strategically. Use sturdy boxes and packing materials to protect fragile items from damage. Label each box clearly with its contents to make it easier to locate specific items when needed. Additionally, consider creating an inventory list to keep track of what you have stored.

5. Protect Fragile Items

If you’re storing fragile items, take extra precautions to protect them. Wrap delicate items in bubble wrap or packing paper and use dividers to separate and cushion them. Place these items towards the back of the unit, away from heavy or sharp objects that could cause damage. Utilize shelves or racks to keep fragile items off the ground and minimize the risk of accidents.

The Don’ts

1. Don’t Neglect Insurance

While storage facilities take measures to ensure the security of their premises, accidents can still happen. Don’t make the mistake of neglecting insurance for your stored belongings. Check if your existing homeowner’s or renter’s insurance policy covers storage units, or consider purchasing separate storage insurance. Having insurance provides peace of mind and financial protection in case of unforeseen circumstances.

2. Don’t Store Perishable or Hazardous Items

Storage units are not suitable for storing perishable items or hazardous materials. Avoid storing food, plants, chemicals, or flammable substances as they can attract pests, cause damage to other items, or even pose a safety risk. Familiarize yourself with the facility’s policies regarding prohibited items to prevent any issues.

3. Don’t Forget to Visit the Unit Regularly

Even if you’re not actively accessing your storage unit, it’s essential to visit it periodically. Regular visits allow you to check on the condition of your belongings and ensure everything is secure. By doing so, you can promptly address any potential issues, such as leaks, pests, or security concerns.

4. Don’t Overlook Climate Control

If you’re storing items that are sensitive to temperature and humidity, consider renting a climate-controlled unit. Extreme variations in temperature and moisture levels can damage delicate items like electronics, artwork, or wooden furniture. Climate-controlled units maintain consistent temperature and humidity levels, protecting your valuables from potential harm.

5. Don’t Share Your Access Code or Key

Keep your storage unit access code or key confidential and avoid sharing it with anyone outside of trusted individuals. This helps maintain the security and privacy of your unit. If you need to grant access to someone, accompany them during their visit to ensure the safety of your belongings.

By following these dos and don’ts of renting a storage unit, you can protect your valuables and enjoy peace of mind. Remember to research different facilities, choose the right size unit, use high-quality locks, pack strategically, and protect fragile items. Don’t neglect insurance, avoid storing perishable or hazardous items, visit your unit regularly, consider climate control, and keep your access code or key confidential. With these tips in mind, you can confidently rent a storage unit and safeguard your valuables.
