How to Make Traditional English Scones: A Simple Recipe


When it comes to classic British cuisine, few treats evoke the charm and comfort of a freshly baked scone. These delightful pastries, often enjoyed with clotted cream and jam, are a staple of afternoon tea and a beloved part of many celebrations. Whether you’re hosting a tea party, enjoying a cozy breakfast, or simply indulging yourself, learning how to make traditional English scones can be a rewarding experience. This guide will walk you through the process, share tips for perfect scones, and explore delightful variations to suit your taste.

The Origins of English Scones

Scones have a rich history that traces back to Scotland in the early 16th century. Originally made from oats, these baked goods evolved over time, gaining popularity across England as they transitioned to using wheat flour. Today, scones are a quintessential part of British culture, often served with afternoon tea, layered with jam and cream. Understanding their history enriches the experience of making and enjoying these treats.

Ingredients You Will Need

To create the perfect traditional English scone, you’ll need the following ingredients:

  • 225g (8 oz) all-purpose flour: The foundation of your scones, providing structure.
  • 75g (3 oz) unsalted butter: Adds richness and flavor.
  • 30g (1 oz) granulated sugar: Sweetens the dough.
  • 1 tbsp baking powder: Helps the scones rise.
  • Pinch of salt: Enhances flavor.
  • 150ml (5 fl oz) whole milk: Hydrates the dough, contributing to tenderness.
  • 1 large egg: Optional, to enrich the flavor and texture.
  • Optional add-ins: Dried fruit (like currants or raisins) or citrus zest for a flavor twist.

Equipment Needed

Before you begin, gather the necessary equipment:

  • Mixing bowl
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Pastry cutter or two forks
  • Baking sheet
  • Rolling pin
  • Dough cutter or sharp knife
  • Sieve or flour sifter (optional)
  • Pastry brush (if using egg wash)

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Preparing the Oven

Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F). This step ensures that the scones will bake evenly and rise beautifully.

Step 2: Mixing the Dry Ingredients

In a large mixing bowl, sift the flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt. Sifting aerates the flour, ensuring a light and fluffy scone.

Step 3: Incorporating the Butter

Cut the unsalted butter into small cubes and add them to the dry ingredients. Using your fingers or a pastry cutter, rub the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs. This process creates small pockets of butter, which are crucial for achieving a flaky texture.

Step 4: Adding Milk and Other Ingredients

Make a well in the center of the flour mixture and pour in the milk. If you’re using an egg, beat it lightly and add it to the milk. Gently stir the mixture with a wooden spoon or your hands until it comes together. Be careful not to overmix; you want a soft dough, not a tough one.

If you’re adding dried fruits or zest, fold them in at this stage.

Step 5: Shaping the Dough

Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface. With floured hands, gently knead the dough just until it holds together—this should take no more than a minute. Roll out the dough to about 2.5cm (1 inch) thick.

Step 6: Cutting the Scones

Using a sharp knife or a round cutter, cut out your scones. Place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, making sure to leave space between them for expansion during baking.

Step 7: The Egg Wash (Optional)

If you want a golden finish, beat an egg and brush it over the tops of the scones with a pastry brush. This step is optional but adds a beautiful sheen.

Step 8: Baking the Scones

Slide the baking sheet into the preheated oven. Bake for about 12-15 minutes or until the scones have risen and are golden brown. Keep an eye on them to prevent overbaking.

Step 9: Cooling and Serving

Once baked, remove the scones from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack. Serve warm with clotted cream and your favorite jam, or enjoy them plain.

Tips for Perfect Scones

  • Chill Your Ingredients: For extra flaky scones, consider chilling the butter and milk beforehand.
  • Don’t Overwork the Dough: Overkneading can produce tough scones. Handle the dough gently.
  • Experiment with Flavors: Try incorporating different flavors, such as orange zest, chocolate chips, or various spices.
  • Use a Sharp Cutter: Using a sharp cutter ensures clean edges, allowing the scones to rise evenly.

Variations on the Classic Recipe

While the traditional scone is delightful, experimenting with variations can elevate your baking game. Here are a few ideas:

Fruit Scones

Add 75-100g of dried fruits like cranberries, apricots, or blueberries to the dough for a burst of flavor.

Cheese Scones

For a savory twist, omit sugar and add 100g of grated cheese (like cheddar) instead. These are perfect with soups or as a snack.

Herb and Garlic Scones

Add finely chopped herbs (like rosemary or thyme) and minced garlic to the dough for a fragrant and flavorful scone.

Chocolate Chip Scones

For those with a sweet tooth, fold in a handful of chocolate chips. Enjoy with a cup of coffee for a delightful treat.

Storing and Reheating Scones

If you have leftover scones, store them in an airtight container at room temperature for up to two days. For longer storage, freeze them individually wrapped. To reheat, place them in a warm oven for a few minutes to regain their freshness.

Conclusion

Learning how to make traditional English scones is a delightful culinary adventure that brings together simplicity and flavor. With this straightforward recipe, you can impress your family and friends with homemade scones that rival any bakery. Whether you stick to the classic version or try your hand at creative variations, the joy of enjoying a warm scone with a cup of tea is a timeless experience. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and let’s bake some scones!
