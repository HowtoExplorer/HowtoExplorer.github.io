The Art of Building a Connection with the Interviewer: Tips and Strategies


When it comes to acing an interview, it’s not just about your qualifications and experience; it’s also about building a connection with the interviewer. Building a rapport can make a significant difference in how you are perceived as a candidate and can ultimately increase your chances of landing that dream job. In this article, we will explore some valuable tips and strategies to help you master the art of building a connection with the interviewer.

1. Research and Preparation

Before you step into the interview room, take the time to research the company, its culture, and the position you are applying for. Familiarize yourself with the company’s values, mission, and recent achievements. This knowledge will not only help you answer questions more confidently but also allow you to engage the interviewer in a meaningful conversation about their organization.

2. Show Genuine Interest

During the interview, show genuine interest in the role and the company. Ask thoughtful questions that demonstrate your eagerness to learn more about the organization. This not only communicates your enthusiasm but also allows you to establish a connection based on mutual curiosity and engagement.

3. Active Listening

One key aspect of building a connection is active listening. Pay attention to what the interviewer is saying, maintain eye contact, and respond appropriately. Show your engagement by nodding, smiling, and providing verbal cues. This not only demonstrates your attentiveness but also helps establish a rapport with the interviewer.

4. Find Common Ground

During the interview, try to find common ground with the interviewer. Look for shared interests, hobbies, or experiences that you can discuss. This can create an instant connection and make the conversation more enjoyable for both parties. However, be genuine in your approach and avoid forcing commonalities that do not exist.

5. Share Relevant Stories

When answering questions, consider incorporating relevant stories from your past experiences. Stories have a way of captivating the audience and making a lasting impression. By sharing personal anecdotes that showcase your skills, problem-solving abilities, or teamwork, you not only engage the interviewer but also provide concrete examples of your qualifications.

6. Use Positive Body Language

Body language plays a vital role in building connections. Maintain good posture, smile genuinely, and use open gestures. Avoid crossing your arms or displaying closed-off body language, as it can create a barrier between you and the interviewer. Positive body language conveys confidence, approachability, and a genuine desire to connect.

7. Follow-Up with a Thank You Note

After the interview, don’t forget to send a personalized thank you note to the interviewer. Express your appreciation for the opportunity to interview and reiterate your interest in the position. A thoughtful follow-up shows your professionalism and can help solidify the connection you’ve built during the interview.

Building a connection with the interviewer is an essential part of the interview process. By conducting thorough research, showing genuine interest, actively listening, finding common ground, sharing relevant stories, using positive body language, and following up with a thank you note, you can greatly enhance your chances of leaving a lasting impression and securing that coveted job offer.

Remember, building connections goes beyond just the interview. It’s about establishing strong professional relationships that can benefit you throughout your career. So, take the time to master the art of building a connection, and watch as doors of opportunity swing wide open. Happy interviewing!
