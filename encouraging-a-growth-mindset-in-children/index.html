Encouraging a Growth Mindset in Children


A growth mindset is the foundation of emotional intelligence, and consciously instilling it in children is a life-changing skill. Encouraging children to embrace the power of their minds and overcome challenges in life encourages growth. This piece seeks to discuss ways of stimulating a growth mindset in children.

  1. Acknowledge Effort and Progress

Often, a child’s inquiry of “Did I get it right?” following a successful demonstration could imply a desire for validation. As such, parents or educators can respond by praising the child’s effort and progress, instead of their innate ability. For example, stating “You worked hard and improved significantly” or “That’s pretty impressive progress for just two weeks” acknowledges the growth and effort while fueling motivation and inspiring greater efforts.

  1. Acknowledge Mistakes as Learning Opportunities

Making mistakes can hinder children who are more concerned with the fear of failing. Mistakes should be perceived as pits to avoid, rather than scarecrows on the road to success. Instead of focusing on the fixable mistake, address the growth by stating how the child learned from it. Mention how their inquiry or the difference in the outcome can promote future progress, framing it as a win, irrespective of the outcome.

  1. Teach Growth Mindset Concepts

One is often the product of one’s conception. Modeling a growth mindset and introducing the idea to your child compellingly is fundamental. Break down the principle of a growth mindset— open-mindedness, arming oneself with information, and facing uncertainties—and teach them the concept. Play games or activities that illustrate the power of the mindset in different scenarios to make it memorable and more fun.

  1. Emphasize Learning from Others

Explain how learning from others paves room for a growth mindset. It’s not all about one’s unique experience; we learn even from those we view as worse off or from documentaries and stories of other cultures. It is okay to learn from mentors or even, from heroes. Emphasizing and appreciating the role of others showing their experiences and endeavors can lead to a more profound understanding in the child. Inspire them to envision themselves overcoming hurdles through the lens of others’ knowledge.

  1. Protect Creativity

Creative expression has deep roots in a growth mindset. Originality requires a mindset that embraces uniqueness and encourages a voice of its own. A sense of freedom and encouragement should be the theme prevalent when children are given free rein to express themselves creatively.

A growth mindset cognition in children correlates with the ability to find positivity and constructive solutions in adversity. Equipping children with the right mindset for overcoming challenges promotes the ability to learn from tough situations, supporting overall resilience and emotional intelligence. Encourage them to develop into robust people with the ability to think positively under pressure as they grow.
