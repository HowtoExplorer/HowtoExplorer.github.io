Easy and Flavorful Mexican Enchilada Recipes for a Crowd


Enchiladas are a delicious and crowd-pleasing Mexican dish that combines the flavors of tender tortillas, savory fillings, and rich sauces. Whether you’re hosting a family gathering, a dinner party, or simply want to indulge in a flavorful meal, enchiladas are the perfect choice. In this blog post, we will explore three easy and flavorful Mexican enchilada recipes that are sure to impress your guests. So, let’s dive right in!

1. Classic Beef Enchiladas

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) tomato sauce
  • 1 can (4 ounces) diced green chilies
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded Mexican blend cheese
  • Optional toppings: sour cream, diced tomatoes, chopped cilantro

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onion is translucent.
  3. Drain any excess grease from the skillet, then stir in the tomato sauce, diced green chilies, chili powder, ground cumin, salt, and pepper. Simmer the mixture for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave to make them more pliable.
  5. Spoon a generous amount of the beef mixture onto each tortilla and roll them up tightly. Place the rolled enchiladas seam-side down in a greased baking dish.
  6. Pour any remaining beef mixture over the enchiladas and sprinkle the shredded cheese on top.
  7. Bake the enchiladas in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Serve the enchiladas warm, garnished with sour cream, diced tomatoes, and chopped cilantro, if desired.

2. Chicken and Spinach Enchiladas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, shredded
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup salsa verde
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 flour tortillas
  • 1 can (10 ounces) red enchilada sauce
  • Optional toppings: guacamole, sliced black olives, chopped green onions

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the shredded chicken, chopped spinach, shredded Monterey Jack cheese, sour cream, salsa verde, ground cumin, salt, and pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.
  3. Warm the flour tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave to make them more pliable.
  4. Spoon a generous amount of the chicken and spinach mixture onto each tortilla and roll them up tightly. Place the rolled enchiladas seam-side down in a greased baking dish.
  5. Pour the red enchilada sauce evenly over the enchiladas.
  6. Bake the enchiladas in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until heated through.
  7. Serve the enchiladas warm, garnished with guacamole, sliced black olives, and chopped green onions, if desired.

3. Vegetarian Black Bean Enchiladas

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (15 ounces each) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (15 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (4 ounces) diced green chilies
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • Optional toppings: sliced avocado, chopped fresh cilantro, lime wedges

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large skillet, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until the onion is translucent.
  3. Add the black beans, diced tomatoes, diced green chilies, ground cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Stir well and let the mixture simmer for about 10 minutes.
  4. Warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave to make them more pliable.
  5. Spoon a generous amount of the black bean mixture onto each tortilla and roll them up tightly. Place the rolled enchiladas seam-side down in a greased baking dish.
  6. Sprinkle the shredded cheddar cheese evenly over the enchiladas.
  7. Bake the enchiladas in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Serve the enchiladas warm, garnished with sliced avocado, chopped fresh cilantro, and lime wedges, if desired.

Conclusion

These easy and flavorful Mexican enchilada recipes are perfect for feeding a crowd. Whether you prefer the classic beef filling, the chicken and spinach combination, or the vegetarian black bean option, each recipe promises to deliver a burst of authentic Mexican flavors. So, gather your loved ones, roll up some enchiladas, and enjoy a delicious and satisfying meal together. Buen provecho!
