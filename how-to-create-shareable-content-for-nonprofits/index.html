How to Create Shareable Content for Nonprofits


Creating shareable content is essential for nonprofits looking to expand their reach and engage more effectively with their audience. Engaging content not only promotes your mission but also encourages supporters to share it with their networks, amplifying your voice and impact. In this guide, you’ll learn how to create compelling, shareable content tailored to your nonprofit organization's goals. Understanding Your Audience Before you can create content that resonates, it’s crucial to understand who your audience is. Start by defining your target demographic. Consider their age, interests, motivations, and challenges. You might ask questions like:

What drives them to support nonprofit causes? What platforms do they use most frequently? What type of content do they engage with the most (videos, articles, infographics, etc.)?

Once you have a clear picture of your audience, you can create content tailored to their needs and preferences. Developing a Content Strategy A solid content strategy is essential for producing shareable content consistently. Here are several strategies to consider when developing your plan:

  1. Set Clear Goals What do you want your content to achieve? Establish measurable goals to help guide your efforts. Examples include:

Increasing brand awareness Boosting engagement on social media Driving traffic to your website Encouraging donations or support

  1. Choose Content Formats Diverse content formats keep your audience engaged. Some effective formats include:

Blog Posts: Share stories, insights, or updates about your organization’s work or the people you serve. Videos: Short video clips can be powerful tools for storytelling. They allow your audience to see your impact visually. Infographics: These visually appealing graphics can present complex information in an easily digestible format. Podcasts: This audio format allows for in-depth discussions about relevant topics.

  1. Create a Content Calendar Planning your content in advance can help ensure a steady stream of fresh, relevant material. Develop a content calendar that outlines:

Topics to cover Formats to use Posting dates Distribution channels

  1. Identify Key Themes Align your content with your organization’s mission and vision. Create content that speaks to the heart of your cause. Some example themes might include:

Impact stories from beneficiaries Volunteer experiences and testimonials Educational material related to your nonprofit’s focus Aligning your messaging with relevant awareness days or campaigns

Crafting Engaging Content Once you have a strategy in place, it’s time to create engaging content. Here are some tips to ensure your material resonates with your audience:

  1. Tell Stories Stories are one of the most powerful ways to connect with your audience emotionally. Share real-life experiences that illustrate your mission. Use testimonials, case studies, and personal anecdotes to bring your cause to life.
  2. Leverage Data and Research Incorporating statistics and research can enhance your credibility and provide essential context. Data-driven insights help illustrate the importance of your cause and show how it affects the community.
  3. Use Compelling Headlines Your headline is often the first impression your audience will have of your content. Craft headlines that are clear, descriptive, and evoke curiosity. Consider using numbers, questions, or power words to make them more enticing.
  4. Write for Readability Online readers tend to skim text rather than read every word. To increase readability:

Use concise sentences and paragraphs. Incorporate bullet points and lists. Use subheadings to break up content. Highlight key points in bold or italics.

  1. Include Calls to Action (CTAs) Always include clear CTAs to guide your audience on the next steps. Whether it’s signing up for a newsletter, volunteering, or donating, CTAs should be prominent and persuasive. Optimizing Content for Shareability To maximize the likelihood that your audience will share your content, you must optimize it for social media and other distribution channels:
  2. Incorporate Visuals Visual content is more likely to be shared than text-only material. Use high-quality images, videos, and infographics relevant to your content. Ensure they are optimized for the platform you’re using.
  3. Create Shareable Formats Consider creating downloadable resources such as eBooks, checklists, or guides. These formats not only provide value to your audience but also encourage sharing.
  4. Utilize Social Media Promote your content across various social media platforms. Tailor your messages for each platform, whether it's Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, or LinkedIn. Engage with your followers by responding to comments and encouraging discussion.
  5. Foster Engagement Encourage your audience to engage with your content by asking questions, prompting discussions, or hosting contests. Engaged audiences are more likely to share content with their networks.
  6. Incorporate Hashtags and Tags Using relevant hashtags can increase your content's visibility, making it easier for new audiences to find your posts. Tagging related organizations or influencers can also widen your reach. Measuring Success To consistently create shareable content, it’s essential to measure its performance. Analyze key metrics to determine what works and what doesn't:
  7. Track Engagement Metrics Monitor likes, shares, comments, and overall reach on social media. These metrics will give you insights into which types of content resonate most with your audience.
  8. Use Website Analytics Utilizing tools such as Google Analytics allows you to track traffic and engagement on your website. Pay attention to metrics like page views, average time spent on each page, and bounce rates to assess content performance.
  9. Adjust and Refine Based on your analyses, refine your content strategy as needed. This might include changing your content formats, adjusting your posting frequency, or experimenting with new themes. Emphasizing Community Involvement Nonprofits thrive on community support and involvement. To create content that resonates, consider ways to involve your community at each stage of the content creation process:
  10. Collaborate with Volunteers and Beneficiaries Involve your volunteers and beneficiaries in the content creation process. Their unique perspectives can offer authenticity and enhance storytelling.
  11. Host Workshops or Events Organizing workshops or events can provide you with fresh content while allowing community members to share their stories. You can create posts or videos from these events, highlighting the community’s involvement.
  12. Engage with Local Influencers Partner with local influencers or community leaders to expand your reach. Their endorsement can lend credibility to your organization and drive engagement with your content. Conclusion Creating shareable content for your nonprofit requires a strategic approach, from understanding your audience to crafting engaging stories. By implementing these strategies, you can increase your content’s visibility, engage supporters, and ultimately enhance your nonprofit's impact. As you develop your content strategy, remember to stay true to your mission, be consistent, and always prioritize storytelling. With the right approach, your nonprofit can create meaningful content that resonates, inspires, and drives change in your community.
