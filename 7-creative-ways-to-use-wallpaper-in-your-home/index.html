7 Creative Ways to Use Wallpaper in Your Home


Wallpaper has made a stunning comeback in interior design, moving beyond its traditional role as a decorative wall covering. Today’s wallpaper options are diverse, ranging from bold prints to textured finishes, and they can transform any space in your home. Here are seven creative ways to utilize wallpaper effectively, enhancing your home’s aesthetic appeal and functionality.

1. Create an Accent Wall

One of the most popular ways to use wallpaper is by creating an accent wall. This technique involves selecting a single wall in a room to cover with a striking wallpaper design that contrasts with the rest of the walls.

Choosing the Right Wall

Typically, the focal point wall is the one behind a significant piece of furniture, such as a bed or a sofa. For instance, in a living room, you might choose the wall where your television is mounted or the one behind a fireplace. In a bedroom, the wall behind the headboard works beautifully.

Design Ideas

Consider using a bold floral print or a geometric pattern to draw attention and add depth to the room. For instance, a navy blue and gold geometric wallpaper can create a sophisticated look in a modern living space. Don’t shy away from vibrant colors or striking patterns; they’re ideal for creating a memorable impression.

2. Wallpaper the Ceiling

While walls are the most common surfaces for wallpaper, the ceiling often goes unnoticed. Using wallpaper on the ceiling can add an unexpected twist to your decor.

Benefits of a Wallpapered Ceiling

A wallpapered ceiling can visually expand a room and create an illusion of height. In smaller spaces, a light-colored wallpaper can make the ceiling feel higher and airier, while darker colors lend a cozy atmosphere.

Inspiring Patterns

Consider using textured wallpaper, such as a subtle linen finish or a metallic pattern, to catch the light and create an interesting visual effect. In a dining room, a delicate, soft-toned pattern can set a charming, intimate mood, enhancing the overall dining experience.

3. DIY Wallpapered Furniture

Transforming furniture with wallpaper is an innovative way to breathe new life into old pieces. This DIY approach allows you to personalize your furniture while giving it a chic makeover.

Steps to Wallpaper Furniture

  1. Choose Your Furniture: Ideal candidates include dressers, bookshelves, or side tables.
  2. Select Your Wallpaper: Choose a design that complements the room’s theme. A bold print can serve as a statement piece.
  3. Prepare the Surface: Clean the furniture thoroughly and remove any old finishes if necessary.
  4. Apply Wallpaper: Cut the wallpaper to size, apply adhesive, and smooth it onto the surface, ensuring there are no bubbles.

Example Projects

Imagine a vintage dresser adorned with a lush tropical print or a bookshelf lined with a classic book-pattern wallpaper. This not only adds personality but also serves as a conversation starter.

4. Use Wallpaper in Unexpected Places

Think outside the box by using wallpaper in unexpected areas of your home. This creative approach can introduce surprising elements that enhance your decor.

Potential Areas

  • Inside Cabinets or Closets: Lining the interior of shelves or closets with a playful wallpaper can make even mundane spaces feel special.
  • Backsplash in the Kitchen: Use waterproof wallpaper as a backsplash for a unique kitchen design. A tile pattern can mimic the elegance of traditional tiles without the hassle of installation.

Creative Applications

Imagine opening a cabinet door to find a cheerful, patterned wallpaper that contrasts with the understated exterior. In a kitchen, a wallpapered backsplash featuring vibrant fruit or floral designs can create a cheerful cooking environment.

5. Bordering with Wallpaper

Instead of covering an entire wall, consider using wallpaper as a border. This technique adds a decorative touch without overwhelming the space.

Implementation Tips

  • Height Matters: Place the border at eye level or higher to create visual interest and define spaces.
  • Complement Existing Decor: Choose a wallpaper that complements your existing color palette or theme.

Ideas for Borders

In a child’s room, a playful border featuring animals or characters can add a fun element without dominating the space. In a hallway, a sophisticated border with a subtle damask pattern can elevate the area, giving it a refined touch.

6. Wallpapered Staircase

Staircases are often an overlooked area in home design, but they provide a unique opportunity to showcase creativity. Wallpapering your staircase can turn this functional space into a standout feature.

Wallpapering Techniques

For stair risers, consider applying wallpaper to each step for a striking effect. Alternatively, use a continuous design that flows from the wall down to the staircase.

Style Suggestions

Opt for a bold, graphic pattern for a modern look or a soft floral design for a more traditional feel. A striking black and white pattern can add a contemporary edge, while a delicate pastel pattern can create a softer, ethereal ambiance.

7. Wallpapered Panels or Screens

If you’re looking to divide spaces within your home without constructing walls, consider using wallpapered panels or screens.

Functional and Decorative

These screens can serve as room dividers, providing privacy while adding style. You can easily move them when needed, making them a flexible design solution.

Creative Combinations

Select vibrant wallpaper for your screen that matches or complements your decor. For instance, a bold botanical print can bring the beauty of the outdoors inside, creating a lively atmosphere in your living area.

Conclusion

Wallpaper is a versatile and creative tool that can enhance your home’s design in numerous ways. Whether it’s creating an accent wall, transforming furniture, or using it in unexpected places, the possibilities are endless. With the right design and application, wallpaper can reflect your style, making your home uniquely yours. Experiment with these ideas and let your creativity flow as you explore the transformative potential of wallpaper in your living space.
