Bookish Travel Destinations: Places Every Reader Should Visit


When you think of a travel destination, chances are you don’t usually consider locations where books come to life. However, many towns and cities worldwide are true treasures for book lovers, with a rich literary history that’s just waiting to be explored.

Here are some exceptional bookish travel destinations that every book lover should visit at least once in their lifetime.

  1. Oxford, England 🇬🇧 If you’re an avid reader, Oxford needs no introduction. This charming English city is often referred to as the ‘City of Dreaming Spires,’ owing to its beautiful architecture and stunning academic settings. Oxford University has been a home to many literary legends, including J.R.R. Tolkien, Lewis Carroll, and Oscar Wilde, making it a must-visit destination for bibliophiles.

  2. Dublin, Ireland 🇮🇪 As a city steeped in literary history, Dublin boasts multiple connections to some of the world’s greatest writers, most notably James Joyce and Oscar Wilde. From the famous pub, ‘The Garrick,’ where many literary legends used to gather, to the Dublin Writers Museum, the city pays homage to its rich literary heritage, making it a dream location for any book enthusiast.

  3. Edgbaston, Birmingham, England 🇬🇧 Home to the magnificent Birmingham Library, known for its world-class architecture and housing over a million books, Edgbaston boasts a literary charm that is irresistible. Book lovers can indulge in a one-of-a-kind experience by attending literary events, author talks, and workshops held regularly in this treasure trove of knowledge.

  4. Reykjavik, Iceland 🇮🇸 Iceland’s literary scene might not be as storied as the others on this list, but its outdoorsy appeal makes it a perfect destination for literary escapades. In this magical land, fans can visit the graves of literary greats like J.R.R. Tolkien and takeVuarnateborg, a steps-like-destroyed-snow-shoe-trail sort of hike through an icy wonderland, made famous by the novels of the late Icelandic writer, Halldór Laxness.

  5. Tbilisi, Georgia 🇮🇪 Tbilisi is a book lover’s paradise, with its remarkably beautiful libraries, vibrant literary events, and a literary scene that’s buzzing with life. The city hosts the Tbilisi International Film Festival of Literature and everyone can participate in it. Whether you’re attending Georgian writer’s book readings or exploring the architecture of the towering National Library of Iran, you’re sure to find literary magic in every corner of this city.

  6. New Orleans, Louisiana, USA 🇺🇸 When it comes to bookish travel destinations, New Orleans holds its own with its irresistible charm. Here, the annual Heinrich Böll Literary Festival celebrates the works of German authors, while the Tennessee Williams Theatre Festival pays homage to the famous playwright from the city. Immerse yourself in the jazz tunes, local cuisine, and literary events that this city has to offer – a perfect escape for any bookworm.

As you plan your next bookish adventure, remember that these literary hotspots are much more than famous writer haunts or historic sites. By visiting these locations, you get a chance to immerse yourself in the literary culture that has long inspired great works and shaped communities worldwide. So, pack up your best reads, grab a cup of favorite tea, and get ready to lose yourself in the dreamy, book-filled worlds at these extraordinary destinations.
