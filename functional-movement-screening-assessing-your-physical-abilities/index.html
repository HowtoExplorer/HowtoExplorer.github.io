Functional Movement Screening: Assessing Your Physical Abilities


Functional Movement Screening (FMS) is a comprehensive assessment tool used by fitness professionals and healthcare practitioners to evaluate an individual’s movement patterns, identify imbalances, and assess physical abilities. This screening method provides valuable insights into an individual’s functional movement patterns, helping to prevent injuries, improve performance, and enhance overall physical well-being.

Why Functional Movement Screening Matters

In today’s fast-paced world, it’s easy to neglect our bodies and overlook the importance of proper movement mechanics. Whether you’re an athlete, a fitness enthusiast, or simply someone who wants to live a healthy and active lifestyle, functional movement is the foundation of optimal physical performance. By assessing your movement patterns through FMS, you can address any existing imbalances or limitations and work towards achieving better movement quality.

How Functional Movement Screening Works

Functional Movement Screening involves a series of simple yet specific movement tests that evaluate an individual’s mobility, stability, and motor control. These tests are designed to identify any limitations, weaknesses, or asymmetries that may hinder optimal movement patterns. The screening process typically includes the following components:

  1. Deep Squat: This test assesses lower body mobility, core stability, and overall movement coordination. It evaluates the ability to perform a full-depth squat while maintaining proper posture and alignment.

  2. Hurdle Step: The hurdle step test focuses on assessing hip mobility, core stability, and balance. It measures the ability to step over a hurdle while maintaining stability and control throughout the movement.

  3. Inline Lunge: This test evaluates hip mobility, lower body stability, and overall movement control. It involves performing a lunge with the front foot aligned with a tape line, assessing the ability to maintain proper alignment and control during the movement.

  4. Shoulder Mobility: The shoulder mobility test assesses upper body mobility, specifically the ability to raise the arms overhead while maintaining proper alignment and stability.

  5. Active Straight Leg Raise: This test evaluates hamstring and hip mobility, as well as core stability. It involves lifting one leg while keeping the other leg straight on the ground, assessing the ability to perform the movement with control and stability.

  6. Trunk Stability Push-Up: The trunk stability push-up test focuses on assessing core stability, upper body strength, and movement control. It involves performing a push-up while maintaining a stable and aligned trunk.

  7. Rotary Stability: This test assesses overall core stability, proprioception, and movement control. It involves performing a series of movements that challenge the individual’s ability to control their core while moving their extremities.

By performing these tests, trained professionals can identify any movement dysfunctions, imbalances, or limitations that may increase the risk of injury or hinder optimal performance.

The Benefits of Functional Movement Screening

Functional Movement Screening offers numerous benefits for individuals of all fitness levels and backgrounds:

  1. Injury Prevention: By identifying movement imbalances or limitations, FMS helps to address potential injury risks before they become major issues. By correcting these imbalances and working on movement patterns, individuals can reduce the likelihood of injuries during physical activities.

  2. Performance Enhancement: Functional Movement Screening provides a baseline assessment of an individual’s movement quality. By addressing any limitations or imbalances, individuals can optimize their movement patterns, leading to improved performance in sports, fitness, and everyday activities.

  3. Individualized Training Programs: FMS allows fitness professionals and healthcare practitioners to design personalized training programs tailored to an individual’s specific needs. By addressing the identified movement dysfunctions, these programs can help individuals achieve their fitness goals more effectively.

  4. Improved Quality of Life: By optimizing movement patterns and addressing imbalances, Functional Movement Screening helps individuals move more efficiently and effectively in their daily lives, reducing the risk of chronic pain, discomfort, and physical limitations.

Conclusion

Functional Movement Screening is a valuable tool for assessing an individual’s movement patterns, identifying imbalances, and optimizing physical abilities. By undergoing FMS, individuals can address any existing limitations or weaknesses, reduce the risk of injuries, improve performance, and enhance their overall quality of life. Whether you’re an athlete, a fitness enthusiast, or someone who simply wants to move better and live a healthier life, investing in Functional Movement Screening can be a game-changer on your journey towards optimal physical well-being.
