DIY Bathroom Storage Solutions: Maximize Your Space


Bathrooms are one of the most essential yet often neglected spaces in any home. They’re meant to be functional, efficient, and serene—yet they can easily become overcrowded with toiletries, towels, cleaning supplies, and other essentials. As a result, finding storage solutions that work effectively can be a challenge, especially in smaller bathrooms where space is limited. Fortunately, there are many creative and practical DIY storage solutions that can transform your bathroom into a more organized and visually appealing space.

In this guide, we’ll explore a variety of DIY bathroom storage ideas to help you maximize your space, declutter your bathroom, and make it a more organized and functional area.

1. Install Floating Shelves

Floating shelves are an excellent way to add vertical storage to your bathroom without taking up valuable floor space. These sleek, minimalistic shelves can be installed above the toilet, sink, or bathtub, providing a perfect place to store extra towels, toiletries, and decorative items. The best part is that floating shelves can be customized to suit your bathroom’s aesthetic, whether you prefer modern, rustic, or industrial designs.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Wood or metal brackets, screws, a level, drill, and measuring tape.
  • Instructions: First, measure the area where you want to install the shelves. Mark the wall for the placement of the brackets. Using a level, drill holes into the wall for the brackets and secure them in place. Then, simply place the shelf on top of the brackets and secure it with screws.

2. Create Storage with a Ladder Shelf

A ladder shelf is a unique and stylish way to add storage space to your bathroom. This design allows you to take advantage of vertical space while maintaining an open, airy feel. The rungs of the ladder can be used to store towels, baskets, plants, and even small decorative objects.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Wood, screws, measuring tape, drill, and a saw (if necessary).
  • Instructions: Cut your wood into the desired lengths for the ladder’s sides and rungs. Assemble the ladder frame by attaching the rungs to the sides using screws. After the ladder is built, lean it against a wall in your bathroom. You can place towels, decorative baskets, or small accessories on the rungs.

3. Use Mason Jars for Small Toiletries

Mason jars can be a versatile storage solution for organizing small toiletries such as cotton balls, swabs, hairpins, and other bathroom essentials. Not only do they keep these items neat and tidy, but they also add a rustic charm to your bathroom decor.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Mason jars, adhesive strips, small hooks (optional).
  • Instructions: Clean the mason jars and remove any labels. You can either place the jars on a countertop or mount them on the wall using adhesive strips or small hooks. Fill the jars with your bathroom essentials and secure them to the wall or place them in a convenient location.

4. Add Pull-Out Drawer Organizers

For those who need extra storage for cosmetics, toiletries, and small items, pull-out drawer organizers are a great way to keep everything neat and accessible. This simple DIY project can be installed under your bathroom sink or within existing cabinets to optimize your bathroom’s storage capacity.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Wood or plastic drawer organizers, screws, drill.
  • Instructions: Measure the space where you want to install the pull-out drawers and cut the organizer to fit. Install a set of tracks along the bottom of the drawer and the cabinet so that it can be pulled in and out smoothly. Secure the drawer in place, and organize your toiletries and supplies.

5. Utilize Over-the-Door Organizers

Over-the-door organizers are an excellent way to maximize space, especially in small bathrooms. These organizers come in various styles, from simple hooks for towels and robes to multi-pocket organizers for toiletries and cleaning supplies. They make use of the often-overlooked space on the back of your bathroom door.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Over-the-door organizer (or materials to make one), hooks, and brackets.
  • Instructions: Purchase a ready-made over-the-door organizer or create one by attaching hooks and pockets to a wooden or fabric panel. Hang it on the bathroom door and use it to store towels, bathrobes, toiletries, or cleaning supplies. The best part is that it doesn’t require any drilling and can be easily removed or rearranged as needed.

6. Install a Tension Rod for Extra Hanging Space

A tension rod is an affordable and flexible storage solution that works wonders in small bathrooms. This simple yet effective tool can be used to hang shower essentials like towels, washcloths, or even extra rolls of toilet paper. A tension rod can also be installed inside a cabinet to hang cleaning supplies, hair tools, or small items.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Tension rod, hooks (optional).
  • Instructions: Simply place the tension rod where you need extra hanging space. For example, you can install it inside a cabinet or behind the shower curtain. Hang towels, cleaning supplies, or other essentials on the rod, and enjoy the added storage.

7. Create Under-Sink Storage Solutions

The space under your sink is often overlooked, but it can be an excellent place to store cleaning supplies, toiletries, and other bathroom essentials. By utilizing baskets, bins, and shelves, you can make the most of this hidden storage space.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Baskets, storage bins, small shelves, drawer organizers.
  • Instructions: Measure the space under your sink and select storage solutions that will fit. Install a small shelf above the pipes to create additional storage, and use baskets or bins to organize your items. You can also use pull-out drawer organizers or tension rods to create even more storage options.

8. Add Hooks for Towels and Robes

Adding hooks to your bathroom walls or behind the door is an effective way to store towels, robes, and other accessories. Hooks are a simple solution that helps reduce clutter while keeping essential items within reach.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Hooks, screws, drill, measuring tape.
  • Instructions: Measure the height at which you want to install the hooks. Install the hooks along the bathroom wall, making sure they are spaced evenly. For added functionality, consider installing multiple hooks for different items like hand towels, bath towels, or robes.

9. Repurpose an Old Wooden Crate

Repurposing an old wooden crate is a fantastic way to create unique and functional bathroom storage. Whether you stack them to form a shelf or mount them on the wall, crates can hold towels, toiletries, and other essentials while adding a rustic, vintage touch to your bathroom decor.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Wooden crate, screws, paint (optional), and a drill.
  • Instructions: Sand down the crate to smooth out any rough edges. You can paint it in a color that matches your bathroom decor. Then, mount the crate on the wall or stack multiple crates for an attractive shelving unit. Use the crate to store rolled towels, hair products, or even small baskets of toiletries.

10. Install Corner Shelves

If you have limited space in your bathroom, installing corner shelves is a great way to make use of every nook and cranny. Corner shelves are perfect for storing items that you use every day, such as soaps, lotions, and shampoos.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Corner shelves, screws, drill.
  • Instructions: Measure the corner where you want to install the shelves. Mark the wall for where the brackets will go, and drill holes to secure the shelves. Arrange your toiletries and other essentials on the corner shelves to keep them organized and within easy reach.

11. Use a Vanity Tray for Countertop Organization

Vanity trays are a simple yet effective way to keep your bathroom countertop organized. These trays can hold perfumes, cosmetics, skincare products, or other small items that would otherwise clutter the counter.

How to Do It:

  • Materials Needed: Tray (wooden, metal, or ceramic), organizational items (such as small containers or jars).
  • Instructions: Select a tray that suits your bathroom’s style. Place the tray on your bathroom countertop and arrange your toiletries, cosmetics, and perfumes on it. You can also use small containers or jars within the tray to further organize smaller items like cotton balls and hair accessories.

Conclusion

Maximizing bathroom space can be a fun and rewarding DIY project. By incorporating some of these simple yet creative storage solutions into your bathroom, you can create a more organized, functional, and aesthetically pleasing space. From floating shelves to repurposed crates, there’s a wide range of options to suit different styles and needs. Whether you have a small apartment bathroom or a larger space, these DIY storage ideas will help you make the most of what you have while keeping your bathroom clutter-free.

Remember, the key to a well-organized bathroom is not just adding storage, but also ensuring that everything has a place. With the right storage solutions, your bathroom can become a serene and efficient space that works for you and your family’s daily needs.
