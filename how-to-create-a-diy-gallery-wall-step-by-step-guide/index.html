How to Create a DIY Gallery Wall: Step-by-Step Guide


A gallery wall is a timeless design element that adds character, warmth, and a personal touch to any space. Whether you’re showcasing cherished family photos, your favorite art prints, or an eclectic mix, creating a DIY gallery wall is the perfect way to transform a blank wall into an inspiring focal point. If you’re unsure of where to start—or worried about achieving a cohesive and polished look—this step-by-step guide will walk you through every detail of the process.

Step 1: Plan Your Vision

Before you start hammering nails into your walls, take time to envision the gallery wall you want to create. Think about the overall mood or style of the room and how your gallery wall can complement it. Do you prefer a minimalist and uniform arrangement, or are you drawn to an eclectic and asymmetrical layout? Identifying your preferences will guide every subsequent decision.

Key Considerations:

  • Theme or Story: Decide what story or theme your gallery wall will tell. It could be a celebration of family memories, travel adventures, abstract art, or a mix of everything that inspires you.
  • Color Palette: Look at the colors already present in the room. Incorporating frames and artwork that tie into these tones will ensure a cohesive look.
  • Wall Size: The size of your wall plays a significant role in design. For smaller walls, stick to fewer pieces or smaller frames. For larger walls, you can go big with multiple pieces and dramatic layouts.

Step 2: Choose Your Artwork and Frames

Curating the right mix of artwork, photographs, and decorative elements is the heart of a successful gallery wall. Take time to gather the pieces you want to display, keeping both aesthetics and personal significance in mind.

Artwork Selection:

  • Mix of Mediums: Combine photographs, paintings, drawings, and prints to add depth and interest.
  • Balance of Sizes: Aim for a mix of large and small pieces to create visual variety and balance.
  • Personal Touches: Highlights such as family heirlooms, handwritten quotes, or personalized prints make the gallery wall uniquely yours.

Frame Choices:

  • Consistent Frames: Matching frames create a uniform and modern look.
  • Eclectic Frames: Mixing frame styles (wood, metallic, or painted) works well in a bohemian or rustic space.
  • Matting and Borders: Adding mat boards to some frames can create separation and enhance the art inside.

Step 3: Decide on a Layout

Before putting anything on the wall, map out your ideal arrangement. Experimenting with your layout in advance ensures you avoid unnecessary holes and achieve a pattern you love.

Methods for Planning:

  1. Floor Layout: Lay all your frames on the floor in front of the wall. Rearrange them until you find a composition that feels balanced and cohesive.
  2. Paper Templates: Use kraft paper or newspaper to cut out shapes that match the size of each frame. Tape them on the wall to visualize spacing and alignment.
  3. Digital Tools: Some apps or software allow you to design your gallery wall virtually.

Popular Layout Options:

  • Grid Layout: Arranging artwork in straight lines or grids works well in modern or formal spaces.
  • Asymmetrical Layout: A looser arrangement feels more organic and works for casual or eclectic designs.
  • Anchor Piece Layout: Start with a large central piece and arrange smaller pieces around it for a balanced focal point.

Step 4: Gather Your Tools

Once you’ve finalized your layout, you’re ready to hang the pieces. Having the right tools at hand will make the process smoother and prevent any last-minute frustration.

Tools You’ll Need:

  • Tape measure
  • Level
  • Hammer and nails (or screws and anchors for heavier pieces)
  • Command strips (for lightweight frames or renters)
  • Pencil
  • Painter’s tape

Step 5: Mark and Hang Your Frames

Precise measuring and careful hanging are essential to achieving a professional-looking gallery wall. Follow these steps to bring your design to life:

  1. Mark Placement: Using your chosen layout as a guide, mark the position of each frame on the wall with a pencil or painter’s tape.
  2. Start With the Biggest Piece: Begin with the largest or most significant piece (often the focal point) to anchor the arrangement. Build outwards with smaller pieces.
  3. Measure and Level: Use a tape measure to ensure even spacing between frames. As you hang each piece, use a level to ensure it’s straight.
  4. Double Check Heights: Keep the center of your gallery wall approximately 57-60 inches from the floor for optimal viewing height. Adjust if your furniture or space requires something different.
  5. Secure Lightweight Items: Command strips or adhesive hooks can be great for lightweight decor pieces, avoiding damage to walls.

Step 6: Add Final Touches

Once all your frames are in place, step back and assess the overall look. Adjust pieces if necessary to ensure balance and harmony.

Tips for Finishing Touches:

  • Fill Gaps: If certain areas feel empty, consider adding small decorative items like wall-mounted vases, mini shelves, or small sculptures.
  • Lighting: Adding focused lighting, like sconces or picture lights, can elevate the gallery wall and highlight your artwork.
  • Refine Arrangement: Step back and study the arrangement from different angles to ensure it aligns with your vision.

Step 7: Maintain and Update Your Gallery Wall

The beauty of a gallery wall is its flexibility. As time goes on, update your wall with new artwork, photos, or decor elements to keep it fresh and reflective of your evolving style.

Maintenance Tips:

  • Regular Cleaning: Dust frames and artwork periodically to keep them looking their best.
  • Seasonal Updates: Swap out a few pieces for seasonal themes or events, like holiday decor or travel finds.
  • Rotate Art: Refresh the wall by rotating artwork, framing new pieces, or experimenting with additional layouts.

Final Thoughts

Creating a DIY gallery wall is a deeply rewarding project. It’s not only an opportunity to personalize your space, but it also allows you to share stories, memories, and inspiration through thoughtful curation. Whether you’re a minimalist at heart or adore maximalist design, the key to success lies in careful planning, a clear vision, and a willingness to try different arrangements until you find what feels just right.

So roll up your sleeves, gather your favorite pieces, and transform your blank wall into one that reflects your personality and creativity. With these steps, your DIY gallery wall is sure to be a stunning success.
