The Superlative Seven: The Best Japanese Light Novels Translated


Japanese light novels have captivated the hearts of avid readers, transcending borders and languages to make their mark in the literary world. With intricately woven plots, dynamic characters, and vibrant worlds, these novels exhibit a unique storytelling craft. In this blog post, we delve into the superlative seven – the best Japanese light novels translated into English that you absolutely must-read.

  1. Re:Zero − Starting Life in Another World

The first novel that’s a must-read on our list is Re:Zero − Starting Life in Another World. It’s no secret that this series has garnered massive popularity in Japan, and it’s easy to see why. The protagonist, Natsuki Subaru, finds himself suddenly transported to a fantasy world after being on an apocalyptic mission with his friends. With each death, Subaru finds himself possessing the ability to rewind time, giving him another chance to alter the course of events. With a mixture of dark humor, intense action, and emotional depth, this novel pulls readers into an adventurous roller coaster ride beyond the realm of life and death.

  1. The Devil is a Part-Timer!

In a world where devil king Satan and his main lieutenant, Alsiel, are thrown into an unexpected twist of fate, they find themselves powerless in modern-day Tokyo, forced to work part-time at a fast food restaurant. As they navigate their lives in a human world, they come across adverts for cheap apartments which unknowingly hold more surprises for them and other demonic beings. With lighthearted humor and endearing characters, this novel offers a comedic take on Japanese folklore with relatable struggles in a modern setting that resonates with readers on a personal level.

  1. KonoSuba: God’s Blessing on this Wonderful World!

Another must-read in the world of Japanese light novels is KonoSuba: God’s Blessing on this Wonderful World! The story revolves around Kazuma Satō, a hikikomori whose life takes an unexpected turn when he dies and is given the opportunity to be reborn in a fantasy world. To his disappointment, the deities only provide him with the weakest female character for a companion, much to Kazuma’s dismay. Together with Aqua, the clumsy Goddess of Water, as well as Megumin, Knockout, and Darkness, their chaotic adventures will have you laughing and questioning the very fabric of what a fantasy-world hero should be like.

  1. Overlord

Overlord is a thrilling novel that makes it to our list for its groundbreaking portrayal of a popular MMORPG game that becomes a virtual reality world. After the game’s administrator, Momonga, finds himself trapped in the game world even after the closure shock, armed with his dealership guild, the Greater Goods of Ainz Ooalms, this tale explores the ruthless power struggles and formidable politics in a world with the unexpected consequence of becoming suspended in reality.

  1. The Saga of Tanya the Evil

TheSagaofTanyatheEvil is a darkly humorous novel that will surely capture your attention. The story follows the life of a Japanese salaryman, reborn as Tanya Degurechaff in a world of magic and warfare during World War I. Faced with harsh realities in this new life, Tanya decides to climb the ranks in the military through cunning tactics and ruthlessness. Full of political intrigue and dark humor, this novel proves that self-preservation is the first law of nature.

  1. Arifureta: From Commonplace to World’s Strongest

Arifureta: From Commonplace to World’s Strongest is a heartwarming tale of getting stronger and finding one’s destiny amidst adversity. When a party of eight Western-style wizards get transported to another world, the protagonist, Hajime Nakajima, faces a harrowing disaster that only leaves him and hisclassmate Kaori Miyazon far beyond hope. With the odds stacked against them, they must struggle to survive in a harsh, unforgiving world. It’s a testament to how setbacks can mold individuals into overcoming their limitations and pursuing their dreams.

  1. Mahouka Koukou no Rettousei (The Irregular at Magic High School)

Mahouka Koukou no Rettousei, the Irregular at Magic High School, is set in a world where magic exists alongside technology and science. How exactly does one distinguish between magic and science? The story follows Tōtoku Miyaki, a student at the First High, an academy for individuals who possess magic abilities. As an irregular, he lacks any remarkable power and seeks to excel in a world governed by both magic and science. The novel uncovers a unique blend of academia, politics, and action, painting a picture of the delicate balance between talent and self-improvement in a world of seemingly inherent abilities.

These seven novels showcase the incredible talent and potential of Japanese light novel authors, transporting readers to a world filled with adventure, excitement, and stories that will remain etched in your memory. Each title, while distinct in its own way, possesses the power to weave its magic into the hearts and minds of its readers, cementing its place as one of the best Japanese light novels translated. Whether you’re a fan of fantasy, science fiction, or a mix of both genres, these novels are certain to captivate your imagination and leave you yearning for more.
