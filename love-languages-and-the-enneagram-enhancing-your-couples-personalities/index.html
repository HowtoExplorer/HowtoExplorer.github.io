Love Languages and the Enneagram: Enhancing Your Couple's Personalities


The bonds between people are strengthened through effective communication. For couples, understanding each other’s needs, desires, and personalities can significantly enhance communication and bonding. In this blog post, we will explore the fascinating phenomena of love languages and the enneagram, as well as how they can be combined to strengthen your couple’s personalities.

Understanding Love Languages

Dr. Gary Chapman’s love languages theory has been influencing relationships worldwide for over three decades. According to Chapman, there are five love languages, which are:

  1. Words of Affirmation: Individuals who speak this love language thrive on receiving words of praise, appreciation, and compliments.

  2. Physical Touch: For those whose love language is physical touch, gentleness, hugs, hand-holding, and other forms of physical affection work wonders.

  3. Acts of Service: These individuals value actions that demonstrate love and care, such as doing chores, surprising with gifts, and helping with daily tasks.

  4. Quality Time: Spending undivided attention and time with loved ones holds immense importance for individuals with this love language.

  5. Receiving Gifts: Giving and receiving gifts hold a special significance for individuals whose love language is receiving gifts.

Understanding and acknowledging your partner’s love language can significantly enhance emotional bonding, trust, and an atmosphere of understanding in the relationship.

Introducing the Enneagram

The enneagram is an ancient personality typing system that originated around 4,000 years ago. It categorizes people into nine distinct personality types, each with unique characteristics, motivations, and fears. Understanding your enneagram type and your partner’s type can provide a framework for a mutual understanding of each other’s personalities and needs.

The nine enneagram types are:

  1. The Perfectionist (Type One)
  2. The Helper (Type Two)
  3. The Achiever (Type Three)
  4. The Idealist (Type Four)
  5. The Loyalist (Type Five)
  6. The Adventurer (Type Six)
  7. The Individualist (Type Seven)
  8. The Investigator (Type Eight)
  9. The Magician (Type Nine)

Combining Love Languages and the Enneagram

By incorporating both love languages and the enneagram, couples can gain a deeper understanding of their individual and collective personalities. This understanding can lead to enhanced mutual support, trust, and emotional intimacy.

Example scenarios:

  • Type Three (The Achiever) speaks Acts of Service as their love language. To fulfill their partner’s needs, they may want to perform chores or surprise their partner with meaningful actions.

  • Type Five (The Loyalist) might express their love through Quality Time. Their partner can plan special events or give them undivided attention to strengthen their bond.

  • Type Eight (The Investigator) may appreciate Words of Affirmation. Their partner can verbalize their appreciation and love to build a more profound connection.

Incorporating love languages and the enneagram can be a fascinating journey. By combining these two frameworks, you can work toward a more profound understanding of yourself and your partner, leading to a healthier, more fulfilling, and enriching relationship. So, take the first step today to embark on this enlightening journey and strengthen your bond with your beloved.
