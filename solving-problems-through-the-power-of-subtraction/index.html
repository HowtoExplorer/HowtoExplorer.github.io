Solving Problems Through the Power of Subtraction


Often, we find ourselves entangled in a labyrinth of problems—personal, professional, or societal. Ranging from failed relationships to time management concerns, these issues can appear overwhelming and complicated. In our quest to seek solutions, we frequently overlook a basic yet profoundly effective strategy: subtraction.

By focusing on the power of subtraction, we can simplify our lives, enhance our well-being, and pave the way for a more fulfilling existence. This blog post will explore various aspects of life where this technique can be applied, empowering you to navigate through challenges with newfound confidence and clarity.

An Italian Enigma: Leonardo da Vinci and the Value of Exclusion

When thinking about master problem solvers, Leonardo da Vinci undoubtedly stands out. Known for his groundbreaking discoveries and innovative designs, the Renaissance genius built his intellectual capital on subtraction. Stories recount how da Vinci was once challenged by an Italian king to solve a complex maze. Rather than focusing on complex calculations or brute force, Leonardo opted to create a “hole” by strategically removing essential areas of the maze. This ultimately led to a direct path to the center.

Drawing Lessons: The Art of Reducing Complexity

As living artists, we are familiar with the struggle of balancing the tension between underpainting and final layers. In essence, Art is the process of carefully adding and subtracting elements to produce a harmonious composition. The same philosophy holds true in solving everyday problems.

For instance, consider decluttering your workspace. By removing irrelevant and obsolete items, like old papers or unused kitchen utensils, you create mental space and room for focused productivity. Similarly, choose a simpler wardrobe to minimize daily decision fatigue. Breathe life into your mornings by deciding between a limited number of outfits, rather than being overwhelmed with choice.

The Minimalist’s Guide to Harmony: Focus on What Matters

Minimalism has become synonymous with simplicity and clarity. Embracing this lifestyle can lead to more meaningful living, shifting your focus to that which truly matters. Notable minimalist Joshua Fields Millburn encapsulates this philosophy beautifully:

“Clutter is clutter, no matter where it’s hiding. It’s a prison—a prison for your soul. To liberate your soul, you must clear your clutter first.”

In this light, subtraction manifests itself within all aspects of life, whether you’re organizing a schedules or reevaluating your relationships. Life blossoms in a more fulfilling light as you remove that which hinders growth.

The primal energy of subtraction: Expanding through withdrawal

In Western culture, we tend to have a fixation with growth and increase. However, many ancient and traditional philosophies recognize the power of retreat and withdrawal. Renowned martial artist Bruce Lee penned a key concept in his book “Tao of Jeet Kune Do:”

“Be empty to be full.”

Here, emptiness represents releasing preconceived notions, held beliefs, and other external constraints to create space for authentic growth. By embracing the power of subtraction, we liberate ourselves from limitations and embark on a journey focused on self-realization.

The Subtraction How-To: Practical Steps to Simplifying Life

To cultivate the art of leveraging subtraction in problem-solving, follow these 7 steps:

  1. Identify matters that are unessential to your goals and values.
  2. Detach emotional ties from these unessential factors for impartial decision-making.
  3. Strategically eliminate or limit those elements from your life.
  4. Monitor the change in your energy levels and overall satisfaction.
  5. Incorporate new habits or routines that empower you to maintain clarity and focus.
  6. Practice gratitude for inner growth and outer achievements by acknowledging what was gained in the process.
  7. Be receptive to future adjustments as you continuously strive to evolve.

By tapping into the power of subtraction and applying it to various aspects of life, we can outweigh the scales in our favor. This transformative approach unveils a newfound clarity and simplicity, ultimately enabling you to navigate through life with grace, purpose, and vitality.

In summary, subtraction is an often underestimated yet formidable tool for solving life’s complexities. By recognizing the essentiality of removing elements that do not serve us, we empower ourselves to craft meaningful and vibrant lives. So the next time you encounter a problem, remember da Vinci’s wisdom: real progress can often be found in the gaps between the lines.
