The Best Plays to Perform in High School Drama Club


As a high school drama club, selecting the right play to perform can make all the difference. It can be a daunting task, especially with so many incredible options to choose from. In this post, we'll explore the best plays to consider for your high school drama club, taking into account factors such as budget, casting, and themes that resonate with teenage audiences. The Classics: Tried and True Options No list of plays for high school drama clubs would be complete without mentioning some of the most iconic and enduring classics. These works have stood the test of time, offering timeless themes and memorable performances that never fail to captivate audiences.

Grease: A musical romp through the 1950s that explores the themes of identity, friendship, and love. This high-energy production is perfect for clubs with a large cast and plenty of resources. Romeo and Juliet: Shakespeare's tragic romance has been a favorite of high school drama clubs for centuries. Its universal themes of love, loyalty, and conflict continue to resonate with young audiences today. The Crucible: Arthur Miller's gripping drama explores the dangers of hysteria, mass hysteria, and the consequences of unchecked power.

Newer Works: Fresh Perspectives and Themes While classic plays offer timeless appeal, newer works can provide fresh perspectives and timely themes that resonate with modern audiences. Here are some newer plays that are perfect for high school drama clubs:

The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee: A hilarious musical about a group of awkward adolescents competing in a local spelling bee. This show is a great choice for clubs with a smaller budget, as it requires minimal sets and costumes. ** Really Really Good Finally Repeats Its Best Hits**: A dark comedy about a group of aimless teenagers trying to find their place in the world. This show explores themes of identity, friendship, and the struggles of growing up. She Kills Monsters: A fantasy horror show about a group of teenagers using a role-playing game to cope with grief. This show is a great choice for clubs looking to tackle more mature themes.

Diverse Voices: Plays that Celebrate Difference and Inclusion High school drama clubs can do more than just perform plays – they can also promote diversity, equity, and inclusion. Here are some plays that celebrate difference and offer fresh perspectives:

The Color Purple: A powerful musical about a young African American woman's struggles with poverty, racism, and abuse. This show is a great choice for clubs looking to explore themes of social justice and activism. Eurydice: A poignant play about a young woman's journey through love, loss, and transformation. This show explores themes of identity, community, and the power of art. Love in a Hold: A modern adaptation of Shakespeare's As You Like It, set in a contemporary American prison. This show explores themes of identity, community, and the power of art.

Final Tips and Recommendations When selecting a play for your high school drama club, consider the following factors:

Casting challenges: Choose a play with diverse casting opportunities to ensure all students have a chance to participate. Budget constraints: Select a play with minimal sets and costumes to save on production costs. Themes and social responsibility: Opt for plays that promote diversity, equity, and inclusion, and tackle timely themes and social issues.

Ultimately, the best play for your high school drama club is one that resonates with your students and audience, while also aligning with your club's goals and resources. In conclusion, high school drama clubs have a wealth of options when it comes to selecting a play to perform. By considering classic plays, newer works, diverse voices, and your club's specific needs and goals, you can find the perfect production for your students.
