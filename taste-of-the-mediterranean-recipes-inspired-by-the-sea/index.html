Moroccan Mousalla Syndrome

Morocco, a meeting point of Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and European flavors, is known for its eclectic cuisine. One dish that truly captures its essence is the famous tagine. A tagine is a Moroccan stew that normally includes meat, fruit, and spices. We suggest trying this exquisite dish with a breath-taking combination of of lamb, apricots, and almonds.

Here’s a simple recipe for your next dinner party:

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. boneless lamb shoulder, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • 1 cup pitted green apricots
  • 1 cup blanched almonds
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz.) crushed tomatoes
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a large culinary pot or tagine. Add onions and garlic, sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add the lamb, cooking until evenly browned.
  3. Mix in the tomatoes, apricots, almonds, paprika, ginger, cinnamon, and black pepper.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth and season with salt.
  5. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low, and let it cook slowly for 2 hours or until the meat is tender.
  6. Remove from heat, let stand for an hour before serving.
  7. Accompany with a glass of rich, dark Moroccan red wine, and enjoy!

Greek Tragedies and Triumphs

Greece, land of theater and mythology, is also home to some truly outstanding seafood dishes. One such dazzler is the bougatsa. This delicious pastry Roll is filled with a delicate custard and pieces of unsurpassed quality fish.

To bring a little piece of Greece to your next gathering, follow this recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 package (17.3 oz.) frozen phyllo cups
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 cups milk
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 cups flaky white fish, such as cod or halibut
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Thaw the phyllo cups according to package instructions.
  3. Mix the egg yolks, sugar, and milk in a medium saucepan. Cook over medium heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens and coats the back of a spoon (about 10 minutes).
  4. Stir in the lemon zest and cinnamon, then remove from heat.
  5. Gently fold the fish into the custard.
  6. Brush the inside of each phyllo cup with melted butter. Place on a baking sheet.
  7. Distribute the custard among the phyllo cups, around 1-2 tablespoons per cup.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the cups are golden brown.
  9. Remove from the oven and allow to cool slightly.
  10. Dust lightly with cinnamon and enjoy!
