How to Create a Family Mission Statement: Defining Your Values


In the fast-paced world we live in, it’s easy for families to become disconnected or feel like they are simply going through the motions of daily life. With everyone juggling busy schedules, commitments, and responsibilities, the importance of unity and purpose can sometimes be lost. One powerful way to ensure that your family stays aligned with your values and goals is by creating a family mission statement. A family mission statement serves as a guiding light that helps family members stay grounded, work toward shared aspirations, and build strong, positive relationships.

But how do you craft a meaningful family mission statement that truly reflects your family’s values, beliefs, and goals? In this guide, we will explore the steps to creating a family mission statement, offer tips on defining your family’s core values, and explain how to ensure that the mission statement becomes a living, breathing part of your family’s everyday life.

What is a Family Mission Statement?

A family mission statement is a written declaration of the guiding principles, beliefs, and values that a family holds dear. It acts as a roadmap for how your family interacts with one another, how you approach challenges, and how you live in accordance with your shared principles. The purpose of the mission statement is to serve as a reminder of the family’s core identity, providing clarity and direction as decisions are made and challenges are encountered.

While a family mission statement is personal to each family, it often includes elements such as:

  • Core values: What does your family stand for? What behaviors and qualities do you want to prioritize in your relationships and daily lives?
  • Family vision: What is the long-term vision for your family’s future? How do you want your family to grow and evolve over the years?
  • Goals and aspirations: What do you want to achieve as a family? These could include both short-term and long-term objectives.
  • Commitments: What are the specific ways in which family members commit to each other in order to live out the mission statement?

Now that we’ve defined what a family mission statement is, let’s look at how you can create one that resonates with everyone in your family and helps strengthen your relationships.

Step 1: Gather Your Family Together

Creating a family mission statement is a collaborative process. It requires input from everyone in the family, regardless of age. To begin, gather everyone together for a dedicated meeting or discussion. It’s important that every family member feels heard and included in the process.

If you have young children, keep in mind that their involvement might look different from older family members. You can engage them by asking questions they can relate to, such as “What is important to you about our family?” or “What do you love most about being part of our family?”

Set aside time for everyone to talk openly, and make sure to create an atmosphere where everyone feels comfortable expressing themselves without judgment. The goal is to be honest and transparent about what matters most to each person.

Step 2: Define Your Family’s Core Values

Core values form the foundation of your family mission statement. These values will guide how your family interacts with one another, responds to challenges, and makes decisions. To define your family’s core values, start by asking each family member to reflect on what is most important to them. This could include things like kindness, honesty, responsibility, faith, generosity, or respect.

It can be helpful to consider these questions when identifying your family’s core values:

  • What qualities do you admire in others?
  • What behaviors do you want to encourage in your family?
  • What is non-negotiable when it comes to how you treat each other?

Once you’ve brainstormed a list of potential values, narrow it down to the ones that resonate most strongly with everyone in your family. A mission statement should be focused, so aim to choose five to seven core values that best reflect your collective vision for your family.

Here are some examples of core values that families may choose to prioritize:

  • Respect: Treating each other with kindness and consideration.
  • Integrity: Being honest and transparent in all interactions.
  • Gratitude: Cultivating an attitude of appreciation and thankfulness.
  • Love: Prioritizing affection and support in family relationships.
  • Community: Emphasizing the importance of helping others and contributing to the greater good.

Remember that values can evolve over time, and that’s okay. You can always revisit your family mission statement as your family grows and changes.

Step 3: Craft Your Family Mission Statement

With your values identified, it’s time to begin drafting your family mission statement. Keep in mind that this statement should reflect both the shared values and goals of the family, while also being specific enough to guide day-to-day decisions and behaviors. Your mission statement should inspire and motivate your family members to live with purpose and intention.

Here are some questions to guide the creation of your family mission statement:

  • What is the purpose of our family? Think about the overall vision you have for your family and how you want your family to impact the world around you.
  • How do we want to treat each other? Reflect on the behaviors and actions that you value most in your family relationships.
  • What are our shared goals? These could be long-term dreams (e.g., a family trip, educational goals, or buying a house) or short-term objectives (e.g., spending more time together, helping others, or adopting healthier habits).
  • What does success look like for us as a family? Define what it means to thrive as a family, whether it’s through love, support, accomplishments, or contributing to your community.

Here is an example of a family mission statement:

“Our family is committed to living with love, integrity, and respect for one another. We support each other’s dreams, prioritize kindness, and strive to make a positive difference in our community. We value honesty, hard work, and gratitude, and we will always work together as a team to achieve our goals and create a happy, fulfilling life.”

Remember, this is your family’s statement, so it should feel authentic to who you are. Keep it concise but meaningful, and don’t be afraid to include aspirational language that reflects the future you envision for your family.

Step 4: Make Your Family Mission Statement Visible

Once you’ve created your family mission statement, it’s time to make it visible and integrate it into your daily life. A mission statement is only effective if it’s consistently reinforced and put into practice. Here are some ways to keep your family mission statement front and center:

Display it in your home

Print your family mission statement and frame it somewhere prominent in your home, such as the living room, kitchen, or hallway. This allows your mission statement to serve as a constant reminder of your family’s shared values and goals.

Refer to it regularly

Make it a habit to refer to your family mission statement during family discussions, especially when making important decisions or addressing conflicts. This will help ensure that your actions are aligned with your core values.

Celebrate progress

Take time to celebrate when your family members demonstrate the values outlined in the mission statement. Whether it’s a small act of kindness or a larger achievement, recognizing and acknowledging these moments will reinforce the importance of the mission statement and encourage everyone to keep striving toward it.

Step 5: Review and Revise Periodically

Your family’s needs and goals will likely change over time. It’s important to revisit your family mission statement periodically to ensure that it remains relevant and aligned with where your family is headed. As children grow older, your family dynamics may shift, and your values and priorities may evolve as well. Make time to review your mission statement together as a family and discuss whether it still reflects who you are and where you’re headed.

Don’t be afraid to make changes as needed. A family mission statement should be a living document that evolves with your family’s journey.

Conclusion

Creating a family mission statement is an empowering process that helps families define their values, strengthen relationships, and set a clear direction for the future. By reflecting on your family’s core values, articulating your vision, and making a commitment to live by those principles, you can build a strong foundation for a purposeful and fulfilling family life. Whether you’re just beginning to create a mission statement or revisiting it after many years, this process can provide clarity, unity, and a sense of shared purpose that will guide your family for years to come.
