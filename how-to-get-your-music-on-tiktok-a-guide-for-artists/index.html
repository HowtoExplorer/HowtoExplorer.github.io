How to Get Your Music on TikTok: A Guide for Artists


TikTok has become one of the most influential platforms in the world of music, offering artists new ways to share their creations and connect with a global audience. With millions of users browsing the app every day, it’s clear that TikTok can be a powerful tool for any musician looking to promote their music, gain followers, and possibly go viral. But how do you get your music onto TikTok? In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process step-by-step, and provide tips on how to leverage the platform’s unique features to get your tracks heard.

1. Understanding the Power of TikTok for Music Artists

Before diving into the specifics of how to upload your music, it’s essential to understand why TikTok is so valuable for music promotion. The app’s short-video format allows users to create content that’s easily shareable, making it easier than ever for songs to go viral. From viral dance challenges to lip-sync trends, TikTok is a platform where music trends are born and rapidly spread across social media platforms.

For artists, this presents an exciting opportunity to not only promote new releases but also engage with their audience in creative ways. TikTok’s algorithm is designed to surface content that resonates with users, meaning that even lesser-known songs can get significant exposure when used in the right context.

2. Distribute Your Music Through a Digital Distributor

TikTok doesn’t directly allow musicians to upload their tracks, so the first step in getting your music on the platform is to distribute it through a digital distribution service. These services work as intermediaries between artists and platforms like TikTok, Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, and others. Here’s how to get started:

Choose a Digital Distributor

There are many digital distributors to choose from, each with its own features, fees, and processes. Some popular distributors that work with TikTok include:

  • DistroKid: A fast and affordable distribution service with an easy-to-use platform. It allows you to get your music on TikTok and other platforms quickly.
  • TuneCore: A reliable service that provides music distribution to a wide range of platforms, including TikTok. TuneCore also offers detailed analytics to track your performance.
  • CD Baby: One of the most well-known distributors, offering music distribution for TikTok, as well as marketing tools to help you reach a larger audience.
  • Amuse: A free distribution service with a paid option that offers more features, including the ability to distribute to TikTok.

Submit Your Music

Once you’ve chosen a distributor, you’ll need to submit your music. The process is fairly straightforward:

  • Upload your track(s) in the appropriate format (usually WAV or MP3).
  • Provide the necessary metadata, including song title, artist name, album art, and genre.
  • Select the platforms where you want your music to appear, including TikTok.
  • Review everything before submitting to ensure accuracy.

Most digital distributors take a few days to process and distribute your music, so it’s important to plan ahead if you have a specific release date in mind.

3. Utilize TikTok’s Music Library

Once your music is distributed to TikTok via your chosen distributor, it will be added to the app’s music library. This allows TikTok users to use your song in their videos. However, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Make Your Track Discoverable

Even if your song is on TikTok, it won’t automatically go viral. To increase the chances of your song being used, it’s important to promote it effectively. Here are some tips:

  • Hashtags: Use relevant hashtags when posting your own TikTok videos to help your song get discovered. Popular hashtags like #Music, #NewMusic, or your song’s title can help users find your track.
  • Collaborations: Partner with influencers or content creators to help promote your song. If a well-known TikTok personality uses your track in their video, it can lead to significant exposure.
  • Challenges: Create or participate in TikTok challenges related to your song. Encouraging your followers or influencers to create content around your music can quickly lead to viral moments.

Monitor Your Performance

TikTok provides analytics that allow you to track how your song is performing on the platform. These analytics can help you understand which videos are using your track, how many times it’s been played, and where the traffic is coming from. Monitoring this data is crucial to refining your promotional strategies and seeing what resonates with your audience.

4. Create Engaging Content for TikTok

While getting your music on TikTok is essential, the next step is to create content that engages users and encourages them to use your song in their own videos. TikTok is all about creativity, so think of ways to showcase your music in fun and interactive ways.

Behind-the-Scenes Content

Share exclusive behind-the-scenes footage of your music-making process, music video shoots, or rehearsals. This gives followers an inside look at your artistry and helps build a connection with your audience.

Music Videos

Create short, creative music videos that showcase your track in a fun, eye-catching way. TikTok’s format works well for clips of 15-60 seconds, so focus on making the most of that time. Use effects, transitions, and visuals that make your video stand out.

Dance Challenges and Trends

One of the most popular ways to get your music trending on TikTok is by creating a dance challenge. If your song has a catchy beat or memorable chorus, choreograph a simple dance routine that TikTok users can easily replicate. Be sure to provide clear instructions and encourage users to participate by using your song in their own posts.

Hashtags and Trends

Keep an eye on trending hashtags and participate in viral challenges that align with your music. By jumping on trends, you can increase the visibility of your song and reach a broader audience. However, be sure to keep your content authentic and aligned with your brand.

5. Engage with Your TikTok Community

TikTok isn’t just a platform for posting content; it’s also a social space where users interact with each other. To maximize your success, you should actively engage with your followers and the broader TikTok community.

Respond to Comments and Messages

Engagement is key to building a loyal fanbase. Respond to comments on your videos, engage with fans, and answer questions. A personal connection with your followers can help build trust and foster loyalty.

Duets and Stitches

TikTok’s Duet and Stitch features allow users to create content alongside other videos. Take advantage of these features by creating Duets or Stitches with videos using your music. This encourages further interaction and expands your reach.

Consistent Posting

Stay active on TikTok by posting regularly. Consistent posting helps keep your audience engaged and ensures your music remains top of mind for TikTok users. The more you post, the more opportunities you have for your music to be heard.

6. Promoting Your TikTok Music Across Other Platforms

While TikTok is an essential tool for music promotion, it’s important not to rely on the app alone. Cross-promotion on other social media platforms can help drive traffic to your TikTok and increase the visibility of your music.

Share Your TikTok Videos on Instagram, Twitter, and Facebook

Repurpose your TikTok videos and share them on your other social media accounts. This helps you reach your existing followers who may not be active on TikTok. Be sure to include a call to action encouraging users to follow you on TikTok or use your music in their own videos.

Collaborate with Other Artists

Collaborating with other artists, influencers, and creators across platforms can help you reach a wider audience. Consider doing cross-promotions, challenges, or guest appearances in each other’s TikTok videos.

7. Track Your Success and Adjust Your Strategy

Finally, it’s crucial to track the success of your efforts on TikTok. Use the app’s analytics to see which types of videos are performing well and which songs are getting the most attention. Based on this data, refine your strategy and focus on creating content that resonates with your audience.

TikTok is a constantly evolving platform, so staying up-to-date with trends, tools, and features is essential to ensuring your music remains relevant and discoverable.

Conclusion

Getting your music on TikTok is just the beginning. The real magic happens when you use the platform creatively to engage with your audience, participate in trends, and share your music in ways that resonate with users. By distributing your tracks, creating engaging content, and actively promoting your music across different channels, you can harness the full potential of TikTok to take your music career to new heights. So, start getting creative, track your progress, and enjoy the ride!
