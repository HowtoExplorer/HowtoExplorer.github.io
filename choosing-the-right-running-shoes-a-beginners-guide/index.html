Choosing the Right Running Shoes: A Beginner's Guide


Embarking on a new running journey can be an electrifying experience, but one crucial element often overlooked by beginners is the importance of choosing the right running shoes. You might be wondering, “How do I choose the perfect running pair for me?” Well, fear not my budding marathoner, for we shall explore this topic together. Armed with knowledge, determination, and a sprinkle of shoe-shopping finesse, you’ll have your perfect pair in no time.

Before diving headfirst into the sea of trainers, consider these key factors that can make or break your running endeavors.

Understanding Your Feet

Before you reach for any old pair of shoes, it’s essential to analyze your foot type and the shape of your arch. This fundamental step ensures you find shoes that suit your unique biomechanics, placing your body in the most optimal position while running. So how do you identify your foot type? Simple – take an old pair of socks and stand on a piece of brown paper or parchment paper. Step off and examine the silhouette formed by your footprint.

There are generally three types of feet:

  1. Flat Feet: A flat footprint with no arch, potentially indicating fallen arches.

  2. High Arch: A high or pronounced arch, often coupled with minimal contact at the heel.

  3. Neutral or Average Arch: A balanced footprint with a moderate arch supporting the entire foot.

Knowing your arch type is crucial because specific running shoe features cater to each foot type, providing the necessary support and preventing injuries.

Finding the Right Fit

With an increased understanding of your feet, you’re now ready to search for that treasured pair of running shoes. TOEBOX, a term that refers to the amount of space you require for your toes to move freely, should also influence your decision. A well-fitting shoe allows for a ½ inch wiggle room across the widest part of your foot, whilst maintaining snug contact with the sides of your foot to prevent slippage. This golden rule prevents the development of blisters and ensures a comfortable running experience.

Key Features

Now that your shoes speak your feet’s language, it’s time to scrutinize their attributes.

  1. Cushioning: Ideally, choose shoes with enough cushioning to absorb impact forces resulting from the impact of your foot strike with the ground. This cushioning should cater to your striking preference – whether you’re a heel, midfoot, or forefoot striker. Intrigued? Feel free to do research on different running techniques to identify your style!

  2. Support: Your running shoes should support your arch and provide the necessary stability. If you’re a pronator (over-supinating your foot), opt for motion control shoes designed for maximum support. For supinators (under-pronating), cushioned stability shoes are the ticket to comfort.

  3. Flexibility: A shoe that bends where your foot naturally does can adapt to your stride more effectively, enhancing the overall running experience.

  4. Breathability: Not exclusively a feature but a priority nonetheless. Running produces sweat, and you’ll thank yourself later if your shoes efficiently allow your feet to breathe.

  5. Weight: Lightweight shoes can serve as valuable assets to your running endeavors, but don’t blindly follow the hype. Experiment with different weights and determine the sweet spot that allows you to feel both supported and weightless.

The Conscientious Marathoner

In summary, choosing the right running shoes requires a substantial understanding of your biomechanics, recognizing your arch type, perfecting the fit, and considering the key features of a shoe. By attentively addressing these factors, you will treat your feet with the care they deserve, allowing running to be the celebratory dance with each stride.

Lastly, remember that everyone’s unique running journey is a gradual process. Embrace trial and error as crucial steps to discovering your ideal running shoes. Welcome to the world of runners: throw away the notion of “one size fits all” and forge your path with the perfect pair by your side. Happy running!
