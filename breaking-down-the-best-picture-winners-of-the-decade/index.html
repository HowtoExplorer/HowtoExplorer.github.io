Breaking Down the Best Picture Winners of the Decade

4 min · 713 words

The past decade has been a remarkable one for the film industry, with numerous outstanding movies captivating audiences and receiving critical acclaim. In this blog post, we will take a deep dive into the best picture winners of the decade, exploring their unique qualities, impact, and why they deserved the prestigious recognition.

2010 - “The King’s Speech”

“The King’s Speech” took home the Best Picture award in 2010, and it truly deserved the honor. Directed by Tom Hooper, the film tells the inspiring story of King George VI and his struggle with a speech impediment. With exceptional performances from Colin Firth, Helena Bonham Carter, and Geoffrey Rush, this historical drama not only showcases the power of friendship and determination but also sheds light on a lesser-known aspect of British history.

2011 - “The Artist”

In 2011, “The Artist” made history by becoming the first silent film to win Best Picture since the inception of the Academy Awards. Directed by Michel Hazanavicius, this black-and-white gem pays homage to the golden age of cinema. With charming performances by Jean Dujardin and Bérénice Bejo, “The Artist” transports us back to a time when movies relied on visual storytelling, reminding us of the magic that can be created without words.

2012 - “Argo”

“Argo” took home the Best Picture award in 2012, leaving a lasting impact on audiences with its intense storytelling. Directed by Ben Affleck, who also stars in the film, “Argo” is based on the true story of a CIA operative who uses a fake Hollywood film production as a cover to rescue American hostages in Iran. This gripping thriller keeps viewers on the edge of their seats, showcasing the lengths people will go to in order to save lives.

2013 - “12 Years a Slave”

Directed by Steve McQueen, “12 Years a Slave” won Best Picture in 2013 and made a profound impact on audiences worldwide. This powerful and emotionally charged drama tells the true story of Solomon Northup, a free African-American man who is kidnapped and sold into slavery. With exceptional performances by Chiwetel Ejiofor, Lupita Nyong’o, and Michael Fassbender, “12 Years a Slave” confronts the horrors of slavery head-on, leaving a lasting impression on viewers.

2014 - “Birdman or (The Unexpected Virtue of Ignorance)”

“Birdman or (The Unexpected Virtue of Ignorance)” won the hearts of both critics and audiences when it won Best Picture in 2014. Directed by Alejandro G. Iñárritu, the film follows Riggan Thomson, a washed-up actor played by Michael Keaton, as he attempts to revive his career by staging a Broadway play. With its innovative cinematography and stellar performances, “Birdman” explores the delicate balance between ego, art, and personal identity.

2015 - “Spotlight”

“Spotlight” took home the Best Picture award in 2015, shedding light on a dark and unsettling subject matter. Directed by Tom McCarthy, the film tells the true story of the Boston Globe’s investigative journalism team that exposed the widespread child molestation scandal within the Catholic Church. With its gripping narrative and superb ensemble cast, including Mark Ruffalo, Rachel McAdams, and Michael Keaton, “Spotlight” serves as a reminder of the power of journalism in uncovering the truth.

2016 - “Moonlight”

In 2016, “Moonlight” made history by becoming the first LGBTQ+ themed film to win Best Picture. Directed by Barry Jenkins, this coming-of-age drama explores the life of a young African-American man named Chiron as he navigates his identity and sexuality in a rough Miami neighborhood. With its stunning cinematography, heartfelt performances, and poignant storytelling, “Moonlight” resonated with audiences around the world, shining a light on the universal themes of love, identity, and self-acceptance.

2017 - “The Shape of Water”

Directed by Guillermo del Toro, “The Shape of Water” won Best Picture in 2017, captivating viewers with its enchanting and unconventional love story. Set against the backdrop of the Cold War era, the film follows the relationship between Elisa, a mute janitor, and an amphibious creature held captive in a government research facility. With its mesmerizing visuals, exceptional performances, and a unique blend of fantasy and romance, “The Shape of Water” proves that love knows no bounds.

2018 - “Green Book”

“Green Book” took home the Best Picture award in 2018, delivering a heartwarming and thought-provoking story. Directed by Peter Farrelly, the film explores the unlikely friendship between a working-class Italian-American bouncer and a talented African-American pianist during a concert tour in the racially segregated South. With its remarkable performances from Viggo Mortensen and Mahershala Ali, “Green Book” tackles important themes of racism, friendship, and acceptance, leaving a lasting impact on viewers.

2019 - “Parasite”

Directed by Bong Joon-ho, “Parasite” made history in 2019 by becoming the first South Korean film to win Best Picture. This dark comedy-thriller tells the story of two families from different social classes whose lives become intertwined in unexpected ways. With its masterful storytelling, brilliant performances, and sharp social commentary, “Parasite” challenges societal norms and explores the complexities of class division, leaving audiences both entertained and deeply provoked.

Conclusion

The Best Picture winners of the past decade have showcased the diversity and excellence in filmmaking. From historical dramas to unconventional love stories, each film has left a lasting impact on audiences around the world. Through their exceptional storytelling, powerful performances, and thought-provoking themes, these films have cemented their place in cinematic history, reminding us of the profound influence that movies can have on our lives. As we eagerly await the Best Picture winners of the next decade, let us celebrate the remarkable achievements of these films and the talented individuals who brought them to life.
