A Global Guide to Unique Museums and Exhibitions


Traveling around the world offers not only magnificent landscapes and vibrant cultures, but also a chance to explore the quirkiest and most fascinating museums and exhibitions that you’ll find nowhere else! If you’re looking for a bucket-list-worthy experience that goes beyond the typical art galleries and history museums, here are some unique picks worldwide sure to spark your curiosity and imagination.

1. Crocodile Museum – Izmir, Turkey

No need to be a shark week aficionado to visit this peculiar museum tucked away in the Aegean coastline city of Izmir. The Crocodile Museum, founded by a former crocodile farmer named Fevin Kudret, houses 318 life-size sculptures of crocodiles made from scrap metal. You’ll find yourself surrounded by these alligator-like creatures, showcasing various sizes, colors, and expressions - offering a somewhat surreal experience.

2. Jellyfish Gallery – Reykjavik, Iceland

Located within the Blue Lagoon geothermal spa in Reykjavik, the unique Jellyfish Gallery exhibits over 15 species of captive jellyfish, suspended in their own viewing chambers. Famed for the mesmerizing beauty of these translucent creatures, the venue offers an interactive experience where visitors can touch, photograph, and even watch them eat. Tedious but well worth it, they’re creatures that provoke a sense of serene Swimmy-like reminiscence.

3. Bonsai Art Museum – Omiya, Saitama, Japan

Decorated with artworks by internationally-renowned bonsai artists, the Bonsai Art Museum is nestled in the heart of the Saitama prefecture, providing aesthetically-pleasing insights into this age-old art. Witness the incredible, living forms of miniature trees that are meticulously cared for and maintained throughout their lifetime. As you stroll through the museum, you’ll wonder how a single tree species can be, at times, grown into a towering giant, and in other occasions, molded into a visual masterpiece.

4. Museum of Everyday Life – Copenhagen, Denmark

This unique museum takes us back to the days when every small object held its particular significance and charm. At the Museum of Everyday Life in Copenhagen, you’ll discover artifacts from the 20th century that speak to the human experience, relating to daily happenings and societal norms around the globe. Prepare to reevaluate and appreciate the little things that may seem commonplace.

5. The Light+Building Exhibition – Frankfurt, Germany

This biannual event, held in Frankfurt, is the world’s largest trade fair for lighting and building technologies, showcasing innovative sustainable solutions and energy-efficient products for the industry. The exhibition gives you a glimpse of the future of technology from countless innovation-driven companies, providing boundless inspiration and an insight into smart cities and modern responsible living.

6. Kate Spade Saturday – Paris, France

Libérée from the restrictions of conventional fashion philosophy, Kate Spade Saturday’s Museum of RFID offered guests a whimsical RFID (Radio-Frequency IDentification) scavenger hunt within the Parisian streets. A foray into the world of technology and fashion, the mammoth-sized game required a certain chutzpah to walk the streets of Paris with a pack of goody bags in tow. An enchanting, innovative way to delve into the digital age.

7. The Why Not Museum – New Orleans, Louisiana

What began as a performance art act by local performer John O’Kane and friends has snowballed into an iconic installation, treasured by Kramer-Mathews galas and infamous gatherings, is the Why Not Museum in New Orleans. Combining art, satire, and pop culture, the museum encourages a sense of playful license in its visitors, making every question, opinion, or musing a perfect fit for your personal display case.

8. ROSA – The Robot Orchestra – Tokyo, Japan

With technology taking over ever more spheres of human life, ROSA - Robot Orchestra in, Tokyo, Japan, is an all-robot symphony that you won’t find anywhere else. Each instrument is played by a mechanical performer, providing an electrifying performance that combines innovative arts and engineering. The masterpiece not only showcases the evolution of human craftsmanship but also marks the dawn of a new era in artificial intelligence.

As we explore the world, there seems to be an endless array of unique and fascinating museums and exhibitions worth experiencing. From crocodiles to jellyfish, bonsai trees to pop culture, these quirky destinations will continually surprise you and provide memories you’ll cherish. So, when embarking on your next adventure, leave behind the usual tourist destinations and uncover the jewels that are hidden in plain sight.
