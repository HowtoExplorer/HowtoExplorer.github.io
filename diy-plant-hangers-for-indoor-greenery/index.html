DIY Plant Hangers for Indoor Greenery


Are you looking to add a touch of green to your living space but don’t have much room? DIY plant hangers are a fantastic way to bring the outdoors in while saving valuable floor space. Not only do they serve a practical purpose by keeping your plants off the ground, but they also add a stylish and decorative element to any room. In this blog post, we will explore some creative and easy-to-make DIY plant hangers that will elevate your indoor greenery game.

Materials You’ll Need

To get started on your DIY plant hangers, you’ll need a few basic materials that you can easily find at your local craft store or even repurpose from items you already have at home. Here’s a list of what you’ll need:

  • Macramé cord or sturdy rope
  • Wooden or metal rings
  • Scissors
  • Pots or containers for your plants
  • Ceiling hooks or a curtain rod

Simple Macramé Plant Hanger

Macramé plant hangers are a timeless and elegant way to display your favorite plants. To make a simple macramé plant hanger, follow these steps:

  1. Cut four pieces of macramé cord to your desired length (depending on how long you want your plant hanger to be).
  2. Fold the cords in half and loop them through a wooden or metal ring.
  3. Tie a knot at the top of the cords to secure the ring in place.
  4. Divide the cords into pairs and knot them together about a third of the way down.
  5. Continue knotting pairs of cords together until you reach the desired length for your plant hanger.
  6. Place your potted plant into the macramé hanger and hang it from a ceiling hook or a curtain rod.

Geometric Plant Hanger

If you’re looking for a more modern and geometric design, try making a geometric plant hanger using metal rings. Here’s how you can create one:

  1. Cut several pieces of cord to different lengths, ensuring they are all longer than the height of your pot.
  2. Thread the cords through a metal ring and tie a knot at the top to secure them.
  3. Separate the cords into groups and braid or knot them together in a geometric pattern.
  4. Once you’ve created the desired shape, place your plant inside the hanger and hang it up.

Hanging Planter Shelf

For a twist on traditional plant hangers, consider making a hanging planter shelf. This DIY project is perfect for displaying multiple plants at varying heights. Here’s how you can make one:

  1. Cut wooden boards to the desired length for your shelf.
  2. Drill holes in each corner of the boards.
  3. Attach metal rings to the boards using rope or cord.
  4. Hang the shelves at different levels and place your potted plants on them.

Conclusion

DIY plant hangers are a fun and creative way to bring the beauty of nature into your home. Whether you prefer the bohemian vibe of macramé hangers or the sleek look of geometric designs, there’s a plant hanger style to suit every taste. Not only do these hangers add a decorative touch to your space, but they also help keep your plants healthy and thriving. So why not roll up your sleeves, gather your materials, and get started on creating your own unique plant hangers today?

Happy crafting and happy gardening!
