Procrastination: Turn It into Your Problem-Solving Advantage


We’ve all been there: a looming deadline, a daunting task ahead, and the overwhelming feeling to procrastinate instead. But what if we reframe our mindset about procrastination? What if this seemingly negative habit could actually be an unexpected problem-solving advantage?

Contrary to popular belief, procrastination is not merely synonymous with laziness or lack of motivation. It’s a complex phenomenon that reflects our psychological needs and tendencies. Dive into our exploration of this fascinating human behavior, and we’ll reveal how you could leverage its potential to perform at your best.

A Deep Dive into the Procrastination Paradox

The first step to utilizing procrastination as a problem-solving tool is to genuinely understand it. You’ve got to embrace the paradox – the delay in starting a task despite knowing the potential negative consequences, and how it can sometimes lead to better outcomes.

Famed psychologist Daniel Kahneman posits that procrastination is a classic battle between two selves – the “near self” that prioritizes immediate gratification and delays work, and the “far self” that takes a longer-term perspective and values future consequences. By recognizing this internal struggle, you can develop techniques to harness the power of procrastination to your advantage.

Procrastination, Creativity, and Productivity

It’s no secret that some of history’s most amazing works were completed under extreme time pressure. From Shakespeare writing his comedies in a furious two weeks to Frank Lloyd Wright finishing his architectural masterpieces just days before deadline, there’s a pattern that emerges when procrastination meets creativity.

So how can you boost your productivity through procrastination? The secret lies in the “online” phase – the moment where you regain focus and start working on a task. Procrastination forces us to bypass the “initiation” phase and dive right into the “online” phase, where we make significant progress, resulting in higher quality work.

7 Midas Touch Steps to Conquer Procrastination

Now that we’ve outlined how procrastination can aid problem-solving, it’s time to turn this knowledge into a tangible skill. Dissecting the “how-to” of procrastination promises an unexpected advantage for both mundane and challenging tasks. Implement the following seven steps to enhance your productivity and unleash the hidden potential of procrastination.

  1. Embrace the Delay

    • Understand that procrastination has benefits by enabling you to step back from the task and gain new perspectives.

  2. Prioritize Tasks

    • Decide what tasks are vital and make a to-do list prioritizing what is most important and urgent.

  3. Chunk Your Time

    • Develop a structured schedule, dividing your day into manageable chunks to avoid feeling overwhelmed.

  4. Create a Positive Environment

    • Set up a space that encourages productivity and inspiration.

  5. Leverage Deadlines

    • Artificially impose deadlines to create an urgency for action without panicking.

  6. Dive into the “Online” Phase

    • From the moment you start your task, plunge headfirst into your work. Avoid second-guessing or doubting yourself.

  7. Adjust and Repeat

    • Evaluate the tactics that work, keep refining, and never stop improving.

The Procrastination Strategy: A Practical Example

To solidify our newfound understanding, let’s examine an everyday example of turning procrastination into an advantage. Imagine writing a blog post or a term paper:

  1. Instead of immersing yourself in research from the get-go, wait until nearer the deadline to commence. This paves the way for an “online” phase with higher energy and concentration, leading to comprehensive research in a much shorter time span.

  2. Develop a well-defined outline, giving yourself a familiar roadmap leading to an efficiently structured piece.

  3. Prioritize your research and structure, focusing on providing value while making your task more manageable.

  4. Set deadlines, whether it’s dedicating daily time slots or mapping your progress against critical points in your outline.

  5. Eliminate distractions, ensuring that once you start, you’re completely devoted to producing quality work.

  6. Dive into writing, channeling your procrastination-induced burst of creativity to draft outstanding content.

  7. Finally, revisit the revised drafts, tweaking and polishing your piece to perfection.

In the end, we’ve debunked the myth that procrastination is an unfortunate hindrance. Rather, it’s a hidden double-edged sword, capable of stimulating creative flow and helping us work productively. By accepting and driving procrastination to our advantage, we can master it to excel in our personal and professional endeavors.

Embrace procrastination as a skill, not a weakness, and become the master of both your time and tasks. The next time this phenomenon strikes, lean into the momentum and enjoy the wonders it may bring. Your productivity and work quality will thank you.

Let’s establish a nuanced perception of procrastination as not a curse but our ally when it comes to problem-solving. Tap into your procrastination, channel it, and witness an unstoppable creative force in action. The world is your canvas — procrastination is your ink.
