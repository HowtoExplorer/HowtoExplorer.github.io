Interview Etiquette: Rules to Follow and Exceptions to Break


When looking for a new job or opportunity, the interview process can feel like a high-pressure game of chess. In today’s cut-throat job market, understanding the rules of engagement can often be the difference between success and failure.

Following standard etiquette during the interview is just as important as knowing your stuff in the technical rounds. It sets the stage for how you want the interviewers to perceive you. Just like every game, there are rules you must follow – but also times when you can break the rules.

Arriving Early and Other Punctuality Best Practices

  • Always arrive at least 10-15 minutes before the scheduled time.
  • Dress professionally and appropriately for the company culture.
  • Greet the interviewer with a firm handshake and a smile.
  • Be polite, enthusiastic, and maintain a positive attitude.

Breaking the Rules:

  • Sometimes, unexpected circumstances may make you arrive late. If that’s the case, call the interviewer to inform them (preferably before they start worrying) and explain the situation.
  • Not every industry or company is strictly formal: some are more casual or creativity-focused. Aim to study the company’s culture beforehand and adapt your attire or communication style accordingly.

Digital Etiquette and Technology Usage

  • Turn off or silence your phone, and avoid checking emails or social media during the interview.
  • Speak clearly and naturally, avoid using jargons unless relevant to the job.
  • Follow up with a thank-you note to the interviewer within 24 hours of the interview.

Breaking the Rules:

  • In certain industries, technology usage can play a big role in the job. If you feel that an app or tool would add value to your presentation, you can mention it briefly. However, don’t let it dominate the conversation.
  • Following up via email may not always be ideal – in some cases, it might be better to send a text message or give a quick phone call. Personalize your interaction based on your interviewer’s preferences.

Body Language and Non-Verbal Communication

  • Make eye contact as much as possible without being aggressive.
  • Sit up straight and use open body language to appear engaged.
  • Do not interrupt the interviewer but wait for your turn to speak.

Breaking the Rules:

  • It’s OK to show genuine enthusiasm by nodding or leaning slightly forward. If you feel that eye contact is causing discomfort, switch to a more comfortable focal point.
  • Disrupting the flow occasionally by adding in relevant and clever remarks can make you stand out.

Asking the Right Questions

  • Listen carefully to questions and provide well-thought-out responses.
  • Show a genuine interest in the company and its culture.
  • Ask questions about the job, the company, and possible advancement opportunities.

Breaking the Rules:

  • It’s alright to ask unconventional questions that highlight your creativity or problem-solving skills.
  • If the interviewer expresses genuine interest in your background or unusual skills, don’t hesitate to seize the opportunity and engage them with related questions or discussions.

In conclusion, following proper interview etiquette is essential to make a great first impression. However, don’t be afraid to break the rules occasionally – when done right, it can set you apart from other candidates. Use your instincts, adapt to the situation, and remember: you know your strengths best, so make sure to showcase them effectively.
