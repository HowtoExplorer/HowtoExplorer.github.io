Journey Through The Decades: The Evolution of Skincare and Beauty Practices


Skincare and beauty practices have come a long way since they first emerged in human history. From the ancient Egyptians using oil-based concoctions to the modern-day obsession with serums and peels, the journey is fascinating and filled with unique perspectives on what it means to look beautiful.

As we venture through the decades, we’ll discover how cultural shifts, scientific advancements, and changing societal norms have influenced and transformed the global landscape of skincare and beauty.

1950s: The Golden Era of Skincare

In the 1950s, the era of Marilyn Monroe and Audrey Hepburn, there was a quintessential beauty look that the entire world aspired to emulate - the porcelain skin combined with bright red lips and fluttery eyelashes. This conjunction of beauty and marketing resulted in the consumption of skincare products at an unprecedented rate.

The readily available foundations, lipstick shades and mascara brands, such as Revlon, Max Factor, and Maybelline, redefined the beauty landscape around the globe. Influenced largely by Hollywood and the concept of glamour embedded in movies, skincare became synonymous to maintenance rather than luxury.

1960s: Moving towards Natural Sounds

In the following decade, the attitudes towards beauty shifted towards a more natural approach. The 1960s saw an explosion of homemade skincare remedies and natural cosmetics. The rise of the hippie movement emphasized a more sustainable and earth-conscious approach to beauty, denouncing the synthetically manufactured products that dominated the previous era.

Products like tear-free baby shampoo and mineral makeup started to gain popularity as women sought after gentler options. Spa treatments, such as body scrubs and facials, became immensely popular, giving way to the birth of the modern skincare industry.

1970s: Tretinoin and Supermodels

Enter the 1970s: the advent of scientific breakthroughs in skincare introduced a third element – medical intervention. The EPA approved tretinoin, a vitamin A derivative, for skincare. Within a few years, dermatologists began to tout its effectiveness, and a skincare trend was born. This period marked the beginning of combining traditional beauty treatments with cutting-edge scientific research.

Beyond tretinoin, basic skincare routines started to include products such as moisurizers and sunscreens as the awareness of UV damage rose. This decade also saw the rise of supermodels, who cemented an entirely new, modern beauty aesthetic for the world to follow.

1980s: The Age of Extravagance

Going into the 1980s, luxury skincare brands emerged, gaining unprecedented popularity. Early innovators such as La Prairie and Estée Lauder incorported the finest levels of consumerism and extravagance within their marketing and product execution.

And so were born skincare practices steeped in decadence, combining both progressive science, such as hydroquinone, as well as luxurious facial appointments – all of which offered the promise of youthful beauty. The 1980s brought an age of extravagance to the beauty industry.

1990s: The High-Tech Decade

With the humble origins of silicone sheet masks in Japan in the early ’90s, it was clear that the next decade would be sci-fi in its approach to skincare. The 1990s saw the adoption of advanced scientific technologies in skincare practices, integrating the use of lasers, IPL, and ultrasound treatments to address a series of cosmetic complaints.

The beginning of the anti-aging movement was born, as skincare practices shifted towards prevention and proactive approaches against signs of skin aging. With technological advancements also came the rise of private label skincare lines, along with the need for supplementings the pricey technology-induced treatments

2000s: Social Media and Self Care

While the Internet barely penetrated mainstream consciousness in the earlier years, its presence grew exponentially and dramatically shifted the landscape of beauty and skincare practices. Blogs, forums, social media, and Youtubers have transformed the ways in which we consume and share beauty recommendations.

Skincare has now transcended just rituals and routines, paving way for self-care movements and a new mentality around wellness and mental health. This shift especially shines through in the K-Beauty boom, as consumer’s pursuit for a holistic approach to skincare became clearer and more pronounced.

2010s: Skincare Turned Inward

The new millennium is slowly coming to an end, and with it, we notice a shift going inward, with a focus on holistic practices, gut-health, gut microbiome, and an emphasis on the concept of “biodiversity” for both skin health and overall wellness.

A return to natural remedies and methods, such as Traditional Chinese Medicine and Ayurveda, has gained traction, merging with modern technology to support the idea that beauty excellence is not restricted to the skin, but rather derived from the holistic and proactive approach to a healthy and balanced lifestyle.

With every decade, it is no doubt that the journey of skincare and beauty practices has evolved, and the future only promises more fascinating changes and perspectives. As the decades progress, the quest for beauty endures, ever changing and captivating. Ultimately, the age of skincare and beauty practices - like human history itself - is a saga of discovery, learning, and self-expression.
