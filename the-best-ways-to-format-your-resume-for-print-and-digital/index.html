The Best Ways to Format Your Resume for Print and Digital


Creating a standout resume is crucial when seeking job opportunities. However, one of the often-overlooked aspects of resume creation is the formatting. Both the physical print version and digital formats of your resume require different considerations to ensure that it presents your skills and experience in the best possible light. Whether you’re submitting your resume in person or via email, the layout, design, and structure can significantly impact how it is perceived by potential employers.

In this guide, we will explore the best ways to format your resume for both print and digital formats. We’ll cover everything from layout and fonts to the specifics of file types, ensuring that your resume looks professional, readable, and optimized for both physical and digital submission.

1. Choose the Right Resume Layout

Your resume layout is the foundation of its overall appearance. Regardless of whether you’re submitting it in print or digitally, a clean, organized layout is essential for making a good first impression. Here are some key layout tips:

1.1. Simple and Clean Design

When formatting your resume, opt for simplicity. Avoid over-complicating the design with too many graphics, borders, or colors. The goal is to ensure that the content is easy to read, and the layout guides the reader’s eye through the document naturally. Using a clean, minimalistic layout ensures that your qualifications and experience are the focus.

1.2. Use Proper Margins and Spacing

Margins are crucial in maintaining a professional appearance. Typically, standard margins of 1 inch on all sides work best for both print and digital resumes. The spacing between sections and lines should be consistent, making the resume easy to read. For print resumes, ensure that your lines aren’t cramped together, as this could make the document feel dense and hard to digest.

1.3. Section Headings and Subheadings

Clear section headings such as “Experience,” “Education,” and “Skills” help organize your resume. Make sure the section headings stand out by using a larger font size or bold text. Subheadings can be used to further break down each section into more specific categories, such as “Technical Skills” or “Volunteer Work.”

2. Choose the Right Fonts for Print and Digital

The font you choose can dramatically affect the readability of your resume. Whether your resume is being read on a screen or printed out, readability should always be your top priority. Below are some guidelines for selecting the right fonts:

2.1. Opt for Professional Fonts

When selecting a font, choose something professional and easy to read. Avoid using overly decorative or quirky fonts, as they can come across as unprofessional. Some of the best fonts for resumes include:

  • Serif fonts: These fonts have small lines or strokes at the ends of letters, which can improve readability in printed documents. Examples include Times New Roman, Garamond, and Georgia.
  • Sans-serif fonts: These fonts are cleaner and more modern, making them ideal for digital resumes. Examples include Arial, Helvetica, and Calibri.

2.2. Font Size Considerations

The ideal font size for your resume text is between 10 and 12 points. This size ensures readability without looking too large or too small. Headings should be slightly larger—around 14 to 16 points—while your name at the top of the resume can be even larger to make it stand out.

2.3. Consistent Font Use

Stick to one font for the entire resume to maintain a uniform and professional look. Using multiple fonts can make the document look cluttered and disjointed. If you want to emphasize a section or line, use bold or italics rather than switching to a completely different font.

3. Optimize for Digital Viewing

While physical resumes are important, in today’s world, digital resumes are just as essential. The way your resume appears on a screen can affect how it is perceived by hiring managers and software systems. Here are some tips to optimize your resume for digital viewing:

3.1. Save as a PDF

When submitting your resume digitally, always save it as a PDF. PDFs preserve the layout, fonts, and design of your resume, ensuring that it looks the same across different devices and platforms. Additionally, PDF files are smaller in size, making them easier to email or upload to job boards.

3.2. Use a File Name That’s Clear and Professional

When naming your resume file, keep it simple and professional. Use your full name and the word “resume” in the file name, such as “JohnDoe_Resume.pdf.” Avoid generic names like “resume.pdf” or “myresume.pdf,” as these can get lost in a sea of other applicants’ documents.

3.3. Optimize for Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)

Many companies use Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to screen resumes before they reach hiring managers. These systems scan resumes for specific keywords and phrases relevant to the job description. To ensure that your resume passes through ATS, follow these tips:

  • Use simple formatting, avoiding tables, headers, and footers that might confuse the system.
  • Incorporate keywords from the job description naturally into your resume, particularly in the “Skills” and “Experience” sections.
  • Avoid using images, graphics, or unusual fonts, as these elements can disrupt ATS processing.

3.4. Hyperlinks for Digital Formats

If you’re submitting your resume digitally, it’s a great idea to include hyperlinks to your online portfolio, LinkedIn profile, or other relevant sites. These links make it easy for employers to learn more about you and your work.

  • Make sure the links are functional and clearly labeled (e.g., “LinkedIn Profile” or “Portfolio”).
  • Use URL shorteners if the link is long or cumbersome, but avoid them if they make the link look untrustworthy.

4. Optimize for Print Versions

While digital versions of resumes are essential, print resumes still play an important role in the job search process. Whether you’re attending a career fair or handing your resume directly to a hiring manager, having a well-formatted print version is crucial. Here are some key considerations for a print-friendly resume:

4.1. Print on High-Quality Paper

For the print version of your resume, use high-quality paper to ensure that the document feels professional. Opt for a smooth, white or off-white paper in a weight range of 24 to 32 pounds. Heavier paper tends to feel more substantial, and the crispness of the print will make your resume stand out.

4.2. Print in Color vs. Black and White

While printing your resume in color can make it more visually appealing, it’s generally best to stick to a black-and-white printout unless you’re applying for a creative role that calls for a more colorful design. A colorful resume can sometimes come off as too gimmicky, especially for more traditional industries.

4.3. Preview Before Printing

Before printing your resume, preview it to ensure that everything appears as intended. Make sure the text is legible, the layout is consistent, and no important information is cut off due to the page size. Printing out a test copy is an essential step to avoid costly mistakes.

5. Formatting Tips for Both Print and Digital Versions

Regardless of the format you’re using, some formatting best practices apply to both print and digital resumes:

5.1. Keep it Concise

Recruiters spend only a few seconds scanning a resume. Aim to keep your resume to one or two pages, focusing on the most relevant and impressive experience. If you have more than a decade of experience, you might extend it to two pages, but don’t exceed that length unless absolutely necessary.

5.2. Use Bullet Points for Readability

Bullet points are an excellent way to break down information, making it easy for readers to scan through key details. Use them to highlight accomplishments, skills, and responsibilities in each job role. Keep each bullet point short and impactful—try to limit them to one or two lines.

5.3. Tailor Your Resume for the Job

It’s essential to customize your resume for each job you apply to. This includes adjusting your skills and experience to match the specific job requirements, using the same terminology and keywords from the job description. Tailoring your resume not only increases your chances of passing ATS filters but also shows the employer that you’ve taken the time to understand the position.

6. Final Thoughts

Formatting your resume properly for both print and digital submission is a crucial step in your job search process. A well-organized, professional-looking resume will help you stand out in a competitive job market. By following these tips for layout, font choice, digital optimization, and print preparation, you’ll create a resume that’s optimized for both worlds. Remember, clarity, professionalism, and attention to detail are key to making a lasting impression on hiring managers.

With these guidelines in mind, you’ll be well on your way to crafting a resume that reflects your skills and experience in the best possible way, whether it’s printed out on paper or sent via email.
