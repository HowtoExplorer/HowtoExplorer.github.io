10 Ways to Stay Safe from Scams: Vigilant Consumers Hold the Power


Consumer scams come in all shapes and sizes. Whether you’re on the phone, surfing the internet, or out and about in the community, it pays to be vigilant. With some simple steps, you can protect yourself and help others stay safe as well. Here are ten practical measures to consider:

  1. Resist the pressure: If you’re urged to make an impromptu decision, especially with a guarantee of limited-time incredible offers, you’re likely dealing with a scam artist. Slow down, do your research, and don’t let sales pressure sway you.

  2. Verify the legitimacy: Before you share your personal information or make any financial transactions, make sure the company, individual, or organization is legitimate. Perform a web search, contact the local Better Business Bureau, or consult consumer protection agencies.

  3. Check for a physical address: Beware of businesses or individuals that don’t have a physical, verifiable address. Scammers are adept at using P.O. boxes, virtual offices, or non-existent addresses to hide their shady dealings.

  4. Guard your private information: Be cautious about sharing personal details such as social security numbers, financial account information, or passport numbers. Only provide this information when absolutely necessary and when dealing with trusted, verified parties.

  5. Stick to reputable retailers: Whenever possible, shop at well-known, established retailers, rather than working with unfamiliar businesses, especially those that operate solely through email or phone. Reach out to friends and family for recommendations or read reviews online.

  6. Get it in writing: When dealing with any business or individual, request all pertinent contractual agreements, receipts, or invoices be provided to you in writing. Review these documents thoroughly before proceeding.

  7. Do not pay upfront for guarantees: If someone promises to recover money for you or grant you some form of compensation, they may be attempting to scam you. In most cases, paying upfront for guarantees is a waste of time and could risk your financial security.

  8. Trust your gut: When something seems off or you feel uneasy about the transaction, trust your instincts. A nagging feeling may be your best defense against a scam.

  9. Know your rights: Becoming educated on consumer protection laws and your rights under these laws can help you recognize potential scams and navigate complaints more effectively.

  10. Stay informed: Scams evolve constantly, so remain vigilant and learn about new types of cons by keeping up with consumer news, alerts, and updates issued by local, national, and international consumer protection agencies.

Criminals prey on unsuspecting consumers, but vigilance is the best defense. Armed with knowledge and preparedness, you can protect your finances, your identity, and your peace of mind. Safeguard these valuable assets by keeping these ten key points in mind and by acting collaboratively with other consumers to stay one step ahead of scammers.
