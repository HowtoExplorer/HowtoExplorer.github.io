The Dos and Don'ts of Follow-Up Emails After an Interview


So, you’ve aced the interview, and now you’re wondering about the next steps. Should you follow up with a thank-you email? How soon is too soon? What should you say, and what should you avoid? Crafting a follow-up email after an interview can be a crucial step in leaving a positive impression on your potential employer. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the dos and don’ts of follow-up emails after an interview, providing you with valuable tips to help you navigate this essential post-interview etiquette.

The Dos

Express Gratitude

First and foremost, it’s crucial to express gratitude for the opportunity to interview for the position. A genuine thank-you goes a long way in showing your appreciation for the time and consideration the interviewer has given you. Remember to personalize your gratitude to reflect specific aspects of the interview that resonated with you.

Reiterate Interest

Use the follow-up email as an opportunity to reiterate your enthusiasm for the position and the company. Emphasize the aspects of the role and the organization that align with your career goals and values. This reaffirmation of interest can reinforce your commitment to the opportunity and demonstrate your proactive approach.

Reference Specific Points

Incorporate specific points from the interview into your email. Reflect on key discussions or topics that were covered during the interview to showcase your attentiveness and engagement. This personalized touch can help the interviewer recall your interaction and leave a lasting impression.

Provide Additional Information

If there were topics or projects discussed during the interview that you could further elaborate on, consider including additional information in your follow-up email. This could be in the form of relevant work samples, portfolio links, or any other materials that showcase your skills and expertise.

Follow-Up Timeline

Respectfully inquire about the timeline for the next steps in the hiring process. This demonstrates your eagerness to move forward while also allowing you to gain clarity on the expected timeframe for decisions.

The Don’ts

Avoid Generic Templates

Steer clear of using generic templates for your follow-up email. Personalization is key, and a generic, cookie-cutter message can come across as insincere. Tailor your email to reflect the specific details of your interview and your genuine feelings about the opportunity.

Don’t Be Pushy

While it’s essential to express your interest, it’s equally important not to come across as pushy or desperate. Avoid bombarding the interviewer with multiple follow-up emails in a short span of time. Respect the interviewer’s time and space while waiting for a response.

Refrain from Grammatical Errors

Take the time to proofread your email meticulously. Grammatical errors or typos can detract from the professionalism of your message. Ensure that your follow-up email is polished and error-free before hitting the send button.

Avoid Redundancy

While it’s acceptable to reiterate your interest, avoid repeating the same points excessively. Your follow-up email should provide new insights or information that adds value to your previous conversations.

Steer Clear of Demanding Language

Maintain a courteous and respectful tone throughout your follow-up email. Avoid using demanding language that may inadvertently put pressure on the interviewer. Your email should convey enthusiasm without crossing the line into assertiveness.

In conclusion, a well-crafted follow-up email can serve as a powerful tool in reinforcing your candidacy after an interview. By expressing gratitude, reiterating interest, and incorporating specific details from the interview, you can leave a positive and memorable impression. Conversely, avoiding generic templates, pushy language, and grammatical errors can help you steer clear of potential pitfalls. Remember, the follow-up email is your opportunity to stand out and leave a lasting impression, so approach it with thoughtfulness and professionalism. Good luck!
