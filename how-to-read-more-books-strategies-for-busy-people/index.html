How to Read More Books: Strategies for Busy People


In the hustle and bustle of modern life, carving out time to read books can feel like an impossible dream. However, reading remains one of the most rewarding habits. Books transport us to other worlds, expand our knowledge, and offer deep insights into the human experience. If you’re someone who’s constantly juggling responsibilities and struggling to fit reading into your schedule, don’t worry—there are strategies that can help you turn reading into an integral part of your daily life. It doesn’t matter how busy you are; with the right mindset and techniques, you can make reading a priority and reap its benefits.

Assess Your Priorities and Time

The first step in reading more books is taking an honest look at how you spend your time. Think about your daily habits: How much time do you spend on social media, streaming TV shows, or scrolling aimlessly on your phone? While these activities provide relaxation, they often siphon time that could be used for reading. Analyze your schedule and identify pockets of time where reading can fit seamlessly. Even a few minutes here and there can add up over weeks and months.

Reading doesn’t have to be a huge commitment all at once; small, consistent efforts are often more sustainable. For example, you might use your commute, lunch break, or downtime before bed to sneak in a few chapters. Tracking your daily activities, whether through a journal or a time-tracking app, can reveal patterns and hidden opportunities for reading.

Start with Manageable Goals

When you’re busy, the thought of tackling a 500-page novel can feel overwhelming. Start small. Set manageable and realistic reading goals that align with your schedule. For instance, commit to reading just 10 pages a day or 15 minutes before bed. These small chunks of reading time may seem insignificant, but they can lead to major progress over time.

If you’re new to reading or trying to rebuild the habit, consider starting with books that are easy to digest and pique your interest. Choose works that excite you—whether they’re fiction, biographies, or self-help. A story or topic that resonates with you will make reading feel less like a chore and more like a treat. These small wins can motivate you to keep going.

Always Keep a Book Handy

One of the easiest ways to read more is to make books accessible. Carry a physical book, e-reader, or audiobook app with you at all times. Waiting in a doctor’s office, standing in line, or taking a short bus ride can all become opportunities to read rather than idle moments spent scrolling through your phone. If you have a book within arm’s reach, you’re more likely to make use of those small gaps in your day.

E-readers and smartphone apps make it even easier by allowing you to carry an entire library in your pocket. Audiobooks, in particular, are a fantastic solution for multitasking. Whether you’re driving, cleaning, cooking, or working out, you can listen to a great story or learn something new by immersing yourself in an audiobook.

Incorporate Reading into Existing Habits

Humans are creatures of habit, and adding reading to habits you already have can make it a natural part of your routine. This strategy is sometimes called “habit stacking.” For example, if you already have a morning coffee ritual, pair it with reading a chapter of a book instead of scrolling through social media. If you’re in the habit of unwinding with TV at night, consider reading for 15 minutes first before switching on the screen.

Similarly, setting aside time for reading before or after an established routine like exercise, meditation, or dinner can help make it stick. Over time, incorporating reading into these moments will feel automatic, not forced.

Create a Distraction-Free Environment

One of the biggest challenges for busy people is staying focused while reading. Distractions—especially those from digital devices—can prevent deep engagement with a book. To get the most out of your reading time, create an environment that fosters concentration.

Consider designating a specific spot for reading, like a cozy chair or a quiet corner. Turn off notifications on your phone or put it in another room. If noise is an issue, noise-canceling headphones or instrumental music can provide the quiet you need to focus. Creating a distraction-free environment helps your mind fully immerse itself in the reading experience, maximizing your time and enjoyment.

Use Technology to Your Advantage

Technology often gets blamed for pulling us away from books, but when used strategically, it can be a tool to help you read more. E-readers like Kindle or Kobo offer convenience and portability, enabling you to carry multiple books wherever you go. Many also come with built-in features like highlighting and note-taking, which can enhance your reading experience.

Audiobook platforms such as Audible, Libby, or Scribd are excellent for listening on the go. If you’re a podcast enthusiast, transitioning to audiobooks might feel like a natural progression. Audiobooks aren’t just limited to fiction; they’re a great resource for non-fiction, memoirs, and self-help as well.

Timing apps or challenges, like Goodreads’ annual reading goals, can keep you motivated and give you a sense of accomplishment as you complete each book. Digital libraries and e-book subscriptions can also remove cost barriers, giving you access to countless books without breaking the bank.

Mix Up Your Reading Material

Reading doesn’t always have to mean plowing through dense novels or academic tomes. Diversifying your reading choices can keep things fresh and help you read more. For example, alternate between fiction and non-fiction to avoid burnout.

Throw in short stories, essays, or poetry collections for quicker reads that can often be completed in one sitting. Magazines, journals, and even high-quality online articles can also count toward your reading goals, offering valuable insights and perspectives. When you’re busy, variety can make reading feel like an adventure instead of another item on your to-do list.

Join a Reading Community or Book Club

Sometimes, having accountability can make all the difference in sticking to a habit. Joining a reading group, whether in person or online, can motivate you to read more. Book clubs offer not only the opportunity to discuss what you’ve read but also a sense of community and shared purpose. Knowing that others are counting on you to finish a book can be a strong driver to prioritize reading.

If a traditional book club feels too demanding, consider joining an online platform like Goodreads or engaging in reading challenges on social media. These forums provide the flexibility to move at your own pace while still connecting with fellow readers.

Learn to Let Go of Books That Don’t Resonate

One common pitfall that prevents many people from reading consistently is feeling obligated to finish every book they start. Life is too short to read books that don’t capture your interest or add value. If a book isn’t engaging you, it’s okay to put it down and try something else.

Think of books as conversations—sometimes, the topic just isn’t for you, and that’s fine. By moving on, you free up time for books that truly resonate and make reading feel enjoyable rather than like homework. Remember: there’s no shame in being selective about your reading material.

Make Reading a Treat, Not a Task

Finally, the most important advice for reading more books as a busy person is to make the experience enjoyable. Reading should never feel like a chore or something on your to-do list; it should feel like a mental escape or an investment in personal growth. Celebrate small victories, like finishing a chapter or completing a book. Talk about what you’re reading with friends or jot down your thoughts in a journal to deepen your engagement.

By creating a positive association with reading, the habit becomes something you look forward to rather than something you feel pressured to do. Let books become your retreat from the noise and chaos of daily life—a safe space where you can explore, learn, and grow.

With these strategies in place, even the busiest person can find the time to read more books. It all comes down to intention, consistency, and finding ways to integrate reading into your already-packed schedule. Start with a small step today: pick up a book, carve out a few quiet minutes, and let the magic of stories and ideas take you away. Reading doesn’t just make you a better thinker—it gives you the tools to lead a richer, more connected life.
