Best TV Shows of the Decade: A Look Back at Iconic Series


The past decade has been a transformative era for television, ushering in a golden age of storytelling that has captivated audiences across the globe. With the rise of streaming giants, groundbreaking narratives, and a push for more diverse and inclusive casts, TV has never been more dynamic or impactful. From intricate dramas to hilarious comedies and mind-bending science fiction, the 2010s and early 2020s have produced some of the most iconic series of all time. Let’s dive into the shows that defined the decade, exploring what made them resonate with viewers and why they continue to leave their mark.

Drama Redefined: The Power of Complex Storytelling

Game of Thrones: A Phenomenon in Narrative Scale

Few series captured the cultural zeitgeist like Game of Thrones. Adapted from George R.R. Martin’s A Song of Ice and Fire, this fantasy epic enthralled audiences with its intricate political intrigues, morally ambiguous characters, and jaw-dropping twists. While debates about its later seasons persist, there’s no denying the monumental impact this series had on global audiences. Its sprawling world-building and taboo-breaking storytelling proved that genre television could transcend niche appeal and become a mainstream powerhouse.

Breaking Bad: The Antihero at His Best

Vince Gilligan’s Breaking Bad became a masterclass in character development and tension-building. Tracking Walter White’s transformation from a mild-mannered high school teacher to a ruthless drug kingpin, this series explored the darker sides of human ambition. Bryan Cranston and Aaron Paul’s performances elevated the show into a cultural touchstone, with unforgettable moments that left viewers on the edge of their seats. Its final season, in particular, is often hailed as one of the most satisfying conclusions in TV history.

The Crown: A Royal Journey

Netflix found a gem in The Crown, a historical drama chronicling the life and reign of Queen Elizabeth II. With its lavish production values, meticulous attention to real-world events, and compelling performances from Claire Foy, Olivia Colman, and Imelda Staunton, the show provided a nuanced look at power, duty, and familial turmoil. The Crown stood out for its ability to blend personal drama with sweeping historical narratives, enticing audiences with each installment.

Comedy Gold: Laughter in Modern Times

The Office and Parks and Recreation: Comfort in Chaos

The mockumentary-style sitcoms The Office and Parks and Recreation continued to collect fans during the early part of the decade through reruns and streaming platforms. The Office, with its relatable workplace humor and endearing characters, solidified its place as a cultural phenomenon. Meanwhile, Parks and Recreation charmed audiences with its heartwarming take on small-town bureaucracy, led by Amy Poehler’s iconic portrayal of Leslie Knope.

Ted Lasso: Optimism as a Remedy

In a time when cynicism seemed to dominate the cultural narrative, Ted Lasso arrived as a breath of fresh air. The story of a relentlessly optimistic American football coach navigating the world of English soccer became an unlikely hit, winning hearts with its humor and kindness. The series tackled issues like mental health, identity, and personal growth, all while delivering expertly crafted comedic moments.

Schitt’s Creek: From Humble Beginnings to International Adoration

Initially a sleeper hit, Schitt’s Creek gained momentum through word of mouth and awards season buzz. Following the journey of the wealthy Rose family as they adjust to life in a small, quirky town they once purchased as a joke, the show offered both humor and heartfelt moments. Dan Levy’s portrayal of David Rose and Catherine O’Hara’s unforgettable performance as Moira cemented its place among the best comedies of the era.

Science Fiction and Fantasy Take Center Stage

Stranger Things: Nostalgia Meets Thrills

Netflix’s Stranger Things became an instant sensation upon its release, blending 1980s nostalgia with supernatural thrills. The story of a group of friends in the fictional town of Hawkins battling otherworldly forces tapped into a collective love for Spielbergian adventure. Its charming young cast, heart-pounding mysteries, and emotionally resonant storytelling ensured its spot as a defining show of the decade.

The Mandalorian: Revitalizing a Galaxy Far, Far Away

Launching alongside Disney+, The Mandalorian revitalized the Star Wars franchise with its episodic storytelling and Western-inspired themes. Mando and Baby Yoda (officially named Grogu) became household names, earning the love of both longtime Star Wars fans and newcomers alike. The show’s ability to balance intimate character moments with action-packed sequences brought new life to a beloved universe.

Black Mirror: A Dark Reflection of Society

Black Mirror delivered chilling cautionary tales about the impact of technology on humanity. Each standalone episode served as a thought-provoking commentary on the potential perils of innovation, often leaving audiences unsettled but deeply reflective. While not all entries received equal acclaim, standout episodes like “San Junipero” and “USS Callister” showcased the anthology’s ability to blend social critique with compelling storytelling.

Diverse Voices Transform the Landscape

Fleabag: A Masterpiece of Emotional Honesty

Phoebe Waller-Bridge’s Fleabag redefined what a dramedy could be. With its razor-sharp wit and innovative use of fourth-wall breaks, the series offered an intimate exploration of grief, relationships, and self-discovery. The second season, featuring an electric performance by Andrew Scott as the “Hot Priest,” elevated the show to cult-classic status, earning universal acclaim for its emotional depth.

Atlanta: Surrealism Meets Social Commentary

Donald Glover’s Atlanta stood out as one of the most inventive shows of the decade. Blending humor, surrealism, and sharp social critique, the series followed the lives of aspiring rapper Paper Boi and his manager Earn as they navigated Atlanta’s music scene. With episodes that veered into experimental storytelling, Atlanta continually pushed the boundaries of what television could achieve, making it a standout in the era.

Pose: Celebrating Love and Resilience

Ryan Murphy’s Pose brought underrepresented communities to the forefront, shining a spotlight on the 1980s and 1990s ballroom culture in New York City. With a predominantly LGBTQ+ and POC cast, the series celebrated resilience and love in the face of adversity. Its groundbreaking representation and emotional storytelling made it a critical and cultural milestone.

Documentary Series: Truth is Stranger Than Fiction

Making a Murderer: A Cultural Obsession

True crime surged in popularity thanks in part to Netflix’s Making a Murderer. This gripping series investigated the controversial case of Steven Avery, sparking widespread debate and public fascination. Its intricate exploration of the legal system and its failures invited viewers to consider deeper questions of justice and fairness.

Tiger King: The Wild Ride

When the world was gripped by lockdowns, Tiger King emerged as the ultimate cultural distraction. Joe Exotic and Carole Baskin’s strange and chaotic feud became must-watch television, offering a bizarre blend of humor, tragedy, and absurdity. Few shows captured the collective subconscious quite like this documentary did.

The Legacy of the Decade’s TV Landscape

The last decade changed the way we consume and appreciate television forever. Streaming platforms allowed for unprecedented creative freedom, fostering unique storytelling and breaking taboos. Shows like Game of Thrones, Ted Lasso, and Stranger Things became rallying points for collective fandom, while series like Fleabag and Pose proved that deeply personal stories could resonate universally.

These TV shows weren’t just entertainment; they reflected the culture, values, and challenges of the time. Whether celebrating diversity, exploring the consequences of modern technology, or simply bringing us laughter when we needed it most, the best series of the last decade have left an indelible mark on both pop culture and viewers’ hearts. Looking back, it’s clear that this era of television will be remembered for its innovation, emotional resonance, and ability to unite audiences around the world.
