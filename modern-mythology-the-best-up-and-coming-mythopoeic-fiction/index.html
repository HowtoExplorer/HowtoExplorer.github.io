Modern Mythology: The Best Up-and-Coming Mythopoeic Fiction


In a world where old myths and legends have become distant memories, a new wave of mythopoeic fiction is emerging on the horizon. Drawing inspiration from both classic stories and contemporary issues, these new tales breathe life into ancient traditions while creating fresh, innovative worlds. This blog post will showcase some of the most captivating and noteworthy up-and-coming mythopoeic fiction creators that are shaping the future of modern storytelling.

1. Kelly Barnhill - “The Girl Who Drank the Moon”

Published in 2016, Kelly Barnhill’s “The Girl Who Drank the Moon” won the Newbery Medal and captivated readers with its enchanting storytelling. Drawing inspiration from Slavic and Eastern European folklore, Barnhill weaves a tale of a young girl raised by a kind witch, a swamp monster, and a tiny dragon. This remarkable book highlights themes of love, acceptance and the power of myth to shape our understanding of the world.

2. Mark Lawrence - “The Red Queen’s War” Trilogy

British author Mark Lawrence presents a daring blend of dark fantasy and political intrigue with his “Red Queen’s War” trilogy. Set in a version of the Mediterranean in a world where dragons rule as the ultimate authority, the trilogy follows a young, unassuming man as he navigates court politics, espionage, and the age-old battle between two ancient empires. Lawrence’s keen understanding of his diverse array of deities and pantheons gives his mythopoeic fiction additional depth and authenticity.

3. Roshani Chokshi - “The Star-Touched Queen” and “A Crown of Wishes”

Roshani Chokshi taps into ancient Hindu cosmology to create vivid, multiethnic worlds in her fantasy novels “The Star-Touched Queen” and “A Crown of Wishes.” Centered around a magical love story, both novels present a tapestry of rich, vibrant folklore that will captivate readers until the very last page.

4. T. Kingfisher - “What the Cat Dragged In”

T. Kingfisher, also known as Ursula Vernon, brings a light-hearted approach to mythopoeic fiction with her novel “What the Cat Dragged In.” Influenced by Slavic mythology and magic realism, the story follows the tale of Dr. Anders More, a man who inadvertently finds himself as the protector of an ancient forest. Vernon’s dry wit and signature storytelling create a unique spin on timeless legends.

5. Laini Taylor - “The Daughter of Smoke and Bone” Trilogy

Laini Taylor’s “Daughter of Smoke and Bone” trilogy effortlessly blends elements of Norse, Egyptian, and Slavic mythology in a spellbinding tale of love, war, and the hidden forces that govern our world. The trilogy follows Karou, a young art student by day and an errand girl for monstrous creatures by night, as she unravels the hidden secrets of her past and embarks on a journey to save her people.

6. N.K. Jemisin - “The Inheritance Trilogy”

N.K. Jemisin’s “Inheritance Trilogy” delves deep into African mythology and reimagines classic tales of gods, demons, and heroes. The novels, “The Inheritance of Lorries, “The Kingdom of Gods,” and “The Broken Kingdoms,” explore the complexities of power dynamics in a world dominated by divine beings and offer a fresh perspective on the timeless stories of yore.

7. Silvia Moreno-Garcia - “Signal to Noise”

Silvia Moreno-Garcia pays homage to classic Mexican myths and legends with her novel “Signal to Noise.” The story follows Meche, a girl who comes to terms with her own identity and roots as she explores a world in the shadow of the past. Moreno-Garcia blends together magical realism and mythic elements seamlessly, creating a transportive experience for the reader.

As these talented authors continue to weave their spellbinding tales, the mythopoeic fiction genre will undoubtedly continue to grow and flourish. For lovers of mythology and fantasy alike, these works are a must-read, offering fresh perspectives on stories we thought we knew and delivering a wealth of enchantment and wonder to avid readers.

Remember to explore this fascinating literary world and find the legends that have captured your heart. In storytelling, there is always room for the old to meld with the new, and it’s these fusions that breathe life into our tales and traditions. So, immerse yourself in the many worlds this genre has to offer and see where your imagination takes you.
