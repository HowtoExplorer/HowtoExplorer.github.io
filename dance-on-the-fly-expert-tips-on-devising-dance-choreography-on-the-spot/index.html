Dance on the Fly: Expert Tips on Devising Dance Choreography on the Spot


Have you ever found yourself in an impromptu dance performance situation, with no prior preparation or planning? It’s a challenge that every dancer faces at some point in their career, and it takes not only skill and creativity but also a great deal of improvisation and flexibility. In today’s blog post, we shall explore the world of improvisational dance and provide some expert tips on how to dance on the fly, as well as devise dance choreography on the spot.

Dancing on the fly calls for a unique set of skills, and it may not come naturally to every dancer. But by honing our creativity, adaptability, and teamwork, even the most unprepared dance performances can become engaging, captivating spectacles.

  1. Get in tune with your emotions and surroundings

As dancers, we often rely on the music as our guide and the structure of the choreography to keep us on track. However, in spontaneous dance performances, we need to be more attuned to our emotions and surroundings. By connecting with your environment and the audience’s energy, you can work with the environment to create a unique performance each time.

  1. Incorporate wings and staging

Wings are small, improvised steps or gestures that help communicate a movement or emotion in the moment, while staging refers to the use and arrangement of spatial position during a dance performance. Incorporating these techniques on the fly can transform an average dance into an incredible one, giving it structure, depth, and character.

  1. Leverage props and costume elements

Props and costumes are not only used for stage performances and musical theater but can also be invaluable tools when it comes to spontaneous dance performance. They can provide inspiration or serve as catalysts for movements, as well as help you establish themes and evoke emotions with your dance.

  1. Collaborate and learn from your dance partners

When you find yourself in a spontaneous dance situation with fellow dancers, remember that collaboration is key. Discussing the mood, theme, and the desired outcome of the performance will help reach a consensus and pool your creativity. Take cues from each other, as watching a fellow dancer may trigger an idea, inspire a movement or provide a game-changing moment for the impromptu performance.

  1. Practice improvisation techniques

Improvisation workshops and classes can significantly improve your ability to dance on the fly. These classes often employ selected exercises to develop your technical, musical, and theatrical skills, fostering independence and communication between partners. Whether it’s a class focused on contact improv or African dance improvisation techniques, these structured improvisation exercises will enhance your skills and prepare you for on-the-spot choreography.

  1. Train regularly

Dedication to regular dance training is crucial if you aim to excel at impromptu dancing. Dance classes, workshops, and other training opportunities allow you to expand your technical fluency, gain a deeper understanding of artistic expressions, and practice working with various music styles.

  1. Create a library of ideas and lyrics

Building a repertoire of choreographic ideas, movements, and lyrics on different music styles and dance genres can be an invaluable resource when called upon to dance on the fly. Save them in your memory or write them down in your choreography notebook. When the moment strikes, you’ll have plenty of options ready to bring to life in that split-second.

  1. Confidence is key

Lastly, it is crucial to have confidence in your abilities when you are faced with the task of devising dance choreography on the spot. With trust in yourself and your fellow dancers, it is far easier to let go and deliver an engaging, creative, and memorable performance.

Remember, dancing on the fly comes with experience and practice. Embrace those moments of improvisation, and let your creativity run wild. You never know, they could be the moments you look back on and feel most alive, truly dancing with your soul.
