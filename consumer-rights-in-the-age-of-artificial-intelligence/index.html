Consumer Rights in the Age of Artificial Intelligence


As artificial intelligence continues to revolutionize the way we live and work, consumers are increasingly vulnerable to manipulation and exploitation. The rapid advancement of AI raises fundamental questions about consumer rights and protections in an era where machines are making decisions on our behalf. Our data, which has become the currency of the digital age, is being collected, stored, and used in ways we might not even be aware of. This opaque landscape of data collection has led to institutions, governments, and corporations adopting AI-driven decision-making, limiting our ability to make informed choices and hold these entities accountable for their actions. It is crucial that we understand the risks and potential consequences of AI on our consumer rights. Data Empires: The Unseen Players in Our Lives Our personal data has become a valuable resource, with AI-driven systems leveraging this data to make decisions that often affect us directly. AI algorithms are trained on vast amounts of data, extracted from a variety of sources including our social media, online browsing habits, and purchasing patterns. This information is used to tailor advertisements, influence our purchasing behavior, and even manipulate our beliefs and attitudes. However, this process is largely unregulated, creating a power imbalance between consumers and the companies involved in the data-driven decision-making process. These new players, such as internet service providers and data brokers, are beginning to supplant traditional organizations in the corridors of power. Without consequence or accountability, their influence reaches far beyond the market dynamics of the past, casting a shadow on institutions and governments alike. The vulnerability of our data is, in essence, giving these new powers control over our lives. Green Light for Predatory Practices In a data-driven world, consumer protections are rapidly becoming built-in obsolescence. Disclosure laws, once designed to safeguard consumer information, now fail to keep pace with the rapid evolution of AI-driven practices. The dark corners of AI systems mean that the areas where our data is being used are straight out within regulation's public oversight. As both parties misuse this information, there is room for abuse to run amok. Rights of the consumer that once were central to sense of fairness and safety in markets are dented by emergence of AI dependency. State and local regulators often debate comprehensive regulation at forums dominated by major players, thus leaving confusion in the air about legal actions they can take in practice, rather than principle. These wheels are then left turning with due care, too slowly and with little collective action resulting for anything drastic to occur.
