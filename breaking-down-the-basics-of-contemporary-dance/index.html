Breaking Down the Basics of Contemporary Dance


Contemporary dance is an incredibly dynamic and expressive genre of dance, blending elements of various styles such as ballet, modern, jazz, and more. As its name suggests, it is a style that has evolved over time, embracing innovation and experimentation. Whether you’re a dancer or simply interested in the art, understanding the fundamentals of contemporary dance can help you appreciate its beauty and enjoy it to the fullest. Below, we delve into the key concepts you should know to get started on this dance journey.

  1. Centers and weight shifts

In contemporary dance, weight shifts and centers play a crucial role in building movement sequences and exploring dynamic relationships between the dancer and their surroundings. Essentially, a center refers to the part of the body from which movement originates – it could be the pelvis, the chest, or a combination of these and other body parts. Shifting your weight from one foot to another, or from the ground to the air, can create different sensations and expressions, reflecting emotions and moods in your dance.

  1. Originality and creativity

One of the core tenets of contemporary dance is the emphasis on creativity and personal expression. While classical and traditional styles often adhere to specific rules and patterns, contemporary dance encourages improvisation, experimentation, and pushing boundaries. As you learn to navigate the dance world, don’t shy away from exploring your unique movement vocabulary and let your creativity flourish. Seeing things from a new perspective is what makes contemporary dance vibrant and ever-evolving.

  1. Fluidity and smooth transitions

As contemporary dance often incorporates elements of modern and jazz, fluidity is a key aspect of its movements. Smooth transitions, an absence of jarring stop-and-go motions, and connecting movements create a continuous, flowing quality in dance. To achieve this fluidity, dancers must focus not only on technique but also on the relationships between body parts and their coordination. This interconnectedness results in a natural, free-flowing performance.

  1. Emotional expression

Contemporary dance often merges music, acting, and dance to create a multi-layered performance that sparks the audience’s emotions. The dancers use their bodies to tell stories without words, conveying emotions through physical movements. As such, contemporary dance encourages you to dive deep into your inner self and breathe life into your feelings.

  1. Musicality and rhythm

Dancers in contemporary styles must possess strong musicality and rhythmic sensibilities. Understanding and articulating rhythm, harmony, and tempo through body movements makes the dance piece more impactful and engaging. Additionally, working with various musical styles, tempos, and beats can add depth and variety to your choreography.

  1. Building an emotional connection with your audience

As contemporary dance seeks to captivate and resonate with viewers on an emotional level, establishing a connection between the dancers and their audience is crucial. Engagement with the audience can include things like maintaining eye contact, incorporating interactions with audience members, or storytelling through movements. Regardless of the specific approach, the goal is to make the viewers feel something, whether it’s joy, sorrow, or intrigue.

By mastering these core concepts, aspiring contemporary dancers can set the stage for their growth and evolution within this captivating art form. So, set your creative muscles warm up and get ready to explore the abundant possibilities that contemporary dance has to offer.
