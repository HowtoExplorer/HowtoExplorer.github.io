10 DIY Projects to Spruce Up Your Entryway


Your entryway is the first thing people see when they visit your home, and it sets the tone for the rest of the space. Whether it’s a grand foyer or a small hallway, the entryway serves as a crucial transitional area that welcomes you and your guests. However, many people often overlook the potential of this space. If you’re looking for a way to transform your entryway into a stylish and functional area, a few DIY projects can make all the difference. Here are 10 creative and easy-to-do projects that will not only enhance the aesthetics of your entryway but also add much-needed practicality.

1. Create a Statement Wall with Bold Paint or Wallpaper

One of the simplest ways to transform your entryway is by adding a statement wall. Choose a bold color or wallpaper design that sets the tone for the rest of your home. Opt for deep, rich colors like navy, emerald green, or charcoal gray, or go for something vibrant and energetic, such as a warm mustard or deep coral.

If you’re not ready to commit to full coverage, consider using wallpaper as an accent behind a console table or mirror. This adds texture and intrigue without overwhelming the space. For those with a smaller entryway, light colors and delicate patterns can make the area feel larger and more open.

2. Install a Floating Shelf for Extra Storage

A floating shelf is a great solution to add functional storage without taking up too much space. It’s perfect for placing small decorative items like plants, candles, or framed photos. Alternatively, you can use it to keep your everyday essentials, such as keys, wallets, or mail.

By installing a floating shelf above a console table or coat rack, you can keep your entryway tidy and organized. You can even paint or stain the shelf to match your entryway’s theme, whether that’s rustic, modern, or traditional. This simple addition not only saves space but also contributes to a clean and inviting atmosphere.

3. DIY Coat Rack for Practicality and Style

A coat rack is a must-have for any entryway. Instead of purchasing a mass-produced coat rack, consider creating your own with a unique design. A rustic wooden coat rack, made from reclaimed wood, can lend a cozy, farmhouse vibe to your entryway, while sleek metal hooks can give it a more modern and industrial look.

To create your own coat rack, you can repurpose old wooden planks or create a design using metal pipes or even tree branches. Add hooks at various heights for versatility, allowing you to store coats, bags, hats, and even scarves. This simple project not only keeps your entryway organized but also adds a touch of personality.

4. Make Your Own Shoe Rack

Shoe clutter can easily take over an entryway, especially in households with multiple people. A DIY shoe rack is an effective and stylish solution to keep footwear organized and out of sight. You can customize the size and design of your shoe rack to fit your space and needs.

A simple wooden shoe rack made of pallets or reclaimed wood can give your entryway a rustic feel. If you’re short on space, a narrow vertical shoe rack can be a great option, or you can install wall-mounted cubby shelves for a more modern touch. You could even add a cushioned seat on top of the rack to create a convenient place for putting on shoes.

5. Repurpose Old Furniture for a Vintage Look

For a unique and personal touch, repurpose an old piece of furniture into something functional for your entryway. For example, an old wooden chest can be turned into a storage bench, offering both a place to sit and extra room for shoes or scarves. A vintage side table can be upcycled into a small entry console, perfect for holding keys and mail.

Don’t shy away from mixing old with new. Incorporating repurposed furniture can give your entryway character and charm, while also reducing waste. Plus, these custom pieces make your home feel more personal and thoughtfully designed.

6. Add a DIY Rug or Doormat

A rug or doormat is an essential part of any entryway, both for aesthetic and practical reasons. You can easily craft your own DIY rug or personalized doormat to bring some warmth and color to your space. If you’re feeling crafty, you can even use fabric scraps, yarn, or old towels to create a unique woven or braided rug.

For a more simple project, purchase a plain doormat and customize it with stencils, paint, or fabric appliqués. You can personalize it with your family name, a fun quote, or even a seasonal design. A welcoming doormat adds a charming touch to your entryway while also serving a functional purpose by helping to trap dirt and moisture.

7. Install Wall-Mounted Lighting Fixtures

Proper lighting is crucial for any entryway, as it ensures the space feels inviting and well-lit. Wall-mounted lighting fixtures, such as sconces, can brighten up the area and add visual interest to your walls. This DIY project can be as simple as mounting a pair of decorative sconces on either side of your entry mirror or installing a modern, minimalist light fixture above a console table.

Not only do lighting fixtures serve a functional purpose, but they can also enhance the overall style of your entryway. Choose fixtures that complement the color scheme and design of the room to create a cohesive look. For a more customized touch, consider painting the sconces in a color that matches your walls or furniture.

8. Create a Mirror Wall or Gallery

Mirrors are not only practical for checking your appearance before heading out the door, but they also have the ability to make a small entryway feel larger and more open. You can create a mirror wall or gallery by arranging multiple mirrors in different sizes and shapes. This DIY project allows you to get creative with the layout and framing of the mirrors.

Opt for mirrors with interesting frames—such as antique, vintage, or modern styles—depending on the look you’re going for. You can also mix in a few decorative items like artwork or photos to complete the gallery effect. The addition of mirrors reflects light, making your entryway feel brighter and more spacious.

9. Build a Custom Entry Table

An entry table is one of the most versatile pieces of furniture you can have in your entryway. Whether it’s a sleek console table or a more traditional piece, this item can hold everything from keys to wallets to decorative accessories. For a fun DIY project, build your own entry table with a custom design.

If you’re looking for a more rustic or industrial look, you can use reclaimed wood and metal pipes to create a unique table. Alternatively, if you want something more modern, a minimalist table with clean lines and a sleek finish may be more appropriate. You can even add some creative touches, such as decorative drawer pulls, to make the piece truly unique.

10. Design Your Own Plant Display

Plants are an easy way to bring life into any space, and your entryway is no exception. Adding greenery not only enhances the aesthetics but also improves air quality. There are several ways you can incorporate plants into your entryway with a DIY approach.

Create a stylish plant display by using vintage crates, tiered shelves, or macramé plant hangers. Place your plants in unique containers like baskets, glass jars, or colorful ceramic pots to add a personalized touch. Choose plants that thrive in low-light conditions, such as snake plants, pothos, or ZZ plants, if your entryway doesn’t get much natural light.

Final Thoughts

Transforming your entryway into a welcoming and functional space doesn’t have to require a huge budget or professional help. With a few simple DIY projects, you can add personality, style, and practicality to one of the most important areas of your home. Whether you’re adding a statement wall, building custom furniture, or incorporating greenery, these projects are sure to make your entryway stand out and impress guests before they even step inside.

So grab your tools, unleash your creativity, and get ready to give your entryway the makeover it deserves!
