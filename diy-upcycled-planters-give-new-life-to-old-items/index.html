DIY Upcycled Planters: Give New Life to Old Items


Are you looking for a creative way to spruce up your indoor or outdoor space? Look no further than DIY upcycled planters! These unique and eco-friendly planters provide a fantastic opportunity to give new life to old items that may otherwise end up in the trash. Not only will you be reducing waste, but you’ll also be adding a touch of charm and personality to your plants. In this blog post, we’ll explore some exciting ideas for DIY upcycled planters that will inspire you to get your hands dirty and unleash your creativity.

Tin Can Planters

One of the simplest and most cost-effective upcycled planters is using tin cans. Whether it’s an old soup can or a coffee tin, these versatile containers can be transformed into stunning planters with just a few simple steps. Start by cleaning the can thoroughly and removing any labels. Then, punch a few drainage holes in the bottom using a nail and hammer. Fill the can with potting soil and plant your favorite herbs, flowers, or succulents. You can even paint the cans to add a pop of color to your space.

Mason Jar Terrariums

Mason jars have become a staple in the world of upcycling, and they make fantastic planters for creating beautiful terrariums. To make a mason jar terrarium, start by layering small stones or pebbles at the bottom for drainage. Add activated charcoal to keep the soil fresh, preventing any unwanted odors. Then, add a layer of potting soil and carefully arrange your choice of plants, such as moss, ferns, or air plants. Finish off by lightly misting the plants with water and sealing the jar. These charming terrariums are not only visually appealing but also require minimal maintenance.

Old Tires Flower Beds

If you have old tires lying around and want to add a unique touch to your garden, consider turning them into flower beds. Start by cleaning the tires thoroughly and removing any debris. Paint the tires in vibrant colors to add a playful element to your outdoor space. Once the paint is dry, fill the tires with nutrient-rich soil and plant your favorite flowers or even vegetables. Stack the tires in different configurations to create an eye-catching display. Not only will this upcycling project add visual interest to your garden, but it will also help prevent the tires from ending up in a landfill.

Vintage Suitcase Planters

Do you have an old vintage suitcase gathering dust in your attic? Why not turn it into a unique planter? Open the suitcase and line it with plastic to protect it from moisture. Create drainage holes in the plastic to prevent waterlogging. Fill the suitcase with potting soil and plant a variety of flowers or even a small herb garden. This repurposed suitcase planter will add a touch of nostalgia and whimsy to your space.

Wooden Pallet Vertical Garden

Wooden pallets are incredibly versatile when it comes to upcycling projects, and creating a vertical garden is no exception. Start by cleaning the pallet and sanding it down to remove any splinters. Paint the pallet in a color that complements your outdoor space or leave it natural for a rustic look. Attach landscape fabric or weed barrier to the back and sides of the pallet to hold the soil in place. Fill each opening with potting soil and plant a variety of herbs, flowers, or succulents. Lean the pallet against a wall or fence, and watch your vertical garden come to life.

Conclusion

DIY upcycled planters offer a fantastic opportunity to give new life to old items while adding a unique touch to your indoor and outdoor spaces. Whether you choose to transform tin cans, mason jars, old tires, vintage suitcases, or wooden pallets, the possibilities are endless. These eco-friendly planters not only help reduce waste but also allow you to unleash your creativity and showcase your green thumb. So, gather your supplies, let your imagination run wild, and start creating your own upcycled planters today!
