The Best Fashion Documentaries to Watch for Inspiration


Fashion is more than just clothing; it’s a form of self-expression, a reflection of culture, and an ever-evolving art form. For those who are passionate about fashion, documentaries offer a unique glimpse into the lives of designers, the intricacies of the industry, and the stories behind iconic pieces. Whether you’re a budding designer, a fashion enthusiast, or simply someone looking for inspiration, these documentaries will provide you with a wealth of knowledge and creativity. Here’s a curated list of the best fashion documentaries that you should watch for inspiration.

1. The September Issue (2009)

Overview:
This documentary takes you behind the scenes of Vogue magazine, focusing on the preparation for the September 2007 issue, which is the most important issue of the year for fashion magazines. It features Anna Wintour, the editor-in-chief, and her team as they navigate the challenges of producing a publication that sets the tone for the fashion season.

Why Watch It:
The film provides insight into the high-stakes world of fashion publishing and the creative process behind curating a magazine. It showcases the tension between artistic vision and commercial viability, making it a must-watch for anyone interested in the intersection of fashion and media.

2. Dior and I (2014)

Overview:
This documentary follows Raf Simons as he takes the helm of the iconic fashion house Dior. It chronicles his first haute couture collection, revealing the intense pressure and creativity involved in the process.

Why Watch It:
“Dior and I” offers a rare look at the inner workings of a luxury fashion house, highlighting the collaboration between designers and artisans. It’s a beautiful exploration of creativity, craftsmanship, and the emotional journey of a designer, making it a source of inspiration for aspiring fashion creators.

3. McQueen (2018)

Overview:
This documentary delves into the life and career of the late Alexander McQueen, one of the most influential designers of our time. It explores his groundbreaking collections, personal struggles, and the impact he had on the fashion industry.

Why Watch It:
“McQueen” is not just a fashion documentary; it’s a poignant story of artistry, innovation, and tragedy. It showcases McQueen’s genius and his ability to challenge norms, making it a powerful source of inspiration for anyone looking to push boundaries in their own work.

4. The First Monday in May (2016)

Overview:
This documentary provides an inside look at the planning of the Met Gala, one of the most prestigious events in the fashion calendar. It focuses on the 2015 exhibition “China: Through the Looking Glass” and the challenges faced by the organizers.

Why Watch It:
The film highlights the intersection of fashion and art, showcasing how cultural themes can influence design. It’s a fascinating exploration of the creative process behind one of fashion’s biggest nights, offering insights into the collaboration between designers, artists, and curators.

5. Bill Cunningham New York (2010)

Overview:
This documentary follows the life of Bill Cunningham, a legendary fashion photographer for The New York Times. It captures his unique perspective on fashion and his dedication to documenting street style.

Why Watch It:
Bill Cunningham’s passion for fashion and his ability to find beauty in everyday life is truly inspiring. This film encourages viewers to appreciate the art of fashion beyond the runway and to recognize the creativity present in everyday attire.

6. The True Cost (2015)

Overview:
This documentary examines the fashion industry’s impact on the environment and the lives of garment workers. It raises important questions about sustainability and ethical practices in fashion.

Why Watch It:
“The True Cost” is a thought-provoking film that challenges viewers to consider the consequences of their fashion choices. It inspires a more conscious approach to fashion, encouraging individuals to think about sustainability and ethical consumption.

7. Advanced Style (2014)

Overview:
This documentary celebrates the style of older women in New York City, showcasing their unique fashion choices and the stories behind them. It highlights the idea that style knows no age.

Why Watch It:
“Advanced Style” is a joyful celebration of individuality and self-expression. It inspires viewers to embrace their personal style, regardless of age, and to find confidence in their fashion choices.

8. The Gospel According to André (2018)

Overview:
This documentary tells the story of André Leon Talley, a fashion icon and former editor-at-large for Vogue. It explores his journey from a small town in North Carolina to the heights of the fashion world.

Why Watch It:
Talley’s story is one of resilience, creativity, and passion. His unique perspective on fashion and culture offers valuable insights, making this documentary a source of inspiration for anyone looking to carve their own path in the industry.
