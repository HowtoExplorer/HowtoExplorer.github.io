The Best Literary Podcasts to Tune Into


In the world of literature, the boundaries of storytelling are constantly being pushed. With the rise of podcasting, authors, writers, and readers alike can connect with each other like never before. From literary fiction to poetry, there's a podcast out there that's sure to satisfy your cravings. Here are some of the best literary podcasts to tune into:

  1. The Read Hosted by Kid Fury and Crissle West, The Read is a witty and incisive podcast that delves deep into the world of pop culture, literature, and social justice. With its humorous take on the intersection of literature and celebrity culture, this podcast is a must-listen for anyone looking to stay up-to-date on the latest trends.
  2. The Slow Reads Hosted by two lit enthusiasts, Eric Nuzum and novelist Joshua Kendall, The Slow Reads is a podcast that encourages listeners to take their time and truly engage with the literary world. With a focus on exploring the emotions and themes that underlie great literature, this podcast is perfect for anyone looking for a more introspective and thoughtful literary experience.
  3. The Changelings Podcast Hosted by Rusty Long, The Changelings Podcast is a beautifully constructed podcast that explores the intersection of literature and art. With its poetic narration and thoughtful discussions, this podcast is perfect for anyone who loves the written word.
  4. The Books Podcast Hosted by Calvin Reid, The Books Publishers Weekly Podcast delves into the world of publishing, exploring the latest trends, bestsellers, and emerging authors. With its comprehensive and insightful analysis, this podcast is perfect for anyone looking for a behind-the-scenes look at the literary world.
  5. Stuff You Should Know About Books Hosted by editor-at-large for Publishers Weekly, Jackie Yuen, Stuff You Should Know About Books is a podcast that takes a fun and lighthearted approach to discussing literature. With its informative and engaging take on authors, books, and the world of publishing, this podcast is perfect for anyone who loves to learn.
  6. Let's Get Critical Hosted by two book bloggers and critics, Lynnette and Emma, Let's Get Critical is a podcast that offers a fresh take on literature. With a focus on dialogue-driven discussions, this podcast is perfect for anyone looking for a engaging and thought-provoking listen.
  7. The Novel Intelligence Hosted by Christine Benvenuto, The Novel Intelligence is a podcast that explores the world of literary fiction and the author behind the latest bestseller. With its deep dive into the minds and processes of writers, this podcast is perfect for anyone looking to understand what makes great literature tick.
  8. The Library Love Podcast Hosted by three book-loving librarians, The Library Love Podcast explores the world of reading, authors, and literary events. With its enthusiastic and knowledgeable hosts, this podcast is perfect for anyone who loves to discover new authors and explore the world of literature.
  9. Maisie Williams Conversation Hosted by Maisie Williams and Alex Barney, Maisie Williams Conversation delves into the world of literature and creative writing. With its thought-provoking discussions and captivating host, this podcast is perfect for anyone looking for a creative and engaging listen.
  10. Dead Darlings Podcast Hosted by writer and editor, Cheyenne Berg, Dead Darlings Podcast digs into the world of creative writing, exploring themes, emotions, and writing processes. With its engaging and insightful discussions, this podcast is perfect for anyone looking to take their writing to the next level. Whether you're a bookworm, a writer, or simply someone who loves the written word, there's a literary podcast out there for you. So why not tune in and start exploring the world of literature in a whole new way? Listen and Learn

Subscribe to these fabulous literary podcasts to never miss an episode. Explore new authors, themes, and genres of literature. Discover hidden gems in literature that you may have never encountered before. Engage with writers, readers, and fans through live discussions and Q&A sessions. Start a book club or reading group with friends, family, or online hosts. Read and connect with others through online platforms like Goodreads, Twitter, or Instagram.

Conclusion In the world of literature, podcasts offer a fresh and exciting way to explore the written word. With so many fantastic options to choose from, the best literary podcasts to tune into can have a profound impact on your reading and writing experience. Take the time to listen, learn, and engage with the world of literature, and you'll be sure to discover new authors, themes, and genres to obsess over.
