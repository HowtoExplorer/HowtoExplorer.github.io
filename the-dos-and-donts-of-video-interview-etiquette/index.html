The Dos and Don'ts of Video Interview Etiquette


In today’s digital age, video interviews have become increasingly popular as a convenient and efficient way for employers to connect with potential candidates. With the rise of remote work and global job opportunities, mastering video interview etiquette is essential for job seekers. Whether you’re a seasoned professional or a recent graduate, here are the dos and don’ts to ensure you make a positive impression during your next video interview.

The Dos:

1. Test your technology beforehand:

Before the interview, make sure you have a stable internet connection and test your audio and video settings. Familiarize yourself with the video conferencing platform being used and ensure that it is up to date. Being prepared technologically will help you avoid any last-minute glitches and ensure a smooth interview experience.

2. Choose a suitable location:

Select a quiet and well-lit area for your video interview. Find a spot where you won’t be interrupted or distracted by background noise or movement. Ensure that the background is clean, professional, and free from any personal items that may be distracting or unprofessional.

3. Dress professionally:

Even though you’re not physically present in the same room as the interviewer, it’s crucial to dress as if you were attending an in-person interview. Dress professionally from head to toe, as you never know when you may need to stand up or move during the interview. Dressing professionally will help you feel more confident and project a polished image.

4. Maintain eye contact:

During a video interview, it’s essential to maintain eye contact with the interviewer. Look directly into the camera rather than at your own image on the screen. This will create the illusion of eye contact and show your engagement and attentiveness.

5. Practice active listening:

Just like in a face-to-face interview, active listening is key during a video interview. Show your interest and engagement by nodding your head, smiling, and providing verbal cues to indicate that you are actively listening. Demonstrate your understanding by paraphrasing or summarizing the interviewer’s questions or comments.

6. Prepare your environment:

Have a copy of your resume, the job description, and any other relevant documents within reach. This will allow you to refer to them easily during the interview without fumbling or interrupting the flow of conversation. Prepare a pen and paper to take notes, as well as a glass of water to stay hydrated.

The Don’ts:

1. Don’t be late:

Punctuality is crucial in any professional setting, including video interviews. Treat the video interview with the same level of importance as an in-person interview and log in a few minutes early. Being punctual demonstrates your reliability and respect for the interviewer’s time.

2. Don’t neglect your body language:

While the interviewer may only see you from the waist up, your body language still plays a significant role in conveying your confidence and interest. Avoid slouching, fidgeting, or crossing your arms, as these gestures can make you appear disengaged or uninterested.

3. Don’t rely too heavily on notes:

While it’s essential to have your documents and notes nearby, avoid reading directly from them during the interview. Over-reliance on notes can make you appear unprepared or lacking in confidence. Use your notes as a reference guide rather than a script, allowing for a more natural and conversational flow.

4. Don’t forget to smile:

Smiling is a simple yet powerful way to build rapport and establish a positive connection with the interviewer. Remember to smile genuinely and naturally throughout the interview, as it can help create a friendly and approachable atmosphere.

5. Don’t multitask:

During a video interview, it’s crucial to give your full attention to the conversation at hand. Avoid multitasking, such as checking emails or browsing the internet, as it can be easily noticed and may give the impression that you are not fully committed to the interview.

6. Don’t rush your answers:

Take your time to gather your thoughts before responding to questions. Speaking too quickly can make you come across as nervous or unsure. Pause briefly before answering to ensure that your responses are thoughtful and well-articulated.

Remember, mastering video interview etiquette requires practice and preparation. By following these dos and don’ts, you can leave a lasting impression and increase your chances of success in your next video interview. Good luck!
