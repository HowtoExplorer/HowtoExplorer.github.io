Can Science Save Us? Evaluating the Potential of Transhumanism's Morals


Transhumanism, with its optimistic promise of utilizing science and technology to enhance human capabilities, has undoubtedly captivated imaginations globally. Rooted in a belief that humanity can overcome its limited biological and intellectual potential through technological intervention, transhumanism has the potential to revolutionize the human experience. However, we must address a critical question: Can science save us by imparting a universal solution to humanity’s moral crises through transhumanism?

Distinguishing Between Mortal and Moral Values

Before delving into the depths of transhumanism’s moral implications, it is essential to distinguish between two primary categories of human values: mortal and moral. Mortal values are rooted in physiological traits - instinctual behaviors driven by our biological makeup - while moral values are derived from our capacity for rational thought and philosophical understanding. These moral values include concepts such as fairness, loyalty, compassion, and justice.

Transhumanism’s primary focus is on enhancing our mortal values through technology, providing us with enhancements in physical strength, memory, speed, and intelligence. However, to fully evaluate transhumanism’s potential to save humanity from moral crises, we must explore whether it can effectively address our moral values or merely enable us to embody and multiply our mortal aptitudes.

The Potential of Transhumanism’s Morals

There are several advantages to integrating transhumanist technology in our pursuit of improved moral values. Firstly, advancements in artificial intelligence (AI) may assist us in identifying, understanding, and defining moral parameters more accurately. AI could provide support in decision-making processes, promoting impartiality and eliminating biases. Furthermore, AI can learn and adapt to changing societal values, enabling moral development in the face of evolving norms and standards.

Additionally, the influence of neurotechnology may enhance our empathetic responses and emotional intuition. By gaining a deeper understanding of other’s emotions and perspectives, transhumanism’s emotional upgrades could promote the cultivation of moral traits such as empathy, sensitivity, and psychological understanding. Consequently, more empathetic individuals could facilitate more considerate choices and policies influencing society.

However, while transhumanism’s tools show potential to augment our mortal values and provide direction for moral development, their actual integration into human experience could bring unintended consequences. For instance, the breadth of cognitive enhancement enabled by neurotechnology may exacerbate the “dilution effect,” in which individuals face difficulties recognizing danger or importance due to exposure to an overwhelming range of choices or priorities. Such technological enhancements may push us further from embracing significant moral concepts, turning the tables on transhumanism’s perceived solution.

A Roadmap for Responsible Transhumanism

To maximize the prospects of transhumanism’s contribution to humanity’s moral development, several safeguards must be incorporated into the progression of technologies and the adoption of enhanced capabilities.

  1. Interdisciplinary Collaboration: Encouraging collaboration between fields such as philosophy, psychology, and neuroscience can inform the development of technologies and practices aimed at moral enhancement.

  2. Consensus on Core Moral Values: Cultivating and maintaining a broad consensus on core moral values within societies ensures that technological advancements promote shared human attributes rather than exacerbating moral fragmentation.

  3. Transparency and Accountability: Ensuring that AI, neurotechnology, and other transhumanist enhancements operate within transparent frameworks and acknowledging their limits can help minimize potential pitfalls.

  4. Flexible Adaptability: Allowing for the reassessment and revision of moral objectives and parameters as society evolves can accommodate changing norms, values, and priorities.

  5. Education and Philosopher-Augmented Transhumanism: Integrating a comprehensive education system emphasizing philosophy, critical thinking, and risk management alongside technological advancements can promote a balance between technical skill and moral understanding.

In conclusion, transhumanism’s potential to enhance and augment human capacities is undoubtedly vast; however, its implications for the moral development of humanity are not guaranteed. Through careful examination, overarching collaboration, and the incorporation of adaptive measures, the visionary optimisms of transhumanism can align with humanity’s quest for morality and ensure that science unites us in our pursuit of a better future. Ultimately, it is through the fusion of transcendent technology and profound moral understanding that we may find salvation – or suffer the consequences of ignoring our moral compasses in the face of innovation.
