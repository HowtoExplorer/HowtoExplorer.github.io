Understanding Job Descriptions: Decoding Employer Needs


When searching for a new role, job seekers often spend hours pouring over job descriptions, trying to decipher what employers are really looking for. But what if you could crack the code and confidently answer "yes" to every employer need? It's not just about scanning for buzzwords like "team player" or "innovative thinker" – it's about understanding the nuanced requirements behind the job description. Ellen, a marketing professional, was a prime example of this scenario. She had applied to numerous roles, but couldn't figure out why she wasn't landing interviews. After analyzing her responses to job postings, she realized she was focusing solely on highlighting her qualifications and experience. While this was a good start, it didn't provide the context employers needed to see her as a strong fit. Employers create job descriptions to convey the skills, competencies, and personal qualities required for the role. However, many job descriptions are written in a formal, generic style, leaving applicants wondering what makes each job unique. To better understand these needs, Ellen started applying a simple framework:

Pause before applying Identify specific requirements Craft tailored answers Socialize your response

Pause before applying Before applying, take a step back and analyze the job description. Look for phrases that highlight essential skills, such as "ambitious and driven individual with expertise in social media marketing." Ask yourself what you can do to demonstrate these qualities in your application. Identify specific requirements Break down the job description into specific requirements and prioritize them. Eliminate generic phrases like "team player" or "strong communication skills" and focus on the specific needs mentioned. Skillset: Develop a list of required skills and competencies.

Technical skills: Access to Facebook page, Social media analytics, Microsoft office.

Required competencies: Attentive, System thinking.

Craft tailored answers Craft your responses to highlight the skills and competencies that match the employer's specific requirements. This is where Ellen realized her mistake. Instead of copying and pasting her generic answers, she tailored her responses to showcase how her skills and experience addressed the needs outlined in the job description.
