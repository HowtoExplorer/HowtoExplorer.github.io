The Best Fashion Documentaries to Watch


Fashion is not just about clothing; it is a form of expression, art, and culture. Behind the scenes of the glamorous runways and glossy magazine covers, there is a world full of passion, creativity, and hard work. If you are curious about the inner workings of the fashion industry or simply want to immerse yourself in its captivating stories, here are some of the best fashion documentaries that offer a peek into this fascinating realm.

1. The September Issue (2009)

Directed by R.J. Cutler, The September Issue takes you behind the scenes of Vogue magazine’s most important issue of the year. Explore the intricate process of creating the iconic September issue, known for its influence on fashion trends worldwide. Witness the dynamic relationship between the legendary editor-in-chief, Anna Wintour, and the talented creative director, Grace Coddington. This documentary offers a captivating glimpse into the high-stakes world of fashion publishing.

2. Dior and I (2014)

Step into the world of haute couture with Dior and I, directed by Frédéric Tcheng. Follow the journey of designer Raf Simons as he takes the helm of the prestigious House of Dior. Experience the pressure and excitement leading up to Simons’ debut collection. This documentary showcases the meticulous craftsmanship and artistic vision required to create a couture collection, providing a deep appreciation for the artistry behind the fashion industry.

3. McQueen (2018)

Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, McQueen is a poignant documentary that delves into the life and career of the legendary British fashion designer, Alexander McQueen. From his humble beginnings to his meteoric rise in the fashion world, witness McQueen’s unrivaled talent and his ability to push boundaries. This film not only celebrates McQueen’s extraordinary creativity but also explores the darker aspects of fame and the toll it can take on an individual.

4. The True Cost (2015)

Directed by Andrew Morgan, The True Cost sheds light on the often overlooked consequences of fast fashion. This eye-opening documentary examines the social and environmental impact of the global fashion industry. It delves into the exploitative labor practices, environmental degradation, and the excessive consumerism that fuels the fast fashion culture. The True Cost encourages viewers to consider the ethical and sustainable choices they can make as consumers.

5. Bill Cunningham New York (2010)

Explore the life and work of the iconic street fashion photographer, Bill Cunningham, in this heartwarming documentary by Richard Press. Cunningham’s passion for capturing New York City’s vibrant street style is infectious, and his dedication to his craft is truly inspiring. Through his lens, he documented the ever-changing fashion landscape, providing a unique perspective on the intersection of fashion and everyday life.

6. Valentino: The Last Emperor (2008)

Directed by Matt Tyrnauer, Valentino: The Last Emperor offers an intimate portrait of the legendary Italian fashion designer, Valentino Garavani. This documentary follows Valentino during the final two years of his career, providing a behind-the-scenes look at his creative process and the elaborate production of his final runway show. The Last Emperor showcases Valentino’s unwavering dedication to his craft and his unwavering commitment to beauty and elegance.

Whether you are a fashion enthusiast or simply curious about the industry, these documentaries offer a unique perspective on the world of fashion. From the glamorous to the thought-provoking, they illuminate the diverse facets of this captivating realm. So grab some popcorn, settle into your favorite spot, and prepare to be inspired, educated, and entertained by these must-watch fashion documentaries.
