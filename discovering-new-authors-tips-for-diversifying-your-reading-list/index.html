Discovering New Authors: Tips for Diversifying Your Reading List


If you’re a bookworm, the world of literature can feel like both a treasure trove and a maze. With countless novels, memoirs, and works of fiction waiting to be discovered, where does one begin? Despite the lure of comfort readings – that familiar, trusty standby – we all know it’s crucial to broaden our horizons to enrich our reading lives.

Curating a diverse reading list made up of authors from different cultures, backgrounds, time periods, and genres may not only help combat reader’s block but also enlighten, challenge, and alter our perspective. But how does one discover these novel voices? Let’s explore a few ways together.

  1. Dive into online book clubs: When a good book club strikes the right balance between thoughtful discourse and witty banter, it can be a joy to participate. Book clubs can introduce you to an author you would have never found otherwise. Plus, the interaction fosters a sense of community around the shared love for literature. From Internet phenomenon such as The Penguin Classics Book Club to Amazon’s Second Chance Books, you’re bound to learn about new authors.

  2. Taste familiarity in stories from afar: If you’re a fan of well-crafted storytelling, don’t miss out on international literature. While Haruki Murakami might be a household name, Jacob Levitt argues in The Global New Yorker that reading international literature deepens our sense of global responsibility. Discover a nuanced, emotional connection and greater understanding of the human experience.

  3. Embrace the recommendation spree: Word of mouth has delivered more than one book recommendation. Whether it’s a study group or a group of friends, don’t underestimate the power of a strong endorsement. As Jesper Juul writes, “People won’t remember what you said, they’ll remember how you made them feel.” A rousing recommendation can make all the difference.

  4. Dabble in the upgrade recommendation system: Ever received a Kindle gift card? Have an unused Audible credit? Consider upgrading your reading gadgets. Amazon’s Recommendation System and Audible’s Feature Recommendations bring an enjoyable flow of fresh narrative voices into your life.

  5. Use goodreads like a personal library: Goodreads, a social cataloging website that allows users to share book reviews and recommendations, provides a virtual library that allows you to track your reading habits. Filter books by “Awards Winners” or “Frequently Read” to unearth novels from criminally underrated authors.

  6. Visit your local library: Visit sections of literature that don’t usually wander into. Make a point to know your librarian. Actors, poets, scientists—many people have more stories to tell than one might initially assume. A sign of a great library? A librarian who highly advocates for peculiar voices.

  7. Immerse in podcasts and interviews: Whether you’re walking the dog or folding laundry, podcasts provide an accessible form of interview-style conversation that can unveil new writers. Catch authors talking about their craft on The cozy Bibliotheca Anon Podcast or listen to compelling storyteller interviews on The Book Review Podcast.

Throughout your literary journey, remember not to get bogged down by the belief that one must only read well-established authors. After all, they were once unknown writers themselves, just waiting for the chance to be discovered.
