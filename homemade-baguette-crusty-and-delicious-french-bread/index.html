Homemade Baguette: Crusty and Delicious French Bread


There’s nothing quite like the aroma of freshly baked bread wafting through your kitchen. And when it comes to bread, the baguette holds a special place in the hearts of bread lovers around the world. The crispy crust, tender interior, and unmistakable flavor make the baguette a true culinary masterpiece. Today, I’ll be sharing with you a simple yet foolproof recipe for homemade baguettes that will transport you straight to the streets of Paris.

The Art of Baguette Making

Making baguettes at home may seem intimidating, but with the right technique and a few key ingredients, you’ll be well on your way to enjoying a crusty and delicious French bread. The secret to a perfect baguette lies in the dough, which requires time and patience to develop its unique texture and flavor.

Ingredients

To make your homemade baguettes, you’ll need:

  • 500g of bread flour
  • 10g of salt
  • 10g of sugar
  • 7g of instant yeast
  • 350ml of warm water

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the bread flour, salt, sugar, and instant yeast. Make a well in the center and gradually add the warm water. Mix everything together until a sticky dough forms.

  2. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes, until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough feels too sticky, you can add a little more flour, but be careful not to overdo it.

  3. Shape the dough into a ball and place it back into the mixing bowl. Cover the bowl with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap and let the dough rise for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size.

  4. Once the dough has risen, gently punch it down to release any air bubbles. Turn it out onto a lightly floured surface and divide it into three equal portions.

  5. Take one portion of the dough and flatten it into a rectangle shape. Fold the top third of the dough down and the bottom third up, like folding a letter. Then, roll the dough tightly from one end to the other, creating the classic baguette shape. Repeat this process with the remaining two portions of dough.

  6. Place the shaped baguettes onto a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Cover them with a clean kitchen towel and let them rise for another 30 minutes.

  7. Meanwhile, preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C). Once the baguettes have finished their final rise, make diagonal slashes across the tops using a sharp knife or a bread lame. This will help the baguettes to expand and create that beautiful crust.

  8. Bake the baguettes in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until they turn golden brown and sound hollow when tapped on the bottom. Remove them from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack before serving.

Enjoy the Fruits of Your Labor

Now that you’ve mastered the art of making homemade baguettes, it’s time to savor the fruits of your labor. Whether you enjoy them fresh from the oven with a pat of butter, or use them as the foundation for a hearty sandwich, these crusty and delicious French breads are sure to impress.

So gather your ingredients, roll up your sleeves, and let the aroma of freshly baked baguettes fill your home. With a little time, patience, and this foolproof recipe, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a master of homemade bread. Bon appétit!
