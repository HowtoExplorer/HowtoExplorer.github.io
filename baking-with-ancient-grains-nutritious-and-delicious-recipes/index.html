Baking with Ancient Grains: Nutritious and Delicious Recipes


In recent years, there has been a resurgence of interest in ancient grains and their culinary uses. These grains, which have been cultivated for thousands of years, offer a unique blend of flavors, textures, and nutritional benefits that make them a fantastic addition to any baker’s pantry. From spelt to teff, these ancient grains bring diversity and depth to baked goods, and today, we’re going to explore some nutritious and delicious recipes that showcase the versatility of these time-honored ingredients.

Why Bake with Ancient Grains?

Before we dive into the recipes, let’s take a moment to understand why baking with ancient grains is worth the effort. Ancient grains such as quinoa, amaranth, and einkorn are rich in nutrients like fiber, protein, and various essential vitamins and minerals. They also offer a wide range of flavors, from nutty to earthy, that can elevate the taste of your baked treats. Additionally, many people find that ancient grains are easier to digest than modern wheat, making them a great option for those with gluten sensitivities.

Nutritious and Delicious Recipes

Quinoa Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, almond flour, coconut sugar, coconut oil, vanilla extract, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Gently fold in the dark chocolate chips.
  4. Using a spoon, scoop the dough and form it into small balls. Place them on the prepared baking sheet and flatten them slightly with the back of the spoon.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the edges are golden brown.
  6. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Spelt Banana Bread

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups spelt flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chopped walnuts (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a 9x5-inch loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the spelt flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the mashed bananas, honey, olive oil, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Fold in the chopped walnuts, if using.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 50-60 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the banana bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Embracing Ancient Grains in Your Baking

These recipes are just a glimpse of the countless possibilities when it comes to baking with ancient grains. Whether you’re making bread, cookies, muffins, or pancakes, incorporating ancient grains into your recipes can bring a new dimension to your baking endeavors. Not only do these grains offer a nutritional boost, but they also impart a delightful complexity to the flavors of your baked goods. So, next time you’re in the kitchen, consider reaching for some quinoa, spelt, or amaranth and discover the joys of baking with ancient grains. Your taste buds and your body will thank you for it!
