Successful Networking: 8 Unconventional Ways to Build Valuable Connections


Networking is one of the most essential skills for career and business development. It helps establish lasting relationships, secure job opportunities, and foster collaborations. While the majority of people associate networking with attending large events or mixing with their industry peers, there is a whole array of unconventional approaches you can take. In this blog post, we discuss eight innovative strategies to help you build valuable connections in your personal and professional life.

1. Step Out of Your Comfort Zone

Once you notice that you’ve started favoring familiar places and people while networking, it’s time to challenge yourself and step out of your comfort zone. Reach out to individuals you wouldn’t usually connect with. Engaging in conversations with different groups of people will expose you to unique perspectives and diverse networks.

2. Create Your Own Networking Events

Instead of merely attending industry events, consider hosting one yourself. In an event, you have the power to cherry-pick the keynote speakers and guests, creating an environment that aligns with your interests and networking goals. This increases the chances of being introduced to quality connections and facilitates meaningful exchanges.

3. Use Social Media Creatively

Social media is a double-edged sword when it comes to networking. While it can be a great platform for expanding your reach and connecting with new people, it can also be overwhelming and distracting. A thriving online community around your interests can be pivotal in creating opportunities and facilitating connections in your physical world.

Use creative content that sparks conversations and encourages engagement from your target audience. Regularly post relevant articles, join forums, and stay active in comment sections to show that you value the community.

4. Adopt the ‘More Icebreakers’ Approach

Striking conversations can be difficult, especially for introverts or people who are naturally shy. In those cases, having a few icebreaker questions in your pocket can be extremely helpful. The key is knowing your audience and having icebreakers that are specific to various circles.

Pay attention to common conversations, interests, and topics that resonate within your industry or particular groups. Gathering information about people before engaging can help you come up with thoughtful questions, ultimately leading to deeper connections.

5. Adopt a Small-Dog Attitude – Be Inquisitive and Eager to Learn

After all, who doesn’t love a friendly little dog that’s eager to learn, explore, and make friends? Networking should be an exchange of ideas, knowledge, and assistance; therefore, always approach conversations with curiosity. Show genuine interest in others’ experiences and be open to sharing your insights in return. This way, you show that you appreciate and value others’ wisdom, eventually enhancing the connections you make.

6. Become an Active Volunteer

Volunteering sheds a positive light on your character and skills while connecting you with like-minded people who share similar interests. Find a cause or organization in your field of work and offer your time and talents. This will give you the opportunity to directly reach out to your target community and create lasting partnerships without pushing for immediate gains.

7. Create a Networking Plan

Setting clear objectives before starting a networking session can dramatically improve the quality of your interactions. Research the people you want to meet, prepare questions, and understand the desired outcomes of your conversations. Keep records after every meeting to track your interactions and provide context for follow-up communications.

8. Follow-Up Professionally

However, a strong follow-up game is just as (if not more) important than the networking itself. Send a personalized email that references the conversation, reiterates your point of connection, and suggests a next step. Approach with competence and consideration while building a relationship of mutual gain.

While traditional networking techniques have their merits, adopting an unconventional approach can make you stand out from the crowd. By expanding your mix of networking strategies, you not only increase the number of valuable connections that you form, but you also improve your ability to identify and seize new opportunities.

Remember, networking has unlimited possibilities, and continually exploring new methods is the key to a thriving network that fuels personal and professional growth. Happy connecting!
