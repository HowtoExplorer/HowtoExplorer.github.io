3 Secrets to Mastering the 'Tell Me About Yourself' Interview Question


The ‘Tell me about yourself’ interview question can make or break your chances of getting the job. It’s a tricky one, isn’t it? Everyone knows they’re supposed to answer it well, but nobody knows how to do it just right. Well, I’m here to help. In this article, we will uncover three secrets to mastering this vexing question and turning it into your new best friend.

  1. Keep it concise and engaging When the interviewer asks you to tell them about yourself, they’re not looking for your life story. You don’t need to go back to the beginning and recount every detail leading up to this point. Instead, aim for a concise and engaging answer. Introduce yourself in 30 seconds or less, highlighting your most relevant experiences and skills.

Start your answer with a strong opening line that grabs their attention. Mention your current job, your recent achievements, and what you love about it. This will demonstrate your enthusiasm for your profession. End by expressing your interest in the position and how you believe your skills would be an asset to the company.

  1. Highlight transferable skills Life is filled with unexpected twists and turns. The same is true for our careers. Even if you’re not applying for a job in the same industry, there’s likely some common ground between your current role and the position you’re seeking. Your task is to find these similarities and highlight them.

When answering the ‘Tell me about yourself’ question, focus on your transferable skills. These are the skills that you’ve developed in one role that can be applied to another. For example, if you worked in retail and are now looking for a position in marketing, you can highlight your communication skills, customer service experience, and problem-solving abilities.

Sharing your transferable skills demonstrates to the interviewer that you’re a versatile candidate. It also shows that you’ve thought about how your current role has prepared you for the position at hand.

  1. Tell a story We’re wired to enjoy stories. They help us relate to others, understand situations, and learn valuable lessons. Your interview is the perfect context to share a small tale.

When responding to ‘Tell me about yourself,’ think about a specific experience or achievement. Choose something that showcases your skills, character, or both. For example, if you organized a successful company event, share what led up to it, how you dealt with challenges, and the results.

A well-told story is engaging, memorable, and relatable. It can leave a lasting impression on the interviewer and help you stand out from the pack.

Conclusion: The ‘Tell me about yourself’ interview question can be a double-edged sword. It’s an opportunity to make a great first impression, but it can also trip you up if you don’t handle it correctly. By following these three secrets - keeping your answer concise and engaging, highlighting your transferable skills, and telling a story - you’ll be well on your way to mastering this tricky question. So, gear up, prepare, and give it your best shot. Best of luck!
