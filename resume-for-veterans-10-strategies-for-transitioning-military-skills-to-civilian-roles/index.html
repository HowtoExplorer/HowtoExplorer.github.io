Resume for Veterans: 10 Strategies for Transitioning Military Skills to Civilian Roles


Transitioning from military to civilian life can be a challenging endeavor, especially when it comes to translating your unique skillset and experiences onto a traditional resume. If you’re feeling overwhelmed, don’t worry – you’re not alone. Many veterans confront the same challenge upon leaving the military. To help you bridge this gap and effectively communicate your skills to potential civilian employers, I have compiled a list of ten crucial strategies for resume development.

1. Understand the Civilian Job Market

Before starting to craft your resume, take some time to research the civilian job market and the types of positions for which you are qualifying. Understand the typical requirements and expectations for each role to ensure a seamless transfer of your military skills. Be aware of essential civilian keywords that employers might use, and incorporate them into your resume for better searchability.

2. Demonstrate Your Unique Skills and Experiences

Military service provides you with a range of unique skills and experiences that may be less common among traditional civilian candidates. Emphasize your expertise in leadership, teamwork, and problem-solving in your body of work. Use specific examples that show how your accomplishments align with the needs of each prospective employer.

3. Translate Military Terminology into Civilian Language

Avoid using or defining acronyms and military-specific jargon unless well-understood in the civilian context. Instead, focus on rephrasing your experiences and achievements with clear, direct language that resonates with the audience reading your resume. This will enable employers to more easily understand how your military background will add value to their organization.

4. Tailor Your Resume for Each Job Application

While it may be tempting to use one resume template for multiple applications,Customizing your resume to the specific job description will greatly improve your chances of landing an interview. Highlight the skills that correspond with the particular position and successfully convey how your military training can benefit your new potential employer.

5. Emphasize Leadership and Correlated Skills

Demonstrate your leadership skills through concrete examples of when you have successfully managed a team or project. For example, mention military experience in providing direction, developing plans, delegating tasks, and achieving desired objectives. This approach showcases your ability to excel in leadership roles, which is a highly sought-after trait in the civilian workforce.

6. Quantify Your Accomplishments

Numbers and data stand out on a resume. Whenever possible, use specific examples and quantifiable achievements to showcase your success. For instance, illustrate how you led a team to complete a project on time or achieved cost savings through resourceful management. Employers favor concrete evidence of your capabilities over generic statements.

7. Emphasize Adaptability and Continuous Learning

Employers look for adaptable, self-motivated, and willing-to-learn individuals. In every role you’ve held, showcase how you have swiftly adapted to change. This could include references to training, professional development courses, or cross-training in new areas of expertise. Be sure to include a relevant skills section on your resume with continued education on your list of proficiencies.

8. Leverage Your Transferrable Skills

Some skills, such as communication, time management, and critical thinking, are universally applicable across industries and positions. Identify the transferable skills you have cultivated during your military service and emphasize them on your resume. Highlight instances where you have effectively used these skills to achieve specific goals in civilian roles, internships, or other experiences.

9. Make it Readable and Visually Appealing

Your resume should be easy to read and visually appealing. Clean, professional formatting creates a positive impression and allows your qualifications to shine. Use clear and appropriate font choices, bullet points, sections that organize your knowledge, skills, and expertise, and maintain consistent spacing throughout.

10. Gain Endorsements and Seek Guidance

Seek support from mentors, colleagues, and friends to provide feedback on your resume. You can collaborate with them to expand upon your achievements or identify further areas where you can emphasize your skills. Additionally, joining professional organizations or seeking endorsements from relevant certificates or affiliations can help strengthen your resume’s credibility.

By keeping these ten strategies in mind when crafting your resume, you’ll be better prepared to effectively demonstrate your military skills and experiences to civilian employers. Take the time to research, tailor, and present your accomplishments effectively, and your transition to the civilian workforce will be a smooth and successful one. Good luck!
