What Makes a Great Collaborative Leader?


Effective collaboration is the backbone of successful organizations, and a key factor in its success is the presence of a great collaborative leader. As Peter Economy, leadership expert, defines, leadership is translated into action, and that action relies more on collaboration than ever before. A great collaborative leader knows how to empower their team members, foster a culture of open communication, and get the best out of their people. To become a great collaborative leader, one must possess a set of unique qualities that set them apart from others. Leadership is not just about issuing directives and managing tasks, but also about understanding the needs of your team, providing guidance and support, and empowering your team members to be their best selves. #1 Communication is Key A collaborative leader knows that effective communication is key to success. They foster an environment where open communication flows freely, and everyone feels comfortable sharing their ideas and concerns. They practice active listening, which involves not only paying attention to what is being said but also understanding the emotions and needs behind the message. This helps build trust and creates a sense of unity within the team. Great collaborative leaders also know how to communicate complex ideas in a simple, clear manner, making it easier for others to understand and engage with them. They use various communication channels to reach their audience, whether it's through one-on-one meetings, group discussions, email, phone calls, or social media platforms. Whether they are formal or informal, the key is to be clear, concise, and engaging. #2 Empowerment is an Essential Quality A collaborative leader empowers their team members by giving them the autonomy to make decisions, set goals, and take ownership of their work. When employees feel trusted and empowered, they are more likely to be motivated and engaged. Empowerment is a delicate balance between providing support and giving freedom. Great collaborative leaders know how to strike that balance, providing the necessary direction and tools for their team members to excel. Empowerment also involves recognizing and rewarding the achievements of team members. This not only boosts their morale but also motivates them to strive for more. Collaborative leaders know how to celebrate their team's successes and acknowledge their failures as opportunities for growth. #3 Flexibility is a Valued Trait Collaborative leaders are adaptable and flexible, able to adjust their approach to suit the ever-changing needs of their team. They understand that every team member is unique and may require different handling. They know how to adjust their communication style, management approach, and expectations to meet the individual needs of each team member. This adaptability also involves being open-minded and willing to try new approaches. A great collaborative leader knows how to innovate and experiment, while still maintaining the core principles that guide their team. They understand that sometimes taking calculated risks can lead to unexpected breakthroughs. #4 Emotional Intelligence is a Must Great collaborative leaders possess emotional intelligence, which involves being aware of and managing their emotions, as well as being sensitive to the emotions of others. This quality is invaluable in building strong, healthy relationships within the team. Collaborative leaders with high emotional intelligence can empathize with others, creating a safe and inclusive environment. They are skillful communicators who can navigate conflict and power struggles without panicking. This ability to handle emotional situations and maintain their composure is highly respected and admired by team members. #5 Taking Accountability is Essential Collaborative leaders are willing to take ownership of their actions and decisions. They understand that accountability is a fundamental trait of leaders and takes responsibility for the successes and failures of their team. Great collaborative leaders know how to acknowledge and learn from mistakes, using them as opportunities for growth and improvement. This sense of accountability extends to the team as well. Collaborative leaders inspire a sense of accountability within their team members, encouraging them to own their work, their successes, and their mistakes. This creates a culture of ownership, shared responsibility, and collective accountability. #6 Fostering Inclusive Cultures is Key Inclusive cultures are essential for successful collaboration. Collaborative leaders know how to create a work environment that is welcoming and inclusive to everyone, regardless of their background, experience, or personality. This involves actively recognizing and countering biases, ensuring that everyone has an equal opportunity to contribute and grow. Great collaborative leaders also value diverse perspectives and experiences, creating an environment that encourages team members to share their thoughts and ideas without fear of judgment or rejection. This fosters an atmosphere of creativity, innovation, and progress. #7 Continuous Learning is Essential A collaborative leader is always learning and seeking ways to improve themselves and their team. They understand that success is a continuous process, requiring ongoing learning and skill acquisition. They encourage their team members to explore new areas of interest and create opportunities for growth and development. Great collaborative leaders make themselves or their team members learn through training programs, workshops, conferences, or self-study activities. They remain current with the latest developments in their industry, integrating the knowledge into their daily work. This ensures that the organization stays ahead of the competition and is equipped to tackle new challenges. #8 Conflict Resolution Skills are a Must Conflicts are inevitable in any team, but a great collaborative leader knows how to resolve them quickly and efficiently. They possess excellent conflict resolution skills, using a solution-focused approach to find a mutually beneficial resolution. Collaborative leaders understand that conflict is an opportunity for growth, not a threat to the team's dynamics. Great collaborative leaders know how to approach conflicts in a neutral, non-judgmental way. They listen carefully to both sides, fostering open and honest communication. They are empathetic, but not biased or swayed by emotions, instead focusing on the issue at hand. #9 Leading by Example is Crucial A collaborative leader leads by example, demonstrating the values, behaviors, and attitudes that the team expects from them. They embody the organizational culture, inspiring their team to adopt the same behaviors. Great collaborative leaders create a sense of authenticity and consistency, building trust and respect from their team members. Leading by example also involves empowering others to take on leadership roles. Collaborative leaders know how to develop their team members, providing them with the tools and resources to lead effectively. They empower others to make decisions, take ownership, and provide guidance to their peers. #10 Cross-Functional Thinking is Essential Collaborative leaders possess cross-functional thinking, which involves the ability to integrate knowledge from multiple disciplines to solve complex problems. They know how to consider multiple perspectives, blurring traditional organizational boundaries and fostering a sense of unity and shared responsibility. Great collaborative leaders understand the interdependencies between different parts of the organization. They navigate the system, influencing outcomes across teams and departments. They create a culture of cross-functional thinking, inspiring their team to look at problems from multiple angles. #11 Creating a Culture of Feedback is Key A collaborative leader encourages a culture of continuous feedback within their team. They understand that feedback is essential for growth, improvement, and informed decision-making. Great collaborative leaders know how to create a safe and supportive environment where team members feel comfortable sharing their feedback and suggestions. This culture of feedback not only helps the team to improve but also promotes mutual respect and trust among team members. Collaborative leaders praise their team members when they deserve it, acknowledging their successes and acknowledging their efforts. They also provide constructive feedback that is actionable, respectful, and focused on improvement rather than criticism. #12 The Ability to Pivot is a Valued Trait Great collaborative leaders are adaptable and able to pivot when necessary. They understand that change is an inevitable part of any organization, and that sometimes it requires shifting direction or strategy. Collaborative leaders know how to navigate change effectively, and to involve their team members in that process. To become a great collaborative leader, one must be willing to experiment, innovate, and try new approaches. This willingness to pivot is an essential quality for leading a team, particularly in uncertain or rapidly changing environments. In conclusion, becoming a great collaborative leader requires a unique set of qualities and skills. It involves being an effective communicator, an empathetic listener, a supportive mentor, and a decision-maker who is open-minded and willing to take calculated risks. Collaborative leaders know how to communicate effectively, foster a culture of inclusivity, empower their team members, and resolve conflicts effectively. They also possess key qualities such as adaptability, emotional intelligence, accountability, continuous learning, and empowerment. In an increasingly complex and interconnected world, developing collaboration skills is crucial for building successful teams and leading with impact. By adopting these strategies and qualities, leaders can unlock the potential of their team and foster a culture of collaboration, innovation, and success.
