Key Elements Every Resume Should Include


A well-crafted resume is the first impression a job seeker provides to a potential employer. Though every profession and individual has their unique style, there are essential elements that should never be overlooked. From the appropriate font to highlighting your relevant skills and accomplishments, make sure your resume contains these key components to increase your chances of standing out in a competitive job market.

  1. Contact Information Your resume must start with your contact information. It typically includes your full name, profession, working email address, and phone number. This information should be presented clearly, making it easy for potential employers to reach you.

  2. Qualifications Summary A qualifications summary statement, sometimes called a self-profile or an objective, gives an employer a concise snapshot of your career goals, qualifications, and what you bring to the table. It is an excellent way to set yourself apart from other applicants, so use this paragraph to highlight your strongest attributes.

  3. Professional Experience The professional experience section should detail your work history in chronological order. Provide the name of the company, your position there, the dates of employment, and your responsibilities and achievements. Use strong verbs and quantifiable accomplishments to make each entry compelling.

  4. Skills and Abilities Experienced professionals often have transferable skills that can make them a valuable asset to a new company. Skills and abilities section on your resume should include both hard and soft skills that you possess. Examples of hard skills include proficiency in software or languages, while soft skills encompass temperament traits. Including these skills can speak to your potential and versatility as an employee.

  5. Education While some employers are more interested in your professional experience, others might prioritize your academic background. Your education section should include your degree(s), major, area of study, and the institution where you earned it. If you graduated with honors or received a notable scholarship, make sure to include it as well.

  6. Awards and Honors Did you win an award or receive accolades for your work? If your achievements deserve recognition, make sure to showcase them on your resume. Awards and honors can add credibility to your application and demonstrate your dedication and excellence in your field.

  7. References While in the past it was customary to include references on a resume, nowadays it is generally understood that one will provide a list of references upon request. However, it can be helpful to communicate to the employer that you have professional contacts that can vouch for your work and character.

  8. Formatting and Presentation A well-formatted and visually appealing resume can make an applicant more memorable to a potential employer. Use a clean and easy-to-read font, such as Arial or Verdana, and maintain a consistent format throughout the document. Additionally, include margins, headings, and bullet points to separate and organize the information.

Closing Thoughts

Remember, a resume is an advertisement of your skills, qualifications, and value to a future employer. With an organized layout and comprehensive elements, you can make a lasting impression. In a competitive job market, presenting an immaculately crafted resume will demonstrate your competence and dedication as a potential candidate. With the right balance of your achievements and a thoughtful presentation, you’ll be one step closer to that dream job.
