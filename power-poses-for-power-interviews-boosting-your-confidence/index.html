Power Poses for Power Interviews: Boosting Your Confidence


Everyone experiences a certain level of nervousness during interviews. Whether it’s for a job, a scholarship, or a guest appearance, the pressure to impress can make even the most confident individuals falter. In this blog post, we’ll discuss how power poses can give you the edge you need to excel and boost your confidence during those crucial moments.

What Are Power Poses?

Power poses are strategic body positions that exude confidence and power. They involve expanding your body and taking up more space, conveying a sense of control and self-assurance. Power poses can be used in various scenarios, but they are particularly helpful during interviews. By adopting a powerful stance, interviewees can project a more confident image, influencing both the interviewer and themselves.

The Science Behind Power Poses

In 2010, two psychologists, Amy Cuddy and Carly Gnony, introduced the concept of power poses to the world in their TED talk, “Your Body Language Shapes Who You Are.” They found that adopting high-power poses for just two minutes could raise testosterone levels in both men and women, while also reducing cortisol, the hormone associated with stress.

Cuddy’s research has shown that power poses have a significant impact on self-confidence, which translates to improved performance in various aspects of life, including interviews.

Top 5 Power Poses for Interviews

Now that we understand the benefits of power poses, let’s explore five empowering poses you can adopt during your next interview to boost your confidence.

  1. The CEO pose – Stand tall with your legs shoulder-width apart, feet flat on the ground, arms crossed in front of your body, and elbows pointing outwards. This powerful pose is perfect for exuding confidence and leadership as it takes up the most space.

  2. The Superman pose – Stand with your feet together, then spread your legs into a wide stance. Raise your arms to the side at shoulder height, fingers pointing downwards. This pose lifts your chest and head, creating an open, confident demeanor ideal for interviews.

  3. The Gatekeeper’s pose – Place your hands on each side of your body, arms straight, fingers pointing towards the ground. This powerful pose can help you project an image of control and authority.

  4. The V-stance – Stand with your feet apart, parallel to one another, and your arms relaxed by your sides. This simple pose allows you to appear grounded, open, and poised.

  5. The Folded Hands pose – Place your hands in front of your chest, fingers interlaced and thumbs pressed together. This pose requires being somewhat self-aware but creates an image of calm determination.

Mastering the Art of PowerPosing

To make the most of these power poses during an interview, follow these tips:

  1. Practice regularly – The more you practice adopting a powerful stance, the more comfortable you’ll become with it during an interview.

  2. Use visual cues – If you’re nervous, adopting a power pose might not come naturally. Visual cues can help you remember to adopt these poses during the interview. For example, you could imagine pretending to hold a tray with a valuable item, like a teddy bear or a book, in both hands to naturally broaden your stance.

  3. Be mindful of timing – Adopt a power pose at key moments during the interview, such as when you’re about to answer a challenging question or when you’re discussing your strengths and experience.

  4. Mix it up – Shifting your body positioning occasionally during the interview can signal engagement and attentiveness, as opposed to appearing stiff or uninterested.

Final Thoughts

In conclusion, power poses have proven to be a valuable tool for boosting confidence during interviews. Incorporating some of these poses into your next interview can help you project confidence, convey your best qualities, and leave a lasting impression. Remember, adopting power poses is just one piece of the puzzle; to truly excel in interviews, focus on developing solid skills, honing your communication abilities, and practicing your interview techniques.

So, the next time you face an important interview, stand tall, take up space, and show the world that you’re confident, capable, and ready to make an impact. After all, it’s all about how you hold your power!
