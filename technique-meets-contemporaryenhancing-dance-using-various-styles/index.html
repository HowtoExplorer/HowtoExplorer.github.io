Technique Meets Contemporary:Enhancing Dance Using Various Styles


Dance is an art form that has been evolving for centuries. It is a way for people to express their emotions, share stories, and connect with each other. However, throughout its history, it has undergone various transformations and integrations of different styles. Contemporary dance, in particular, has demonstrated a compelling capability to absorb and enhance various techniques. In this blog post, we will explore how the fusion of different dance styles has contributed to the development and sophistication of contemporary dance.

A Bit of History

Contemporary dance originated around the middle of the 20th century, integrating influences from modern, ballet, improvisation, and jazz. Unlike traditional dance forms, contemporary dance focuses more on self-expression and exploration of personal creativity. As time went on, it continued to evolve, embracing and incorporating new styles from around the world.

Styles That Shaped Contemporary Dance

Several styles have played an instrumental role in shaping contemporary dance. Let’s take a closer look at some prominent dance styles that have influenced and enhanced contemporary dance.

  1. Ballet

A classical basis for modern dance, ballet’s technical precision, nuanced body placement, and overall grace have significantly influenced contemporary dance’s form. Ballet teaches contemporary dancers discipline, control, and elegant lines that help them create captivating choreography, elevating their performance quality.

  1. Modern Dance

Modern dance is closely related to contemporary dance. Developed at the turn of the 20th century, it broke away from classical ballet’s rigid structures, encouraging personal expression through movement. Modern dance introduced new ways of moving the body, incorporating floor work, contractions, and the use of the weight of the body.

  1. Jazz

Jazz dance’s improvisational nature, syncopated rhythms, and energetic movement styles have added excitement and creativity to contemporary dance. Popular in Broadway and Hollywood in the early 20th century, jazz dancing has contributed significantly to contemporary alternatives in today’s commercial dance world.

  1. Hip-hop

Hip-hop dance emerged in the late 1970s, with roots in inner-city African American and Latino youth culture. Aggressive movements, intricate footwork, and street style have all made hip-hop an irresistible force in contemporary dance’s evolution – impacting body movements, energy, and overall aesthetic.

  1. Ballroom Dance

Traditional ballroom dances like waltz, tango, and foxtrot have also played a vital role in enhancing contemporary dance. All these elegant styles have added complexity to contemporary choreography, providing diverse cultural influences and nuanced partner work.

  1. Contact Improvisation

A dance form that emphasizes spontaneous and improvisational movement between dancers, contact improvisation promotes trust, communication, and collaboration. This intimate form of dance has enriched contemporary dance vocabulary, focusing on connection, weight-sharing, and fluidity in movement.

The Latest Innovations

As we continue to see contemporary dance evolve, we’re witnessing groundbreaking innovations by dancers and choreographers integrating styles across the globe. For example, street dancers have started incorporating conventional ballet moves, hip-hop meets classical Bharatanatyam, and contemporary dance integrates elements of traditional Chinese dance. These hybrid styles are indicative of the fluid nature of contemporary dance and how it continually upgrades its technical capabilities.

The Future of Contemporary Dance

With abundant creativity and endless possibilities, contemporary dance will continue to embrace and incorporate various dance styles into its massive tapestry of influences. It’s an exhilarating time for the art form as dancers and choreographers worldwide continue to develop innovative, boundary-pushing work.

As contemporary dance continues to expand, we must remember that it serves as more than entertainment; it’s an expression of human emotion, introspection, and a powerful lens through which we can better understand the world around us. So as we move forward, let’s celebrate these diverse and dynamic styles that have shaped contemporary dance and continue to inspire dancers worldwide.
