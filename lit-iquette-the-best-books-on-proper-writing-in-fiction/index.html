Lit-iquette: The Best Books on Proper Writing in Fiction


Good writing is as much an art as it is a craft. Whether you’re a budding novelist or an aspiring short-story writer, it’s essential to understand and apply the basic principles of fiction writing. Lit-iquette, or proper writing etiquette in fiction, encompasses a wide range of concepts and techniques, from the establishment of believable characters to the creation of vivid and immersive settings. By delving into the literature of fiction writing, it is possible to refine your skills and elevate your own work to the next level. In this blog post, we explore the best books on proper writing in fiction, to help you become the esteemed writer you aspire to be.

So, You Think You Can Write?

Don’t be mistaken – writing is not just about expressing your thoughts and ideas on paper. Successful fiction writing demands a profound understanding and mastery of literary concepts, structures, and techniques. We’ll begin our exploration by looking at some foundational texts that will give you the knowledge you need to start crafting your own literary world.

1. “Writing Fiction: Epic Worlds” by Orson Scott Card

Orson Scott Card’s “Writing Fiction: Epic Worlds” is a comprehensive book that focuses on constructing a strong foundation for your writing. Card is an esteemed science fiction writer, best known for his New York Times bestseller, Ender’s Game. In this book, he delves deep into the world-building aspect of fiction writing, presenting exercises and examples that help you craft the intricate tapestry of your novel’s universe.

2. “On Writing” by Stephen King

No list of books on proper writing in fiction would be complete without the inclusion of Stephen King’s memoir and writing guide. “On Writing” intertwines personal experiences and anecdotes with profound writing lessons. King sheds light on crucial aspects of fiction writing, including narrative voice, pacing, and the construction of suspense and horror. It’s essential to remember that proper writing etiquette applies not only to conventional fiction but also to horror, science fiction, and fantasy.

Characters Drive the Plot: Creating Lifelike Characters

Characters are the heart and soul of any fiction work. Without them, there would be no story to tell, no emotional journey for the reader to embark on. An essential component of lit-iquette is the understanding of character development and portrayal. Here are some books that could help take your character development to the next level.

1. “Characters & Viewpoints” by Orson Scott Card

Card’s expertise doesn’t end at world-building – in “Characters & Viewpoints,” he explores the different facets of crafting believable and relatable characters. This thought-provoking and extensive book delves into personality, motivation, speech patterns, and cultural backgrounds. By understanding the complexities of human nature in detail, you can create characters that will linger in the reader’s mind long after the book is closed.

2. “Writing the Breakout Novel” by Donald Maass

In “Writing the Breakout Novel,” Donald Maass focuses on the aspects of character that elevate a novel from ordinary to exceptional. This guidebook offers tangible advice and exercises that will help you strengthen the characters that anchor your story. With a deep understanding of character-driven fiction, your readers will grow genuinely invested in the struggles and triumphs of your characters.

The Language Evokes Emotion: Finding Your Voice

Your voice as a writer is the individual style and perspective you bring to your work. Exploring your voice, as well as incorporating the language that evokes emotion, is an essential part of mastering proper writing etiquette. Consider reading these books to enhance your linguistic skills and learn the art of storytelling through the written word.

1. “Bird by Bird: Some Instructions on Writing and Life” by Anne Lamott

Anne Lamott approaches writing with warmth and humor, offering sheer wisdom on the craft of writing. “Bird by Bird” teaches readers the importance of celebrating imperfection and finding solace in the small victories. In addition to character and plot development, Lamott provides advice on the subtler aspects of writing, such as point of view and voice, making this book a must-read for aspiring and seasoned fiction writers alike.

2. “The Elements of Style” by William Strunk Jr. and E.B. White

First published in 1959, “The Elements of Style” is a timeless book about the principles of English writing. In this concise guide, Strunk and White provide clear advice on sentence structure, word choice, and the art of using language effectively. Mastery of language is a defining aspect of proper writing etiquette in fiction, and this book demystifies the mechanics of strong, clear writing.

Embrace the Community: Discussion and Critique

No writer is an island, and the world of writing is a vibrant, supportive community. In the spirit of embracing this supportive atmosphere, it can be helpful to read books that focus on the collaboration and discussion within the realm of fiction writing.

1. “Writing the Natural Way: Toward a More Holistic Approach” by Gabriele Lusser Rico

In this practical guide, Gabriele Lusser Rico encourages readers to foster their connection to the world through writing. The book provides exercises and activities that tap into the natural creative process, further reflecting the importance of community, collaboration, and discussion within the writing realm.

2. “The Writer’s Journey: Mythic Structure for Writers” by Christopher Vogler

Drawing on the theories of mythic structures, “The Writer’s Journey” explores the framework shared by many classic stories. Vogler’s book provides a roadmap for creating compelling fiction, rooted in the timeless narrative patterns our ancestors have employed for generations. Sitting at the intersection of storytelling and strong writing etiquette, this book serves as an invaluable resource for writers seeking to resonate with readers on a deep level.

Lit-iquette: The Road to Literary Perfection

Mastering the craft of fiction writing takes time and practice, but the key is to explore the vast wealth of knowledge and resources available to you. By understanding and applying the lit-iquette outlined in these disparate books, you pave the path toward becoming a strong, confident writer, ready to share their stories with the world.

Although individuals learn differently, familiarizing yourself with the time-honored conventions of fiction writing is crucial to honing your skills. As you delve into these books, you’ll discover a stimulating exchange of ideas and discover the joys of engaging with other writers – both present and past. Transforming yourself into a skilled and proper writer of fiction is an ongoing journey, but with these books as your companions, you’ll find the terrain easier to navigate and the literary world more expansive.

By seeking to expand your knowledge of the intricacies of fiction writing, you not only become a better writer but also contribute to a thriving community of writers who passionately strive for perfection in their literary works.

An essential aspect of writing etiquette is the practice of continuous learning and growth. By diving into the world of fiction writing through these compelling and influential books, you’ll inevitably foster your talent and cultivate a sense of camaraderie within the community. In the pursuit of mastery in fiction, your work will gain depth, nuance, and the unforgettable artistry that literary works demand. Embrace the journey, and let the realm of lit-iquette guide you toward greater literary success.
