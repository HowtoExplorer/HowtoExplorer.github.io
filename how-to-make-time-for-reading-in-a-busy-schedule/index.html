How to Make Time for Reading in a Busy Schedule


In today’s fast-paced world, it’s easy to feel like there simply isn’t enough time for everything, especially for activities like reading. Between work, family responsibilities, social engagements, and other commitments, carving out time for a good book can seem like a luxury that most people can’t afford. However, the benefits of reading are undeniable, from personal growth and education to relaxation and stress reduction. If you’ve been wanting to make more time for reading but don’t know where to start, this post will guide you through some practical and effective strategies to incorporate reading into even the busiest schedules.

1. Set Clear Reading Goals

Setting clear and achievable reading goals is one of the most effective ways to make time for reading. Start by identifying what you want to accomplish with your reading—whether it’s finishing a certain number of books per month, reading for 20 minutes every day, or tackling specific genres or authors. When you know what you want to achieve, it becomes easier to prioritize reading in your day-to-day routine.

Start small to avoid feeling overwhelmed. If you’re not a regular reader, aim for one book a month or reading for 15 minutes a day. As you build the habit, you can gradually increase your goals.

2. Incorporate Reading into Your Daily Routine

One of the best ways to make time for reading is by integrating it into your existing routine. You don’t need to set aside hours at a time—little moments throughout the day can add up. Here are some ideas to consider:

  • Morning Routine: Many people start their day with a cup of coffee or tea. Why not pair that with a few minutes of reading? Even if you can only manage 10-15 minutes in the morning, it’s a great way to begin your day on a positive note.

  • Commute Time: If you take public transportation or carpool, use that time to read. Audiobooks are a fantastic option for commuters who drive, while reading on a tablet or e-reader is ideal for those who take buses, trains, or subways.

  • Lunch Breaks: Rather than spending your entire lunch hour scrolling through social media, try setting aside 10-15 minutes of that time to read. A few chapters can easily fit into this break, allowing you to finish books faster.

  • Before Bed: Instead of winding down with screens, consider reading for 20-30 minutes before going to sleep. Not only does this help you get in more reading, but it can also help you sleep better by replacing the blue light from your devices with the calming effect of a good book.

3. Utilize Audiobooks and Podcasts

Sometimes, traditional reading isn’t possible due to time constraints or physical limitations, but that doesn’t mean you have to miss out on books altogether. Audiobooks provide an excellent alternative, allowing you to listen while you’re on the go. Whether you’re doing household chores, cooking, walking, or exercising, audiobooks can turn otherwise wasted time into productive reading time.

Many audiobook platforms, such as Audible and Google Play Books, offer a wide range of titles across various genres. You can even adjust the speed of narration to fit your pace, which can be helpful if you’re trying to get through books quickly.

Similarly, podcasts are another great medium that often features book discussions or author interviews. You can listen to book summaries, literary insights, or even fiction through podcasts, which can enhance your reading experience and keep you engaged with new books and authors.

4. Create a Reading Nook or Space

Having a designated space for reading can make it easier to prioritize the activity in your daily routine. Whether it’s a cozy corner in your living room, a comfortable chair near a window, or even a spot in your local coffee shop, having a place where you can escape with a book is invaluable.

When you create a specific environment dedicated to reading, it mentally signals to your brain that it’s time to focus. This can make the experience more enjoyable and immersive, reducing distractions and helping you read more effectively.

If you don’t have the luxury of a quiet space at home, consider checking out libraries or bookstores with reading areas. Many public libraries are designed for comfort, and they offer a peaceful atmosphere where you can focus on reading without interruptions.

5. Make Use of Waiting Time

Throughout the day, there are plenty of moments when you’re waiting around. These often go unnoticed, but they can be the perfect time to get some reading in. Consider these examples:

  • Waiting for appointments: Whether it’s at the doctor’s office, the DMV, or any other location, waiting for an appointment often involves idle time. Instead of scrolling through your phone or aimlessly browsing, bring a book or e-reader with you.

  • Waiting for kids: If you’re a parent, you’re probably familiar with waiting for your children at extracurricular activities, playdates, or school pick-ups. Use this time to read, whether it’s a physical book or an e-book on your phone or tablet.

  • In line at the store: While waiting in line at a grocery store or retail outlet, pull out a book and read a few pages. These short bursts of reading can help you fit in a few chapters throughout the week.

6. Join a Reading Challenge or Book Club

Having external accountability is a powerful motivator for many people. Joining a reading challenge, whether it’s an online challenge like the Goodreads Reading Challenge or one organized by your local library, can encourage you to set reading goals and stick to them.

Similarly, joining a book club can provide motivation, as you’ll need to read a specific book each month in preparation for meetings or discussions. This also makes reading a social activity, where you can connect with others who share your love for books and enjoy lively discussions.

7. Make the Most of Shorter, Bite-Sized Reading

If you’re really struggling to find time for long reading sessions, consider reading shorter books or bite-sized content. There are many genres and formats to explore that can fit into your busy schedule. Novellas, short stories, and essays are all excellent options for those looking for shorter reads.

You can also explore content that can be consumed in smaller chunks, such as articles, essays, or excerpts of books. Many platforms, including Medium and Longreads, offer high-quality content that can be read in 15-30 minute segments.

Additionally, using apps or websites that aggregate news or book summaries can help you stay informed and engaged without needing to dedicate hours at a time.

8. Turn Reading into a Habit, Not a Task

Sometimes, the biggest obstacle to reading is simply not seeing it as part of your normal routine. If you treat reading like a task or another thing to check off your to-do list, it may feel burdensome rather than enjoyable. The key is to make reading a habit, something you naturally incorporate into your day.

To build this habit, start by setting realistic expectations and being patient with yourself. Even if you only read a few pages a day, the consistency will eventually add up. Over time, you’ll start looking forward to your reading sessions, and it won’t feel like something you have to make time for—it will become something you naturally want to do.

9. Minimize Distractions

In a world full of distractions, it’s easy to put off reading in favor of more immediate forms of entertainment, like watching TV or scrolling through social media. If you’re serious about making time for reading, consider minimizing distractions during your reading time.

  • Turn off notifications: Before you begin reading, put your phone on “Do Not Disturb” mode or turn off notifications to avoid interruptions.

  • Create a quiet environment: If you’re reading at home, choose a time when the house is relatively quiet. If you’re at a coffee shop, pick a corner away from loud conversations.

  • Avoid multitasking: While it might be tempting to try to read while doing other things, such as eating or watching TV, multitasking often reduces your ability to focus. It’s better to devote your full attention to the book, even if it’s just for a short period of time.

10. Be Kind to Yourself

Life is busy, and there will be days when it’s impossible to find time for reading. It’s important not to beat yourself up about it. Instead of focusing on missed opportunities, celebrate the progress you’ve made, no matter how small. Reading should be enjoyable, not a source of stress.

Remember that every page you read adds to your knowledge and experience. Even if you only manage a few minutes a day, those minutes can have a lasting impact on your personal and intellectual growth.

By incorporating these strategies into your daily routine, you’ll be able to find time for reading, even in the busiest of schedules. Whether you’re an avid reader looking to read more or someone who’s just starting to develop the habit, the key is to make reading a regular part of your life. With patience and consistency, you’ll soon be devouring books in ways you never thought possible. Happy reading!
