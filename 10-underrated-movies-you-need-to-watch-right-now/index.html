10 Underrated Movies You Need to Watch Right Now


In the world of cinema, there are countless gems that fly under the radar, often overshadowed by big-budget blockbusters or mainstream hits. These underrated movies may not have received the attention they deserve during their theatrical releases, but they offer compelling stories, brilliant performances, and unique perspectives. Whether you’re a fan of drama, comedy, horror, or thrillers, there’s something here for everyone. If you’re tired of the same old Hollywood fare, here’s a list of ten underrated movies that you need to watch right now.

1. A Ghost Story (2017)

Directed by David Lowery, A Ghost Story is a haunting and meditative film about life, death, and the passage of time. The film follows a recently deceased ghost (Casey Affleck) who returns to his home, donning a white sheet, to observe the lives of those who remain. Unlike typical ghost stories, this film is slow, reflective, and emotionally powerful, focusing on existential themes of grief, loss, and the passage of time.

What sets A Ghost Story apart is its ability to capture the essence of human experience in a way that is both subtle and profound. The minimalistic storytelling, paired with long takes and an ethereal score by Daniel Hart, elevates the film to a level of emotional depth rarely seen in mainstream cinema.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A thought-provoking exploration of life and death.
  • Beautiful cinematography and powerful performances.
  • A meditative, atmospheric pace that lingers long after the credits roll.

2. The Fall (2006)

Directed by Tarsem Singh, The Fall is a visually stunning film set in a 1920s Los Angeles hospital. The movie centers around a young girl named Alexandria (Catinca Untaru) who befriends a paralyzed stuntman (Lee Pace). As they form a bond, the stuntman spins an epic tale of heroes and villains that blurs the line between fantasy and reality.

The film is an unforgettable experience due to its breathtaking visuals, which were achieved by filming in real locations across the world, from India to South Africa. The cinematography and costume design are some of the most elaborate and beautiful seen in modern cinema, making it a true work of art. The performances from Pace and Untaru are also incredibly heartfelt, adding depth to the fantastical narrative.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A visual masterpiece that will leave you mesmerized.
  • A captivating and imaginative story that blends fantasy and reality.
  • Strong performances that bring the emotional heart of the film to life.

3. The Man from Earth (2007)

If you’re a fan of science fiction that doesn’t rely on flashy special effects, The Man from Earth is a hidden gem. Directed by Richard Schenkman, this low-budget film centers on a college professor (David Lee Smith) who announces to his colleagues that he is an immortal man who has lived for over 14,000 years. The entire film is set in one location, as the professor’s friends and colleagues challenge his extraordinary claim.

While The Man from Earth may not have the action-packed thrills of typical sci-fi films, its philosophical exploration of immortality, history, and human nature makes it a truly thought-provoking experience. The dialogue-driven plot and strong performances make this a standout film for those who appreciate intellectual cinema.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A unique and intellectual take on the sci-fi genre.
  • A small-scale, dialogue-driven film that sparks deep reflection.
  • Fascinating exploration of immortality and the human experience.

4. The Invitation (2015)

The Invitation, directed by Karyn Kusama, is a slow-burn psychological thriller that grips you from the very beginning and doesn’t let go. The story follows a man named Will (Logan Marshall-Green) who attends a dinner party at his ex-wife’s house, only to become increasingly suspicious of the strange behavior of the guests. As tensions rise and secrets are revealed, Will begins to question whether something far more sinister is going on.

What makes The Invitation so effective is its atmosphere. The film masterfully builds suspense and dread through subtle clues and unnerving interactions, rather than relying on shock value or gore. It’s a film that slowly and quietly gets under your skin, leading to a shocking and unsettling climax.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A slow-burn thriller that builds tension throughout.
  • Terrific performances that enhance the suspense and mystery.
  • A film that subverts expectations and delivers a chilling payoff.

5. Leave No Trace (2018)

Directed by Debra Granik, Leave No Trace is a heartfelt and quietly powerful drama about a father and daughter who live off the grid in the forests of Oregon. When their secluded lifestyle is discovered, they are forced to navigate the complexities of the outside world. The film explores themes of independence, isolation, and family in a profound way, with outstanding performances from Ben Foster and Thomasin McKenzie.

What sets Leave No Trace apart is its subtlety. It doesn’t rely on melodrama or overt sentimentality. Instead, it tells its story with quiet grace, capturing the emotional resonance of its characters’ experiences through understated moments and a patient pace. It’s a film that sticks with you long after you’ve seen it.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A quietly moving drama about family and independence.
  • Stunning performances that ground the film in reality.
  • A minimalist storytelling approach that leaves a lasting emotional impact.

6. Moon (2009)

Directed by Duncan Jones, Moon is a thought-provoking science fiction film that takes place on a lunar base where a man named Sam Bell (Sam Rockwell) is nearing the end of his three-year contract. As his time on the moon nears its conclusion, Sam begins to experience strange occurrences that make him question his sanity and the truth about his mission.

Moon is an intelligent and introspective film that delves deep into themes of identity, isolation, and what it means to be human. Sam Rockwell’s performance is nothing short of exceptional, carrying the film with his emotional depth and vulnerability. The film’s minimalist style and haunting score by Clint Mansell make it a truly unforgettable experience.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A cerebral and emotionally engaging sci-fi film.
  • Sam Rockwell’s exceptional performance.
  • A thought-provoking exploration of isolation and identity.

7. Coherence (2013)

Coherence, directed by James Ward Byrkit, is a low-budget sci-fi thriller that will leave you questioning everything you thought you knew about reality. The film takes place during a dinner party, where a group of friends begins to experience strange and inexplicable occurrences when a comet passes overhead. As the night progresses, the group finds themselves confronting alternate realities and parallel universes.

What makes Coherence so compelling is its ability to create tension and intrigue with a limited budget and minimalistic setting. The film is dialogue-driven and relies heavily on its characters’ reactions to the surreal events unfolding around them. It’s a mind-bending thriller that will keep you on the edge of your seat until the very end.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A gripping, mind-bending thriller that explores alternate realities.
  • Clever storytelling that keeps you guessing.
  • A low-budget film that makes the most of its concept.

8. The Proposition (2005)

Set in the harsh, unforgiving Australian outback of the 1880s, The Proposition is a brutal and poetic western directed by John Hillcoat. The film follows a lawman (Guy Pearce) who is tasked with capturing his older brother, Arthur (Danny Huston), a notorious outlaw. As the story unfolds, the characters are forced to confront the violent and morally complex nature of their actions.

With a screenplay by Nick Cave, the film features sharp dialogue, strong character development, and stunning cinematography. The atmosphere is oppressive and visceral, creating a sense of dread that lingers throughout. The Proposition is a bleak, uncompromising western that is often overlooked but deserves far more recognition.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A gripping, morally complex western with a unique setting.
  • Stunning cinematography that captures the brutality of the Australian outback.
  • Strong performances and sharp writing.

9. The Voices (2014)

The Voices, directed by Marjane Satrapi, is a darkly comedic horror film that follows Jerry (Ryan Reynolds), a seemingly ordinary man who works at a factory and lives in a small town. However, Jerry begins to hear voices from his pets, a dog and a cat, and his reality starts to unravel in increasingly disturbing ways.

What makes The Voices so unique is its blend of dark comedy, psychological horror, and emotional depth. Reynolds gives an exceptional performance, balancing the film’s absurd humor with its more unsettling elements. The movie is a strange and offbeat ride that explores mental illness, loneliness, and the consequences of one’s actions in an unpredictable and memorable way.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A unique blend of horror, comedy, and psychological drama.
  • Ryan Reynolds’ impressive performance.
  • A dark and twisted film that will keep you entertained and engaged.

10. Cold in July (2014)

Cold in July, directed by Jim Mickle, is a neo-noir thriller set in 1980s Texas. The film follows Richard Dane (Michael C. Hall), a family man who inadvertently kills a home intruder in self-defense. As he becomes embroiled in a web of conspiracy and violence, he uncovers dark secrets that force him to confront his own morality and sense of justice.

The film is an atmospheric, tension-filled thriller with strong performances from the cast, particularly Hall, Sam Shepard, and Don Johnson. It’s a gripping exploration of guilt, revenge, and the blurred lines between good and evil. Cold in July is a hidden gem in the neo-noir genre that deserves more attention.

Why You Should Watch It:

  • A tense, atmospheric neo-noir thriller.
  • Strong performances and a gripping plot.
  • A morally complex story that keeps you hooked until the end.

Whether you’re looking for a deep emotional experience, a thrilling ride, or a visually stunning masterpiece, these underrated movies offer something unique and worthwhile. They might not have received the widespread recognition they deserve, but they each bring something special to the table. So, if you’re in the mood for something different, consider watching one of these hidden gems—you won’t be disappointed.
