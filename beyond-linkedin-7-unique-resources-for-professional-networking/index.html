Beyond LinkedIn: 7 Unique Resources for Professional Networking


Welcome fellow professionals! In today’s interconnected world, networking plays a crucial role in career growth and personal development. But let’s face it, we can’t just rely on LinkedIn for all our networking needs. So, I’ve rounded up seven unique resources that can help you expand your professional network while keeping things interesting. From niche-based communities to skill-sharing platforms, these tools will not only spark your creativity but also enhance your professional toolkit.

  1. Meetup Who says networking needs to be formal? Meetup is a platform that encourages people from all walks of life to connect through their shared interests. Create or join groups focused on professional development or industry interests, and you’ll be amazed at the connections you’ll form. Whether you’re looking for casual get-togethers or more formal networking opportunities, Meetup offers a variety of options to step out of your comfort zone and expand your network.

  2. Slack For those in the tech industry or working remotely, Slack is the perfect platform to network with like-minded individuals. With communities ranging from startups to specific programming languages, Slack offers a mix of open channels and private groups, tailored to your professional needs. You might find your next freelancer, mentor, or job opportunity all within the same platform – simply by engaging in conversations and sharing your expertise.

  3. Xing Xing is the go-to professional networking platform for German-speaking countries. With features similar to LinkedIn, Xing takes networking to the next level by also offering conference sharing, event management, and a job board. This platform not only allows you to expand your network but also keeps you informed about the latest industry trends.

  4. Hashnode For those seeking to grow their community around writing, Hashnode is an amazing resource. It’s a blogging community that features different topics, tech being the most prevalent. By blogging and connecting with fellow writers, not only can you share your knowledge and expertise, but you’ll also receive tailored feedback and grow a trusted network of peers and potential collaborators.

  5. Remo Looking for virtual networking experiences? Remo is an interactive online platform that brings the feeling of a face-to-face event to your screen. Host virtual conferences, job fairs, or networking events on Remo and connect with professionals from around the world, all from the safety of your home.

  6. Skillshare Skillshare is a platform where you can grow both personally and professionally by learning new skills or sharing your expertise. By creating and participating in classes, you’ll form connections with individuals who share common goals or are curious about your field. This can lead to collaborations, job opportunities, or even friendships.

  7. Eventbrite Eventbrite connects you with global events in your area of interest – be it conferences, workshops, or meet-ups. This platform is perfect not just for attending, but also organizing your own events to foster a sense of community with like-minded professionals. Use Eventbrite to discover new ideas, forge connections, and showcase your event hosting skills.

In conclusion, the world of professional networking is vast and full of possibilities beyond LinkedIn. Embrace these resources to connect with different professionals and create lasting connections. Your career is sure to thrive with the help of these unique platforms, so go ahead and explore the vast networking landscape today!
