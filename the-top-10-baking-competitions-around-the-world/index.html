The Top 10 Baking Competitions Around the World


Baking competitions have become a staple in popular culture, with reality TV shows like "The Great British Baking Show" and "Cupcake Wars" captivating audiences worldwide. But have you ever wondered where these talented bakers hone their skills and compete against the best of the best? From France to Australia, here are the top 10 baking competitions around the world that are not to be missed.

  1. Meilleur Ouvrier Patissier (MOP), France Held every three years in Paris, MOP is considered the holy grail of baking competitions. This prestigious event attracts top pastry chefs from around the world, who are put to the test in a series of rigorous challenges. Contestants must demonstrate their skills in classic French baking techniques, as well as their ability to innovate and think on their feet. French patisserie is renowned for its precision and intricacy, and MOP is no exception. Contestants must showcase their mastery of intricate designs, precise temperature control, and flawless technique. The competition is intense, with only the most skilled bakers emerging victorious.
  2. Bakels International Cup, Australia Held in Melbourne, Australia, the Bakels International Cup is one of the most respected baking competitions in the world. This biennial event attracts top bakers from around the world, who compete in teams of four to create exquisite pastries, cakes, and breads. The competition is incredibly demanding, with contestants working under intense time pressure to create 15 separate desserts and breads within a five-hour window. A panel of experts then judges the creations, looking for perfection in taste, texture, and presentation.
  3. Eurolisse World Pastry Cup, France Held in Paris, the Eurolisse World Pastry Cup is another esteemed baking competition that showcases the skills of the world's top pastry chefs. Established in 1993, this prestigious event attracts an all-star lineup of bakers, who compete in a series of thrilling challenges. From intricate sugar sculptures to decadent chocolate cakes, contestants must demonstrate their mastery of a wide range of pastry techniques. The judges, a panel of expert pastry chefs, are notoriously tough, and contestants must bring their A-game to impress.
  4. Cloth Men age Blood Sugar Competition, Japan In this fascinating baking competition, Japanese pastry chefs compete in teams to create innovative, all-natural pastries that showcase the beauty of the natural world. Called "blood sugar," this unique form of pastry art combines botanical and culinary expertise, resulting in exquisite confections that blur the line between art and food. Judges praise contestants who can bring "delicious of art" to life, crafting innovative sweet and savory concoctions that transport judges to the countryside. This celebrated sweet art tradition continues to reverberate around the globe.
  5. BiênHuỗi Bake Competition, Vietnam Unusually situated in Southeast Asia, the BiênHuồi Bake Competition brings talented bakers from around the world to the sweet cultural capital – Hanoi. Vietnamese desserts like pink rice cakes are famously contemporary and evoke an astonishing coincidence between its traditional classics.
