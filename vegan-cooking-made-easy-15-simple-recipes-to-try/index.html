Vegan Cooking Made Easy: 15 Simple Recipes to Try


Vegan cooking is a fantastic way to nourish your body while supporting a sustainable lifestyle. Whether you’re a seasoned plant-based chef or just beginning your culinary journey, this blog will guide you through 15 easy vegan recipes that are perfect for any occasion. These dishes are not only delicious but also simple to make, ensuring you spend less time in the kitchen and more time enjoying your meals.

1. Vegan Lentil Soup

Lentil soup is one of the most comforting and nutritious meals. It’s rich in fiber, protein, and iron, making it an excellent choice for a hearty vegan dinner. With a few basic ingredients, you can prepare this simple soup in less than an hour.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried green lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, celery, and garlic, sautéing until softened (about 5 minutes).
  2. Add the lentils, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, and bay leaf. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  3. Lower the heat and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the lentils are tender.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove the bay leaf before serving.

This soup is full of flavor, and its thick, satisfying texture makes it an ideal winter meal.

2. Chickpea Salad Sandwich

For a quick and easy lunch, chickpea salad sandwiches are perfect. They’re creamy, flavorful, and packed with protein.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and mashed
  • 1/4 cup vegan mayo
  • 1 tbsp mustard
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 slices whole-grain bread
  • Lettuce, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mash the chickpeas with a fork until they’re slightly chunky.
  2. Mix in the vegan mayo, mustard, celery, onion, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread the chickpea mixture onto one slice of bread, then top with lettuce and the second slice of bread.
  4. Serve with a side of fresh veggies or chips.

This chickpea salad is an excellent alternative to tuna salad, offering the same creamy texture and satisfying flavor.

3. Vegan Tacos

Tacos are a fun and customizable meal, and they’re just as delicious when made vegan. The beauty of this recipe lies in its versatility—you can add your favorite veggies or beans and top them with various sauces and condiments.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Corn tortillas
  • Salsa, cilantro, and lime for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion, corn, cumin, and chili powder. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until the veggies are tender.
  2. Stir in the black beans and cook for another 2-3 minutes to warm through.
  3. Warm the tortillas in a dry pan or microwave.
  4. Assemble the tacos by filling each tortilla with the veggie and bean mixture, then top with avocado slices, salsa, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.

These vegan tacos are fresh, spicy, and packed with flavor, making them a go-to for Taco Tuesday or any casual meal.

4. Vegan Stir-Fry

A quick stir-fry is a great option when you’re short on time but still want something healthy and filling. This simple stir-fry is loaded with colorful vegetables, and the savory sauce ties it all together.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1/2 cup snap peas
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp ginger, grated
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and ginger and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Add the vegetables and stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes, until they are tender but still crisp.
  3. Stir in the soy sauce and hoisin sauce and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice for a filling meal.

This vegan stir-fry is simple, healthy, and packed with vibrant flavors.

5. Vegan Pasta Primavera

Pasta primavera is a light, vegetable-filled dish that’s perfect for warmer weather. The fresh vegetables pair wonderfully with the pasta, and a light olive oil dressing keeps the flavors bright and fresh.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pasta of your choice
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to the package directions.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a skillet and sauté the garlic for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Add the zucchini, bell pepper, and cherry tomatoes. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  4. Toss the cooked pasta with the sautéed vegetables, fresh basil, salt, and pepper.
  5. Serve warm with extra basil on top.

This simple yet flavorful dish is perfect for a light lunch or dinner.

6. Vegan Buddha Bowl

Buddha bowls are a great way to pack a variety of nutrients into one meal. By combining grains, veggies, and a protein source, you’ll have a well-rounded, satisfying dish.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup chickpeas, roasted
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup cucumber, sliced
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook quinoa according to package instructions.
  2. Roast chickpeas by tossing them in olive oil, salt, and pepper, then baking at 400°F for 20-25 minutes until crispy.
  3. Assemble the bowl by adding quinoa, roasted chickpeas, avocado slices, carrots, and cucumber.
  4. Drizzle tahini and lemon juice over the top, and season with salt and pepper.

This Buddha bowl is a nutritious and filling meal that combines healthy fats, protein, and fiber.

7. Vegan Pancakes

Start your day with a stack of fluffy vegan pancakes. These pancakes are soft, light, and perfectly sweet without the need for eggs or dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix flour, baking powder, sugar, and salt.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together almond milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until just combined.
  4. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and pour the batter in, forming small pancakes.
  5. Cook each pancake for about 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown.
  6. Serve with maple syrup and fresh fruit.

These pancakes are perfect for a weekend brunch or a sweet breakfast treat.

8. Vegan Chili

Vegan chili is a hearty, filling dish that’s perfect for meal prep or a cozy dinner. Packed with beans and vegetables, it’s full of nutrients and flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can kidney beans, drained
  • 1 can black beans, drained
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot. Add onion, bell pepper, and garlic, and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the beans, tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  3. Simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, and adjust seasoning as needed.
  4. Serve hot with vegan sour cream or avocado on top.

This chili is a flavorful, filling dish that can be enjoyed year-round.

9. Vegan Mac and Cheese

A vegan twist on a classic comfort food, this creamy mac and cheese is made with cashews for a rich, cheesy flavor without any dairy.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pasta
  • 1/2 cup raw cashews, soaked
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 garlic clove
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the pasta according to package directions.
  2. In a blender, combine soaked cashews, nutritional yeast, water, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  3. Toss the cooked pasta with the creamy sauce and serve warm.

This vegan mac and cheese is indulgent and comforting, perfect for any time you’re craving something cheesy.

10. Vegan Smoothie Bowl

Smoothie bowls are a great way to start the day with a nutrient-packed breakfast. With a base of blended fruits and a topping of granola, nuts, and seeds, this meal is both delicious and energizing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 frozen banana
  • 1/2 cup frozen berries
  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • Granola and sliced fruits for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the banana, berries, and almond milk. Blend until smooth.
  2. Pour the smoothie into a bowl and top with granola, sliced fruits, and chia seeds.

This smoothie bowl is a great way to pack in your daily servings of fruits and fiber.

11. Vegan Veggie Burgers

Making your own vegan burgers is a fun and rewarding experience. These veggie burgers are hearty, flavorful, and easy to customize.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can black beans, mashed
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup grated carrot
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine mashed beans, breadcrumbs, carrot, onion, soy sauce, and garlic powder.
  2. Form the mixture into patties.
  3. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat and cook the patties for 3-4 minutes on each side.
  4. Serve on a bun with your favorite toppings.

These vegan veggie burgers are a healthy and satisfying alternative to traditional burgers.

12. Vegan Pizza

Pizza night is always a hit, and with a vegan pizza, you don’t have to miss out on the fun. Use a store-bought dough or make your own for a truly homemade pizza experience.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pizza dough
  • 1/2 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup vegan mozzarella cheese
  • Toppings: mushrooms, onions, bell peppers, olives, etc.

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven according to pizza dough instructions.
  2. Roll out the dough on a baking sheet and spread tomato sauce over the surface.
  3. Top with vegan cheese and your desired toppings.
  4. Bake according to the dough instructions until golden and crispy.

Vegan pizza is perfect for a casual dinner with friends or family.

13. Vegan Sweets: Chocolate Avocado Mousse

Who says you can’t enjoy dessert while eating plant-based? This creamy chocolate mousse is made with avocado for a healthy, rich dessert.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine all ingredients and blend until smooth.
  2. Chill in the fridge for an hour before serving.

This mousse is a decadent yet healthy treat that will satisfy any sweet tooth.

14. Vegan Burrito Bowls

Burrito bowls are a customizable and satisfying meal. Layered with rice, beans, veggies, and your favorite toppings, they’re perfect for meal prepping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked brown rice
  • 1 can black beans, drained
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 cup corn kernels
  • 1/4 cup salsa
  • Lime wedges for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Layer cooked rice, beans, corn, and avocado in a bowl.
  2. Top with salsa and a squeeze of lime juice.

This simple burrito bowl is a quick and easy meal that’s full of flavor.

15. Vegan Pesto Pasta

Pesto pasta is a fresh, vibrant dish that’s full of flavor. The pesto sauce can be made with fresh basil and nutritional yeast for a cheesy, plant-based version.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pasta
  • 2 cups fresh basil
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions.
  2. In a blender, combine basil, pine nuts, garlic, olive oil, and nutritional yeast. Blend until smooth.
  3. Toss the cooked pasta with the pesto and serve warm.

This vegan pesto pasta is a quick and flavorful meal that anyone will love.

With these 15 easy and delicious vegan recipes, you can embrace plant-based cooking without the stress. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or feeding a family, these dishes offer variety and flavors to suit every palate. Happy cooking!
