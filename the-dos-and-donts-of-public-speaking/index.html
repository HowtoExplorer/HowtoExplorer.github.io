The Dos and Don'ts of Public Speaking


Have you ever wondered how some people can confidently engage an audience, while others stumble and fumble through their words? Public speaking is an art that can be mastered with practice and understanding. From big stages to company meetings, having the ability to effectively communicate your ideas can be incredibly powerful. In this guide, we’ll delve into the dos and don’ts of public speaking, ensuring you leave your audience both enlightened and entertained.

Ready, Set…Speak!

A great start to any speech is an engaging opening. A powerful opener can capture your audience’s attention and ignite their curiosity. Be it a surprising statistic, a personal story, or a thought-provoking question, a strong opening will set the tone for the rest of your speech.

Be Prepared

When it comes to public speaking, preparation is key. Take the time to research your topic thoroughly and create an outline for your speech. Structure your thoughts logically and make notes to guide you during your presentation. Additionally, consider practicing your delivery, ensuring smooth transitions and a natural flow to your speech.

Avoid Readiness

While it’s excellent to be prepared, it’s equally important not to overdo it. Avoid the temptation to memorize your speech word-for-word. Memorizing a speech can result in a stiff delivery, losing the natural and engaging feel that an unprepared speech carries. It can also become problematic if you encounter an unforeseen situation, such as a technical glitch or a floor collapse, leaving you helpless without your lines. Embrace the power of your knowledge and be ready to adapt.

Master the Art of Eye Contact

Engaging your audience is crucial for maintaining their attention. Eye contact is an effective tool for maintaining this connection. Scan the room, taking turns connecting with individuals, ensuring you address everyone throughout the space. This technique can also help alleviate anxiety and make you appear more confident.

Don’t Ignore Your Audience

Public speaking is not a one-way street. Remember that you’re delivering a message for a purpose - to engage your audience. Ask them questions, invite comments, and elicit laughter or even tears. Send and receive energy throughout the room, creating a lively and engaging atmosphere.

Own the Stage

A successful speech requires confidence and conviction. Trust in your knowledge and deliver your message passionately. Be authentic and express your emotions freely. Embody your message and show your audience that you genuinely care.

Leave Your Audience Wanting More

End your speech with a bang! A strong conclusion can be the deciding factor in whether your audience remembers your speech long after it’s over. Leave your audience with a memorable statement, question, or call-to-action. This can provoke thought, incite change, or spur further conversation.

Closing Remarks

Public speaking can be a terrifying experience, but with proper preparation, mindset, and a desire to engage your audience, you’ll be well on your way to delivering mesmerizing speeches. Remember to follow the dos and don’ts outlined, and continue practicing to become the captivating public speaker you aspire to be. With practice, your confidence in public speaking will flourish, and so will your opportunities to captivate any audience.
