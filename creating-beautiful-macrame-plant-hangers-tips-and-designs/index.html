Creating Beautiful Macrame Plant Hangers: Tips and Designs


If you’re looking to add a touch of bohemian charm to your home decor, macrame plant hangers are the perfect solution. These beautiful, handcrafted creations not only elevate the look of your indoor or outdoor space but also provide a stylish way to display your favorite plants. In this blog post, we’ll explore some tips and designs to help you create stunning macrame plant hangers that will be the envy of all your friends.

Getting Started

To begin your macrame plant hanger journey, you’ll need a few basic supplies:

  • Macrame cord: Choose a sturdy and durable cord that complements your aesthetic preferences. Cotton cord is a popular choice due to its natural look and feel.
  • Scissors: A sharp pair of scissors will come in handy for cutting the cord to the desired length.
  • Plant pot: Select a pot that fits well with the size of your plant and complements the overall design of your hanger.
  • Beads (optional): If you want to add a touch of visual interest to your hanger, consider incorporating beads into the design. Choose beads that match your personal style and the color scheme of your space.

Basic Knots

Macrame plant hangers are created using various knots, and mastering these basic knots is crucial to creating beautiful designs. Here are three essential knots to get you started:

  1. Square Knot: This knot forms the foundation of most macrame designs. To create a square knot, follow these steps:
  • Take four cords and divide them into two groups of two.
  • Cross the left group of cords over the right group.
  • Pass the left cords under the right cords and through the loop created on the right side.
  • Pull both sides tightly to secure the knot.
  1. Double Half Hitch Knot: This knot is used to create a series of loops that hold the plant pot securely. To make a double half hitch knot, follow these steps:
  • Take two adjacent cords and position them in an inverted “V” shape.
  • Bring the right cord over the left cord and then behind it.
  • Pass the right cord through the loop created on the left side.
  • Pull both cords tightly to secure the knot.
  • Repeat the process with the next set of adjacent cords.
  1. Lark’s Head Knot: This simple knot is used to attach the cord to the plant pot. To create a lark’s head knot, follow these steps:
  • Fold a cord in half and position the folded part behind the plant pot.
  • Pull the loose ends of the cord through the folded part, creating a loop.
  • Pull both ends of the cord tightly to secure the knot.

Design Inspiration

Now that you have a grasp of the basic knots, let’s explore some design ideas to inspire your macrame plant hanger creations:

  1. Classic Spiral Design: Create a classic macrame plant hanger by using a series of square knots and alternating them with double half hitch knots. Add beads to the design for an extra touch of elegance.

  2. Tassel Time: Incorporate tassels into your design by leaving some extra length at the bottom of your plant hanger. Trim the ends to create a tassel effect.

  3. Multiple Tiers: Create a stunning visual impact by incorporating multiple tiers into your macrame plant hanger. Start with a longer cord length and use double half hitch knots to create the tiers at different heights.

  4. Colorful Accents: Add pops of color to your macrame plant hanger by using cords in different shades. Experiment with ombre effects or create a rainbow-inspired design.

Remember, the possibilities are endless when it comes to macrame plant hanger designs. Let your creativity flow and experiment with different knots, patterns, and embellishments to create unique and beautiful hangers that reflect your personal style.

Caring for Your Macrame Plant Hanger

To ensure the longevity of your macrame plant hanger, it’s essential to provide proper care. Here are a few tips:

  • Regularly dust your plant hanger to prevent dirt buildup.
  • Avoid placing your hanger in direct sunlight for extended periods, as this can cause the cords to fade or weaken.
  • If your plant hanger gets wet, allow it to air dry completely to prevent mold or mildew growth.
  • Consider using a saucer or tray under your plant pot to catch any excess water and protect your hanger from potential water damage.

By following these care tips, your macrame plant hanger will continue to bring beauty and charm to your space for years to come.

Conclusion

Creating beautiful macrame plant hangers is a rewarding and creative endeavor. With a few basic supplies and some practice, you can elevate your home decor and showcase your green thumb in style. Remember to experiment with different knots, designs, and colors to create unique hangers that reflect your personal taste. Happy knotting!
