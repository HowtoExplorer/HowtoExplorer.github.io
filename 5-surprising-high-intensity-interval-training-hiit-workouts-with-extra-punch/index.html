5 Surprising High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) Workouts with Extra Punch


Have you gotten stuck in a fitness routine rut and seeking ways to spice up your workouts? High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) can be an effective solution to this problem. These workouts not only help boost your endurance, strength, and speed but also help enhance your metabolism and fat loss capabilities. Here are five surprising and invigorating HIIT workouts that will give your exercise routine an extra punch.

1. Trail Running HIIT

Embrace the great outdoors and combine the thrill of running with HIIT workouts. Choose a nearby trail or dirt road with ample obstacles like hills or roots to incorporate into your workout. Perform your running HIIT session by integrating a series of short sprints, hills, and uphill running. Regularly changing speeds and inclines will keep your workout unpredictable and high-intensity.

Interval Example:

  • Warm-up: Walk for 5 minutes, and then start the intervals.
  • High-Intensity Interval: 30-second sprint, followed by 60 seconds of walking at your most relaxed pace.
  • Recovery Interval: 2 minutes of light jogging or walking.
  • Repeat the interval six times in total.

2. Kickboxing HIIT

Kickboxing is an excellent full-body workout that will leave you feeling energized and strong. Pair it with HIIT to maximize the benefits and learn some powerful self-defense techniques at the same time. Set up a boxing bag or use sturdy pads, and integrate various punches and kicks into your workout.

Interval Example:

  • Warm-up: 5 minutes of shadowboxing.
  • High-Intensity Interval: 45 seconds of kickboxing combos (alternating punches and kicks).
  • Recovery Interval: 15 seconds of relaxation.
  • Repeat the interval ten times in total.

3. Rowing Machine HIIT

Rowing is an underappreciated full-body workout that complements any HIIT routine. Set up a rowing machine, and choose a monitor with speed and distance metrics. Having this data will allow you to track your improvements and adapt the workouts accordingly.

Interval Example:

  • Warm-up: Five minutes of easy rowing with minimal resistance.
  • High-Intensity Interval: Row 500 meters as quickly as possible, focusing on maintaining a fast stroke rate.
  • Recovery Interval: Three minutes of leisurely rowing.
  • Repeat the interval five times in total.

4. Plyometric HIIT

Plyometric exercises are explosive movements that develop your muscles’ ability to recruit and generate power. Break outs of plyometric exercises will provide a refreshing twist to traditional HIIT workouts and challenge both your strength and agility.

Interval Example:

  • Warm-up: Practice 5 minutes of jumping jacks and bodyweight squats.
  • High-Intensity Interval: Perform 10 hill jumps (jumping over a bench or box), followed by 3 jump squats.
  • Recovery Interval: Walk for one minute to recover.
  • Repeat the interval ten times in total.

5. Obstacle Course HIIT

Add an element of surprise to your HIIT workouts by constructing a simple obstacle course. Incorporate various fitness-friendly elements like hurdles, cones, rope climbs, and suspension straps to create a challenging full-body workout.

Interval Example:

  • Warm-up: Walk for 5 minutes around the obstacle course.
  • High-Intensity Interval: Perform each obstacle 3 times without stopping:
    • 30-second squat jumps over a hurdle.
    • Jump rope for 60 seconds.
    • Plank hold while pulling yourself across the room using a suspension strap.
  • Recovery Interval: A one-minute walk to recover between each series of obstacles.
  • Repeat the interval four times in total.

HIIT workouts have consistently proven to challenge and benefit those who consistently engage in this training method. By incorporating these five unique workout styles, you’ll continually surprise your body and mind. Keep pushing the limits, but remember to mix things up and give yourself adequate rest and recovery periods to avoid injuries or burnouts. Be persistent, and you’ll see the advantages this powerful workout style offers in no time. Happy training!
