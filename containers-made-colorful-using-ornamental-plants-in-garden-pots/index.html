Containers Made Colorful: Using Ornamental Plants in Garden Pots


Welcome to container gardening, where imagination knows no bounds! Today, we’ll be diving deep into the world of colorful containers filled with ornamental plants that will breathe life into your outdoor space. No matter the size of your space or your experience with gardening, you’ll find this guide a treasure trove of inspiration.

Why go for ornamental plants in garden pots?

Ornamental plants have long been used by garden enthusiasts to create a sensory experience that goes beyond the visual. Their unique scents, textures, and sounds can add a new dimension to your garden. In containers, they offer the advantage of taking up less space, making them ideal additions for small gardens, patios, terraces, or balconies.

Choosing the right plants

Consider your space’s environment

The first step in choosing ornamental plants is taking your garden’s environmental factors into account. Consider the light conditions, soil type, and preferred microclimate. This information will help narrow down your choices so that the plants you select will thrive in your garden.

Pick your theme

Now that you know your garden’s conditions well, it’s time to decide on a theme for your container garden. Themes can be anything from combinations of color, contrasting heights, or a focal point with subtler accents. Remember to factor in the overall maintenance required for your chosen theme while selecting plants.

Selecting the perfect container

Function over form

While choosing containers, always keep in mind their primary purpose: to support and enhance the plants. Durability and good drainage are more important than the aesthetic appeal of the container. You can also upcycle old bottles, barrels, or baskets to add uniqueness to your garden.

Earthenware or terracotta, plastic, or metal

Different materials offer unique benefits. Earthenware or terracotta containers help regulate moisture in the soil, while plastic ones are lightweight, making them easier to handle and reposition when needed. Metal containers, especially copper, add an elegance and shine that can elevate the beauty of your ornamental plants.

Size does matter

The size of the container has a direct impact on the health and growth of your ornamental plants. Smaller containers are more suited to plants that grow compactly or have shallow root systems. If you’re going for a taller design, ensure the container has sturdy support to prevent toppling over in stormy weather.

Planting and maintenance tips

Make a lush first impression

Choose a vibrant, bold plant to be your “anchor” and place it in the center or at the back of your container, where it will make the most impact.

Don’t forget the fillers and spill-over

Fillers and spill-over plants are the perfect way to add drama and contrast to your container garden. These might cascade over the edge of your container or trail along the ground.

Regular maintenance

All plants have specific maintenance requirements, but container gardening simplifies this process. Regularly monitor the health of the plants, insuring sufficient water and nutrients. While deadheading (the removal of dead or dying flowers) is less essential with ornamental plants, a snip here and there can enhance the beauty of your container garden.

Embrace the change

Remember that container gardens are always evolving. As plants grow or change, don’t hesitate to adjust their positions in the container garden to create the desired look. Embrace the flexibility that comes with container gardening and enjoy tailor-made seasonal arrangements.

In conclusion, with the right plants, selected in harmony with your garden’s environment, contained within appealing containers, you can create a breathtaking garden space. Embrace the joy and creativity that containers offer, and don’t be afraid to swap out plants to create new combinations as the seasons change. So, start your journey into the visually stimulating and sensorially enriching world of ornamental plants in containers today!
