Chasing the Email Fence: Interview Follow-Ups Made Easy


Interview follow-ups, sometimes known as ‘soft follow-ups,’ are a candidate’s best chance to stand out in a crowded job market. While most will send a thank you email after meeting with a potential employer, even less will find the courage to chase the email fence and confidently reach out for additional updates. In this article, we explore how to make follow-up emails efficient, targeted, and productive by discussing essential tips related to the timing of follow-ups, the substance of follow-up emails, and the post-script etiquette.

The Timing of Interview Follow-Ups

The timing of follow-ups varies depending on whether the interview has resulted in a favorable or unfavorable outcome. In the case of an in-person interview or video interview, follow-up emails are typically sent within 24 to 48 hours. On the other hand, during bulk phone interviews or when the hiring process is extended over several days, a longer interval is considered appropriate.

The Substance of Your Follow-Up Emails

In crafting a well-structured follow-up email, start by expressing your gratitude for the opportunity to interview. Reflect on each discussion point brought up in the interview, demonstrating that you retain the key details. If possible, also reference any concrete questions you are excited to continue exploring with them. Whether you received a job offer or you’re just waiting to hear back, show your interest and eagerness to receive updates at an appropriate time.

Post-Script Etiquette

While some professionals recommend including a post-script in follow-up emails, a more direct and personal approach is preferred. Including a P.S. can make your email appear too commercial, as if you’ve relied on a template. If you’d like to include a statement about them reviewing your application materials, consider adding it directly into the body of the email, rather than tacking it onto the end.

To perform the pursuit of the email fence effectively, consistency is essential. Keep the following suggestions in mind:

  1. Keep it Personal: Rather than using a time-worn formula or cranked-out email, show some personality and convey the individuality that makes you a unique candidate.
  2. Authentically Express Interest: Underline your passion for the job and organization and communicate your longing for the opportunity to contribute.
  3. Demonstrate Credibility: Follow-up emails present another chance to strengthen your case. Display again the skills, credentials and experience that could bring value to the role.
  4. Be Direct: Make it easy for the recipient to respond. Be sincere and direct in your requests, ensuring that you make yourself available for any additional questions or a second round of discussions.

In conclusion, chasing the email fence can be an intimidating prospect, but it’s a gamble worth taking. There’s no better time than now to chase your opportunities and distinguish yourself as a top candidate. Engage your audience, build your credibility, and keep at it – the payoff will be worth it in the long run.
