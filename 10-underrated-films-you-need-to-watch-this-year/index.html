10 Underrated Films You Need to Watch This Year


In a world filled with blockbuster hits and mainstream cinema, it’s easy to overlook some hidden gems that deserve our attention. These underrated films often provide unique storytelling, innovative filmmaking techniques, and profound themes that resonate deeply. Here’s a curated list of ten underrated films that you must watch this year. Each of these films offers a distinct experience that will leave you thinking long after the credits roll.

1. The Fall (2006)

Directed by Tarsem Singh, The Fall is a visually stunning film that combines fantasy and reality in a mesmerizing way. Set in a Los Angeles hospital in the 1920s, the story follows Roy Walker, a stuntman who befriends a young immigrant girl named Alexandria. As Roy spins an epic tale of adventure, the lines between fiction and reality begin to blur. The film is notable for its breathtaking cinematography, filmed in over 20 countries, and its exploration of themes such as friendship, imagination, and the power of storytelling. The Fall might not have garnered mainstream success, but its artistic merit and emotional depth make it a must-see.

2. The Secret of Kells (2009)

This animated feature, directed by Tomm Moore and Nora Twomey, beautifully combines history with folklore in a way that few films achieve. The Secret of Kells tells the story of a young boy named Brendan who embarks on an adventure to complete the legendary Book of Kells, a beautifully illustrated manuscript. The film is renowned for its unique animation style, inspired by Celtic art, and its rich narrative that explores themes of creativity and the importance of preserving culture. The lush visuals and enchanting score elevate this film into the realm of art, making it a treasure that deserves more recognition.

3. A Ghost Story (2017)

David Lowery’s A Ghost Story is a profound exploration of time, loss, and the human experience. Starring Casey Affleck and Rooney Mara, the film follows a recently deceased man who returns as a ghost to observe the world around him. The minimalist approach, combined with haunting visuals and a poignant score, creates an atmosphere that invites deep reflection. The film’s slow pace and contemplative nature may not appeal to everyone, but those who are willing to engage with its themes will find a rewarding cinematic experience that lingers long after viewing.

4. Columbus (2017)

Columbus, directed by Kogonada, is a quietly powerful film that navigates the intricacies of human connection and architecture. Set in the stunning Midwestern town of Columbus, Indiana, the story revolves around two strangers, Jin and Casey, who form an unlikely friendship. As they explore the town’s modernist buildings, they also confront their personal dilemmas. The film’s meditative pace and exquisite cinematography make it a visual treat, while its exploration of themes such as longing and the search for belonging resonate on a deeply personal level.

5. Leave No Trace (2018)

Directed by Debra Granik, Leave No Trace is a heart-wrenching tale of survival and the bond between a father and daughter living off the grid in the forests of Oregon. Based on the novel My Abandonment by Peter Rock, the film follows Will and his daughter, Tom, as they navigate their unconventional lifestyle and the challenges that arise when they are discovered by the outside world. The film’s subtle storytelling and powerful performances capture the essence of parental love and the struggle for independence. It’s a poignant reminder of the beauty and fragility of human relationships.

6. Paterson (2016)

Jim Jarmusch’s Paterson is a charming and contemplative film that celebrates the beauty of mundane life. Starring Adam Driver as a bus driver named Paterson who writes poetry, the film takes place over a week and follows his daily routine in Paterson, New Jersey. Through its simple yet profound narrative, the film explores the themes of creativity, routine, and the small joys that life offers. With its gentle pacing and rich character development, Paterson is a love letter to the art of observation and the poetry found in everyday moments.

7. The Rider (2017)

The Rider, directed by Chloé Zhao, tells the story of Brady Blackburn, a young cowboy who faces the harsh realities of life after a rodeo accident leaves him with a traumatic brain injury. The film is a blend of fiction and documentary, featuring real-life cowboys portraying themselves. Zhao’s delicate direction captures the beauty of the South Dakota landscape while exploring themes of identity, masculinity, and the struggle to find one’s place in the world. Its authenticity and emotional depth make The Rider a powerful cinematic experience that shouldn’t be missed.

8. Short Term 12 (2013)

Directed by Destin Daniel Cretton, Short Term 12 is a poignant drama set in a residential treatment facility for at-risk youth. The film follows Grace, a supervisor played by Brie Larson, as she navigates her relationships with the troubled teens and her own personal challenges. The film’s raw and honest portrayal of mental health issues, combined with strong performances from its ensemble cast, creates a deeply affecting narrative. Short Term 12 shines a light on the importance of empathy and understanding, making it a significant film that resonates with many.

9. The Farewell (2019)

Lulu Wang’s The Farewell is a heartfelt exploration of family, culture, and the complexities of life and death. The film follows Billi, played by Awkwafina, who returns to China to visit her terminally ill grandmother under the guise of a wedding. The film is a beautiful blend of humor and poignancy, as it navigates the cultural differences in how families deal with grief. With its relatable characters and heartfelt storytelling, The Farewell is a touching reminder of the importance of family and the different ways we cope with loss.

10. The Last Black Man in San Francisco (2019)

Joe Talbot’s The Last Black Man in San Francisco is a visually stunning and emotionally charged exploration of gentrification, identity, and the meaning of home. The film follows Jimmie Fails, who is on a quest to reclaim his childhood home in a rapidly changing San Francisco. The film’s poetic storytelling and striking visuals create a powerful narrative that speaks to the heart of urban displacement and the longing for belonging. With its unique style and thought-provoking themes, this film is an essential watch for those seeking a deeper understanding of contemporary issues.

Conclusion

These ten underrated films offer a diverse range of storytelling and filmmaking styles that challenge conventional narratives. Each film provides a unique perspective on life, love, loss, and the human experience, making them essential viewing for any cinephile. As you embark on your cinematic journey this year, don’t overlook these hidden gems—they might just resonate with you in ways you never expected. So grab some popcorn, settle in, and enjoy the magic of cinema with these remarkable films.
