The Dos and Don'ts of Personal Hygiene When Exercising


When you hit the gym or head out for a run, you’re not just working up a sweat; you’re also exposing yourself to various hygiene pitfalls. Proper personal hygiene during exercise is crucial not only for your own comfort but also for the health and safety of those around you. Here are some essential dos and don’ts to keep in mind to maintain good hygiene practices while working out.

The Dos:

1. Do Shower Before Working Out:

Starting your exercise routine with a clean body is essential. Showering before working out helps remove dirt, bacteria, and sweat, ensuring you begin your session feeling fresh.

2. Do Wear Clean Workout Clothes:

Always put on fresh, clean workout attire before hitting the gym. Sweaty, dirty clothes can harbor bacteria and cause skin irritation or infections.

3. Do Use Antiperspirant:

Using antiperspirant before exercising can help control sweat and odor. Look for products that are specifically designed for active individuals.

4. Do Bring a Towel:

Carry a clean towel with you to wipe away sweat during your workout. It’s not only hygienic but also helps prevent the spread of germs on shared equipment.

5. Do Stay Hydrated:

Drinking an adequate amount of water before, during, and after your workout is vital for maintaining good overall hygiene and preventing dehydration.

6. Do Wash Your Hands:

Before and after your workout, wash your hands with soap and water. This simple step can help prevent the spread of germs and bacteria.

7. Do Clean Your Equipment:

If you’re using shared gym equipment, always wipe it down before and after use with disinfectant wipes. This practice shows consideration for others’ hygiene.

8. Do Change Out of Workout Clothes Promptly:

After your workout, change out of your sweaty clothes as soon as possible to avoid prolonged exposure to bacteria and prevent skin issues.

The Don’ts:

1. Don’t Re-Wear Workout Clothes:

Avoid the temptation to re-wear sweaty workout clothes. Moisture and bacteria build-up can lead to skin irritation and unpleasant odors.

2. Don’t Skip Showering After Exercising:

Post-workout showers are just as crucial as pre-workout ones. Skipping a shower allows bacteria to thrive on your skin and can result in body acne and infections.

3. Don’t Touch Your Face:

During your workout, try to avoid touching your face to prevent transferring sweat, dirt, and bacteria from your hands to your skin.

4. Don’t Share Personal Items:

To maintain good hygiene, avoid sharing personal items such as towels, water bottles, or hairbrushes with others at the gym.

5. Don’t Ignore Skin Irritations:

If you notice any skin irritations or infections, such as athlete’s foot or rashes, don’t ignore them. Seek medical advice promptly to prevent them from worsening.

6. Don’t Forget to Wash Your Workout Gear:

Regularly wash your workout clothes, gym bag, and any accessories you use to prevent the build-up of bacteria and keep them fresh and hygienic.

7. Don’t Neglect Your Feet:

Proper foot hygiene is essential when exercising. Keep your feet clean, dry, and wear moisture-wicking socks to prevent fungal infections.

8. Don’t Use Strong Fragrances:

While it’s important to smell fresh, avoid using strong perfumes or body sprays during your workout, as they can mix with sweat and create an overpowering scent.

By following these dos and don’ts of personal hygiene when exercising, you not only promote your well-being but also contribute to a cleaner and safer workout environment for everyone. Remember, good hygiene is an integral part of a healthy fitness routine. Stay clean, stay healthy, and keep sweating it out with confidence!

Enjoy your workouts and stay fresh!
