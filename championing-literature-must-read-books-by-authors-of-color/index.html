Championing Literature: Must-Read Books by Authors of Color


Diverse voices have long contributed to the rich tapestry of literary works around the world. Unfortunately, for far too long, these voices – specifically those of authors of color – have been underrepresented and often overlooked in contemporary literature. It’s crucial that we seek out and share the incredible work produced by these talented writers, showcasing their unique perspectives and experiences. For those looking for a fresh, fascinating reading list, here are some critically-acclaimed must-read books by authors of color that you may not have encountered yet.

Part One: Non-fiction by Authors of Color

1. “Between the World and Me” by Ta-Nehisi Coates

In this thought-provoking and emotionally resonant book, Coates addresses the struggle for African Americans in the United States. Using letters written to his son, he navigates the history of this country and its impact on people of color. His piercing insights into race, identity, and power make this book a must-read for anyone interested in deepening their understanding of these essential topics.

2. “The Color of Law: A Forgotten History of How Our Government Segregated America” by Richard Rothstein

A critical analysis of how government policies have perpetuated housing segregation, Rothstein’s work uncovers the often-overlooked role of American government in shaping our present-day reality. Understanding the roots of this problem is essential for aiding in its solution, making this book a crucial read.

3. “Eloquent Rage: A Black Women’s Guide to Empowering Yourself and All of Those Harsh Words” by Brittany Cooper

In this lively and humorous exploration of black womanhood, Cooper digs into the complexities of race, gender, and power, encouraging readers to embrace their righteous anger as a tool for change. This book should be on every woman’s booklist.

Part Two: Fiction by Authors of Color

4. “The Sympathizer” by Viet Thanh Nguyen

This Pulitzer Prize-winning novel tells the story of a Vietnamese immigrant who doubles as a spy, navigating life in Vietnam and the United States. Nguyen masterfully weaves together history, politics, and culture, exploring themes of identity and loyalty.

5. “Sing, Unburied, Sing” by Jesmyn Ward

Drawing inspiration from her own family history, Ward crafts an affecting and haunting story about a young girl trying to make sense of her family’s past and her own identity. Set in the Mississippi Delta, this novel illuminates the struggles of a community tragically scarred by racism, poverty, and tragedy.

6. “Americanah” by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

Adichie presents a poignant and incisive exploration of race and identity in this novel that follows two Nigerian friends through their time in America and England. The protagonist’s journey through her experiences as an immigrant woman of color will resonate with anyone fighting for representation and equality in their own lives.

7. “The Kite Runner” by Khaled Hosseini

Set against the backdrop of Afghanistan’s tumultuous history, this novel explores themes of guilt, betrayal, and redemption. Hosseini’s poignant storytelling invites readers into a world they may have never experienced, sharing wisdom and empathy along the way.

Part Three: Poetry by Authors of Color

8. “The Collected Poems of Langston Hughes”

A cornerstone of African American literature, Hughes’ work delves deep into the black experience, exploring themes of love, alienation, and injustice. Rich in emotion and sharp in imagery, these poems will immerse you in a world you won’t soon forget.

9. “Bush.iddiction” by Suzanne Juhasz

A landmark book in the Chicano literary movement, Juhasz’s poetry deftly addresses issues of gender, politics, and identity. Through bold imagery and sharp, verse, readers are invited to rethink their understanding of the world around them.

10. “The Wave” by Susan Kang

Drawing from her experiences growing up in North Korea, Kang’s poetry illuminates the complexity of identity, love, and loss. Her work touches on universal emotions that transcend boundaries, making her a powerful voice within the literary world.

By championing these literary voices, we not only enrich our reading experience but broaden our perspectives, fostering empathy and understanding. By featuring diverse authors of color, we continue to disrupt and dismantle systems that have historically suppressed and misrepresented different narratives. So, if you’re craving a fresh, enlightening experience, pick up one of these remarkable books and lose yourself in the vivid worlds created by some of today’s most talented authors of color.
