Using Action Verbs to Power Up Your Resume


Have you ever read through a resume and felt underwhelmed by the lack of enthusiasm and dynamism it exudes? It’s time to switch to action verbs and create a powerful resume that stands out from the rest! Action verbs not only make your resume more engaging, but they also effectively demonstrate your skills and accomplishments to potential employers.

Action verbs refer to words that express energy, enthusiasm, and assertiveness. They are often used to describe actions and occurrences in the past and are exceedingly beneficial for resumes. By incorporating these words, you can transform your resume from a monotonous read to a compelling and persuasive piece of work that can land you the dream job.

Here are some high-impact action verbs you can use in your resume:

  1. Achieved: Use this verb to highlight the successful completion of a task or project.

    • Achieved sales targets by 15%
    • Implemented streamlined processes, achieving a 20% increase in productivity

  2. Collaborated: Employ this verb to showcase your teamwork and collaboration skills.

    • Collaborated with cross-functional teams to meet project deadlines

  3. Created: This verb works well for candidates in creative and technical fields.

    • Created new marketing campaigns that drove a 30% increase in sales
    • Developed cutting-edge software, enhancing user experience and interface design

  4. Driven: This verb is suitable for candidates with a strong work ethic and initiative.

    • Driven by a self-starter mentality, consistently exceeded sales targets
    • Took ownership of projects, driving them to successful completion

  5. Improved: This verb is perfect for those with a history of making positive changes.

    • Improved customer satisfaction ratings by 25% through exceptional customer service
    • Streamlined inventory management, reducing costs by 10%

  6. Led: Use this verb to highlight your leadership skills and accomplishments.

    • Led a team of 5 sales representatives, achieving a 50% increase in sales
    • Successfully onboarded and mentored new team members, enhancing overall team performance

  7. Praised: This verb can demonstrate that your work has been recognized and appreciated.

    • Consistently received positive feedback from clients and colleagues for exceptional attention to detail and diligence

  8. Revolutionized: This verb is suitable for those who have brought about significant change.

    • Revolutionized the content creation process, streamlining it and cutting production time in half

  9. Secured: This verb is perfect for showcasing successful accomplishments in negotiations or deals.

    • Secured a strategic partnership with a leading industry player, driving increased profitability and market share

  10. Transformed: Use this verb for instances where you have significantly transformed a process, project, or department.

    • Transformed the marketing department’s strategies, doubling lead generation and driving record-breaking revenue quarters

Tips to Utilize Action Verbs Effectively

  1. Customize for each job: Tailor your resume to the specific job you’re applying for by incorporating action verbs relevant to the position’s requirements.
  2. Quantify your achievements: Including specific numbers and statistics illustrates the impact of your actions and makes your accomplishments more impressive.
  3. Upgrade your adjectives: Replace weak descriptors such as ‘worked’ and ‘assisted’ with strong action verbs to add life and vitality to your resume.
  4. Use consistent tense: Stick to either past tense or present tense throughout your resume to create a coherent narrative.

By incorporating powerful action verbs into your resume, you can showcase your accomplishments, experiences, and skills effectively. This strategic choice of words can help you stand out as a dynamic and accomplished candidate, increasing your chances of landing your dream job. Happy job hunting!
