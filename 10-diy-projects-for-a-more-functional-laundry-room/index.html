10 DIY Projects for a More Functional Laundry Room


A well-organized laundry room can make a world of difference in your home. Not only does it streamline the laundry process, but it also creates a more pleasant environment for tackling this often-dreaded chore. If you’re looking to enhance the functionality of your laundry space, here are ten DIY projects that can transform your laundry room into a more efficient and enjoyable area.

1. Create a Folding Station

One of the most practical additions to any laundry room is a dedicated folding station. This can be as simple as a countertop or a table where you can sort and fold clothes right after they come out of the dryer.

Materials Needed:

  • A sturdy table or countertop
  • Brackets for wall mounting (if applicable)
  • Sandpaper and paint or stain (optional)

Steps:

  1. Measure the available space in your laundry room.
  2. Choose a table or countertop that fits your dimensions.
  3. If you’re mounting it on the wall, secure the brackets and attach the countertop.
  4. Sand and paint or stain the surface to match your decor.

2. Install Shelving for Storage

Maximizing vertical space is key in a laundry room. Installing shelves can help you store detergents, fabric softeners, and other laundry essentials neatly.

Materials Needed:

  • Wooden boards or pre-made shelves
  • Brackets
  • Drill and screws

Steps:

  1. Decide on the height and location of your shelves.
  2. Use a level to mark where the brackets will go.
  3. Drill holes and secure the brackets to the wall.
  4. Place the shelves on the brackets and organize your supplies.

3. Build a Laundry Basket Station

Having a designated area for laundry baskets can keep your laundry room tidy. You can create a multi-basket station for sorting clothes by color or type.

Materials Needed:

  • Wooden crates or baskets
  • A wooden board for the top
  • Paint or stain (optional)

Steps:

  1. Arrange the crates or baskets in a row.
  2. Secure them together with screws or brackets.
  3. Place a wooden board on top to create a flat surface.
  4. Optionally, paint or stain the wood to match your decor.

4. Add a Drying Rack

A drying rack is essential for air-drying delicate items. You can build a foldable drying rack that can be stored away when not in use.

Materials Needed:

  • Wooden dowels
  • Hinges
  • A wooden frame

Steps:

  1. Construct a rectangular frame using the wooden boards.
  2. Attach the dowels horizontally across the frame, spaced evenly.
  3. Use hinges to allow the rack to fold down when not in use.
  4. Secure it to the wall or a nearby surface.

5. Create a Cleaning Supply Caddy

A portable cleaning supply caddy can help you keep all your laundry essentials in one place. This is especially useful if you need to move supplies around the house.

Materials Needed:

  • A small plastic or wooden caddy
  • Containers for organizing supplies

Steps:

  1. Choose a caddy that fits your needs.
  2. Use small containers to separate different types of supplies.
  3. Label each container for easy identification.
  4. Keep the caddy in your laundry room for quick access.

6. Install a Pegboard for Tools

A pegboard can be a fantastic way to keep tools and supplies organized and easily accessible. You can hang everything from scissors to measuring cups.

Materials Needed:

  • A pegboard
  • Pegboard hooks
  • Paint (optional)

Steps:

  1. Cut the pegboard to fit your wall space.
  2. Secure the pegboard to the wall using screws.
  3. Arrange the hooks and hang your tools.
  4. Optionally, paint the pegboard to match your decor.

7. Make a Stain Removal Station

Having a dedicated area for stain removal can save you time and frustration. You can create a small station with all the necessary supplies.

Materials Needed:

  • A small table or shelf
  • Containers for stain removal products
  • Labels

Steps:

  1. Choose a location for your stain removal station.
  2. Set up a small table or shelf.
  3. Organize your stain removal products in containers.
  4. Label each container for easy access.

8. Design a Pet Laundry Area

If you have pets, consider creating a designated area for washing their items. This can include a space for washing pet beds, toys, and even grooming supplies.

Materials Needed:

  • A small tub or sink
  • Shelving for pet supplies
  • Hooks for leashes

Steps:

  1. Install a small tub or sink in your laundry room.
  2. Add shelves for storing pet supplies.
  3. Install hooks for leashes and other accessories.
  4. Keep pet towels and grooming tools nearby for convenience.
