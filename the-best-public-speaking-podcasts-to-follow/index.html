The Best Public Speaking Podcasts to Follow


Public speaking is a skill that many people strive to master, whether for professional advancement, personal growth, or simply the confidence to speak in front of a crowd. While it can seem intimidating, with the right guidance and consistent practice, anyone can become a confident and effective speaker. One of the best ways to improve your public speaking skills is by listening to podcasts that offer valuable insights, expert advice, and practical tips. Whether you’re a novice or an experienced speaker looking to enhance your abilities, there’s a podcast for you.

In this article, we will explore the best public speaking podcasts you should follow to develop your communication skills, gain valuable knowledge, and be inspired by the stories of successful speakers. These podcasts offer a wealth of content that can transform the way you approach public speaking and help you become a more persuasive, confident, and impactful communicator.

1. The Public Speaker’s Quick and Dirty Tips for Improving Your Communication Skills

If you’re looking for quick, actionable tips on public speaking, this podcast is an excellent resource. Hosted by Lisa B. Marshall, The Public Speaker’s Quick and Dirty Tips for Improving Your Communication Skills offers concise, easy-to-follow advice on a wide range of communication topics. From mastering your tone to dealing with nervousness, the podcast offers simple yet effective strategies that you can implement immediately.

Lisa’s approach to public speaking is practical and relatable, which makes this podcast perfect for beginners and experienced speakers alike. Her ability to distill complex concepts into bite-sized, actionable advice makes it an ideal choice for anyone looking to improve their speaking skills in a short amount of time.

Why Listen:

  • Focuses on communication tips that you can implement right away.
  • Covers various speaking scenarios, including presentations, meetings, and social interactions.
  • Offers both short-form and in-depth content that fits your schedule.

2. The Speak Up Podcast

The Speak Up Podcast is hosted by entrepreneur and speaker, Matt Abrahams. Matt’s goal is to help individuals improve their ability to speak confidently in any situation. The podcast is known for its focus on real-world scenarios, providing practical techniques for speaking in front of a group, whether it’s for a business presentation or a casual conversation.

Each episode features interviews with expert speakers, communication coaches, and other professionals who share their tips for effective public speaking. Matt’s accessible style and engaging conversation make the podcast enjoyable and informative. The combination of expert interviews and Matt’s own insights into overcoming common speaking challenges provides a well-rounded approach to developing speaking skills.

Why Listen:

  • Focuses on practical techniques for overcoming speaking anxiety.
  • Features expert interviews that provide diverse perspectives on communication.
  • Helps you develop clear and confident communication, whether you’re giving a formal speech or making a casual presentation.

3. The Moth Podcast

The Moth Podcast brings the art of storytelling to the forefront of public speaking. The Moth is a platform where people share true stories from their lives, and the podcast features some of the most captivating tales told live on stage. The stories are raw, emotional, and incredibly relatable, which makes them an excellent source of inspiration for anyone looking to improve their storytelling abilities in public speaking.

One of the key aspects of public speaking is being able to connect with your audience, and storytelling is one of the most effective ways to do so. Listening to The Moth Podcast allows you to hear how great storytellers structure their narratives, engage their audience, and make their message memorable. Whether you’re speaking at a wedding, a corporate event, or a community gathering, the skills learned from The Moth can enhance your storytelling abilities.

Why Listen:

  • Offers real-life examples of compelling storytelling.
  • Provides insights into how to connect with an audience through personal stories.
  • Helps you build emotional intelligence and engage listeners on a deeper level.

4. The Toastmasters Podcast

Toastmasters International is one of the most well-known organizations for public speaking and leadership development. The Toastmasters Podcast is an invaluable resource for anyone looking to improve their speaking skills. Hosted by Toastmasters members, this podcast covers topics like speech preparation, overcoming nervousness, and mastering body language.

What makes the Toastmasters Podcast unique is that it’s produced by a community of speakers who have firsthand experience with the challenges and rewards of public speaking. You’ll hear from experienced Toastmasters who share their personal journeys, along with practical tips for advancing in public speaking. Whether you’re a member of Toastmasters or just a fan of their mission, this podcast is a must-listen for anyone dedicated to improving their speaking skills.

Why Listen:

  • Offers insights from experienced Toastmasters members and coaches.
  • Focuses on a wide range of speaking topics, including leadership, feedback, and growth.
  • Perfect for those involved in Toastmasters or looking to join the community.

5. How to Speak

Hosted by speaker and author Patrick King, How to Speak is a podcast that focuses on improving verbal communication in all aspects of life. Patrick’s episodes are full of tips for overcoming nervousness, using your voice effectively, and engaging your audience. His practical advice covers everything from pacing to the use of humor, making it a great resource for anyone looking to polish their public speaking skills.

What sets How to Speak apart is its emphasis on vocal delivery. Many people focus solely on the content of their speeches, but the delivery of that content is just as important. Patrick’s focus on vocal techniques, body language, and presentation style helps listeners create a more polished and effective public speaking performance.

Why Listen:

  • Focuses on the vocal delivery aspect of public speaking.
  • Offers advice on how to structure your speech for maximum impact.
  • Covers strategies for keeping an audience engaged throughout your talk.

6. The Communication Guys Podcast

The Communication Guys Podcast, hosted by professional communicators, offers a deep dive into various aspects of communication, including public speaking. The hosts cover everything from persuasion to conflict resolution, making this podcast an excellent choice for those who want to enhance their overall communication abilities.

While the podcast touches on a variety of communication topics, the public speaking episodes are particularly valuable. You’ll find practical advice on crafting effective speeches, using nonverbal communication, and connecting with diverse audiences. The Communication Guys are experts in the field, and their engaging discussions provide valuable insights into the art of speaking well.

Why Listen:

  • Provides a comprehensive look at communication, with a focus on public speaking.
  • Offers in-depth discussions on speech delivery, audience engagement, and persuasive techniques.
  • Ideal for individuals looking to enhance their overall communication effectiveness.

7. The Storytelling Lab Podcast

Storytelling is one of the most powerful tools in public speaking, and The Storytelling Lab Podcast is designed to help you harness this tool effectively. Hosted by a team of storytelling experts, this podcast dives deep into the principles of storytelling and how to use them to captivate your audience.

The episodes cover a wide range of topics, from understanding your audience to structuring your story for maximum impact. If you’re looking to improve your storytelling abilities and engage your listeners with memorable narratives, this podcast is a must-listen. The practical tips and insights shared in each episode can help you become a more persuasive and compelling speaker.

Why Listen:

  • Focuses on the art of storytelling in public speaking.
  • Provides actionable advice for crafting and delivering powerful stories.
  • Helps you create memorable and impactful speeches that resonate with your audience.

8. The Art of Charm

The Art of Charm podcast is a must-follow for anyone interested in mastering the social dynamics of public speaking. Hosted by Jordan Harbinger, this podcast focuses on personal development, communication skills, and building meaningful connections with others. While it’s not specifically about public speaking, many of the lessons taught are directly applicable to improving your speaking abilities.

Jordan interviews experts from various fields, including communication, psychology, and influence, to help listeners become more charismatic and confident in their interactions. Whether you’re speaking at a formal event or having a one-on-one conversation, the insights from The Art of Charm can help you build rapport, engage your audience, and improve your overall speaking presence.

Why Listen:

  • Focuses on developing charisma and social confidence, which are key to public speaking.
  • Offers expert interviews on topics like influence, body language, and communication.
  • Provides actionable advice that applies to a wide range of speaking scenarios.

9. Accidental Creative

For those who want to take a creative approach to public speaking, the Accidental Creative podcast is a great choice. Hosted by Todd Henry, this podcast explores how to be creative in all areas of life, including public speaking. Todd talks about topics such as ideation, presentation, and how to communicate your creative ideas to others effectively.

If you’re a speaker looking to incorporate creativity into your presentations, Accidental Creative provides insightful episodes on how to make your speeches more engaging and innovative. Whether you’re delivering a keynote address or sharing an idea at a meeting, this podcast helps you think outside the box and deliver your message in a compelling way.

Why Listen:

  • Focuses on creativity and how it applies to public speaking.
  • Offers tips for delivering more engaging and unique presentations.
  • Ideal for speakers who want to differentiate themselves and leave a lasting impact.

10. The Presentation Boss Podcast

For those looking to master the art of presentation, the Presentation Boss Podcast is a fantastic resource. Hosted by professional presentation designers and speakers, this podcast provides tips on designing presentations that captivate and engage audiences. The hosts dive into everything from slide design to storytelling techniques, offering listeners a holistic approach to improving their presentations.

The podcast emphasizes the importance of delivering presentations that not only communicate the message clearly but also leave a lasting impression on the audience. If you’re looking to take your presentation skills to the next level, this podcast is filled with actionable advice and expert insights.

Why Listen:

  • Offers expert advice on creating and delivering effective presentations.
  • Focuses on both the design and delivery aspects of presentations.
  • Ideal for individuals looking to improve their presentation skills for business, conferences, or public events.

Conclusion

Improving your public speaking skills doesn’t have to be a solitary endeavor. By listening to these insightful and informative podcasts, you can learn from some of the best in the field, pick up valuable tips, and practice techniques that will help you become a more confident and effective speaker. Whether you’re looking to overcome stage fright, learn how to engage your audience, or simply refine your presentation skills, these podcasts provide a wealth of knowledge that will assist you on your journey to becoming a master public speaker.

By incorporating the lessons and advice from these podcasts into your own speaking endeavors, you’ll be well on your way to becoming a persuasive, engaging, and confident communicator.
