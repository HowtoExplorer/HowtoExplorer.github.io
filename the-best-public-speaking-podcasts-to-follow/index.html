The Best Public Speaking Podcasts to Follow


Public speaking is both an art and a skill. For some, it comes naturally, while others need to nurture their ability to capture an audience’s attention. Regardless of where you stand, there’s always room for improvement. One of the best ways to enhance your skills is by immersing yourself in the wisdom of seasoned public speakers and communication experts. Podcasts offer a convenient and comprehensive medium to do just that. You can learn on the go—during commutes, workouts, or leisure time—by tuning in to conversations, tips, and techniques shared by those who have mastered the skill of speaking confidently.

This article highlights some of the most impactful public speaking podcasts, each designed to help you refine, elevate, and truly own your speaking style. Whether you’re preparing for a career in keynote speaking, aspiring to crush an upcoming presentation at work, or simply want to become more articulate in your daily interactions, these podcasts will offer the tools and insights you need.

1. The Public Speaking Power Podcast

As its name suggests, The Public Speaking Power Podcast is dedicated to empowering speakers of all levels. Hosted by seasoned speaking coach Ryan McLean, this podcast focuses on actionable strategies that can elevate your speaking abilities. Each episode dives deep into crafting compelling speeches, overcoming stage fright, and using voice modulation effectively. The beauty of this podcast lies in its practical takeaways—providing you with step-by-step guidance that you can immediately apply.

Listeners consistently rave about the simplicity of the advice presented, as it’s ideal for both beginners and intermediate speakers. Whether you’re drafting a TEDx talk or addressing a small committee, The Public Speaking Power Podcast equips you with the knowledge to walk onto any stage with confidence.

2. The Speaker Lab Podcast

Grant Baldwin, a renowned keynote speaker and author, brings his vast experience to this engaging podcast. The Speaker Lab Podcast is particularly tailored for individuals who want to break into the professional speaking industry. But the insights shared extend far beyond the business of speaking. Grant offers practical tools for storytelling, audience engagement, and presenting ideas with clarity.

What makes The Speaker Lab Podcast stand out is its dual approach—offering resources both for honing your speaking skills and learning how to market yourself as a speaker. From episodes on finding your niche to discussions on the nuances of non-verbal communication, listeners are left with a treasure trove of valuable knowledge.

3. Talk About Talk

Communication coach Dr. Andrea Wojnicki hosts Talk About Talk, a podcast that’s all about honing your communication skills. While it covers topics broader than public speaking, the lessons directly translate to the stage. Episodes delve into areas like commanding attention, handling Q&A sessions seamlessly, and managing nervous energy.

Dr. Andrea’s academic background and hands-on experience make each episode a perfect mix of theory and practice. For anyone looking to master the art of persuasion, influence audiences, and eliminate filler words, Talk About Talk offers deep insights in an engaging format.

4. The Big Talk Podcast

This podcast, hosted by Tricia Brouk, focuses on helping speakers step into their unique voice while crafting presentations that captivate audiences. From learning how to structure your speech for emotional impact to understanding how to connect with an audience at a deeper level, The Big Talk Podcast covers everything you need to create memorable moments on stage.

Tricia invites an incredible mix of guests, including professional speakers, thought leaders, and coaches, who share both their success stories and challenges. These real-world insights resonate with listeners striving to find their authentic voice in public speaking.

5. The Toastmasters Podcast

Toastmasters International is synonymous with public speaking and leadership development. The Toastmasters Podcast provides insights into improving presentation skills, mastering impromptu speaking, and building leadership capabilities through communication. As the official podcast of this trusted institution, it draws on the expertise of its members and mentors to deliver high-quality episodes.

Whether you’re already part of Toastmasters or curious about joining, this podcast serves as a valuable companion in your public speaking journey. It excels in providing relatable examples from members all over the world, illustrating what works and why.

6. The Moth Podcast

If you want to truly master the art of storytelling as part of your public speaking toolkit, look no further than The Moth Podcast. Rooted in real, raw, and emotional stories told by ordinary people, this podcast is a masterclass in connecting with audiences. Stories are at the heart of powerful public speaking, and The Moth’s episodes allow you to observe nuances in pacing, tone, and structure.

Each episode is a reminder that vulnerability and authenticity are integral to forging a meaningful connection with your audience. Aspiring speakers can listen to these stories to discover how to translate personal experiences into impactful performances.

7. No Sweat Public Speaking

Fred Miller, the host of No Sweat Public Speaking, brings a straightforward and approachable style to the subject. This podcast focuses on making your speeches and presentations easier and more effective. Episodes provide clear instructions on preparing and delivering speeches without fear or fumbles.

Fred’s mantra? “Speaking opportunities are business, career, and leadership opportunities.” With this mindset, each episode is built to empower you to nail every opportunity that comes your way—whether it’s a personal event, corporate meeting, or high-stakes presentation.

8. Steal the Show

Michael Port’s Steal the Show podcast is dedicated to helping listeners stand out in front of any audience. From methods to captivate attention in the first moments to ensuring a strong finish, Michael covers it all with remarkable clarity.

With an emphasis on performance techniques drawn from his acting background, Steal the Show encourages speakers to push their creative boundaries. It’s especially valuable for professionals who want to bring a fresh, dynamic energy to their presentations.

9. The Presentation Boss Podcast

Hosted by Kate Norris & Thomas Krafft, The Presentation Boss Podcast blends psychology, communication theory, and years of presentation experience. The podcast breaks down the good, the bad, and the innovative techniques speakers use to transform their presentations.

From crafting slides that actually work to reading audience feedback, this show is filled with value-packed discussions. For those who often struggle with visual aids or get tripped up when addressing unfamiliar audiences, The Presentation Boss Podcast provides solutions grounded in practicality.

Why Listen to Public Speaking Podcasts?

The beauty of podcasts lies in their ability to immerse you in knowledge while allowing flexibility in your schedule. By listening to public speaking podcasts, you’re exposing yourself to diverse perspectives and methods from leaders in the field.

These podcasts cover critical skill sets, including:

  • Overcoming stage fright and boosting confidence.
  • Engaging your audience through storytelling.
  • Fine-tuning your vocal delivery and physical presence.
  • Navigating audience interaction and unpredictability.
  • Structuring a compelling narrative to maximize impact.

Even more than that, podcasts often feature guests and expert speakers, meaning you gain exposure to a wealth of personal experiences and industry secrets you may not find in traditional resources.

Final Thoughts

Public speaking is a craft that thrives on continuous learning and constant practice. By incorporating just one or two of these podcasts into your routine, you’ll find yourself armed with a wealth of strategies, inspiration, and techniques designed to enhance your speaking prowess. Whether you’re stepping foot into this arena for the first time or looking to refine the skills you’ve been developing for years, these shows provide an accessible platform to elevate your abilities.

If change happens when preparation meets opportunity, then these podcasts are the preparation you need to seize every speaking opportunity with confidence and impact.
