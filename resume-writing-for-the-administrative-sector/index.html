Resume Writing for the Administrative Sector


When it comes to applying for a job in the administrative sector, having a well-crafted resume is crucial. Your resume is essentially your first impression on potential employers, so it’s important to make it stand out. In this blog post, we will discuss the key components of a strong administrative resume and provide tips on how to make yours shine.

1. Contact Information

Begin your resume by including your full name, phone number, and professional email address. Make sure the contact information is clear and easy to read. It’s also a good idea to include a link to your LinkedIn profile, as it can showcase your professional network and additional qualifications.

2. Professional Summary

Next, write a concise professional summary that highlights your skills, experience, and career goals. This section should be a brief overview of your qualifications and what you can bring to the table. Use targeted keywords here to improve your resume’s searchability.

3. Skills

In the administrative sector, there are several key skills that employers look for. Some of these include:

  • Strong organizational and time management abilities
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • Proficiency in office software such as Microsoft Office
  • Attention to detail and accuracy
  • Ability to multitask and prioritize tasks

Make sure to tailor your skills section to match the requirements of the job you’re applying for. Be specific and provide examples where possible to demonstrate your proficiency.

4. Work Experience

In this section, detail your previous work experience in reverse chronological order. Include the company name, your job title, the dates of employment, and a bulleted list of your responsibilities and achievements. Focus on quantifiable results, such as cost savings or process improvements, to showcase your impact on previous employers.

5. Education

List your educational background, starting with the most recent degree or certification. Include the institution’s name, the degree or certification earned, and the dates of attendance. If you have any relevant coursework or academic achievements, be sure to highlight them as well.

6. Additional Information

If you have any additional certifications, languages spoken, or relevant volunteer experience, include them in this section. This information can help set you apart from other candidates and demonstrate your commitment to professional development.

7. References

It’s generally not necessary to include references on your resume, but you can note that they are available upon request. Make sure to have a separate document with your references’ contact information and inform them in advance that they may be contacted.

Final Thoughts

Writing a strong resume for the administrative sector is all about highlighting your relevant skills and experience in a clear and concise manner. Remember to tailor your resume to each job application and use keywords naturally throughout the content to enhance its search engine optimization. By following these tips, you’ll be well on your way to securing your dream administrative position.

Note: This blog post is intended as a general guide and may not cover all specific resume requirements for every administrative job application. It’s always a good idea to research the company and position you’re applying for and tailor your resume accordingly.
