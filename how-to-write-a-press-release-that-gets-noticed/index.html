How to Write a Press Release That Gets Noticed


In today’s fast-paced digital world, crafting a press release that stands out is more crucial than ever. With countless businesses vying for media attention, a well-written press release can be the key to getting your news noticed. This guide will walk you through the essential steps to create a compelling press release that captures the interest of journalists and your target audience alike.

Understanding the Purpose of a Press Release

Before diving into the writing process, it’s important to understand what a press release is and its purpose. A press release is a brief, compelling news story that is sent to targeted members of the media. Its primary goal is to announce something newsworthy, such as a product launch, an event, a partnership, or a significant achievement. A well-crafted press release can lead to media coverage, increased visibility, and enhanced credibility for your brand.

Key Elements of a Press Release

To write an effective press release, you need to include several key elements:

1. Headline

The headline is the first thing readers will see, so it must be attention-grabbing and informative. Aim for a concise, clear, and compelling headline that summarizes the main point of your press release. Use strong action verbs and avoid jargon to ensure it resonates with a broad audience.

2. Subheadline

While not always necessary, a subheadline can provide additional context or detail that complements the headline. It should be a single sentence that elaborates on the main point and encourages readers to continue.

3. Dateline

The dateline includes the location and date of the press release. This information helps establish the context and timeliness of your announcement. Format it as follows: “City, State – Month Day, Year.”

4. Introduction

The introduction should answer the who, what, when, where, and why of your announcement. This section should be engaging and informative, providing enough detail to entice readers to learn more. Aim for a strong opening sentence that captures attention and sets the tone for the rest of the release.

5. Body

The body of the press release expands on the information presented in the introduction. Use this section to provide additional details, quotes from key stakeholders, and relevant statistics or data. Organize the content in a logical flow, starting with the most important information and gradually providing more context. Keep paragraphs short and focused to maintain readability.

6. Quotes

Including quotes from company executives, industry experts, or satisfied customers can add credibility and a human touch to your press release. Ensure that the quotes are relevant and provide insight into the significance of the announcement. Use quotes to highlight the impact of your news on the industry or community.

7. Boilerplate

The boilerplate is a brief paragraph at the end of the press release that provides background information about your company. This section should include your company’s mission, history, and any relevant achievements. Keep it concise and focused, as this information is often used by journalists when writing their articles.

8. Contact Information

Finally, include contact information for a media representative who can provide additional details or answer questions. This should include the name, phone number, and email address of the contact person. Make it easy for journalists to reach out for more information.

Tips for Writing an Effective Press Release

1. Keep It Newsworthy

To capture the attention of journalists, your press release must be newsworthy. Focus on what makes your announcement unique or significant. Consider the “news value” of your story and how it impacts your audience or industry.

2. Be Concise

Journalists receive numerous press releases daily, so it’s essential to be concise. Aim for a length of 400-600 words, and avoid unnecessary jargon or fluff. Stick to the facts and present them clearly.

3. Use a Professional Tone

While it’s important to be engaging, maintain a professional tone throughout your press release. Avoid overly promotional language and focus on delivering factual information. Journalists appreciate straightforward, well-structured content.

4. Optimize for SEO

Incorporate relevant keywords naturally throughout your press release to enhance its visibility online. Use keywords in the headline, subheadline, and body, but avoid keyword stuffing. Aim for a natural flow that reads well for both humans and search engines.

5. Include Multimedia Elements

While this guide does not cover images, consider including multimedia elements such as videos or infographics in your press release when distributing it online. These elements can enhance engagement and provide additional context for your announcement.

6. Distribute Strategically

Once your press release is written, it’s time to distribute it. Identify the right media outlets, journalists, and influencers who cover your industry. Tailor your distribution list to ensure your press release reaches the most relevant audience.

7. Follow Up

After sending out your press release, consider following up with key journalists or media outlets.
