Handling Illegal or Inappropriate Interview Questions


Interviews are an integral part of the job search process. They allow employers to assess a candidate’s qualifications, skills, and suitability for a particular role. However, not all questions asked during an interview are legal or appropriate. As a job seeker, it is important to know how to handle such questions tactfully and assertively. In this blog post, we will explore strategies for handling illegal or inappropriate interview questions, empowering you to navigate these situations effectively.

Understanding Illegal and Inappropriate Interview Questions

Before we delve into handling these questions, let’s clarify what constitutes an illegal or inappropriate question. Illegal questions are those that violate employment laws, such as questions related to age, race, gender, religion, marital status, disability, or sexual orientation. Inappropriate questions, on the other hand, may not necessarily violate laws but are unprofessional or irrelevant to the job.

Stay Calm and Collected

When faced with an illegal or inappropriate question, it is crucial to remain calm and composed. Take a deep breath and gather your thoughts before responding. Remember, your reaction can influence the outcome of the interview. Maintain a professional demeanor and focus on addressing the issue at hand rather than becoming defensive.

Evaluate the Intent

Sometimes, interviewers may ask a question without realizing its inappropriateness. In such cases, it can be helpful to evaluate the intent behind the question. Is the interviewer seeking information relevant to the job or trying to assess your personal life? Understanding their motive will enable you to respond appropriately.

Deflect Diplomatically

If you encounter an illegal or inappropriate question, you can choose to deflect diplomatically. For instance, if an interviewer asks about your marital status, you can respond by redirecting the conversation to your qualifications and experience. For example:

“While my personal life is important to me, I believe my qualifications and experience are more relevant to this position. I would be happy to discuss how my skills align with the requirements of the role.”

By deflecting the question tactfully, you shift the focus back to your professional abilities, steering the conversation in the right direction.

Address the Issue Directly

In some cases, you may feel comfortable addressing the issue directly with the interviewer. This approach requires assertiveness and confidence. Politely inform the interviewer that the question asked is inappropriate or illegal, and explain why. For example:

“I appreciate your interest in getting to know me better, but I believe that question may be considered inappropriate as it pertains to my personal life. I would prefer to focus on discussing how my qualifications align with the requirements of the role.”

By addressing the issue directly, you assert your boundaries and demonstrate your professionalism. This approach also gives the interviewer an opportunity to correct their behavior and steer the interview in a more appropriate direction.

Seek Guidance if Necessary

If you encounter repeated illegal or inappropriate questions during an interview, it may be worth seeking guidance from a professional or legal advisor. They can provide valuable insights into your rights as a job seeker and offer advice on how to handle such situations effectively.

Final Thoughts

Handling illegal or inappropriate interview questions can be challenging, but it is essential to navigate these situations confidently and assertively. By staying calm, evaluating the intent, deflecting diplomatically, addressing the issue directly, and seeking guidance if necessary, you can protect your rights and maintain your professionalism throughout the interview process. Remember, you have the right to be treated fairly and respectfully, and addressing these issues head-on can help create a positive impact not only for yourself but also for future job seekers. Good luck with your interviews!
