The Best Online Brokers for New Investors


When you’re just starting your investing journey, choosing the right online broker can be overwhelming. With a plethora of options available, it’s essential to find a platform that not only offers a user-friendly experience but also suits your financial goals. Whether you’re looking to build long-term wealth or dip your toes into stock trading, the best online brokers for new investors will provide the tools, education, and support you need to succeed.

In this guide, we’ll explore some of the best online brokers for new investors, helping you make an informed decision based on key factors such as fees, account types, educational resources, and customer support. Let’s dive in.

What to Look for in an Online Broker

Before we get into specific broker recommendations, it’s crucial to understand what makes a good online broker for beginners. As a new investor, you should consider the following factors:

1. Low Fees and Commissions

Investing can be expensive, especially when you’re just getting started. High trading fees, commissions, or account maintenance charges can eat into your profits. Look for brokers that offer commission-free trades, low fees, and free access to essential tools like research reports and stock screeners.

2. Educational Resources

As a beginner, education is key to building a strong foundation. Many brokers offer educational content in the form of articles, webinars, videos, and tutorials. Look for a platform that provides easy-to-understand resources, especially ones that cover investment strategies, market analysis, and the basics of asset allocation.

3. User-Friendly Platforms

A complicated interface can be frustrating, especially for beginners. Choose a broker that offers an intuitive and easy-to-navigate platform. Look for features such as customizable dashboards, easy order placements, and smooth mobile app experiences. This ensures that you can manage your investments confidently, whether you’re at home or on the go.

4. Account Options and Flexibility

When starting, you’ll want a broker that offers a range of account types to suit your financial goals. From individual brokerage accounts to tax-advantaged retirement accounts like IRAs, a good online broker will allow you to easily switch between account types or even open multiple accounts as your investment strategy evolves.

5. Customer Support

Customer support is an essential factor to consider when choosing an online broker. Make sure the broker offers responsive customer service through various channels, such as phone, email, and live chat. Strong customer support ensures that you can resolve any issues quickly and get answers to any questions you may have.

Top Online Brokers for New Investors

Now that we know what to look for, here’s a breakdown of some of the best online brokers for new investors, each offering unique features and benefits.

1. Fidelity

Fidelity is widely regarded as one of the best online brokers for new investors due to its reputation for strong customer service, low fees, and educational resources.

  • Fees: Fidelity offers commission-free trades on stocks and ETFs, with low fees for mutual funds and options.
  • Educational Resources: Fidelity has a vast library of educational materials, including articles, webinars, and courses on topics such as investing strategies, portfolio management, and financial planning.
  • Platform: Fidelity’s platform is user-friendly, offering a streamlined experience for beginners and advanced investors alike. The mobile app is also highly rated for its ease of use.
  • Account Types: Fidelity offers a range of accounts, including taxable brokerage accounts, IRAs, and 529 college savings plans. They also offer managed accounts if you’re looking for a hands-off approach to investing.

2. Charles Schwab

Charles Schwab is another excellent choice for new investors due to its ease of use, solid research tools, and strong educational offerings.

  • Fees: Schwab offers commission-free trading on stocks and ETFs. They also have low-cost mutual funds and an excellent selection of low-cost index funds.
  • Educational Resources: Schwab provides new investors with access to webinars, articles, and tutorials. They even offer in-person workshops in some areas.
  • Platform: The Schwab website and mobile app are designed for simplicity. You can quickly place trades, track investments, and access financial tools with ease.
  • Account Types: Schwab offers brokerage accounts, retirement accounts (including IRAs), custodial accounts, and more. Their robo-advisor service, Schwab Intelligent Portfolios, is a great option for those who want a hands-off investment approach.

3. TD Ameritrade

TD Ameritrade stands out for its user-friendly platform and excellent customer support, making it an ideal choice for beginners.

  • Fees: TD Ameritrade offers commission-free trading on stocks, ETFs, and options. Mutual funds can have transaction fees, though, so be sure to check before making a purchase.
  • Educational Resources: TD Ameritrade is known for its educational content, which includes articles, videos, podcasts, and live webinars. Their “Investing 101” section is perfect for new investors.
  • Platform: TD Ameritrade’s thinkorswim platform is a powerful tool for technical analysis and trading, but the basic web platform is very accessible to beginners. The mobile app is also excellent for on-the-go investors.
  • Account Types: TD Ameritrade offers a wide range of account options, including brokerage accounts, retirement accounts, and even custodial accounts for minors.

4. E*TRADE

E*TRADE is another top contender for new investors due to its beginner-friendly platform and range of resources.

  • Fees: E*TRADE offers commission-free trades on stocks and ETFs. They also have a wide variety of no-load mutual funds available without transaction fees.
  • Educational Resources: E*TRADE offers an extensive collection of educational materials, including articles, webinars, and tutorials. Their platform also provides educational resources within the trading interface, which makes it easy to learn while trading.
  • Platform: E*TRADE’s platform is clean and simple to navigate. The mobile app is also very popular among beginner investors.
  • Account Types: E*TRADE offers various accounts, including individual brokerage accounts, retirement accounts, and more. They also provide managed portfolio services for those who prefer a hands-off approach.

5. Robinhood

For those just getting started with investing and looking for a simple, commission-free option, Robinhood is a great choice.

  • Fees: Robinhood is well-known for offering commission-free trading on stocks, ETFs, options, and even cryptocurrencies.
  • Educational Resources: Robinhood’s educational resources are more limited compared to other brokers, but they do offer basic articles and explanations for beginner investors.
  • Platform: The Robinhood app is minimalistic and incredibly easy to use, making it a popular choice for beginners. However, it may lack some of the advanced features that other brokers offer.
  • Account Types: Robinhood offers a taxable brokerage account, as well as a cash management feature, but does not offer retirement accounts like IRAs or 401(k)s.

6. Merrill Edge

Merrill Edge, a subsidiary of Bank of America, is a great choice for new investors who want access to both banking services and investment opportunities in one place.

  • Fees: Merrill Edge offers commission-free trading on stocks and ETFs. They also offer a wide range of low-cost mutual funds.
  • Educational Resources: Merrill Edge offers a range of articles, videos, and tools for new investors. You can also access personalized guidance if you’re a Bank of America customer.
  • Platform: Merrill Edge’s platform is user-friendly, with easy-to-understand charts, stock screeners, and investment tools.
  • Account Types: Merrill Edge offers taxable brokerage accounts, retirement accounts, and custodial accounts.

7. SoFi Invest

SoFi Invest is another great online broker for beginners, especially for those looking for a mobile-first experience with easy-to-use tools.

  • Fees: SoFi Invest offers commission-free trading on stocks and ETFs. They also offer a range of automated investment options with no management fees.
  • Educational Resources: SoFi Invest provides access to educational content such as articles, blogs, and financial guides. They also offer financial planning tools and support.
  • Platform: The SoFi mobile app is simple and easy to navigate. It allows you to track your portfolio, make trades, and access financial tools on the go.
  • Account Types: SoFi Invest offers taxable brokerage accounts, retirement accounts, and even fractional shares.

Final Thoughts

Choosing the right online broker is a crucial decision for any new investor. The best online brokers for new investors will offer commission-free trades, low fees, educational resources, and a user-friendly platform that helps you feel confident as you begin your investment journey. Each broker on our list provides different strengths, so be sure to choose the one that best suits your financial goals, risk tolerance, and investing style.

Remember, investing is a long-term game. Start small, learn as you go, and don’t be afraid to seek help along the way. With the right tools and a solid foundation of knowledge, you’ll be well on your way to building a prosperous financial future.
