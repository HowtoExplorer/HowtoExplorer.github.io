Professional Growth Resolutions: Setting SMART Goals and Conquering them in 2022


The New Year is an exciting time filled with opportunities for growth, improvement, and achievement. As we approach 2022, people across the globe are setting resolutions and goals for themselves, with the hope of improving their personal and professional lives. But, more importantly, how can we ensure those resolutions lead to actual change and growth? One effective method is by using the SMART goal technique.

SMART stands for Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. In this blog post, I’ll guide you on how to set SMART goals to conquer your professional growth resolutions in 2022.

1. Specific

The first step in setting SMART goals is to create clear, specific objectives. General resolutions often lack direction and focus, making them challenging to achieve. Instead of saying, “I want to improve my management skills in 2022,” specify a target, such as “I want to become a project manager within the next two years.”

When defining your goal, consider asking yourself relevant questions such as where you want to be, which skills you need to develop, or what challenges you aim to overcome. By focusing on specific aspects of your career, you enhance the goal’s clarity, increasing your chances of achieving it.

Example

General: Improve your management skills.

Specific: Become a certified project manager within two years.

2. Measurable

Once you have a specific goal, ensure that you can measure progress and success. Measurable goals provide a clear understanding of what steps need to be taken, allowing you to track progress or identify when adjustments are necessary.

Set milestones to divide the journey into manageable tasks, creating a sense of accomplishment along the way. By tracking these milestones, you can assess whether the progress aligns with your goal, providing you with the opportunity to update or further define your objective if necessary.

Example

Measurable: Complete Project Management Professional (PMP) certification by attending classes, preparing for exams, and practicing project management during each study interval.

3. Achievable

Although setting ambitious goals can push your professional growth, they must also be realistic. Achievable goals provide motivation and help avoid burnout, giving you the confidence to pursue bigger objectives in the future.

Reflect on your current skills, work environment, and personal circumstances, identifying where you can reasonably exert effort. If the goal seems too far-fetched or unrealistic, consider breaking it into smaller milestones or shorter timeframes.

Example

Achievable: Boost your overall performance by improving your time-management and communication skills by 15%, allowing you to better manage projects and build stronger relationships with your team members.

4. Relevant

Goals should contribute to your professional growth in a meaningful way and align with your career aspirations. Before setting your objectives, consider how each goal ties into your long-term goals and ambitions.

By ensuring that your goals are relevant, you can maintain motivation and avoid wasting time on unimportant tasks. In turn, this enables you to focus on achieving objectives that truly matter to your career development.

Example

Relevant: If your long-term career goal is to manage a remote team, then developing goal-oriented communication skills is relevant to your professional growth.

5. Time-bound

Setting a deadline for achieving your goal keeps you accountable and encourages a sense of urgency. Time-bound goals provide structure, guiding you on the appropriate pace for working toward that vision.

A well-defined timeframe creates a clear starting point for tracking your progress and allows you to identify if adjustments are necessary. Be mindful when setting the time frame, considering personal circumstances or any external dependencies that may affect the timeline.

Example

Time-bound: Develop new project management techniques and complete your PMP certification at the end of the 12 months.

Conclusion

Using the SMART goal framework to set and achieve professional growth resolutions allows you to stay focused and motivated throughout the year. The clarity and structure provided by this technique can lead to significant professional development and personal satisfaction in 2022.

By putting forth your best effort, tracking progress, and adapting as needed, you can truly conquer your professional growth resolutions through the power of SMART goals. Now, go ahead and reach for your dream career, one targeted step at a time. Good luck!
