Quick Fixes for Common Household Plumbing Problems


Plumbing issues can cause a great deal of inconvenience, especially when they occur without warning. From clogged drains to leaky pipes, these problems can impact the efficiency of your home’s plumbing system and lead to wasted resources, water damage, and more. Thankfully, there are several quick and easy fixes you can try to resolve these common issues before calling a professional.

  1. Clearing Clogged Drains

Clogged drains are a common plumbing problem that can be caused by hair, soap scum, grease, and other debris. To unclog a slow-moving drain, begin by pouring a pot of boiling water down the drain. This can help dislodge any materials that have started to accumulate. If needed, follow up with a mixture of one cup of baking soda and one cup of vinegar. Pour the mixture down the drain, let it sit for about five minutes, then flush with cold water. If the drain is still slow, consider using a plunger to further clear the clogs.

  1. Fixing Leaky Pipes

A leaky pipe can waste significant amounts of water and cause damage to your home’s structure. To fix a small leak, determine the source of the problem by turning off the water supply to the area and checking for signs of moisture or dripping water. If you find a leak, apply a pipe patching kit or wrap the area with a waterproof tape like Teflon tape. If the problem persists, consider replacing the section of pipe entirely.

  1. Replacing a Worn-Out Faucet

A worn-out faucet can cause inconsistent water pressure and lead to water waste. To replace a faulty faucet, first turn off the water supply to the sink. Remove the handle, aerator, and any other parts holding the faucet in place. Installation of a new faucet is straightforward; just screw the new faucet into the remaining parts and turn on the water supply.

  1. Addressing Low Water Pressure

Low water pressure may stem from several sources, such as clogs, mineral buildup in the pipes, or worn-out fixtures. To boost water pressure, start by removing the aerator and cleaning any debris that might be restricting the flow. If water pressure remains low, consider using a pipe cleaner to remove any obstructions inside the pipes. Additionally, you can add a water pressure booster to help increase pressure throughout your plumbing system.

  1. Fixing a Running Toilet

A running toilet can waste several gallons of water per day and lead to higher water bills. To stop a running toilet, first check the chain connecting the float to the valve. Ensure that the chain is properly adjusted to stop the flow of water when the tank is full. If you still encounter issues, consider replacing the flapper valve, which controls the flow of water from the tank into the bowl.

While these solutions can help address common plumbing problems, remember that some issues may require the expertise of a professional plumber. Keep an emergency contact on hand and rely on their services when necessary. By being proactive with smaller issues, you can prevent more extensive and costly repairs down the line. So, grab your toolbox, gather your materials, and make necessary fixes to improve your home’s plumbing efficiency.
