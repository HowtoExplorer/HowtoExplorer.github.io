Through the Looking Glass: The Disappearing Line Between Good and Evil


In Lewis Carroll’s classic novel, “Through the Looking Glass, Alice asks the White Queen, “Why do you sit out here all alone?” To which the Queen replies, “Why, in this world, it is a sad thing to be alone, let alone in another!” This response highlights the dual nature of good and evil that exists in every individual, society, and world - and the ever-shifting line that separates them.

Throughout history, we have seen this dichotomy of morality play out in countless ways. From the fall of great civilizations to the rise of powerful leaders, good and evil have worked together in a delicate dance that shapes our reality. Unfortunately, as the influence of good seems to wane, the line between good and evil is becoming increasingly blurred, raising the question: Can we find our way back before it’s too late?

Good and Evil in the Modern World

As we move further into the digital age, society has undergone an unprecedented transformation. The development of technology has given us a vast amount of knowledge and power, but it has also blurred the lines between right and wrong by granting access to the darkest aspects of human nature.

The rapid advancement of technology has allowed us to create powerful weapons, with seemingly little regard for the consequences. The state of global affairs can be traced back to this shifting balance - the cold war, the chaos in the Middle East, and the ever-present threat of terrorism. Nowadays, it is becoming increasingly evident that the dichotomy between good and evil is becoming increasingly disjointed.

Surprisingly, it is not only external factors influencing this shift, but also our internal perceptions. Case in point: the phenomenon of social media has redefined our understanding of social norms, and consequently, our perception of right and wrong. With the ability to create a desired image of ourselves online, the true essence of good and evil has become lost in translation, further worsening the situation.

But all hope is not lost. By paying close attention to the actions of those around us and remaining diligent in our pursuit of understanding, we may still have a chance to re-define and restore this fragile balance.

Embracing Duality: The Reintroduction of Balance

It’s essential to acknowledge that humans are inherently complex, and neither solely good nor evil. As members of a civilized society, it’s our collective duty to nurture the inherent light within one another, while also recognizing evil for what it is and standing against it. Only by achieving this delicate balance can we hope to minimize the blurs between right and wrong.

To start this process, we must first learn to accept the existence of such opposing forces. Instead of rejecting the duality in our lives, we should strive to maintain connections that foster openness and understanding with people around us. This will encourage a genuine curiosity into the good and evil present within everyone.

Moreover, it is crucial to foster empathy, to understand the origins of an individual’s motives and encourage a healthy sense of responsibility. It is through strong moral foundations that we can nurture a connection with others and contribute positively to our world.

Being accountable for our actions is also essential. This not only includes recognizing the consequences of our choices but also acknowledging our faults. This recognition is a crucial first step on the potentially long road towards moral betterment and playing our part in maintaining and nurturing the delicate balance between good and evil.

The continuous evolution of society will always challenge our moral compass, but by building our resilience to shift and change in response to the world around us, we will be better equipped to face these challenges. The process may be slow and difficult, but the rewards are immeasurable.

Through the Looking Glass: Lessons for Today and Tomorrow

The continued success of our civilization is dependent upon the conscious effort of each one of us to strive for balance. Acknowledging the duality inherent in our core, as well as the shifting line between good and evil, can instigate positive change in society.

Having broadened our perspective, witnessed history’s lessons, and utilized our moral compass, we may yet find a way to steer a path that honors both the light and dark elements within us as we navigate the modern world in pursuit of lasting harmony.

As we strive to maintain the balance between good and evil, we must remember the words of the White Queen in “Through the Looking Glass”: “Why, in this world, it is a sad thing to be alone, let alone in another.” Together, our efforts may provide the keys to unlock the wisdom and courage to challenge this chaos and march towards a brighter tomorrow.

And so, the clock continues to tick, the leaves continue to fall, and the story of humanity’s moral compass drives forward. But in the end, it is up to each of us to decide where our allegiances lie and which reality we choose to shape: one where the line between good and evil still stands, defiant in the face of impermanence, or one where it slowly fades away, surrendering to the tide of forces beyond our control.
