The Dos and Don'ts of Using Balance Transfer Cards


Balance transfer credit cards can be a powerful tool for managing debt and saving money on interest payments. However, they come with certain risks and pitfalls that can turn a seemingly smart financial move into a costly mistake. In this guide, we’ll explore the dos and don’ts of using balance transfer cards to help you make informed decisions and maximize the benefits while avoiding potential drawbacks.

The Dos

1. Do Understand the Terms and Conditions

Before applying for a balance transfer card, it’s crucial to carefully read and understand the terms and conditions. Pay close attention to the introductory APR, balance transfer fees, ongoing interest rates, and any other fees associated with the card. Knowing these details will help you make an informed decision and avoid unpleasant surprises down the line.

2. Do Compare Offers

Not all balance transfer cards are created equal. Take the time to compare offers from different card issuers to find the one that best suits your needs. Look for cards with a lengthy introductory period, low or no balance transfer fees, and competitive ongoing interest rates. By doing your research, you can maximize the benefits of a balance transfer card and minimize the costs.

3. Do Create a Repayment Plan

While balance transfer cards offer a temporary reprieve from high-interest debt, they are not a long-term solution. It’s essential to have a clear repayment plan in place to pay off the transferred balance before the introductory period ends. Calculate how much you need to pay each month to clear the debt within the promotional period, and stick to the plan to avoid accumulating more interest.

4. Do Use the Card Responsibly

Once you’ve transferred your balance to a new card, avoid making new purchases on it. The goal is to pay off the existing debt, not add to it. Using the card for new expenses can complicate your repayment plan and lead to further financial stress. Keep the card solely for the purpose of paying down the transferred balance.

The Don’ts

1. Don’t Miss Payments

Late payments on a balance transfer card can have severe consequences. Not only will you likely incur late fees and penalties, but your introductory APR may also be revoked, causing your outstanding balance to accrue interest at a higher rate. Make it a priority to pay at least the minimum amount due on time each month to preserve the benefits of the balance transfer.

2. Don’t Close Your Old Accounts Immediately

Transferring a balance to a new card may be tempting, but it’s generally unwise to close the old accounts right away. Doing so can negatively impact your credit score by reducing your available credit and shortening your credit history. Keep the old accounts open until you’ve paid off the transferred balance and are confident in your financial stability.

3. Don’t Use the Card for Cash Advances

Cash advances on credit cards often come with exorbitant fees and high-interest rates, and balance transfer cards are no exception. Avoid using the card for cash advances, as it can significantly increase the cost of borrowing and hinder your debt repayment efforts.

4. Don’t Rely Solely on the Introductory Period

While the promotional period of a balance transfer card can provide breathing room for paying off debt, it’s essential to have a backup plan. Unexpected financial challenges or changes in your circumstances can derail your repayment efforts. Always have a contingency plan in place to deal with any unforeseen obstacles.

By adhering to these dos and don’ts of using balance transfer cards, you can harness the benefits of these financial tools while mitigating the associated risks. Remember, informed and responsible use of balance transfer cards can be a step towards financial freedom and debt relief.
