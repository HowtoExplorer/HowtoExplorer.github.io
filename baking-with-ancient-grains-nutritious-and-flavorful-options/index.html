Baking with Ancient Grains: Nutritious and Flavorful Options

3 min · 586 words

Baking is not only a delicious way to create mouthwatering treats, but it can also be an opportunity to explore unique and nutritious ingredients. One such category of ingredients that has gained popularity in recent years is ancient grains. These grains, which have been cultivated for centuries, offer a range of health benefits and add a delightful depth of flavor to your baked goods. In this blog post, we will delve into the world of baking with ancient grains, exploring their nutritional value and providing you with some delectable recipe ideas.

The Nutritional Value of Ancient Grains

Ancient grains are a diverse group of grains that have been largely unchanged over the centuries. They are often praised for their nutrient density and unique flavor profiles. Let’s take a closer look at some popular ancient grains and their nutritional benefits:

Quinoa

Quinoa, pronounced “keen-wah,” is a versatile grain that is naturally gluten-free and packed with protein. It contains all nine essential amino acids, making it a complete protein source. Quinoa is also rich in fiber, iron, magnesium, and antioxidants. Incorporating quinoa into your baked goods can add a nutty flavor and a delightful crunch.

Amaranth

Amaranth is a grain that was a staple food for the Aztecs and is now making a comeback in modern cuisine. It is gluten-free and an excellent source of protein, fiber, and several essential minerals, including calcium, iron, and phosphorus. Amaranth has a slightly peppery taste and can be ground into flour or used as a topping for bread and pastries.

Spelt

Spelt is an ancient grain with a mild nutty flavor and a lower gluten content than modern wheat varieties. It is rich in fiber, protein, and B vitamins. Spelt flour can be used as a substitute for wheat flour in many baking recipes, adding a unique flavor and texture to your creations.

Baking with Ancient Grains: Recipe Ideas

Now that we understand the nutritional benefits of ancient grains let’s explore some delightful recipes that incorporate these wholesome ingredients:

Quinoa Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 1/4 cup pure maple syrup
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, almond flour, coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla extract. Mix well.
  3. Fold in the dark chocolate chips.
  4. Using a tablespoon, drop spoonfuls of the cookie dough onto the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the edges turn golden brown.
  6. Allow the cookies to cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Amaranth Banana Bread

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mashed ripe bananas (about 2 medium bananas)
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups amaranth flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the mashed bananas, melted coconut oil, coconut sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the amaranth flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and smooth the top.
  6. Bake for 45-50 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. Allow the banana bread to cool in the pan for 10 minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.

Spelt Blueberry Muffins

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups spelt flour
  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • 1/3 cup melted coconut oil
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the spelt flour, coconut sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the almond milk, melted coconut oil, and vanilla extract.
  4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Gently fold in the fresh blueberries.
  6. Divide the batter evenly among the muffin cups.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Allow the muffins to cool in the tin for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

Conclusion

Baking with ancient grains not only adds a unique flavor and texture to your creations but also provides a wealth of nutritional benefits. Whether you choose to incorporate quinoa, amaranth, spelt, or any other ancient grain, you are sure to create wholesome and satisfying baked goods. So, grab your apron, preheat your oven, and embark on a culinary adventure with these nutritious and flavorful options. Happy baking!
