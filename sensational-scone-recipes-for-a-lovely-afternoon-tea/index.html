Sensational Scone Recipes for a Lovely Afternoon Tea


Afternoon tea is a quintessential British tradition that has gained popularity worldwide. It’s a delightful way to slow down, indulge in delicious treats, and enjoy the company of loved ones. At the heart of this beloved ritual is the humble scone, a sweet or savory pastry that is often served with tea, jam, and cream. In this post, we’ll explore some sensational scone recipes that are sure to elevate your afternoon tea game.

Classic Buttermilk Scones

For a traditional afternoon tea experience, you can’t go wrong with a classic buttermilk scone recipe. These tender, flaky pastries are infused with the tanginess of buttermilk and the sweetness of sugar. To make them, you’ll need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Add the cold butter and use a pastry blender or your fingertips to work it into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk and a pinch of salt. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until the dough comes together in a shaggy mass. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it forms a cohesive ball. Pat the dough into a circle that’s about 1 inch (2.5 cm) thick. Use a biscuit cutter or the rim of a glass to cut out scones. Gather the scraps, re-form the dough, and cut out additional scones. Place the scones on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, leaving about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of space between each scone. Brush the tops with a little extra buttermilk and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the scones are golden brown.

Cheddar and Chive Scones

For a savory twist on the classic scone, try adding some sharp cheddar and chives to the dough. These scones are perfect for serving with a warm cup of tea and a plate of finger sandwiches.

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup grated cheddar cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh chives
  • 1/2 cup buttermilk

Follow the same instructions as the classic buttermilk scones, but add the grated cheddar and chopped chives to the dry ingredients before stirring in the wet ingredients.

Cranberry Orange Scones

For a sweet and tangy treat, try adding some dried cranberries and orange zest to your scone dough. These scones are perfect for serving with a cup of tea and a dollop of whipped cream.

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 cup dried cranberries
  • 1 tablespoon orange zest
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream

Follow the same instructions as the classic buttermilk scones, but add the dried cranberries and orange zest to the dry ingredients before stirring in the wet ingredients. Brush the tops of the scones with a little extra heavy cream and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the scones are golden brown.

Lemon Poppy Seed Scones

For a bright and citrusy treat, try adding some lemon zest and poppy seeds to your scone dough. These scones are perfect for serving with a cup of tea and a slice of lemon.

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream

Follow the same instructions as the classic buttermilk scones, but add the lemon zest and poppy seeds to the dry ingredients before stirring in the wet ingredients. Brush the tops of the scones with a little extra heavy cream and bake for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the scones are golden brown.

Tips and Variations

  • To make your scones more tender, try using cold ingredients and handling the dough as little as possible.
  • To add some extra flavor to your scones, try substituting some of the all-purpose flour with whole wheat flour or adding some chopped nuts or seeds to the dough.
  • To make your scones more indulgent, try serving them with clotted cream and jam instead of butter and honey.
  • To make your scones more portable, try baking them in muffin tin liners instead of on a baking sheet.

With these sensational scone recipes, you’ll be well on your way to hosting a lovely afternoon tea that will impress your friends and family. Whether you’re a seasoned baker or a beginner, these recipes are sure to delight your taste buds and leave you feeling satisfied and content. So go ahead, get baking, and enjoy the delightful ritual of afternoon tea!
