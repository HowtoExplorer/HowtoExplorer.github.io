How to Make a Rental Feel Like Home: Decoration Tips


Renting a home can sometimes feel temporary, but with the right decor, you can transform your rental into a warm and inviting space that truly feels like home. Whether you’re living in a small apartment or a cozy house, these decoration tips will help you infuse your personality and style into your rental while maintaining the landlord’s guidelines.

Embrace Temporary Solutions

One of the challenges of decorating a rental is dealing with restrictions on permanent changes. To work around this, embrace temporary solutions that can easily be removed when you move out. Command hooks are great for hanging art and mirrors without damaging the walls, while removable wallpaper can add a pop of color or pattern to your space without leaving a trace when it’s time to leave.

Personalize with Textiles

Textiles are a fantastic way to add warmth and personality to a rental. Layering rugs can instantly cozy up a room, while throw pillows and blankets can inject color and style. Consider adding curtains to soften the windows and create a sense of privacy. Opt for lightweight, tension rod curtains for a non-permanent window treatment option.

Create a Gallery Wall

Gallery walls are a versatile and impactful way to showcase your favorite art, photographs, and mementos. Mix and match frames and artwork to create a visually appealing display that reflects your personal taste. Utilize command strips or picture hanging strips to avoid putting holes in the walls, and you can easily rearrange or remove the gallery wall when needed.

Utilize Multi-Functional Furniture

In a rental, space is often limited, so make the most of it by choosing multi-functional furniture pieces. Look for a storage ottoman that can double as a coffee table, a sofa bed for accommodating guests, or a dining table with foldable leaves to save space when not in use. This not only adds functionality to your space but also reduces clutter.

Add Greenery

Bringing nature indoors can breathe life into a rental and make it feel more inviting. Low-maintenance houseplants like pothos, snake plants, or succulents are perfect for renters, as they require minimal care and can thrive in various lighting conditions. Place them on shelves, countertops, or in hanging planters to infuse your space with a touch of nature.

Personalize with Lighting

Lighting plays a crucial role in setting the mood and ambiance of a home. Since permanent changes to lighting fixtures might not be allowed in a rental, consider using floor lamps, table lamps, and string lights to create a warm and inviting atmosphere. Choose bulbs with warm color temperatures to add a cozy glow to your space.

Showcase Your Collections

If you have a collection of books, vinyl records, or other items that reflect your interests, use them as decor to personalize your rental. Display your collection on shelves, in bookcases, or on wall-mounted racks. Not only does this add character to your space, but it also serves as a conversation starter for guests.

Final Thoughts

Decorating a rental doesn’t have to be limiting. By embracing temporary decor solutions, personalizing with textiles and artwork, and maximizing the functionality of your furniture, you can create a space that truly feels like home. Remember, the key is to infuse your personality and style into your rental while respecting the guidelines set by your landlord. With these decoration tips, you can turn your rental into a place that reflects who you are and where you feel truly comfortable and at home.
