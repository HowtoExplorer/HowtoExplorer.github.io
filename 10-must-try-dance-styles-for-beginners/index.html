10 Must-Try Dance Styles for Beginners


Beginning a new hobby can be both exciting and intimidating. When it comes to dance, there’s an abundance of styles to explore. To help you find your rhythm, we’ve compiled a list of ten dance styles perfect for beginners. Whether you’ve always wanted to dance or are looking for a fun way to stay active, these styles are sure to get your feet tapping!

  1. Ballroom Dance: This classical dance style dates back to the Victorian era and encompasses a wide range of dances, including the waltz, foxtrot, and tango. Ballroom dancing teaches precise footwork and elaborate partner work - an excellent choice for those seeking a structured dance style.
  2. Salsa: Originating in Cuba, this high-energy dance style is all about passion and rhythm. Salsa combines quick footwork with intricate body movements, creating an exhilarating experience for both partner and audience.
  3. Hip Hop: Born from the streets, hip hop dance has evolved into various styles, including breakdancing, popping, and locking. This contemporary style allows for self-expression through fluid and dynamic movements, making it popular among young dancers.
  4. Tap Dance: With its roots in African American vaudeville, tap dance is a percussive style that uses sounds made by the feet to create rhythm. This engaging form of dance requires Training and coordination, but its impressive sound effects make it a thrilling experience for both performer and spectator.
  5. Belly Dance: Originating in the Middle East, belly dance emphasizes control, fluidity, and sensuous movements of the hips and torso. This dance style offers a unique workout, engaging both the body and spirit.
  6. Zumba: A Latin-inspired fusion of dance and aerobics, Zumba combines steps from various styles like salsa, cumbia, and merengue. This dance workout offers non-stop fun, accompanied by energetic music, making it perfect for individuals looking to shed calories while dancing.
  7. Pilates Mat: While not technically a dance style, Pilates mat exercises involve coordinated movements that are similar to dancing. By engaging both the mind and body, Pilates improves core strength, flexibility, and balance in a ballet-inspired setting.
  8. Line Dance: Perfect for those who prefer dancing in groups, line dance involves a series of steps performed in a pre-planned sequence. With countless varieties, this accessible dance style caters to all skill levels and musical tastes.
  9. Hula Hoop Dance: Originating in ancient Hawaii, hula hoop dance tells stories through rhythmic movements. This creative dance style can be tailored to your preferences, incorporating elements of various cultures to tell tales through the spin of a hoop.
  10. Country Two-Step: With roots in Southern American culture, this dance style is a perfect blend of simplicity and elegance. Country two-step consists of fluid, flowing movements that need no partner, making it an accessible option for beginners.

Starting a new dance journey can be intimidating, but these ten styles offer a diverse range of options to match your interests and inclinations. So, whether you’re seeking a structured ballroom waltz, an energetic Zumba class, or a soulful belly dance, get ready to explore the world of dance and create lifelong memories on the dance floor!
