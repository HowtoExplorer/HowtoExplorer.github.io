Artisanal Pizza Making: From Dough to Toppings


Artisanal pizza making is an art form that requires patience, skill, and attention to detail. From the dough to the toppings, every step of the process is crucial in creating a truly exceptional pizza. In this blog post, we’ll take you on a journey through the art of artisanal pizza making, exploring the techniques, ingredients, and philosophies that set this type of pizza apart from its mass-produced counterparts.

The Dough

The journey begins with the dough. A good artisanal pizza dough is the foundation of a great pizza, and it’s the first step in the process. The dough should be made with high-quality flour, water, yeast, salt, and a touch of sugar. The ingredients should be mixed together in a specific order, with the yeast being the last addition, to allow for proper fermentation.

The dough should then be kneaded for a minimum of 10 minutes to develop the gluten, which gives the crust its structure and texture. After the dough has been kneaded, it should be placed in a lightly oiled bowl, covered with plastic wrap, and allowed to rise in a warm, draft-free place for at least 2 hours.

Once the dough has risen, it should be punched down and divided into smaller balls, each of which will become a pizza crust. The dough should then be shaped into its desired form, whether it’s a circle, rectangle, or square.

The Sauce

The sauce is the next important component of artisanal pizza making. A good sauce should be made with high-quality tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, salt, and sugar. The ingredients should be sautéed together in a specific order, with the garlic being the first addition, to allow for proper flavor development.

The sauce should then be simmered for at least 30 minutes to reduce the acidity and enhance the flavor. Once the sauce has finished simmering, it should be strained through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any excess liquid and ensure a smooth consistency.

The Cheese

The cheese is the third crucial component of artisanal pizza making. A good artisanal pizza should have a blend of cheeses, including mozzarella, parmesan, and ricotta. The cheese should be shredded or sliced into thin strips, depending on the desired texture.

The cheese should then be sprinkled evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. The cheese should be melted in a hot oven, either with or without the sauce, depending on the desired texture and flavor.

The Toppings

The toppings are the final component of artisanal pizza making. The toppings should be fresh, seasonal, and of high quality. Some popular toppings include vegetables, meats, seafood, and fungi.

The toppings should be added to the pizza in a specific order, with the sauce being the first addition, followed by the cheese and finally the toppings. The pizza should then be baked in a hot oven for a minimum of 15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

The Philosophies

Artisanal pizza making is not just about the ingredients and techniques, but also about the philosophies and philosophies behind it. The philosophy of artisanal pizza making is centered around the idea of creating a truly exceptional pizza that is greater than the sum of its parts.

This means that the artisanal pizza maker should be dedicated to using only the highest-quality ingredients, should be willing to take the time to perfect their craft, and should be committed to creating a pizza that is truly exceptional. The philosophy of artisanal pizza making is not just about creating a pizza, but about creating an experience.

The Techniques

Artisanal pizza making requires a range of techniques, from kneading the dough to shaping the crust. The key to success is to develop a consistent technique that allows for precise control over the dough and the toppings.

Some key techniques include:

  • Kneading the dough for a minimum of 10 minutes to develop the gluten
  • Allowing the dough to rise for at least 2 hours to ensure proper fermentation
  • Punching down the dough to release any air bubbles and ensure a smooth texture
  • Shaping the dough into its desired form
  • Adding the toppings in a specific order to ensure proper flavor development
  • Baking the pizza in a hot oven for a minimum of 15 minutes to ensure a crispy crust and melted cheese

The Conclusion

Artisanal pizza making is a complex and nuanced process that requires patience, skill, and attention to detail. From the dough to the toppings, every step of the process is crucial in creating a truly exceptional pizza. By understanding the techniques, ingredients, and philosophies behind artisanal pizza making, you can create a pizza that is truly greater than the sum of its parts.

Whether you’re a seasoned pizza maker or just starting out, artisanal pizza making is a journey that requires dedication and passion. With the right techniques, ingredients, and philosophies, you can create a pizza that is truly exceptional and will delight your senses.
