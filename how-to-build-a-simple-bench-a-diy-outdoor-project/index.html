How to Build a Simple Bench: A DIY Outdoor Project


Building a simple bench can be a rewarding DIY project that enhances your outdoor space. Whether you want a place to relax in your garden, a spot to enjoy your morning coffee, or additional seating for gatherings, a homemade bench can serve multiple purposes. This guide will walk you through the steps to create a sturdy and stylish bench that you can be proud of.

Materials Needed

Before you start, gather the following materials:

  • Wood: Choose weather-resistant wood like cedar, redwood, or pressure-treated lumber. For a simple bench, you will need:

    • 2 pieces of 2x4 inches, 6 feet long (for the top)
    • 4 pieces of 2x4 inches, 4 feet long (for the legs)
    • 2 pieces of 2x4 inches, 5 feet long (for the support beams)

  • Wood screws: 3-inch screws are ideal for securing the pieces together.

  • Wood glue: This will add extra strength to your joints.

  • Sandpaper: Use 120-grit and 220-grit sandpaper for smoothing the wood.

  • Outdoor wood finish or sealant: This will protect your bench from the elements.

  • Tools: You will need a saw (circular or miter saw), a drill, a measuring tape, a square, and a screwdriver.

Step 1: Planning Your Bench

Before cutting any wood, take a moment to plan your bench. Consider the following:

  • Dimensions: A standard bench is typically 4 to 6 feet long, 18 inches high, and 15 to 20 inches deep. Adjust the dimensions based on your space and needs.

  • Design: Decide if you want a simple rectangular bench or something more elaborate. For beginners, a straightforward design is recommended.

  • Location: Choose a spot in your yard or garden where the bench will be placed. Ensure it’s level and has a good view.

Step 2: Cutting the Wood

Once you have your plan, it’s time to cut the wood. Measure and mark your cuts carefully:

  1. Top: Cut the two 2x4 pieces to 6 feet in length for the bench top.
  2. Legs: Cut the four 2x4 pieces to 18 inches for the legs.
  3. Support Beams: Cut the two 2x4 pieces to 5 feet for the support beams.

Use a square to ensure your cuts are straight, and always wear safety goggles when using power tools.

Step 3: Assembling the Bench

Now that you have all your pieces cut, it’s time to assemble the bench.

Step 3.1: Attaching the Legs

  1. Position the Legs: Take one of the 5-foot support beams and lay it on the ground. Position two of the 18-inch leg pieces at each end, forming a rectangle.
  2. Secure the Legs: Use wood glue at the joints for added strength, then drill in the 3-inch screws to secure the legs to the support beam. Repeat this for the other side.

Step 3.2: Attaching the Top

  1. Position the Top: Once both sides are assembled, flip them upright. Place the two 6-foot pieces on top, ensuring they are evenly spaced.
  2. Secure the Top: Use wood glue and screws to attach the top pieces to the legs. Make sure everything is square and level.

Step 3.3: Adding Support Beams

  1. Install the Support Beams: To add stability, attach the remaining 5-foot support beams between the legs. Position them about 6 inches from the bottom of the legs and secure them with screws.

Step 4: Sanding the Bench

After the bench is assembled, it’s time to sand it down. Use 120-grit sandpaper to smooth out any rough edges and surfaces. Follow up with 220-grit sandpaper for a finer finish. This step is crucial, as it will prevent splinters and ensure a comfortable seating experience.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

To protect your bench from the elements, apply an outdoor wood finish or sealant. This will help prevent warping, cracking, and fading due to sun exposure and moisture. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions for application, and allow the bench to dry completely before using it.

Step 6: Placing Your Bench

Once the finish is dry, it’s time to place your bench in its designated spot. If you’re placing it on grass or soil, consider using small pieces of wood or bricks under the legs to prevent them from sinking into the ground over time.

Step 7: Personalizing Your Bench

Now that your bench is complete, consider personalizing it to make it your own.
