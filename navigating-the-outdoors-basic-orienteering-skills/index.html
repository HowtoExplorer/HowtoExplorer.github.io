Navigating the Outdoors: Basic Orienteering Skills


Orienteering is a thrilling outdoor activity that offers countless opportunities for exploration, exercise, and exhilaration. Masters of this ancient craft rely on their wits and a keen sense of direction to successfully navigate their way through uncharted territories, natural landscapes, and wrought obstacles. But don’t worry, you don’t have to be a seasoned veteran to get started - anyone can learn the basics of orienteering!

The Fundamental Tools of Orienteering

Before you embark on your orienteering journey, it’s essential to familiarize yourself with the standard gear most orienteers use. These include:

  1. Compass: Your trusty compass will be your best friend in the wilderness. Always use a compass with a clear and accurate map of the area you’ll be navigating. Leader:
  2. Map: A detailed and topographical map of the region will guide your exploration. Topographical maps display natural features like elevation, where to find water sources, and the general layout of the terrain.
  3. Map Case: Protect your precious map with a robust and waterproof map case. It will keep it safe from the elements and any accidental spills.
  4. Watch: A watch with an electronic compass, timer, and chronograph is your best bet for keeping track of time and distances in the outdoors. As a beginner, though, a simple watch is enough.

Orienting Yourself on the Map

Before venturing out into the wilderness, learn to familiarize yourself with the four cardinal directions: North, South, East, and West. Orienting yourself on the map involves aligning your compass and map in the same way that the cardinal directions align with real-world geography.

  1. Grid Coordinates: Maps often have a grid coordinate system that allows you to pinpoint your location and plot courses across the terrain. Study the grid lines and how they correspond to the physical features you see on the map.

  2. Magnetic North: Your compass’s iris points to magnetic north, not true north. Ensure to account for this 11-degree variance when orienting your map and compass.

  3. Backbearing and Forward Planning: A ‘back-bearing’ indicates the direction you must face to align your compass with the map when you’re going forward. Practice taking bearings - it’s an essential skill for a successful orienteering adventure.

Navigation Techniques

Once you’ve mastered the essential tools and orienting techniques, it’s time to learn two crucial navigation skills: triangulation and resection.

  1. Triangulation: To triangulate a point, you’ll need to measure two angles that have a fixed point in common. Find two prominent points on the map, plot them on your compass, and draw your course.

  2. Resection: The reverse of triangulation, resection helps you pinpoint your location using two known points on the map. Plot the points on your compass, align them with the corresponding points in your environment, and voilà - your approximate location!

Mastering Orienteering

Remember, the key to success in orienteering is steady practice. As you hone in on your skills, try to challenge yourself with more intricate courses and varied terrain.

Orienteering combines agility, resourcefulness, and an unbeatable sense of adventure. This captivating outdoor activity offers the unparalleled opportunity to get in touch with nature while developing valuable navigation skills. So what are you waiting for? Pack your gear, study your map, and set out for your first orienteering expedition!
