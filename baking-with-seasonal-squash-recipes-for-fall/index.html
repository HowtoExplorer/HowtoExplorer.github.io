Baking with Seasonal Squash: Recipes for Fall


As the air cools and the leaves begin to change, our kitchens are filled with the warmth and comfort of seasonal squash. From the familiar butternut and acorn varieties to the less common delicata and kabocha, each type brings its own unique flavor and texture to the table. In this post, we'll explore the world of baking with seasonal squash, highlighting recipes that showcase the versatility of these beloved vegetables. Understanding the Basics Before diving into recipes, it's essential to understand the different types of squash and their characteristics. Butternut squash, for example, has a sweet, nutty flavor and a smooth, creamy texture, making it perfect for soups and purees. Acorn squash, on the other hand, has a slightly sweet, earthy taste and a tender, crumbly flesh that pairs well with sweet and savory flavors. Sweet Baking Delights While squash is often associated with savory dishes, it also lends itself beautifully to sweet creations. Here are a few recipes to try: Spiced Butternut Squash Cake

1 large butternut squash, peeled and cubed 1 cup granulated sugar 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon 1 teaspoon ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup vegetable oil 2 large eggs 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Combine the squash, sugar, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt in a blender or food processor. Process until smooth, then transfer to a large mixing bowl. Whisk in the flour, oil, eggs, and vanilla. Pour into two greased 9-inch round cake pans and bake at 350°F for 35-40 minutes. Roasted Delicata Squash Pie

2 large delicata squash, sliced into 1/2-inch thick rounds 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 cup granulated sugar 1/2 cup packed brown sugar 1/4 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted 2 large eggs 1 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 cup heavy cream or half-and-half

Toss the squash slices with the melted butter, salt, and a pinch of sugar. Spread on a baking sheet and roast at 400°F for 20-25 minutes or until tender. Combine the flour, granulated sugar, and brown sugar in a large mixing bowl. Add the roasted squash, eggs, vanilla, and heavy cream. Mix until smooth, then pour into a 9-inch pie dish. Savory Squash Delights Squash is a natural fit for savory creations, from hearty soups to satisfying side dishes. Here are a few recipes to Try: Winter Squash Risotto

2 cups arborio rice 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth, warmed 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese 2 tablespoons olive oil 1 small butternut squash, peeled and cubed Salt and black pepper, to taste 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley 2 tablespoons chopped fresh sage

Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the squash and cook until tender, about 5 minutes. Add the rice and cook, stirring, for 1-2 minutes. Gradually add the broth, stirring constantly, until the rice is tender and creamy. Stir in the Parmesan, parsley, and sage. Season with salt and black pepper to taste. Kabocha Squash Gratin

2 large kabocha squash, peeled and cubed 2 tablespoons unsalted butter 1/4 cup granulated sugar 1/4 cup all-purpose flour 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese 1/2 cup heavy cream Salt and black pepper, to taste Fresh thyme, for garnish

Preheat the oven to 375°F. Toss the squash with the butter, sugar, flour, and a pinch of salt in a large mixing bowl. Transfer to a 9x13-inch baking dish and top with the cheddar and heavy cream. Bake for 25-30 minutes or until the squash is tender and the top is golden brown. Garnish with fresh thyme. Tips and Variations When working with seasonal squash, keep the following tips in mind:

Choose squash that's heavy for its size and has a hard, unblemished rind. Store squash in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight. To roast squash, cut it in half lengthwise, scoop out the seeds, and place on a baking sheet. Drizzle with oil and season with salt and pepper before roasting at 400°F for 30-40 minutes or until tender. To puree squash, cook and mash it in a blender or food processor. Strain the mixture through a cheesecloth or fine-mesh sieve for a smooth, velvety texture.

Conclusion Baking with seasonal squash is a true delight, offering a world of flavors and textures to explore. Whether you're a seasoned cook or just starting to experiment with new ingredients, these recipes are sure to inspire your creativity and warmth your kitchen. As the seasons change and the squash abounds, take advantage of this versatile vegetable and discover the magic that happens when it meets the oven.
