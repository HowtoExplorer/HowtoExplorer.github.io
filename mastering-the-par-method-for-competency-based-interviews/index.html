Mastering the PAR Method for Competency-Based Interviews


Are you gearing up for a competency-based interview and looking to ace it with flying colors? One powerful technique that can significantly boost your performance in such interviews is the PAR method. Understanding and mastering this method can give you a competitive edge and help you showcase your skills and experiences effectively. In this comprehensive guide, we will delve into what the PAR method is, why it’s important, and how you can use it to excel in your next competency-based interview.

What is the PAR Method?

The PAR method is a structured approach used to respond to behavioral questions in competency-based interviews. PAR stands for Problem, Action, and Result. This method is designed to help you provide specific examples from your past experiences to demonstrate your competencies and suitability for the role you are applying for. By following the PAR framework, you can craft compelling and detailed responses that highlight your capabilities and achievements.

Breakdown of PAR:

  • Problem: Start by describing a specific situation or challenge you encountered in a previous role. Clearly outline the context and the issue you faced. Be concise but provide enough detail for the interviewer to understand the scenario.

  • Action: Outline the actions you took to address the problem. Explain the steps you followed, the skills you utilized, and the decisions you made. Focus on your contributions and the rationale behind your choices.

  • Result: Finally, share the outcomes of your actions. Highlight the impact of your efforts, whether it led to a successful project completion, cost savings, improved efficiency, or any other positive result. Quantify your achievements if possible to add credibility to your response.

Why is the PAR Method Important?

Competency-based interviews are structured to assess specific skills and behaviors that are essential for success in a particular role. Employers use these interviews to evaluate how candidates have handled situations in the past as an indicator of how they may perform in the future. The PAR method allows you to demonstrate your abilities through real-life examples, making your responses more convincing and memorable.

By using the PAR method, you can provide compelling evidence of your skills and capabilities. This structured approach helps you stay focused, ensures that your responses are relevant to the question, and enables you to showcase your strengths effectively. Moreover, by sharing concrete results, you can showcase your achievements and the value you can bring to the prospective employer.

How to Master the PAR Method:

To excel in competency-based interviews using the PAR method, follow these key tips:

  1. Prepare Relevant Examples: Before the interview, identify specific examples from your past experiences that align with the key competencies required for the role. Select examples that demonstrate a range of skills and achievements.

  2. Practice Structuring Your Responses: Practice framing your responses using the PAR method. Start by describing the problem or situation, then detail the actions you took, and finally, discuss the results you achieved.

  3. Quantify Your Achievements: Whenever possible, quantify the results of your actions. Use numbers, percentages, or any relevant metrics to provide concrete evidence of your impact.

  4. Focus on Your Role: Emphasize your individual contributions within a team context. Highlight the skills you utilized and the decisions you made to overcome challenges and achieve success.

  5. Be Succinct and Specific: Keep your responses concise and focused. Avoid unnecessary details and ensure that your examples directly address the competencies being assessed.

In Conclusion:

Mastering the PAR method for competency-based interviews can give you a significant advantage in the job application process. By effectively applying this structured approach, you can showcase your skills, experiences, and achievements in a compelling manner. Remember to prepare thoroughly, practice articulating your responses, and focus on providing clear and impactful examples. By following the PAR framework, you can confidently navigate competency-based interviews and impress potential employers with your expertise and capabilities. Good luck!

Remember, preparation and practice are key to success in competency-based interviews. By mastering the PAR method and honing your storytelling skills, you can boost your confidence and present yourself as a strong candidate. Embrace the opportunity to share your experiences and demonstrate your competencies effectively. Best of luck on your upcoming interviews!
