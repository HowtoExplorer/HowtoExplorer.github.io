How to Engage with Interviewers: Building Rapport


Have you ever walked out of a job interview, feeling confident and prepared, only to wonder why it didn't quite live up to your expectations? Perhaps you received a generic email weeks later, stating that the position had gone to someone else. This is often due to the ability to connect with interviewers, establishing rapport and creating a lasting impression. The unfortunate truth is that many job seekers underestimate the value of building relationships during the hiring process. A simple conversation can be the difference between landing your dream job and watching it slip away. Understanding the Psychology of Opposites: Leverage Shared Experiences to Create Common Ground According to Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs, one of the fundamental human drives is the need for connection and belonging. People often establish relationships based on shared values, interests, and experiences. This commonality establishes a foundation for building rapport, fostering a connection with the interviewer. A conversation starter might be asking about a specific college or university, or a common hobby or interest. This allows for transparency and builds a sense of mutual understanding. The art of creating common ground, however, requires finesse. Do not fall into the trap of overusing clunky generic questions, such as "Where are you from?"
