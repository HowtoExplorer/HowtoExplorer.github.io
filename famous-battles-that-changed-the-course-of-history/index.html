Famous Battles That Changed the Course of History


The history of mankind is saturated with extraordinary battles that have redirected the course of events. Conflicts that have left a lasting impression and altered the way the future unfolded. Here are some of the pivotal battles that changed the course of history.

The Battle of Marathon (490 BC)

The Battle of Marathon remains one of the earliest panhellenic victories recorded in Greek historiography. It altered the course of history, leading to the emergence of the mighty Athenian Empire. As the Persian king Darius sent troops into the heart of Greece, approximately 6,400 strong Athenian soldiers led by their commander Miltiades, changed the course of Western Civilization forever. The victory at Marathon marked a significant change in the way ancient Greeks perceived themselves and their role in the world. This marked the retreat and failure of the invasion, ultimately changing the course of history and leaving military tactics crucial on the outcome of wars.

The Battle of Hastings (1066 AD)

Then and now, the Battle of Hastings remains one of the most important battles in world history. William, Duke of Normandy, defeated the Anglo-Saxon King Harold II, washing away the last remnants of the ancient English royal house. This battle is critical in English history as it marks the dawn of a new era. The Battle of Hastings gave rise to the Normans ruling England, leading to a flush of stimulating cultural changes, from sustenance of the now historic English language to the construction of the famous Windsor Castle.

The Battle of Gettysburg (1863 AD)

The Battle of Gettysburg marks a significant turning point in the American Civil War, ultimately making the battle a major factor shaping the course of history. The conflict resulted in huge casualties for the Confederate Army, played a pivotal role in turning the tide of the Civil War, and substantially influenced President Lincoln’s Emancipation Proclamation. This turning point eventually contributed to the abolition of slavery.

The Battle of Tsushima (1905 AD)

The ingenius deployment of naval power exhibited in the battle of Tsushima (also known as the Sea of Japan) drastically changed the course of the Russo-Japanese War and further shaped the modern-world balance of power in the Pacific. This incredible use of naval intelligence shattered the pre-conceived notion of the world’s top naval power, Russia. The battle ended in a decisive Russian naval defeat, greatly impacting the balance of power and resulting in the Treaty of Portsmouth.

Lastly, the repercussions of each of these battles serve as a reminder of the enormous significance of military prowess and understanding in the shaping of the course of history. Each of these battles led to significant changes in power dynamics, culture, and religion. Fierce and historic, these battles are etched into the annals of history as events that changed the world as we know it.

To recap, five of the most important battles throughout history have forever altered the course of humanity. The battles of Marathon, Hastings, Gettysburg, Tsushima, and many others will be remembered for centuries to come. These pits of struggle and strife mark significant shifts in power, culture, tradition, and even language, producing a ripple effect that engulfs the world and shapes it until the next war or event comes along to instigate change once more. To this day, we study these battles and mimics their impacts to comprehend and learn from the pivotal moments of history.
