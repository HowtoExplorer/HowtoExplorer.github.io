The Lowdown on high-altitude Skincare: Tips for Keeping Your Glowing in High-pressure Environments


Have you ever felt parched, dehydrated, or dried out after returning from a mountainous retreat or a ski trip? The air is thinner at high altitudes, which means that the oxygen levels are lower. This drastic change can impact your skin’s natural moisture balance and deprive it of much-needed hydration. Don’t let high altitudes wreak havoc on your skin. Here are some tips and tricks to keep your skin glowing seamlessly amidst high-pressure environments.

1. Stay Hydrated – Drink Plenty of Water

It’s crucial to drink adequate amounts of water at high altitudes because the dry atmosphere can lead to a constant feeling of thirst. Dehydration affects our skin’s elasticity, causing it to appear dry, dull, and wrinkled. To keep your skin feeling supple and healthy, make sure to sip water consistently and avoid beverages like tea, coffee, and alcohol that can dehydrate you further.

2. Protect Your Skin from Harsh Elements

Sun damage and snow blindness go hand in hand in high altitude areas, so be sure to wear sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30 that also offers protection from both UVA and UVB rays. Don’t forget to reapply every few hours, especially if you’re skiing or snowboarding. Protective clothing, such as ski masks and sunglasses, can also go a long way in shielding your skin from extreme weather.

3. Moisturize Regularly

A dry climate like the one found at high altitudes can make your skin feel tight and flaky. Carrying a travel-sized moisturizer in your bag can help keep skin hydrated and soft throughout your snowy adventures. Look for a moisturizer that is rich in hydrating ingredients such as hyaluronic acid, ceramides, and glycerin.

4. Provide a Protective Barrier with Sheet Masks

Facial sheet masks are an excellent solution for replenishing lost hydration and nutrients when your skin is screaming out for extra care. With active ingredients like vitamins, antioxidants, and hyaluronic acid, sheet masks can provide the nourishment that high-altitude skin needs to stay glowing. Opt for single-usage, pre-soaked masks to make them portable and easy to carry during your travels.

5. Pamper Your Skin with Overnight Products

At night, after coming in from the chilly weather, give your skin some breathing time. Deep moisturizing masks or overnight treatments can lock in hydration while you sleep, providing your skin with essential nourishment for healthier and radiant skin. Consider using overnight masks that contain ingredients like plant-based extracts and essential oils to help soothe and mitigate the effects of high altitude stress on your skin.

6. Nourish Your Skin Internally with Supplements

Be mindful of changing your diet to accommodate your body’s increased need for vital nutrients at high altitude. Consider taking supplements that contain vitamins like C and E, omega-3 fatty acids, and antioxidants, as these will help protect your skin against damage from both UVA radiation and a climate that’s devoid of moisture. Make sure you discuss your diet and supplement plans with a healthcare provider before you head out to a high-altitude location.

7. Give Your Skin Some R&R - Rest and Repair

Last but not least, make sure to give your skin a break when you’re in a high-altitude environment. Plan a quiet night in with a warm bath and a gentle exfoliation or a calming face mask to keep your skin relaxed and rejuvenated.

Remember, maintaining your skincare routine during high-altitude trips may take more effort and awareness, but your skin will thank you with a healthy, radiant glow after your adventure. Ready to conquer those peaks while keeping your skin looking fabulous?
