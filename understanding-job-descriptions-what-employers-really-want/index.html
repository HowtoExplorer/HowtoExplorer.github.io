Understanding Job Descriptions: What Employers Really Want


When it comes to job searching, one of the most critical steps is understanding the job description. This might seem straightforward - after all, it's a written summary of the job's responsibilities and requirements, right? Wrong. While it's true that job descriptions aim to convey the job's duties and expectations, they often conceal just as much as they reveal. Employers may use job descriptions to set clear expectations, but they also have other motivations that can impact the job's actual requirements. It's Not Just About the Job When you first read a job description, you might feel a sense of culpability. "Oh, I should definitely be able to do this," you think. But such a reaction often stems from overlooking the job description's broader purpose. Employers use job descriptions as a means of evaluating applicants. As such, they often tailor the description to showcase desirable qualities that the employer seeks. By doing so, employers aim to attract potential candidates who fit a specific profile. Looking Beyond the Surface Level The job description's primary goal is to provide an accurate portrayal of the job's duties. Nevertheless, even well-intentioned job descriptions can be misleading. Employers frequently omit or downplay parts of the job's role that might deter highly-qualified candidates. For example, a job description might gloss over the company's work-life balance or ignore the fact that many of the job's responsibilities involve ongoing training. Cultural and Organizational Are a Must A study found that work environments that prioritize employee well-being tend to retain their best talent. The same applies to jobs that appeal to employees' emotions and values. Employers attribute significant importance to these qualities when hiring employees. Incorporating open-ended statements and descriptive language in the job description reveals to potential job applicants how they would fit into a company or work environment. Data-Driven Job Descriptions Automated tools have improved the accuracy of job descriptions by digging deep into the job's needs. These software solutions scrutinize existing job descriptions, the needs of HR, and worker aptitudes. They separate all the hard data from ambiguous descriptions allowing readers to navigate job listings more efficiently and avoid being misled. While it's not perfect, these tools have made significant strides in reversing job applicants' confusions concerning position duties and responsibilities.
