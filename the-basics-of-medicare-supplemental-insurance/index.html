The Basics of Medicare Supplemental Insurance


Medicare is a federal health insurance program designed for individuals 65 and older, as well as certain younger people with disabilities. While Medicare provides a broad range of health coverage, it has its limitations. A significant portion of Medicare recipients rely on Medicare supplemental insurance to fill the gaps in coverage. Choosing the Right Medicare Supplement Insurance Medicare supplemental insurance, also known as Medigap, is designed to supplement Medicare Part A and Part B. It covers expenses not covered by Medicare, such as deductible and coinsurance fees, copolymer and procedures. There are different types of Medigap policies, each with its unique set of benefits.

Plan A: The most basic of all the plans, offering the least amount of coverage. Plan B: Providing a bit more coverage than Plan A, this plan offers a broader range of benefits. Plan C: Offers more coverage, including between in hospitals. Plan D: Supplements Medicare Part A and B fully. Plan F: Offers the most extensive coverage available, making financial projections and future goal setting much easier. Plan G: Supplements Plan B and helps cover hospital bills and medical expenses Plan K: Covers 50% of doctor charges. Plan L: Covers 75% of doctor charges.
