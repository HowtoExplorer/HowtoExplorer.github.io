Winter Skincare Survival Guide: Tips for Keeping Your Skin Happy All Season Long


Winter is here, and as the temperature drops, our skin tends to suffer as a result. Harsh winds, freezing temperatures, and indoor heating can strip the skin of its natural oils, causing dryness, flaking, and irritation. This season, let’s make a pact to ensure our skin survives the winter months, and even thrives. In this blog post, we’re sharing some skincare tips that will leave you with a glowing complexion all season long.

1. Be Kind to Your Moisture Barrier

One of the key aspects of winter skincare is fortifying the moisture barrier in your skin. The barrier works to keep the water inside the skin and protect it from external irritants. When it’s dry and damaged, it becomes more permeable and allows irritants to sneak in, causing inflammatory skin conditions like eczema and psoriasis to flare up.

To keep your moisture barrier strong and healthy, look for skincare products that contain ceramides, which are fatty substances that are already present in your skin. They play a crucial role in maintaining the skin’s natural barrier. Ingredients like hyaluronic acid and glycerin also help to draw and retain moisture to the skin.

2. Exfoliate Gently

Exfoliating helps to remove dead cells from the surface of the skin, making it smoother and more receptive to moisture. However, it’s vital to be gentle during the cold, dry months. For starters, exfoliate less frequently – maybe once a week instead of weekly – to avoid irritating the skin. Opt for a gentle scrub or a chemical exfoliator with mild acids like lactic acid or mandelic acid.

3. Slip on Lip Protection

Chapped, cracked, and sore lips are a well-known winter side effect. To prevent this, always carry a lip balm with you and apply it several times throughout the day. Remember, the earlier you start using lip balm in the fall, the better-off your lips will be in the winter months. When selecting a lip balm, choose one with an SPF rating to protect from UV damage.

4. Wear Gloves

We all know that gloves can help to prevent frostbite, but taking this one step further by wearing liners, socks, and insulated gloves can help protect your hands from the elements and maintain their moisture, keeping them soft and supple all season long.

5. Stay Hydrated Internally

Hydration plays an important role in maintaining skin health. Drinking water and staying hydrated inside helps to ensure that your skin stays moisturized. Target a daily goal of at least 8-10 glasses of water to keep your skin functioning at peak performance. Green tea, even more, is full of antioxidants that can help to fight free-radical damage.

6. Don’t Forget Sun Protection

Many people think that sun protection is unnecessary during the winter months, but ultraviolet radiation is just as strong, and sometimes even stronger in the winter, due to the reflection off of snow. Use a moisturizing and protective sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30, preferably one with broad-spectrum protection.

7. Soak in a Warm Bubble Bath

Nothing says winter skincare quite like a relaxed bubble bath. The warm water can help to soften and nourish the skin, while the soothing effect helps you to relax – which, in turn, ensures that you are not stripping the skin of its moisture by frequently wiping it with a towel.

8. Ditch That Hot Shower Routine

While a warm bubble bath might sound like a luxury, hot showers can be a nightmare for your skin. The extreme temperature change from hot water to cold air outside can wreak havoc on your skin, stripping it of essential moisture. Opt for warm showers instead, and take them for a shorter duration to protect your skin.

9. Upgrade Your Skincare Routine

Consider incorporating nourishing ingredients to your skincare routine throughout the winter season. Rosehip oil, which is rich in essential fatty acids and vitamin A, is perfect for those with dry or mature skin. Oils high in vitamins C and E are essential antioxidants that can help reverse sun damage and improve skin’s elasticity.

10. Invest in a Humidifier

If you’re battling Central heating or you live in a particularly dry area, using a humidifier in your bedroom can help to add moisture to the air, and therefore your skin. Brisk, clean, filtered water should usually be chosen to leverage the benefits without any potential irritants that tap water may contain.

In closing, winter skin care is not just about slapping on a heavy coat of lotion or hunting down the best lip balm. It’s about making long-lasting changes to ensure that your skin stays supple, healthy, and protected during the chilly months. Keep these crucial tips in mind as you navigate the cold days and nights, and bid farewell to winter with a new and improved skincare routine. Enjoy the season and your glowing skincare results!
