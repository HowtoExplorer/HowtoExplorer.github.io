Following Footsteps: Trailblazing Women Who Redefined Travel


Travel has long been a predominantly male-dominated sphere, with women often finding it challenging to navigate a world that wasn’t intentionally built with their comfort and security in mind. But, in recent times, the tables have turned as a plethora of fearless women broke barriers, defied norms, and redefined the concept of travel, making it more inclusive and accessible for generations to come. Today, we pay homage to these pioneering women, who left their indelible mark on the world through their tireless travels.

Mary Kingsley: Trailblazing Anthropologist and Adventurer

Mary Henrietta Kingsley, the intrepid British adventurer, has been an inspiring figure for countless women for whom the world was vast and unknown. Starting her journey in the late 19th century, she traveled extensively to West Africa, where she became fascinated by the native cultures and fiercely defended them from cultural exploitation.

Through her travels, Kingsley wrote two influential books: “Travels in West Africa” and “West African Studies.” She contributed immensely to the fields of anthropology and biology, studying native tribes, for which she was awarded several honors posthumously.

Amelia Earhart: The Fearless Aviator

Amelia Earhart, an American aviator, served as the embodiment of the daring spirit of women travelers. Breaking gender stereotypes and barriers one flight at a time, Earhart completed an ambitious transatlantic journey, becoming the first woman to cross the Atlantic solo.

Earhart’s book “The Fun of It: Random Records of My Own Flying” showcased her courage and shaped the world’s opinion about women pilots. She eventually lost her life on what was to be her round-the-world flight but continues to be an icon of trailblazing travel.

Freya Stark: The Archaeologist and Travel Writer

A testament to exploring the less traveled paths of the world, Freya Stark was an intrepid traveler, archaeologist, and writer hailing from the early 20th century. She embarked on extensive journeys through the Middle East, including Iran, Iraq, and the Arabian Peninsula, leaving an indelible mark wherever she went.

Through her narratives, she painted vivid images of the places and people she encountered – all of which are chronicled in over thirty books. Freya’s fearless approach not only inspired others to travel but also rediscover ancient cultures and practices.

Jane Goodall: Lady of the Apes

Renowned conservationist and primatologist Jane Goodall had a passion for travel and understanding animal behavior. Her quest led her to the remote Gombe Stream in Tanzania, where she lived with chimpanzees – a field that had predominantly been held by male researchers.

Through her discipline, tenacity, and relentless curiosity, Dr. Goodall revolutionized the understanding of primate behavior, human origins, and conservation. Although her exploration was limited to her intimate fieldwork with the chimpanzees, it showcased the depth of travel in the name of knowledge and research.

Annapurna’s Solo Climber, Junko Tabei

When it comes to mountain climbing, the name of Junko Tabei resonates with the pursuit of the extraordinary. In 1975, Tabei became the first woman to reach the summit of Mount Everest, an extraordinary accomplishment that stirred the world. But what’s more astounding is that she didn’t stop there.

Tabei later conquered the 6,000-meter peaks – one of the great feats in mountaineering – a feat which only a handful of women have achieved. Her memoir, “Secrets of the Summit,” remains a testament to her thirst for the mountains and the women who dare to ascend them.

The journeys of these brave women have gone beyond merely traveling – they have defied societal norms, blurred geographical boundaries, and infused the spirit of adventure into the world of travel.

For all aspiring female travelers, we can look upon the fearless footsteps of these pioneering women for inspiration. They remind us that there is no_remote_ or challenging journey too daunting for a woman who aspires to explore the world.

Keep blazing the trails, ladies, and remember – the sky’s the limit!
