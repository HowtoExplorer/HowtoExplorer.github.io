How to Conduct an Informational Interview


Are you looking to gain insights into a particular industry or profession? Do you want to learn more about a company or organization without the formalities of an interview process? If so, an informational interview is the perfect way to get the information you need. But what exactly is an informational interview, and how do you conduct one effectively? What is an Informational Interview? An informational interview is an informal meeting between you and a professional in a field or industry you're interested in. This type of interview is designed to provide you with valuable information and insights about a particular career path, industry, or company. It's an opportunity to ask questions, learn from someone's experiences, and gain a deeper understanding of what it takes to succeed in that area. Why Conduct an Informational Interview? Conducting an informational interview can be incredibly beneficial in several ways:

Career research: It allows you to explore different industries, companies, or roles in depth. Networking: You can establish connections with professionals in your desired field, potentially leading to future job opportunities. Skill development: You'll gain knowledge and insights that can help you improve your skills and make informed decisions about your career. Networking: It can help you learn more about the inner workings of a company, its culture, and the day-to-day activities of various roles.

Preparing for the Interview Before conducting an informational interview, it's essential to be prepared. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get ready: Research the Professional

Look up the professional's online presence, including their LinkedIn profile, biography, and any relevant articles or interviews. Understand their current role, accomplishments, and experience in the field. Familiarize yourself with their work and recent projects to show your interest and enthusiasm.

Identify Your Goals

Reflect on what you want to achieve from the informational interview. Are you looking for career advice? Want to learn about a specific industry? Need insights on company culture? Be clear about your objectives to ensure you get the most out of the conversation.

Choose the Right Person

Reach out to people you know who work in the field or industry you're interested in. Utilize your network and ask for recommendations. Consider reaching out to industry leaders, experts, or successful professionals in your desired field.

Crafting Your Questions

Develop a list of thoughtful questions that show you've done your research and are genuinely interested in the professional's experience. Tailor your questions to the professional's background, industry, and any areas you'd like to explore further.

Communication and Etiquette

Schedule the interview in advance through email or phone. Confirm the details of the interview day, time, and location. Show up on time, be prepared, and be respectful of the professional's time.

During the Interview Follow these tips to ensure the interview flows smoothly and you get the most out of it: Listen Actively

Pay attention to the professional's responses, and ask follow-up questions to delve deeper into the topics. Avoid interrupting or dominating the conversation.

Maintain a Positive Attitude

Show genuine interest and enthusiasm for the professional's experience and insight. Express your gratitude for the opportunity to learn from them.

Take Notes

Bring a notebook and pen to take notes during the interview. This will help you recall key points and share them with your network. You can also record the conversation (with permission, of course), but it's not necessary to do so.

Follow Up

Send a thank-you note or email within 24 hours of the interview to reiterate your gratitude and share any key takeaways. Follow up on any additional questions or next steps discussed during the interview.

Making the Most of the Experience Informational interviews are an excellent opportunity to turn your curiosity into concrete knowledge. When conducting an informational interview, you should:

Actively listen to the professional's insights and experiences. Show genuine enthusiasm and interest in the field or industry. Leave with actions and ideas to improve your skills and network in the desired area.

After the Interview Take the time to share the insights and knowledge you gained during the interview with your network, either through social media or your next piece of outreach. By leveraging the connections and experiences gained, and focusing your professional development on specific areas of high interest, you will be well-equipped to take your career to the next level. Conducting an informational interview is your opportunity to amplify your curiosity and develop meaningful professional connections. By understanding what this type of interview entails, getting ready, crafting thoughtful questions, asking meaningful follow-up questions, and maintaining a positive attitude while taking notes, you can make the most of the experience. Don't hold back: lean in and use the insights gained to steer your career forward.
