Mind Your Ps and Qs: Politeness Strategies for Stronger Relationships


Politeness is a lost art in our modern fast-paced world. However, taking a moment to mind your Ps and Qs (considering your P’s and Q’s) can lead to stronger, deeper connections with others. This all-encompassing phrase refers to making sure your actions are polite and acknowledge the feelings and thoughts of those around you. Everyone can benefit from consciously practicing these politeness strategies, from teenagers navigating social situations to adults in their professional lives.

First and foremost, cultivate empathy. Empathy is the ability to understand and share the emotions of others. When you empathize with someone, you put yourself in their shoes and see the situation from their perspective. This is key to forming stronger relationships because it shows you care about their feelings and experiences. One way to practice empathy is to listen actively. When someone is speaking, try to truly listen to what they have to say instead of just waiting for your turn to talk. This requires patience, but it can lead to more meaningful conversations.

In addition to empathy, be tactful in your communications. Saying the right thing can help avoid sensitive topics or awkward situations. This doesn’t mean being dishonest or sugar-coating everything. Rather, it means finding a gentle and appropriate way to express your thoughts or ask difficult questions. Tactful communication can diffuse tension and promote understanding.

Another important politeness strategy is being on time. Punctuality shows respect for other people’s time and demonstrates that you value the plans you’ve made with them. If you know you’re going to be late, reach out to the person and let them know as soon as possible. Explain the situation, and if it’s a recurring issue, consider setting boundaries for yourself to ensure better punctuality in the future.

Manners are another crucial element of politeness. Simple acts like holding doors open, saying “please” and “thank you,” and offering a handshake or hug can strengthen relationships. In fact, in a study conducted by Vicki Hower, 88% of respondents said that tactfulness, manners, and a positive attitude are highly important in forming strong relationships.

Another aspect of politeness is maintaining eye contact. This may vary depending on cultural norms, but maintaining eye contact while speaking with someone can show that you’re genuinely engaged in the conversation. It can also convey empathy and understanding.

Humor is another great tool for strengthening relationships. A well-timed joke or witty remark can defuse tension, show rapport between two people, and simply make life more enjoyable. However, be cautious with humor, as not everyone finds the same jokes or antics amusing. It’s essential to gauge the recipient’s feelings and reactions.

Lastly, offering support can enhance your relationships. Be there for friends, family, and colleagues when they need you. Whether it’s lending an ear to listen or offering a helping hand, your support will be appreciated and remembered.

Politeness strategies like these may seem simple or old-fashioned, but incorporating them into your daily interpersonal interactions can have a profound impact on your relationships. By mind-ing your Ps and Qs, you’ll cultivate empathy, tact, punctuality, manners, eye contact, humor, and support. This, in turn, will lead to stronger connections and a more fulfilling life. So go ahead, practice these politeness strategies, and enjoy the benefits of deeper, more meaningful relationships.
