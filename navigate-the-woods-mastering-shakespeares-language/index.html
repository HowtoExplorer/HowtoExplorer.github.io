Navigate the Woods: Mastering Shakespeare's Language


Shakespeare’s works have been acclaimed as some of the greatest pieces in literature. His mastery of language and the depth of his characters are unparalleled. But for those unfamiliar with his prose, navigating through the woods of his linguistic complexities can be a daunting task. Nevertheless, with these tips, you can master his language and discover the true beauty of his works.

Embrace the iambic pentameter

Shakespearean verse follows a structure called iambic pentameter. It consists of ten syllables per line, with a pattern of unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable. This rhythmical structure impacts the way characters speak and adds an extra layer to the language of his works. Familiarizing yourself with the rhythm can aid in understanding and appreciating the emotions of the characters better.

Become familiar with the figures of speech

Shakespeare’s works are rich in poetic language, which includes metaphors, similes, and many other figures of speech. Understanding these literary devices will deepen your comprehension of the works. There are numerous figures of speech in Shakespeare, including:

  • Metaphor: Comparing two unlike things by asserting that one is the other. Example: “All the world’s a stage, and all the men and women merely players.”

  • Simile: Comparing two unlike things by using “like” or “as.” Example: “As a rose by any other name would smell as sweet.”

  • Hyperbole: An exaggerated statement or claim for effect. Example: “Oh, I am a very following your affairs with most acute judgment, and steady gait.”

Play with the historical language

Shakespearean language, though familiar today, was once significantly different from the English spoken at the time. This language evolved over the centuries, with some words falling out of use while new terms were coined. Understanding some of the historical vocabulary can take you closer to Shakespeare’s world. The websites like The Oxford Shakespeare Corpus and The Shakespeare Superpack can help you learn this unique language.

Embrace the humor

Shakespeare’s comedies often present witty, clever dialogues filled with puns, wordplay, and other forms of humor. His characters could hilarity with some precise pronunciation or casino reviews. His works are full of opportunities to let out laughter. Engaging with the humor in Shakespeare’s works can also shed light on the humor in the characters’ personalities.

Never fear the prose

Shakespeare’s tragedies might be darker in theme, but his prose remains rich and expressive. The characters’ soliloquies, monologues, and dialogues are profound and moving. Even though the speech may be prosaic, it still carries the weight of poetic language, rich with emotional depth. Shakespeare’s capacity to convey complex emotions through simple language has been unmatched throughout history.

Mastering Shakespeare’s language is a thrilling voyage. Shakespeare’s works are simultaneously timeless and contemporary, and his language continues to challenge and influence modern literature. Learning this language, despite its complexity, can reveal the intricate world of his stories and the depth of his characters. So, embrace the iambic pentameter, familiarize yourself with the figures of speech, understand the historical language, and revel in the humor and prose. And more than anything, keep exploring and appreciating the literary genius that is William Shakespeare.
