The Process of Becoming a Theater Critic: Key Components and Pitfalls


For many, attending theater performances and analyzing them comes naturally. But can this passion transform into a career? Can anyone become a theater critic? The simple answer is no. The art of theater criticism is a skill that requires dedication, a good understanding of the industry, and - most importantly - an ability to recognize the essential components of a show.

To become an effective theater critic, it’s crucial to familiarize oneself with the major aspects of performance evaluation. Here, we delve into the key components of theater criticism and the possible pitfalls one must watch out for.

Understanding the Theater Industry

Immersing oneself in the theater world is an essential first step. Attending shows, keeping up with recent productions, following relevant news – these are just some of the ways you can familiarize yourself with the landscape of the performing arts.

It’s also important to understand the various theatrical genres. Knowing the difference between a tragedy and a farce, a musical and an opera, a play and a ballet, helps to properly critique a performance. Experience in diverse theater styles enriches the critic’s perspective and makes for more dynamic critiques.

Analyzing the Show and Its Individual Elements

Analyzing a show goes beyond observing the whole production – dissecting its parts gives a deeper understanding. A good theater critic examines multiple elements such as:

  1. Script and Storytelling: Analyze the plot, character development, dialogue, and themes. A skilled playwright navigates complex character arcs and delivers profound themes worth critiquing.

  2. Direction and Choreography: A theater critic should assess how the director brings the text to life, handles the pace of the performance, and possibly averts or embraces theatrical styles.

  3. Performance and Acting: Evaluate the individual performances and the ensemble acting, focusing on the talents of the actors and their resonant portrayals of their characters.

  4. Design and Production: Analyze set and costume design, lighting, sound, and other technical elements. What role do these play in shaping the theatrical experience?

Developing a Style and Voice as a Critic

Becoming a theater critic is more than just evaluating performances - it involves developing a distinctive writing style. As your style emerges, you must also be aware of your voice and perspective as a critic. It’s essential to be genuine, assertive, and consistent in conveying your thoughts and opinions.

Develop a system where you can monitor your thoughts, emotions, and reactions. This helps in articulating your perceptions clearer and more thoughtfully. You may also want to consider reading other critics who you admire and see how they approach their reviews to learn more.

Avoiding Pitfalls

As you embark on your theater criticism journey, watch out for these common pitfalls:

  1. Bias: It’s natural to favor certain theatrical genres or productions. However, falling into this trap narrows your coverage and ultimately prejudices your reviews.

  2. Overlooking Nuances: Avoid reducing performances to just one-dimensional evaluations. While keeping the bigger picture in mind, nuance and subtlety in performances should not be overlooked.

  3. Lacking Empathy: In reviewing performances, develop a sincere understanding and empathy for characters and their development that you critique. This puts you in better stead to appreciate the outcomes and comment on their relevancy.

Cultivating Relationships

Lastly, cultivate relationships with theater professionals. Engage with the actors, directors, designers, and playwrights, learn from them, and occasionally critique their work afterwards. This not only provides you with invaluable insight but also expands your network.

As with any career, nothing short of diligent practice can make you an expert theater critic. The most important components of this role are passion and commitment, tempered by self-awareness and humility. With time and effort, you will surely find success in your journey as a theater critic.
