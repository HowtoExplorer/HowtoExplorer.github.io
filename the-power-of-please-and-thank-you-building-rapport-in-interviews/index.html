The Power of 'Please' and 'Thank You': Building Rapport in Interviews


In the world of interviews, first impressions are everything. Studies have consistently shown that politeness can be a decisive factor in establishing rapport and achieving desired outcomes. In this guide, we will explore the significance of ‘please’ and ’thank you’ in building rapport during interviews.

The Art of Polite Communication

Distinguished professionals are aware of the value of polite communication. It is common knowledge that exhibiting courteous behavior can go a long way in enhancing a good impression. The power of ‘please’ and ’thank you’ is undeniable, as they convey respect and attentiveness to the interviewer. By incorporating such politeness into an interview, the interviewee can create a positive environment that fosters meaningful connections.

Building Rapport Through ‘Please’

Using the word ‘please’ in an interview can create a submissive, yet polite atmosphere, signaling to the interviewer that you are keenly aware of their needs and are eager to fulfill them. However, using ‘please’ too often can border on excessive and even annoying; it is crucial to employ it judiciously.

A feasible approach would be to use ‘please’ to indicate your requests or inquiries, such as “Could you please provide more details about this role?” or “May I take a moment to share some important accomplishments from my past experience?”. This approach establishes a refined and professional demeanor without being excessively meek.

The Power of ‘Thank You’

The humble ’thank you,’ a simple two-word phrase, holds significant power in the realm of interviews. Expressing gratitude in an interview demonstrates humility and sincerity, enhancing the candidate’s likability and establishing a friendly, cooperative rapport with the interviewer.

Grateful language such as “Thank you for considering me for this opportunity,” or “I appreciate your time and the questions you’ve asked,” can signify the candidate’s admiration for the interviewer and the organization. It also reveals the candidate’s positive attitude, making them a valuable addition to any team.

Practicing ‘Please’ and ‘Thank You’ in Action

To successfully use ‘please’ and ’thank you’ during an interview, consider the following strategies:

  1. Repetition: Find the right balance that makes your interactions with the interviewer seem natural and sincere. Too much politeness can sound insincere or comical, so finding balance is key.
  2. Be specific: Show your gratitude and request for help by mentioning details about the interviewer or the organization, such as “Thank you for providing me with this insightful company overview, Mr. Smith.”
  3. Authenticity: Be genuine in your expressions of politeness. Ensuring that politeness is in alignment with your personality and values will make your interaction more genuine and persuasive.

In Conclusion

By putting the power of ‘please’ and ’thank you’ to work in interviews, candidates demonstrate respect, attentiveness, and a genuine interest in the opportunity. This politeness demonstrates professionalism and sets a strong foundation for building rapport with interviewers. So, the next time you enter an interview, remember to be polite and make the most of your interactions. The connection you forge could influence your success significantly.
