Resume FAQs: Your Most Burning Questions Answered by Resume Experts


Whether you’re an experienced professional looking to switch careers or a recent graduate starting your job search, crafting a compelling and insightful resume is an essential skill. While there are countless articles and resources on how to write a resume, many people still have lingering questions and uncertainties. That’s where our team of resume experts come in. We’re breaking down some of the most frequently asked resume questions to provide you with the best guidance and techniques to elevate your job application.

1. How long should my resume be?

While there’s no hard and fast rule, in general, keep your resume to one or two pages at the most. Our experts recommend prioritizing relevant information and tailoring your resume to the specific job you’re applying for. Aim for concise bullet points and clear formatting to make your resume easy to read. Remember, recruiters and hiring managers often spend less than 30 seconds on each resume, so make yours count!

2. When should I include references on my resume?

References are generally not necessary unless specifically requested by the employer. In fact, including references on your resume can clutter the document and distract from your accomplishments. Instead, experts recommend having a separate reference sheet available upon request. This allows you to be selective in choosing who you want to recommend and provides a more professional appearance to your resume.

3. What’s the best resume format to use?

There are three primary resume formats: chronological, functional, and combination. The chronological format is the most common and preferred by most employers, as it highlights your work history and progression over time. The functional format focuses on your skills and abilities, making it suitable for career changers or those with gaps in their employment history. The combination format is a hybrid of the first two, showcasing both chronological work experience and functional skill sets. Choose the format that best highlights your strengths and fits the job you’re seeking.

4. How do I address experience and skills on my resume?

When listing your work experience, start with your most recent position and work backward. For each role, include your job title, the company name, and dates of employment. Use concise bullet points to detail your responsibilities and achievements, emphasizing your contributions and relevant skills. To describe your skills, use action verbs and incorporate specific examples like quantifiable results or skill application in past roles.

5. Do I need to include my GPA on my resume?

While GPA and college major may have been important to include on a resume a few decades ago, these details are no longer necessary for most job applications. As long as you’ve earned a bachelor’s degree from an accredited university, recent graduates typically don’t need to include their GPA or college major. If you’re a grad student or have an exceptional academic background, you may consider including your GPA, but only if it’s a 3.5 or higher.

6. How should I handle employment gaps?

Employment gaps can be a challenge to address on your resume, but our experts suggest being upfront and honest. If you took time off to pursue further education, care for a family member, or travel, briefly explain the gap in a cover letter or during the interview. Focus on what you accomplished during that time and how it may have contributed to your professional development. If you were between jobs and volunteering or freelancing during the gap, those experiences can be great conversation starters.

7. Is it necessary to get my resume reviewed by a professional resume service?

While professional resume services may seem like the perfect shortcut, hiring one can be costly and often doesn’t provide personalized attention. Instead, focus on tailoring your resume to each job and requesting feedback from friends, mentors, and professional network members. Use online resources like resume templates and forums for additional guidance, and consider investing in a paid platform if you need help optimizing your resume for Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).

8. How should I handle multiple career shifts or a non-linear employment history?

If you’ve had multiple career shifts or an unconventional job history, consider a functional or combination resume format to highlight your transferable skills and common themes across your roles. Emphasize relevant achievements in your work experience section, and be prepared to explain the reasoning behind your career choices during interviews. Showcasing a strong cover letter can help complement your resume and provide context for your career shifts.

A well-crafted resume is a vital tool in your job search arsenal. By addressing these common resume FAQs, you’ll be better equipped to create an impressive resume tailored to your unique background and career goals. Our expert advice can help you stand out among competitors and land the interviews you deserve. Happy job hunting!
