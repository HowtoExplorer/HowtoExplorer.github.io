Say goodbye to ASE: Building your Functional Resume


Are you tired of the traditional chronological resume? Have you been told that your resume is getting lost in the sea of application tracking systems (ATS) used by recruiters and hiring managers? It may be time to say goodbye to ASE (Application-Shell Employment) and devise a functional resume that highlights your skills and experiences. In the modern world of job hunting, a functional resume can differentiate you from other candidates, emphasizing your strengths, skills, and achievements in a more organized and clear manner.

Breaking Away from the Traditional Chronological Resume

The chronological resume, where your work history is presented in reverse-chronological order, has been the standard format for job applications. However, employers today are looking for candidates that present skills and experiences that are relevant to their current needs. Here’s why a functional resume can outshine the traditional chronological version:

  1. Emphasizes skills and accomplishments: By categorizing your skills into functional areas, you can accentuate your accomplishments in those areas. This allows hiring managers to quickly gauge your compatibility with the job requirements.

  2. Adaptable to job transitions: A functional resume is excellent for those who have gone through job transitions, career changes, or gaps in employment. By focusing on skills and achievements, you can demonstrate value to future employers, irrespective of the chronological order of your work history.

  3. Highlights transferable skills: Whether you’re switching industries or moving to a new field, your functional resume can effectively convey relevant transferable skills. This will make your application stand out to recruiters in different areas, increasing your chances of success.

Crafting the Perfect Functional Resume

When crafting a functional resume, you’ll want to follow a few key strategies to ensure it captures the attention of your desired employers.

  1. Tailor your resume: While a functional resume is all about skills and achievements, customizing your resume for each job posting significantly increases your chances of getting a call for an interview. Understand the job requirements and reformulate your resume to align with the skills and experiences recruiters are looking for.

  2. Clear headings and sections: A well-structured resume makes reading more convenient for you and the hiring manager. Use clear headings and section titles such as “Skills,” “Achievements,” “Professional Experiences,” and “Education.”

  3. Explicit statement of objective: At the top of your resume, include a concise, objective statement that clearly indicates the position you’re aiming for and the value you bring to the table.

  4. Summarize skills and achievements: In the “Skills” section, list your most relevant and marketable skills, including both technical and soft skills. When summarizing achievements, use the STAR method - Situation, Task, Action, and Result - to give clear examples of how you’ve utilized your skills effectively in the past.

  5. Keep it concise: Functional resumes can often run longer than traditional resumes due to the added matrix of skills and experiences. However, make sure to keep it concise and to the point. Aim for a length of one to two pages.

  6. Proofread and customize formatting: Before sending your functional resume out to potential employers, make sure to proofread it several times and customize the formatting to match the industry you’re applying to. This will give your resume a polished, professional appearance.

Final Thoughts

The Functional Resume era might just be what you need to bypass ATS filtering and capture the attention of hiring managers. Show them what you can bring to the table, not just what you’ve done in the past. By building a functional resume, you’ll showcase your skills and experience effectively and give yourself the competitive edge in a busy job market. Say goodbye to ASE and hello to the new world of job hunting where the functional resume reigns supreme.
