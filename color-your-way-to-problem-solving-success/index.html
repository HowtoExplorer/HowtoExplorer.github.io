Color Your Way to Problem Solving Success


Are you aware of the incredible power that a single hue has on our everyday lives? Colors directly influence our emotions, decisions, and perceptions. In his book Blink, Malcolm Gladwell explores the power of split-second decision-making, and how colors can sometimes affect our responses without us consciously realizing it. Armed with this knowledge, we can harness the potential of colors to enhance our problem-solving skills. Let’s explore how our favorite shades can help us create a colorful solution!

Step 1: Identify Your Problem

Begin by clearly identifying the issue or challenge at hand. This first step is crucial, as it lays the foundation for the entire process. Observe the problem and gather all the relevant information as you work towards understanding it as deeply as possible. Remember, this is where context, definitions, limitations, constraints, and related challenges play an important role.

Step 2: Choose Your Colors

With your problem laid out, the next step is to pick the right colors that resonate with it. Different colors carry their own symbolic meanings and evoke specific emotions. Consider the mood you want to create and the message you want to convey with your solution. Here’s a brief guide to get you started:

  1. Blue: This color represents trust, stability, and calmness. Use blue when you require clear communication, professionalism, and a sense of dependability.
  2. Green: Green symbolizes growth, balance, and harmony. Harness this shade when you need to brainstorm innovative ideas or find a balance between opposing forces.
  3. Yellow: Yellow conveys energy, optimism, and cheerfulness. When your problem requires a creative and playful approach, opt for yellow.
  4. Red: Red sparks passion, action, and urgency. Reach for it when the problem demands an immediate and assertive response.
  5. Black: Black represents power, sophistication, and elegance. Use black to design a solution that requires concentration, determination, and authority.

Step 3: Visualization

Combine your selected colors on paper or a digital canvas. Begin mapping out the connections between different aspects of the problem, and see how the colors interact with each other. You may find patterns, links, or notifications you hadn’t seen previously. Visualization allows you to explore the problem’s geometry from various angles, providing fresh insights and ideas to overcome the challenge.

Step 4: Abstraction

Now that you have colors and shapes outlining the problem, start seeking a more abstract solution. Extract key points from the visualization, which can be visual or other (such as metaphors, concepts, or theories). By focusing on the most significant elements, you’ll have a higher chance of developing an effective solution.

Step 5: Implementation

With your abstract solution in mind, it’s time to put it into action. Remember to respect the constraints and limitations you identified earlier in the process. Thoroughly test your solution to ensure it genuinely addresses the problem and performs as intended.

Step 6: Evaluation and Revision

See how well your solution performs and make necessary adjustments. This step might involve going back through the process to tweak colors, refine the idea, and make improvements where needed. An essential element of problem-solving is flexibility and adaptability – we need to remain open to modifying the solution based on new data or insights that arise during the implementation process.

Embrace Your Inner Artist, Enhance Your Problem-Solving Skills

Colors play a critical role in helping us develop a deeper understanding of challenges. They allow us to see the invisible solutions and to break free from conventional thinking. So the next time you face a complex problem, grab your favorite drawing pad and a set of colored pencils, or open up your digital design software. Let the vibrant world of colors guide you on your path to problem-solving success!
